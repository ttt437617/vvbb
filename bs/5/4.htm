Chapter 5: Hymn to the Absolute Truth

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Brahma-samhitā 5.4

tat-kiñjalkam tad-amśānām

tat-patrāni śriyām api

SYNONYMS

tat — of that (lotus); kiñjalkam — the petals; tat-amśānām — of His (Krishna's) fragmental portions; tat — of that (lotus); patrāni — the leaves; śriyām — of the gopīs (headed by Śrīmatī Rādhārānī); api — also.

TRANSLATION

The whorl of that eternal realm Gokula is the hexagonal abode of Krishna. Its petals are the abodes of gopīs who are part and parcel of Krishna to whom they are most lovingly devoted and are similar in essence. The petals shine beautifully like so many walls. The extended leaves of that lotus are the gardenlike dhāma, i.e. spiritual abode of Śrī Rādhikā, the most beloved of Krishna.

PURPORT

The transcendental Gokula is shaped like the lotus. The eternal world is like a hexagonal figure; in that the entities Śrī Rādhā-Krishna, appearing in the form of a mantra consisting of eighteen transcendental letters, are centered. The propagating manifestations emanating from the cit potency are present there with the said entities as the center. Śrī Rādhā-Krishna is the primary cause or the seed Himself. Gopāla-tāpanī says, "Omkāra" signifies the All-Powerful Gopāla and His potency; and "klīm" is the same as omkāra. Hence kāma-bīja or the primary cause of all-love, is connotative of the entities Śrī Rādhā-Krishna.
