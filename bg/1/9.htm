Chapter 1: Observing the Armies on the Battlefield of Kurukshetra

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Bhagavad-gītā As It Is 1.9

anye ca bahavah śūrā

mad-arthe tyakta-jīvitāh

nānā-śastra-praharanāh

sarve yuddha-viśāradāh

SYNONYMS

anye — others; ca — also; bahavah — in great numbers; śūrāh — heroes; mat-arthe — for my sake; tyakta-jīvitāh — prepared to risk life; nānā — many; śastra — weapons; praharanāh — equipped with; sarve — all of them; yuddha-viśāradāh — experienced in military science.

TRANSLATION

There are many other heroes who are prepared to lay down their lives for my sake. All of them are well equipped with different kinds of weapons, and all are experienced in military science.

PURPORT

As far as the others are concerned — like Jayadratha, Kritavarmā and Śalya — all are determined to lay down their lives for Duryodhana's sake. In other words, it is already concluded that all of them would die in the Battle of Kurukshetra for joining the party of the sinful Duryodhana. Duryodhana was, of course, confident of his victory on account of the above-mentioned combined strength of his friends.
