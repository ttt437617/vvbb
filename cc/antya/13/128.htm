Antya-līlāChapter 13: Pastimes with Jagadānanda Pandita and Raghunātha Bhatta Gosvāmī

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 13.128

pika-svara-kantha, tāte rāgera vibhāga

eka-śloka padite phirāya tina-cāri rāga

SYNONYMS

pika-svara-kantha — a very sweet voice like a cuckoo's; tāte — above that; rāgera — of tunes; vibhāga — division; eka-śloka — one verse; padite — reciting; phirāya — changes; tina-cāri rāga — three or four different tunes.

TRANSLATION

His voice was as sweet as a cuckoo's, and he would recite each verse of Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam in three or four tunes. Thus his recitations were very sweet to hear.
