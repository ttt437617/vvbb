Antya-līlāChapter 13: Pastimes with Jagadānanda Pandita and Raghunātha Bhatta Gosvāmī

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 13.76

mukhe tāra jhāla gela, jihvā kare jvālā

vrindāvanera 'pīlu' khāite ei eka līlā

SYNONYMS

mukhe tāra — in their mouths; jhāla — the taste of chili; gela — went; jihvā — the tongue; kare jvālā — was burning; vrindāvanera — of Vrindāvana; pīlu — the pīlu fruit; khāite — eating; ei — this; eka līlā — a pastime.

TRANSLATION

The hot chili-like taste burned the tongues of those who chewed the seeds. Thus the eating of pīlu fruits from Vrindāvana became a pastime of Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu's.
