Antya-līlāChapter 13: Pastimes with Jagadānanda Pandita and Raghunātha Bhatta Gosvāmī

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 13.55

sanātana tāńre jāni' lajjita ha-ilā

balite lāgilā pandita hāndi culāte dharilā

SYNONYMS

sanātana — Sanātana Gosvāmī; tāńre — him; jāni' — knowing; lajjita ha-ilā — became ashamed; balite lāgilā — began to speak; pandita — Jagadānanda Pandita; hāndi — the cooking pot; culāte — on the stove; dharilā — kept.

TRANSLATION

Sanātana Gosvāmī, however, knew Jagadānanda Pandita very well and was consequently somewhat ashamed. Jagadānanda therefore left the cooking pot on the stove and spoke as follows.
