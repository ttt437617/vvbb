Antya-līlāChapter 13: Pastimes with Jagadānanda Pandita and Raghunātha Bhatta Gosvāmī

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 13.75

ye keha jāne, āńti cushite lāgila

ye nā jāne gaudiyā pīlu cāvāñā khāila

SYNONYMS

ye — those who; keha — some; jāne — knew; āńti — seeds; cushite lāgila — began to lick; ye — those who; nā jāne — did not know; gaudiyā — Bengali devotees; pīlu — the pīlu fruit; cāvāñā — chewing; khāila — ate.

TRANSLATION

Those devotees who were familiar with pīlu fruits sucked on the seeds, but the Bengali devotees who did not know what they were chewed the seeds and swallowed them.
