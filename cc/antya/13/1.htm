Antya-līlāChapter 13: Pastimes with Jagadānanda Pandita and Raghunātha Bhatta Gosvāmī

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 13.1

krishna-viccheda-jātārtyā

kshīne cāpi manas-tanū

dadhāte phullatām bhāvair

yasya tam gauram āśraye

SYNONYMS

krishna-viccheda — by separation from Krishna; jāta — produced; ārtyā — by the pain; kshīne — thin, exhausted; ca — and; api — although; manah — mind; tanū — and body; dadhāte — assumes; phullatām — developed state; bhāvaih — by ecstatic emotions; yasya — of whom; tam — unto Him; gauram — Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu; āśraye — I take shelter.

TRANSLATION

Let me take shelter at the lotus feet of Lord Gauracandra. His mind became exhausted and His body very thin from the pain of separation from Krishna, but when He felt ecstatic love for the Lord, He again became fully developed.
