Antya-līlāChapter 13: Pastimes with Jagadānanda Pandita and Raghunātha Bhatta Gosvāmī

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 13.52

rātula vastra dekhi' pandita premāvishta ha-ilā

'mahāprabhura prasāda' jāni' tāńhāre puchilā

SYNONYMS

rātula — red; vastra — cloth; dekhi' — seeing; pandita — Jagadānanda Pandita; prema-āvishta ha-ilā — became overwhelmed in ecstatic love; mahāprabhura prasāda — the blessed gift of Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu; jāni' — thinking; tāńhāre puchilā — inquired from him.

TRANSLATION

Assuming the reddish cloth to be a gift from Caitanya Mahāprabhu, Jagadānanda Pandita was overwhelmed with ecstatic love. Thus he questioned Sanātana Gosvāmī.
