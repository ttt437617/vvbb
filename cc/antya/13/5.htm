Antya-līlāChapter 13: Pastimes with Jagadānanda Pandita and Raghunātha Bhatta Gosvāmī

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 13.5

kalāra śaralāte, śayana, ati kshīna kāya

śaralāte hāda lāge, vyathā haya gāya

SYNONYMS

kalāra śaralāte — on the dry bark of a plantain tree; śayana — lying down; ati — very; kshīna kāya — skinny body; śaralāte — on the dry bark of a plantain tree; hāda lāge — bones contact; vyathā — pain; haya — is; gāya — in the body.

TRANSLATION

Because He was very thin, when He lay down to rest on the dry bark of plantain trees, it caused Him pain in His bones.
