Antya-līlāChapter 13: Pastimes with Jagadānanda Pandita and Raghunātha Bhatta Gosvāmī

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 13.67

rāsa-sthalīra vālu āra govardhanera śilā

śushka pakka pīlu-phala āra guñjā-mālā

SYNONYMS

rāsa-sthalīra vālu — sand from the place where Lord Krishna held His rāsa dance; āra — and; govardhanera śilā — a stone from Govardhana Hill; śushka — dry; pakka — mature; pīlu-phala — pīlu fruit; āra — also; guñjā-mālā — a garland of small conchshells.

TRANSLATION

The gifts consisted of some sand from the site of the rāsa-līlā, a stone from Govardhana Hill, dry ripened pīlu fruits and a garland of small conchshells.
