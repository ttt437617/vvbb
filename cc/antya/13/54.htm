Antya-līlāChapter 13: Pastimes with Jagadānanda Pandita and Raghunātha Bhatta Gosvāmī

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 13.54

śuni' panditera mane krodha upajila

bhātera hāndi hāte lañā mārite āila

SYNONYMS

śuni' — hearing; panditera — of Jagadānanda Pandita; mane — in the mind; krodha — anger; upajila — arose; bhātera hāndi — the cooking pot; hāte — in his hand; lañā — taking; mārite āila — was ready to beat.

TRANSLATION

Hearing this, Jagadānanda Pandita immediately became very angry and took a cooking pot in his hand, intending to beat Sanātana Gosvāmī.
