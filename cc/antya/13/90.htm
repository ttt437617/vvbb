Antya-līlāChapter 13: Pastimes with Jagadānanda Pandita and Raghunātha Bhatta Gosvāmī

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 13.90

kāśī haite calilā teńho gauda-patha diyā

sańge sevaka cale tāńra jhāli vahiyā

SYNONYMS

kāśī haite — from Kāśī; calilā — proceeded; teńho — he; gauda-patha diyā — by the path through Bengal; sańge — along with him; sevaka — one servant; cale — goes; tāńra — his; jhāli — baggage; vahiyā — carrying.

TRANSLATION

Accompanied by a servant carrying his baggage, Raghunātha Bhatta started from Vārānasī and traveled along the path leading through Bengal.
