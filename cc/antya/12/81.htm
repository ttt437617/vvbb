Antya-līlāChapter 12: The Loving Dealings Between Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu and Jagadānanda Pandita

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 12.81

nityānande kahilā — "tumi nā āsiha bāra-bāra

tathāi āmāra sańga ha-ibe tomāra"

SYNONYMS

nityānande — unto Nityānanda Prabhu; kahilā — said; tumi — You; nā āsiha — do not come; bāra-bāra — again and again; tathāi — there (in Bengal); āmāra — My; sańga — association; ha-ibe — there will be; tomāra — Your.

TRANSLATION

The Lord specifically advised Nityānanda Prabhu, "You should not come here again and again. You will have My association in Bengal."
