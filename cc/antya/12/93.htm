Antya-līlāChapter 12: The Loving Dealings Between Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu and Jagadānanda Pandita

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 12.93

mātā kahe, — "kata rāndhi uttama vyañjana

nimāñi ihāń khāya, — icchā haya mora mana

SYNONYMS

mātā kahe — mother said; kata — how many; rāndhi — I cook; uttama vyañjana — first-class vegetables; nimāñi — Nimāi; ihāń — here; khāya — may eat; icchā — desire; haya — is; mora mana — my mind.

TRANSLATION

Śacīmātā said, "I wish Nimāi would eat all the nice vegetables I cook. That is my desire.
