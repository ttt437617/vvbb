Antya-līlāChapter 18: Rescuing the Lord from the Sea

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 18.61

'āmi — bada ojhā jāni bhūta chādāite'

mantra padi' śrī-hasta dilā tāhāra māthāte

SYNONYMS

āmi — I; bada — big; ojhā — exorcist; jāni — I know; bhūta — ghost; chādāite — how to exorcise; mantra padi' — chanting hymns; śrī-hasta — his hand; dilā — placed; tāhāra māthāte — on his head.

TRANSLATION

"I am a famous exorcist," he said, "and I know how to rid you of this ghost." He then chanted some mantras and placed his hand on top of the fisherman's head.
