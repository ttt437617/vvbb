Antya-līlāChapter 18: Rescuing the Lord from the Sea

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 18.57

ekā rātrye buli' matsya māriye nirjane

bhūta-preta āmāra nā lāge 'nrisimha'-smarane

SYNONYMS

ekā — alone; rātrye — at night; buli' — wandering; matsya — fish; māriye — I kill; nirjane — in solitary places; bhūta-preta — ghosts; āmāra — me; nā lāge — cannot touch; nrisimha-smarane — by remembering Nrisimha.

TRANSLATION

"I wander alone at night killing fish in solitary places, but because I remember the hymn to Lord Nrisimha, ghosts do not touch me.
