Antya-līlāChapter 18: Rescuing the Lord from the Sea

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 18.92

krishnera kalaha rādhā-sane, gopī-gana sei-kshane,

hemābja-vane gelā lukāite

ākantha-vapu jale paiśe, mukha-mātra jale bhāse,

padme-mukhe nā pāri cinite

SYNONYMS

krishnera — of Krishna; kalaha — quarrel; rādhā-sane — with Rādhā; gopī-gana — the gopīs; sei-kshane — at that moment; hema-abja — of white lotus flowers; vane — in the forest; gelā — went; lukāite — to hide; ākantha — up to the neck; vapu — body; jale — into the water; paiśe — enter; mukha-mātra — only the lotus flowers and the faces; jale — in the water; bhāse — float; padme-mukhe — between the lotus flowers and the faces; nā pāri — not able; cinite — to discern.

TRANSLATION

"Then Krishna quarreled with Rādhārānī, and all the gopīs hid themselves in a cluster of white lotus flowers. They submerged their bodies up to their necks in the water. Only their faces floated above the surface, and the faces were indistinguishable from the lotuses.
