Antya-līlāChapter 18: Rescuing the Lord from the Sea

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 18.46

"kaha, jāliyā, ei dike dekhilā eka-jana?

tomāra ei daśā kene, — kahata' kārana?"

SYNONYMS

kaha — please say; jāliyā — O fisherman; ei dike — in this direction; dekhilā — did you see; eka-jana — someone; tomāra — your; ei — this; daśā — condition; kene — why; kahata' — kindly speak; kārana — the cause.

TRANSLATION

"My dear fisherman," he said, "why are you behaving like this? Have you seen someone hereabouts? What is the cause of your behavior? Please tell us."
