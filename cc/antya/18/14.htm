Antya-līlāChapter 18: Rescuing the Lord from the Sea

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 18.14

koti-yuga paryanta yadi likhaye ganeśa

eka-dinera līlāra tabu nāhi pāya śesha

SYNONYMS

koti-yuga — millions of millenniums; paryanta — to the extent of; yadi — if; likhaye — writes; ganeśa — the demigod Ganeśa (son of Lord Śiva); eka-dinera — of one day; līlāra — of pastimes; tabu — still; nāhi pāya — can not reach; śesha — the limit.

TRANSLATION

If Ganeśa, Lord Śiva's son and the expert scribe of the demigods, tried for millions of millenniums to fully describe one day of the Lord's pastimes, he would be unable to find their limit.
