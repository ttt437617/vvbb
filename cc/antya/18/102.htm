Antya-līlāChapter 18: Rescuing the Lord from the Sea

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 18.102

vrindāvane taru-latā, adbhuta tāhāra kathā,

bāra-māsa dhare phula-phala

vrindāvane devī-gana, kuñja-dāsī yata jana,

phala pādi' āniyā sakala

SYNONYMS

vrindāvane — at Vrindāvana; taru-latā — trees and creepers; adbhuta — wonderful; tāhāra kathā — their story; bāra-māsa — twelve months; dhare — produce; phula-phala — fruits and flowers; vrindāvane — at Vrindāvana; devī-gana — all the gopīs; kuñja-dāsī — maidservants in the bowers; yata jana — as many persons as there are; phala pādi' — picking fruits; āniyā — bringing; sakala — all varieties.

TRANSLATION

"In Vrindāvana, the trees and creepers are wonderful because throughout the entire year they produce all kinds of fruits and flowers. The gopīs and maidservants in the bowers of Vrindāvana picked these fruits and flowers and brought them before Rādhā and Krishna.
