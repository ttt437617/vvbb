Antya-līlāChapter 18: Rescuing the Lord from the Sea

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 18.83

patta-vastra, alańkāre, samarpiyā sakhī-kare,

sūkshma-śukla-vastra-paridhāna

krishna lañā kāntā-gana, kailā jalāvagāhana,

jala-keli racilā suthāma

SYNONYMS

patta-vastra — silk garments; alańkāre — ornaments; samarpiyā — entrusting; sakhī-kare — in the hands of their gopī friends; sūkshma — very fine; śukla-vastra — white cloth; paridhāna — putting on; krishna — Lord Krishna; lañā — taking; kāntā-gana — the beloved gopīs; kailā — performed; jala-avagāhana — bathing in the water; jala-keli — pastimes in the water; racilā — planned; su-thāma — very nice.

TRANSLATION

"All the gopīs entrusted their silken garments and ornaments to the care of their friends and then put on fine white cloth. Lord Krishna, taking His beloved gopīs with Him, bathed and performed very nice pastimes in the water of the Yamunā.
