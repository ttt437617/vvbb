Antya-līlāChapter 18: Rescuing the Lord from the Sea

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 18.21

kshane kshane uthe premāra tarańga ananta

jīva chāra kāhāń tāra pāibeka anta?

SYNONYMS

kshane kshane — moment after moment; uthe — rise; premāra — of love of Krishna; tarańga — waves; ananta — unlimited; jīva — a living entity; chāra — insignificant; kāhāń — where; tāra — of that; pāibeka — will get; anta — the limit.

TRANSLATION

Endless waves arise moment after moment in that ocean of love. How could an insignificant living entity estimate their limits?
