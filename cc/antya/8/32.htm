Antya-līlāChapter 8: Rāmacandra Purī Criticizes the Lord

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 8.32

mahad-anugraha-nigrahera 'sākshī' dui-jane

ei dui-dvāre śikhāilā jaga-jane

SYNONYMS

mahat — of an exalted personality; anugraha — of the blessing; nigrahera — of chastisement; sākshī — giving evidence; dui-jane — two persons; ei dui-dvāre — by these two; śikhāilā — instructed; jaga-jane — the people of the world.

TRANSLATION

Īśvara Purī received the blessing of Mādhavendra Purī, whereas Rāmacandra Purī received a rebuke from him. Therefore these two persons, Īśvara Purī and Rāmacandra Purī, are examples of the objects of a great personality's benediction and punishment. Mādhavendra Purī instructed the entire world by presenting these two examples.
