Antya-līlāChapter 8: Rāmacandra Purī Criticizes the Lord

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 8.49

"rātrāv atra aikshavam āsīt, tena

pipīlikāh sañcaranti aho! viraktānām

sannyāsinām iyam indriya-lālaseti

bruvann utthāya gatah"

SYNONYMS

rātrau — at night; atra — here; aikshavam — sugar candy; āsīt — was; tena — by that; pipīlikāh — ants; sañcaranti — wander about; aho — alas; viraktānām — renounced; sannyāsinām — of sannyāsīs; iyam — this; indriya — to the senses; lālasa — attachment; iti — thus; bruvan — speaking; utthāya — getting up; gatah — left.

TRANSLATION

"Last night there was sugar candy here," he said. "Therefore ants are wandering about. Alas, this renounced sannyāsī is attached to such sense gratification!" After speaking in this way, he got up and left.
