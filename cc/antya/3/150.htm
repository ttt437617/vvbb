Antya-līlāChapter 3: The Glories of Śrīla Haridāsa Thākura

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 3.150

sarvajña nityānanda āilā tāra ghare

āsiyā vasilā durgā-mandapa-upare

SYNONYMS

sarva-jña — omniscient; nityānanda — Lord Nityānanda; āilā — came; tāra ghare — at his house; āsiyā — coming; vasilā — sat down; durgā-mandapa-upare — on the altar of the Durgā-mandapa.

TRANSLATION

Lord Nityānanda, who is omniscient because He is the Supreme Personality of Godhead, came to the house of Rāmacandra Khān and sat down on the altar of the Durgā-mandapa.

PURPORT

Well-to-do Hindu gentlemen constructed their houses with a place called the Durgā-mandapa for the worship of the goddess Durgā. There they generally held worship of the goddess every year in the month of Āśvina (September-October). Rāmacandra Khān possessed such a Durgā-mandapa at his residence.
