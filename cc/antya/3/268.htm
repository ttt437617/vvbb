Antya-līlāChapter 3: The Glories of Śrīla Haridāsa Thākura

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 3.268

krishna-ādi, āra yata sthāvara-jańgame

krishna-preme matta kare krishna-sańkīrtane

SYNONYMS

krishna-ādi — beginning from Krishna; āra — and; yata — ail; sthāvara-jańgame — moving and nonmoving creatures; krishna-preme — in love of Krishna; matta — maddened; kare — make; krishna-sańkīrtane — chanting the holy name of Krishna.

TRANSLATION

The holy name of Krishna is so attractive that anyone who chants it — including all living entities, moving and nonmoving, and even Lord Krishna Himself — becomes imbued with love of Krishna. This is the effect of chanting the Hare Krishna mahā-mantra.
