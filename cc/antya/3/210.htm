Antya-līlāChapter 3: The Glories of Śrīla Haridāsa Thākura

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 3.210

campaka-kali-sama hasta-padāńguli

końkada ha-ila saba, kushthe gela gali'

SYNONYMS

campaka — of a golden-hued flower; kali — buds; sama — like; hasta-pada-ańguli — fingers and toes; końkada ha-ila — became crumpled; saba — all; kushthe — because of leprosy; gela gali' — melted away.

TRANSLATION

The brāhmana's toes and fingers were beautiful like golden-colored campaka buds, but because of leprosy they all withered and gradually melted away.
