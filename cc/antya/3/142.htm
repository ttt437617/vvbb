Antya-līlāChapter 3: The Glories of Śrīla Haridāsa Thākura

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 3.142

prasiddhā vaishnavī haila parama-mahāntī

bada bada vaishnava tāńra darśanete yānti

SYNONYMS

prasiddhā — celebrated; vaishnavī — devotee of the Lord; haila — became; parama-mahāntī — very advanced; bada bada vaishnava — many recognized, highly situated devotees; tāńra — her; darśanete — to see; yānti — used to go.

TRANSLATION

Thus the prostitute became a celebrated devotee. She became very advanced in spiritual life, and many stalwart Vaishnavas would come to see her.

PURPORT

Stalwart, highly advanced Vaishnava devotees are not interested in seeing prostitutes, but when a prostitute or any other fallen soul becomes a Vaishnava, stalwart Vaishnavas are interested in seeing them. Anyone can be turned into a Vaishnava if he or she follows the Vaishnava principles. A devotee who follows these principles is no longer on the material platform. Therefore, it is one's strict adherence to the principles that should be considered, not the country of one's birth. Many devotees join our Krishna consciousness movement from Europe and America, but one should not therefore consider them European Vaishnavas or American Vaishnavas. A Vaishnava is a Vaishnava and should therefore be given all the respect due a Vaishnava.
