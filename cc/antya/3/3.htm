Antya-līlāChapter 3: The Glories of Śrīla Haridāsa Thākura

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 3.3

purushottame eka udiyā-brāhmana-kumāra

pitri-śūnya, mahā-sundara, mridu-vyavahāra

SYNONYMS

purushottame — in Jagannātha Purī; eka — one; udiyā-brāhmana-kumāra — young son of a brāhmana from Orissa; pitri-śūnya — without his father; mahā-sundara — possessing very beautiful bodily features; mridu-vyavahāra — having very gentle behavior.

TRANSLATION

In Jagannātha Purī there was a young boy who had been born of an Orissan brāhmana but had later lost his father. The boy's features were very beautiful, and his behavior was extremely gentle.
