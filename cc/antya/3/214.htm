Antya-līlāChapter 3: The Glories of Śrīla Haridāsa Thākura

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 3.214

viprera kushtha śuni' haridāsa mane duhkhī hailā

balāi-purohite kahi' śāntipura āilā

SYNONYMS

viprera — of the brāhmana; kushtha — leprosy; śuni' — hearing; haridāsa — Haridāsa Thākura; mane — within the mind; duhkhī hailā — became unhappy; balāi-purohite — unto Balarāma Ācārya; kahi' — speaking; śāntipura āilā — came to Śāntipura.

TRANSLATION

Haridāsa Thākura was unhappy when he heard that the brāhmana Gopāla Cakravartī had been attacked by leprosy. Thus after informing Balarāma Ācārya, the priest of Hiranya Majumadāra, he went to Śāntipura, the home of Advaita Ācārya.
