Antya-līlāChapter 3: The Glories of Śrīla Haridāsa Thākura

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 3.189

sālokya-sārshti-sārūpya-

sāmīpyaikatvam apy uta

dīyamānam na grihnanti

vinā mat-sevanam janāh

SYNONYMS

sālokya — to live on the same planet; sārshti — to acquire the same opulence; sārūpya — to achieve the same bodily features; sāmīpya — to live always near the Supreme Lord; ekatvam — to merge into the existence of the Lord; api — even; uta — certainly; dīyamānam — being offered; na grihnanti — do not take; vinā — without; mat-sevanam — My service; janāh — the devotees.

TRANSLATION

"'My devotees do not accept sālokya, sārshti, sārūpya, sāmīpya or oneness with Me — even if I offer these liberations — in preference to serving Me.'"

PURPORT

This verse is spoken by Lord Kapila, an avatāra of the Supreme Personality of Godhead, in Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam (3.29.13).
