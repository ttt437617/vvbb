Antya-līlāChapter 3: The Glories of Śrīla Haridāsa Thākura

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 3.177

keha bale, — 'nāma haite haya pāpa-kshaya'

keha bale, — 'nāma haite jīvera moksha haya'

SYNONYMS

keha bale — some of them said; nāma haite — by chanting the Hare Krishna mantra; haya — there is; pāpa-kshaya — disappearance of all reactions to sinful activities; keha bale — some of them said; nāma haite — by chanting the holy name; jīvera — of the living entities; moksha haya — there is liberation.

TRANSLATION

Some of them said, "By chanting the holy name of the Lord, one is freed from the reactions of all sinful life."

Others said, "Simply by chanting the holy name of the Lord, a living being is liberated from material bondage."
