Antya-līlāChapter 2: The Chastisement of Junior Haridāsa

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 2.108

snehe rāndhila prabhura priya ye vyañjana

deula prasāda, ādā-cāki, lembu-salavana

SYNONYMS

snehe — in great affection; rāndhila — cooked; prabhura — of Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu; priya — dear; ye — whatever; vyañjana — vegetables; deula prasāda — remnants from the temple of Jagannātha; ādā-cāki — ground ginger; lembu — lime; sa-lavana — with salt.

TRANSLATION

In great affection, Bhagavān Ācārya cooked varieties of vegetables and other preparations dear to Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu. He also obtained remnants of food from Lord Jagannātha and digestive aids such as ground ginger and also lime with salt.
