Antya-līlāChapter 2: The Chastisement of Junior Haridāsa

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 2.153-154

eka-dina jagadānanda, svarūpa, govinda

kāśīśvara, śańkara, dāmodara, mukunda

samudra-snāne gelā sabe, śune katho dūre

haridāsa gāyena, yena dāki' kantha-svare

SYNONYMS

eka-dina — one day; jagadānanda — Jagadānanda; svarūpa — Svarūpa; govinda — Govinda; kāśīśvara — Kāśīśvara; śańkara — Śańkara; dāmodara — Dāmodara; mukunda — Mukunda; samudra-snāne — bathing in the sea; gelā — went; sabe — all of them; śune — could hear; katho dūre — from a distant place; haridāsa gāyena — Junior Haridāsa was singing; yena — as if; dāki' — calling; kantha-svare — in his original voice.

TRANSLATION

One day Jagadānanda, Svarūpa, Govinda, Kāśīśvara, Śańkara, Dāmodara and Mukunda all went to bathe in the sea. They could hear Haridāsa singing from a distant place as if calling them in his original voice.
