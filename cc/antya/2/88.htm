Antya-līlāChapter 2: The Chastisement of Junior Haridāsa

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 2.88

tāńra pitā 'vishayī' bada śatānanda-khāńna

'vishaya-vimukha' ācārya — 'vairāgya-pradhāna'

SYNONYMS

tāńra pitā — his father; vishayī — a statesman; bada — expert; śatānanda-khāńna — named Śatānanda Khān; vishaya-vimukha — not interested in state management; ācārya — Bhagavān Ācārya; vairāgya-pradhāna — mostly in the renounced order of life.

TRANSLATION

Bhagavān Ācārya's father, whose name was Śatānanda Khān, was an expert statesman, whereas Bhagavān Ācārya was not at all interested in the management of the state. Indeed, he was almost in the renounced order of life.
