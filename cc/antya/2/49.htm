Antya-līlāChapter 2: The Chastisement of Junior Haridāsa

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 2.48-49

ācambite nrisimhānanda tāhāńi āilā

duńhe tāńre mili' tabe sthāne vasāilā

duńhe duhkhī dekhi' tabe kahe nrisimhānanda

'tomā duńhākāre kene dekhi nirānanda?'

SYNONYMS

ācambite — all of a sudden; nrisimhānanda — Nrisimhānanda; tāhāńi āilā — came there; duńhe — Śivānanda and Jagadānanda; tāńre — him; mili' — meeting; tabe — then; sthāne vasāilā — caused to sit; duńhe — both; duhkhī — unhappy; dekhi' — seeing; tabe — then; kahe nrisimhānanda — Nrisimhānanda began to speak; tomā duńhākāre — both of you; kene — why; dekhi — I see; nirānanda — unhappy.

TRANSLATION

Suddenly Nrisimhānanda arrived, and Jagadānanda and Śivānanda arranged for him to sit near them. Seeing them both so unhappy, Nrisimhānanda inquired, "Why do I see that you are both despondent?"
