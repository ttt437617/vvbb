Antya-līlāChapter 2: The Chastisement of Junior Haridāsa

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 2.59

prātah-kāla haite pāka karilā apāra

nānā vyañjana, pithā, kshīra nānā upahāra

SYNONYMS

prātah-kāla haite — beginning from the morning; pāka karilā apāra — cooked many varieties of food; nānā vyañjana — varieties of vegetables; pithā — cakes; kshīra — sweet rice; nānā — various; upahāra — offerings of food.

TRANSLATION

Beginning early in the morning, Nrisimhānanda Brahmacārī cooked many varieties of food, including vegetables, cakes, sweet rice and other preparations.
