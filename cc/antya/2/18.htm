Antya-līlāChapter 2: The Chastisement of Junior Haridāsa

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 2.18

graha-grasta-prāya nakula premāvishta hañā

hāse, kānde, nāce, gāya unmatta hañā

SYNONYMS

graha-grasta-prāya — exactly like one haunted by a ghost; nakula — Nakula Brahmacārī; prema-āvishta hañā — being overwhelmed by ecstatic love of God; hāse — laughs; kānde — cries; nāce — dances; gāya — chants; unmatta hañā — just like a madman.

TRANSLATION

Nakula Brahmacārī became exactly like a man haunted by a ghost. Thus he sometimes laughed, sometimes cried, sometimes danced and sometimes chanted like a madman.
