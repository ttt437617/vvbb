Antya-līlāChapter 15: The Transcendental Madness of Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 15.22

krishnāńga — saurabhya-bhara, mriga-mada-mada-hara,

nīlotpalera hare garva-dhana

jagat-nārīra nāsā, tāra bhitara pāte vāsā,

nārī-gane kare ākarshana

SYNONYMS

krishna-ańga — the body of Krishna; saurabhya-bhara — full of fragrance; mriga-mada — of musk; mada-hara — intoxicating power; nīlotpalera — of the bluish lotus flower; hare — takes away; garva-dhana — the pride of the treasure; jagat-nārīra — of the women in the world; nāsā — nostrils; tāra bhitara — within them; pāte vāsā — constructs a residence; nārī-gane — women; kare ākarshana — attracts.

TRANSLATION

"The fragrance of Krishna's body is more maddening than the aroma of musk, and it surpasses the fragrance of the bluish lotus flower. It enters the nostrils of all the women of the world and, making a nest there, thus attracts them.
