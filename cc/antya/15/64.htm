Antya-līlāChapter 15: The Transcendental Madness of Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 15.64

nava-ghana-snigdha-varna, dalitāñjana-cikkana,

indīvara-nindi sukomala

jini' upamāna-gana, hare sabāra netra-mana,

krishna-kānti parama prabala

SYNONYMS

nava-ghana — a newly formed cloud; snigdha — attractive; varna — bodily complexion; dalita — powdered; añjana — ointment; cikkana — polished; indīvara — a blue lotus flower; nindi — defeating; su-komala — soft; jini' — surpassing; upamāna-gana — all comparison; hare — attracts; sabāra — of all; netra-mana — the eyes and mind; krishna-kānti — the complexion of Krishna; parama prabala — supremely powerful.

TRANSLATION

Caitanya Mahāprabhu continued, "Śrī Krishna's complexion is as polished as powdered eye ointment. It surpasses the beauty of a newly formed cloud and is softer than a blue lotus flower. Indeed, His complexion is so pleasing that it attracts the eyes and minds of everyone, and it is so powerful that it defies all comparison.
