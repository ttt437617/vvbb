Antya-līlāChapter 15: The Transcendental Madness of Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 15.70

vīkshyālakāvrita-mukham tava kundala-śrī-

ganda-sthalādhara-sudham hasitāvalokam

dattābhayam ca bhuja-danda-yugam vilokya

vakshah śriyaika-ramanam ca bhavāma dāsyah

SYNONYMS

vīkshya — seeing; alaka-āvrita — decorated with curling tresses of hair; mukham — face; tava — Your; kundala-śrī — the beauty of the earrings; ganda-sthala — falling on Your cheeks; adhara-sudham — and the nectar of Your lips; hasita-avalokam — Your smiling glance; datta-abhayam — which assure fearlessness; ca — and; bhuja-danda-yugam — the two arms; vilokya — by seeing; vakshah — chest; śriyā — by the beauty; eka-ramanam — chiefly producing conjugal attraction; ca — and; bhavāma — we have become; dāsyah — Your maidservants.

TRANSLATION

"'Dear Krishna, by seeing Your beautiful face decorated with tresses of hair, by seeing the beauty of Your earrings falling on Your cheeks, and by seeing the nectar of Your lips, the beauty of Your smiling glances, Your two arms, which assure complete fearlessness, and Your broad chest, whose beauty arouses conjugal attraction, we have simply surrendered ourselves to becoming Your maidservants.'"

PURPORT

This verse quoted from Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam (10.29.39) was spoken by the gopīs when they arrived before Krishna for the rāsa dance.
