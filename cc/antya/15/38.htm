Antya-līlāChapter 15: The Transcendental Madness of Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 15.38

e kene kahibe krishnera uddeśa āmāya?

e — strī-jāti latā, āmāra sakhī-prāya

SYNONYMS

e — these; kene — why; kahibe — will say; krishnera — of Lord Krishna; uddeśa — direction; āmāya — to us; e — these; strī-jāti — belonging to the class of women; latā — creepers; āmāra — our; sakhī-prāya — like friends.

TRANSLATION

"'Why should the trees tell us where Krishna has gone? Let us rather inquire from the creepers; they are female and are therefore like friends to us.
