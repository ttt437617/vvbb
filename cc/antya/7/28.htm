Antya-līlāChapter 7: The Meeting of Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu and Vallabha Bhatta

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 7.28

'ātma-bhūta'-śabde kahe 'pārishada-gana'

aiśvarya-jñāne lakshmī nā pāilā vrajendra-nandana

SYNONYMS

ātma-bhūta-śabde — the word ātma-bhūta; kahe — means; pārishada-gana — personal associates; aiśvarya-jñāne — in the understanding of opulence; lakshmī — the goddess of fortune; nā pāilā — could not get; vrajendra-nandana — the shelter of Krishna, the son of Nanda Mahārāja.

TRANSLATION

"The word 'ātma-bhūta' means 'personal associates.' Through the understanding of the Lord's opulence, the goddess of fortune could not receive the shelter of Krishna, the son of Nanda Mahārāja.

PURPORT

Lakshmī, the goddess of fortune, has complete knowledge of Krishna's opulences, but she could not achieve the association of Krishna by dint of such knowledge. The devotees in Vrindāvana, however, actually enjoy the association of Krishna.
