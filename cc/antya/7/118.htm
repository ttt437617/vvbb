Antya-līlāChapter 7: The Meeting of Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu and Vallabha Bhatta

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 7.118

nānā avajñāne bhatte śodhena bhagavān

krishna yaiche khandilena indrera abhimāna

SYNONYMS

nānā — various; avajñāne — by disrespect; bhatte — Vallabha Bhatta; śodhena — purifies; bhagavān — the Supreme Personality of Godhead; krishna — Lord Krishna; yaiche — as; khandilena — cuts down; indrera abhimāna — the pride of Indra.

TRANSLATION

By various hints and refutations, Lord Caitanya, the Supreme Personality of Godhead, corrected Vallabha Bhatta exactly as Krishna had cut down the false pride of Indra.

PURPORT

Indra, the King of heaven, was very proud of his position. Therefore when the residents of Vrindāvana decided not to perform the Indra-yajña but instead to perform the Govardhana-yajña in accordance with the instructions of Krishna, Indra, because of his false pride, wanted to chastise the residents of Vrindāvana. Thinking himself extremely powerful, Indra poured incessant rain upon Vrindāvana, but Lord Krishna immediately cut down his pride by lifting Govardhana Hill as an umbrella to save the residents of Vrindāvana. In this way Krishna proved Indra's power most insignificant in the presence of His own omnipotence.
