Antya-līlāChapter 7: The Meeting of Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu and Vallabha Bhatta

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 7.124

āmāra 'hita' karena, — iho āmi māni 'duhkha'

krishnera upare kaila yena indra mahā-mūrkha"

SYNONYMS

āmāra — my; hita — benefit; karena — He is doing; iho — this; āmi — I; māni — consider; duhkha — unhappiness; krishnera upare — upon Krishna; kaila — did; yena — as; indra — Indra; mahā-mūrkha — the great fool.

TRANSLATION

"He is actually acting for my benefit, although I interpret His actions as insults. This is exactly like the incident in which Lord Krishna cut down Indra, the great, puffed-up fool, to correct him."
