Antya-līlāChapter 7: The Meeting of Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu and Vallabha Bhatta

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 7.25

dāsya, sakhya, vātsalya, āra ye śrińgāra

dāsa, sakhā, guru, kāntā, — 'āśraya' yāhāra

SYNONYMS

dāsya — servitude; sakhya — friendship; vātsalya — parental love; āra — and; ye — that; śrińgāra — conjugal love; dāsa — the servant; sakhā — friend; guru — superior; kāntā — lover; āśraya — the shelter; yāhāra — of which.

TRANSLATION

"The servant, friend, superior and conjugal lover are the shelters of the transcendental mellows called dāsya, sakhya, vātsalya and śrińgāra.
