Antya-līlāChapter 7: The Meeting of Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu and Vallabha Bhatta

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 7.128

āmi — ajña, 'hita'-sthāne māni 'apamāne'

indra yena krishnera nindā karila ajñāne

SYNONYMS

āmi — I; ajña — ignorant fool; hita-sthāne — what is for my benefit; māni — I consider; apamāne — as an insult; indra — King Indra; yena — as; krishnera — of Lord Krishna; nindā — offense; karila — did; ajñāne — out of ignorance.

TRANSLATION

"I am an ignorant fool, for I interpret as an insult what is meant for my benefit. In this way I am just like King Indra, who out of ignorance tried to surpass Krishna, the Supreme Lord.
