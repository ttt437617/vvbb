Antya-līlāChapter 20: The Śikshāshtaka Prayers

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 20.132

krishnera śabda-gune prabhura mana ākarshilā

"kā stry ańga te" ślokera artha āveśe karilā

SYNONYMS

krishnera — of Lord Krishna; śabda-gune — by the qualities of the sound; prabhura — of Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu; mana — the mind; ākarshilā — was attracted; kā stry ańga te ślokera — of the verse beginning with the words kā stry ańga te; artha — the meaning; āveśe — in ecstasy; karilā — described.

TRANSLATION

That chapter also tells how the attributes of Krishna's sound attracted the mind of Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu, who then described in ecstasy the meaning of the "kā stry ańga te" verse.
