Antya-līlāChapter 20: The Śikshāshtaka Prayers

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 20.5

nānā-bhāva uthe prabhura harsha, śoka, rosha

dainyodvega-ārti utkanthā, santosha

SYNONYMS

nānā-bhāva — all kinds of emotion; uthe — awaken; prabhura — of Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu; harsha — jubilation; śoka — lamentation; rosha — anger; dainya — humility; udvega — anxiety; ārti — grief; utkanthā — more eagerness; santosha — satisfaction.

TRANSLATION

He relished the symptoms of various transcendental emotions, such as jubilation, lamentation, anger, humility, anxiety, grief, eagerness and satisfaction.
