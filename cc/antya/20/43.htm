Antya-līlāChapter 20: The Śikshāshtaka Prayers

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 20.43

eteka cintite rādhāra nirmala hridaya

svābhāvika premāra svabhāva karila udaya

SYNONYMS

eteka — thus; cintite — thinking; rādhāra — of Śrīmatī Rādhārānī; nirmala hridaya — possessing a pure heart; svābhāvika — natural; premāra — of love of Krishna; svabhāva — the character; karila udaya — awakes.

TRANSLATION

While Śrīmatī Rādhārānī was thinking in this way, the characteristics of natural love became manifest because of Her pure heart.
