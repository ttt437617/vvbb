Antya-līlāChapter 10: Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu Accepts Prasādam from His Devotees

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 10.32

phutkalāi cūrna kari' ghrite bhājāila

cini-pāke karpūrādi diyā nādu kaila

SYNONYMS

phutkalāi — fused peas fried in ghee and soaked in sugar juice; cūrna kari' — making into powder; ghrite bhājāila — fried with ghee; cini-pāke — cooking with sugar; karpūra-ādi — camphor and other ingredients; diyā — adding; nādu kaila — made round sweetmeat balls.

TRANSLATION

Another variety of sweet was made with fused peas that were powdered, fried in ghee and then cooked in sugar juice. Camphor was added, and then the mixture was rolled into balls.
