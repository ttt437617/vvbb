Antya-līlāChapter 10: Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu Accepts Prasādam from His Devotees

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 10.23

śunthi-khanda nādu, āra āma-pitta-hara

prithak prithak bāndhi' vastrera kuthalī bhitara

SYNONYMS

śunthi-khanda nādu — sweetmeat balls made with dried ginger; āra — and; āma-pitta-hara — which removes mucus caused by too much bile; prithak prithak — separately; bāndhi' — packaging; vastrera — of cloth; kuthalī — small bags; bhitara — within.

TRANSLATION

She made sweetmeat balls with dried ginger to remove mucus caused by too much bile. She put all these preparations separately into small cloth bags.
