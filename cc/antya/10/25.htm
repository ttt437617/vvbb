Antya-līlāChapter 10: Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu Accepts Prasādam from His Devotees

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 10.25

nārikela-khanda nādu, āra nādu gańgā-jala

cira-sthāyī khanda-vikāra karilā sakala

SYNONYMS

nārikela-khanda nādu — sweetmeat balls made with coconut powder; āra — and; nādu gańgā-jala — a sweetmeat ball as white as Ganges water; cira-sthāyī — long-lasting; khanda-vikāra — modified forms of sugar-candy sweets; karilā — made; sakala — all.

TRANSLATION

She made many sweetmeats in the shape of balls. Some were made with powdered coconut, and others looked as white as the water of the Ganges. In this way she made many varieties of long-lasting sugar confections.
