Antya-līlāChapter 10: Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu Accepts Prasādam from His Devotees

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 10.83-84

sarva-kāla āche ei sudridha 'niyama'

'prabhu yadi prasāda pāñā karena śayana

govinda āsiyā kare pāda-samvāhana

tabe yāi' prabhura 'śesha' karena bhojana'

SYNONYMS

sarva-kāla — all the time; āche — there is; ei — this; su-dridha — hard-and-fast; niyama — regulation; prabhu — Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu; yadi — when; prasāda pāñā — after taking His meals; karena śayana — lies down; govinda — Govinda; āsiyā — coming; kare — performs; pāda-samvāhana — massaging the legs; tabe — thereafter; yāi' — going; prabhura — of Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu; śesha — the remnants of food; karena bhojana — eats.

TRANSLATION

It was a steady, long-standing rule that Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu would lie down to rest after lunch and Govinda would come to massage His legs. Then Govinda would honor the remnants of food left by Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu.
