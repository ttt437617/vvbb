Antya-līlāChapter 19: The Inconceivable Behavior of Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 19.101

mātri-bhakti, pralāpana, bhittye mukha-gharshana,

krishna-gandha-sphūrtye divya-nritya

ei cāri-līlā-bhede, gāila ei paricchede,

krishnadāsa rūpa-gosāñi-bhritya

SYNONYMS

mātri-bhakti — devotion to His mother; pralāpana — words of madness; bhittye — on the walls; mukha-gharshana — rubbing His face; krishna-gandha — of Lord Krishna's fragrance; sphūrtye — on the appearance; divya-nritya — transcendental dancing; ei — these; cāri — four; līlā — pastimes; bhede — different; gāila — has sung; ei paricchede — in this chapter; krishnadāsa — Krishnadāsa Kavirāja; rūpa-gosāñi-bhritya — servant of Śrīla Rūpa Gosvāmī.

TRANSLATION

Thus, I, Krishnadāsa, the servant of Śrīla Rūpa Gosvāmī, have sung of four divisions of the Lord's pastimes in this chapter: the Lord's devotion to His mother, His words of madness, His rubbing His face against the walls at night, and His dancing at the appearance of Lord Krishna's fragrance.

PURPORT

Krishnadāsa Kavirāja Gosvāmī says that he has been able to describe these four pastimes of Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu by the blessings of Śrīla Rūpa Gosvāmī. Krishnadāsa Kavirāja Gosvāmī was not actually a direct disciple of Śrīla Rūpa Gosvāmī, but he followed the instructions given by Śrīla Rūpa Gosvāmī in the Bhakti-rasāmrita-sindhu. He therefore acted according to the directions of Rūpa Gosvāmī and prayed in every chapter for his mercy.
