Antya-līlāChapter 19: The Inconceivable Behavior of Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 19.94

netra-nābhi, vadana, kara-yuga carana,

ei ashta-padma krishna-ańge

karpūra-lipta kamala, tāra yaiche parimala,

sei gandha ashta-padma-sańge

SYNONYMS

netra — the eyes; nābhi — the navel; vadana — the face; kara-yuga — the palms; carana — the feet; ei — these; ashta — eight; padma — lotus flowers; krishna-ańge — in the body of Krishna; karpūra — with camphor; lipta — smeared; kamala — the lotus flower; tāra — of that; yaiche — like; parimala — the fragrance; sei gandha — that fragrance; ashta-padma-sańge — is associated with the eight lotus flowers.

TRANSLATION

"Krishna's eyes, navel and face, hands and feet are like eight lotus flowers on His body. From those eight lotuses emanates a fragrance like a mixture of camphor and lotus. That is the scent associated with His body.
