Antya-līlāChapter 19: The Inconceivable Behavior of Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 19.1

vande tam krishna-caitanyam

mātri-bhakta-śiromanim

pralapya mukha-sańgharshī

madhūdyāne lalāsa yah

SYNONYMS

vande — I offer my respectful obeisances; tam — unto Him; krishna-caitanyam — Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu; mātri-bhakta — of great devotees of mothers; śiro-manim — the crown jewel; pralapya — talking like a madman; mukha-sańgharshī — who used to rub His face; madhu-udyāne — in the garden known as Jagannātha-vallabha; lalāsa — enjoyed; yah — who.

TRANSLATION

Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu, the most exalted of all devotees of mothers, spoke like a madman and rubbed His face against the walls. Overwhelmed by emotions of ecstatic love, He would sometimes enter the Jagannātha-vallabha garden to perform His pastimes. I offer my respectful obeisances unto Him.
