Antya-līlāChapter 19: The Inconceivable Behavior of Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 19.49

'akrūra kare tomāra dosha, āmāya kene kara rosha',

ihā yadi kaha 'durācāra'

tui akrūra-mūrti dhari', krishna nili curi kari',

anyera nahe aiche vyavahāra

SYNONYMS

akrūra — Akrūra; kare — does; tomāra dosha — fault to You; āmāya — unto me; kene — why; kara — You do; rosha — anger; ihā — this; yadi — if; kaha — you say; durācāra — O misbehaved (Providence); tui — you; akrūra-mūrti dhari' — taking the form of Akrūra; krishna — Lord Krishna; nili — you have taken; curi kari' — stealing; anyera — of others; nahe — there is not; aiche — this type of; vyavahāra — behavior.

TRANSLATION

"O misbehaved Providence! If you reply to Us, 'Akrūra is actually at fault; why are You angry with me?' then I say to you, 'Providence, you have taken the form of Akrūra and have stolen Krishna away. No one else would behave like this.'
