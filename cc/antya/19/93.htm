Antya-līlāChapter 19: The Inconceivable Behavior of Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 19.93

sakhi he, krishna-gandha jagat mātāya

nārīra nāsāte paśe, sarva-kāla tāhāń vaise,

krishna-pāśa dhari' lañā yāya

SYNONYMS

sakhi he — O My dear friend; krishna-gandha — the fragrance of the body of Krishna; jagat mātāya — enchants the whole world; nārīra — of women; nāsāte — in the nostrils; paśe — enters; sarva-kāla — always; tāhāń — there; vaise — remains; krishna-pāśa — to the side of Lord Krishna; dhari' — catching; lañā yāya — takes one.

TRANSLATION

"My dear friend, the scent of Krishna's body enchants the entire world. It especially enters the nostrils of women and remains seated there. Thus it captures them and forcibly brings them to Krishna.
