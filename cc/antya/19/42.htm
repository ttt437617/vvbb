Antya-līlāChapter 19: The Inconceivable Behavior of Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 19.42

kāhāń se muralī-dhvani, navābhra-garjita jini',

jagat ākarshe śravane yāhāra

uthi' dhāya vraja-jana, trishita cātaka-gana,

āsi' piye kānty-amrita-dhāra

SYNONYMS

kāhāń — where; se — that; muralī-dhvani — the vibration of the flute; nava-abhra-garjita jini' — conquering the vibrations of new clouds; jagat — the whole world; ākarshe — attracts; śravane — the hearing; yāhāra — whose; uthi' — standing up; dhāya — run; vraja-jana — the inhabitants of Vrajabhūmi; trishita cātaka-gana — like lusty cātaka birds; āsi' — coming; piye — drink; kānti-amrita-dhāra — the showers of the nectar of Krishna's bodily luster.

TRANSLATION

"The deep vibration of Krishna's flute surpasses the thundering of new clouds and attracts the aural reception of the entire world. Thus the inhabitants of Vrindāvana rise and pursue that sound, drinking the showering nectar of Krishna's bodily luster like thirsty cātaka birds.
