Antya-līlāChapter 19: The Inconceivable Behavior of Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 19.91

kurańga-mada-jid-vapuh-parimalormi-krishtāńganah

svakāńga-nalināshtake śaśi-yutābja-gandha-prathah

madenduvara-candanāguru-sugandhi-carcārcitah

sa me madana-mohanah sakhi tanoti nāsā-sprihām

SYNONYMS

kurańga-mada-jit — conquering the aromatic smell of musk; vapuh — of His transcendental body; parimala-ūrmi — with waves of scent; krishta-ańganah — attracting the gopīs of Vrindāvana; svaka-ańga-nalina-ashtake — on all eight lotuslike parts of the body (face, navel, eyes, palms and feet); śaśi-yuta-abja-gandha-prathah — who distributes the scent of the lotus mixed with camphor; mada-induvara-candana-aguru-sugandhi-carcā-arcitah — smeared with the unguents of musk, camphor, white sandalwood and aguru; sah — He; me — My; madana-mohanah — Lord Krishna, who enchants even Cupid; sakhi — O My dear friends; tanoti — increases; nāsā-sprihām — the desire of My nostrils.

TRANSLATION

"'The scent of Krishna's transcendental body surpasses the aroma of musk and attracts the minds of all women. The eight lotuslike parts of His body distribute the fragrance of lotuses mixed with that of camphor. His body is anointed with aromatic substances like musk, camphor, sandalwood and aguru. O My dear friend, that Personality of Godhead, also known as the enchanter of Cupid, always increases the desire of My nostrils.'

PURPORT

This verse is found in the Govinda-līlāmrita (8.6).
