Antya-līlāChapter 19: The Inconceivable Behavior of Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 19.47

are vidhi, tui bada-i nithura

anyo'nya durl abha jana, preme karāñā sammilana,

'akritārthān' kene karis dūra?

SYNONYMS

are — oh; vidhi — Providence; tui — you; bada-i — very; nithura — cruel; anyah anya — for one another; durlabha jana — persons rarely obtained; preme — in love; karāñā sammilana — causing to meet together; akrita-arthān — unsuccessful; kene — why; karis — you do; dūra — far apart.

TRANSLATION

"Oh, cruel Providence! You are very unkind, for you bring together in love people who are rarely in touch with each other. Then, after you have made Them meet but before They are fulfilled, you again spread Them far apart.
