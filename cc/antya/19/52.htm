Antya-līlāChapter 19: The Inconceivable Behavior of Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 19.52

krishne kene kari rosha, āpana durdaiva-dosha,

pākila mora ei pāpa-phala

ye krishna — mora premādhīna, tāre kaila udāsīna,

ei mora abhāgya prabala"

SYNONYMS

krishne — unto Krishna; kene — why; kari rosha — I am angry; āpana — of My own; durdaiva — of misfortune; dosha — fault; pākila — has ripened; mora — My; ei — this; pāpa-phala — sinful reaction; ye — that; krishna — Krishna; mora — My; prema-adhīna — dependent on love; tāre — Him; kaila — has made; udāsīna — indifferent; ei mora — this is My; abhāgya — misfortune; prabala — very strong.

TRANSLATION

"Yet why should I be angry with Krishna? It is the fault of My own misfortune. The fruit of My sinful activities has ripened, and therefore Krishna, who has always been dependent on My love, is now indifferent. This means that My misfortune is very strong."
