Antya-līlāChapter 19: The Inconceivable Behavior of Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 19.99

ei-mata gaurahari, gandhe kaila mana curi,

bhrińga-prāya iti-uti dhāya

yāya vriksha-latā-pāśe, krishna sphure — sei āśe,

krishna nā pāya, gandha-mātra pāya

SYNONYMS

ei-mata — in this way; gaurahari — Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu; gandhe — by the scent; kaila — did; mana curi — stealing of the mind; bhrińga-prāya — just like a bumblebee; iti-uti dhāya — wanders here and there; yāya — goes; vriksha-latā-pāśe — by the side of the trees and creepers; krishna sphure — Lord Krishna will appear; sei āśe — by that hope; krishna nā pāya — does not obtain Krishna; gandha-mātra pāya — gets only the scent.

TRANSLATION

Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu, His mind thus stolen by the scent of Krishna's body, ran here and there like a bumblebee. He ran to the trees and plants, hoping that Lord Krishna would appear, but instead He found only that scent.
