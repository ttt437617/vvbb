Antya-līlāChapter 5: How Pradyumna Miśra Received Instructions from Rāmānanda Rāya

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 5.41

nirvikāra deha-mana — kāshtha-pāshāna-sama!

āścarya, — tarunī-sparśe nirvikāra mana

SYNONYMS

nirvikāra — unchanged; deha-mana — body and mind; kāshtha-pāshāna-sama — like wood or stone; āścarya — wonderful; tarunī-sparśe — in touching young girls; nirvikāra — unchanged; mana — mind.

TRANSLATION

"His mind is as steady as wood or stone. Indeed, it is wonderful that even when he touches such young girls, his mind never changes.
