Antya-līlāChapter 5: How Pradyumna Miśra Received Instructions from Rāmānanda Rāya

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 5.65

vaktā śrotā kahe śune duńhe premāveśe

ātma-smriti nāhi, kāhāń jāniba dina-śeshe

SYNONYMS

vaktā — the speaker; śrotā — the listener; kahe — speaks; śune — hears; duńhe — both of them; prema-āveśe — in ecstatic love; ātma-smriti nāhi — there was no bodily consciousness; kāhāń — where; jāniba — can understand; dina-śeshe — the end of day.

TRANSLATION

The speaker and listener spoke and heard in ecstatic love. Thus they forgot their bodily consciousness. How, then, could they perceive the end of the day?
