Antya-līlāChapter 5: How Pradyumna Miśra Received Instructions from Rāmānanda Rāya

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 5.48

vikrīditam vraja-vadhūbhir idam ca vishnoh

śraddhānvito 'nuśrinuyād atha varnayed yah

bhaktim parām bhagavati pratilabhya kāmam

hrid-rogam āśv apahinoty acirena dhīrah

SYNONYMS

vikrīditam — the activity of the rāsa dance; vraja-vadhūbhih — the damsels of Vraja, the gopīs; idam — this; ca — and; vishnoh — of Lord Krishna; śraddhā-anvitah — with transcendental faith; anuśrinuyāt — continually hears in the paramparā system; atha — also; varnayet — describes; yah — one who; bhaktim — devotional service; parām — transcendental; bhagavati — unto the Supreme Personality of Godhead; pratilabhya — attaining; kāmam — lusty material desires; hrit-rogam — the disease of the heart; āśu — very soon; apahinoti — gives up; acirena — without delay; dhīrah — one who is sober because of advanced devotional service.

TRANSLATION

"'A transcendentally sober person who, with faith and love, continually hears from a realized soul about the activities of Lord Krishna in His rāsa dance with the gopīs, or one who describes such activities, can attain full transcendental devotional service at the lotus feet of the Supreme Personality of Godhead. Thus lusty material desires, which are the heart disease of all materialistic persons, are for him quickly and completely vanquished.'

PURPORT

All the activities of Lord Krishna are transcendental, and the gopīs are also transcendentally situated. Therefore the activities of the gopīs and Lord Krishna, if seriously understood, will certainly free one from material attachment. Then there is no possibility that lusty material desires will awaken.
