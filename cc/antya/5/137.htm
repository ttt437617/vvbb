Antya-līlāChapter 5: How Pradyumna Miśra Received Instructions from Rāmānanda Rāya

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 5.137

vācālam bāliśam stabdham

ajñam pandita-māninam

krishnam martyam upāśritya

gopā me cakrur apriyam

SYNONYMS

vācālam — talkative; bāliśam — childish; stabdham — impudent; ajñam — foolish; pandita-māninam — thinking Himself a very learned scholar; krishnam — Krishna; martyam — an ordinary mortal human being; upāśritya — taking shelter of; gopāh — the cowherd men; me — unto me; cakruh — committed; apriyam — that which is not very much appreciated.

TRANSLATION

[Lord Indra said:] "This Krishna, who is an ordinary human being, is talkative, childish, impudent and ignorant, although He thinks Himself very learned. The cowherd men in Vrindāvana have offended me by accepting Him. This has not been greatly appreciated by me."

PURPORT

This verse is from Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam (10.25.5).
