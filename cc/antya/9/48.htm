Antya-līlāChapter 9: The Deliverance of Gopīnātha Pattanāyaka

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 9.48

yathārtha mūlye ghodā laha, yebā bākī haya

krame krame dibe, vyartha prāna kene laya"

SYNONYMS

yathā-artha mūlye — for the proper price; ghodā laha — accept the horses; yebā — whatever; bākī haya — balance is due; krame krame — gradually; dibe — he will give; vyartha — unnecessarily; prāna — his life; kene — why; laya — are you taking.

TRANSLATION

"It would be better to take the horses for a proper price and let him gradually repay the balance. Why are you killing him unnecessarily?"
