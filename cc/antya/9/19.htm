Antya-līlāChapter 9: The Deliverance of Gopīnātha Pattanāyaka

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 9.19

dui-laksha kāhana tāra thāñi bākī ha-ila

dui-laksha kāhana kaudi rājā ta' māgila

SYNONYMS

dui-laksha — 200,000 kāhanas of kaudis (one kāhana equals 1,280 kaudis); tāra thāñi — from him; bākī ha-ila — there was a balance due; dui-laksha kāhana — 200,000 kāhanas; kaudi — conchshells; rājā — the King; ta' — certainly; māgila — demanded.

TRANSLATION

"Once when he deposited the collection, however, a balance of 200,000 kāhanas of conchshells was due from him. Therefore the King demanded this sum.
