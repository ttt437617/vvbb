Antya-līlāChapter 9: The Deliverance of Gopīnātha Pattanāyaka

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 9.78

ethā tumi vasi' raha, kene yābe ālālanātha?

keha tomā nā śunābe vishayīra bāt

SYNONYMS

ethā — here; tumi — You; vasi' — residing; raha — kindly stay; kene — why; yābe — shall You go; ālālanātha — to Ālālanātha; keha tomā nā śunābe — no one will inform You; vishayīra bāt — about the affairs of materialistic persons.

TRANSLATION

"Kindly stay here at Jagannātha Purī. Why should You go to Ālālanātha? Henceforward, no one will approach You about material affairs."
