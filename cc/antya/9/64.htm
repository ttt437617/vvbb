Antya-līlāChapter 9: The Deliverance of Gopīnātha Pattanāyaka

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 9.64

bhikshuka sannyāsī āmi nirjana-vāsī

āmāya duhkha deya, nija-duhkha kahi' āsi'

SYNONYMS

bhikshuka — beggar; sannyāsī — mendicant; āmi — I; nirjana-vāsī — living in a solitary place; āmāya — to Me; duhkha — unhappiness; deya — they give; nija-duhkha — their own unhappiness; kahi' — speaking; āsi' — coming.

TRANSLATION

"As a beggar sannyāsī, a mendicant, I wish to live alone in a solitary place, but these people come to tell Me about their unhappiness and disturb Me.
