Antya-līlāChapter 9: The Deliverance of Gopīnātha Pattanāyaka

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 9.119

'prabhu yena nāhi jānena, — rājā āmāra lāgiyā

dui-laksha kāhana kaudi dileka chādiyā

SYNONYMS

prabhu — Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu; yena — so that; nāhi jānena — may not think; rājā — the King; āmāra lāgiyā — for Me; dui-laksha kāhana kaudi — 200,000 kāhanas of kaudis; dileka chādiyā — has remitted.

TRANSLATION

"The King said, 'Speak to the Lord in such a way that He will not think, "For My sake the King has forfeited 200,000 kāhanas of kaudis."

PURPORT

The kaudi is like an American cent or Japanese yen. In the old medium of exchange, the first unit of currency was a small conchshell called a kaudi. Four kaudis made one gandā, twenty gandās made one pana, and sixteen panas made one kāhana. Gopīnātha Pattanāyaka owed the government 200,000 kāhanas. The King absolved him of this debt, reappointed him to his post and doubled his salary.
