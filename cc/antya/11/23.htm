Antya-līlāChapter 11: The Passing of Haridāsa Thākura

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 11.23

prabhu kahe, — 'kon vyādhi, kaha ta' nirnaya?'

teńho kahe, — 'sańkhyā-kīrtana nā pūraya'

SYNONYMS

prabhu kahe — Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu said; kon vyādhi — what disease; kaha ta' nirnaya — can you ascertain; teńho kahe — he said; sańkhyā-kīrtana — fixed amount of chanting; nā pūraya — has not become complete.

TRANSLATION

Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu further inquired from Haridāsa, "Can you ascertain what your disease is?"

Haridāsa Thākura replied, "My disease is that I cannot complete my rounds."

PURPORT

If one cannot complete the fixed number of rounds he is assigned, he should be considered to be in a diseased condition of spiritual life. Śrīla Haridāsa Thākura is called nāmācārya. Of course, we cannot imitate Haridāsa Thākura, but everyone must chant a prescribed number of rounds. In our Krishna consciousness movement we have fixed sixteen rounds as the minimum so that the Westerners will not feel burdened. These sixteen rounds must be chanted, and chanted loudly, so that one can hear himself and others.
