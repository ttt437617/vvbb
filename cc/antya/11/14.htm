Antya-līlāChapter 11: The Passing of Haridāsa Thākura

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 11.14

dine dine bāde vikāra, rātrye atiśaya

cintā, udvega, pralāpādi yata śāstre kaya

SYNONYMS

dine dine — day after day; bāde — increase; vikāra — transformations; rātrye atiśaya — especially at night; cintā — anxiety; udvega — agitation; pralāpa — talking like a madman; ādi — and so on; yata — as many as; śāstre kaya — are mentioned in the śāstras.

TRANSLATION

Day after day the symptoms increased, and at night they increased even more. All these symptoms, such as transcendental anxiety, agitation and talking like a madman, were present, just as they are described in the śāstras.
