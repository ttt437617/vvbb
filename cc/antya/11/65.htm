Antya-līlāChapter 11: The Passing of Haridāsa Thākura

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 11.65

haridāsera pādodaka piye bhakta-gana

haridāsera ańge dilā prasāda-candana

SYNONYMS

haridāsera — of Haridāsa Thākura; pāda-udaka — the water that touched the lotus feet; piye — drink; bhakta-gana — the devotees; haridāsera — of Haridāsa Thākura; ańge — on the body; dilā — smeared; prasāda-candana — remnants of sandalwood pulp offered to Lord Jagannātha.

TRANSLATION

Everyone drank the water that had touched the lotus feet of Haridāsa Thākura, and then they smeared remnants of Lord Jagannātha's sandalwood pulp over Haridāsa Thākura's body.
