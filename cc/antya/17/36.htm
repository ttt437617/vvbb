Antya-līlāChapter 17: The Bodily Transformations of Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 17.36

dharma chādāya venu-dvāre, hāne katāksha-kāma-śare,

lajjā, bhaya, sakala chādāya

ebe āmāya kari' rosha, kahi' pati-tyāge 'dosha',

dhārmika hañā dharma śikhāya!

SYNONYMS

dharma — religious principles; chādāya — induces to reject; venu-dvāre — through the flute; hāne — pierces; katāksha — glancing; kāma-śare — by the arrows of lust; lajjā — shame; bhaya — fear; sakala — all; chādāya — induces to give up; ebe — now; āmāya — at us; kari' rosha — becoming angry; kahi' — saying; pati-tyāge — to give up one's husband; dosha — fault; dhārmika — very religious; hañā — becoming; dharma — religious principles; śikhāya — You teach.

TRANSLATION

"'The vibration of Your flute, accompanied by Your glance, which pierces us forcibly with the arrows of lust, induces us to ignore the regulative principles of religious life. Thus we become excited by lusty desires and come to You, giving up all shame and fear. But now You are angry with us. You are finding fault with our violating religious principles and leaving our homes and husbands. And as You instruct us about religious principles, we become helpless.
