Antya-līlāChapter 17: The Bodily Transformations of Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 17.44

se śrī-mukha-bhāshita, amrita haite parāmrita,

smita-karpūra tāhāte miśrita

śabda, artha, — dui-śakti, nānā-rasa kare vyakti,

pratyakshara — narma-vibhūshita

SYNONYMS

se — that; śrī — beautiful; mukha — by the mouth; bhāshita — spoken; amrita — nectar; haite — than; para-amrita — more nectarean; smita — smiling; karpūra — camphor; tāhāte — in that; miśrita — mixed; śabda — sound; artha — meaning; dui-śakti — two energies; nānā — various; rasa — mellows; kare vyakti — express; prati-akshara — every word; narma-vibhūshita — full of meaning.

TRANSLATION

"Krishna's speech is far sweeter than nectar. Each of His jubilant words is full of meaning, and when His speech mixes with His smile, which is like camphor, the resultant sound and the deep meaning of Krishna's words create various transcendental mellows.
