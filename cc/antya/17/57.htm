Antya-līlāChapter 17: The Bodily Transformations of Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 17.57

rādhā-bhāvera svabhāva āna, krishne karāya 'kāma'-jñāna,

kāma-jñāne trāsa haila citte

kahe — "ye jagat māre, se paśila antare,

ei vairī nā deya pāsarite"

SYNONYMS

rādhā-bhāvera — of the ecstasy of Śrīmatī Rādhārānī; svabhāva — characteristic; āna — another; krishne — to Krishna; karāya — causes Her to do; kāma-jñāna — understanding as Cupid; kāma — as Cupid; jñāne — in the understanding; trāsa — fear; haila — was; citte — in the mind; kahe — She says; ye — the person who; jagat — the whole world; māre — conquers; se — that person; paśila — entered; antare — within My heart; ei vairī — this enemy; nā deya — does not allow; pāsarite — to forget.

TRANSLATION

Śrīmatī Rādhārānī's ecstasy also made Her think of Krishna as Cupid, and this understanding frightened Her. She said, "This Cupid, who has conquered the whole world and entered My heart, is My greatest enemy, for He does not allow Me to forget Him."
