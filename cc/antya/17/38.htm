Antya-līlāChapter 17: The Bodily Transformations of Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 17.38

venu-nāda amrita-ghole, amrita-samāna mithā bole,

amrita-samāna bhūshana-śiñjita

tina amrite hare kāna, hare mana, hare prāna,

kemane nārī dharibeka cita?"

SYNONYMS

venu-nāda — the vibration of the flute; amrita-ghole — like nectarean buttermilk; amrita-samāna — equal to nectar; mithā bole — sweet talking; amrita-samāna — exactly like nectar; bhūshana-śiñjita — the vibration of ornaments; tina — three; amrite — nectars; hare — attract; kāna — the ear; hare — attract; mana — the mind; hare — attract; prāna — the life; kemane — how; nārī — women; dharibeka — will keep; cita — patience or consciousness.

TRANSLATION

"'The nectarean buttermilk of Your flute's vibration, the nectar of Your sweet words and the nectarean sound of Your ornaments mix together to attract our ears, minds and lives. In this way You are killing us.'"
