Antya-līlāChapter 17: The Bodily Transformations of Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 17.16

petera bhitara hasta-pada — kūrmera ākāra

mukhe phena, pulakāńga, netre aśru-dhāra

SYNONYMS

petera — the abdomen; bhitara — within; hasta-pada — the arms and legs; kūrmera ākāra — just like a tortoise; mukhe — in the mouth; phena — foam; pulaka-ańga — eruptions on the body; netre — in the eyes; aśru-dhāra — a flow of tears.

TRANSLATION

His arms and legs had entered the trunk of His body, exactly like those of a tortoise. His mouth was foaming, there were eruptions on His body, and tears flowed from His eyes.
