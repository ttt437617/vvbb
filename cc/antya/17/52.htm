Antya-līlāChapter 17: The Bodily Transformations of Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 17.52

"ei krishnera virahe, udvege mana sthira nahe,

prāpty-upāya-cintana nā yāya

yebā tumi sakhī-gana, vishāde bāula mana,

kāre puchoń, ke kahe upāya?

SYNONYMS

ei — this; krishnera — of Krishna; virahe — in separation; udvege — in anxiety; mana — mind; sthira — patient; nahe — is not; prāpti-upāya — the means for obtaining; cintana nā yāya — I cannot think of; yebā — all; tumi — you; sakhī-gana — friends; vishāde — in lamentation; bāula — maddened; mana — minds; kāre — whom; puchoń — shall I ask; ke — who; kahe — will speak; upāya — the means.

TRANSLATION

"The anxiety caused by separation from Krishna has made Me impatient, and I can think of no way to meet Him. O My friends, you are also deranged by lamentation. Who, therefore, will tell Me how to find Him?
