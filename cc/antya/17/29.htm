Antya-līlāChapter 17: The Bodily Transformations of Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 17.29

bhāvāveśe svarūpe kahena gadgada-vānī

'karna trishnāya mare, pada rasāyana, śuni'

SYNONYMS

bhāva-āveśe — in great ecstasy; svarūpe — to Svarūpa Dāmodara; kahena — says; gadgada-vānī — in a faltering voice; karna — the ears; trishnāya — because of thirst; mare — dies; pada — recite; rasa-āyana — something relishable; śuni — let Me hear.

TRANSLATION

In great ecstasy, Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu said to Svarūpa Dāmodara in a faltering voice, "My ears are dying of thirst. Please recite something to quench this thirst. Let Me hear it."
