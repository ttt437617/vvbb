Antya-līlāChapter 17: The Bodily Transformations of Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 17.47

yebā lakshmī-thākurānī, teńho ye kākalī śuni',

krishna-pāśa āise pratyāśāya

nā pāya krishnera sańga, bāde trishnā-tarańga,

tapa kare, tabu nāhi pāya

SYNONYMS

yebā — even; lakshmī-thākurānī — the goddess of fortune; teńho — she; ye — which; kākalī — vibration of the flute; śuni' — hearing; krishna-pāśa — to Lord Krishna; āise — comes; pratyāśāya — with great hope; nā pāya — does not get; krishnera sańga — association with Krishna; bāde — increase; trishnā — of thirst; tarańga — the waves; tapa kare — undergoes austerity; tabu — still; nāhi pāya — does not get.

TRANSLATION

"When she hears the vibration of Krishna's flute, even the goddess of fortune comes to Him, greatly hoping for His association, but nevertheless she does not get it. When the waves of thirst for His association increase, she performs austerities, but still she cannot meet Him.
