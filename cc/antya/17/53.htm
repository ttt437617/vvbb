Antya-līlāChapter 17: The Bodily Transformations of Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 17.53

hā hā sakhi, ki kari upāya!

kāńhā karoń, kāhāń yāńa, kāhāń gele krishna pāńa,

krishna vinā prāna mora yāya"

SYNONYMS

hā hā — O; sakhi — friends; ki — what; kari — shall I do; upāya — means; kāńhā karoń — what shall I do; kāhāń yāńa — where shall I go; kāhāń gele — where going; krishna pāńa — I can get Krishna; krishna vinā — without Krishna; prāna — life; mora — My; yāya — is leaving.

TRANSLATION

"O My dear friends, how shall I find Krishna? What shall I do? Where shall I go? Where can I meet Him? Because I cannot find Krishna, My life is leaving Me."
