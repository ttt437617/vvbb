Antya-līlāChapter 17: The Bodily Transformations of Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 17.56

kahitei ha-ila smriti, citte haila krishna-sphūrti,

sakhīre kahe hañā vismite

"yāre cāhi chādite, sei śuñā āche citte,

kona rīte nā pāri chādite"

SYNONYMS

kahitei — while speaking; ha-ila — there was; smriti — remembrance; citte — in the heart; haila — there was; krishna-sphūrti — the appearance of Krishna; sakhīre — to the friends; kahe — said; hañā vismite — being very much astonished; yāre — He whom; cāhi chādite — I want to give up; sei — that person; śuñā āche — is lying; citte — in the heart; kona rīte — by any process; nā pāri — I am not able; chādite — to give up.

TRANSLATION

While speaking in this way, Śrīmatī Rādhārānī suddenly remembered Krishna. Indeed, He appeared within Her heart. Greatly astonished, She told Her friends, "The person I want to forget is lying in My heart."
