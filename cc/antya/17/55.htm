Antya-līlāChapter 17: The Bodily Transformations of Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 17.55

"dekhi ei upāye, krishna-āśā chādi' diye,

āśā chādile sukhī haya mana

chāda' krishna-kathā adhanya, kaha anya-kathā dhanya,

yāte haya krishna-vismarana"

SYNONYMS

dekhi — I see; ei upāye — this means; krishna-āśā — hope for Krishna; chādi' diye — I give up; āśā — hope; chādile — if I give up; sukhī — happy; haya — becomes; mana — the mind; chāda' — give up; krishna-kathā — talks of Krishna; adhanya — most inglorious; kaha — speak; anya-kathā — other topics; dhanya — glorious; yāte — by which; haya — there is; krishna-vismarana — forgetfulness of Krishna.

TRANSLATION

Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu said, "If I give up hope of meeting Krishna, I shall then be happy. Therefore, let us stop this most inglorious discussion of Krishna. It would be better for us to talk of glorious topics and forget Him."
