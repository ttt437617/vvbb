Antya-līlāChapter 17: The Bodily Transformations of Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 17.59

"mana mora vāma-dīna, jala vinā yena mīna,

krishna vinā kshane mari' yāya

madhura-hāsya-vadane, mana-netra-rasāyane,

krishna-trishnā dviguna bādāya

SYNONYMS

mana mora — My mind; vāma-dīna — unagreeably poor; jala — water; vinā — without; yena — as if; mīna — a fish; krishna — Lord Krishna; vinā — without; kshane — in a moment; mari' yāya — dies; madhura — sweet; hāsya — smiling; vadane — face; mana — the mind; netra — the eyes; rasa-āyane — very pleasing to; krishna-trishnā — the thirst for Krishna; dvi-guna — twice as much; bādāya — increases.

TRANSLATION

"If I do not think of Krishna, My impoverished mind will die within a moment like a fish out of water. But when I see Krishna's sweetly smiling face, My mind and eyes are so pleased that My desire for Him redoubles.
