Antya-līlāChapter 17: The Bodily Transformations of Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 17.49

karite aiche vilāpa, uthila udvega, bhāva,

mane kāho nāhi ālambana

udvega, vishāda, mati, autsukya, trāsa, dhriti, smriti,

nānā-bhāvera ha-ila milana

SYNONYMS

karite — doing; aiche — such; vilāpa — lamentation; uthila — there arose; udvega — agitation; bhāva — ecstasy; mane — in the mind; kāho — anywhere; nāhi — there is not; ālambana — shelter; udvega — anxiety; vishāda — lamentation; mati — attention; autsukya — eagerness; trāsa — fear; dhriti — determination; smriti — remembrance; nānā-bhāvera — of various ecstasies; ha-ila — there was; milana — combining.

TRANSLATION

While Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu lamented in this way, agitation and ecstasy awoke in His mind, and He became very restless. Many transcendental ecstasies combined in Him, including anxiety, lamentation, attention, eagerness, fear, determination and remembrance.
