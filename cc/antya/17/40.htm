Antya-līlāChapter 17: The Bodily Transformations of Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 17.40

nadaj-jalada-nisvanah śravana-karshi-sac-chiñjitah

sanarma-rasa-sūcakākshara-padārtha-bhańgy-uktikah

ramādika-varāńganā-hridaya-hāri-vamśī-kalah

sa me madana-mohanah sakhi tanoti karna-sprihām

SYNONYMS

nadat — resounding; jalada — the cloud; nisvanah — whose voice; śravana — the ears; karshi — attracting; sat-śiñjitah — the tinkling of whose ornaments; sa-narma — with deep meaning; rasa-sūcaka — joking; akshara — letters; pada-artha — meanings; bhańgi — indications; uktikah — whose talk; ramā-ādika — beginning with the goddess of fortune; vara-ańganā — of beautiful women; hridaya-hāri — attracting the hearts; vamśī-kalah — the sound of whose flute; sah — that; me — My; madana-mohanah — Madana-mohana; sakhi — My dear friend; tanoti — expands; karna-sprihām — the desire of the ears.

TRANSLATION

Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu continued, "'My dear friend, the Supreme Personality of Godhead, Krishna, has a voice as deep as a cloud resounding in the sky. With the tinkling of His ornaments He attracts the ears of the gopīs, and with the sound of His flute He attracts even the goddess of fortune and other beautiful women. That Personality of Godhead, known as Madana-mohana, whose joking words carry many indications and deep meanings, is increasing the lusty desires of My ears.'

PURPORT

This verse is found in the Govinda-līlāmrita (8.5).
