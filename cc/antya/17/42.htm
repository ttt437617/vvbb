Antya-līlāChapter 17: The Bodily Transformations of Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 17.42

kaha, sakhi, ki kari upāya?

krishnera se śabda-gune, harile āmāra kāne,

ebe nā pāya, trishnāya mari' yāya

SYNONYMS

kaha — please say; sakhi — My dear friend; ki — what; kari — can I do; upāya — means; krishnera — of Krishna; se — that; śabda — of the sound; gune — the qualities; harile — having attracted; āmāra — My; kāne — ears; ebe — now; nā pāya — do not get; trishnāya — from thirst; mari' yāya — I am dying.

TRANSLATION

"My dear friend, please tell Me what to do. My ears have been plundered by the qualities of Krishna's sound. Now, however, I cannot hear His transcendental sound, and I am almost dead for want of it.
