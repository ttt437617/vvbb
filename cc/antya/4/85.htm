Antya-līlāChapter 4: Sanātana Gosvāmī Visits the Lord at Jagannātha Purī

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 4.85

kāshthera putalī yena kuhake nācāya

āpane nā jāne, putalī kibā nāce gāya!

SYNONYMS

kāshthera putalī — a doll made of wood; yena — as; kuhake nācāya — magician causes to dance; āpane — personally; nā jāne — does not know; putalī — the doll; kibā — how; nāce — dances; gāya — sings.

TRANSLATION

"A wooden doll chants and dances according to the direction of a magician but does not know how he is dancing and singing.
