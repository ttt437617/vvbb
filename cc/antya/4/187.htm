Antya-līlāChapter 4: Sanātana Gosvāmī Visits the Lord at Jagannātha Purī

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 4.187

'lālyāmedhya' lālakera candana-sama bhāya

sanātanera klede āmāra ghrinā nā upajāya"

SYNONYMS

lālya — of the maintained child; amedhya — stool and urine; lālakera — of the maintainer; candana-sama — like sandalwood pulp; bhāya — appears; sanātanera — of Sanātana Gosvāmī; klede — unto the moisture of the sores; āmāra — My; ghrinā — hatred; nā — not; upajāya — arises.

TRANSLATION

"The stool and urine of the maintained child appear like sandalwood pulp to the mother. Similarly, when the foul moisture oozing from the sores of Sanātana touches My body, I have no hatred for him."
