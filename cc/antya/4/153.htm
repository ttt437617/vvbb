Antya-līlāChapter 4: Sanātana Gosvāmī Visits the Lord at Jagannātha Purī

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 4.153

tāhāte āmāra ańge kandu-rasā-rakta cale

tomāra ańge lāge, tabu sparśaha tumi bale

SYNONYMS

tāhāte — over and above this; āmāra — my; ańge — on the body; kandu-rasā — from wet, itching sores; rakta — blood; cale — runs, tomāra ańge lāge — touches Your body; tabu — still; sparśaha — touch; tumi — You; bale — by force.

TRANSLATION

"Moreover, blood is running from infected itching sores on my body, smearing Your body with moisture, but still You touch me by force.
