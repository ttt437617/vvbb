Antya-līlāChapter 14: Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu's Feelings of Separation from Krishna

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 14.25

dekhiyā govinda āste-vyaste strīke varjilā

tāre nāmāite prabhu govinde nishedhilā

SYNONYMS

dekhiyā — seeing; govinda — the personal servant of Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu; āste-vyaste — with great haste; strīke — the woman; varjilā — got down; tāre — her; nāmāite — to get down; prabhu — Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu; govinde — Govinda; nishedhilā — forbade.

TRANSLATION

When he saw this, Caitanya Mahāprabhu's personal servant, Govinda, hastily got her down from her position. Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu, however, chastised him for this.

PURPORT

Because Garuda is the carrier of Lord Vishnu, he is the supreme Vaishnava. Therefore to touch his body with one's feet or to climb the column of Garuda is certainly a vaishnava-aparādha, an offense to a Vaishnava. The woman was also offensive to Krishna by putting her foot on the shoulder of Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu. Seeing all these offenses, Govinda very hastily made her get down.
