Antya-līlāChapter 14: Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu's Feelings of Separation from Krishna

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 14.45

cintā-kānthā udhi gāya, dhūli-vibhūti-malina-kāya,

'hāhā krishna' pralāpa-uttara

udvega dvādaśa hāte, lobhera jhulani māthe,

bhikshābhāve kshīna kalevara

SYNONYMS

cintā — of anxiety; kānthā — the torn quilt; udhi — covering; gāya — on the body; dhūli — dust; vibhūti — ashes; malina-kāya — dirty body; hāhā — alas; krishna — Krishna; pralāpa-uttara — crazy replies; udvega — distress; dvādaśa — twelve (bangles); hāte — on the wrist; lobhera — of greed; jhulani — turban; māthe — on the head; bhikshā-abhāve — in the absence of alms; kshīna — skinny; kalevara — body.

TRANSLATION

"The yogī of My mind wears the torn quilt of anxiety on his dirty body, which is covered with dust and ashes. His only words are 'Alas! Krishna!' He wears twelve bangles of distress on his wrist and a turban of greed on his head. Because he has not eaten anything, he is very thin.
