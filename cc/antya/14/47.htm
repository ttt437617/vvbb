Antya-līlāChapter 14: Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu's Feelings of Separation from Krishna

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 14.47

daśendriye śishya kari', 'mahā-bāula' nāma dhari',

śishya lañā karila gamana

mora deha sva-sadana, vishaya-bhoga mahā-dhana,

saba chādi' gelā vrindāvana

SYNONYMS

daśa-indriye — the ten senses; śishya kari' — making disciples; mahā-bāula — of a great mendicant; nāma dhari' — taking the name; śishya lañā — taking disciples; karila gamana — has gone; mora — My; deha — body; sva-sadana — own home; vishaya-bhoga — material enjoyment; mahā-dhana — great treasure; saba chādi' — leaving aside everything; gelā vrindāvana — has gone to Vrindāvana.

TRANSLATION

"The mystic yogī of My mind has assumed the name Mahābāula and made disciples of My ten senses. Thus My mind has gone to Vrindāvana, leaving aside the home of My body and the great treasure of material enjoyment.

PURPORT

Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu compares His mind to one of the mystic yogīs known as bāulas, who make at least ten disciples.
