Antya-līlāChapter 14: Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu's Feelings of Separation from Krishna

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 14.42

prāpta-ratna hārāñā, tāra guna sańariyā,

mahāprabhu santāpe vihvala

rāya-svarūpera kantha dhari', kahe 'hāhā hari hari',

dhairya gela, ha-ilā capala

SYNONYMS

prāpta — acquired; ratna — gem; hārāñā — having lost; tāra — of it; guna — attributes; sańariyā — remembering; mahāprabhu — Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu; santāpe — in lamentation; vihvala — overwhelmed; rāya — of Rāmānanda Rāya; svarūpera — of Svarūpa Dāmodara Gosvāmī; kantha dhari' — catching the necks; kahe — said; hāhā hari hari — alas, where is Hari, where is Hari; dhairya — patience; gela — lost; ha-ilā capala — became restless.

TRANSLATION

Having lost His acquired gem, Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu became overwhelmed with lamentation by remembering its attributes. Then, grasping the necks of Rāmānanda Rāya and Svarūpa Dāmodara Gosvāmī, He cried, "Alas, where is My Lord Hari? Where is Hari?" Finally He became restless and lost all patience.
