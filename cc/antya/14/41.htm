Antya-līlāChapter 14: Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu's Feelings of Separation from Krishna

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 14.41

prāpta-pranashtācyuta-vitta ātmā

yayau vishādojjhita-deha-gehah

grihīta-kāpālika-dharmako me

vrindāvanam sendriya-śishya-vrindah

SYNONYMS

prāpta — achieved; pranashta — lost; acyuta — Krishna; vittah — the treasure; ātmā — mind; yayau — went; vishāda — by lamentation; ujjhita — given up; deha-gehah — body and home; grihīta — accepted; kāpālika-dharmakah — the religious principles of a kāpālika-yogī, a kind of mendicant; me — My; vrindāvanam — to Vrindāvana; sa — with; indriya — senses; śishya-vrindah — disciples.

TRANSLATION

Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu said, "At first My mind somehow achieved the treasure of Krishna, but it again lost Him. Therefore it gave up My body and home because of lamentation and accepted the religious principles of a kāpālika-yogī. Then My mind went to Vrindāvana with its disciples, My senses."

PURPORT

This verse is clearly metaphorical.
