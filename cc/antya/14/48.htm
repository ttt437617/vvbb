Antya-līlāChapter 14: Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu's Feelings of Separation from Krishna

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 14.48

vrindāvane prajā-gana, yata sthāvara-jańgama,

vriksha-latā grihastha-āśrame

tāra ghare bhikshātana, phala-mūla-patrāśana,

ei vritti kare śishya-sane

SYNONYMS

vrindāvane — in Vrindāvana; prajā-gana — the citizens; yata — all; sthāvara-jańgama — nonmoving and moving; vriksha-latā — the trees and creepers; grihastha-āśrame — in household life; tāra ghare — at his house; bhikshā-atana — begging from door to door; phala-mūla-patra — fruits, roots and leaves; aśana — eating; ei vritti — this business; kare — performs; śishya-sane — with the disciples.

TRANSLATION

"In Vrindāvana, he goes from door to door begging alms with all his disciples. He begs from both the moving and the inert inhabitants — the citizens, the trees and the creepers. In this way he lives on fruits, roots and leaves.
