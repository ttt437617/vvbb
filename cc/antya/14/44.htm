Antya-līlāChapter 14: Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu's Feelings of Separation from Krishna

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 14.44

krishna-līlā-mandala, śuddha śańkha-kundala,

gadiyāche śuka kārikara

sei kundala kāne pari', trishnā-lāu-thālī dhari',

āśā-jhuli kāndhera upara

SYNONYMS

krishna-līlā-mandala — the ring of the pastimes of Krishna; śuddha — pure; śańkha-kundala — an earring of conchshell; gadiyāche — has manufactured; śuka — Śukadeva Gosvāmī; kārikara — craftsman; sei kundala — that earring; kāne pari' — putting on the ear; trishnā — aspiration; lāu — of squash or gourd; thālī — the bowl; dhari' — taking; āśā — expectation; jhuli — bag; kāndhera upara — on the shoulder.

TRANSLATION

"The ring of Krishna's rāsa-līlā, manufactured by Śukadeva Gosvāmī, the most auspicious craftsman, is as pure as an earring made from a conchshell. The yogī of My mind is wearing that earring upon his ear. From a gourd he has carved out the bowl of My aspirations, and he has taken the bag of My expectations on his shoulder.
