Antya-līlāChapter 14: Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu's Feelings of Separation from Krishna

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 14.43

"śuna, bāndhava, krishnera mādhurī

yāra lobhe mora mana, chādi' loka-veda-dharma,

yogī hañā ha-ila bhikhārī

SYNONYMS

śuna — please hear; bāndhava — My friends; krishnera mādhurī — the sweetness of Lord Krishna; yāra — for which; lobhe — by the greed; mora mana — My mind; chādi' — giving up; loka-veda-dharma — social and Vedic religious principles; yogī hañā — having become a yogī; ha-ila bhikhārī — has become a beggar.

TRANSLATION

"My dear friends," He said, "please hear of Krishna's sweetness. Because of a great desire for that sweetness, My mind has given up all social and Vedic religious principles and taken to the profession of begging, exactly like a mystic yogī.
