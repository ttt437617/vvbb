Antya-līlāChapter 6: The Meeting of Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu and Raghunatha dasa Gosvāmī

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 6.316

simha-dvāre gābhī-āge sei bhāta dāre

sadā-gandhe tailańgī-gāi khāite nā pāre

SYNONYMS

simha-dvāre — at the gate known as Simha-dvāra; gābhī-āge — in front of the cows; sei bhāta — that food; dāre — they throw; sadā-gandhe — because of a rotten smell; tailańgī-gāi — the cows from Tailańga; khāite nā pāre — cannot eat.

TRANSLATION

All the decomposed food is thrown before the cows from Tailańga at the Simha-dvāra gate. Because of its rotten odor, even the cows cannot eat it.
