Antya-līlāChapter 6: The Meeting of Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu and Raghunatha dasa Gosvāmī

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 6.170

"sevaka rakshaka āra keha nāhi sańge

palāite āmāra bhāla eita prasańge"

SYNONYMS

sevaka — servant; rakshaka — watchman; āra — and; keha nāhi — there is no one; sańge — along; palāite — to go away; āmāra — my; bhāla — good; eita — this; prasańge — opportunity.

TRANSLATION

Raghunātha dāsa thought, "This is the greatest opportunity to go away because this time there are no servants or watchmen with me."
