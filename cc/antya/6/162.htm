Antya-līlāChapter 6: The Meeting of Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu and Raghunatha dasa Gosvāmī

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 6.162

advaita-ācāryera teńha 'śishya antarańga'

ācārya-ājñāte māne — caitanya 'prāna-dhana'

SYNONYMS

advaita-ācāryera — of Advaita Ācārya; teńha — Yadunandana Ācārya; śishya — disciple; antarańga — very confidential; ācārya-ājñāte — by the order of Advaita Ācārya; māne — he accepted; caitanya prāna-dhana — Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu as his life and soul.

TRANSLATION

Yadunandana Ācārya had been officially initiated by Advaita Ācārya. Thus he considered Lord Caitanya his life and soul.

PURPORT

Śrīla Bhaktisiddhānta Sarasvatī Thākura comments that although the atheists who have deviated from the order of Śrī Advaita Ācārya introduce themselves as followers of Advaita Ācārya, they do not accept Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu as the Supreme Personality of Godhead, Krishna. Yadunandana Ācārya, one of the most confidential followers of Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu, was the initiated disciple of Advaita Ācārya. He was not polluted by sentimental distinctions classifying Vaishnavas according to birth. Therefore, although Vāsudeva Datta had not been born in a brāhmana family, Yadunandana Ācārya also accepted him as his spiritual master.
