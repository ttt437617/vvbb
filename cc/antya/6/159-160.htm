Antya-līlāChapter 6: The Meeting of Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu and Raghunatha dasa Gosvāmī

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 6.159-160

ei-mata cintite daive eka-dine

bāhire devī-mandape kariyāchena śayane

danda-cāri rātri yabe āche avaśesha

yadunandana-ācārya tabe karilā praveśa

SYNONYMS

ei-mata — in this way; cintite — while he was thinking; daive — by chance; eka dine — one day; bāhire — outside the house; devī-mandape — on the Durgā platform; kariyāchena śayane — was sleeping; danda-cāri — four dandas (ninety-six minutes); rātri — night; yabe — when; āche avaśesha — there remained; yadunandana-ācārya — the priest of the name Yadunandana Ācārya; tabe — then; karilā praveśa — entered.

TRANSLATION

Thus Raghunātha dāsa thought deeply about how to escape, and one night while he was sleeping on the Durgā-mandapa, the priest Yadunandana Ācārya entered the house when only four dandas remained until the end of the night.
