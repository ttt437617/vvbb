Antya-līlāChapter 6: The Meeting of Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu and Raghunatha dasa Gosvāmī

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 6.19

bāra laksha deya rājāya, sādhe biśa laksha

se 'turuk' kichu nā pāñā haila pratipaksha

SYNONYMS

bāra laksha — 1,200,000 coins; deya — delivers; rājāya — unto the Muslim government; sādhe — collects; biśa laksha — 2,000,000 coins; se turuk — that Turk; kichu — anything; nā pāñā — not getting; haila pratipaksha — became his rival.

TRANSLATION

Hiranya dāsa was collecting 2,000,000 coins and therefore should have delivered 1,500,000 to the government. Instead, he was giving only 1,200,000, thus making an extra profit of 300,000 coins. Seeing this, the Muslim caudhurī, who was a Turk, became his rival.
