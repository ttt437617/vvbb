Antya-līlāChapter 1: Śrīla Rūpa Gosvāmī's Second Meeting With the Lord

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 1.18

eka-dina eka-sthāne nadī pāra haite

udiyā nāvika kukkura nā cadāya naukāte

SYNONYMS

eka-dina — one day; eka-sthāne — in one place; nadī — a river; pāra — crossing; haite — to do; udiyā nāvika — a boatman who was an Oriyā (Orissan); kukkura — the dog; nā cadāya — does not allow to mount; naukāte — on the boat.

TRANSLATION

One day, when they needed to cross a river, an Orissan boatman would not allow the dog to get in the boat.
