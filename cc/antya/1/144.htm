Antya-līlāChapter 1: Śrīla Rūpa Gosvāmī's Second Meeting With the Lord

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 1.144

dhari-a padicchanda-gunam

sundara maha mandire tumam vasasi

taha taha rundhasi bali-am

jaha jaha ca-idā palāemhi

SYNONYMS

dhari-a — capturing; padicchanda-gunam — the quality of an artistic picture; sundara — O most beautiful one; maha — My; mandire — within the heart; tumam — You; vasasi — reside; taha taha — that much; rundhasi — You block; bali-am — by force; jaha jaha — as much as; ca-idā — being disturbed; palāemhi — I try to escape.

TRANSLATION

"'"O dearly beautiful one, the artistic loveliness of Your picture is now impressed within My mind. Since You are now living within My mind, wherever I wish to run because I am agitated by impressions of You, I find that You, O My friend, are blocking My way."'

PURPORT

This verse (Vidagdha-mādhava 2.33) is written in the Prakrit language, not in Sanskrit. When transformed into Sanskrit, it reads as follows:

dhritvā praticchanda-gunam sundara mama mandire tvam vasasi

tathā tathā runatsi balitam yathā yathā cakitā palāye

The meaning is the same, but the native language is different. It was spoken to Krishna by Madhumańgala as he read Him a letter from Śrīmatī Rādhārānī.
