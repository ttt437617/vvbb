Antya-līlāChapter 1: Śrīla Rūpa Gosvāmī's Second Meeting With the Lord

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 1.101

krishna-nāmera mahimā śāstra-sādhu-mukhe jāni

nāmera mādhurī aiche kāhāń nāhi śuni

SYNONYMS

krishna-nāmera mahimā — the glories of the holy name of Lord Krishna; śāstra — of the revealed scriptures; sādhu — of the devotees; mukhe — in the mouth; jāni — we can understand; nāmera mādhurī — the sweetness of the holy name; aiche — in that way; kāhāń — anywhere else; nāhi śuni — we do not hear.

TRANSLATION

One has to learn about the beauty and transcendental position of the holy name of the Lord by hearing the revealed scriptures from the mouths of devotees. Nowhere else can we hear of the sweetness of the Lord's holy name.

PURPORT

It is said in the Padma Purāna, atah śrī-krishna-nāmādi na bhaved grāhyam indriyaih [BRS. 1.2.234]. Chanting and hearing of the transcendental holy name of the Lord cannot be performed by the ordinary senses. The transcendental vibration of the Lord's holy name is completely spiritual. Thus it must be received from spiritual sources and must be chanted after having been heard from a spiritual master. One who hears the chanting of the Hare Krishna mantra must receive it from the spiritual master by aural reception. Śrīla Sanātana Gosvāmī has forbidden us to hear the holy name of Krishna chanted by non-Vaishnavas, such as professional actors and singers, for it will have no effect. It is like milk touched by the lips of a serpent, as stated in the Padma Purāna:

avaishnava-mukhodgīrnam pūtam hari-kathāmritam

śravanam naiva kartavyam sarpocchishtam yathā payah

As far as possible, therefore, the devotees in the Krishna consciousness movement gather to chant the holy name of Krishna in public so that both the chanters and the listeners may benefit.
