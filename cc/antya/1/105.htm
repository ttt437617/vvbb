Antya-līlāChapter 1: Śrīla Rūpa Gosvāmī's Second Meeting With the Lord

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 1.105

dui śloka kahi' prabhura haila mahā-sukha

nija-bhaktera guna kahe hañā pañca-mukha

SYNONYMS

dui śloka kahi' — reciting two verses; prabhura — of Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu; haila — there was; mahā-sukha — great pleasure; nija-bhaktera — of His own devotee; guna — the qualities; kahe — describes; hañā — as if becoming; pañca-mukha — five-mouthed.

TRANSLATION

When Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu recited the two important verses, He felt great pleasure; thus, as if He had five mouths, He began to praise His devotee.

PURPORT

The two verses referred to are those beginning with priyah so 'yam (79) and tunde tāndavinī (99).
