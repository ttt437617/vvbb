Antya-līlāChapter 1: Śrīla Rūpa Gosvāmī's Second Meeting With the Lord

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 1.179

"kāńhā tomāra krishna-rasa-kāvya-sudhā-sindhu

tāra madhye mithyā kene stuti-kshāra-bindu"

SYNONYMS

kāńhā — where; tomāra — your; krishna-rasa-kāvya — of exalted poetry concerning the mellows of Lord Krishna's pastimes; sudhā-sindhu — ocean of the nectar; tāra madhye — within that; mithyā — false; kene — why; stuti — prayer; kshāra-bindu — like a drop of alkali.

TRANSLATION

"Your exalted poetic descriptions of the mellows of Lord Krishna's pastimes are like an ocean of nectar. But why have you put in a false prayer about Me? It is like a drop of detestable alkali."
