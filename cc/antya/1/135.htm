Antya-līlāChapter 1: Śrīla Rūpa Gosvāmī's Second Meeting With the Lord

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 1.135

ākshiptah kāla-sāmyena

praveśah syāt pravartakah

SYNONYMS

ākshiptah — set in motion; kāla-sāmyena — by a suitable time; praveśah — the entrance; syāt — should be; pravartakah — named pravartaka.

TRANSLATION

"'When the entrance of the actors is set in motion by the arrival of a suitable time, the entrance is called pravartaka.'

PURPORT

This verse is from the Nātaka-candrikā (12), by Śrīla Rūpa Gosvāmī.
