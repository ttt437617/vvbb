Antya-līlāChapter 16: Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu Tastes Nectar from the Lips of Lord Śrī Krishna

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 16.127

adharāmrita nija-svare, sañcāriyā sei bale,

ākarshaya trijagat-jana

āmarā dharma-bhaya kari', rahi' yadi dhairya dhari',

tabe āmāya kare vidambana

SYNONYMS

adhara-amrita — the nectar of the lips; nija-svare — with the vibration of the flute; sañcāriyā — combining; sei — that; bale — by strength; ākarshaya — attract; tri-jagat-jana — the people of the three worlds; āmarā — we; dharma — religion; bhaya — fear; kari' — because of; rahi' — remaining; yadi — if; dhairya dhari' — keeping patient; tabe — then; āmāya — us; kare vidambana — criticizes.

TRANSLATION

"The nectar of Krishna's lips, combined with the vibration of His flute, attracts all the people of the three worlds. But if we gopīs remain patient out of respect for religious principles, the flute then criticizes us.
