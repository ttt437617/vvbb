Antya-līlāChapter 16: Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu Tastes Nectar from the Lips of Lord Śrī Krishna

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 16.130

adharera ei rīti, āra śuna kunīti,

se adhara-sane yāra melā

sei bhakshya-bhojya-pāna, haya amrita-samāna,

nāma tāra haya 'krishna-phelā'

SYNONYMS

adharera — of the lips; ei — this; rīti — policy; āra — other; śuna — hear; kunīti — injustices; se — those; adhara — lips; sane — with; yāra — of which; melā — meeting; sei — those; bhakshya — eatables; bhojya — foods; pāna — drink or betel; haya — become; amrita-samāna — like nectar; nāma — the name; tāra — of those; haya — becomes; krishna-phelā — the remnants of Krishna.

TRANSLATION

"Such is the policy of these lips. Just consider some other injustices. Everything that touches those lips — including food, drink or betel — becomes just like nectar. It is then called krishna-phelā, or remnants left by Krishna.
