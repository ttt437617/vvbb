Antya-līlāChapter 16: Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu Tastes Nectar from the Lips of Lord Śrī Krishna

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 16.108-109

prabhu kahe, — "ei saba haya 'prākrita' dravya

aikshava, karpūra, marica, elāica, lavańga, gavya

rasavāsa, gudatvaka-ādi yata saba

'prākrita' vastura svāda sabāra anubhava

SYNONYMS

prabhu kahe — Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu said; ei — these; saba — all; haya — are; prākrita — material; dravya — ingredients; aikshava — sugar; karpūra — camphor; marica — black pepper; elāica — cardamom; lavańga — cloves; gavya — butter; rasavāsa — spices; gudatvaka — licorice; ādi — and so on; yata saba — each and every one of them; prākrita — material; vastura — of ingredients; svāda — taste; sabāra — everyone's; anubhava — experience.

TRANSLATION

Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu said, "These ingredients, such as sugar, camphor, black pepper, cardamom, cloves, butter, spices and licorice, are all material. Everyone has tasted these material substances before.

PURPORT

The word prākrita refers to things tasted for the sense gratification of the conditioned soul. Such things are limited by the material laws. Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu wanted to make the point that material things have already been experienced by materially absorbed persons who are interested only in sense gratification.
