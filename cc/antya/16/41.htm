Antya-līlāChapter 16: Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu Tastes Nectar from the Lips of Lord Śrī Krishna

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 16.41

simha-dvārera uttara-dike kapātera āde

bāiśa 'pāhāca'-tale āche eka nimna gāde

SYNONYMS

simha-dvārera — of the Simha-dvāra; uttara-dike — on the northern side; kapātera āde — behind the door; bāiśa pāhāca — of the twenty-two steps; tale — at the bottom; āche — there is; eka — one; nimna — low; gāde — ditch.

TRANSLATION

On the northern side of the Simha-dvāra, behind the door, there are twenty-two steps leading to the temple, and at the bottom of those steps is a ditch.
