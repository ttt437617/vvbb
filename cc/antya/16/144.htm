Antya-līlāChapter 16: Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu Tastes Nectar from the Lips of Lord Śrī Krishna

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 16.144

hena krishnādhara-sudhā, ye kaila amrita mudhā,

yāra āśāya gopī dhare prāna

ei venu ayogya ati, sthāvara 'purusha-jāti',

sei sudhā sadā kare pāna

SYNONYMS

hena — such; krishna-adhara — of Krishna's lips; sudhā — nectar; ye — which; kaila — made; amrita — nectar; mudhā — surpassed; yāra āśāya — by hoping for which; gopī — the gopīs; dhare prāna — continue to live; ei venu — this flute; ayogya — unfit; ati — completely; sthāvara — dead; purusha-jāti — belonging to the male class; sei sudhā — that nectar; sadā — always; kare pāna — drinks.

TRANSLATION

"'This flute is utterly unfit because it is merely a dead bamboo stick. Moreover, it belongs to the male sex. Yet this flute is always drinking the nectar of Krishna's lips, which surpasses nectarean sweetness of every description. Only by hoping for that nectar do the gopīs continue to live.
