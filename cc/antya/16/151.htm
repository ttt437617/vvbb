Antya-līlāChapter 16: Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu Tastes Nectar from the Lips of Lord Śrī Krishna

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 16.151

svarūpa, rūpa, sanātana, raghunāthera śrī-carana,

śire dhari' kari yāra āśa

caitanya-caritāmrita, amrita haite parāmrita,

gāya dīna-hīna krishnadāsa

SYNONYMS

svarūpa — Svarūpa Dāmodara Gosvāmī; rūpa — Śrīla Rūpa Gosvāmī; sanātana — Sanātana Gosvāmī; raghunāthera — of Raghunātha dāsa Gosvāmī; śrī-carana — the lotus feet; śire — on the head; dhari' — taking; kari yāra āśa — hoping for their mercy; caitanya-caritāmrita — the book named Caitanya-caritāmrita; amrita haite — than nectar; para-amrita — more nectarean; gāya — chants; dīna-hīna — the most wretched; krishnadāsa — Krishnadāsa Kavirāja Gosvāmī.

TRANSLATION

Expecting the mercy of Svarūpa, Rūpa, Sanātana and Raghunātha dāsa, and taking their lotus feet on my head, I, the most fallen Krishnadāsa, continue chanting the epic Śrī Caitanya-caritāmrita, which is sweeter than the nectar of transcendental bliss.

Thus end the Bhaktivedanta purports to Śrī Caitanya-caritāmrita, Antya-līlā, Sixteenth Chapter, describing the nectar flowing from Śrī Krishna's lotus lips.
