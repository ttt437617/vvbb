Antya-līlāChapter 16: Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu Tastes Nectar from the Lips of Lord Śrī Krishna

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Antya 16.146

mānasa-gańgā, kālindī, bhuvana-pāvanī nadī,

krishna yadi tāte kare snāna

venura jhutādhara-rasa, hañā lobhe paravaśa,

sei kāle harshe kare pāna

SYNONYMS

mānasa-gańgā — the Ganges of the celestial world; kālindī — the Yamunā; bhuvana — the world; pāvanī — purifying; nadī — rivers; krishna — Lord Krishna; yadi — if; tāte — in those; kare snāna — takes a bath; venura — of the flute; jhuta-adhara-rasa — remnants of the juice of the lips; hañā — being; lobhe — by greed; paravaśa — controlled; sei kāle — at that time; harshe — in jubilation; kare pāna — drink.

TRANSLATION

"'When Krishna takes His bath in universally purifying rivers like the Yamunā and the Ganges of the celestial world, the great personalities of those rivers greedily and jubilantly drink the remnants of the nectarean juice from His lips.
