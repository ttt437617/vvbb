Ādi-līlāChapter 17: The Pastimes of Lord Caitanya Mahāprabhu in His Youth

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Ādi 17.69

murāri-gupta-mukhe śuni' rāma-guna-grāma

lalāte likhila tāńra 'rāmadāsa' nāma

SYNONYMS

murāri-gupta — of Murāri Gupta; mukhe — from the mouth; śuni' — hearing; rāma — of Lord Rāmacandra; guna-grāma — glories; lalāte — on the forehead; likhila — wrote; tāńra — of Murāri Gupta; rāma-dāsa — the eternal servant of Lord Rāmacandra; nāma — the name .

TRANSLATION

Murāri Gupta was a great devotee of Lord Rāmacandra. When Lord Caitanya heard Lord Rāmacandra's glories from his mouth, He immediately wrote on his forehead "rāmadāsa" [the eternal servant of Lord Rāmacandra].
