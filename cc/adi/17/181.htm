Ādi-līlāChapter 17: The Pastimes of Lord Caitanya Mahāprabhu in His Youth

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Ādi 17.181

mora buke nakha diyā ghora-svare bale

phādimu tomāra buka mridańga badale

SYNONYMS

mora — my; buke — on the chest; nakha — nails; diyā — placing; ghora — roaring; svare — in a voice; bale — says; phādimu — I shall bifurcate; tomāra — your; buka — chest; mridańga — for the drum; badale — in exchange.

TRANSLATION

"Placing its nails on my chest, the lion said in a grave voice, 'I shall immediately bifurcate your chest as you broke the mridańga drum!
