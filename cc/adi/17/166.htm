Ādi-līlāChapter 17: The Pastimes of Lord Caitanya Mahāprabhu in His Youth

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Ādi 17.166

go-ańge yata loma, tata sahasra vatsara

go-vadhī raurava-madhye pace nirantara

SYNONYMS

go-ańge — on the body of the cow; yata — as many; loma — hairs; tata — so many; sahasra — a thousand; vatsara — years; go-vadhī — the killer of a cow; raurava-madhye — in a hellish condition of life; pace — decomposes; nirantara — always.

TRANSLATION

"Cow-killers are condemned to rot in hellish life for as many thousands of years as there are hairs on the body of the cow.
