Ādi-līlāChapter 17: The Pastimes of Lord Caitanya Mahāprabhu in His Youth

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Ādi 17.29

ei-mata vaishnava kāre kichu nā māgiba

ayācita-vritti, kimvā śāka-phala khāiba

SYNONYMS

ei-mata — in this way; vaishnava — a devotee; kāre — from anyone; kichu — anything; nā — not; māgiba — shall ask for; ayācita-vritti — the profession of not asking for anything; kimvā — or; śāka — vegetables; phala — fruits; khāiba — shall eat.

TRANSLATION

"Thus a Vaishnava should not ask anything from anyone else. If someone gives him something without being asked, he should accept it, but if nothing comes, a Vaishnava should be satisfied to eat whatever vegetables and fruits are easily available.
