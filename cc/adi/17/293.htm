Ādi-līlāChapter 17: The Pastimes of Lord Caitanya Mahāprabhu in His Youth

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Ādi 17.293

rāsārambha-vidhau nilīya vasatā kuñje mrigākshī-ganair

drishtam gopayitum svam uddhura-dhiyā yā sushthu sandarśitā

rādhāyāh pranayasya hanta mahimā yasya śriyā rakshitum

sā śakyā prabhavishnunāpi harinā nāsīc catur-bāhutā

SYNONYMS

rāsa-ārambha-vidhau — in the matter of beginning the rāsa dance; nilīya — having hidden; vasatā — sitting; kuñje — in a grove; mriga-akshī-ganaih — by the gopīs, who had eyes resembling those of deer; drishtam — being seen; gopayitum — to hide; svam — Himself; uddhura-dhiyā — by first-class intelligence; yā — which; sushthu — perfectly; sandarśitā — exhibited; rādhāyāh — of Śrīmatī Rādhārānī; pranayasya — of the love; hanta — just see; mahimā — the glory; yasya — of which; śriyā — the opulence; rakshitum — to protect that; sā — that; śakyā — able; prabhavishnunā — by Krishna; api — even; harinā — by the Supreme Personality of Godhead; na — not; āsīt — was; catuh-bāhutā — four-armed form.

TRANSLATION

"Prior to the rāsa dance, Lord Krishna hid Himself in a grove just to have fun. When the gopīs came, their eyes resembling those of deer, by His sharp intelligence He exhibited His beautiful four-armed form to hide Himself. But when Śrīmatī Rādhārānī came there, Krishna could not maintain His four arms in Her presence. This is the wonderful glory of Her love."

PURPORT

This is a quotation from the Ujjvala-nīlamani, by Śrīla Rūpa Gosvāmī.
