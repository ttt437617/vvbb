Ādi-līlāChapter 17: The Pastimes of Lord Caitanya Mahāprabhu in His Youth

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Ādi 17.125

krodhe sandhyā-kāle kājī eka ghare āila

mridańga bhāńgiyā loke kahite lāgila

SYNONYMS

krodhe — in anger; sandhyā-kāle — in the evening; kājī — the Chand Kazi; eka ghare — in one home; āila — came; mridańga — drum; bhāńgiyā — breaking; loke — unto the people; kahite — to speak; lāgila — began.

TRANSLATION

Chand Kazi angrily came to one home in the evening, and when he saw kīrtana going on, he broke a mridańga and spoke as follows.
