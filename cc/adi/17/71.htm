Ādi-līlāChapter 17: The Pastimes of Lord Caitanya Mahāprabhu in His Youth

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Ādi 17.71

haridāsa thākurere karila prasāda

ācārya-sthāne mātāra khandāila aparādha

SYNONYMS

haridāsa thākurere — unto Haridāsa Thākura; karila — did; prasāda — benediction; ācārya-sthāne — in the home of Advaita Ācārya; mātāra — of Śacīmātā; khandāila — vanquished; aparādha — the offense.

TRANSLATION

After this incident the Lord blessed Haridāsa Thākura and vanquished the offense of His mother at the home of Advaita Ācārya.

PURPORT

On the mahā-prakāśa day, Lord Caitanya Mahāprabhu embraced Haridāsa Thākura and informed him that he was none other than an incarnation of Prahlāda Mahārāja. When Viśvarūpa took sannyāsa, Śacīmātā thought that Advaita Ācārya had persuaded Him to do so. Therefore she accused Advaita Ācārya of this, which was an offense at His lotus feet. Later Lord Caitanya induced His mother to take the dust of Advaita Ācārya's lotus feet, and thus her vaishnava-aparādha was nullified.
