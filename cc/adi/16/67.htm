Ādi-līlāChapter 16: The Pastimes of the Lord in His Childhood and Youth

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Ādi 16.67

tina pāde anuprāsa dekhi anupama

eka pāde nāhi, ei dosha 'bhagna-krama'

SYNONYMS

tina pāde — in three lines; anuprāsa — alliteration; dekhi — I see; anupama — extraordinary; eka pāde — in one line; nāhi — there is not (alliteration); ei dosha — this fault; bhagna-krama — deviation.

TRANSLATION

"There is extraordinary alliteration in three lines of the verse, but in one line there is no such alliteration. This is the fault of deviation.
