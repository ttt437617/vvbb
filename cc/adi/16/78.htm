Ādi-līlāChapter 16: The Pastimes of the Lord in His Childhood and Youth

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Ādi 16.78

'lakshmīr iva' arthālańkāra — upamā-prakāśa

āra arthālańkāra āche, nāma — 'virodhābhāsa'

SYNONYMS

lakshmīr iva — the words lakshmīr iva ("like Lakshmī"); artha-alańkāra — ornamental use of the meaning; upamā — analogy; prakāśa — manifestation; āra — also; artha-alańkāra — ornamental use of meaning; āche — there is; nāma — which is named; virodha-ābhāsa — possibility of contradiction.

TRANSLATION

"The use of the words 'lakshmīr iva' ['like Lakshmī'] manifests the ornament of meaning called upamā [analogy]. There is also the further ornament of meaning called virodhābhāsa, or a contradictory indication.
