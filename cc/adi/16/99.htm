Ādi-līlāChapter 16: The Pastimes of the Lord in His Childhood and Youth

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Ādi 16.99

tumi bada pandita, mahākavi-śiromani

yāńra mukhe bāhirāya aiche kavya-vānī

SYNONYMS

tumi — you; bada pandita — greatly learned scholar; mahā-kavi — of all great poets; śiromani — the topmost; yāńra — of whom; mukhe — in the mouth; bāhirāya — emanates; aiche — such; kāvya-vānī — poetic language.

TRANSLATION

"You are the most learned scholar and the topmost of all great poets, for otherwise how could such fine poetry come from your mouth?
