Ādi-līlāChapter 16: The Pastimes of the Lord in His Childhood and Youth

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Ādi 16.3

jīyāt kaiśora-caitanyo

mūrti-matyā grihāśramāt

lakshmyārcito 'tha vāg-devyā

diśām jayi-jaya-cchalāt

SYNONYMS

jīyāt — long live; kaiśora — situated in the kaiśora age; caitanyah — Lord Caitanya Mahāprabhu; mūrti-matyā — having accepted such a body; griha-āśramāt — from a householder's life; lakshmyā — by Lakshmī; arcitah — being worshiped; atha — then; vāk-devyā — by the goddess of learning; diśām — of all directions; jayi — the conqueror; jaya-chalāt — on the plea of conquering.

TRANSLATION

Long live Lord Caitanya Mahāprabhu in His kaiśora age! Both the goddess of fortune and the goddess of learning worshiped Him. The goddess of learning, Sarasvatī, worshiped Him in His victory over the scholar who had conquered all the world, and the goddess of fortune, Lakshmīdevī, worshiped Him at home. Since He is therefore the husband or Lord of both goddesses, I offer my obeisances unto Him.
