Ādi-līlāChapter 16: The Pastimes of the Lord in His Childhood and Youth

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Ādi 16.53

kavi kahe, — kaha dekhi, kon guna-dosha

prabhu kahena, — kahi, śuna, nā kariha rosha

SYNONYMS

kavi kahe — the poet said; kaha dekhi — you say "I see"; kon — what; guna — good qualities; dosha — faults; prabhu kahena — the Lord replied; kahi — let Me say; śuna — please hear; nā — do not; kariha — become; rosha — angry.

TRANSLATION

The poet said, "All right, let me see what good qualities and faults You have found."

The Lord replied, "Let Me speak, and please hear Me without becoming angry.
