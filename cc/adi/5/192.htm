Ādi-līlāChapter 5: The Glories Of Lord Nityānanda Balarāma

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Ādi 5.192

śińgā vāmśī bājāya keha, keha nāce gāya

sevaka yogāya tāmbūla, cāmara dhulāya

SYNONYMS

śińgā vāmśī — horns and flutes; bājāya — play; keha — some; keha — some of them; nāce — dance; gāya — sing; sevaka — a servant; yogāya — supplies; tāmbūla — betel nut; cāmara — fan; dhulāya — moves.

TRANSLATION

Some of them played horns and flutes, and others danced and sang. Some of them offered betel nuts, and others waved cāmara fans about Him.
