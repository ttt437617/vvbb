Ādi-līlāChapter 5: The Glories Of Lord Nityānanda Balarāma

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Ādi 5.188

koti-candra jini' mukha ujjvala-varana

dādimba-bīja-sama danta tāmbūla-carvana

SYNONYMS

koti-candra — millions upon millions of moons; jini' — surpassing; mukha — face; ujjvala-varana — bright and brilliant; dādimba-bīja — pomegranate seeds; sama — like; danta — teeth; tāmbūla-carvana — chewing betel nut.

TRANSLATION

His face was more beautiful than millions upon millions of moons, and His teeth were like pomegranate seeds because of His chewing betel.
