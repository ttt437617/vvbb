Ādi-līlāChapter 5: The Glories Of Lord Nityānanda Balarāma

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Ādi 5.105

rudra-rūpa dhari' kare jagat samhāra

srishti-sthiti-pralaya — icchāya yāńhāra

SYNONYMS

rudra-rūpa — the form of Lord Śiva; dhari' — accepting; kare — does; jagat samhāra — annihilation of the material world; srishti-sthiti-pralaya — creation, maintenance and annihilation; icchāya — by the will; yāńhāra — of whom.

TRANSLATION

Assuming the form of Rudra, He destroys the creation. Thus creation, maintenance and dissolution are created by His will.

PURPORT

Maheśvara, or Lord Śiva, is not an ordinary living being, nor is he equal to Lord Vishnu. Effectively comparing Lord Vishnu and Lord Śiva, the Brahma-samhitā says that Vishnu is like milk, whereas Śiva is like yogurt. Yogurt is nothing like milk, but nevertheless it is milk also.
