Ādi-līlāChapter 5: The Glories Of Lord Nityānanda Balarāma

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Ādi 5.166

kabhu kona ańge dekhi pulaka-kadamba

eka ańge jādya tāńra, āra ańge kampa

SYNONYMS

kabhu — sometimes; kona — some; ańge — in parts of the body; dekhi — I see; pulaka-kadamba — eruptions of ecstasy like kadamba flowers; eka ańge — in one part of the body; jādya — stunned; tāńra — his; āra ańge — in another limb; kampa — trembling.

TRANSLATION

Sometimes there were eruptions of ecstasy like kadamba flowers on some parts of his body, and sometimes one limb would be stunned while another would be trembling.
