Ādi-līlāChapter 2: Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu Is the Supreme Personality of Godhead

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Ādi 2.56

sei tina janera tumi parama āśraya

tumi mūla nārāyana — ithe ki samśaya

SYNONYMS

sei — these; tina — three; janera — of the plenary portions; tumi — You; parama — ultimate; āśraya — shelter; tumi — You; mūla — primeval; nārāyana — Nārāyana; ithe — in this; ki — what; samśaya — doubt.

TRANSLATION

"You are the ultimate shelter of these three plenary portions. Thus there is not the slightest doubt that You are the primeval Nārāyana.

PURPORT

Brahmā has confirmed that Lord Krishna is the Supreme, the source of the three manifestations known as Kshīrodakaśāyī Vishnu, Garbhodakaśāyī Vishnu and Kāranodakaśāyī Vishnu (Mahā-Vishnu). For His pastimes, Lord Krishna has four original manifestations — namely Vāsudeva, Sańkarshana, Pradyumna and Aniruddha. The first purusha-avatāra, Mahā-Vishnu in the Causal Ocean, who is the creator of the aggregate material energy, is an expansion of Sańkarshana; the second purusha, Garbhodakaśāyī Vishnu, is an expansion of Pradyumna; and the third purusha, Kshīrodakaśāyī Vishnu, is an expansion of Aniruddha. All these are within the category of manifestations of Nārāyana, who is a manifestation of Śrī Krishna.
