Ādi-līlāChapter 2: Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu Is the Supreme Personality of Godhead

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Ādi 2.61

avatārī nārāyana, krishna avatāra

teńha catur-bhuja, ińha manushya-ākāra

SYNONYMS

avatārī — source of incarnations; nārāyana — Lord Nārāyana; krishna — Lord Krishna; avatāra — incarnation; teńha — that; catuh-bhuja — four arms; ińha — this; manushya — like a man; ākāra — form.

TRANSLATION

Because Nārāyana has four hands whereas Krishna looks just like a man, they say that Nārāyana is the original God whereas Krishna is but an incarnation.

PURPORT

Some scholars argue that because Nārāyana has four hands whereas Śrī Krishna has only two, Nārāyana is the original Personality of Godhead and Krishna is His incarnation. Such unintelligent scholars do not understand the features of the Absolute.
