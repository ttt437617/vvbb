Ādi-līlāChapter 2: Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu Is the Supreme Personality of Godhead

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Ādi 2.74

anuvādam anuktvā tu

na vidheyam udīrayet

na hy alabdhāspadam kiñcit

kutracit pratitishthati

SYNONYMS

anuvādam — the subject; anuktvā — not stating; tu — but; na — not; vidheyam — the predicate; udīrayet — one should speak; na — not; hi — certainly; alabdha-āspadam — without a secure position; kiñcit — something; kutracit — anywhere; pratitishthati — stands.

TRANSLATION

"'One should not state a predicate before its subject, for it cannot thus stand without proper support.'

PURPORT

This rhetorical rule appears in the Ekādaśī-tattva, Thirteenth Canto, in connection with the metaphorical use of words. An unknown object should not be put before the known subject because the object has no meaning if the subject is not first given.
