Ādi-līlāChapter 4: The Confidential Reasons for the Appearance of Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Ādi 4.118

harir esha na ced avātarishyan

mathurāyām madhurākshi rādhikā ca

abhavishyad iyam vrithā visrishtir

makarāńkas tu viśeshatas tadātra

SYNONYMS

harih — Lord Krishna; eshah — this; na — not; cet — if; avātarishyat — would have descended; mathurāyām — in Mathurā; madhura-akshi — O lovely-eyed one (Paurnamāsī); rādhikā — Śrīmatī Rādhikā; ca — and; abhavishyat — would have been; iyam — this; vrithā — useless; visrishtih — the whole creation; makara-ańkah — the demigod of love, Cupid; tu — then; viśeshatah — above all; tadā — then; atra — in this.

TRANSLATION

"O Paurnamāsī, if Lord Hari had not descended in Mathurā with Śrīmatī Rādhārānī, this entire creation — and especially Cupid, the demigod of love — would have been useless."

PURPORT

This verse is spoken by Śrī Vrindā-devī in the Vidagdha-mādhava (7.3) of Śrīla Rūpa Gosvāmī.
