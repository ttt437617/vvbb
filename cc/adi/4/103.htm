Ādi-līlāChapter 4: The Confidential Reasons for the Appearance of Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Ādi 4.103

avatārera āra eka āche mukhya-bīja

rasika-śekhara krishnera sei kārya nija

SYNONYMS

avatārera — of the incarnation; āra — another; eka — one; āche — there is; mukhya-bīja — principal seed; rasika-śekhara — the foremost enjoyer of the mellows of love; krishnera — of Lord Krishna; sei — that; kārya — business; nija — own.

TRANSLATION

There is a principal cause for Lord Krishna's appearance. It grows from His own engagements as the foremost enjoyer of loving exchanges.
