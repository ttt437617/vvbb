Ādi-līlāChapter 4: The Confidential Reasons for the Appearance of Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Ādi 4.93

kimvā 'kānti'-śabde krishnera saba icchā kahe

krishnera sakala vāñchā rādhātei rahe

SYNONYMS

kimvā — or; kānti-śabde — by the word kānti; krishnera — of Lord Krishna; saba — all; icchā — desires; kahe — says; krishnera — of Lord Krishna; sakala — all; vāñchā — desires; rādhātei — in Śrīmatī Rādhārānī; rahe — remain.

TRANSLATION

"Kānti" may also mean "all the desires of Lord Krishna." All the desires of Lord Krishna rest in Śrīmatī Rādhārānī.
