Ādi-līlāChapter 4: The Confidential Reasons for the Appearance of Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Ādi 4.84

'devī' kahi dyotamānā, paramā sundarī

kimvā, krishna-pūjā-krīdāra vasati nagarī

SYNONYMS

devī — the word devī; kahi — I say; dyotamānā — shining; paramā — most; sundarī — beautiful; kimvā — or; krishna-pūjā — of the worship of Lord Krishna; krīdāra — and of sports; vasati — the abode; nagarī — the town.

TRANSLATION

"Devī" means "resplendent and most beautiful." Or else it means "the lovely abode of the worship and love sports of Lord Krishna."
