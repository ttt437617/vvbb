Ādi-līlāChapter 4: The Confidential Reasons for the Appearance of Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Ādi 4.253

anukūla-vāte yadi pāya mora gandha

udiyā padite cāhe, preme haya andha

SYNONYMS

anukūla-vāte — in a favorable breeze; yadi — if; pāya — there is; mora — My; gandha — fragrance; udiyā — flying; padite — to drop; cāhe — She wants; preme — in ecstatic love; haya — becomes; andha — blind.

TRANSLATION

"When a favorable breeze carries to Her the fragrance of My body, She is blinded by love and tries to fly into that breeze.
