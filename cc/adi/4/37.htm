Ādi-līlāChapter 4: The Confidential Reasons for the Appearance of Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Ādi 4.36-37

ei vāñchā yaiche krishna-prākatya-kārana

asura-samhāra — ānushańga prayojana

ei mata caitanya-krishna pūrna bhagavān

yuga-dharma-pravartana nahe tāńra kāma

SYNONYMS

ei — this; vāñchā — desire; yaiche — just as; krishna — of Lord Krishna; prākatya — for the manifestation; kārana — reason; asura-samhāra — the killing of demons; ānushańga — secondary; prayojana — reason; ei mata — like this; caitanya — as Lord Caitanya Mahāprabhu; krishna — Lord Krishna; pūrna — full; bhagavān — the Supreme Personality of Godhead; yuga-dharma — the religion of the age; pravartana — initiating; nahe — is not; tāńra — of Him; kāma — the desire.

TRANSLATION

Just as these desires are the fundamental reason for Krishna's appearance whereas destroying the demons is only an incidental necessity, so for Śrī Krishna Caitanya, the Supreme Personality of Godhead, promulgating the dharma of the age is incidental.
