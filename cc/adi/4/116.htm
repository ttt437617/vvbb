Ādi-līlāChapter 4: The Confidential Reasons for the Appearance of Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Ādi 4.116

so 'pi kaiśoraka-vayo

mānayan madhusūdanah

reme strī-ratna-kūta-sthah

kshapāsu kshapitāhitah

SYNONYMS

sah — He; api — especially; kaiśoraka-vayah — the age of adolescence; mānayan — honoring; madhu-sūdanah — the killer of the Madhu demon; reme — enjoyed; strī-ratna — of the gopīs; kūta — in multitudes; sthah — situated; kshapāsu — in the autumn nights; kshapita-ahitah — who destroys misfortune.

TRANSLATION

"Lord Madhusūdana enjoyed His youth with pastimes on autumn nights in the midst of the jewellike milkmaids. Thus He dispelled all the misfortunes of the world."

PURPORT

This is a verse from the Vishnu Purāna (5.13.60).
