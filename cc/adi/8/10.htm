Ādi-līlāChapter 8: The Author Receives the Orders of Krishna and Guru

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Ādi 8.10

more nā mānile saba loka habe nāśa

ithi lāgi' kripārdra prabhu karila sannyāsa

SYNONYMS

more — unto Me; nā — without; mānile — accepting; saba — all; loka — people in general; habe — will go to; nāśa — destruction; ithi — for this; lāgi' — for the reason of; kripā-ārdra — all merciful; prabhu — Lord Caitanya; karila — accepted; sannyāsa — the sannyāsa order.

TRANSLATION

Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu thought, "Unless people accept Me they will all be destroyed." Thus the merciful Lord accepted the sannyāsa order.

PURPORT

In Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam (12.3.51) it is said, kīrtanād eva krishnasya mukta-sańgah param vrajet: "Simply by chanting the Hare Krishna mantra, or Lord Krishna's name, one is liberated and goes back home, back to Godhead." This Krishna consciousness must be achieved through the mercy of Lord Caitanya Mahāprabhu. One cannot be complete in Krishna consciousness unless he accepts Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu and His associates as the only means for success. It is because of these considerations that the Lord accepted sannyāsa, for thus people would offer Him respect and very quickly come to the platform of Krishna consciousness. Since Lord Caitanya Mahāprabhu, who is Krishna Himself, inaugurated the Krishna consciousness movement, without His mercy one cannot be elevated to the transcendental platform of Krishna consciousness.
