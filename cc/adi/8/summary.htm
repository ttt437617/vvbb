Ādi-līlāChapter 8: The Author Receives the Orders of Krishna and Guru

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Ādi 8 Summary

The Eighth Chapter of Śrī Caitanya-caritāmrita is summarized by Śrīla Bhaktivinoda Thākura in his Amrita-pravāha-bhāshya. In this chapter the glories of Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu and Nityānanda are described, and it is also stated that one who commits offenses in chanting the Hare Krishna mantra does not achieve love of Godhead, even after chanting for many years. In this connection, Śrīla Bhaktivinoda Thākura warns against artificial displays of the bodily symptoms called ashta-sāttvika-vikāra. That is also an offense. One should seriously and sincerely continue to chant the Pañca-tattva names śrī-krishna-caitanya prabhu-nityānanda śrī-advaita gadādhara śrīvāsādi-gaura-bhakta-vrinda. All these ācāryas will bestow their causeless mercy upon a devotee and gradually purify his heart. When he is actually purified, automatically he will experience ecstasy in chanting the Hare Krishna mahā-mantra. Previous to the composition of Śrī Caitanya-caritāmrita, Śrīla Vrindāvana dāsa Thākura wrote a book called Śrī Caitanya-bhāgavata. Only those subjects which were not discussed by Śrīla Vrindāvana dāsa Thākura in his Caitanya-bhāgavata have been taken up by Krishnadāsa Kavirāja Gosvāmī to be depicted in Śrī Caitanya-caritāmrita. In his very old age, Krishnadāsa Kavirāja Gosvāmī went to Vrindāvana, and by the order of Śrī Madana-mohanajī he wrote Śrī Caitanya-caritāmrita. Thus we are now able to relish its transcendental bliss.
