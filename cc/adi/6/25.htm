Ādi-līlāChapter 6: The Glories of Śrī Advaita Ācārya

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Ādi 6.25

'amśa' nā kahiyā, kene kaha tāńre 'ańga'

'amśa' haite 'ańga,' yāte haya antarańga

SYNONYMS

amśa — part; nā kahiyā — not saying; kene — why; kaha — you say; tāńre — Him; ańga — limb; amśa haite — than a part; ańga — limb; yāte — because; haya — is; antarańga — more.

TRANSLATION

Why has Śrī Advaita been called a limb and not a part? The reason is that "limb" implies greater intimacy.
