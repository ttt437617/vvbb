Ādi-līlāChapter 6: The Glories of Śrī Advaita Ācārya

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Ādi 6.5

advaitam harinādvaitād

ācāryam bhakti-śamsanāt

bhaktāvatāram īśam tam

advaitācāryam āśraye

SYNONYMS

advaitam — known as Advaita; harinā — with Lord Hari; advaitāt — from being nondifferent; ācāryam — known as Ācārya; bhakti-śamsanāt — from the propagation of devotional service to Śrī Krishna; bhakta-avatāram — the incarnation as a devotee; īśam — to the Supreme Lord; tam — to Him; advaita-ācāryam — to Advaita Ācārya; āśraye — I surrender.

TRANSLATION

Because He is nondifferent from Hari, the Supreme Lord, He is called Advaita, and because He propagates the cult of devotion, He is called Ācārya. He is the Lord and the incarnation of the Lord's devotee. Therefore I take shelter of Him.
