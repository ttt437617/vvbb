Ādi-līlāChapter 1: The Spiritual Masters

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Ādi 1.71

citram bataitad ekena

vapushā yugapat prithak

griheshu dvy-ashta-sāhasram

striya eka udāvahat

SYNONYMS

citram — wonderful; bata — oh; etat — this; ekena — with one; vapushā — form; yugapat — simultaneously; prithak — separately; griheshu — in the houses; dvi-ashta-sāhasram — sixteen thousand; striyah — all the queens; ekah — the one Śrī Krishna; udāvahat — married.

TRANSLATION

"It is astounding that Lord Śrī Krishna, who is one without a second, expanded Himself in sixteen thousand similar forms to marry sixteen thousand queens in their respective homes."

PURPORT

This verse is from Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam (10.69.2).
