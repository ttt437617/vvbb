Ādi-līlāChapter 1: The Spiritual Masters

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Ādi 1.77

svarūpam anyākāram yat

tasya bhāti vilāsatah

prāyenātma-samam śaktyā

sa vilāso nigadyate

SYNONYMS

sva-rūpam — the Lord's own form; anya — other; ākāram — features of the body; yat — which; tasya — His; bhāti — appears; vilāsatah — from particular pastimes; prāyena — almost; ātma-samam — self-similar; śaktyā — by His potency; sah — that; vilāsah — the vilāsa (pastime) form; nigadyate — is called.

TRANSLATION

"When the Lord displays numerous forms with different features by His inconceivable potency, such forms are called vilāsa-vigrahas."

PURPORT

This is another quotation from the Laghu-bhāgavatāmrita (1.15).
