Ādi-līlāChapter 13: The Advent of Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Ādi 13.90

simha-rāśi, simha-lagna, ucca graha-gana

shad-varga, ashta-varga, sarva sulakshana

SYNONYMS

simha — the lion; rāśi — sign of the zodiac; simha — the lion; lagna — birth moment; ucca — high; graha-gana — all planets; shat-varga — six divisions; ashta-varga — eight divisions; sarva — all; su-lakshana — symptoms of auspiciousness.

TRANSLATION

[According to the Jyotir-veda, or Vedic astrology, the auspicious birth moment is described as follows:] The moon was in Leo [the figure of the lion in the zodiac], Leo was the ascendant, several planets were strongly positioned, and the shad-varga and ashta-varga showed all-auspicious influences.

PURPORT

Śrīla Bhaktisiddhānta Sarasvatī Thākura, who was previously a great astrologer, explains this verse as follows: The shad-varga (six divisions) are technically called kshetra, horā, drekkāna, navāmśa, dvādaśāmśa and trimśāmśa. According to Jyotir-vedic astrology, when the relationship between the planets and the rulers of these six divisions is determined, the auspiciousness of the moment of birth can be calculated. In the book named Brihaj-jātaka and other books there are directions for interpreting the movements of the stars and planets. One who knows the process of calculating the ashta-varga (eight divisions) can predict auspicious and inauspicious events. This science is known especially by persons who are called horā-śāstra-vit, or those who know the astrological scriptures. On the strength of astrological calculations from the horā scriptures, Nīlāmbara Cakravartī, the grandfather of Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu, had ascertained the auspicious moment when the Lord would appear.
