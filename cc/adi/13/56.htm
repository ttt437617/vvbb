Ādi-līlāChapter 13: The Advent of Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Ādi 13.56

śrīhatta-nivāsī śrī-upendra-miśra-nāma

vaishnava, pandita, dhanī, sad-guna-pradhāna

SYNONYMS

śrī-hatta-nivāsī — a resident of Śrīhatta; śrī-upendra-miśra-nāma — by the name of Upendra Miśra; vaishnava — a devotee of Lord Vishnu; pandita — learned; dhanī — rich; sat-guna-pradhāna — qualified with all good qualities.

TRANSLATION

There was also Śrī Upendra Miśra, a resident of the district of Śrīhatta. He was a great devotee of Lord Vishnu, a learned scholar, a rich man and a reservoir of all good qualities.

PURPORT

Upendra Miśra is described in the Gaura-ganoddeśa-dīpikā (35) as the gopāla named Parjanya. The same personality who was formerly the grandfather of Lord Krishna appeared as Upendra Miśra at Śrīhatta and begot seven sons. He was a resident of Dhākā-dakshina-grāma, in the district of Śrīhatta. There are still many residents of that part of the country who introduce themselves as belonging to the Miśra family of Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu.
