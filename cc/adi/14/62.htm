Ādi-līlāChapter 14: Lord Caitanya's Childhood Pastimes

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Ādi 14.62

eka-dina vallabhācārya-kanyā 'lakshmī' nāma

devatā pūjite āila kari gańgā-snāna

SYNONYMS

eka-dina — one day; vallabhācārya-kanyā — the daughter of Vallabhācārya; lakshmī — Lakshmī; nāma — named; devatā — demigods; pūjite — to worship; āila — came; kari — taking; gańgā-snāna — bath in the Ganges.

TRANSLATION

One day a girl of the name Lakshmī, the daughter of Vallabhācārya, came to the bank of the Ganges to take a bath in the river and worship the demigods.

PURPORT

According to the Gaura-ganoddeśa-dīpikā (45), Lakshmī was formerly Jānakī, the wife of Lord Rāmacandra, and Rukminī, the wife of Lord Krishna in Dvārakā. The same goddess of fortune descended as Lakshmī to become the wife of Lord Caitanya Mahāprabhu.
