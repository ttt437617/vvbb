Ādi-līlāChapter 14: Lord Caitanya's Childhood Pastimes

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Ādi 14.67

lakshmī tāńra ańge dila pushpa-candana

mallikāra mālā diyā karila vandana

SYNONYMS

lakshmī — Lakshmīdevī; tāńra — His; ańge — on the body; dila — gave; pushpa — flowers; candana — sandalwood pulp; mallikāra — of the flower of the name mallikā; mālā — garland; diyā — giving; karila — offered; vandana — prayers.

TRANSLATION

On hearing the order of the Supreme Lord, Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu, Lakshmī immediately worshiped Him, offering sandalwood pulp and flowers for His body, garlanding Him with mallikā flowers, and offering prayers.
