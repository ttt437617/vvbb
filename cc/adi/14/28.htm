Ādi-līlāChapter 14: Lord Caitanya's Childhood Pastimes

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Ādi 14.28

kha-i-sandeśa-anna yateka — mātira vikāra

eho māti, seha māti, ki bheda-vicāra

SYNONYMS

kha-i — fused rice; sandeśa — sweetmeat; anna — eatables; yateka — all; mātira — of dirt; vikāra — transformations; eho — this is also; māti — dirt; seha — that; māti — dirt; ki — what; bheda — of difference; vicāra — consideration.

TRANSLATION

"Fused rice, sweetmeats and all other eatables are but transformations of dirt. This is dirt, that is dirt. Please consider. What is the difference between them?
