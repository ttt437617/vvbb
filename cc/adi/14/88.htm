Ādi-līlāChapter 14: Lord Caitanya's Childhood Pastimes

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Ādi 14.88

vipra kahe, — putra yadi daiva-siddha haya

svatah-siddha-jñāna, tabe śikshā vyartha haya

SYNONYMS

vipra kahe — the brāhmana replied; putra — son; yadi — if; daiva — transcendental; siddha — mystic; haya — were; svatah-siddha-jñāna — self-illuminated perfect knowledge; tabe — at that time; śikshā — education; vyartha — futile; haya — becomes.

TRANSLATION

The brāhmana replied, "If your son is a transcendental mystic boy with self-effulgent perfect knowledge, what is the use of your education?"

PURPORT

The brāhmana Jagannātha Miśra saw in his dream told him that his son was not an ordinary human being. If He were a transcendental person, He would have self-effulgent knowledge, and thus there would be no need to educate Him.
