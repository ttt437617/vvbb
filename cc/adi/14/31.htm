Ādi-līlāChapter 14: Lord Caitanya's Childhood Pastimes

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Ādi 14.31

mātira vikāra anna khāile deha-pushti haya

māti khāile roga haya, deha yāya kshaya

SYNONYMS

mātira — of the dirt; vikāra — transformation; anna — food grains; khāile — by eating; deha — of the body; pushti — nourishment; haya — becomes; māti — the dirt; khāile — by eating; roga — disease; haya — becomes; deha — the body; yāya — goes; kshaya — to destruction.

TRANSLATION

Replying to the Māyāvāda idea of the child philosopher, mother Śacī said, "My dear boy, if we eat earth transformed into grain, our body is nourished, and it becomes strong. But if we eat dirt in its crude state, the body becomes diseased instead of nourished, and thus it is destroyed.
