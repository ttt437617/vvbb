Ādi-līlāChapter 7: Lord Caitanya in Five Features

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Ādi 7.43

e saba śuniyā prabhu hāse mane mane

upekshā kariyā kāro nā kaila sambhāshane

SYNONYMS

e saba — all these; śuniyā — after hearing; prabhu — the Lord; hāse — smiled; mane mane — within His mind; upekshā — rejection; kariyā — doing so; kāro — with anyone; na — did not; kaila — make; sambhāshane — conversation.

TRANSLATION

Hearing all this blasphemy, Lord Caitanya Mahāprabhu merely smiled to Himself, rejected all these accusations and did not talk with the Māyāvādīs.

PURPORT

As Krishna conscious devotees, we do not like to converse with Māyāvādī philosophers simply to waste valuable time, but whenever there is an opportunity we impress our philosophy upon them with great vigor and success.
