Ādi-līlāChapter 7: Lord Caitanya in Five Features

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Ādi 7.125

tathāpi acintya-śaktye haya avikārī

prākrita cintāmani tāhe drishtānta ye dhari

SYNONYMS

tathāpi — yet; acintya-śaktye — by inconceivable potency; haya — remains; avikārī — without change; prākrita — material; cintāmani — touchstone; tāhe — in that respect; drishtānta — example; ye — which; dhari — we accept.

TRANSLATION

"Using the example of a touchstone, which by its energy turns iron to gold and yet remains the same, we can understand that although the Supreme Personality of Godhead transforms His innumerable energies, He remains unchanged.
