Ādi-līlāChapter 11: The Expansions of Lord Nityānanda

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Ādi 11.16

rāmadāsa — mukhya-śākhā, sakhya-prema-rāśi

sholasāńgera kāshtha yei tuli' kaila vamśī

SYNONYMS

rāma-dāsa — Rāmadāsa; mukhya-śākhā — chief branch; sakhya-prema-rāśi — full of fraternal love; sholasa-ańgera — of sixteen knots; kāshtha — wood; yei — that; tuli' — raising; kaila — made; vāmśī — flute.

TRANSLATION

Rāmadāsa, one of the chief branches, was full of fraternal love of Godhead. He made a flute from a stick with sixteen knots.
