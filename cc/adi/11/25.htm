Ādi-līlāChapter 11: The Expansions of Lord Nityānanda

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Ādi 11.25

sūryadāsa sarakhela, tāńra bhāi krishnadāsa

nityānande dridha viśvāsa, premera nivāsa

SYNONYMS

sūryadāsa sarakhela — Sūryadāsa Sarakhela; tāńra bhāi — his brother; krishnadāsa — Krishnadāsa; nityānande — unto Lord Nityānanda; dridha viśvāsa — firm faith; premera nivāsa — the reservoir of all love of Godhead.

TRANSLATION

Sūryadāsa Sarakhela and his younger brother Krishnadāsa Sarakhela both possessed firm faith in Nityānanda Prabhu. They were a reservoir of love of Godhead.

PURPORT

In the Bhakti-ratnākara (Twelfth Wave), it is stated that a few miles from Navadvīpa is a place called Śāligrāma that was the residence of Sūryadāsa Sarakhela. He was employed as a secretary in the Muslim government of that time, and thus he amassed a good fortune. Sūryadāsa had four brothers, all of whom were pure Vaishnavas. Vasudhā and Jāhnavā were two daughters of Sūryadāsa Sarakhela.
