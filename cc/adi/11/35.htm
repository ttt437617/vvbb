Ādi-līlāChapter 11: The Expansions of Lord Nityānanda

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Ādi 11.35

mahā-bhāgavata yadunātha kavicandra

yāńhāra hridaye nritya kare nityānanda

SYNONYMS

mahā-bhāgavata — a great devotee; yadunātha kavicandra — Yadunātha Kavicandra; yāńhāra — whose; hridaye — in the heart; nritya — dancing; kare — does; nityānanda — Lord Nityānanda Prabhu.

TRANSLATION

Yadunātha Kavicandra was a great devotee. Lord Nityānanda Prabhu always danced in his heart.

PURPORT

In the Caitanya-bhāgavata, Madhya-khanda, Chapter One, it is said that a gentleman known as Ratnagarbha Ācārya was a friend of Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu's father. They had been residents of the same village. Ratnagarbha Ācārya had three sons — Krishnānanda, Jīva and Yadunātha Kavicandra.
