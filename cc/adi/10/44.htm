Ādi-līlāChapter 10: The Trunk, Branches and Subbranches of the Caitanya Tree

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Ādi 10.44

tāńhāra ananta guna — kahi dińmātra

ācārya gosāñi yāńre bhuñjāya śrāddha-pātra

SYNONYMS

tāńhāra — Haridāsa Thākura's; ananta — unlimited; guna — qualities; kahi — I speak; diń-mātra — only a small part; ācārya gosāñi — Śrī Advaita Ācārya Prabhu; yāńre — to whom; bhuñjāya — offered to eat; śrāddha-pātra — prasādam offered to Lord Vishnu.

TRANSLATION

There was no end to the transcendental qualities of Haridāsa Thākura. Here I mention but a fraction of his qualities. He was so exalted that Advaita Gosvāmī, when performing the śrāddha ceremony of his father, offered him the first plate.
