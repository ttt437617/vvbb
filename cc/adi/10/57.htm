Ādi-līlāChapter 10: The Trunk, Branches and Subbranches of the Caitanya Tree

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Ādi 10.57

'sākshāte' sakala bhakta dekhe nirviśesha

nakula brahmacāri-dehe prabhura 'āveśa'

SYNONYMS

sākshāte — directly; sakala — all; bhakta — devotees; dekhe — see; nirviśesha — nothing peculiar but as He is; nakula brahmacārī — Nakula Brahmacārī; dehe — in the body; prabhura — the Lord's; āveśa — symptoms of power.

TRANSLATION

The appearance of Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu in every devotee's presence is called sākshāt. His appearance in Nakula Brahmacārī as a symptom of special prowess is an example of āveśa.
