Ādi-līlāChapter 10: The Trunk, Branches and Subbranches of the Caitanya Tree

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Ādi 10.98

anna-jala tyāga kaila anya-kathana

pala dui-tina māthā karena bhakshana

SYNONYMS

anna-jala — food and drink; tyāga — renunciation; kaila — did; anya-kathana — talking of other things; pala dui-tina — a few drops of; māthā — sour milk; karena — does; bhakshana — eat.

TRANSLATION

Raghunātha dāsa Gosvāmī gradually gave up all food and drink but a few drops of buttermilk.
