Ādi-līlāChapter 3: The External Reasons for the Appearance of Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Ādi 3.36

āsan varnās trayo hy asya

grihnato 'nu-yugam tanūh

śuklo raktas tathā pīta

idānīm krishnatām gatah

SYNONYMS

āsan — were; varnāh — colors; trayah — three; hi — certainly; asya — of this one; grihnatah — who is manifesting; anu-yugam — according to the age; tanūh — bodies; śuklah — white; raktah — red; tathā — thus; pītah — yellow; idānīm — now; krishnatām — blackness; gatah — obtained.

TRANSLATION

"This boy [Krishna] has three other colors — white, red and yellow — as He appears in different ages. Now He has appeared in a transcendental blackish color."

PURPORT

This is a verse from Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam (10.8.13).
