Ādi-līlāChapter 3: The External Reasons for the Appearance of Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Ādi 3.112

ei ślokera artha kahi sańkshepera sāra

bhaktera icchāya krishnera sarva avatāra

SYNONYMS

ei — this; ślokera — of the verse; artha — the meaning; kahi — I relate; sańkshepera — of conciseness; sāra — the pith; bhaktera — of the devotee; icchāya — by the desire; krishnera — of Lord Krishna; sarva — all; avatāra — incarnations.

TRANSLATION

The essence of the meaning of this verse is that Lord Krishna appears in all His innumerable eternal forms because of the desires of His pure devotees.
