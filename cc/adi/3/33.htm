Ādi-līlāChapter 3: The External Reasons for the Appearance of Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Ādi 3.33

dubhriñ dhātura artha — poshana, dhārana

pushila, dharila prema diyā tri-bhuvana

SYNONYMS

dubhriñ — known as dubhriñ (bhri); dhātura — of the verbal root; artha — the meaning; poshana — nourishing; dhārana — maintaining; pushila — nourished; dharila — maintained; prema diyā — distributing love of God; tri-bhuvana — in the three worlds.

TRANSLATION

The verbal root "dubhriñ" [which is the root of the word "viśvambhara"] indicates nourishing and maintaining. He [Lord Caitanya] nourishes and maintains the three worlds by distributing love of God.
