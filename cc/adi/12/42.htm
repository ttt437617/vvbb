Ādi-līlāChapter 12: The Expansions of Advaita Ācārya and Gadādhara Pandita

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Ādi 12.42

ye danda pāila śrī-śacī bhāgyavatī

se danda prasāda anya loka pābe kati

SYNONYMS

ye danda — the punishment; pāila — got; śrī-śacī bhāgyavatī — the most fortunate mother Śacīdevī; se danda — the same punishment; prasāda — favor; anya — other; loka — person; pābe — can get; kati — how.

TRANSLATION

"A similar punishment was awarded to mother Śacīdevī. Who could be more fortunate than she to receive such punishment?"

PURPORT

Mother Śacīdevī was similarly punished, as mentioned in the Caitanya-bhāgavata, Madhya-khanda, Chapter Twenty-two. Mother Śacīdevī, apparently showing her feminine nature, accused Advaita Prabhu of encouraging her son to become a sannyāsī. Caitanya Mahāprabhu, taking this accusation as an offense, asked Śacīdevī to touch the lotus feet of Advaita Ācārya to mitigate the offense she had supposedly committed.
