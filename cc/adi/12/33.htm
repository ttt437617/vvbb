Ādi-līlāChapter 12: The Expansions of Advaita Ācārya and Gadādhara Pandita

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Ādi 12.33

patra padiyā prabhura mane haila duhkha

bāhire hāsiyā kichu bale candra-mukha

SYNONYMS

patra — note; padiyā — reading; prabhura — of Lord Caitanya Mahāprabhu; mane — in the mind; haila — became; duhkha — unhappiness; bāhire — externally; hāsiyā — smiling; kichu — something; bale — says; candra-mukha — the moon-faced.

TRANSLATION

Lord Caitanya Mahāprabhu became unhappy upon reading the note, although His face still shone as brightly as the moon. Thus, smiling, He spoke as follows.
