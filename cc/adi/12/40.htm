Ādi-līlāChapter 12: The Expansions of Advaita Ācārya and Gadādhara Pandita

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Ādi 12.40

mukti — śreshtha kari' kainu vāśishtha vyākhyāna

kruddha hañā prabhu more kaila apamāna

SYNONYMS

mukti — liberation; śreshtha — the topmost; kari' — accepting; kainu — I did; vāśishtha — the book known as Yoga-vāśishtha; vyākhyāna — explanation; kruddha — angry; hañā — becoming; prabhu — the Lord; more — unto Me; kaila — did; apamāna — disrespect.

TRANSLATION

"Thus I expounded the Yoga-vāśishtha, which considers liberation the ultimate goal of life. For this the Lord became angry at Me and treated Me with apparent disrespect.

PURPORT

There is a book of the name Yoga-vāśishtha that Māyāvādīs greatly favor because it is full of impersonal misunderstandings regarding the Supreme Personality of Godhead, with no touch of Vaishnavism. Factually, all Vaishnavas should avoid such a book, but Advaita Ācārya Prabhu, wanting punishment from the Lord, began to support the impersonal statements of the Yoga-vāśishtha. Thus Lord Caitanya Mahāprabhu became extremely angry at Him and seemingly treated Him disrespectfully.
