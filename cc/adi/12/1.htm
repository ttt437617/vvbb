Ādi-līlāChapter 12: The Expansions of Advaita Ācārya and Gadādhara Pandita

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Ādi 12.1

advaitāńghry-abja-bhrińgāms tān

sārāsāra-bhrito 'khilān

hitvāsārān sāra-bhrito

naumi caitanya-jīvanān

SYNONYMS

advaita-ańghri — the lotus feet of Advaita Ācārya; abja — lotus flower; bhrińgān — bumblebees; tān — all of them; sāra-asāra — real and not real; bhritah — accepting; akhilān — all of them; hitvā — giving up; asārān — not real; sāra-bhritah — those who are real; naumi — offer my obeisances; caitanya-jīvanān — whose life and soul was Lord Caitanya Mahāprabhu.

TRANSLATION

The followers of Śrī Advaita Prabhu were of two kinds. Some were real followers, and the others were false. Rejecting the false followers, I offer my respectful obeisances to Śrī Advaita Ācārya's real followers, whose life and soul was Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu.
