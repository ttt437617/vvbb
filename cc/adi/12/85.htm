Ādi-līlāChapter 12: The Expansions of Advaita Ācārya and Gadādhara Pandita

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Ādi 12.85

śrī-hari ācārya, sādi-puriyā gopāla

krishnadāsa brahmacārī, pushpa-gopāla

SYNONYMS

śrī-hari ācārya — Śrī Hari Ācārya; sādi-puriyā gopāla — Sādipuriyā Gopāla; krishnadāsa brahmacārī — Krishnadāsa Brahmacārī; pushpa-gopāla — Pushpagopāla.

TRANSLATION

The nineteenth branch was Śrī Hari Ācārya; the twentieth, Sādipuriyā Gopāla; the twenty-first, Krishnadāsa Brahmacārī; and the twenty-second, Pushpagopāla.

PURPORT

The Gaura-ganoddeśa-dīpikā (196 and 207) mentions that Hari Ācārya was formerly the gopī named Kālākshī. Sādipuriyā Gopāla is celebrated as a preacher of the Hare Krishna movement in Vikramapura, in East Bengal (now Bangladesh). Krishnadāsa Brahmacārī was formerly among the group of sakhīs known as the ashta-sakhīs. His name was Indulekhā. Krishnadāsa Brahmacārī lived in Vrindāvana. There is a tomb in the Rādhā-Dāmodara temple known as Krishnadāsa's tomb. Some say that this is the tomb of Krishnadāsa Brahmacārī, and others say it is that of Krishnadāsa Kavirāja Gosvāmī. In either case we offer our respects because both of them were expert in distributing love of Godhead to the fallen souls of this age. The Śākhā-nirnaya mentions that Pushpagopāla was formerly known as Svarnagrāmaka.
