Ādi-līlāChapter 12: The Expansions of Advaita Ācārya and Gadādhara Pandita

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Ādi 12.87

amogha pandita, hasti-gopāla, caitanya-vallabha

yadu gāńguli āra mańgala vaishnava

SYNONYMS

amogha pandita — Amogha Pandita; hasti-gopāla — Hastigopāla; caitanya-vallabha — Caitanya-vallabha; yadu gāńguli — Yadu Gāńguli; āra — and; mańgala vaishnava — Mańgala Vaishnava.

TRANSLATION

The twenty-eighth branch was Amogha Pandita; the twenty-ninth, Hastigopāla; the thirtieth, Caitanya-vallabha; the thirty-first, Yadu Gāńguli; and the thirty-second, Mańgala Vaishnava.

PURPORT

Śrīla Bhaktisiddhānta Sarasvatī Thākura writes in his Anubhāshya, "Śrī Mańgala Vaishnava was a resident of the village Titakanā in the district of Murśidābād. His forefathers were śāktas who worshiped the goddess Kirīteśvarī. It is said that Mańgala Vaishnava, formerly a staunch brahmacārī, left home and later married the daughter of his disciple Prānanātha Adhikārī in the village of Mayanādāla. The descendants of this family are known as the Thākuras of Kāńdadā, which is a village in the district of Burdwan near Katwa. Scattered descendants of Mańgala Vaishnava, thirty-six families altogether, still live there. Among the celebrated disciples of Mańgala Thākura are Prānanātha Adhikārī, Purushottama Cakravartī of the village of Kāńdadā, and Nrisimha-prasāda Mitra, whose family members are well-known mridańga players. Sudhākrishna Mitra and Nikuñjavihārī Mitra are both especially famous mridańga players. In the family of Purushottama Cakravartī there are famous persons like Kuñjavihārī Cakravartī and Rādhāvallabha Cakravartī, who now live in the district of Birbhum. They professionally recite songs from Caitanya-mańgala. It is said that when Mańgala Thākura was constructing a road from Bengal to Jagannātha Purī, he found a Deity of Rādhāvallabha while digging a lake. At that time he was living in the locality of Kāńdadā, in the village named Rānīpura. The śālagrāma-śilā personally worshiped by Mańgala Thākura still exists in the village of Kāńdadā. A temple has been constructed there for the worship of Vrindāvana-candra. Mańgala Thākura had three sons — Rādhikāprasāda, Gopīramana and Śyāmakiśora. The descendants of these three sons are still living."
