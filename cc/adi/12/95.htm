Ādi-līlāChapter 12: The Expansions of Advaita Ācārya and Gadādhara Pandita

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Ādi 12.95

tāhāra mādhurya-gandhe lubdha haya mana

ataeva tate rahi' cāki eka kana

SYNONYMS

tāhāra — His; mādhurya — sweet and mellow; gandhe — by the fragrance; lubdha — attracted; haya — becomes; mana — mind; ataeva — therefore; tate — on the beach; rahi' — standing; cāki — I taste; eka — one; kana — particle.

TRANSLATION

It is not possible to dip into that great ocean, but its sweet mellow fragrance attracts my mind. I therefore stand on the shore of that ocean to try to taste but a drop of it.
