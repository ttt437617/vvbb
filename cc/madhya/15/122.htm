Madhya-līlāChapter 15: The Lord Accepts Prasādam at the House of Sārvabhauma Bhattācārya

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 15.122

hena-kāle eka mayūra-pucchera ādānī

rāja-śiropari dhare eka sevaka āni'

SYNONYMS

hena-kāle — at this time; eka — one; mayūra-pucchera — of peacock feathers; ādānī — fan; rāja-śira-upari — above the head of the King; dhare — holds; eka — one; sevaka — servant; āni' — bringing.

TRANSLATION

"While the King and Mukunda dāsa were conversing, a servant brought a fan made of peacock feathers to shade the King's head from the sun. Consequently he held the fan above the King's head.
