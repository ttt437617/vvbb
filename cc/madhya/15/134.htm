Madhya-līlāChapter 15: The Lord Accepts Prasādam at the House of Sārvabhauma Bhattācārya

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 15.134

'dāru'-jala'-rūpe krishna prakata samprati

'daraśana'-'snāne' kare jīvera mukati

SYNONYMS

dāru — wood; jala — water; rūpe — in the forms of; krishna — Lord Krishna; prakata — manifested; samprati — at the present moment; daraśana — by seeing; snāne — by bathing; kare — does; jīvera mukati — the deliverance of the conditioned souls.

TRANSLATION

Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu said, "In this Age of Kali, Krishna is manifest in two forms — wood and water. Thus, by enabling the conditioned souls to see the wood and bathe in the water, He helps them become liberated.
