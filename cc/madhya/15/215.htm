Madhya-līlāChapter 15: The Lord Accepts Prasādam at the House of Sārvabhauma Bhattācārya

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 15.215

mudga-badā, māsha-badā, kalā-badā mishta

kshīra-puli, nārikela-puli āra yata pishta

SYNONYMS

mudga-badā — fried cakes made of mung dhal; māsha-badā — fried cakes made of urad dhal; kalā-badā — fried cakes made of banana; mishta — very sweet; kshīra-puli — cakes made with sweet rice; nārikela-puli — coconut cake; āra — and; yata — varieties of; pishta — cakes.

TRANSLATION

There were badās made of mung dhal, of urad dhal and of sweet bananas, and there were sweet-rice cakes, coconut cakes and various other cakes.
