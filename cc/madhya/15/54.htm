Madhya-līlāChapter 15: The Lord Accepts Prasādam at the House of Sārvabhauma Bhattācārya

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 15.54-55

eka-dina śāly-anna, vyañjana pāńca-sāta

śāka, mocā-ghanta, bhrishta-patola-nimba-pāta

lembu-ādā-khanda, dadhi, dugdha, khanda-sāra

śālagrāme samarpilena bahu upahāra

SYNONYMS

eka-dina — one day; śāli-anna — cooked rice made of śāli paddy; vyañjana — vegetables; pāńca-sāta — five to seven different kinds; śāka — spinach; mocā-ghanta — curry made from banana flowers; bhrishta — fried; patola — patola vegetables; nimba-pāta — with leaves of the nimba tree; lembu — lemon; ādā-khanda — pieces of ginger; dadhi — yogurt; dugdha — milk; khanda-sāra — sugar candy; śālagrāme — unto Lord Vishnu in the form of the śālagrāma; samarpilena — offered; bahu upahāra — many other varieties of food.

TRANSLATION

"One day My mother, Śacī, offered food to Śālagrāma Vishnu. She offered rice cooked from śāli paddies, various kinds of vegetables, spinach, curry made of banana flowers, fried patola with nimba leaves, pieces of ginger with lemon, and also yogurt, milk, sugar candy and many other foods.
