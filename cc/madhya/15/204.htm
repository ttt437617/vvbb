Madhya-līlāChapter 15: The Lord Accepts Prasādam at the House of Sārvabhauma Bhattācārya

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 15.204

pāka-śālāra dakshine — dui bhogālaya

eka-ghare śālagrāmera bhoga-sevā haya

SYNONYMS

pāka-śālāra dakshine — on the southern side of the kitchen; dui bhoga-ālaya — two rooms for offering food; eka-ghare — in one room; śālagrāmera — of Lord Śālagrāma; bhoga-sevā — offering of food; haya — there is.

TRANSLATION

On the southern side of the kitchen were two rooms for offering food, and in one of them the food was offered to Śālagrāma Nārāyana.

PURPORT

Among the followers of the Vedic way, the śālagrāma-śilā, the vigraha of Nārāyana, is worshiped in the form of a stone ball. In India, every brāhmana still worships the śālagrāma-śilā in his home. The vaiśyas and kshatriyas may also engage in this worship, but it is compulsory in the house of a brāhmana.
