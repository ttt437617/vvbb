Madhya-līlāChapter 24: The Sixty-One Explanations of the Ātmārāma Verse

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 24.147

"nirgranthāh" — avidyā-hīna, keha — vidhi-hīna

yāhāń yei yukta, sei arthera adhīna

SYNONYMS

nirgranthāh — nirgranthāh; avidyā-hīna — without ignorance; keha — some of them; vidhi-hīna — without following any regulative principles; yāhāń — wherever; yei — which; yukta — appropriate; sei arthera adhīna — comes under that different import.

TRANSLATION

"The word 'nirgranthāh' means 'without ignorance' and 'devoid of rules and regulations.' Whichever meaning fits may be applied.
