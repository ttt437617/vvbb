Madhya-līlāChapter 24: The Sixty-One Explanations of the Ātmārāma Verse

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 24.50

vīkshyālakāvrita-mukham tava kundala-śrī-

ganda-sthalādhara-sudham hasitāvalokam

dattābhayam ca bhuja-danda-yugam vilokya

vakshah śriyaika-ramanam ca bhavāma dāsyah

SYNONYMS

vīkshya — seeing; alaka-āvrita-mukham — face decorated with curling tresses of hair; tava — Your; kundala-śrī — beauty of earrings; ganda-sthala — falling on Your cheeks; adhara-sudham — and the nectar from Your lips; hasita-avalokam — Your smiling glance; datta-abhayam — which assure fearlessness; ca — and; bhuja-danda-yugam — the two arms; vilokya — by seeing; vakshah — chest; śriyā — by the beauty; eka-ramanam — chiefly producing conjugal attraction; ca — and; bhavāma — we have become; dāsyah — Your maidservants.

TRANSLATION

"'Dear Krishna, we have simply surrendered ourselves as Your maidservants, for we have seen Your beautiful face decorated with tresses of hair, Your earrings falling upon Your cheeks, the nectar of Your lips, and the beauty of Your smile. Indeed, because we have also been embraced by Your arms, which give us courage, and seen Your chest, which is beautiful and broad, we have surrendered ourselves.'

PURPORT

This verse from Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam (10.29.39) was spoken by the gopīs when they arrived near Krishna for the rāsa dance on a full-moon night. The attracted gopīs were awestruck, and they began to speak about how they came to Krishna to enjoy the rāsa dance.
