Madhya-līlāChapter 24: The Sixty-One Explanations of the Ātmārāma Verse

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 24.243

ardha-mārā jīva yadi dhad-phada kare

tabe ta' ānanda mora bādaye antare"

SYNONYMS

ardha-mārā jīva — half-killed living beings; yadi — if; dhad-phada kare — twist and turn because of suffering; tabe — then; ta' — certainly; ānanda — pleasure; mora — my; bādaye antare — increases within.

TRANSLATION

"'When I see half-killed animals suffer, I feel great pleasure.'
