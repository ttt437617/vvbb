Madhya-līlāChapter 24: The Sixty-One Explanations of the Ātmārāma Verse

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 24.99

'duhsańga' kahiye — 'kaitava', 'ātma-vañcanā'

krishna, krishna-bhakti vinu anya kāmanā

SYNONYMS

duhsańga — bad, unwanted association; kahiye — I say; kaitava — cheating; ātma-vañcanā — cheating oneself; krishna — Lord Krishna; krishna-bhakti — devotional service to Krishna; vinu — without; anya — other; kāmanā — desires.

TRANSLATION

"Cheating oneself and cheating others is called kaitava. Associating with those who cheat in this way is called duhsańga, bad association. Those who desire things other than Krishna's service are also called duhsańga, bad association.
