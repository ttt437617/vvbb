Madhya-līlāChapter 24: The Sixty-One Explanations of the Ātmārāma Verse

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 24.226

'nirgrantha hañā' — ei duńhāra 'viśeshana'

āra artha śuna, yaiche sādhura sańgama

SYNONYMS

nirgrantha hañā — becoming nirgrantha; ei — this; duńhāra — of both; viśeshana — the adjective; āra artha — another import; śuna — please hear; yaiche — in which; sādhura sańgama — there is association with devotees.

TRANSLATION

"The word 'nirgrantha' can be taken as an adjective modifying 'muni' and 'ātmārāma.' There is another meaning, which you may hear from Me, indicating association with a devotee. Now I shall explain how it is that through the association of devotees, even a nirgrantha can become a devotee.
