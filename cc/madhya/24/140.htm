Madhya-līlāChapter 24: The Sixty-One Explanations of the Ātmārāma Verse

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 24.140

śreyah-sritim bhaktim udasya te vibho

kliśyanti ye kevala-bodha-labdhaye

teshām asau kleśala eva śishyate

nānyad yathā sthūla-tushāvaghātinām

SYNONYMS

śreyah-sritim — the auspicious path of liberation; bhaktim — devotional service; udasya — giving up; te — of You; vibho — O my Lord; kliśyanti — accept increased difficulties; ye — all those persons who; kevala — only; bodha-labdhaye — for obtaining knowledge; teshām — for them; asau — that; kleśalah — trouble; eva — only; śishyate — remains; na — not; anyat — anything else; yathā — as much as; sthūla — bulky; tusha — husks of rice; avaghātinām — of those beating.

TRANSLATION

"'My dear Lord, devotional service unto You is the only auspicious path. If one gives it up simply for speculative knowledge or the understanding that these living beings are spirit souls and the material world is false, he undergoes a great deal of trouble. He only gains troublesome and inauspicious activities. His actions are like beating a husk that is already devoid of rice. His labor becomes fruitless.'

PURPORT

This is a quotation from Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam (10.14.4).
