Madhya-līlāChapter 24: The Sixty-One Explanations of the Ātmārāma Verse

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 24.244

nārada kahe, — 'eka-vastu māgi tomāra sthāne'

vyādha kahe, — "mrigādi laha, yei tomāra mane

SYNONYMS

nārada kahe — Nārada Muni said; eka-vastu māgi — I wish to beg one thing; tomāra sthāne — from you; vyādha kahe — the hunter replied; mriga-ādi laha — take some of the hunted animals; yei tomāra mane — whatever you like.

TRANSLATION

"Nārada Muni then told the hunter, 'I have one thing to beg of you.'

"The hunter replied, 'You may take whatever animals or anything else you would like.
