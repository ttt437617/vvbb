Madhya-līlāChapter 24: The Sixty-One Explanations of the Ātmārāma Verse

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 24.266

grāme dhvani haila, — vyādha 'vaishnava' ha-ila

grāmera loka saba anna ānite lāgila

SYNONYMS

grāme — in the village; dhvani haila — there was news; vyādha — the hunter; vaishnava ha-ila — has become a Vaishnava, a lover and servitor of Lord Vishnu; grāmera loka — the villagers; saba — all kinds of; anna — food; ānite lāgila — began to bring.

TRANSLATION

"The news that the hunter had become a Vaishnava spread all over the village. Indeed, all the villagers brought alms and presented them to the Vaishnava who had formerly been a hunter.

PURPORT

It is the duty of the public to present a gift to a saintly person, Vaishnava or brāhmana when going to see him. Every Vaishnava is dependent on Krishna, and Krishna is ready to supply all of life's necessities, provided a Vaishnava follows the principles set forth by the spiritual master. There are certainly many householders in our Krishna consciousness movement. They join the movement and live in the society's centers, but if they take advantage of this opportunity and do not work but live at the expense of the movement, eating prasādam and simply sleeping, they place themselves in a very dangerous position. It is therefore advised that grihasthas should not live in the temple. They must live outside the temple and maintain themselves. Of course, if the grihasthas are fully engaged in the Lord's service according to the directions of the authorities, there is no harm in their living in a temple. In any case, a temple should not be a place to eat and sleep. A temple manager should be very careful about these things.
