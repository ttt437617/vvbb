Madhya-līlāChapter 24: The Sixty-One Explanations of the Ātmārāma Verse

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 24.276

kampa-pulakāśru haila krishna-nāma gāñā

ūrdhva bāhu nritya kare vastra udāñā

SYNONYMS

kampa — trembling; pulaka-aśru — tears and jubilation; haila — there were; krishna-nāma gāñā — chanting the Hare Krishna mantra; ūrdhva bāhu — raising the arms; nritya kare — began to dance; vastra udāñā — waving his garments up and down.

TRANSLATION

"When the hunter chanted the Hare Krishna mahā-mantra before his spiritual master, his body trembled and tears welled up in his eyes. Filled with ecstatic love, he raised his hands and began to dance, waving his garments up and down.
