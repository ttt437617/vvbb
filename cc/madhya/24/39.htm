Madhya-līlāChapter 24: The Sixty-One Explanations of the Ātmārāma Verse

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 24.39

bhukti-mukti-siddhi-sukha chādaya yāra gandhe

alaukika śakti-gune krishna-kripāya bāndhe

SYNONYMS

bhukti — material enjoyment; mukti — liberation from material suffering; siddhi — the perfection of mystic yoga; sukha — the happiness derived from these things; chādaya — one gives up; yāra — of which; gandhe — simply by the slight fragrance; alaukika — uncommon, transcendental; śakti-gune — by the power and quality; krishna-kripāya — by the mercy of Lord Krishna; bāndhe — one becomes bound.

TRANSLATION

"Pure devotional service is so sublime that one can very easily forget the happiness derived from material enjoyment, material liberation and mystic or yogic perfection. Thus the devotee is bound by Krishna's mercy and His uncommon power and qualities.
