Madhya-līlāChapter 24: The Sixty-One Explanations of the Ātmārāma Verse

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 24.299

aśvattha-vrikshāś ca bata-vrikshāś ca kapittha-vrikshāś ca āmra-vrikshāś ca vrikshāh

SYNONYMS

aśvattha-vrikshāh — banyan trees; ca — and; bata-vrikshāh — fig trees; ca — and; kapittha-vrikshāh — a type of tree named kapittha; ca — and; āmra-vrikshāh — mango trees; ca — and; vrikshāh — all indicated by the word "trees."

TRANSLATION

"'By the plural word 'vrikshāh' ['trees'], all trees, such as banyan trees, fig trees, kapittha trees and mango trees, are indicated.'
