Madhya-līlāChapter 24: The Sixty-One Explanations of the Ātmārāma Verse

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 24.187

buddhye rame ātmārāma — dui ta' prakāra

'pandita' muni-gana, nirgrantha 'mūrkha' āra

SYNONYMS

buddhye — in intelligence; rame — who enjoys; ātmārāma — is an ātmārāma; dui ta' prakāra — two varieties; pandita — learned; muni-gana — philosophers; nirgrantha — without education; mūrkha — foolish; āra — also.

TRANSLATION

"Everyone has some kind of intelligence, and one who utilizes his intelligence is called an ātmārāma. There are two types of ātmārāmas. One is a learned scholar and philosopher, and the other is an uneducated, illiterate, foolish person.
