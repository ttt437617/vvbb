Madhya-līlāChapter 24: The Sixty-One Explanations of the Ātmārāma Verse

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 24.232

āra kata-dūre eka dekhena śūkara

taiche viddha bhagna-pāda kare dhad-phada

SYNONYMS

āra kata-dūre — still farther along; eka — one; dekhena — sees; śūkara — a boar; taiche — similarly; viddha — pierced; bhagna-pāda — broken legs; kare dhad-phada — twists in pain.

TRANSLATION

"Farther ahead, Nārada Muni saw a boar pierced by an arrow. Its legs were also broken, and it was twisting in pain.
