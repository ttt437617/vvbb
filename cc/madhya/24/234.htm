Madhya-līlāChapter 24: The Sixty-One Explanations of the Ātmārāma Verse

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 24.234

kata-dūre dekhe vyādha vrikshe ońta hañā

mriga māribāre āche bāna yudiyā

SYNONYMS

kata-dūre — somewhat farther; dekhe — Nārada Muni saw; vyādha — the hunter; vrikshe ońta hañā — hiding behind a tree; mriga māribāre — to kill the animals; āche — was there; bāna yudiyā — with arrows in the hand.

TRANSLATION

"When Nārada Muni advanced farther, he saw a hunter behind a tree. This hunter was holding arrows, and he was ready to kill more animals.
