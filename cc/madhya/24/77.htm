Madhya-līlāChapter 24: The Sixty-One Explanations of the Ātmārāma Verse

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 24.77

'ātma'-śabde kahe krishna brihattva-svarūpa

sarva-vyāpaka, sarva-sākshī, parama-svarūpa

SYNONYMS

ātma-śabde — by the word ātmā; kahe — it is said; krishna — the Supreme Lord Krishna; brihattva — the greatest of all; svarūpa — identity; sarva-vyāpaka — all-pervasive; sarva-sākshī — the witness of all; parama-svarūpa — the supreme form.

TRANSLATION

"The word 'ātmā' ['self'] indicates the highest truth, Krishna. He is the all-pervasive witness of all, and He is the supreme form.
