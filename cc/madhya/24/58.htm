Madhya-līlāChapter 24: The Sixty-One Explanations of the Ātmārāma Verse

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 24.58

pakshī, mriga, vriksha, latā, cetanācetana

preme matta kari' ākarshaye krishna-guna

SYNONYMS

pakshī — birds; mriga — animals; vriksha — trees; latā — creepers; cetana-acetana — living entities and even the stones and wood; preme — in ecstatic love; matta — captivated; kari' — making; ākarshaye — attract; krishna-guna — the qualities of Krishna.

TRANSLATION

"The qualities of Krishna captivate and attract everything, living and nonliving. Even birds, animals and trees are attracted to Krishna's qualities.
