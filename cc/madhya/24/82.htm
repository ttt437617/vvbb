Madhya-līlāChapter 24: The Sixty-One Explanations of the Ātmārāma Verse

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 24.82

'brahma-ātmā'-śabde yadi krishnere kahaya

'rūdhi-vrittye' nirviśesha antaryāmī kaya

SYNONYMS

brahma-ātmā-śabde — by the words brahma and ātmā; yadi — if; krishnere kahaya — Krishna is indicated; rūdhi-vrittye — by the direct meaning; nirviśesha — impersonal; antaryāmī — the Supersoul; kaya — is said.

TRANSLATION

"Although the words 'brahma' and 'ātmā' indicate Krishna, their direct meaning refers only to the impersonal Brahman and the Supersoul respectively.
