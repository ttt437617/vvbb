Madhya-līlāChapter 24: The Sixty-One Explanations of the Ātmārāma Verse

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 24.231

vana-pathe dekhe mriga āche bhūme padi'

bāna-viddha bhagna-pāda kare dhad-phadi

SYNONYMS

vana-pathe — on the forest path; dekhe — saw; mriga — deer; āche — there was; bhūme padi' — lying on the ground; bāna-viddha — pierced by an arrow; bhagna-pāda — broken legs; kare dhad-phadi — twisting with pain.

TRANSLATION

"Nārada Muni saw that a deer was lying on the path through the forest and that it was pierced by an arrow. It had broken legs and was twisting due to much pain.
