Madhya-līlāChapter 24: The Sixty-One Explanations of the Ātmārāma Verse

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 24.242

vyādha kahe, — "śuna, gosāñi, 'mrigāri' mora nāma

pitāra śikshāte āmi kari aiche kāma

SYNONYMS

vyādha kahe — the hunter replied; śuna — please hear; gosāñi — O great saintly person; mriga-ari — the enemy of the animals; mora nāma — my name; pitāra śikshāte — by the teaching of my father; āmi — I; kari — do; aiche kāma — such acts.

TRANSLATION

"The hunter replied, 'My dear saintly person, my name is Mrigāri, enemy of animals. My father taught me to kill them in that way.
