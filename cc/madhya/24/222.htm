Madhya-līlāChapter 24: The Sixty-One Explanations of the Ātmārāma Verse

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 24.222

'nirgranthāh' hañā ihāń 'api' — nirdhārane

'rāmaś ca krishnaś ca' yathā viharaye vane

SYNONYMS

nirgranthāh hañā — being liberated saintly persons; ihāń — here; api — the word api; nirdhārane — in the sense of certainty; rāmah ca krishnah ca — both Rāma and Krishna; yathā — as; viharaye — enjoy walking; vane — in the forest.

TRANSLATION

"The word 'nirgranthāh' can be used as an adjective, and 'api' can be used in the sense of certainty. For instance, rāmaś ca krishnaś ca means that both Rāma and Krishna enjoy walking in the forest.

PURPORT

Because it is said that both Rāma and Krishna enjoy wandering in the forest, it is understood that both of Them are enjoying Their tour within the forest.
