Madhya-līlāChapter 24: The Sixty-One Explanations of the Ātmārāma Verse

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 24.114

sanakādyera krishna-kripāya saurabhe hare mana

gunākrishta hañā kare nirmala bhajana

SYNONYMS

sanaka-ādyera — of the four Kumāras, headed by Sanaka; krishna-kripāya — by the mercy of the Lord; saurabhe — the fragrance; hare — took away; mana — the minds; guna-ākrishta hañā — thus being attracted by the qualities of Krishna; kare — perform; nirmala bhajana — pure devotional service.

TRANSLATION

"The minds of the four Kumāras were attracted by the aroma of the flowers offered to Krishna's lotus feet. Being thus attracted by the transcendental qualities of Krishna, they engaged in pure devotional service.
