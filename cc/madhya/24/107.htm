Madhya-līlāChapter 24: The Sixty-One Explanations of the Ātmārāma Verse

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 24.107

jñāna-mārge upāsaka — duita' prakāra

kevala brahmopāsaka, mokshākāńkshī āra

SYNONYMS

jñāna-mārge — on the path of philosophical speculation; upāsaka — worshipers; duita' prakāra — two varieties; kevala — only; brahma-upāsaka — the worshiper of impersonal Brahman; moksha-ākāńkshī — desiring liberation; āra — and.

TRANSLATION

"There are two kinds of worshipers on the path of philosophical speculation — one is called brahma-upāsaka, a worshiper of the impersonal Brahman, and the other is called mokshākāńkshī, one who desires liberation.
