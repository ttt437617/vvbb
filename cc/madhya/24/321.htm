Madhya-līlāChapter 24: The Sixty-One Explanations of the Ātmārāma Verse

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 24.321

krishne sva-dhāmopagate

dharma-jñānādibhih saha

kalau nashta-driśām esha

purānārko 'dhunoditah

SYNONYMS

krishne — Lord Krishna; sva-dhāma — to His personal abode; upagate — having returned; dharma-jñāna-ādibhih saha — along with religious principles, transcendental knowledge, and so on; kalau — in this Age of Kali; nashta-driśām — of persons who have lost their spiritual vision; eshah — this; purāna-arkah — Purāna or Vedic literature that shines like the sun; adhunā — at the present moment; uditah — has arisen.

TRANSLATION

"'After Lord Krishna departed for His abode along with religious principles and transcendental knowledge, this Purāna, Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam, has arisen like the sun in this Age of Kali to enlighten those who have no spiritual vision.'
