Madhya-līlāChapter 24: The Sixty-One Explanations of the Ātmārāma Verse

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 24.38

sarvākarshaka, sarvāhlādaka, mahā-rasāyana

āpanāra bale kare sarva-vismārana

SYNONYMS

sarva-ākarshaka — all-attractive; sarva-āhlādaka — all-pleasing; mahā-rasa-ayana — the complete abode of transcendental mellows; āpanāra bale — by His own strength; kare — causes; sarva-vismārana — forgetfulness of all other bliss.

TRANSLATION

"Lord Krishna is so exalted that He is more attractive than anything else and more pleasing than anything else. He is the most sublime abode of bliss. By His own strength, He causes one to forget all other ecstasies.
