Madhya-līlāChapter 24: The Sixty-One Explanations of the Ātmārāma Verse

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 24.118

nava-yogīśvara janma haite 'sādhaka' jñānī

vidhi-śiva-nārada-mukhe krishna-guna śuni'

SYNONYMS

nava — nine; yogī-īśvara — great saintly yogīs; janma haite — from the very birth; sādhaka — practicers; jñānī — well versed in transcendental knowledge; vidhi — Lord Brahmā; śiva — Lord Śiva; nārada — the great sage Nārada; mukhe — in their mouths; krishna-guna śuni' — hearing the transcendental qualities of Krishna.

TRANSLATION

"From their very births, the nine great mystic yogīs [Yogendras] were impersonal philosophers of the Absolute Truth. But because they heard about Lord Krishna's qualities from Lord Brahmā, Lord Śiva and the great sage Nārada, they also became Krishna's devotees.
