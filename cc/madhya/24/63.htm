Madhya-līlāChapter 24: The Sixty-One Explanations of the Ātmārāma Verse

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 24.63

nija-gune tabe hare dehendriya-mana

aiche kripālu krishna, aiche tāńra guna

SYNONYMS

nija-gune — by transcendental qualities; tabe — then; hare — attracts; deha-indriya-mana — the body, senses and mind; aiche — in that way; kripālu krishna — merciful Krishna; aiche — in that way; tāńra — His; guna — transcendental qualities.

TRANSLATION

"When the devotee is freed from all sinful material activities, Krishna attracts his body, mind and senses to His service. Thus Krishna is very merciful, and His transcendental qualities are very attractive.
