Madhya-līlāChapter 24: The Sixty-One Explanations of the Ātmārāma Verse

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 24.71

'brahma' śabdera artha — tattva sarva-brihattama

svarūpa aiśvarya kari' nāhi yāńra sama

SYNONYMS

brahma — brahma; śabdera artha — the meaning of the word; tattva — the truth; sarva-brihat-tama — the summum bonum among relative truths; sva-rūpa — the original identity; aiśvarya — opulence; kari' — accepting; nāhi — not; yāńra — whose; sama — equal.

TRANSLATION

"The word 'brahma' indicates the summum bonum, the Absolute Truth, which is greater than all other truths. It is the original identity, and there can be nothing equal to that Absolute Truth.
