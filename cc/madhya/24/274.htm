Madhya-līlāChapter 24: The Sixty-One Explanations of the Ātmārāma Verse

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 24.274

tabe sei vyādha dońhāre ańgane ānila

kuśāsana āni' dońhāre bhaktye vasāila

SYNONYMS

tabe — thereafter; sei — that; vyādha — hunter; dońhāre — both Nārada Muni and Parvata; ańgane ānila — brought into the courtyard of his house; kuśa-āsana āni' — bringing straw mats for sitting; dońhāre — both of them; bhaktye — with great devotion; vasāila — made to sit down.

TRANSLATION

"The hunter then received the two great sages in the courtyard of his house. He spread out a straw mat for them to sit upon, and with great devotion he begged them to sit down.
