Madhya-līlāChapter 24: The Sixty-One Explanations of the Ātmārāma Verse

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 24.88

yac ca vrajanty animishām rishabhānuvrittyā

dūre-yamā hy upari nah sprihanīya-śīlāh

bhartur mithah su-yaśasah kathanānurāga-

vaiklavya-bāshpa-kalayā pulakī-kritāńgāh

SYNONYMS

yat — which; ca — also; vrajanti — go; animishām — of the demigods; rishabha-anuvrittyā — by practicing the best means of spiritual life; dūre — keeping at a distance; yamāh — the regulative principles; hi — certainly; upari — above; nah — our; sprihanīya-śīlāh — decorated with desirable qualities; bhartuh — of the master; mithah — mutually; su-yaśasah — who has all transcendental qualities; kathana-anurāga — attracted to discussions; vaiklavya — transformation; bāshpa-kalayā — with tears in the eyes; pulakī-krita — jubilation; ańgāh — bodily limbs.

TRANSLATION

"'Those who discuss the activities of Lord Krishna are on the highest platform of devotional life, and they evince the symptoms of tears in the eyes and bodily jubilation. Such persons discharge devotional service to Krishna without practicing the rules and regulations of the mystic yoga system. They possess all spiritual qualities, and they are elevated to the Vaikuntha planets, which exist above us.'

PURPORT

This is a quotation from Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam (3.15.25). In this verse Lord Brahmā is speaking to all the demigods, who feared the two asuras in Diti's womb. Lord Brahmā described the Kumāras' visit to Vaikuntha, and this was again explained by Maitreya, the friend of Vyāsadeva, when he gave instructions to Vidura.
