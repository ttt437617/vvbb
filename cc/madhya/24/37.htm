Madhya-līlāChapter 24: The Sixty-One Explanations of the Ātmārāma Verse

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 24.37

tvat-sākshāt-karanāhlāda-

viśuddhābdhi-sthitasya me

sukhāni gosh-padāyante

brāhmāny api jagad-guro

SYNONYMS

tvat — Your; sākshāt — meeting; karana — such action; āhlāda — pleasure; viśuddha — spiritually purified; abdhi — ocean; sthitasya — being situated; me — by me; sukhāni — happiness; gosh-padāyante — becomes like a small hole created by the hoof of a calf; brāhmāni — the pleasure derived from impersonal Brahman understanding; api — also; jagat-guro — O master of the universe.

TRANSLATION

"'My dear Lord, O master of the universe, since I have directly seen You, my transcendental bliss has taken the shape of a great ocean. Being situated in that ocean, I now realize all other so-called happiness to be like the water contained in the hoofprint of a calf.'

PURPORT

This is a verse from the Hari-bhakti-sudhodaya (14.36).
