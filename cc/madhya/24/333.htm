Madhya-līlāChapter 24: The Sixty-One Explanations of the Ātmārāma Verse

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 24.333

gopīcandana-mālya-dhriti, tulasī-āharana

vastra-pītha-griha-samskāra, krishna-prabodhana

SYNONYMS

gopī-candana — gopī-candana (available in Vrindāvana and Dvārakā); mālya — beads on the neck; dhriti — wearing regularly; tulasī-āharana — collecting tulasī leaves; vastra — cloth; pītha — temple; griha — the house; samskāra — cleansing; krishna-prabodhana — awakening the Deity of Lord Krishna.

TRANSLATION

"After this, you should describe how one should decorate his body with gopīcandana, wear neck beads, collect tulasī leaves from the tulasī tree, cleanse his cloth and the altar, cleanse his own house or apartment and go to the temple and ring the bell just to draw the attention of Lord Krishna.
