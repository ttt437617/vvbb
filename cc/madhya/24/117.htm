Madhya-līlāChapter 24: The Sixty-One Explanations of the Ātmārāma Verse

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 24.117

harer gunākshipta-matir

bhagavān bādarāyanih

adhyagān mahad-ākhyānam

nityam vishnu-jana-priyah

SYNONYMS

hareh — of Lord Krishna; guna-ākshipta-matih — whose mind was agitated by the qualities; bhagavān — the most powerful transcendentalist; bādarāyanih — Śukadeva, son of Vyāsadeva; adhyagāt — studied; mahat-ākhyānam — the great epic description; nityam — eternally; vishnu-jana-priyah — who is very dear to the Vaishnavas, devotees of Lord Vishnu.

TRANSLATION

"'Being very much attracted by the transcendental pastimes of the Lord, the mind of Śrīla Śukadeva Gosvāmī was agitated by Krishna consciousness. He therefore began to study Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam by the grace of his father.'

PURPORT

This is a quotation from Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam (1.7.11).
