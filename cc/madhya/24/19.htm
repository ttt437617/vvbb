Madhya-līlāChapter 24: The Sixty-One Explanations of the Ātmārāma Verse

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 24.19

'urukrama'-śabde kahe, bada yāńra krama

'krama'-śabde kahe ei pāda-vikshepana

SYNONYMS

urukrama-śabde — by the word urukrama; kahe — one means; bada — great; yāńra — whose; krama — step; krama-śabde — by the word krama; kahe — one means; ei — this; pāda-vikshepana — throwing forth of the foot.

TRANSLATION

"The word 'urukrama' refers to one whose krama [step] is great. The word 'krama' means 'throwing the foot forward,' that is, 'stepping.'
