Madhya-līlāChapter 16: The Lord's Attempt to Go to Vrindāvana

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 16.158

madyapa yavana-rājāra āge adhikāra

tāńra bhaye pathe keha nāre calibāra

SYNONYMS

madyapa — drunkard; yavana — Muslim; rājāra — of a king; āge — ahead; adhikāra — the government; tāńra bhaye — being afraid of such a king; pathe — on the road; keha — anyone; nāre — not able; calibāra — to travel.

TRANSLATION

He informed the Lord that the territory ahead was ruled by a Muslim governor who was a drunkard. Out of fear of this king, no one could walk the road freely.
