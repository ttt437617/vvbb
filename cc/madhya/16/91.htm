Madhya-līlāChapter 16: The Lord's Attempt to Go to Vrindāvana

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 16.91

gauda-deśa diyā yāba tāń-sabā dekhiyā

tumi duńhe ājñā deha' parasanna hañā

SYNONYMS

gauda-deśa — the country known as Bengal; diyā — through; yāba — I shall go; tāń-sabā — both of them; dekhiyā — seeing; tumi duńhe — both of you; ājñā deha' — give Me permission; parasanna hañā — being very pleased.

TRANSLATION

"I shall go to Vrindāvana through Bengal and see both My mother and the river Ganges. Now would you two be pleased to give Me permission?"
