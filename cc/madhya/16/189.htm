Madhya-līlāChapter 16: The Lord's Attempt to Go to Vrindāvana

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 16.189

go-brāhmana-vaishnave himsā karyāchi apāra

sei pāpa ha-ite mora ha-uka nistāra

SYNONYMS

go-brāhmana-vaishnave — to the cows, brāhmanas and Vaishnavas; himsā — violence and envy; karyāchi — I have done; apāra — unlimitedly; sei pāpa ha-ite — from those sinful activities; mora — my; ha-uka — let there be; nistāra — liberation.

TRANSLATION

The Muslim governor then prayed for liberation from the unlimited sinful reactions he had previously incurred by being envious of brāhmanas and Vaishnavas and killing cows.

PURPORT

By chanting the holy names Krishna and Hari, one is certainly liberated from the reactions to such sinful activities as killing cows or insulting brāhmanas and Vaishnavas. It is most sinful to kill cows and insult brāhmanas and Vaishnavas. The karma incurred by such activity is very great, but one can immediately nullify all this karma by surrendering to Lord Krishna and chanting His holy name. After being released from one's sinful reactions (karma), one becomes eager to serve the Lord. This is the test. Since the Muslim governor was immediately purified in the presence of Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu, he could utter the names of Krishna and Hari. Consequently he was eager to render some service, and the Lord, eager to fulfill his desires, immediately had the devotee Mukunda Datta inform the governor that there was some service to render.
