Madhya-līlāChapter 16: The Lord's Attempt to Go to Vrindāvana

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 16.208

mādhava-dāsa-grihe tathā śacīra nandana

laksha-koti loka tathā pāila daraśana

SYNONYMS

mādhava-dāsa-grihe — at the house of Mādhava dāsa; tathā — there; śacīra nandana — the son of mother Śacī; laksha-koti loka — many hundreds of thousands of people; tathā — there; pāila daraśana — got His audience.

TRANSLATION

When the Lord stayed at the house of Mādhava dāsa, many hundreds of thousands of people came to see Him.

PURPORT

Mādhava dāsa is identified as follows. In the family of Śrīkara Cattopādhyāya, Yudhishthira Cattopādhyāya took his birth. Formerly, he and his family members lived in Bilvagrāma and Pātūli. From there he went to Kuliyā-pāhādapura, formerly known as Pādapura. The eldest son of Yudhishthira Cattopādhyāya was known as Mādhava dāsa, the second son was called Haridāsa, and the youngest son was called Krishnasampatti Cattopādhyāya. The three brothers' nicknames were Chakadi, Tinakadi and Dukadi. The grandson of Mādhava dāsa was named Vamśīvadana, and, during the time of Śrīla Bhaktisiddhānta Sarasvatī Thākura, his grandson Rāmacandra and their descendants were still living at Vāghnāpādā, or Vaiñcī.
