Madhya-līlāChapter 6: The Liberation of Sārvabhauma Bhattācārya

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 6.112

ācārya — bhaginī-pati, śyālaka — bhattācārya

nindā-stuti-hāsye śikshā karā'na ācārya

SYNONYMS

ācārya — Gopīnātha Ācārya; bhaginī-pati — sister's husband; śyālaka — wife's brother; bhattācārya — Sārvabhauma Bhattācārya; nindā — sometimes blaspheming; stuti — sometimes by praising; hāsye — sometimes by laughing; śikshā — instruction; karā'na — causes; ācārya — Gopīnātha Ācārya.

TRANSLATION

Gopīnātha Ācārya was the brother-in-law of Sārvabhauma Bhattācārya; therefore their relationship was very sweet and intimate. Under the circumstances, Gopīnātha Ācārya taught him by sometimes blaspheming him, sometimes praising him and sometimes laughing at him. This had been going on for some time.
