Madhya-līlāChapter 6: The Liberation of Sārvabhauma Bhattācārya

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 6.139

veda-purāne kahe brahma-nirūpana

sei brahma — brihad-vastu, īśvara-lakshana

SYNONYMS

veda-purāne — in the Vedas and the Purānas; kahe — it is stated; brahma-nirūpana — explaining the Supreme; sei brahma — that Supreme; brihat-vastu — the greatest; īśvara-lakshana — feature of the Supreme Personality.

TRANSLATION

"All the Vedas and literature that strictly follows the Vedic principles explain that the Supreme Brahman is the Absolute Truth, the greatest of all, and a feature of the Supreme Lord.

PURPORT

The greatest of everything is Śrī Krishna. Lord Krishna states in the Bhagavad-gītā (15.15), vedaiś ca sarvair aham eva vedyah: "By all the Vedas, I am to be known." In Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam (1.2.11) it is said that the Absolute Truth is understood in three phases — namely, Brahman, Paramātmā and Bhagavān, the Supreme Personality of Godhead (brahmeti paramātmeti bhagavān iti śabdyate). Thus the Supreme Personality of Godhead is the last word in understanding the Absolute Truth, Brahman.
