Madhya-līlāChapter 9: Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu's Travels to the Holy Places

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 9.152

duhkha nā bhāviha, bhatta, kailuń parihāsa

śāstra-siddhānta śuna, yāte vaishnava-viśvāsa

SYNONYMS

duhkha — unhappiness; nā — do not; bhāviha — bear; bhatta — My dear Veńkata Bhatta; kailuń parihāsa — I was simply making a joke; śāstra-siddhānta — the conclusion of the revealed scriptures; śuna — hear; yāte — in which; vaishnava-viśvāsa — the faith of the Vaishnavas.

TRANSLATION

The Lord pacified Veńkata Bhatta by saying, "Actually whatever I have said is by way of jest. Now you can hear from Me the conclusion of the śāstras, in which every Vaishnava devotee has firm faith.
