Madhya-līlāChapter 9: Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu's Travels to the Holy Places

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 9.305

brāhmana-samāja saba — vaishnava-carita

vaishnava sakala pade 'krishna-karnāmrita'

SYNONYMS

brāhmana-samāja — the community of brāhmanas; saba — all; vaishnava-carita — pure devotees; vaishnava sakala — all the Vaishnavas; pade — study; krishna-karnāmrita — the Krishna-karnāmrita of Bilvamańgala Thākura.

TRANSLATION

The brāhmana community there was composed of pure devotees. They regularly studied a book entitled Krishna-karnāmrita, which was composed by Bilvamańgala Thākura.

PURPORT

This book was composed by Bilvamańgala Thākura in 112 verses. There are two or three other books bearing the same name, and there are also two commentaries on Bilvamańgala's book. One commentary was written by Krishnadāsa Kavirāja Gosvāmī and the other by Caitanya dāsa Gosvāmī.
