Madhya-līlāChapter 9: Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu's Travels to the Holy Places

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 9.33

sahasra-nāmnām punyānām

trir-āvrittyā tu yat phalam

ekāvrittyā tu krishnasya

nāmaikam tat prayacchati

SYNONYMS

sahasra-nāmnām — of one thousand names; punyānām — holy; trih-āvrittyā — by thrice chanting; tu — but; yat — which; phalam — result; eka-āvrittyā — by one repetition; tu — but; krishnasya — of Lord Krishna; nāma — holy name; ekam — only one; tat — that result; prayacchati — gives.

TRANSLATION

"'The pious results derived from chanting the thousand holy names of Vishnu three times can be attained by only one utterance of the holy name of Krishna.'

PURPORT

This verse from the Brahmānda Purāna is found in the Laghu-bhāgavatāmrita (1.5.354), by Rūpa Gosvāmī. Simply by chanting the name of Krishna once, one can attain the same results achieved by chanting the holy name of Rāma three times.
