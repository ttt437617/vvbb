Madhya-līlāChapter 9: Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu's Travels to the Holy Places

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 9.351

divya mahā-prasāda aneka ānāila

pīthā-pānā ādi jagannātha ye khāila

SYNONYMS

divya — very nice; mahā-prasāda — remnants of food from Jagannātha; aneka — various; ānāila — brought; pīthā-pānā ādi — such as cakes and condensed milk; jagannātha — Lord Jagannātha; ye — which; khāila — ate.

TRANSLATION

Sārvabhauma Bhattācārya brought various types of food remnants that had been left by Lord Jagannātha. He brought all kinds of cakes and condensed-milk preparations.
