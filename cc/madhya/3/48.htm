Madhya-līlāChapter 3: Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu's Stay at the House of Advaita Ācārya

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 3.48

nārikela-śasya, chānā, śarkarā madhura

mocā-ghanta, dugdha-kushmānda, sakala pracura

SYNONYMS

nārikela-śasya — the pulp of coconut; chānā — curd; śarkarā — fruit sugar; madhura — very sweet; mocā-ghanta — a semisolid preparation made with banana flowers; dugdha-kushmānda — newly grown squash cut into pieces and boiled in milk; sakala — all; pracura — plentiful.

TRANSLATION

The preparation made with coconut pulp mixed with curd and rock candy was very sweet. There was a curry made of banana flowers and squash boiled in milk, all in great quantity.
