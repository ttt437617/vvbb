Madhya-līlāChapter 3: Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu's Stay at the House of Advaita Ācārya

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 3.110

gaura-deha-kānti sūrya jiniyā ujjvala

aruna-vastra-kānti tāhe kare jhala-mala

SYNONYMS

gaura — fair-complexioned; deha — of the body; kānti — the luster; sūrya — the sun; jiniyā — conquering; ujjvala — bright; aruna — reddish; vastra-kānti — the beauty of the garments; tāhe — in that; kare — does; jhala-mala — glittering.

TRANSLATION

They saw Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu's very fair-complexioned body and its bright luster, which conquered the brilliance of the sun. Over and above this was the beauty of the saffron garments that glittered upon His body.
