Madhya-līlāChapter 20: Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu Instructs Sanātana Gosvāmī in the Science of the Absolute Truth

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 20.61

akshnoh phalam tvādriśa-darśanam hi

tanoh phalam tvādriśa-gātra-sańgah

jihvā-phalam tvādriśa-kīrtanam hi

su-durlabhā bhāgavatā hi loke

SYNONYMS

akshnoh — of the eyes; phalam — the perfect result of the action; tvādriśa — a person like you; darśanam — to see; hi — certainly; tanoh — of the body; phalam — the perfection of activities; tvādriśa — of a person like you; gātra-sańgah — touching the body; jihvā-phalam — the perfection of the tongue; tvādriśa — a person like you; kīrtanam — glorifying; hi — certainly; su-durlabhāh — very rare; bhāgavatāh — pure devotees of the Lord; hi — certainly; loke — in this world.

TRANSLATION

"'My dear Vaishnava, seeing a person like you is the perfection of one's eyesight, touching your lotus feet is the perfection of the sense of touch, and glorifying your good qualities is the tongue's real activity, for in the material world it is very difficult to find a pure devotee of the Lord.'"
