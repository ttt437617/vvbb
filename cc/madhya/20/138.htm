Madhya-līlāChapter 20: Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu Instructs Sanātana Gosvāmī in the Science of the Absolute Truth

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 20.138

bhaktyāham ekayā grāhyah

śraddhayātmā priyah satām

bhaktih punāti man-nishthā

śva-pākān api sambhavāt

SYNONYMS

bhaktyā — by devotional service; aham — I, the Supreme Personality of Godhead; ekayā — unflinching; grāhyah — obtainable; śraddhayā — by faith; ātmā — the most dear; priyah — to be served; satām — by the devotees; bhaktih — the devotional service; punāti — purifies; mat-nishthā — fixed only on Me; śva-pākān — the lowest of human beings, who are accustomed to eating dogs; api — certainly; sambhavāt — from all faults due to birth and other circumstances.

TRANSLATION

"'Being very dear to the devotees and sādhus, I am attained through unflinching faith and devotional service. This bhakti-yoga system, which gradually increases attachment for Me, purifies even a human being born among dog-eaters. That is to say, everyone can be elevated to the spiritual platform by the process of bhakti-yoga.'
