Madhya-līlāChapter 20: Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu Instructs Sanātana Gosvāmī in the Science of the Absolute Truth

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 20.267

ādyo 'vatārah purushah parasya

kālah svabhāvah sad-asan manaś ca

dravyam vikāro guna indriyāni

virāt svarāt sthāsnu carishnu bhūmnah

SYNONYMS

ādyah avatārah — the original incarnation; purushah — Kāranābdhiśāyī Vishnu; parasya — of the Supreme Lord; kālah — time; svabhāvah — space; sat-asat — cause and effect; manah ca — as well as the mind; dravyam — the five elements; vikārah — transformation or the false ego; gunah — modes of nature; indriyāni — senses; virāt — the universal form; svarāt — Garbhodakaśāyī Vishnu; sthāsnu — immovable; carishnu — movable; bhūmnah — of the Supreme Personality of Godhead.

TRANSLATION

"'Kāranābdhiśāyī Vishnu [Mahā-Vishnu] is the first incarnation of the Supreme Lord, and He is the master of eternal time, space, cause and effects, the mind, the elements, the material ego, the modes of nature, the senses, the universal form of the Lord, Garbhodakaśāyī Vishnu and the sum total of all living beings, both moving and nonmoving.'

PURPORT

This is a quotation from Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam (2.6.42). For an explanation, refer to Ādi-līlā, Chapter Five, verse 83.
