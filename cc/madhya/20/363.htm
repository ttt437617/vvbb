Madhya-līlāChapter 20: Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu Instructs Sanātana Gosvāmī in the Science of the Absolute Truth

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 20.363

avatāra-kāle haya jagate gocara

ei dui lakshane keha jānaye īśvara"

SYNONYMS

avatāra-kāle — at the time of incarnation; haya — there is; jagate — in the world; gocara — information; ei dui lakshane — by these two characteristics, namely svarūpa and tatastha; keha — some persons; jānaye — know; īśvara — the incarnation of the Supreme Lord.

TRANSLATION

"At the time of Their appearance, the incarnations of the Lord are known in the world because people can consult the śāstras to understand an incarnation's chief characteristics, known as svarūpa and tatastha. In this way the incarnations become known to great saintly persons."
