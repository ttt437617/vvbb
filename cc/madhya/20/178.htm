Madhya-līlāChapter 20: Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu Instructs Sanātana Gosvāmī in the Science of the Absolute Truth

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 20.178

saundarya, aiśvarya, mādhurya, vaidagdhya-vilāsa

vrajendra-nandane ihā adhika ullāsa

SYNONYMS

saundarya — the beauty; aiśvarya — the opulence; mādhurya — the sweetness; vaidagdhya-vilāsa — the intellectual pastimes; vrajendra-nandane — of the son of Nanda Mahārāja and Yaśodā; ihā — all these; adhika ullāsa — more jubilant.

TRANSLATION

"When one compares the beauty, opulence, sweetness and intellectual pastimes of Vāsudeva, the warrior, to Krishna, the cowherd boy, son of Nanda Mahārāja, one sees that Krishna's attributes are more pleasant.
