Madhya-līlāChapter 20: Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu Instructs Sanātana Gosvāmī in the Science of the Absolute Truth

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 20.369

'sanakādi', 'nārada', 'prithu' 'paraśurāma'

jīva-rūpa 'brahmāra' āveśāvatāra-nāma

SYNONYMS

sanaka-ādi — the four Kumāras; nārada — Nārada; prithu — Mahārāja Prithu; paraśurāma — Paraśurāma; jīva-rūpa — as the living entity; brahmāra — of Lord Brahmā; āveśa-avatāra-nāma — all of them are called empowered incarnations.

TRANSLATION

"Some śaktyāveśa-avatāras are the four Kumāras, Nārada, Mahārāja Prithu and Paraśurāma. When a living being is empowered to act as Lord Brahmā, he is also considered a śaktyāveśa-avatāra.
