Madhya-līlāChapter 20: Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu Instructs Sanātana Gosvāmī in the Science of the Absolute Truth

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 20.250

prathamei kare krishna 'purushāvatāra'

seita purusha haya trividha prakāra

SYNONYMS

prathamei — in the beginning; kare — does; krishna — Lord Krishna; purusha-avatāra — the incarnation of the three Vishnus (Mahā-Vishnu, Garbhodakaśāyī Vishnu and Kshīrodakaśāyī Vishnu); seita — that; purusha — Vishnu; haya — becomes; tri-vidha prakāra — three different manifestations.

TRANSLATION

"In the beginning, Krishna incarnates Himself as the purusha-avatāras, or Vishnu incarnations. These are of three types.

PURPORT

Up to this verse, the many types of expansions have been described. Now the manifestations of the Lord's different potencies will be described.
