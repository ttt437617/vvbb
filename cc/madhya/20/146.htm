Madhya-līlāChapter 20: Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu Instructs Sanātana Gosvāmī in the Science of the Absolute Truth

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 20.146

mukhya-gauna-vritti, kimvā anvaya-vyatireke

vedera pratijñā kevala kahaye krishnake

SYNONYMS

mukhya — chief; gauna — secondary; vritti — meaning; kimvā — or; anvaya-vyatireke — directly or indirectly; vedera pratijñā — ultimate declaration of the Vedas; kevala — only; kahaye — speaks; krishnake — about Krishna.

TRANSLATION

"When one accepts the Vedic literature by interpretation or even by dictionary meaning, directly or indirectly the ultimate declaration of Vedic knowledge points to Lord Krishna.
