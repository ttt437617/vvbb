Madhya-līlāChapter 12: The Cleansing of the Gundicā Temple

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 12.31

kintu anurāgī lokera svabhāva eka haya

ishta nā pāile nija prāna se chādaya

SYNONYMS

kintu — still; anurāgī — affectionate; lokera — of the people; svabhāva — nature; eka — one; haya — there is; ishta — desirable; nā pāile — without getting; nija — own; prāna — life; se — he; chādaya — gives up.

TRANSLATION

"Still, isn't it the nature of an attached man to give up his life if he does not attain his desired object?
