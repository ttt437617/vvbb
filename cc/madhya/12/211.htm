Madhya-līlāChapter 12: The Cleansing of the Gundicā Temple

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 12.211

trishārta prabhura netra — bhramara-yugala

gādha trishnāya piye krishnera vadana-kamala

SYNONYMS

trishā-ārta — thirsty; prabhura — of Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu; netra — eyes; bhramara-yugala — like two bumblebees; gādha — deep; trishnāya — in thirst; piye — drinks; krishnera — of Lord Krishna; vadana-kamala — the lotuslike face.

TRANSLATION

Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu was very thirsty to see the Lord, and His eyes became like two bumblebees drinking the honey from the lotuslike eyes of Lord Jagannātha, who is Krishna Himself.
