Madhya-līlāChapter 12: The Cleansing of the Gundicā Temple

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 12.66

vidāya hañā rāya āila rāja-putre lañā

rājā sukha pāila putrera ceshtā dekhiyā

SYNONYMS

vidāya hañā — taking leave; rāya — Rāmānanda Rāya; āila — came back; rāja-putre lañā — taking the King's son; rājā — the King; sukha pāila — felt great happiness; putrera — of his son; ceshtā — activities; dekhiyā — seeing.

TRANSLATION

Rāmānanda Rāya and the boy then departed from Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu, and Rāmānanda took him back to the King's palace. The King was very happy when he heard of his son's activities.
