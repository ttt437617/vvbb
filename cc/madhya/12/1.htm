Madhya-līlāChapter 12: The Cleansing of the Gundicā Temple

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 12.1

śrī-gundicā-mandiram ātma-vrindaih

sammārjayan kshālanatah sa gaurah

sva-citta-vac chītalam ujjvalam ca

krishnopaveśaupayikam cakāra

SYNONYMS

śrī-gundicā — known as Gundicā; mandiram — the temple; ātma-vrindaih — with His associates; sammārjayan — washing; kshālanatah — by cleansing; sah — that; gaurah — Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu; sva-citta-vat — like His own heart; śītalam — cool and calm; ujjvalam — bright and clean; ca — and; krishna — of Lord Śrī Krishna; upaveśa — for the sitting; aupayikam — befitting; cakāra — made.

TRANSLATION

Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu washed and cleansed the Gundicā temple with His devotees and associates. In this way He made it as cool and bright as His own heart, and thus He made the temple a befitting place for Lord Śrī Krishna to sit.
