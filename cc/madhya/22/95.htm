Madhya-līlāChapter 22: The Process of Devotional Service

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 22.95

bhakta-vatsala, kritajña, samartha, vadānya

hena krishna chādi' pandita nāhi bhaje anya

SYNONYMS

bhakta-vatsala — very kind to the devotees; krita-jña — grateful; samartha — full of all abilities; vadānya — magnanimous; hena — such; krishna — Lord Krishna; chādi' — giving up; pandita — a learned man; nāhi — does not; bhaje — worship; anya — anyone else.

TRANSLATION

"Lord Krishna is very kind to His devotees. He is always very grateful and magnanimous, and He possesses all abilities. A learned man does not give up Krishna to worship anyone else.

PURPORT

An intelligent person gives up the company of those who are attached to women and bereft of Krishna consciousness. One should be free from all kinds of material attachment and should take full shelter under the lotus feet of Krishna. Krishna is very kind to His devotees. He is always grateful, and He never forgets the service of a devotee. He is also completely opulent and all-powerful. Why, then, should one take shelter of a demigod and leave Lord Krishna's shelter? If one worships a demigod and leaves Krishna, he must be considered the lowest fool.
