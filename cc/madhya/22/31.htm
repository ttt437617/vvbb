Madhya-līlāChapter 22: The Process of Devotional Service

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 22.31

krishna — sūrya-sama; māyā haya andhakāra

yāhāń krishna, tāhāń nāhi māyāra adhikāra

SYNONYMS

krishna — Krishna, the Supreme Personality of Godhead; sūrya-sama — like the sun planet; māyā — the illusory energy; haya — is; andhakāra — darkness; yāhāń krishna — wherever there is Krishna; tāhāń — there; nāhi — not; māyāra — of māyā, or the darkness of illusion; adhikāra — the jurisdiction.

TRANSLATION

"Krishna is compared to sunshine, and māyā is compared to darkness. Wherever there is sunshine, there cannot be darkness. As soon as one takes to Krishna consciousness, the darkness of illusion (the influence of the external energy) will immediately vanish.

PURPORT

In Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam (2.9.34) it is stated:

rite 'rtham yat pratīyeta na pratīyeta cātmani

tad vidyād ātmano māyām yathābhāso yathā tamah

"What appears to be truth without Me is certainly My illusory energy, for nothing can exist without Me. It is like a reflection of a real light in the shadows, for in the light there are neither shadows nor reflections."

Wherever there is light, there cannot be darkness. When a living entity becomes Krishna conscious, he is immediately relieved of all material lusty desires. Lusty desires and greed are associated with rajas and tamas, passion and darkness. When one becomes Krishna conscious, the modes of passion and darkness immediately vanish, and then the mode of goodness (sattva-guna) remains. When one is situated in the mode of goodness, he can make spiritual advancement and understand things clearly. This position is not possible for everyone. When a person is Krishna conscious, he continuously hears about Krishna, thinks about Him, worships Him and serves Him as a devotee. If he remains in Krishna consciousness in this way, the darkness of māyā certainly will not be able to touch him.
