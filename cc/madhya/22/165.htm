Madhya-līlāChapter 22: The Process of Devotional Service

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 22.165

prīty-ańkure 'rati', 'bhāva' — haya dui nāma

yāhā haite vaśa hana śrī-bhagavān

SYNONYMS

prīti-ańkure — in the seed of affection; rati — attachment; bhāva — emotion; haya — there are; dui nāma — two names; yāhā haite — from which; vaśa — controlled; hana — is; śrī-bhagavān — the Supreme Personality of Godhead.

TRANSLATION

"In the seed of affection, there is attachment which goes by two names, rati and bhāva. The Supreme Personality of Godhead comes under the control of such attachment.

PURPORT

Śrīla Bhaktisiddhānta Sarasvatī Thākura comments on this verse. Externally a devotee performs all the items of devotional service in nine different ways, beginning with śravana and kīrtana, and within his mind he always thinks of his eternal relationship with Krishna and follows in the footsteps of the devotees of Vrindāvana. If one engages himself in the service of Rādhā and Krishna in this way, he can transcend the regulative principles enjoined in the śāstras and, through his spiritual master, fully engage in rendering spontaneous love to Krishna. In this way, he attains affection at the lotus feet of Krishna. Krishna actually comes under the control of such spontaneous feelings, and ultimately one can attain association with the Lord.
