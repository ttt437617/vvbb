Madhya-līlāChapter 5: The Activities of Sākshi-gopāla

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 5.102

nūpurera dhvani śuni' ānandita mana

uttamānna pāka kari' karāya bhojana

SYNONYMS

nūpurera — of the ankle bells; dhvani — the sounds; śuni' — hearing; ānandita — very pleased; mana — the mind; uttama-anna — first-class rice; pāka — cooking; kari' — doing; karāya — causes; bhojana — eating.

TRANSLATION

While Gopāla followed the young brāhmana, the tinkling sound of His ankle bells could be heard. The brāhmana became very pleased, and he cooked first-class rice for Gopāla to eat.
