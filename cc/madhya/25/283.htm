Madhya-līlāChapter 25: How All the Residents of Vārānasī Became Vaishnavas

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 25.283

tad idam ati-rahasyam gaura-līlāmritam yat

khala-samudaya-kolair nādritam tair alabhyam

kshatir iyam iha kā me svāditam yat samantāt

sahridaya-sumanobhir modam eshām tanoti

SYNONYMS

tat — that (Śrī Caitanya-caritāmrita); idam — this; ati-rahasyam — full of spiritual mysteries; gaura-līlā-amritam — the nectar of the pastimes of Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu; yat — which; khala-samudaya — envious rascals; kolaih — by pigs; na — never; ādritam — praised; taih — by them; alabhyam — not obtainable; kshatih iyam iha kā — what is the loss in this connection; me — of me; svāditam — tasted; yat — which; samantāt — completely; sahridaya-sumanobhih — by those who are friendly and whose minds are very clean; modam — enjoyment; eshām — of them; tanoti — expands.

TRANSLATION

The Caitanya-caritāmrita pastimes of Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu constitute a very secret literature. It is the life and soul of all devotees. Those who are not fit to relish this literature, who are envious like hogs and pigs, will certainly not adore it. However, this will not harm my attempt. These pastimes of Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu will certainly please all saintly people who have clear hearts. They will certainly enjoy it. We wish that this will enhance their enjoyment more and more.

Thus end the Bhaktivedanta purports to Śrī Caitanya-caritāmrita, Madhya-līlā, Twenty-fifth Chapter, describing how the inhabitants of Vārānasī were converted to Vaishnavism.

Note:

The author of Śrī Caitanya-caritāmrita, Krishnadāsa Kavirāja Gosvāmī, has condemned all his enemies by comparing them to envious hogs and pigs. The Krishna consciousness movement, which is spreading throughout the world, is being appreciated by sincere people, although they have never previously heard of Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu and Krishna's pastimes. Now even the higher, priestly circles are appreciating this movement. They have concluded that this movement is very nice and that they have something to learn from it. Nonetheless, in India there are some people who say that they belong to this cult but who are actually very envious of the ācārya. They have tried to suppress our activities in many ways, but as far as we are concerned, we follow in the footsteps of Krishnadāsa Kavirāja Gosvāmī and take them as envious pigs and hogs. We simply wish to present the pastimes of Lord Krishna and Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu to the best of our ability so that those who are really honest can cleanse their hearts. We hope that they enjoy this literature and bestow their blessings upon us. It appears that even such a great personality as Krishnadāsa Kavirāja Gosvāmī met with some envious obstacles; what, then, to speak of us, who are only insignificant creatures in this universe. We are simply trying to execute the orders of our spiritual master to the best of our ability.

END OF THE MADHYA-LĪLĀ
