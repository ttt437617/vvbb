Madhya-līlāChapter 25: How All the Residents of Vārānasī Became Vaishnavas

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 25.100

ataeva brahma-sūtrera bhāshya — śrī-bhāgavata

bhāgavata-śloka, upanishat kahe 'eka' mata

SYNONYMS

ataeva — therefore; brahma-sūtrera bhāshya — the commentary on the Brahma-sūtra aphorisms; śrī-bhāgavata — Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam; bhāgavata-śloka — the verses in Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam; upanishat — the explanations in the Upanishads; kahe — state; eka mata — the same version.

TRANSLATION

"Therefore it is to be concluded that the Brahma-sūtra is explained vividly in Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam. Also, what is explained in the verses of Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam has the same purport as what is explained in the Upanishads.
