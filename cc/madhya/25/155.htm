Madhya-līlāChapter 25: How All the Residents of Vārānasī Became Vaishnavas

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 25.155

brahma-bhūtah prasannātmā

na śocati na kāńkshati

samah sarveshu bhūteshu

mad-bhaktim labhate parām

SYNONYMS

brahma-bhūtah — freed from material conceptions of life but attached to an impersonal situation; prasanna-ātmā — fully joyful; na śocati — he does not lament; na kāńkshati — he does not hanker; samah — equally disposed; sarveshu — all; bhūteshu — to the living entities; mat-bhaktim — My devotional service; labhate — achieves; parām — transcendental.

TRANSLATION

"'One who is thus transcendentally situated at once realizes the Supreme Brahman and becomes fully joyful. He never laments or desires to have anything. He is equally disposed toward every living entity. In that state he attains pure devotional service unto Me.'

PURPORT

This is a verse from the Bhagavad-gītā (18.54).
