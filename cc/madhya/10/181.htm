Madhya-līlāChapter 10: The Lord's Return to Jagannātha Purī

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 10.181

prema vinā kabhu nahe tāńra sākshātkāra

ińhāra kripāte haya daraśana ińhāra

SYNONYMS

prema vinā — without ecstatic love; kabhu nahe — there is never; tāńra — His; sākshātkāra — direct meeting; ińhāra kripāte — by the mercy of Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu; haya — becomes possible; daraśana — visit; ińhāra — of Brahmānanda Bhāratī.

TRANSLATION

"Without having ecstatic love for Krishna, one cannot see Him directly. Therefore through the mercy of Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu, Brahmānanda Bhāratī has acquired direct vision of the Lord."

PURPORT

Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu said, "You are Brahmānanda Bhāratī, an advanced devotee who ecstatically loves the Supreme Lord. Therefore you see Krishna everywhere, and there is no doubt about it." Sārvabhauma Bhattācārya was a mediator between Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu and Brahmānanda Bhāratī, and his judgment was that an advanced devotee like Brahmānanda Bhāratī was seeing Krishna by Krishna's mercy. Krishna directly presents Himself before the vision of an advanced devotee. Since Brahmānanda Bhāratī was an advanced devotee, he saw Krishna in the person of Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu. In the words of the Brahma-samhitā (5.38):

premāñjana-cchurita-bhakti-vilocanena

santah sadaiva hridayeshu vilokayanti

yam śyāmasundaram acintya-guna-svarūpam

govindam ādi-purusham tam aham bhajāmi

"I worship the primeval Lord, Govinda, who is always seen by the devotee whose eyes are anointed with the pulp of love. He is seen in His eternal form of Śyāmasundara, situated within the heart of the devotee."
