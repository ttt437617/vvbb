Madhya-līlāChapter 1: The Later Pastimes of Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 1.203

na mrishā paramārtham eva me

śrinu vijñāpanam ekam agratah

yadi me na dayishyase tadā

dayanīyas tava nātha durlabhah

SYNONYMS

na — not; mrishā — untruth; parama-artham — full of meaning; eva — certainly; me — my; śrinu — kindly hear; vijñāpanam — submission; ekam — one; agratah — first; yadi — if; me — unto me; na dayishyase — You will not show mercy; tadā — then; dayanīyah — candidate for mercy; tava — Your; nātha — O Lord; durlabhah — difficult to find.

TRANSLATION

"'Let us submit one piece of information before You, dear Lord. It is not at all false but is full of meaning. It is this: If You are not merciful upon us, then it will be very, very difficult to find more suitable candidates for Your mercy.'

PURPORT

This verse is from the Stotra-ratna (47), by Śrī Yāmunācārya.
