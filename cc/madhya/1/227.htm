Madhya-līlāChapter 1: The Later Pastimes of Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 1.227

prāte cali' āilā prabhu 'kānāira nātaśālā'

dekhila sakala tāhāń krishna-caritra-līlā

SYNONYMS

prāte — in the morning; cali' — departing; āilā — came; prabhu — the Lord; kānāira nātaśālā — to the place of the name Kānāi Nātaśālā; dekhila — saw; sakala — all; tāhāń — there; krishna-caritra-līlā — the pastimes of Krishna.

TRANSLATION

In the morning, the Lord left and went to a place known as Kānāi Nātaśālā. While there, He saw many pastimes of Lord Krishna.

PURPORT

In those days in Bengal there were many places known as Kānāi Nātaśālā, where pictures of the pastimes of Lord Krishna were kept. People used to go there to see them. This is called krishna-caritra-līlā. In Bengal there are still many places called hari-sabhā, which indicates a place where local people gather to chant the Hare Krishna mahā-mantra and discuss the pastimes of Lord Krishna. The word kānāi means "Lord Krishna's," and nātaśālā indicates a place where pastimes are demonstrated. So those places which at the present moment are called hari-sabhā may previously have been known as Kānāi Nātaśālā.
