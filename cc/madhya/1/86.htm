Madhya-līlāChapter 1: The Later Pastimes of Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 1.86

tri-bhańga-sundara vraje vrajendra-nandana

kāhāń pāba, ei vāñchā bāde anukshana

SYNONYMS

tri-bhańga — bent in three places; sundara — beautiful; vraje — in Vrindāvana; vrajendra-nandana — the son of Nanda Mahārāja; kāhāń — where; pāba — shall I get; ei — this; vāñchā — desire; bāde — increases; anukshana — incessantly.

TRANSLATION

Absorbed in the ecstasy of the gopīs, Lord Caitanya Mahāprabhu wished to see Lord Jagannātha in His original form as Krishna, the son of Nanda Mahārāja, standing in Vrindāvana and appearing very beautiful, His body curved in three places. His desire to see that form was always increasing.
