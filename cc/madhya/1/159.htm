Madhya-līlāChapter 1: The Later Pastimes of Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 1.159

śītala samīra vahe nānā gandha lañā

'kānāira nātaśālā' paryanta la-ila bāndhiñā

SYNONYMS

śītala — very cool; samīra — breezes; vahe — blowing; nānā — various; gandha — fragrances; lañā — carrying; kānāira nāta-śālā — the place named Kānāi Nātaśālā; paryanta — as far as; la-ila — carried; bāndhiñā — constructing.

TRANSLATION

The entire road was surcharged with many cool breezes, which carried the fragrances from various flowers. He carried the construction of this road as far as Kānāi Nātaśālā.

PURPORT

Kānāi Nātaśālā is about two hundred miles from Calcutta on the Loop line of the Eastern Railway. The railway station is named Tālajhādi, and after one gets off at that station, he has to go about two miles to find Kānāi Nātaśālā.
