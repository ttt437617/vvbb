Madhya-līlāChapter 1: The Later Pastimes of Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 1.230

ekākī yāiba, kimvā sańge eka jana

tabe se śobhaye vrindāvanere gamana

SYNONYMS

ekākī — alone; yāiba — I shall go; kimvā — or; sańge — with; eka — one; jana — person; tabe — then only; se — that; śobhaye — becomes beautiful; vrindāvanere — to Vrindāvana; gamana — going.

TRANSLATION

The Lord concluded that He would go alone to Vrindāvana or, at most, would take only one person as His companion. In that way, going to Vrindāvana would be very pleasant.
