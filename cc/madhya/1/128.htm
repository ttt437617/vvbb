Madhya-līlāChapter 1: The Later Pastimes of Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 1.128

rāja-ājñā lañā teńho āilā kata dine

rātri-dine krishna-kathā rāmānanda-sane

SYNONYMS

rāja-ājñā — the permission of the King, Pratāparudra; lañā — getting; teńho — Rāmānanda Rāya; āilā — returned; kata dine — in some days; rātri-dine — day and night; krishna-kathā — talks of Lord Krishna and His pastimes; rāmānanda-sane — in the company of Rāmānanda Rāya.

TRANSLATION

Upon the order of Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu, Śrī Rāmānanda Rāya took leave of the King and returned to Jagannātha Purī. After he arrived, Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu very much enjoyed talking with him both day and night about Lord Krishna and His pastimes.
