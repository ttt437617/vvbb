Madhya-līlāChapter 1: The Later Pastimes of Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 1.106

tabe ta' pāshandi-gane karila dalana

ahovala-nrisimhādi kaila daraśana

SYNONYMS

tabe ta' — thereafter; pāshandi-gane — unto the atheists; karila — did; dalana — subduing; ahovala-nrisimha-ādi — Nrisimhadeva, named Ahovala or at Ahovala; kaila — did; daraśana — visit.

TRANSLATION

After visiting the temples of Tirumala and Tirupati, Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu had to subdue some atheists. He then visited the temple of Ahovala-nrisimha.

PURPORT

The Ahovala temple is situated in Dākshinātya, in the district of Karnula, within the subdivision of Sārbela. Throughout the whole district this very famous temple is much appreciated by the people. There are eight other temples also, and all of them together are called the Nava-nrisimha temples. There is much wonderful architecture and artistic engraving work in these temples. However, as stated in the local gazette, the Karnula Manual, the work is not complete.
