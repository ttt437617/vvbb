Madhya-līlāChapter 1: The Later Pastimes of Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 1.97

kshīra-curi-kathā, sākshi-gopāla-vivarana

nityānanda kaila prabhura danda-bhañjana

SYNONYMS

kshīra-curi-kathā — the narration of the stealing of the condensed milk; sākshi-gopāla-vivarana — the description of witness Gopāla; nityānanda — Nityānanda Prabhu; kaila — did; prabhura — of the Lord; danda-bhañjana — breaking the sannyāsa rod.

TRANSLATION

From Nityānanda Prabhu, Lord Caitanya Mahāprabhu heard the story of Kshīra-curī Gopīnātha and of the witness Gopāla. Then Nityānanda Prabhu broke the sannyāsa rod belonging to Lord Caitanya Mahāprabhu.

PURPORT

This Kshīra-curī Gopīnātha is situated in Remunā, about four miles away from the Bāleśvara (Balasore) station on the Northeastern Railway, formerly known as the Bengal Māyāpura Railway. This station is situated a few miles away from the famous Kargapura junction station. Some time ago the charge of the temple was given to Śyāmasundara Adhikārī from Gopīvallabhapura, which lies on the border of the district of Medinīpura. Śyāmasundara Adhikārī was a descendant of Rasikānanda Murāri, the chief disciple of Śyāmānanda Gosvāmī.

A few miles before the Jagannātha Purī station is a small station called Sākshi-gopāla. Near this station is a village named Satyavādī, where the temple of Sākshi-gopāla is situated.
