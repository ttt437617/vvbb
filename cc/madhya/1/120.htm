Madhya-līlāChapter 1: The Later Pastimes of Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 1.120

brahma-samhitā, karnāmrita, dui puńthi pāñā

dui pustaka lañā āilā uttama jāniñā

SYNONYMS

brahma-samhitā — the book named Brahma-samhitā; karnāmrita — the book named Krishna-karnāmrita; dui — two; puńthi — scriptures; pāñā — obtaining; dui — two; pustaka — books; lañā — carrying; āilā — came back; uttama — very good; jāniñā — knowing.

TRANSLATION

Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu also found two other books — namely, the Brahma-samhitā and Krishna-karnāmrita. Knowing these books to be excellent, He took them to present to His devotees.

PURPORT

In the olden days there were no presses, and all the important scriptures were handwritten and kept in large temples. Caitanya Mahāprabhu found the Brahma-samhitā and Krishna-karnāmrita in handwritten texts, and knowing them to be very authoritative, He took them with Him to present to His devotees. Of course, He obtained the permission of the temple commander. Now both the Brahma-samhitā and Krishna-karnāmrita are available in print with commentaries by Śrīla Bhaktisiddhānta Sarasvatī Thākura.
