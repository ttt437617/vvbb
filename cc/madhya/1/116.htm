Madhya-līlāChapter 1: The Later Pastimes of Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 1.116

tabe prabhu kaila sapta-tāla vimocana

setu-bandhe snāna, rāmeśvara daraśana

SYNONYMS

tabe — thereafter; prabhu — the Lord; kaila — did; sapta-tāla-vimocana — deliverance of the Sapta-tāla trees; setu-bandhe — at Cape Comorin; snāna — bathing; rāmeśvara — temple of Rāmeśvara; daraśana — visit.

TRANSLATION

After that, Lord Caitanya Mahāprabhu delivered the celebrated Sapta-tāla trees, took His bath at Setubandha Rāmeśvara and visited the temple of Lord Śiva known as Rāmeśvara.

PURPORT

It is said that the Sapta-tāla trees were very old, massive palm trees. There was once a fight between Vāli and his brother Sugrīva, and Lord Rāmacandra took the side of Sugrīva and killed Vāli, keeping Himself behind one of those celebrated trees. When Lord Caitanya Mahāprabhu toured south India, He embraced the trees, which were delivered and directly promoted to Vaikuntha.
