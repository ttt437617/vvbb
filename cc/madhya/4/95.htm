Madhya-līlāChapter 4: Śrī Mādhavendra Puri's Devotional Service

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 4.95

vraja-vāsī lokera krishne sahaja pirīti

gopālera sahaja-prīti vraja-vāsi-prati

SYNONYMS

vraja-vāsī — the inhabitants of Vrindāvana (Vrajabhūmi); lokera — of the people; krishne — unto Lord Krishna; sahaja — natural; pirīti — love; gopālera — of Lord Gopāla; sahaja — natural; prīti — love; vraja-vāsi-prati — toward the inhabitants of Vrajabhūmi.

TRANSLATION

The ideal place to execute Krishna consciousness is Vrajabhūmi, or Vrindāvana, where the people are naturally inclined to love Krishna and Krishna is naturally inclined to love them.

PURPORT

In the Bhagavad-gītā (4.11) it is said, ye yathā mām prapadyante tāms tathaiva bhajāmy aham. There is a responsive cooperation between the Supreme Lord Krishna and His devotees. The more a devotee sincerely loves Krishna, the more Krishna reciprocates, so much so that a highly advanced devotee can talk with Krishna face to face. Krishna confirms this in the Bhagavad-gītā (10.10):

teshām satata-yuktānām bhajatām prīti-pūrvakam

dadāmi buddhi-yogam tam yena mām upayānti te

"To those who are constantly devoted to serving Me with love, I give the understanding by which they can come to Me." The actual mission of human life is to understand Krishna and return home, back to Godhead. Therefore one who is sincerely engaged in the service of the Lord with love and faith can talk with Krishna and receive instructions by which he can speedily return home, back to Godhead. Today many scholars defend the science of religion, and they have some conception of the Supreme Personality of Godhead, but religion without practical experience of the Supreme Personality of Godhead is no religion at all. Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam describes this as a form of cheating. Religion means abiding by the orders of Krishna, the Supreme Personality of Godhead. If one is not qualified to talk with Him and take lessons from Him, how can one understand the principles of religion? Thus talks of religion or religious experience without Krishna consciousness are a useless waste of time.
