Madhya-līlāChapter 4: Śrī Mādhavendra Puri's Devotional Service

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 4.59

tulasī ādi, pushpa, vastra āila aneka

āpane mādhava-purī kaila abhisheka

SYNONYMS

tulasī — tulasī leaves; ādi — and others; pushpa — flowers; vastra — garments; āila — arrived; aneka — in great quantity; āpane — personally; mādhava-purī — Śrī Mādhavendra Purī; kaila — executed; abhisheka — the bathing of the Deity at the beginning of the installation ceremony.

TRANSLATION

The villagers brought a large quantity of tulasī leaves, flowers, and various kinds of garments. Then Śrī Mādhavendra Purī personally began the abhisheka [bathing ceremony].

PURPORT

In the Hari-bhakti-vilāsa (6.30) it is stated that the Deity should be bathed in water mixed with yogurt and milk, accompanied by the sounds of conchshells, bells and other instruments and the chanting of the mantra om bhagavate vāsudevāya namah, as well as the chanting of the Brahma-samhitā verses beginning cintāmani-prakara-sadmasu kalpa-vriksha-lakshāvriteshu surabhīr abhipālayantam [Bs. 5.29].
