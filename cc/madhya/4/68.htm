Madhya-līlāChapter 4: Śrī Mādhavendra Puri's Devotional Service

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 4.68

kumbhakāra ghare chila ye mrid-bhājana

saba ānāila prāte, cadila randhana

SYNONYMS

kumbhakāra — of the potters of the village; ghare — in the houses; chila — there was; ye — whatever; mrid-bhājana — clay pots; saba — all; ānāila — brought; prāte — in the morning; cadila — started; randhana — cooking.

TRANSLATION

When the villagers brought their stock of rice, dhal and flour, the potters of the village brought all kinds of cooking pots, and in the morning the cooking began.
