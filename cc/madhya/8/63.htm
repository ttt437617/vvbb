Madhya-līlāChapter 8: Talks Between Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu and Rāmānanda Rāya

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 8.63

sarva-dharmān parityajya

mām ekam śaranam vraja

aham tvām sarva-pāpebhyo

mokshayishyāmi mā śucah

SYNONYMS

sarva-dharmān — all kinds of occupational duties; parityajya — giving up; mām ekam — unto Me only; śaranam — as shelter; vraja — go; aham — I; tvām — unto you; sarva-pāpebhyah — from all the reactions of sinful life; mokshayishyāmi — will give liberation; mā — don't; śucah — worry.

TRANSLATION

"As stated in scripture [Bg. 18.66], 'After giving up all kinds of religious and occupational duties, if you come to Me, the Supreme Personality of Godhead, and take shelter, I will give you protection from all of life's sinful reactions. Do not worry.'"

PURPORT

In this connection, Śrīla Raghunātha dāsa Gosvāmī instructs in his book Manah-śikshā (2):

na dharmam nādharmam śruti-gana-niruktam kila kuru

vraje rādhā-krishna-pracura-paricaryām iha tanu

He has thus enjoined that we should not perform religious or irreligious activities as prescribed in the Vedas. The best course is to engage always in the service of Lord Krishna and Rādhārānī. That is the perfection of everything in this life. Similarly, in Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam (4.29.46) it is said by Nārada Muni:

yadā yasyānugrihnāti bhagavān ātma-bhāvitah

sa jahāti matim loke vede ca parinishthitām

"When one actually takes to the loving service of the Supreme Personality of Godhead, he gives up all duties in the material world, as well as all duties prescribed by the Vedic literatures. In this way one is fixed in the service of the Lord."
