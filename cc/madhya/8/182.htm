Madhya-līlāChapter 8: Talks Between Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu and Rāmānanda Rāya

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 8.182

kā krishnasya pranaya-jani-bhūh śrīmatī rādhikaikā

kāsya preyasy anupama-gunā rādhikaikā na cānyā

jaihmyam keśe driśi taralatā nishthuratvam kuce 'syā

vāñchā-pūrtyai prabhavati hare rādhikaikā na cānyā

SYNONYMS

kā — who; krishnasya — of Lord Krishna; pranaya-jani-bhūh — the birthplace of love of Krishna; śrīmatī — all-beautiful; rādhikā — Śrīmatī Rādhārānī; ekā — alone; kā — who; asya — His; preyasī — most dear friend; anupama-gunā — having unparalleled qualities; rādhikā — Śrīmatī Rādhārānī; ekā — alone; na — not; ca — also; anyā — anyone else; jaihmyam — crookedness; keśe — in the hair; driśi — in the eyes; taralatā — unsteadiness; nishthuratvam — firmness; kuce — in the breasts; asyāh — Her; vāñchā — of the desires; pūrtyai — to fulfill; prabhavati — manifests; hareh — of Lord Krishna; rādhikā — Śrīmatī Rādhārānī; ekā — alone; na — not; ca anyā — anyone else.

TRANSLATION

"'If one asks about the origin of love of Krishna, the answer is that the origin is in Śrīmatī Rādhārānī alone. Who is the most dear friend of Krishna? The answer again is Śrīmatī Rādhārānī alone. No one else. Śrīmatī Rādhārānī's hair is very curly, Her two eyes are always moving to and fro, and Her breasts are firm. Since all transcendental qualities are manifested in Śrīmatī Rādhārānī, She alone is able to fulfill all the desires of Krishna. No one else.'

PURPORT

This is a quotation from Śrī Govinda-līlāmrita (11.122) by Krishnadāsa Kavirāja Gosvāmī. It is a verse in the form of questions and answers describing the glories of Śrīmatī Rādhārānī.
