Madhya-līlāChapter 8: Talks Between Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu and Rāmānanda Rāya

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 8.18

sūrya-śata-sama kānti, aruna vasana

subalita prakānda deha, kamala-locana

SYNONYMS

sūrya-śata — hundreds of suns; sama — like; kānti — luster; aruna — saffron; vasana — garments; subalita — very strongly built; prakānda — big; deha — body; kamala-locana — eyes like lotus petals.

TRANSLATION

Śrīla Rāmānanda Rāya then saw Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu to be as brilliant as a hundred suns. The Lord was covered by a saffron garment. He was large in body and very strongly built, and His eyes were like lotus petals.
