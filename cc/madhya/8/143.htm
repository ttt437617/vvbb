Madhya-līlāChapter 8: Talks Between Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu and Rāmānanda Rāya

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 8.143

śrińgāra-rasarāja-maya-mūrti-dhara

ataeva ātma-paryanta-sarva-citta-hara

SYNONYMS

śrińgāra-rasa-rāja-maya — consisting of the mellow of conjugal love, which is the king of mellows; mūrti-dhara — Krishna, the personified reservoir of all pleasure; ataeva — therefore; ātma-paryanta — even up to His own self; sarva — all; citta — of hearts; hara — the attractor.

TRANSLATION

"Krishna is all-attractive for devotees in all mellows because He is the personification of the conjugal mellow. Krishna is attractive not only to all the devotees, but to Himself as well.
