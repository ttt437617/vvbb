Madhya-līlāChapter 8: Talks Between Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu and Rāmānanda Rāya

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 8.177

madhya-vayasa, sakhī-skandhe kara-nyāsa

krishnalīlā-manovritti-sakhī āśa-pāśa

SYNONYMS

madhya-vayasa — adolescence; sakhī — of a friend; skandhe — on the shoulder; kara — hand; nyāsa — keeping; krishna — of Lord Krishna; līlā — the pastimes; manah — of the mind; vritti — activities; sakhī — gopīs; āśa-pāśa — here and there.

TRANSLATION

"Śrīmatī Rādhārānī's gopī friends are Her mental activities, which are concentrated on the pastimes of Śrī Krishna. She keeps Her hand on the shoulder of a friend, who represents youth.

PURPORT

Rādhārānī's eight companions (ashta-sakhī) are different varieties of pleasure connected with the pastimes of Krishna. Following those pastimes of Śrī Krishna are other activities, which are represented by the assistants of the gopīs.
