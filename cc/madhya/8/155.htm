Madhya-līlāChapter 8: Talks Between Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu and Rāmānanda Rāya

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 8.155

ānandāmśe 'hlādinī', sad-amśe 'sandhinī'

cid-amśe 'samvit', yāre jñāna kari' māni

SYNONYMS

ānanda-amśe — in bliss; hlādinī — the pleasure-giving potency; sat-amśe — in eternity; sandhinī — the creative potency; cit-amśe — in knowledge; samvit — the knowledge potency; yāre — which; jñāna — knowledge; kari' — taking as; māni — I accept.

TRANSLATION

"Hlādinī is His aspect of bliss; sandhinī, of eternal existence; and samvit, of cognizance, which is also accepted as knowledge.
