Madhya-līlāChapter 8: Talks Between Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu and Rāmānanda Rāya

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 8.219

yat te sujāta-caranāmburuham staneshu

bhītāh śanaih priya dadhīmahi karkaśeshu

tenātavīm atasi tad vyathate na kim svit

kūrpādibhir bhramati dhīr bhavad-āyushām nah

SYNONYMS

yat — because; te — Your; sujāta — delicate; carana-ambu-ruham — lotus feet; staneshu — on the breasts; bhītāh — being afraid of; śanaih — very carefully; priya — O dear one; dadhīmahi — we place; karkaśeshu — very rough and hard; tena — by such lotus feet; atavīm — the forest; atasi — You wander; tat vyathate — that are pained; na — not; kim svit — whether; kūrpa-ādibhih — by the small particles of stone; bhramati — bewilders; dhīh — intelligence; bhavat-āyushām — of persons who consider You as the duration of life; nah — of us.

TRANSLATION

"[All the gopīs said:] 'Dear Krishna, we carefully hold Your delicate lotus feet upon our hard breasts. When You walk in the forest, Your soft lotus feet are pricked by small bits of stone. We fear that this is paining You. Since You are our life and soul, our minds are very much disturbed when Your lotus feet are pained.'

PURPORT

This is a quotation from Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam (10.31.19).
