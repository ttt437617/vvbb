Madhya-līlāChapter 8: Talks Between Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu and Rāmānanda Rāya

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 8.239

yaiche śuniluń, taiche dekhiluń tomāra mahimā

rādhā-krishna-premarasa-jñānera tumi sīmā

SYNONYMS

yaiche — as much; śuniluń — as I have heard; taiche — that much; dekhiluń — I have seen; tomāra mahimā — your glories; rādhā-krishna-prema-rasa-jñānera — of transcendental knowledge about the loving affairs of Rādhā and Krishna; tumi — you; sīmā — the ultimate goal.

TRANSLATION

"Now that I have actually seen your glories, what I heard about you is confirmed. As far as the pastimes of Rādhā and Krishna in a loving mood are concerned, you are the limit of knowledge."

PURPORT

Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu found Rāmānanda Rāya to be the best authority in transcendental knowledge of the loving affairs between Rādhā and Krishna. In this verse the Lord actually states that Rāmānanda Rāya was the limit of this knowledge.
