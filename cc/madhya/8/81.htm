Madhya-līlāChapter 8: Talks Between Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu and Rāmānanda Rāya

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 8.81

tāsām āvirabhūc chaurih

smayamāna-mukhāmbujah

pītāmbara-dharah sragvī

sākshān manmatha-manmathah

SYNONYMS

tāsām — among them; āvirabhūt — appeared; śaurih — Lord Krishna; smayamāna — smiling; mukha-ambujah — with a face like a lotus flower; pīta-ambara-dharah — wearing yellow garments; sragvī — garlanded with flowers; sākshāt — directly; manmatha — of Cupid; manmathah — the bewilderer.

TRANSLATION

"'Suddenly, due to their feelings of separation, Lord Krishna appeared among the gopīs dressed in yellow garments and wearing a flower garland. His lotus face was smiling, and He was directly attracting the mind of Cupid.'

PURPORT

This verse is from Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam (10.32.2). When the rāsa dance was going on, Krishna suddenly disappeared, and the gopīs became so overwhelmed, due to His separation and their intense love for Him, that Krishna was obliged to appear again.
