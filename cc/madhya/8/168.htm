Madhya-līlāChapter 8: Talks Between Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu and Rāmānanda Rāya

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 8.168

lāvanyāmrita-dhārāya tad-upari snāna

nija-lajjā-śyāma-pattasāti-paridhāna

SYNONYMS

lāvanya-amrita-dhārāya — in the shower of the nectar of bodily luster; tat-upari — over and above that; snāna — the bath; nija — own; lajjā — shyness; śyāma — blackish; patta — silk; sāti — garments; paridhāna — wearing.

TRANSLATION

"After Her midday bath, Rādhārānī takes another bath in the nectar of bodily luster, and She puts on the garment of shyness, which is Her black silk sari.

PURPORT

Over and above the other baths, the bath taken in the afternoon is taken in the nectar of full beauty. This nectar represents the personal qualities of beauty and luster. Thus there are three baths in different kinds of water. Rādhārānī then puts on two garments — a lower and an upper garment. The upper garment is pinkish and is Her affection and attraction for Krishna, and the lower garment, a blackish silk sari, is Her shyness.
