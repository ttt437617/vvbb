Madhya-līlāChapter 8: Talks Between Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu and Rāmānanda Rāya

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 8.86

gunādhikye svādādhikya bāde prati-rase

śānta-dāsya-sakhya-vātsalyera guna madhurete vaise

SYNONYMS

guna-ādhikye — by the increase of transcendental qualities; svāda-ādhikya — increase of taste; bāde — increases; prati-rase — in each mellow; śānta — of neutrality; dāsya — of servitude; sakhya — of fraternity; vātsalyera — and of parental affection; guna — the qualities; madhurete — in the conjugal mellow; vaise — appear.

TRANSLATION

"As the qualities increase, so the taste also increases in each and every mellow. Therefore the qualities found in śānta-rasa, dāsya-rasa, sakhya-rasa and vātsalya-rasa are all manifested in conjugal love [mādhurya-rasa].
