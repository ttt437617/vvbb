Madhya-līlāChapter 8: Talks Between Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu and Rāmānanda Rāya

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 8.140

tāsām āvirabhūc chaurih

smayamāna-mukhāmbujah

pītāmbara-dharah sragvī

sākshān manmatha-manmathah

SYNONYMS

tāsām — among them; āvirabhūt — appeared; śaurih — Lord Krishna; smayamāna — smiling; mukha-ambujah — lotus face; pīta-ambara-dharah — dressed with yellow garments; sragvī — decorated with a flower garland; sākshāt — directly; manmatha — of Cupid; manmathah — Cupid.

TRANSLATION

"'When Krishna left the rāsa-līlā dance, the gopīs became very morose, and when they were grieving, Krishna reappeared dressed in yellow garments. Wearing a flower garland and smiling, He was attractive even to Cupid. In this way Krishna appeared among the gopīs.'

PURPORT

This verse is from Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam (10.32.2).
