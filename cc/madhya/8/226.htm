Madhya-līlāChapter 8: Talks Between Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu and Rāmānanda Rāya

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 8.226

'ańghri-padma-sudhā'ya kahe 'krishna-sańgānanda'

vidhi-mārge nā pāiye vraje krishna-candra

SYNONYMS

ańghri-padma-sudhāya — by the nectar derived from the lotus feet of Krishna; kahe — it says; krishna-sańga-ānanda — transcendental bliss by the association of Krishna; vidhi-mārge — on the path of regulative principles; nā pāiye — one does not get; vraje — in Goloka Vrindāvana; krishna-candra — Lord Krishna.

TRANSLATION

"The word 'ańghri-padma-sudhā' means 'associating intimately with Krishna.' One can attain such perfection only by spontaneous love of God. One cannot obtain Krishna in Goloka Vrindāvana simply by serving the Lord according to regulative principles.
