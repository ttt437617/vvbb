Madhya-līlāChapter 8: Talks Between Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu and Rāmānanda Rāya

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 8.106

kamsārir api samsāra-

vāsanā-baddha-śrińkhalām

rādhām ādhāya hridaye

tatyāja vraja-sundarīh

SYNONYMS

kamsa-arih — the enemy of Kamsa; api — moreover; samsāra-vāsanā — desirous of the essence of enjoyment (rāsa-līlā); baddha-śrińkhalām — being perfectly attracted to such activities; rādhām — Śrīmatī Rādhārānī; ādhāya — taking; hridaye — within the heart; tatyāja — left aside; vraja-sundarīh — the other beautiful gopīs.

TRANSLATION

"'Lord Krishna, the enemy of Kamsa, took Śrīmatī Rādhārānī within His heart, for He desired to dance with Her. Thus He left the arena of the rāsa dance and the company of all the other beautiful damsels of Vraja.
