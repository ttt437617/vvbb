Madhya-līlāChapter 8: Talks Between Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu and Rāmānanda Rāya

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 8.170

saundarya — kuńkuma, sakhī-pranaya — candana

smita-kānti — karpūra, tine — ańge vilepana

SYNONYMS

saundarya — Her personal beauty; kuńkuma — a red powder known as kuńkuma; sakhī-pranaya — Her love for Her associates; candana — the sandalwood pulp; smita-kānti — the sweetness of Her smile; karpūra — camphor; tine — by these three things; ańge — on the body; vilepana — smearing.

TRANSLATION

"Śrīmatī Rādhārānī's personal beauty is the reddish powder known as kuńkuma, Her affection for Her associates is sandalwood pulp, and the sweetness of Her smile is camphor. All these, combined together, are smeared over Her body.
