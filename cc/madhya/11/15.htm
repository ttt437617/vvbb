Madhya-līlāChapter 11: The Bedā-kīrtana Pastimes of Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 11.15

rāmānanda rāya āilā gajapati-sańge

prathamei prabhure āsi' mililā bahu-rańge

SYNONYMS

rāmānanda rāya — Rāmānanda Rāya; āilā — came; gajapati-sańge — with the King; prathamei — in the first instance; prabhure — unto Lord Caitanya Mahāprabhu; āsi' — coming; mililā — met; bahu-rańge — with great pleasure.

TRANSLATION

When King Pratāparudra returned to Jagannātha Purī, Rāmānanda Rāya came with him. Rāmānanda Rāya immediately went to meet Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu with great pleasure.

PURPORT

All Indian kings are given titles. Sometimes they are known as Chatrapati, sometimes as Narapati, sometimes as Aśvapati, and so on. The King of Orissa is addressed as Gajapati.
