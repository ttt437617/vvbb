Madhya-līlāChapter 11: The Bedā-kīrtana Pastimes of Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 11.57

bāhya-jñāna nāhi, se-kāle krishna-nāma śuni,

ālińgana karibena tomāya 'vaishnava' 'jāni'

SYNONYMS

bāhya-jñāna nāhi — without external consciousness; se-kāle — at that time; krishna-nāma śuni' — by hearing the holy name of Lord Krishna; ālińgana karibena — He will embrace; tomāya — you; vaishnava jāni' — taking you to be a Vaishnava.

TRANSLATION

"Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu will be in a mood of ecstatic love, without external consciousness. At that time, as you recite those chapters from Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam, He will embrace you, knowing you to be a pure Vaishnava.

PURPORT

A Vaishnava is always ready to help another Vaishnava progress toward realization of the Absolute Truth. Sārvabhauma Bhattācārya could understand the King's position as a pure Vaishnava. The King was always thinking of Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu, and the Bhattācārya wanted to help him approach the Lord. A Vaishnava is always compassionate, especially when he sees a prospective devotee very determined (dridha-vrata). Consequently the Bhattācārya was ready to help the King.
