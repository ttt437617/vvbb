Madhya-līlāChapter 21: The Opulence and Sweetness of Lord Śrī Krishna

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 21.116

tāte sākshī sei ramā, nārāyanera priyatamā,

pativratā-ganera upāsyā

tińho ye mādhurya-lobhe, chādi' saba kāma-bhoge,

vrata kari' karilā tapasyā

SYNONYMS

tāte — in this regard; sākshī — the evidence; sei ramā — that goddess of fortune; nārāyanera priya-tamā — the most dear consort of Nārāyana; pati-vratā-ganera — of all chaste women; upāsyā — worshipable; tińho — she; ye — that; mādhurya-lobhe — being attracted by the same sweetness; chādi' — giving up; saba — all; kāma-bhoge — to enjoy with Krishna; vrata kari' — taking a vow; karilā tapasyā — executed austerities.

TRANSLATION

"The vivid evidence in this regard is that the dearest consort of Nārāyana, the goddess of fortune, who is worshiped by all chaste women, gave up everything in her desire to enjoy Krishna, being captivated by His unparalleled sweetness. Thus she took a great vow and underwent severe austerities.
