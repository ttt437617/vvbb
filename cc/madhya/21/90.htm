Madhya-līlāChapter 21: The Opulence and Sweetness of Lord Śrī Krishna

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 21.90

'tryadhīśvara'-śabdera artha 'gūdha' āra haya

'tri'-śabde krishnera tina loka kaya

SYNONYMS

tri-adhīśvara śabdera — of the word try-adhīśvara; artha — a meaning; gūdha — confidential; āra — another; haya — there is; tri-śabde — by the word "three"; krishnera — of Krishna; tina loka kaya — the three places or properties of Lord Krishna.

TRANSLATION

"There is a very deep meaning in the word 'try-adhīśvara,' which indicates that Krishna possesses three different lokas, or natures.

PURPORT

The word try-adhīśvara means "proprietor of the three worlds." There are three worlds, and Krishna is the supreme proprietor of them all. This is explained by Lord Krishna in the Bhagavad-gītā (5.29):

bhoktāram yajña-tapasām sarva-loka-maheśvaram

suhridam sarva-bhūtānām jñātvā mām śāntim ricchati

"A person in full consciousness of Me, knowing Me to be the ultimate beneficiary of all sacrifices and austerities, the Supreme Lord of all planets and demigods, and the benefactor and well-wisher of all living entities, attains peace from the pangs of material miseries."

The word sarva-loka means "all three worlds," and the word maheśvara means "the supreme proprietor." Krishna is the proprietor of both the material and the spiritual world. The spiritual world is divided into two portions — Goloka Vrindāvana and the Vaikunthas. The material world is a combination of universes unlimited in number.
