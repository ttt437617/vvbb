Madhya-līlāChapter 21: The Opulence and Sweetness of Lord Śrī Krishna

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 21.146

āmi ta' bāula, āna kahite āna kahi

krishnera mādhuryāmrita-srote yāi vahi'

SYNONYMS

āmi ta' bāula — I am a madman; āna kahite — to speak something; āna kahi — I speak on something else; krishnera — of Lord Krishna; mādhurya-amrita — of the nectar of the sweetness; srote — in the waves of; yāi — I go; vahi' — being carried away.

TRANSLATION

"Since I have become a madman, I am saying one thing instead of another. This is because I am being carried away by the waves of the nectarean ocean of Lord Krishna's transcendental sweetness."
