Madhya-līlāChapter 21: The Opulence and Sweetness of Lord Śrī Krishna

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 21.13

nāntam vidāmy aham amī munayo 'grajās te

māyā-balasya purushasya kuto 'varā ye

gāyan gunān daśa-śatānana ādi-devah

śesho 'dhunāpi samavasyati nāsya pāram

SYNONYMS

na antam — no limit; vidāmi — know; aham — I; amī — those; munayah — great saintly persons; agrajāh — brothers; te — of you; māyā-balasya — who has multi-energies; purushasya — of the Personality of Godhead; kutah — how; avarāh — less intelligent; ye — those who; gāyan — chanting; gunān — the qualities; daśa-śata-ānanah — who has a thousand hoods; ādi-devah — the Personality of Godhead; śeshah — Ananta Śesha; adhunā api — even until now; samavasyati — reaches; na — not; asya — of the Lord; pāram — limit.

TRANSLATION

"'If I, Lord Brahmā, and your elder brothers, the great saints and sages, cannot understand the limits of the Supreme Personality of Godhead, who is full of various energies, who else can understand them? Although constantly chanting about His transcendental qualities, the thousand-hooded Lord Śesha has not yet reached the end of the Lord's activities.'

PURPORT

This verse, spoken to Nārada Muni by Lord Brahmā, is also from Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam (2.7.41).
