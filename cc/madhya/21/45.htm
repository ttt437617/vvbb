Madhya-līlāChapter 21: The Opulence and Sweetness of Lord Śrī Krishna

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 21.45

karunā-nikuramba-komale

madhuraiśvarya-viśesha-śālini

jayati vraja-rāja-nandane

na hi cintā-kanikābhyudeti nah

SYNONYMS

karunā-nikuramba-komale — who is very soft because of great mercy; madhura-aiśvarya-viśesha-śālini — especially by the opulence of conjugal love; jayati — all glories; vraja-rāja-nandane — to the son of Mahārāja Nanda; na — not; hi — certainly; cintā — of anxiety; kanikā — even a particle; abhyudeti — awakens; nah — of us.

TRANSLATION

"'Vrindāvana-dhāma is very soft due to the mercy of the Supreme Lord, and it is especially opulent due to conjugal love. The transcendental glories of the son of Mahārāja Nanda are exhibited here. Under the circumstances, not the least anxiety is awakened within us.'
