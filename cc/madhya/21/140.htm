Madhya-līlāChapter 21: The Opulence and Sweetness of Lord Śrī Krishna

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 21.140

smita-kirana-sukarpūre, paiśe adhara-madhure,

sei madhu mātāya tribhuvane

vamśī-chidra ākāśe, tāra guna śabde paiśe,

dhvani-rūpe pāñā parināme

SYNONYMS

smita-kirana — the shining of Krishna's smile; su-karpūre — compared to camphor; paiśe — enters; adhara-madhure — within the sweetness of the lips; sei madhu — that ambrosia; mātāya — maddens; tri-bhuvane — the three worlds; vamśī-chidra — of the holes in the flute; ākāśe — in the space; tāra guna — the quality of that sweetness; śabde — in sound vibration; paiśe — enters; dhvani-rūpe — the form of sound vibration; pāñā — obtaining; parināme — by transformation.

TRANSLATION

"His slight smiling and fragrant illumination are compared to camphor, which enters the sweetness of His lips. That sweetness is transformed and enters into space as vibrations from the holes of His flute.
