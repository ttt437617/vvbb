Madhya-līlāChapter 21: The Opulence and Sweetness of Lord Śrī Krishna

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 21.42

ei artha — madhyama, śuna 'gūdha' artha āra

tina āvāsa-sthāna krishnera śāstre khyāti yāra

SYNONYMS

ei artha — this explanation; madhyama — middle; śuna — please hear; gūdha — confidential; artha — meaning; āra — another; tina — three; āvāsa-sthāna — residential places; krishnera — of Lord Krishna; śāstre — in the revealed scriptures; khyāti — fame; yāra — of which.

TRANSLATION

"This is the middle meaning. Now please hear the confidential meaning. Lord Krishna has three places of residence, which are well known from the revealed scriptures.

PURPORT

Krishna has three abodes — His internal abode (Goloka Vrindāvana), His intermediate abode (the spiritual sky) and His external abode (this material world).
