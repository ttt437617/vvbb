Madhya-līlāChapter 21: The Opulence and Sweetness of Lord Śrī Krishna

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 21.117

sei ta' mādhurya-sāra, anya-siddhi nāhi tāra,

tińho — mādhuryādi-guna-khani

āra saba prakāśe, tāńra datta guna bhāse,

yāhāń yata prakāśe kārya jāni

SYNONYMS

sei ta' mādhurya-sāra — that is the quintessence of sweetness; anya-siddhi — perfection due to anything else; nāhi — there is not; tāra — of that; tińho — Lord Krishna; mādhurya-ādi-guna-khani — the mine of transcendental mellows, headed by sweetness; āra saba — all other; prakāśe — in manifestations; tāńra — His; datta — given; guna — transcendental qualities; bhāse — are exhibited; yāhāń — where; yata — as much as; prakāśe — in that manifestation; kārya — to be done; jāni — I understand.

TRANSLATION

"The quintessence of Krishna's sweet bodily luster is so perfect that there is no perfection above it. He is the immutable mine of all transcendental qualities. In His other manifestations and personal expansions, there is only a partial exhibition of such qualities. We understand all His personal expansions in this way.
