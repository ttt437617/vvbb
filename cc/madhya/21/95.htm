Madhya-līlāChapter 21: The Opulence and Sweetness of Lord Śrī Krishna

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 21.95

mani-pīthe thekātheki, uthe jhanjhani

pīthera stuti kare mukuta — hena anumāni

SYNONYMS

mani-pīthe — between the gems and the lotus feet or the throne; thekātheki — collision; uthe — arises; jhanjhani — a jingling sound; pīthera — to the lotus feet or the throne; stuti — prayers; kare — offer; mukuta — all the helmets; hena — thus; anumāni — we can imagine.

TRANSLATION

"When the gems on the helmets of all the predominating deities collided before the throne and the Lord's lotus feet, there was a jingling sound, which seemed like prayers offered by the helmets at Krishna's lotus feet.
