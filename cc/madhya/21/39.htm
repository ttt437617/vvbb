Madhya-līlāChapter 21: The Opulence and Sweetness of Lord Śrī Krishna

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 21.39

mahā-vishnu, padmanābha, kshīrodaka-svāmī

ei tina — sthūla-sūkshma-sarva-antaryāmī

SYNONYMS

mahā-vishnu — Mahā-Vishnu; padmanābha — Padmanābha (Garbhodakaśāyī Vishnu); kshīra-udaka-svāmī — Kshīrodakaśāyī Vishnu; ei tina — all these three; sthūla-sūkshma — gross and subtle; sarva — of all; antaryāmī — the Supersoul.

TRANSLATION

"Mahā-Vishnu, Padmanābha and Kshīrodakaśāyī Vishnu are the Supersouls of all subtle and gross existences.

PURPORT

Lord Mahā-Vishnu is known as Kāranodakaśāyī Vishnu, the Supersoul of everything. Garbhodakaśāyī Vishnu, from whose lotus navel Brahmā was created, is also called Hiranyagarbha and is the total Supersoul and the subtle Supersoul. Kshīrodakaśāyī Vishnu is the universal form and the gross Supersoul.
