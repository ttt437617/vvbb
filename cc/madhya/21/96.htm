Madhya-līlāChapter 21: The Opulence and Sweetness of Lord Śrī Krishna

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 21.96

nija-cic-chakte krishna nitya virājamāna

cic-chakti-sampattira 'shad-aiśvarya' nāma

SYNONYMS

nija — His own; cit-śakte — in spiritual potency; krishna — Lord Krishna; nitya — eternally; virājamāna — existing; cit-śakti — of the spiritual potency; sampattira — of the opulence; shat-aiśvarya — the six opulences; nāma — named.

TRANSLATION

"Krishna is thus situated eternally in His spiritual potency, and the opulence of that spiritual potency is called shad-aiśvarya, indicating six kinds of opulences.
