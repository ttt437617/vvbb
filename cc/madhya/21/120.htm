Madhya-līlāChapter 21: The Opulence and Sweetness of Lord Śrī Krishna

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 21.120

sei-rūpa vrajāśraya, aiśvarya-mādhuryamaya,

divya-guna-gana-ratnālaya

ānera vaibhava-sattā, krishna-datta bhagavattā,

krishna — sarva-amśī, sarvāśraya

SYNONYMS

sei-rūpa — that supernatural beauty; vraja-āśraya — whose abode is in Vrindāvana; aiśvarya-mādhurya-maya — full of opulence and the sweetness of love; divya-guna-gana — of transcendental qualities; ratna-ālaya — the source of all the gems; ānera — of others; vaibhava-sattā — the presence of opulences; krishna-datta — all bestowed by Krishna; bhagavattā — qualities of the Supreme Personality of Godhead; krishna — Lord Krishna; sarva-amśī — the original source of all of them; sarva-āśraya — the shelter of all of them.

TRANSLATION

"Such ecstatic transactions between Krishna and the gopīs are possible only in Vrindāvana, which is full of the opulences of transcendental love. The form of Krishna is the original source of all transcendental qualities. It is like a mine of gems. The opulences belonging to all the personal expansions of Krishna are to be understood to be bestowed by Krishna; therefore Krishna is the original source and shelter of everyone.
