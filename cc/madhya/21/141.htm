Madhya-līlāChapter 21: The Opulence and Sweetness of Lord Śrī Krishna

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 21.141

se dhvani caudike dhāya, anda bhedi' vaikunthe yāya,

bale paiśe jagatera kāne

sabā mātoyāla kari', balātkāre āne dhari',

viśeshatah yuvatīra gane

SYNONYMS

se dhvani — that vibration; cau-dike — in the four directions; dhāya — runs; anda bhedi' — piercing the coverings of the universe; vaikunthe yāya — goes to the spiritual sky; bale — by force; paiśe — enters; jagatera — of the three worlds; kāne — in the ears; sabā — everyone; mātoyāla kari' — making drunk; balātkāre — by force; āne — brings; dhari' — catching; viśeshatah — specifically; yuvatīra gane — all the young damsels of Vrajabhūmi.

TRANSLATION

"The sound of Krishna's flute spreads in the four directions. Even though Krishna vibrates His flute within this universe, its sound pierces the universal covering and goes to the spiritual sky. Thus the vibration enters the ears of all inhabitants. It especially enters Goloka Vrindāvana-dhāma and attracts the minds of the young damsels of Vrajabhūmi, bringing them forcibly to where Krishna is present.
