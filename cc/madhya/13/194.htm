Madhya-līlāChapter 13: The Ecstatic Dancing of the Lord at Ratha-yātrā

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 13.194

vāme — 'vipra-śāsana' nārikela-vana

dāhine ta' pushpodyāna yena vrindāvana

SYNONYMS

vāme — on the left; vipra-śāsana — the place where brāhmanas lived; nārikela-vana — coconut grove; dāhine — on the right side; ta' — indeed; pushpa-udyāna — flower gardens; yena — as if; vrindāvana — Vrindāvana.

TRANSLATION

On the left side, Lord Jagannātha saw a neighborhood of brāhmanas and a coconut-tree grove. On the right side, He saw nice flower gardens resembling those in the holy place Vrindāvana.

PURPORT

Vipra-śāsana is a word generally used in the Orissa province to indicate the quarters where brāhmanas live.
