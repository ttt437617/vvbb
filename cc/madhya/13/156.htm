Madhya-līlāChapter 13: The Ecstatic Dancing of the Lord at Ratha-yātrā

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 13.156

yādavera vipaksha, yata dushta kamsa-paksha,

tāhā āmi kailuń saba kshaya

āche dui-cāri jana, tāhā māri' vrindāvana,

āilāma āmi, jāniha niścaya

SYNONYMS

yādavera vipaksha — all the enemies of the Yadu dynasty; yata — all; dushta — mischievous; kamsa-paksha — the party of Kamsa; tāhā — them; āmi — I; kailuń saba kshaya — have annihilated all; āche — there are still; dui-cāri jana — two or four demons; tāhā māri' — after killing them; vrindāvana — to Vrindāvana; āilāma āmi — I am coming very soon; jāniha niścaya — please know it very well.

TRANSLATION

"I have already killed all the mischievous demons who are enemies of the Yadu dynasty, and I have also killed Kamsa and his allies. But there are two or four demons still living. I want to kill them, and after doing so I shall very soon return to Vrindāvana. Please know this for certain.

PURPORT

Just as Krishna does not take a step away from Vrindāvana, Krishna's devotee also does not like to leave Vrindāvana. However, when he has to tend to Krishna's business, he leaves Vrindāvana. After finishing his mission, a pure devotee returns home, back to Vrindāvana, back to Godhead. Krishna assured Rādhārānī that after killing the demons outside Vrindāvana, He would return. "I am coming back very soon," He promised, "as soon as I have killed the few remaining demons."
