Madhya-līlāChapter 13: The Ecstatic Dancing of the Lord at Ratha-yātrā

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 13.146

tomāra ye anya veśa, anya sańga, anya deśa,

vraja-jane kabhu nāhi bhāya

vraja-bhūmi chādite nāre, tomā nā dekhile mare,

vraja-janera ki habe upāya

SYNONYMS

tomāra — Your; ye — that; anya veśa — different dress; anya sańga — other associates; anya deśa — other countries; vraja-jane — to the inhabitants of Vrindāvana; kabhu — at any time; nāhi — does not; bhāya — appeal; vraja-bhūmi — the land of Vrindāvana; chādite nāre — they do not like to leave; tomā — You; nā — not; dekhile — seeing; mare — they die; vraja-janera — of the inhabitants of Vrindāvana; ki — what; habe — will be; upāya — means.

TRANSLATION

"The inhabitants of Vrindāvana do not want You dressed like a prince, nor do they want You to associate with great warriors in a different country. They cannot leave the land of Vrindāvana, and without Your presence, they are all dying. What is their condition to be?
