Madhya-līlāChapter 13: The Ecstatic Dancing of the Lord at Ratha-yātrā

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 13.109

kabhu netre nāsāya jala, mukhe pade phena

amritera dhārā candra-bimbe vahe yena

SYNONYMS

kabhu — sometimes; netre — from the eyes; nāsāya — from the nostrils; jala — water; mukhe — from the mouth; pade — fell; phena — foam; amritera — of nectar; dhārā — torrents; candra-bimbe — from the moon; vahe — flow; yena — as if.

TRANSLATION

Water flowed from His eyes and sometimes through His nostrils, and foam fell from His mouth. These flowings appeared to be torrents of nectar descending from the moon.
