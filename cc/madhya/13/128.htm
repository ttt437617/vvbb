Madhya-līlāChapter 13: The Ecstatic Dancing of the Lord at Ratha-yātrā

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 13.128

ihāń lokāranya, hātī, ghodā, ratha-dhvani

tāhāń pushpāranya, bhrińga-pika-nāda śuni

SYNONYMS

ihāń — at this place, Kurukshetra; loka-aranya — too great a crowd of people; hātī — elephants; ghodā — horses; ratha-dhvani — the rattling sound of chariots; tāhāń — there, in Vrindāvana; pushpa-aranya — the garden of flowers; bhrińga — of bumblebees; pika — of the birds; nāda — sound; śuni — I hear.

TRANSLATION

"In Kurukshetra there are crowds of people, elephants and horses, and also the rattling of chariots. But in Vrindāvana there are flower gardens, and the humming of the bees and chirping of the birds can be heard.
