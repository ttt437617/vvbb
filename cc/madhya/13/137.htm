Madhya-līlāChapter 13: The Ecstatic Dancing of the Lord at Ratha-yātrā

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 13.137

anyera hridaya — mana, mora mana — vrindāvana,

'mane' 'vane' eka kari' jāni

tāhāń tomāra pada-dvaya, karāha yadi udaya,

tabe tomāra pūrna kripā māni

SYNONYMS

anyera — of others; hridaya — consciousness; mana — mind; mora mana — My mind; vrindāvana — Vrindāvana consciousness; mane — with the mind; vane — with Vrindāvana; eka kari' — as one and the same; jāni — I know; tāhāń — there, at Vrindāvana; tomāra — Your; pada-dvaya — two lotus feet; karāha — You do; yadi — if; udaya — appearance; tabe — then; tomāra — Your; pūrna — complete; kripā — mercy; māni — I accept.

TRANSLATION

Speaking in the mood of Śrīmatī Rādhārānī, Caitanya Mahāprabhu said, "For most people, the mind and heart are one, but because My mind is never separated from Vrindāvana, I consider My mind and Vrindāvana to be one. My mind is already Vrindāvana, and since You like Vrindāvana, will You please place Your lotus feet there? I would deem that Your full mercy.

PURPORT

The mind's activities are thinking, feeling and willing, by which the mind accepts materially favorable things and rejects the unfavorable. This is the consciousness of people in general. But when one's mind does not accept and reject but simply becomes fixed on the lotus feet of Krishna, then one's mind becomes as good as Vrindāvana. Wherever Krishna is, there also are Śrīmatī Rādhārānī, the gopīs, the cowherd boys and all the other inhabitants of Vrindāvana. Thus as soon as one fixes Krishna in his mind, his mind becomes identical with Vrindāvana. In other words, when one's mind is completely free from all material desires and is engaged only in the service of the Supreme Personality of Godhead, then one always lives in Vrindāvana, and nowhere else.
