Madhya-līlāChapter 13: The Ecstatic Dancing of the Lord at Ratha-yātrā

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 13.102

māmsa-vrana sama roma-vrinda pulakita

śimulīra vriksha yena kantaka-veshtita

SYNONYMS

māmsa — skin; vrana — pimples; sama — like; roma-vrinda — the hairs of the body; pulakita — erupted; śimulīra vriksha — cotton tree; yena — as if; kantaka — by thorns; veshtita — surrounded.

TRANSLATION

His skin erupted with goose pimples, and the hairs of His body stood on end. His body resembled the śimulī [silk cotton tree], all covered with thorns.
