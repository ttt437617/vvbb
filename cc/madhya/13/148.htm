Madhya-līlāChapter 13: The Ecstatic Dancing of the Lord at Ratha-yātrā

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 13.148

śuniyā rādhikā-vānī, vraja-prema mane āni,

bhāve vyākulita deha-mana

vraja-lokera prema śuni', āpanāke 'rinī' māni',

kare krishna tāńre āśvāsana

SYNONYMS

śuniyā — after hearing; rādhikā-vānī — the statement of Śrīmatī Rādhārānī; vraja-prema — the love of Vraja; mane āni — remembering; bhāve — in that ecstasy; vyākulita — very much perturbed; deha-mana — the body and mind; vraja-lokera — of the inhabitants of Vrindāvana; prema śuni' — after hearing of the loving affairs; āpanāke — Himself; rinī māni' — considering very much indebted; kare — does; krishna — Lord Krishna; tāńre — unto Her; āśvāsana — pacification.

TRANSLATION

After hearing Śrīmatī Rādhārānī's statements, Lord Krishna's love for the inhabitants of Vrindāvana was evoked, and His body and mind became very much perturbed. After hearing of their love for Him, He immediately thought Himself to be always indebted to the residents of Vrindāvana. Then Krishna began to pacify Śrīmatī Rādhārānī as follows.
