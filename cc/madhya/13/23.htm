Madhya-līlāChapter 13: The Ecstatic Dancing of the Lord at Ratha-yātrā

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 13.23

pañca-daśa dina īśvara mahā-lakshmī lañā

tāńra sańge krīdā kaila nibhrite vasiyā

SYNONYMS

pañca-daśa dina — fifteen days; īśvara — the Lord; mahā-lakshmī — the supreme goddess of fortune; lañā — with; tāńra sańge — in her company; krīdā — enjoyment; kaila — performed; nibhrite — in a solitary place; vasiyā — sitting.

TRANSLATION

For fifteen days the Lord had remained in a secluded place with the supreme goddess of fortune and had performed His pastimes with her.

PURPORT

The fifteen-day period of anavasara is also called nibhrita, in honor of the solitary place where the supreme goddess of fortune lives. After living there a fortnight, Lord Jagannātha took permission from the goddess of fortune to leave.
