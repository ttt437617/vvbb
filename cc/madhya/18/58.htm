Madhya-līlāChapter 18: Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu's Visit to Śrī Vrindāvana

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 18.58

'pāvanādi' saba kunde snāna kariyā

lokere puchila, parvata-upare yāñā

SYNONYMS

pāvana-ādi — Pāvana and others; saba kunde — in every lake; snāna kariyā — taking a bath; lokere puchila — inquired from persons there; parvata-upare yāñā — going up a hill.

TRANSLATION

Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu bathed in all the celebrated lakes, beginning with Lake Pāvana. Thereafter He climbed a hill and spoke to the people.

PURPORT

The Pāvana-sarovara is described in the Mathurā-māhātmya:

pāvane sarasi snātvā krishnam nandīśvare girau

drishtvā nandam yaśodām ca sarvābhīshtam avāpnuyāt

"One who bathes in Pāvana Lake by Nandīśvara Hill will see Krishna there along with Nanda and Yaśodā and will fufill all his desires."
