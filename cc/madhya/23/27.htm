Madhya-līlāChapter 23: Life's Ultimate Goal — Love of Godhead

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 23.27

harau ratim vahann esha

narendrānām śikhā-manih

bhikshām atann ari-pure

śva-pākam api vandate

SYNONYMS

harau — toward the Supreme Personality of Godhead; ratim — affection; vahan — carrying; eshah — this one; nara-indrānām — of all the kings; śikhā-manih — brilliant crown jewel; bhikshām — begging alms; atan — wandering for; ari-pure — even in the city of enemies; śva-pākam — the fifth-grade candālas; api — even; vandate — worships.

TRANSLATION

"'Bharata Mahārāja always carried affection for Krishna within his heart. Although Bharata Mahārāja was the crown jewel of kings, he was still wandering about and begging alms in the city of his enemies. He was even offering respects to candālas, low-class men who eat dogs.'

PURPORT

This is a quotation from the Padma Purāna.
