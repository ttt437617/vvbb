Madhya-līlāChapter 23: Life's Ultimate Goal — Love of Godhead

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 23.31

tvac-chaiśavam tri-bhuvanādbhutam ity avehi

mac-cāpalam ca tava vā mama vādhigamyam

tat kim karomi viralam muralī-vilāsi

mugdham mukhāmbujam udīkshitum īkshanābhyām

SYNONYMS

tvat — Your; śaiśavam — early age; tri-bhuvana — within the three worlds; adbhutam — wonderful; iti — thus; avehi — know; mat-cāpalam — My unsteadiness; ca — and; tava — of You; vā — or; mama — of Me; vā — or; adhigamyam — to by understood; tat — that; kim — what; karomi — I do; viralam — in solitude; muralī-vilāsi — O player of the flute; mugdham — attractive; mukha-ambujam — lotuslike face; udīkshitum — to see sufficiently; īkshanābhyām — by the eyes.

TRANSLATION

"'O Krishna, O flute-player, the sweetness of Your early age is wonderful within these three worlds. You know My unsteadiness, and I know Yours. No one else knows about this. I want to see Your beautiful, attractive face somewhere in a solitary place, but how can this be accomplished?'

PURPORT

This is a verse from the Krishna-karnāmrita (32).
