Madhya-līlāChapter 23: Life's Ultimate Goal — Love of Godhead

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 23.6

e dui, — bhāvera 'svarūpa', 'tatastha' lakshana

premera lakshana ebe śuna, sanātana

SYNONYMS

ei dui — these two; bhāvera — of emotion; svarūpa — constitutional; tatastha — marginal; lakshana — symptoms; premera — of love; lakshana — the symptoms; ebe — now; śuna — hear; sanātana — O Sanātana.

TRANSLATION

"Bhāva [emotion] has two different symptoms — constitutional and marginal. Now, My dear Sanātana, listen to the symptoms of love.

PURPORT

The word śuddha-sattva-viśeshātmā means "situated on the transcendental platform of pure goodness." In this way the soul is purified of all material contamination, and this position is called svarūpa-lakshana, the constitutional symptom of bhāva, emotion. By various tastes, one's heart is softened, and there is an awakening of one's loving propensity to render spontaneous service to the Lord. This is called tatastha-lakshana, the marginal symptom of bhāva.
