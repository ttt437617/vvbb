Madhya-līlāChapter 23: Life's Ultimate Goal — Love of Godhead

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 23.84-85

līlā premnā priyādhikyam

mādhuryam venu-rūpayoh

ity asādhāranam proktam

govindasya catushtayam

evam gunāś catur-bhedāś

catuh-shashtir udāhritāh

SYNONYMS

līlā — pastimes; premnā — with transcendental love; priya-ādhikyam — an abundance of highly elevated devotees; mādhuryam — sweetness; venu-rūpayoh — of the flute and the beauty of Krishna; iti — thus; asādhāranam — uncommon; proktam — said; govindasya — of Lord Krishna; catushtayam — four special features; evam — thus; gunāh — transcendental qualities; catuh-bhedāh — having four divisions; catuh-shashtih — sixty-four; udāhritāh — declared.

TRANSLATION

"'Above Nārāyana, Krishna has four specific transcendental qualities — His wonderful pastimes, an abundance of wonderful associates who are very dear to Him [like the gopīs], His wonderful beauty and the wonderful vibration of His flute. Lord Krishna is more exalted than ordinary living beings and demigods like Lord Śiva. He is even more exalted than His personal expansion Nārāyana. In all, the Supreme Personality of Godhead has sixty-four transcendental qualities in full.'
