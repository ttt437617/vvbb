Madhya-līlāChapter 23: Life's Ultimate Goal — Love of Godhead

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 23.23

vāgbhih stuvanto manasā smarantas

tanvā namanto 'py aniśam na triptāh

bhaktāh śravan-netra-jalāh samagram

āyur harer eva samarpayanti

SYNONYMS

vāgbhih — by words; stuvantah — offering prayers to the Supreme Personality of Godhead; manasā — by the mind; smarantah — remembering; tanvā — by the body; namantah — offering obeisances; api — although; aniśam — all the time; na triptāh — not satisfied; bhaktāh — the devotees; śravat — shedding; netra-jalāh — tears from the eyes; samagram — the whole; āyuh — life; hareh — to Krishna; eva — only; samarpayanti — dedicate.

TRANSLATION

"'With their words, they offer prayers to the Lord. With their minds, they always remember the Lord. With their bodies, they offer obeisances to the Lord. Despite all these activities, they are still not satisfied. This is the nature of pure devotees. Shedding tears from their eyes, they dedicate their whole lives to the Lord's service.'

PURPORT

This verse from the Hari-bhakti-sudhodaya is found in the Bhakti-rasāmrita-sindhu (1.3.29).
