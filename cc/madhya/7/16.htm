Madhya-līlāChapter 7: The Lord Begins His Tour of South India

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 7.16

dui-eka sańge caluka, nā pada hatha-rańge

yāre kaha sei dui caluk tomāra sańge

SYNONYMS

dui — two; eka — or one; sańge — with You; caluka — let go; nā — do not; pada — fall; hatha-rańge — in the clutches of thieves and rogues; yāre — whoever; kaha — You say; sei — those; dui — two; caluk — let go; tomāra — You; sańge — along with.

TRANSLATION

"Let one or two of us go with You; otherwise You may fall into the clutches of thieves and rogues along the way. They may be whomever You like, but two persons should go with You.
