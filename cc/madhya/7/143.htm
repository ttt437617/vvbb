Madhya-līlāChapter 7: The Lord Begins His Tour of South India

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 7.143

kvāham daridrah pāpīyān

kva krishnah śrī-niketanah

brahma-bandhur iti smāham

bāhubhyām parirambhitah

SYNONYMS

kva — who; aham — I; daridrah — poor; pāpīyān — sinful; kva — who; krishnah — the Supreme Personality of Godhead; śrī-niketanah — the transcendental form of all opulence; brahma-bandhuh — the friend of a brāhmana, not fit even to be called a brāhmana; iti — thus; sma — certainly; aham — I; bāhubhyām — by the arms; parirambhitah — embraced.

TRANSLATION

He said, "'Who am I? A sinful, poor friend of a brāhmana. And who is Krishna? The Supreme Personality of Godhead, full in six opulences. Nonetheless, He has embraced me with His two arms.'"

PURPORT

This verse was spoken by Sudāmā Brāhmana in Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam (10.81.16), in connection with his meeting Lord Krishna.
