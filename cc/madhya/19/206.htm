Madhya-līlāChapter 19: Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu Instructs Śrīla Rūpa Gosvāmī

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 19.206

uvāha krishno bhagavān

śrīdāmānam parājitah

vrishabham bhadrasenas tu

pralambo rohinī-sutam

SYNONYMS

uvāha — carried; krishnah — Lord Krishna; bhagavān — the Supreme Personality of Godhead; śrīdāmānam — Śrīdāmā; parājitah — being defeated; vrishabham — Vrishabha; bhadrasenah — Bhadrasena; tu — and; pralambah — Pralamba; rohinī-sutam — Balarāma.

TRANSLATION

"'When Krishna was defeated by Śrīdāmā, He had to carry him on His shoulders. Similarly, Bhadrasena carried Vrishabha, and Pralamba carried Balarāma, the son of Rohinī.'

PURPORT

This verse is from Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam (10.18.24). When all the cowherd boys were playing in the forest of Vrindāvana, the demon Pralambāsura appeared in order to kidnap Krishna and Balarāma. The asura appeared disguised in the form of a cowherd boy, but Krishna could understand his trick. Krishna therefore divided all the cowherd boys into two parties. One party belonged to Balarāma, and the other party belonged to Krishna Himself. Ultimately Krishna was defeated in this play, and according to the wager, the defeated party had to carry the victorious party on their shoulders. Krishna had to carry Śrīdāmā on His shoulders, and Bhadrasena had to carry Vrishabha. The demon Pralambāsura had to carry Balarāma, and when Balarāma mounted his shoulders, the demon ran far away. Finally the demon began to expand his body to a gigantic size, and Balarāma understood that he intended to kill Him. Balarāma immediately struck the demon's head with His strong fist, and the demon fell down dead as if he were a snake whose head had been smashed.
