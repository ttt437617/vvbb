Madhya-līlāChapter 19: Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu Instructs Śrīla Rūpa Gosvāmī

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 19.211

śānta-rase — 'svarūpa-buddhye krishnaika-nishthatā'

"śamo man-nishthatā buddheh" iti śrī-mukha-gāthā

SYNONYMS

śānta-rase — in the stage of śānta-rasa, or neutrality; svarūpa-buddhye — by self-realization; krishna-eka-nishthatā — full devotion to the lotus feet of Krishna; śamah — equilibrium; mat — to Me; nishthatā — the quality of attachment; buddheh — of the mind; iti — thus; śrī-mukha — from the mouth of the Supreme Lord; gāthā — a verse.

TRANSLATION

"When one is fully attached to Krishna's lotus feet, one attains the śamatā stage. The word 'śamatā' is derived from the word 'śama'; therefore śānta-rasa, the position of neutrality, means being fully attached to the lotus feet of Krishna. This is the verdict from the mouth of the Supreme Personality of Godhead Himself. This state is called self-realization.
