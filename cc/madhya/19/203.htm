Madhya-līlāChapter 19: Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu Instructs Śrīla Rūpa Gosvāmī

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 19.203

'kevalā'ra śuddha-prema 'aiśvarya' nā jāne

aiśvarya dekhileo nija-sambandha se māne

SYNONYMS

kevalāra — of unmixed attraction for Krishna; śuddha-prema — unalloyed love; aiśvarya — opulence; nā jāne — does not know; aiśvarya — opulence; dekhileo — in spite of experiencing; nija-sambandha — one's own relationship with Krishna; se māne — he takes very seriously.

TRANSLATION

"In the stage of kevalā [unalloyed devotion] a devotee does not consider the unlimited opulence of Krishna, even though he experiences it. He takes seriously only his own relationship with Krishna.

PURPORT

When a devotee reaches the stage of pure, unalloyed devotion, especially in friendship with Krishna, he forgets the Lord's opulences, although he sees them, and he considers himself equal to Krishna. There is no question of actually comparing oneself to Krishna, but because the devotee is so advanced in Krishna consciousness, he is able to behave with Krishna as he would with an ordinary man.
