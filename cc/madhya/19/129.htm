Madhya-līlāChapter 19: Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu Instructs Śrīla Rūpa Gosvāmī

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 19.129

karońyā-mātra hāte, kāńthā chińdā, bahirvāsa

krishna-kathā, krishna-nāma, nartana-ullāsa

SYNONYMS

karońyā — the waterpot of a sannyāsī; mātra — only; hāte — in the hand; kāńthā chińdā — torn quilt; bahirvāsa — outer garments; krishna-kathā — discussion of Krishna's pastimes; krishna-nāma — chanting the holy name of Lord Krishna; nartana-ullāsa — dancing in jubilation.

TRANSLATION

"They carry only waterpots, and they wear torn quilts. They always chant the holy names of Krishna and discuss His pastimes. In great jubilation, they also dance.
