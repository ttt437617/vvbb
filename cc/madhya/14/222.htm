Madhya-līlāChapter 14: Performance of the Vrindāvana Pastimes

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 14.222

kalpavriksha-latāra — yāhāń sāhajika-vana

pushpa-phala vinā keha nā māge anya dhana

SYNONYMS

kalpa-vriksha-latāra — of creepers and kalpa-vriksha, or desire trees; yāhāń — where; sāhajika-vana — natural forest; pushpa-phala vinā — except for fruits and flowers; keha — anyone; nā māge — does not want; anya — any other; dhana — riches.

TRANSLATION

"Vrindāvana is a natural forest of desire trees and creepers, and the inhabitants do not want anything but the fruits and flowers of those desire trees.
