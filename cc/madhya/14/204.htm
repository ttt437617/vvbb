Madhya-līlāChapter 14: Performance of the Vrindāvana Pastimes

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 14.204

vrindāvanera sampad dekha, — pushpa-kisalaya

giridhātu-śikhipiccha-guñjāphala-maya

SYNONYMS

vrindāvanera — of Vrindāvana; sampad — the opulence; dekha — see; pushpa-kisalaya — a few flowers and twigs; giri-dhātu — some minerals from the hills; śikhi-piccha — some peacock feathers; guñjā-phala-maya — some guñjā-phala.

TRANSLATION

"As far as Vrindāvana's opulence is concerned, it consists of a few flowers and twigs, some minerals from the hills, a few peacock feathers and the plant known as guñjā.
