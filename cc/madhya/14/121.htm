Madhya-līlāChapter 14: Performance of the Vrindāvana Pastimes

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 14.121

nānā-pushpodyāne tathā khele rātri-dine

lakshmīdevīre sańge nāhi laya ki kārane?

SYNONYMS

nānā-pushpa-udyāne — in the various flower gardens; tathā — there; khele — He plays; rātri-dine — both day and night; lakshmī-devīre — Lakshmīdevī, the goddess of fortune; sańge — with Him; nāhi — does not; laya — take; ki kārane — what is the reason.

TRANSLATION

"The Lord enjoys His pastimes day and night in various flower gardens there. But why does He not take Lakshmīdevī, the goddess of fortune, with Him?"
