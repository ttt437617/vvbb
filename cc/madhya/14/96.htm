Madhya-līlāChapter 14: Performance of the Vrindāvana Pastimes

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 14.96

bhakta-gana-sańge prabhu udyāne āsiyā

vrindāvana-vihāra kare bhakta-gana lañā

SYNONYMS

bhakta-gana-sańge — with the devotees; prabhu — Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu; udyāne — in the garden; āsiyā — coming; vrindāvana-vihāra — the pastimes of Vrindāvana; kare — performs; bhakta-gana lañā — with all the devotees.

TRANSLATION

Accompanied by His devotees, Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu then went into the garden and enjoyed the pastimes of Vrindāvana.

PURPORT

Śrīla Bhaktisiddhānta Sarasvatī Thākura has pointed out that this vrindāvana-vihāra — the pastimes of Vrindāvana — does not refer to Krishna's mixing with the gopīs or the transcendental mellow of parakīya-rasa. Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu's vrindāvana-līlā in the garden of Jagannātha Purī did not involve association with women or with other people's wives in the fashion transcendentally demonstrated by Śrī Krishna. In His vrindāvana-līlā, Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu conceived of Himself as the assistant of Śrīmatī Rādhārānī. When Śrīmatī Rādhārānī enjoyed the company of Krishna, Her maidservants were very pleased. One should not compare Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu's vrindāvana-vihāra in the garden of Jagannātha with the activities of the gaurāńga-nāgarīs.
