Madhya-līlāChapter 14: Performance of the Vrindāvana Pastimes

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 14.218

svarūpa kahe, — śrīvāsa, śuna sāvadhāne

vrindāvana-sampad tomāra nāhi pade mane?

SYNONYMS

svarūpa kahe — Svarūpa Dāmodara said; śrīvāsa — my dear Śrīvāsa; śuna sāvadhāne — carefully please hear; vrindāvana-sampad — the opulence of Vrindāvana; tomāra — your; nāhi — not; pade — falls; mane — in the mind.

TRANSLATION

Svarūpa Dāmodara then retorted, "My dear Śrīvāsa, please hear me with attention. You have forgotten the transcendental opulence of Vrindāvana.
