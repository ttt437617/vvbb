Madhya-līlāChapter 14: Performance of the Vrindāvana Pastimes

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 14.118

yadyapi jagannātha karena dvārakāya vihāra

sahaja prakata kare parama udāra

tathāpi vatsara-madhye haya eka-bāra

vrindāvana dekhite tāńra utkanthā apāra

SYNONYMS

yadyapi — although; jagannātha — Lord Jagannātha; karena — does; dvārakāya — in Dvārakā-dhāma; vihāra — enjoyment; sahaja — natural; prakata — manifestation; kare — does; parama — sublime; udāra — liberal; tathāpi — still; vatsara-madhye — within a year; haya — becomes; eka-bāra — once; vrindāvana dekhite — to visit Vrindāvana; tāńra — His; utkanthā — eagerness; apāra — unlimited.

TRANSLATION

"Although Lord Jagannātha enjoys His pastimes at Dvārakā-dhāma and naturally manifests sublime liberality there, still, once a year He becomes unlimitedly eager to see Vrindāvana."
