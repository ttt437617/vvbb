Madhya-līlāChapter 14: Performance of the Vrindāvana Pastimes

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 14.87

śushka-tarka-khali khāite janma gela yāńra

tāńre līlāmrita piyāo, — e kripā tomāra

SYNONYMS

śushka-tarka — of dry logic; khali — oil cakes; khāite — eating; janma — the whole life; gela — passed; yāńra — of whom; tāńre — him; līlā-amrita — the nectar of Your pastimes; piyāo — You caused to drink; e — this; kripā — mercy; tomāra — Your.

TRANSLATION

"Logic is like a dry oil cake from which all the oil has been extracted. The Bhattācārya passed his life in eating such dry cakes, but now You have made him drink the nectar of transcendental pastimes. It is certainly Your great mercy upon him."
