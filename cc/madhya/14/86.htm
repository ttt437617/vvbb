Madhya-līlāChapter 14: Performance of the Vrindāvana Pastimes

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 14.86

meru-mandara-parvata dubāya yathā tathā

ei dui — ganda-śaila, ihāra kā kathā

SYNONYMS

meru-mandara — Sumeru and Mandara; parvata — big mountains; dubāya — drowns; yathā tathā — anywhere; ei dui — these two; ganda-śaila — very small hills; ihāra kā kathā — what to speak of these.

TRANSLATION

"A drop from the ocean of Your mercy can drown great mountains like Sumeru and Mandara. Since these two gentlemen are little hills by comparison, it is no wonder that they are being drowned in the ocean of Your mercy.
