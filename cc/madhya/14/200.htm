Madhya-līlāChapter 14: Performance of the Vrindāvana Pastimes

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 14.200

pāni-rodham avirodhita-vāñcham

bhartsanāś ca madhura-smita-garbhāh

mādhavasya kurute karabhorur

hāri śushka-ruditam ca mukhe 'pi

SYNONYMS

pāni — the hand; rodham — obstructing; avirodhita — unobstructed; vāñcham — the desire of Krishna; bhartsanāh — admonitions; ca — and; madhura — sweet; smita-garbhāh — containing a gentle smiling attitude; mādhavasya — of Śrī Krishna; kurute — does; karabha-ūruh — whose thigh is like the trunk of a baby elephant; hāri — charming; śushka-ruditam — dry crying; ca — and; mukhe — on the face; api — also.

TRANSLATION

"'Actually She has no desire to stop Krishna's endeavor to touch Her body with His hands, yet Śrīmatī Rādhārānī, whose thighs are like the trunk of a baby elephant, protests His advances and, sweetly smiling, admonishes Him. At such times She cries without tears on Her charming face.'
