Madhya-līlāChapter 17: The Lord Travels to Vrindāvana

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 17.42

vyāghra-mriga anyonye kare ālińgana

mukhe mukha diyā kare anyonye cumbana

SYNONYMS

vyāghra-mriga — the tigers and deer; anyonye — one another; kare — do; ālińgana — embracing; mukhe mukha diyā — touching one another's mouths; kare — do; anyonye cumbana — kissing one another.

TRANSLATION

Indeed, the tigers and deer began to embrace one another, and touching mouths, they began to kiss.
