Madhya-līlāChapter 17: The Lord Travels to Vrindāvana

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 17.199

śuka, pika, bhrińga prabhure dekhi' 'pañcama' gāya

śikhi-gana nritya kari' prabhu-āge yāya

SYNONYMS

śuka — parrots; pika — cuckoos; bhrińga — bumblebees; prabhure — Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu; dekhi' — seeing; pañcama — the fifth musical note; gāya — sing; śikhi-gana — peacocks; nritya — dancing; kari' — performing; prabhu-āge — in front of Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu; yāya — go.

TRANSLATION

Bumblebees and birds like the parrot and cuckoo all began to sing loudly on the fifth note, and the peacocks began to dance in front of the Lord.
