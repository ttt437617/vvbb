Madhya-līlāChapter 17: The Lord Travels to Vrindāvana

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 17.153

pūrve yena 'dakshina' yāite loka nistārilā

'paścima'-deśe taiche saba 'vaishnava' karilā

SYNONYMS

pūrve — formerly; yena — as; dakshina — South India; yāite — going to; loka — the people; nistārilā — He delivered; paścima-deśe — in the western countries; taiche — similarly; saba — all; vaishnava — devotees; karilā — made.

TRANSLATION

When the Lord toured South India, He delivered many people, and when He traveled in the western sector, He similarly converted many people to Vaishnavism.

PURPORT

Formerly Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu converted people when He toured southern and western India. Similarly, the Hare Krishna movement is now delivering the people of the Western world wherever devotees are chanting the holy names. This is all being done by the Lord's mercy. Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu predicted that He would deliver people in every city and village of the world by giving them a chance to chant the Hare Krishna mahā-mantra.
