Madhya-līlāChapter 17: The Lord Travels to Vrindāvana

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 17.80

mūkam karoti vācālam

pańgum lańghayate girim

yat-kripā tam aham vande

paramānanda-mādhavam

SYNONYMS

mūkam — a person who cannot speak; karoti — makes; vācālam — an eloquent speaker; pańgum — a person who cannot even walk; lańghayate — causes to cross over; girim — the mountain; yat-kripā — whose mercy; tam — unto Him; aham — I; vande — offer obeisances; parama-ānanda — the transcendentally blissful; mādhavam — Supreme Personality of Godhead.

TRANSLATION

"'The Supreme Personality of Godhead has the form of sac-cid-ānanda-vigraha [Bs. 5.1] — transcendental bliss, knowledge and eternity. I offer my respectful obeisances unto Him, who turns the dumb into eloquent speakers and enables the lame to cross mountains. Such is the mercy of the Lord.'"

PURPORT

This is a quotation from the Bhāvārtha-dīpikā commentary on Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam (1.1.1).
