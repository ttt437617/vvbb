Madhya-līlāChapter 2: The Ecstatic Manifestations of Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 2.59

tomāra darśana-vine, adhanya e rātri-dine,

ei kāla nā yāya kātana

tumi anāthera bandhu, apāra karunā-sindhu,

kripā kari' deha daraśana

SYNONYMS

tomāra — Your; darśana — audience; vine — without; adhanya — inauspicious; e — this; rātri-dine — night and day; ei kāla — this time; nā yāya — does not go; kātana — passing; tumi — You; anāthera bandhu — friend of the helpless; apāra — unlimited; karunā-sindhu — ocean of mercy; kripā kari' — showing mercy; deha — kindly give; daraśana — audience.

TRANSLATION

"All these inauspicious days and nights are not passing, for I have not met You. It is difficult to know how to pass all this time. But You are the friend of the helpless and an ocean of mercy. Kindly give Me Your audience, for I am in a precarious position."
