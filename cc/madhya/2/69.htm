Madhya-līlāChapter 2: The Ecstatic Manifestations of Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 2.69

tomāra capala-mati, ekatra nā haya sthiti,

tā'te tomāra nāhi kichu dosha

tumi ta' karunā-sindhu, āmāra parāna-bandhu,

tomāya nāhi mora kabhu rosha

SYNONYMS

tomāra — Your; capala-mati — restless mind; ekatra — in one place; nā — never; haya — is; sthiti — established; tā'te — in that; tomāra — Your; nāhi — there is not; kichu — any; dosha — fault; tumi — You are; ta' — certainly; karunā-sindhu — the ocean of mercy; āmāra — My; parāna-bandhu — friend of the heart; tomāya — toward You; nāhi — there is not; mora — My; kabhu — any time; rosha — anger.

TRANSLATION

"My dear Krishna, Your mind is always restless. You cannot remain in one place, but You are not at fault for this. You are actually the ocean of mercy, the friend of My heart. Therefore I have no reason to be angry with You.
