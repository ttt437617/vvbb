Madhya-līlāChapter 2: The Ecstatic Manifestations of Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 2.49

śuddha-prema-sukha-sindhu, pāi tāra eka bindu,

sei bindu jagat dubāya

kahibāra yogya naya, tathāpi bāule kaya,

kahile vā kebā pātiyāya

SYNONYMS

śuddha-prema — unalloyed love; sukha-sindhu — an ocean of happiness; pāi — if I get; tāra — of that; eka — one; bindu — drop; sei bindu — that drop; jagat — the whole world; dubāya — drowns; kahibāra — to speak; yogya naya — is not fit; tathāpi — still; bāule — a madman; kaya — speaks; kahile — if spoken; vā — or; kebā pātiyāya — who believes.

TRANSLATION

"Unalloyed love of Krishna is like an ocean of happiness. If someone gets one drop of it, the whole world can drown in that drop. It is not befitting to express such love of Godhead, yet a madman must speak. But even though he speaks, no one believes him."
