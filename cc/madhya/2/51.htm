Madhya-līlāChapter 2: The Ecstatic Manifestations of Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 2.51

ei premā-āsvādana, tapta-ikshu-carvana,

mukha jvale, nā yāya tyajana

sei premā yāńra mane, tāra vikrama sei jāne,

vishāmrite ekatra milana

SYNONYMS

ei — this; premā — love of Krishna; āsvādana — tasting; tapta — hot; ikshu-carvana — chewing sugarcane; mukha jvale — the mouth burns; nā yāya tyajana — still not possible to give up; sei — that; premā — love of Godhead; yāńra mane — in someone's mind; tāra — of that; vikrama — the power; sei jāne — he knows; visha-amrite — poison and nectar; ekatra — in oneness; milana — meeting.

TRANSLATION

If one tastes such love of Godhead, he can compare it to hot sugarcane. When one chews hot sugarcane, his mouth burns, yet he cannot give it up. Similarly, if one has but a little love of Godhead, he can perceive its powerful effects. It can only be compared to poison and nectar mixed together.
