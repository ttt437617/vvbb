Madhya-līlāChapter 2: The Ecstatic Manifestations of Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 2.29

vamśī-gānāmrita-dhāma, lāvanyāmrita-janma-sthāna,

ye nā dekhe se cāńda vadana

se nayane kibā kāja, paduka tāra munde vāja,

se nayana rahe ki kārana

SYNONYMS

vamśī-gāna-amrita-dhāma — the abode of the nectar derived from the songs of the flute; lāvanya-amrita-janma-sthāna — the birthplace of the nectar of beauty; ye — anyone who; nā — not; dekhe — sees; se — that; cāńda — moonlike; vadana — face; se — those; nayane — eyes; kibā kāja — what is the use; paduka — let there be; tāra — his; munde — on the head; vāja — thunderbolt; se — those; nayana — eyes; rahe — keeps; ki — what; kārana — reason.

TRANSLATION

"Of what use are the eyes of one who does not see the face of Krishna, which resembles the moon and is the birthplace of all beauty and the reservoir of the nectarean songs of His flute? Oh, let a thunderbolt strike his head! Why does he keep such eyes?

PURPORT

The moonlike face of Krishna is the reservoir of nectarean songs and the abode of His flute. It is also the root of all bodily beauty. The gopīs think that if their eyes are not engaged in seeing the beautiful face of Krishna, it would be better for them to be struck by a thunderbolt. For the gopīs, to see anything but Krishna is uninteresting and, indeed, detestable. The gopīs are never pleased to see anything but Krishna. The only solace for their eyes is the beautiful moonlike face of Krishna, the worshipful object of all senses. When they cannot see the beautiful face of Krishna, they actually see everything as vacant, and they desire to be struck by a thunderbolt. They do not find any reason to maintain their eyes when they are bereft of the beauty of Krishna.
