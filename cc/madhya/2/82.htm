Madhya-līlāChapter 2: The Ecstatic Manifestations of Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 2.82

ei gupta bhāva-sindhu, brahmā nā pāya eka bindu,

hena dhana vilāila samsāre

aiche dayālu avatāra, aiche dātā nāhi āra,

guna keha nāre varnibāre

SYNONYMS

ei — this; gupta — confidential; bhāva-sindhu — ocean of ecstasies; brahmā — Lord Brahmā; nā — does not; pāya — get; eka — one; bindu — drop; hena — such; dhana — wealth; vilāila — distributed; samsāre — all over the world; aiche — such; dayālu — merciful; avatāra — incarnation; aiche — such; dātā — charitable donor; nāhi — there is not; āra — anyone else; guna — this quality; keha — anyone; nāre — not able; varnibāre — to describe.

TRANSLATION

No one, not even Lord Brahmā, can ascertain or even taste a drop of this confidential ocean of ecstasy, but Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu, out of His causeless mercy, has distributed this love of Godhead all over the world. Thus there cannot be any incarnation more munificent than Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu. There is no greater donor. Who can describe His transcendental qualities?
