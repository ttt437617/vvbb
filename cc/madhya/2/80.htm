Madhya-līlāChapter 2: The Ecstatic Manifestations of Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 2.80

pūrve vraja-vilāse, yei tina abhilāshe,

yatneha āsvāda nā haila

śrī-rādhāra bhāva-sāra, āpane kari' ańgīkāra,

sei tina vastu āsvādila

SYNONYMS

pūrve — formerly; vraja-vilāse — in the pastimes of Vrindāvana; yei tina — those three; abhilāshe — in desires; yatneha — by great endeavor; āsvāda — taste; nā haila — there was not; śrī-rādhāra — of Śrīmatī Rādhārānī; bhāva-sāra — the essence of the ecstasy; āpane — personally; kari' — making; ańgīkāra — acceptance; sei — those; tina vastu — three subjects; āsvādila — tasted.

TRANSLATION

During His previous pastimes in Vrindāvana, Lord Krishna desired to enjoy the three different types of ecstasy, but despite great endeavor, He could not taste them. Such ecstasies are the monopoly of Śrīmatī Rādhārānī. Therefore, in order to taste them, Śrī Krishna accepted the position of Śrīmatī Rādhārānī in the form of Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu.
