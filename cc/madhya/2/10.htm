Madhya-līlāChapter 2: The Ecstatic Manifestations of Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 2.10

upavanodyāna dekhi' vrindāvana-jñāna

tāhāń yāi' nāce, gāya, kshane mūrcchā yā'na

SYNONYMS

upavana-udyāna — small parks; dekhi' — seeing; vrindāvana-jñāna — took them to be the forests of Vrindāvana; tāhāń — there; yāi' — going; nāce — dances; gāya — sings; kshane — in a moment; mūrcchā — unconsciousness; yā'na — goes.

TRANSLATION

Sometimes Caitanya Mahāprabhu mistook the small parks of the city for Vrindāvana. Sometimes He would go there, dance and chant and sometimes fall unconscious in spiritual ecstasy.
