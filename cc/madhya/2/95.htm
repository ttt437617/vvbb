Madhya-līlāChapter 2: The Ecstatic Manifestations of Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 2.95

pāñā yāńra ājñā-dhana, vrajera vaishnava-gana,

vandoń tāńra mukhya haridāsa

caitanya-vilāsa-sindhu- kallolera eka bindu,

tāra kanā kahe krishnadāsa

SYNONYMS

pāñā — getting; yāńra — whose; ājñā-dhana — order; vrajera — of Vrindāvana; vaishnava-gana — all the Vaishnavas; vandoń — I worship; tāńra — of them; mukhya — the chief; haridāsa — Haridāsa; caitanya-vilāsa-sindhu — of the ocean of the pastimes of Lord Caitanya; kallolera eka bindu — one drop of one wave; tāra — of it; kanā — a particle only; kahe — describes; krishnadāsa — Krishnadāsa Kavirāja Gosvāmī.

TRANSLATION

Receiving orders from the above authorities and the Vaishnavas of Vrindāvana, especially from Haridāsa, the priest of Govindajī, I, Krishnadāsa Kavirāja Gosvāmī, have tried to describe one small particle of one drop of one wave of the ocean of the pastimes of Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu.

Thus end the Bhaktivedanta purports to Śrī Caitanya-caritāmrita, Madhya-līlā, Second Chapter, describing the ecstatic manifestations of Lord Caitanya Mahāprabhu.
