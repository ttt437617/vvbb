Madhya-līlāChapter 2: The Ecstatic Manifestations of Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 2.6

roma-kūpe raktodgama, danta saba hāle

kshane ańga kshīna haya, kshane ańga phule

SYNONYMS

roma-kūpe — the pores of the body; rakta-udgama — exuding blood; danta — teeth; saba — all; hāle — loosen; kshane — in one moment; ańga — the whole body; kshīna — slender; haya — becomes; kshane — in another moment; ańga — the body; phule — fattens.

TRANSLATION

Blood flowed from all the pores of His body, and all His teeth were loosened. At one moment His whole body became slender, and at another moment His whole body became fat.
