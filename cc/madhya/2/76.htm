Madhya-līlāChapter 2: The Ecstatic Manifestations of Lord Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrī Caitanya Caritāmrita Madhya 2.76

guru — nānā bhāva-gana, śishya — prabhura tanu-mana,

nānā rīte satata nācāya

nirveda, vishāda, dainya, cāpalya, harsha, dhairya, manyu,

ei nritye prabhura kāla yāya

SYNONYMS

guru — the teacher; nānā — various; bhāva-gana — ecstasies; śishya — disciples; prabhura — of Lord Caitanya; tanu-mana — body and mind; nānā — various; rīte — in ways; satata — always; nācāya — causes to dance; nirveda — despondency; vishāda — moroseness; dainya — humility; cāpalya — restlessness; harsha — joy; dhairya — endurance; manyu — anger; ei — this; nritye — in dancing; prabhura — of the Lord; kāla — time; yāya — passes.

TRANSLATION

As the spiritual master chastises the disciple and teaches him the art of devotional service, so all the ecstatic symptoms of Lord Caitanya Mahāprabhu — including despondency, moroseness, humility, restlessness, joy, endurance and anger — instructed His body and mind. In this way, Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu passed His time.
