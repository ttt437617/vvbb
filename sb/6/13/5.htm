Canto 6: Prescribed Duties for MankindChapter 13: King Indra Afflicted by Sinful Reaction

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 6.13.5

indra uvāca

strī-bhū-druma-jalair eno

viśvarūpa-vadhodbhavam

vibhaktam anugrihnadbhir

vritra-hatyām kva mārjmy aham

SYNONYMS

indrah uvāca — King Indra replied; strī — by women; bhū — the earth; druma — the trees; jalaih — and water; enah — this (sin); viśvarūpa — of Viśvarūpa; vadha — from the killing; udbhavam — produced; vibhaktam — divided; anugrihnadbhih — showing their favor (to me); vritra-hatyām — the killing of Vritra; kva — how; mārjmi — shall become free from; aham — I.

TRANSLATION

King Indra replied: When I killed Viśvarūpa, I received extensive sinful reactions, but I was favored by the women, land, trees and water, and therefore I was able to divide the sin among them. But now if I kill Vritrāsura, another brāhmana, how shall I free myself from the sinful reactions?
