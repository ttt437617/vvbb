Canto 6: Prescribed Duties for MankindChapter 13: King Indra Afflicted by Sinful Reaction

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 6.13.10

śrī-śuka uvāca

evam sañcodito viprair

marutvān ahanad ripum

brahma-hatyā hate tasminn

āsasāda vrishākapim

SYNONYMS

śrī-śukah uvāca — Śrī Śukadeva Gosvāmī said; evam — thus; sañcoditah — being encouraged; vipraih — by the brāhmanas; marutvān — Indra; ahanat — killed; ripum — his enemy, Vritrāsura; brahma-hatyā — the sinful reaction for killing a brāhmana; hate — was killed; tasmin — when he (Vritrāsura); āsasāda — approached; vrishākapim — Indra, who is also named Vrishākapi.

TRANSLATION

Śrī Śukadeva Gosvāmī said: Encouraged by the words of the sages, Indra killed Vritrāsura, and when he was killed the sinful reaction for killing a brāhmana [brahma-hatyā] certainly took shelter of Indra.

PURPORT

After killing Vritrāsura, Indra could not surpass the brahma-hatyā, the sinful reactions for killing a brāhmana. Formerly he had killed one brāhmana, Viśvarūpa, out of circumstantial anger, but this time, following the advice of the sages, he killed another brāhmana purposely. Therefore the sinful reaction was greater than before. Indra could not be relieved from the reaction simply by performing sacrifices for atonement. He had to undergo a severe series of sinful reactions, and when he was freed by such suffering, the brāhmanas allowed him to perform the horse sacrifice. The planned execution of sinful deeds on the strength of chanting the holy name of the Lord or undergoing prāyaścitta, atonement, cannot give relief to anyone, even to Indra or Nahusha. Nahusha was officiating for Indra while Indra, absent from heaven, was going here and there to gain release from his sinful reactions.
