Canto 6: Prescribed Duties for MankindChapter 13: King Indra Afflicted by Sinful Reaction

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 6.13.1

śrī-śuka uvāca

vritre hate trayo lokā

vinā śakrena bhūrida

sapālā hy abhavan sadyo

vijvarā nirvritendriyāh

SYNONYMS

śrī-śukah uvāca — Śrī Śukadeva Gosvāmī said; vritre hate — when Vritrāsura was killed; trayah lokāh — the three planetary systems (upper, middle and lower); vinā — except; śakrena — Indra, who is also called Śakra; bhūri-da — O Mahārāja Parīkshit, giver of great charity; sa-pālāh — with the rulers of the various planets; hi — indeed; abhavan — became; sadyah — immediately; vijvarāh — without fear of death; nirvrita — very much pleased; indriyāh — whose senses.

TRANSLATION

Śrī Śukadeva Gosvāmī said: O King Parīkshit, who are so charitably disposed, when Vritrāsura was killed, all the presiding deities and everyone else in the three planetary systems was immediately pleased and free from trouble — everyone, that is, except Indra.
