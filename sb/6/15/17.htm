Canto 6: Prescribed Duties for MankindChapter 15: The Saints Nārada and Ańgirā Instruct King Citraketu

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 6.15.17

śrī-ańgirā uvāca

aham te putra-kāmasya

putrado 'smy ańgirā nripa

esha brahma-sutah sākshān

nārado bhagavān rishih

SYNONYMS

śrī-ańgirāh uvāca — the great sage Ańgirā said; aham — I; te — of you; putra-kāmasya — desiring to have a son; putra-dah — the giver of the son; asmi — am; ańgirāh — Ańgirā Rishi; nripa — O King; eshah — this; brahma-sutah — the son of Lord Brahmā; sākshāt — directly; nāradah — Nārada Muni; bhagavān — the most powerful; rishih — sage.

TRANSLATION

Ańgirā said: My dear King, when you desired to have a son, I approached you. Indeed, I am the same Ańgirā Rishi who gave you this son. As for this rishi, he is the great sage Nārada, the direct son of Lord Brahmā
