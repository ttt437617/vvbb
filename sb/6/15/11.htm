Canto 6: Prescribed Duties for MankindChapter 15: The Saints Nārada and Ańgirā Instruct King Citraketu

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 6.15.11

caranti hy avanau kāmam

brāhmanā bhagavat-priyāh

mādriśām grāmya-buddhīnām

bodhāyonmatta-lińginah

SYNONYMS

caranti — wander; hi — indeed; avanau — on the surface of the world; kāmam — according to desire; brāhmanāh — the brāhmanas; bhagavat-priyāh — who are also Vaishnavas, very dear to the Personality of Godhead; mā-driśām — of those like me; grāmya-buddhīnām — who are obsessed with temporary material consciousness; bodhāya — for the awakening; unmatta-lińginah — who dress as if madmen.

TRANSLATION

Brāhmanas who are exalted to the position of Vaishnavas, the most dear servants of Krishna, sometimes dress like madmen. Just to benefit materialists like us, who are always attached to sense gratification, and just to dissipate our ignorance, these Vaishnavas wander on the surface of the globe according to their desire.
