Canto 6: Prescribed Duties for MankindChapter 11: The Transcendental Qualities of Vritrāsura

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 6.11.19

sureśa kasmān na hinoshi vajram

purah sthite vairini mayy amogham

mā samśayishthā na gadeva vajrah

syān nishphalah kripanārtheva yācñā

SYNONYMS

sura-īśa — O King of the demigods; kasmāt — why; na — not; hinoshi — you hurl; vajram — the thunderbolt; purah sthite — standing in front; vairini — your enemy; mayi — at me; amogham — which is infallible (your thunderbolt); mā — do not; samśayishthāh — doubt; na — not; gadā iva — like the club; vajrah — the thunderbolt; syāt — may be; nishphalah — with no result; kripana — from a miserly person; arthā — for money; iva — like; yācñā — a request.

TRANSLATION

O King of the demigods, since I, your enemy, am standing before you, why don't you hurl your thunderbolt at me? Although your attack upon me with your club was certainly useless, like a request of money from a miser, the thunderbolt you carry will not be useless. You need have no doubts about this.

PURPORT

When King Indra threw his club at Vritrāsura, Vritrāsura caught it in his left hand and retaliated by using it to strike the head of Indra's elephant. Thus Indra's attack was a disastrous failure. Indeed, Indra's elephant was injured and thrown back fourteen yards. Therefore even though Indra stood with the thunderbolt to hurl against Vritrāsura, he was doubtful, thinking that the thunderbolt might also fail. Vritrāsura, however, being a Vaishnava, assured Indra that the thunderbolt would not fail, for Vritrāsura knew that it had been prepared in accordance with the instructions of Lord Vishnu. Although Indra had doubts because he could not understand that Lord Vishnu's order never fails, Vritrāsura understood Lord Vishnu's purpose. Vritrāsura was eager to be killed by the thunderbolt manufactured according to Lord Vishnu's instructions because he was sure that he would thus return home, back to Godhead. He was simply waiting for the opportunity of the thunderbolt's being released. In effect, therefore, Vritrāsura told Indra, "If you want to kill me, since I am your enemy, take this opportunity. Kill me. You will gain victory, and I shall go back to Godhead. Your deed will be equally beneficial for both of us. Do it immediately."
