Canto 6: Prescribed Duties for MankindChapter 11: The Transcendental Qualities of Vritrāsura

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 6.11.5

yadi vah pradhane śraddhā

sāram vā kshullakā hridi

agre tishthata mātram me

na ced grāmya-sukhe sprihā

SYNONYMS

yadi — if; vah — of you; pradhane — in battle; śraddhā — faith; sāram — patience; vā — or; kshullakāh — O insignificant ones; hridi — in the core of the heart; agre — in front; tishthata — just stand; mātram — for a moment; me — of me; na — not; cet — if; grāmya-sukhe — in sense gratification; sprihā — desire.

TRANSLATION

O insignificant demigods, if you truly have faith in your heroism, if you have patience in the cores of your hearts and if you are not ambitious for sense gratification, please stand before me for a moment.

PURPORT

Rebuking the demigods, Vritrāsura challenged, "O demigods, if you are actually heroes, stand before me now and try to show your prowess. If you do not wish to fight, if you are afraid of losing your lives, I shall not kill you, for unlike you, I am not so evil minded as to kill persons who are neither heroic nor willing to fight. If you have faith in your heroism, please stand before me."
