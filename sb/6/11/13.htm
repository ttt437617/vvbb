Canto 6: Prescribed Duties for MankindChapter 11: The Transcendental Qualities of Vritrāsura

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 6.11.13

sa tam nripendrāhava-kāmyayā ripum

vajrāyudham bhrātri-hanam vilokya

smaramś ca tat-karma nri-śamsam amhah

śokena mohena hasañ jagāda

SYNONYMS

sah — he (Vritrāsura); tam — him (the King of heaven, Indra); nripa-indra — O King Parīkshit; āhava-kāmyayā — with a desire to fight; ripum — his enemy; vajra-āyudham — whose weapon was the thunderbolt (made from the bones of Dadhīci); bhrātri-hanam — who was the killer of his brother; vilokya — seeing; smaran — remembering; ca — and; tat-karma — his activities; nri-śamsam — cruel; amhah — a great sin; śokena — with lamentation; mohena — by bewilderment; hasan — laughing; jagāda — said.

TRANSLATION

O King, when the great hero Vritrāsura saw Indra, his enemy, the killer of his brother, standing before him with a thunderbolt in his hand, desiring to fight, Vritrāsura remembered how Indra had cruelly killed his brother. Thinking of Indra's sinful activities, he became mad with lamentation and forgetfulness. Laughing sarcastically, he spoke as follows.
