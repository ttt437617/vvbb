Canto 6: Prescribed Duties for MankindChapter 11: The Transcendental Qualities of Vritrāsura

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 6.11.1

śrī-śuka uvāca

ta evam śamsato dharmam

vacah patyur acetasah

naivāgrihnanta sambhrāntāh

palāyana-parā nripa

SYNONYMS

śrī-śukah uvāca — Śrī Śukadeva Gosvāmī said; te — they; evam — thus; śamsatah — praising; dharmam — the principles of religion; vacah — the words; patyuh — of their master; acetasah — their minds being very disturbed; na — not; eva — indeed; agrihnanta — accepted; sambhrāntāh — fearful; palāyana-parāh — intent upon fleeing; nripa — O King.

TRANSLATION

Śrī Śukadeva Gosvāmī said: O King, Vritrāsura, the commander in chief of the demons, advised his lieutenants in the principles of religion, but the cowardly demoniac commanders, intent upon fleeing the battlefield, were so disturbed by fear that they could not accept his words.
