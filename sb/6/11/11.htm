Canto 6: Prescribed Duties for MankindChapter 11: The Transcendental Qualities of Vritrāsura

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 6.11.11

airāvato vritra-gadābhimrishto

vighūrnito 'drih kuliśāhato yathā

apāsarad bhinna-mukhah sahendro

muñcann asrik sapta-dhanur bhriśārtah

SYNONYMS

airāvatah — Airāvata, the elephant of King Indra; vritra-gadā-abhimrishtah — struck by the club in Vritrāsura's hand; vighūrnitah — shaken; adrih — a mountain; kuliśa — by a thunderbolt; āhatah — struck; yathā — just like; apāsarat — was pushed back; bhinna-mukhah — having a broken mouth; saha-indrah — with King Indra; muñcan — spitting; asrik — blood; sapta-dhanuh — a distance measured by seven bows (approximately fourteen yards); bhriśa — very severely; ārtah — aggrieved.

TRANSLATION

Struck with the club by Vritrāsura like a mountain struck by a thunderbolt, the elephant Airāvata, feeling great pain and spitting blood from its broken mouth, was pushed back fourteen yards. In great distress, the elephant fell, with Indra on its back.
