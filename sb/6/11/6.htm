Canto 6: Prescribed Duties for MankindChapter 11: The Transcendental Qualities of Vritrāsura

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 6.11.6

evam sura-ganān kruddho

bhīshayan vapushā ripūn

vyanadat sumahā-prāno

yena lokā vicetasah

SYNONYMS

evam — thus; sura-ganān — the demigods; kruddhah — being very angry; bhīshayan — terrifying; vapushā — by his body; ripūn — his enemies; vyanadat — roared; su-mahā-prānah — the most powerful Vritrāsura; yena — by which; lokāh — all people; vicetasah — unconscious.

TRANSLATION

Śukadeva Gosvāmī said: Vritrāsura, the angry and most powerful hero, terrified the demigods with his stout and strongly built body. When he roared with a resounding voice, nearly all living entities fainted.
