Canto 6: Prescribed Duties for MankindChapter 11: The Transcendental Qualities of Vritrāsura

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 6.11.2-3

viśīryamānām pritanām

āsurīm asurarshabhah

kālānukūlais tridaśaih

kālyamānām anāthavat

drishtvātapyata sańkruddha

indra-śatrur amarshitah

tān nivāryaujasā rājan

nirbhartsyedam uvāca ha

SYNONYMS

viśīryamānām — being shattered; pritanām — the army; āsurīm — of the demons; asura-rishabhah — the best of the asuras, Vritrāsura; kāla-anukūlaih — following the circumstances presented by time; tridaśaih — by the demigods; kālyamānām — being chased; anātha-vat — as if no one were there to protect them; drishtvā — seeing; atapyata — felt pain; sańkruddhah — being very angry; indra-śatruh — Vritrāsura, the enemy of Indra; amarshitah — unable to tolerate; tān — them (the demigods); nivārya — blocking; ojasā — with great force; rājan — O King Parīkshit; nirbhartsya — rebuking; idam — this; uvāca — said; ha — indeed.

TRANSLATION

O King Parīkshit, the demigods, taking advantage of a favorable opportunity presented by time, attacked the army of the demons from the rear and began driving away the demoniac soldiers, scattering them here and there as if their army had no leader. Seeing the pitiable condition of his soldiers, Vritrāsura, the best of the asuras, who was called Indraśatru, the enemy of Indra, was very much aggrieved. Unable to tolerate such reverses, he stopped and forcefully rebuked the demigods, speaking the following words in an angry mood.
