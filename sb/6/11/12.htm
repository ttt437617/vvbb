Canto 6: Prescribed Duties for MankindChapter 11: The Transcendental Qualities of Vritrāsura

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 6.11.12

na sanna-vāhāya vishanna-cetase

prāyuńkta bhūyah sa gadām mahātmā

indro 'mrita-syandi-karābhimarśa-

vīta-vyatha-kshata-vāho 'vatasthe

SYNONYMS

na — not; sanna — fatigued; vāhāya — upon him whose carrier; vishanna-cetase — morose in the core of his heart; prāyuńkta — used; bhūyah — again; sah — he (Vritrāsura); gadām — the club; mahā-ātmā — the great soul (who refrained from striking Indra with the club when he saw Indra morose and aggrieved); indrah — Indra; amrita-syandi-kara — of his hand, which produces nectar; abhimarśa — by the touch; vīta — was relieved; vyatha — from pains; kshata — and cuts; vāhah — whose carrier elephant; avatasthe — stood there.

TRANSLATION

When he saw Indra's carrier elephant thus fatigued and injured and when he saw Indra morose because his carrier had been harmed in that way, the great soul Vritrāsura, following religious principles, refrained from again striking Indra with the club. Taking this opportunity, Indra touched the elephant with his nectar-producing hand, thus relieving the animal's pain and curing its injuries. Then the elephant and Indra both stood silently.
