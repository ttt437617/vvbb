Canto 6: Prescribed Duties for MankindChapter 11: The Transcendental Qualities of Vritrāsura

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 6.11.16

śrī-hrī-dayā-kīrtibhir ujjhitam tvām

sva-karmanā purushādaiś ca garhyam

kricchrena mac-chūla-vibhinna-deham

asprishta-vahnim samadanti gridhrāh

SYNONYMS

śrī — opulence or beauty; hrī — shame; dayā — mercy; kīrtibhih — and glory; ujjhitam — bereft of; tvām — you; sva-karmanā — by your own activities; purusha-adaih — by the Rākshasas (man-eaters); ca — and; garhyam — condemnable; kricchrena — with great difficulty; mat-śūla — by my trident; vibhinna — pierced; deham — your body; asprishta-vahnim — not even touched by fire; samadanti — will eat; gridhrāh — the vultures.

TRANSLATION

Indra, you are bereft of all shame, mercy, glory and good fortune. Deprived of these good qualities by the reactions of your fruitive activities, you are to be condemned even by the man-eaters [Rākshasas]. Now I shall pierce your body with my trident, and after you die with great pain, even fire will not touch you; only the vultures will eat your body.
