Canto 6: Prescribed Duties for MankindChapter 11: The Transcendental Qualities of Vritrāsura

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 6.11.7

tena deva-ganāh sarve

vritra-visphotanena vai

nipetur mūrcchitā bhūmau

yathaivāśaninā hatāh

SYNONYMS

tena — by that; deva-ganāh — the demigods; sarve — all; vritra-visphotanena — the tumultuous sound of Vritrāsura; vai — indeed; nipetuh — fell; mūrcchitāh — fainted; bhūmau — on the ground; yathā — just as if; eva — indeed; aśaninā — by a thunderbolt; hatāh — struck.

TRANSLATION

When all the demigods heard Vritrāsura's tumultuous roar, which resembled that of a lion, they fainted and fell to the ground as if struck by thunderbolts.
