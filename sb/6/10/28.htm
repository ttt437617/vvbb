Canto 6: Prescribed Duties for MankindChapter 10: The Battle Between the Demigods and Vritrāsura

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 6.10.28

sarve prayāsā abhavan vimoghāh

kritāh kritā deva-ganeshu daityaih

krishnānukūleshu yathā mahatsu

kshudraih prayuktā ūshatī rūksha-vācah

SYNONYMS

sarve — all; prayāsāh — endeavors; abhavan — were; vimoghāh — futile; kritāh — performed; kritāh — again performed; deva-ganeshu — unto the demigods; daityaih — by the demons; krishna-anukūleshu — who were always protected by Krishna; yathā — just as; mahatsu — unto the Vaishnavas; kshudraih — by insignificant persons; prayuktāh — used; ūshatīh — unfavorable; rūksha — rough; vācah — words.

TRANSLATION

When insignificant persons use rough words to cast false, angry accusations against saintly persons, their fruitless words do not disturb the great personalities. Similarly, all the efforts of the demons against the demigods, who were favorably situated under the protection of Krishna, were futile.

PURPORT

There is a Bengali saying that if a vulture curses a cow to die, the curse will not be effective. Similarly, accusations made by demoniac persons against devotees of Krishna cannot have any effect. The demigods are devotees of Lord Krishna, and therefore the curses of the demons were futile.
