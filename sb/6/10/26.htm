Canto 6: Prescribed Duties for MankindChapter 10: The Battle Between the Demigods and Vritrāsura

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 6.10.26

atha kshīnāstra-śastraughā

giri-śrińga-drumopalaih

abhyavarshan sura-balam

cicchidus tāmś ca pūrvavat

SYNONYMS

atha — thereupon; kshīna — being reduced; astra — of the arrows released by mantras; śastra — and weapons; oghāh — the multitudes; giri — of mountains; śrińga — with the peaks; druma — with trees; upalaih — and with stones; abhyavarshan — showered; sura-balam — the soldiers of the demigods; cicchiduh — broke to pieces; tān — them; ca — and; pūrva-vat — as before.

TRANSLATION

As their weapons and mantras decreased, the demons began showering mountain peaks, trees and stones upon the demigod soldiers, but the demigods were so powerful and expert that they nullified all these weapons by breaking them to pieces in the sky as before.
