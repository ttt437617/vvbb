Canto 6: Prescribed Duties for MankindChapter 10: The Battle Between the Demigods and Vritrāsura

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 6.10.33

dvau sammatāv iha mrityū durāpau

yad brahma-sandhāranayā jitāsuh

kalevaram yoga-rato vijahyād

yad agranīr vīra-śaye 'nivrittah

SYNONYMS

dvau — two; sammatau — approved (by śāstra and great personalities); iha — in this world; mrityū — deaths; durāpau — extremely rare; yat — which; brahma-sandhāranayā — with concentration on Brahman, Paramātmā or Parabrahma, Krishna; jita-asuh — controlling the mind and senses; kalevaram — the body; yoga-ratah — being engaged in the performance of yoga; vijahyāt — one may leave; yat — which; agranīh — taking the lead; vīra-śaye — on the battlefield; anivrittah — not turning back.

TRANSLATION

There are two ways to meet a glorious death, and both are very rare. One is to die after performing mystic yoga, especially bhakti-yoga, by which one can control the mind and living force and die absorbed in thought of the Supreme Personality of Godhead. The second is to die on the battlefield, leading the army and never showing one's back. These two kinds of death are recommended in the śāstra as glorious.

Thus end the Bhaktivedanta purports of the Sixth Canto, Tenth Chapter, of the Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam, entitled "The Battle Between the Demigods and Vritrāsura."
