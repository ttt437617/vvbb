Canto 6: Prescribed Duties for MankindChapter 10: The Battle Between the Demigods and Vritrāsura

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 6.10.30

vritro 'surāms tān anugān manasvī

pradhāvatah prekshya babhāsha etat

palāyitam prekshya balam ca bhagnam

bhayena tīvrena vihasya vīrah

SYNONYMS

vritrah — Vritrāsura, the commander of the demons; asurān — all the demons; tān — them; anugān — his followers; manasvī — the great-minded; pradhāvatah — fleeing; prekshya — observing; babhāsha — spoke; etat — this; palāyitam — fleeing; prekshya — seeing; balam — army; ca — and; bhagnam — broken; bhayena — out of fear; tīvrena — intense; vihasya — smiling; vīrah — the great hero.

TRANSLATION

Seeing his army broken and all the asuras, even those known as great heroes, fleeing the battlefield out of intense fear, Vritrāsura, who was truly a great-minded hero, smiled and spoke the following words.
