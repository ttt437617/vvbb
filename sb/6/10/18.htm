Canto 6: Prescribed Duties for MankindChapter 10: The Battle Between the Demigods and Vritrāsura

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 6.10.17-18

rudrair vasubhir ādityair

aśvibhyām pitri-vahnibhih

marudbhir ribhubhih sādhyair

viśvedevair marut-patim

drishtvā vajra-dharam śakram

rocamānam svayā śriyā

nāmrishyann asurā rājan

mridhe vritra-purahsarāh

SYNONYMS

rudraih — by the Rudras; vasubhih — by the Vasus; ādityaih — by the Ādityas; aśvibhyām — by the Aśvinī-kumāras; pitri — by the Pitās; vahnibhih — and the Vahnis; marudbhih — by the Maruts; ribhubhih — by the Ribhus; sādhyaih — by the Sādhyas; viśve-devaih — by the Viśvadevas; marut-patim — Indra, the heavenly King; drishtvā — seeing; vajra-dharam — bearing the thunderbolt; śakram — another name of Indra; rocamānam — shining; svayā — by his own; śriyā — opulence; na — not; amrishyan — tolerated; asurāh — all the demons; rājan — O King; mridhe — in the fight; vritra-purahsarāh — headed by Vritrāsura.

TRANSLATION

O King, when all the asuras came onto the battlefield, headed by Vritrāsura, they saw King Indra carrying the thunderbolt and surrounded by the Rudras, Vasus, Ādityas, Aśvinī-kumāras, Pitās, Vahnis, Maruts, Ribhus, Sādhyas and Viśvadevas. Surrounded by his company, Indra shone so brightly that his effulgence was intolerable to the demons.
