Canto 6: Prescribed Duties for MankindChapter 10: The Battle Between the Demigods and Vritrāsura

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 6.10.29

te sva-prayāsam vitatham nirīkshya

harāv abhaktā hata-yuddha-darpāh

palāyanāyāji-mukhe visrijya

patim manas te dadhur ātta-sārāh

SYNONYMS

te — they (the demons); sva-prayāsam — their own endeavors; vitatham — fruitless; nirīkshya — seeing; harau abhaktāh — the asuras, those who are not devotees of the Supreme Personality of Godhead; hata — defeated; yuddha-darpāh — their pride in fighting; palāyanāya — for leaving the battlefield; āji-mukhe — in the very beginning of the battle; visrijya — leaving aside; patim — their commander, Vritrāsura; manah — their minds; te — all of them; dadhuh — gave; ātta-sārāh — whose prowess was taken away.

TRANSLATION

The asuras, who are never devotees of Lord Krishna, the Supreme Personality of Godhead, lost their pride in fighting when they found all their endeavors futile. Leaving aside their leader even in the very beginning of the fight, they decided to flee because all their prowess had been taken away by the enemy.
