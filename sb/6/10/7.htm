Canto 6: Prescribed Duties for MankindChapter 10: The Battle Between the Demigods and Vritrāsura

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 6.10.7

śrī-rishir uvāca

dharmam vah śrotu-kāmena

yūyam me pratyudāhritāh

esha vah priyam ātmānam

tyajantam santyajāmy aham

SYNONYMS

śrī-rishih uvāca — the great saint Dadhīci said; dharmam — the principles of religion; vah — from you; śrotu-kāmena — by the desire to hear; yūyam — you; me — by me; pratyudāhritāh — replied to the contrary; eshah — this; vah — for you; priyam — dear; ātmānam — body; tyajantam — leaving me anyway, today or tomorrow; santyajāmi — give up; aham — I.

TRANSLATION

The great sage Dadhīci said: Just to hear from you about religious principles, I refused to offer my body at your request. Now, although my body is extremely dear to me, I must give it up for your better purposes since I know that it will leave me today or tomorrow.
