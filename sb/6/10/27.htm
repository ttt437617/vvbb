Canto 6: Prescribed Duties for MankindChapter 10: The Battle Between the Demigods and Vritrāsura

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 6.10.27

tān akshatān svastimato niśāmya

śastrāstra-pūgair atha vritra-nāthāh

drumair drishadbhir vividhādri-śrińgair

avikshatāms tatrasur indra-sainikān

SYNONYMS

tān — them (the soldiers of the demigods); akshatān — not injured; svasti-matah — being very healthy; niśāmya — seeing; śastra-astra-pūgaih — by the bunches of weapons and mantras; atha — thereupon; vritra-nāthāh — the soldiers led by Vritrāsura; drumaih — by the trees; drishadbhih — by the stones; vividha — various; adri — of mountain s; śrińgaih — by the peaks; avikshatān — not injured; tatrasuh — became afraid; indra-sainikān — the soldiers of King Indra.

TRANSLATION

When the soldiers of the demons, commanded by Vritrāsura, saw that the soldiers of King Indra were quite well, having not been injured at all by their volleys of weapons, not even by the trees, stones and mountain peaks, the demons were very much afraid.
