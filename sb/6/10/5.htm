Canto 6: Prescribed Duties for MankindChapter 10: The Battle Between the Demigods and Vritrāsura

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 6.10.5

śrī-devā ūcuh

kim nu tad dustyajam brahman

pumsām bhūtānukampinām

bhavad-vidhānām mahatām

punya-ślokedya-karmanām

SYNONYMS

śrī-devāh ūcuh — the demigods said; kim — what; nu — indeed; tat — that; dustyajam — difficult to give up; brahman — O exalted brāhmana; pumsām — of persons; bhūta-anukampinām — who are very sympathetic toward the suffering living entities; bhavat-vidhānām — like Your Lordship; mahatām — who are very great; punya-śloka-īdya-karmanām — whose pious activities are praised by all great souls.

TRANSLATION

The demigods replied: O exalted brāhmana, pious persons like you, whose activities are praiseworthy, are very kind and affectionate to people in general. What can't such pious souls give for the benefit of others? They can give everything, including their bodies.
