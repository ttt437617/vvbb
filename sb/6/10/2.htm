Canto 6: Prescribed Duties for MankindChapter 10: The Battle Between the Demigods and Vritrāsura

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 6.10.2

tathābhiyācito devair

rishir ātharvano mahān

modamāna uvācedam

prahasann iva bhārata

SYNONYMS

tathā — in that manner; abhiyācitah — being begged; devaih — by the demigods; rishih — the great saintly person; ātharvanah — Dadhīci, the son of Atharvā; mahān — the great personality; modamānah — being merry; uvāca — said; idam — this; prahasan — smiling; iva — somewhat; bhārata — O Mahārāja Parīkshit.

TRANSLATION

O King Parīkshit, following the Lord's instructions, the demigods approached Dadhīci, the son of Atharvā. He was very liberal, and when they begged him to give them his body, he at once partially agreed. However, just to hear religious instructions from them, he smiled and jokingly spoke as follows.
