Canto 6: Prescribed Duties for MankindChapter 14: King Citraketu's Lamentation

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 6.14.41

dāsīnām ko nu santāpah

svāminah paricaryayā

abhīkshnam labdha-mānānām

dāsyā dāsīva durbhagāh

SYNONYMS

dāsīnām — of the maidservants; kah — what; nu — indeed; santāpah — lamentation; svāminah — unto the husband; paricaryayā — by rendering service; abhīkshnam — constantly; labdha-mānānām — honored; dāsyāh — of the maidservant; dāsī iva — like a maidservant; durbhagāh — most unfortunate.

TRANSLATION

Even maidservants who are constantly engaged in rendering service to the husband are honored by the husband, and thus they have nothing for which to lament. Our position, however, is that we are maidservants of the maidservant. Therefore we are most unfortunate.
