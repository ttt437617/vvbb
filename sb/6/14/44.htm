Canto 6: Prescribed Duties for MankindChapter 14: King Citraketu's Lamentation

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 6.14.44

kritadyutir ajānantī

sapatnīnām agham mahat

supta eveti sañcintya

nirīkshya vyacarad grihe

SYNONYMS

kritadyutih — Queen Kritadyuti; ajānantī — being unaware of; sapatnīnām — of her co-wives; agham — sinful act; mahat — very great; suptah — sleeping; eva — indeed; iti — thus; sañcintya — thinking; nirīkshya — looking at; vyacarat — was walking; grihe — at home.

TRANSLATION

Unaware of the poison administered by her co-wives, Queen Kritadyuti walked within the house, thinking that her son was sleeping deeply. She did not understand that he was dead.
