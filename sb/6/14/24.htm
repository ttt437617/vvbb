Canto 6: Prescribed Duties for MankindChapter 14: King Citraketu's Lamentation

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 6.14.24

tathāpi pricchato brūyām

brahmann ātmani cintitam

bhavato vidushaś cāpi

coditas tvad-anujñayā

SYNONYMS

tathāpi — still; pricchatah — asking; brūyām — let me speak; brahman — O great brāhmana; ātmani — in the mind; cintitam — anxiety; bhavatah — to you; vidushah — who know everything; ca — and; api — although; coditah — being inspired; tvat — your; anujñayā — by the order.

TRANSLATION

O great soul, you are aware of everything, yet you are asking me why I am full of anxiety. Therefore, in response to your order, let me disclose the cause.
