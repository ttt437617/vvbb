Canto 6: Prescribed Duties for MankindChapter 12: Vritrāsura's Glorious Death

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 6.12.18

śrī-śuka uvāca

indro vritra-vacah śrutvā

gatālīkam apūjayat

grihīta-vajrah prahasams

tam āha gata-vismayah

SYNONYMS

śrī-śukah uvāca — Śrī Śukadeva Gosvāmī said; indrah — King Indra; vritra-vacah — the words of Vritrāsura; śrutvā — hearing; gata-alīkam — without duplicity; apūjayat — worshiped; grihīta-vajrah — taking up the thunderbolt; prahasan — smiling; tam — unto Vritrāsura; āha — said; gata-vismayah — giving up his wonder.

TRANSLATION

Śukadeva Gosvāmī said: Hearing the straightforward, instructive words of Vritrāsura, King Indra praised him and again took the thunderbolt in his hand. Without bewilderment or duplicity, he then smiled and spoke to Vritrāsura as follows.

PURPORT

King Indra, the greatest of the demigods, was astonished to hear the instructions of Vritrāsura, who was supposed to be a demon. He was struck with wonder that a demon could speak so intelligently. Then he remembered great devotees like Prahlāda Mahārāja and Bali Mahārāja, who had been born in the families of demons, and thus he came to his senses. Even so-called demons sometimes have exalted devotion for the Supreme Personality of Godhead. Therefore Indra smiled reassuringly at Vritrāsura.
