Canto 6: Prescribed Duties for MankindChapter 12: Vritrāsura's Glorious Death

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 6.12.34

tadā ca khe dundubhayo vinedur

gandharva-siddhāh samaharshi-sańghāh

vārtra-ghna-lińgais tam abhishtuvānā

mantrair mudā kusumair abhyavarshan

SYNONYMS

tadā — at that time; ca — also; khe — in the higher planetary systems in the sky; dundubhayah — the kettledrums; vineduh — sounded; gandharva — the Gandharvas; siddhāh — and the Siddhas; sa-maharshi-sańghāh — with the assembly of saintly persons; vārtra-ghna-lińgaih — celebrating the prowess of the killer of Vritrāsura; tam — him (Indra); abhishtuvānāh — praising; mantraih — by various mantras; mudā — with great pleasure; kusumaih — with flowers; abhyavarshan — showered.

TRANSLATION

When Vritrāsura was killed, the Gandharvas and Siddhas in the heavenly planets beat kettledrums in jubilation. With Vedic hymns they celebrated the prowess of Indra, the killer of Vritrāsura, praising Indra and showering flowers upon him with great pleasure.
