Canto 6: Prescribed Duties for MankindChapter 12: Vritrāsura's Glorious Death

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 6.12.33

vajras tu tat-kandharam āśu-vegah

krintan samantāt parivartamānah

nyapātayat tāvad ahar-ganena

yo jyotishām ayane vārtra-hatye

SYNONYMS

vajrah — the thunderbolt; tu — but; tat-kandharam — his neck; āśu-vegah — although very fast; krintan — cutting; samantāt — all around; parivartamānah — revolving; nyapātayat — caused to fall; tāvat — so many; ahah-ganena — by days; yah — which; jyotishām — of the luminaries like the sun and moon; ayane — in moving to both sides of the equator; vārtra-hatye — at the time suitable for killing Vritrāsura.

TRANSLATION

Although the thunderbolt revolved around Vritrāsura's neck with great speed, separating his head from his body took one complete year — 360 days, the time in which the sun, moon and other luminaries complete a northern and southern journey. Then, at the suitable time for Vritrāsura to be killed, his head fell to the ground.
