Canto 6: Prescribed Duties for MankindChapter 12: Vritrāsura's Glorious Death

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 6.12.30

vritra-grastam tam ālokya

saprajāpatayah surāh

hā kashtam iti nirvinnāś

cukruśuh samaharshayah

SYNONYMS

vritra-grastam — swallowed by Vritrāsura; tam — him (Indra); ālokya — seeing; sa-prajāpatayah — with Lord Brahmā and other prajāpatis; surāh — all the demigods; hā — alas; kashtam — what a tribulation; iti — thus; nirvinnāh — being very morose; cukruśuh — lamented; sa-mahā-rishayah — with the great sages.

TRANSLATION

When the demigods, along with Brahmā, other prajāpatis and other great saintly persons, saw that Indra had been swallowed by the demon, they became very morose. "Alas," they lamented. "What a calamity ! What a calamity !"
