Canto 6: Prescribed Duties for MankindChapter 12: Vritrāsura's Glorious Death

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 6.12.24

āvidhya parigham vritrah

kārshnāyasam arindamah

indrāya prāhinod ghoram

vāma-hastena mārisha

SYNONYMS

āvidhya — whirling; parigham — the club; vritrah — Vritrāsura; kārshna-ayasam — made of iron; arim-damah — who was competent to subdue his enemy; indrāya — at Indra; prāhinot — threw; ghoram — very fearful; vāma-hastena — with his left hand; mārisha — O best of kings, Mahārāja Parīkshit.

TRANSLATION

O Mahārāja Parīkshit, Vritrāsura, who was completely able to subdue his enemy, took his iron club, whirled it around, aimed it at Indra and then threw it at him with his left hand.
