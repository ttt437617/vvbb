Canto 6: Prescribed Duties for MankindChapter 12: Vritrāsura's Glorious Death

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 6.12.25

sa tu vritrasya parigham

karam ca karabhopamam

ciccheda yugapad devo

vajrena śata-parvanā

SYNONYMS

sah — he (King Indra); tu — however; vritrasya — of Vritrāsura; parigham — the iron club; karam — his hand; ca — and; karabha-upamam — as strong as the trunk of an elephant; ciccheda — cut to pieces; yugapat — simultaneously; devah — Lord Indra; vajrena — with the thunderbolt; śata-parvanā — having one hundred joints.

TRANSLATION

With his thunderbolt named Śataparvan, Indra simultaneously cut to pieces Vritrāsura's club and his remaining hand.
