Canto 6: Prescribed Duties for MankindChapter 6: The Progeny of the Daughters of Daksha

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 6.6.29-31

arishtāyās tu gandharvāh

kāshthāyā dviśaphetarāh

sutā danor eka-shashtis

teshām prādhānikāñ śrinu

dvimūrdhā śambaro 'rishto

hayagrīvo vibhāvasuh

ayomukhah śańkuśirāh

svarbhānuh kapilo 'runah

pulomā vrishaparvā ca

ekacakro 'nutāpanah

dhūmrakeśo virūpāksho

vipracittiś ca durjayah

SYNONYMS

arishtāyāh — from the womb of Arishtā; tu — but; gandharvāh — the Gandharvas; kāshthāyāh — from the womb of Kāshthā; dvi-śapha-itarāh — animals such as horses, which do not have cloven hooves; sutāh — sons; danoh — from the womb of Danu; eka-shashtih — sixty-one; teshām — of them; prādhānikān — the important ones; śrinu — hear; dvimūrdhā — Dvimūrdhā; śambarah — Śambara; arishtah — Arishta; hayagrīvah — Hayagrīva; vibhāvasuh — Vibhāvasu; ayomukhah — Ayomukha; śańkuśirāh — Śańkuśirā; svarbhānuh — Svarbhānu; kapilah — Kapila; arunah — Aruna; pulomā — Pulomā; vrishaparvā — Vrishaparvā; ca — also; ekacakrah — Ekacakra; anutāpanah — Anutāpana; dhūmrakeśah — Dhūmrakeśa; virūpākshah — Virūpāksha; vipracittih — Vipracitti; ca — and; durjayah — Durjaya.

TRANSLATION

The Gandharvas were born from the womb of Arishtā, and animals whose hooves are not split, such as the horse, were born from the womb of Kāshthā. O King, from the womb of Danu came sixty-one sons, of whom these eighteen were very important: Dvimūrdhā, Śambara, Arishta, Hayagrīva, Vibhāvasu, Ayomukha, Śańkuśirā, Svarbhānu, Kapila, Aruna, Pulomā, Vrishaparvā, Ekacakra, Anutāpana, Dhūmrakeśa, Virūpāksha, Vipracitti and Durjaya.
