Canto 6: Prescribed Duties for MankindChapter 6: The Progeny of the Daughters of Daksha

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 6.6.15

vāstor āńgirasī-putro

viśvakarmākritī-patih

tato manuś cākshusho 'bhūd

viśve sādhyā manoh sutāh

SYNONYMS

vāstoh — of Vāstu; āńgirasī — of his wife named Āńgirasī; putrah — the son; viśvakarmā — Viśvakarmā; ākritī-patih — the husband of Ākritī; tatah — from them; manuh cākshushah — the Manu named Cākshusha; abhūt — was born; viśve — the Viśvadevas; sādhyāh — the Sādhyas; manoh — of Manu; sutāh — the sons.

TRANSLATION

From Āńgirasī, the wife of the Vasu named Vāstu, was born the great architect Viśvakarmā. Viśvakarmā became the husband of Ākritī, from whom the Manu named Cākshusha was born. The sons of Manu were known as the Viśvadevas and Sādhyas.
