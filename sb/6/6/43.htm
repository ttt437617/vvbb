Canto 6: Prescribed Duties for MankindChapter 6: The Progeny of the Daughters of Daksha

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 6.6.43

pūshānapatyah pishtādo

bhagna-danto 'bhavat purā

yo 'sau dakshāya kupitam

jahāsa vivrita-dvijah

SYNONYMS

pūshā — Pūshā; anapatyah — without children; pishta-adah — who lives by eating flour; bhagna-dantah — with broken teeth; abhavat — became; purā — formerly; yah — who; asau — that; dakshāya — at Daksha; kupitam — very angry; jahāsa — laughed; vivrita-dvijah — uncovering his teeth.

TRANSLATION

Pūshā had no sons. When Lord Śiva was angry at Daksha, Pūshā had laughed at Lord Śiva and shown his teeth. Therefore he lost his teeth and had to live by eating only ground flour.
