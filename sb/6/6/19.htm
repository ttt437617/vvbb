Canto 6: Prescribed Duties for MankindChapter 6: The Progeny of the Daughters of Daksha

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 6.6.19

prajāpater ańgirasah

svadhā patnī pitrin atha

atharvāńgirasam vedam

putratve cākarot satī

SYNONYMS

prajāpateh ańgirasah — of another prajāpati, known as Ańgirā; svadhā — Svadhā; patnī — his wife; pitrin — the Pitās; atha — thereafter; atharva-āńgirasam — Atharvāńgirasa; vedam — the personified Veda; putratve — as the son; ca — and; akarot — accepted; satī — Satī.

TRANSLATION

The prajāpati Ańgirā had two wives, named Svadhā and Satī. The wife named Svadhā accepted all the Pitās as her sons, and Satī accepted the Atharvāńgirasa Veda as her son.
