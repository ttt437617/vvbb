Canto 6: Prescribed Duties for MankindChapter 6: The Progeny of the Daughters of Daksha

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 6.6.10-11

sańkalpāyās tu sańkalpah

kāmah sańkalpajah smritah

vasavo 'shtau vasoh putrās

teshām nāmāni me śrinu

dronah prāno dhruvo 'rko 'gnir

dosho vāstur vibhāvasuh

dronasyābhimateh patnyā

harsha-śoka-bhayādayah

SYNONYMS

sańkalpāyāh — from the womb of Sańkalpā; tu — but; sańkalpah — Sańkalpa; kāmah — Kāma; sańkalpa-jah — the son of Sańkalpā; smritah — known; vasavah ashtau — the eight Vasus; vasoh — of Vasu; putrāh — the sons; teshām — of them; nāmāni — the names; me — from me; śrinu — just hear; dronah — Drona; prānah — Prāna; dhruvah — Dhruva; arkah — Arka; agnih — Agni; doshah — Dosha; vāstuh — Vāstu; vibhāvasuh — Vibhāvasu; dronasya — of Drona; abhimateh — from Abhimati; patnyāh — the wife; harsha-śoka-bhaya-ādayah — the sons named Harsha, Śoka, Bhaya and so on.

TRANSLATION

The son of Sańkalpā was known as Sańkalpa, and from him lust was born. The sons of Vasu were known as the eight Vasus. Just hear their names from me: Drona, Prāna, Dhruva, Arka, Agni, Dosha, Vāstu and Vibhāvasu. From Abhimati, the wife of the Vasu named Drona, were generated the sons named Harsha, Śoka, Bhaya and so on.
