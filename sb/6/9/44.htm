Canto 6: Prescribed Duties for MankindChapter 9: Appearance of the Demon Vritrāsura

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 6.9.44

atho īśa jahi tvāshtram

grasantam bhuvana-trayam

grastāni yena nah krishna

tejāmsy astrāyudhāni ca

SYNONYMS

atho — therefore; īśa — O supreme controller; jahi — kill; tvāshtram — the demon Vritrāsura, son of Tvashtā; grasantam — who is devouring; bhuvana-trayam — the three worlds; grastāni — devoured; yena — by whom; nah — our; krishna — O Lord Krishna; tejāmsi — all strength and prowess; astra — arrows; āyudhāni — and other weapons; ca — also.

TRANSLATION

Therefore, O Lord, O supreme controller, O Lord Krishna, please annihilate this dangerous demon Vritrāsura, Tvashtā's son, who has already swallowed all our weapons, our paraphernalia for fighting, and our strength and influence.

PURPORT

The Lord says in Bhagavad-gītā (7.15-16):

na mām dushkritino mūdhāh

prapadyante narādhamāh

māyayāpahrita-jñānā

āsuram bhāvam āśritāh

catur-vidhā bhajante mām

janāh sukritino 'rjuna

ārto jijñāsur arthārthī

jñānī ca bharatarshabha

"Those miscreants who are grossly foolish, lowest among mankind, whose knowledge is stolen by illusion, and who partake of the atheistic nature of demons, do not surrender unto Me. O best among the Bhāratas [Arjuna], four kinds of pious men render devotional service unto Me — the distressed, the desirer of wealth, the inquisitive, and he who is searching for knowledge of the Absolute."

The four classes of neophyte devotees who approach the Supreme Personality of Godhead to offer devotional service because of material motives are not pure devotees, but the advantage for such materialistic devotees is that they sometimes give up their material desires and become pure. When the demigods are utterly helpless, they approach the Supreme Personality of Godhead in grief and with tears in their eyes, praying to the Lord, and thus they become almost pure devotees, free from material desires. Admitting that they have forgotten pure devotional service because of extensive material opportunities, they fully surrender to the Lord, leaving to His consideration whether to maintain them or annihilate them. Such surrender is necessary. Bhaktivinoda Thākura sings, mārabi rākhabi — yo icchā tohārā: "O Lord, I fully surrender unto Your lotus feet. Now, as You desire, You may protect me or annihilate me. You have the full right to do either."
