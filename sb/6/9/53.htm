Canto 6: Prescribed Duties for MankindChapter 9: Appearance of the Demon Vritrāsura

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 6.9.53

dadhyańń ātharvanas tvashtre

varmābhedyam mad-ātmakam

viśvarūpāya yat prādāt

tvashtā yat tvam adhās tatah

SYNONYMS

dadhyań — Dadhyañca; ātharvanah — the son of Atharvā; tvashtre — unto Tvashtā; varma — the protective covering known as Nārāyana-kavaca; abhedyam — invincible; mat-ātmakam — consisting of Myself; viśvarūpāya — unto Viśvarūpa; yat — which; prādāt — delivered; tvashtā — Tvashtā; yat — which; tvam — you; adhāh — received; tatah — from him.

TRANSLATION

Dadhyañca's invincible protective covering known as the Nārāyana-kavaca was given to Tvashtā, who delivered it to his son Viśvarūpa, from whom you have received it. Because of this Nārāyana-kavaca, Dadhīci's body is now very strong. You should therefore beg him for his body.
