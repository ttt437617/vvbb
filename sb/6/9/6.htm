Canto 6: Prescribed Duties for MankindChapter 9: Appearance of the Demon Vritrāsura

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 6.9.6

brahma-hatyām añjalinā

jagrāha yad apīśvarah

samvatsarānte tad agham

bhūtānām sa viśuddhaye

bhūmy-ambu-druma-yoshidbhyaś

caturdhā vyabhajad dharih

SYNONYMS

brahma-hatyām — the sinful reaction for killing a brāhmana; añjalinā — with folded hands; jagrāha — assumed the responsibility for; yat api — although; īśvarah — very powerful; samvatsara-ante — after one year; tat agham — that sinful reaction; bhūtānām — of the material elements; sah — he; viśuddhaye — for purification; bhūmi — unto the earth; ambu — water; druma — trees; yoshidbhyah — and unto women; caturdhā — in four divisions; vyabhajat — divided; harih — King Indra.

TRANSLATION

Although Indra was so powerful that he could neutralize the sinful reactions for killing a brāhmana, he repentantly accepted the burden of these reactions with folded hands. He suffered for one year, and then to purify himself he distributed the reactions for this sinful killing among the earth, water, trees and women.
