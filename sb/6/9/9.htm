Canto 6: Prescribed Duties for MankindChapter 9: Appearance of the Demon Vritrāsura

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 6.9.9

śaśvat-kāma-varenāmhas

turīyam jagrihuh striyah

rajo-rūpena tāsv amho

māsi māsi pradriśyate

SYNONYMS

śaśvat — perpetual; kāma — of sexual desire; varena — because of the benediction; amhah — the sinful reaction for killing a brāhmana; turīyam — one fourth; jagrihuh — accepted; striyah — women; rajah-rūpena — in the form of the menstrual period; tāsu — in them; amhah — the sinful reaction; māsi māsi — every month; pradriśyate — is visible.

TRANSLATION

In return for Lord Indra's benediction that they would be able to enjoy lusty desires continuously, even during pregnancy for as long as sex is not injurious to the embryo, women accepted one fourth of the sinful reactions. As a result of those reactions, women manifest the signs of menstruation every month.

PURPORT

Women as a class are very lusty, and apparently their continuous lusty desires are never satisfied. In return for Lord Indra's benediction that there would be no cessation to their lusty desires, women accepted one fourth of the sinful reactions for killing a brāhmana.
