Canto 6: Prescribed Duties for MankindChapter 9: Appearance of the Demon Vritrāsura

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 6.9.2

sa vai barhishi devebhyo

bhāgam pratyaksham uccakaih

adadad yasya pitaro

devāh sapraśrayam nripa

SYNONYMS

sah — he (Viśvarūpa); vai — indeed; barhishi — in the sacrificial fire; devebhyah — unto the particular demigods; bhāgam — the proper share; pratyaksham — visibly; uccakaih — by loud chanting of the mantras; adadat — offered; yasya — of whom; pitarah — the fathers; devāh — demigods; sa-praśrayam — very humbly in a gentle voice; nripa — O King Parīkshit.

TRANSLATION

O Mahārāja Parīkshit, the demigods were related to Viśvarūpa from his father's side, and therefore he visibly offered clarified butter in the fire while chanting mantras such as indrāya idam svāhā ["this is meant for King Indra"] and idam agnaye ["this is for the demigod of fire"]. He loudly chanted these mantras and offered each of the demigods his proper share.
