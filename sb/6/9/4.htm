Canto 6: Prescribed Duties for MankindChapter 9: Appearance of the Demon Vritrāsura

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 6.9.4

tad deva-helanam tasya

dharmālīkam sureśvarah

ālakshya tarasā bhītas

tac-chīrshāny acchinad rushā

SYNONYMS

tat — that; deva-helanam — offense to the demigods; tasya — of him (Viśvarūpa); dharma-alīkam — cheating in religious principles (pretending to be the priest of the demigods, but secretly acting as the priest of the demons also); sura-īśvarah — the king of the demigods; ālakshya — observing; tarasā — quickly; bhītah — being afraid (that the demons would gain strength by being blessed by Viśvarūpa); tat — his (Viśvarūpa's); śīrshāni — heads; acchinat — cut off; rushā — with great anger.

TRANSLATION

Once upon a time, however, the King of heaven, Indra, understood that Viśvarūpa was secretly cheating the demigods by offering oblations on behalf of the demons. He became extremely afraid of being defeated by the demons, and in great anger at Viśvarūpa he cut Viśvarūpa's three heads from his shoulders.
