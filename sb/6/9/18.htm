Canto 6: Prescribed Duties for MankindChapter 9: Appearance of the Demon Vritrāsura

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 6.9.18

yenāvritā ime lokās

tapasā tvāshtra-mūrtinā

sa vai vritra iti proktah

pāpah parama-dārunah

SYNONYMS

yena — by whom; āvritāh — covered; ime — all these; lokāh — planets; tapasā — by the austerity; tvāshtra-mūrtinā — in the form of the son of Tvashtā; sah — he; vai — indeed; vritrah — Vritra; iti — thus; proktah — called; pāpah — personified sin; parama-dārunah — very fearful.

TRANSLATION

That very fearful demon, who was actually the son of Tvashtā, covered all the planetary systems by dint of austerity. Therefore he was named Vritra, or one who covers everything.

PURPORT

In the Vedas it is said, sa imān lokān āvrinot tad vritrasya vritratvam: because the demon covered all the planetary systems, his name was Vritrāsura.
