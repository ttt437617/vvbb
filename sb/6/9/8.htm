Canto 6: Prescribed Duties for MankindChapter 9: Appearance of the Demon Vritrāsura

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 6.9.8

turyam cheda-virohena

varena jagrihur drumāh

teshām niryāsa-rūpena

brahma-hatyā pradriśyate

SYNONYMS

turyam — one fourth; cheda — although being cut; virohena — of growing again; varena — because of the benediction; jagrihuh — accepted; drumāh — the trees; teshām — of them; niryāsa-rūpena — by the liquid oozing from the trees; brahma-hatyā — the reaction for killing a brāhmana; pradriśyate — is visible.

TRANSLATION

In return for Indra's benediction that their branches and twigs would grow back when trimmed, the trees accepted one fourth of the reactions for killing a brāhmana. These reactions are visible in the flowing of sap from trees. [Therefore one is forbidden to drink this sap.]
