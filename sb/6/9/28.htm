Canto 6: Prescribed Duties for MankindChapter 9: Appearance of the Demon Vritrāsura

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 6.9.28

śrī-śuka uvāca

iti teshām mahārāja

surānām upatishthatām

pratīcyām diśy abhūd āvih

śańkha-cakra-gadā-dharah

SYNONYMS

śrī-śukah uvāca — Śrī Śukadeva Gosvāmī said; iti — thus; teshām — of them; mahārāja — O King; surānām — of the demigods; upatishthatām — praying; pratīcyām — inside; diśi — in the direction; abhūt — became; āvih — visible; śańkha-cakra-gadā-dharah — bearing the transcendental weapons: the conchshell, disc and club.

TRANSLATION

Śrī Śukadeva Gosvāmī said: My dear King, when all the demigods offered Him their prayers, the Supreme Personality of Godhead, Lord Hari, carrying His weapons, the conchshell, disc and club, appeared first within their hearts and then before them.
