Canto 6: Prescribed Duties for MankindChapter 9: Appearance of the Demon Vritrāsura

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 6.9.3

sa eva hi dadau bhāgam

paroksham asurān prati

yajamāno 'vahad bhāgam

mātri-sneha-vaśānugah

SYNONYMS

sah — he (Viśvarūpa); eva — indeed; hi — certainly; dadau — offered; bhāgam — share; paroksham — without the knowledge of the demigods; asurān — the demons; prati — unto; yajamānah — performing sacrifice; avahat — offered; bhāgam — share; mātri-sneha — by affection for his mother; vaśa-anugah — being compelled.

TRANSLATION

Although offering clarified butter in the sacrificial fire in the name of the demigods, without the knowledge of the demigods he also offered oblations to the demons because they were his relatives through his mother.

PURPORT

Because of Viśvarūpa's affection for the families of both the demigods and the demons, he appeased the Supreme Lord on behalf of both dynasties. When he offered oblations in the fire on behalf of the asuras, he did so secretly. without the knowledge of the demigods.
