Canto 6: Prescribed Duties for MankindChapter 7: Indra Offends His Spiritual Master, Brihaspati.

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 6.7.19

tair visrishteshubhis tīkshnair

nirbhinnāńgoru-bāhavah

brahmānam śaranam jagmuh

sahendrā nata-kandharāh

SYNONYMS

taih — by them (the demons); visrishta — thrown; ishubhih — by the arrows; tīkshnaih — very sharp; nirbhinna — pierced all over; ańga — bodies; uru — thighs; bāhavah — and arms; brahmānam — of Lord Brahmā; śaranam — the shelter; jagmuh — approached; saha-indrāh — with King Indra; nata-kandharāh — their heads bent downward.

TRANSLATION

The demigods' heads, thighs and arms and the other parts of their bodies were injured by the sharp arrows of the demons. The demigods, headed by Indra, saw no other course than to immediately approach Lord Brahmā with bowed heads for shelter and proper instruction.
