Canto 6: Prescribed Duties for MankindChapter 7: Indra Offends His Spiritual Master, Brihaspati.

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 6.7.26

śrī-śuka uvāca

ta evam uditā rājan

brahmanā vigata-jvarāh

rishim tvāshtram upavrajya

parishvajyedam abruvan

SYNONYMS

śrī-śukah uvāca — Śukadeva Gosvāmī said; te — all the demigods; evam — thus; uditāh — being advised; rājan — O King Parīkshit; brahmanā — by Lord Brahmā; vigata-jvarāh — being relieved from the aggrievement caused by the demons; rishim — the great sage; tvāshtram — to the son of Tvashtā; upavrajya — going; parishvajya — embracing; idam — this; abruvan — spoke.

TRANSLATION

Śrīla Śukadeva Gosvāmī continued: Thus advised by Lord Brahmā and relieved of their anxiety, all the demigods went to the sage Viśvarūpa, the son of Tvashtā. My dear King, they embraced him and spoke as follows.
