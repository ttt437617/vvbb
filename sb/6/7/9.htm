Canto 6: Prescribed Duties for MankindChapter 7: Indra Offends His Spiritual Master, Brihaspati.

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 6.7.9

tato nirgatya sahasā

kavir āńgirasah prabhuh

āyayau sva-griham tūshnīm

vidvān śrī-mada-vikriyām

SYNONYMS

tatah — thereafter; nirgatya — going out; sahasā — suddenly; kavih — the great learned sage; āńgirasah — Brihaspati; prabhuh — the master of the demigods; āyayau — returned; sva-griham — to his home; tūshnīm — being silent; vidvān — having known; śrī-mada-vikriyām — deterioration because of madness due to opulence.

TRANSLATION

Brihaspati knew everything that would happen in the future. Seeing Indra's transgression of etiquette, he completely understood that Indra was puffed up by his material opulence. Although able to curse Indra, he did not do so. Instead, he left the assembly and in silence returned to his home.
