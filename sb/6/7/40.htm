Canto 6: Prescribed Duties for MankindChapter 7: Indra Offends His Spiritual Master, Brihaspati.

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 6.7.40

yayā guptah sahasrāksho

jigye 'sura-camūr vibhuh

tām prāha sa mahendrāya

viśvarūpa udāra-dhīh

SYNONYMS

yayā — by which; guptah — protected; sahasra-akshah — the thousand-eyed demigod, Indra; jigye — conquered; asura — of the demons; camūh — military power; vibhuh — becoming very powerful; tām — that; prāha — spoke; sah — he; mahendrāya — unto the King of heaven, Mahendra; viśvarūpah — Viśvarūpa; udāra-dhīh — very broad-minded.

TRANSLATION

Viśvarūpa, who was most liberal, spoke to King Indra [Sahasrāksha] the secret hymn that protected Indra and conquered the military power of the demons.

Thus end the Bhaktivedanta purports to the Sixth Canto, Seventh Chapter, of the Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam, entitled "Indra Offends His Spiritual Master, Brihaspati."
