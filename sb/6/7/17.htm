Canto 6: Prescribed Duties for MankindChapter 7: Indra Offends His Spiritual Master, Brihaspati.

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 6.7.17

guror nādhigatah samjñām

parīkshan bhagavān svarāt

dhyāyan dhiyā surair yuktah

śarma nālabhatātmanah

SYNONYMS

guroh — of his spiritual master; na — not; adhigatah — finding; samjñām — trace; parīkshan — searching vigorously all around; bhagavān — the most powerful Indra; svarāt — independent; dhyāyan — meditating; dhiyā — by wisdom; suraih — by the demigods; yuktah — surrounded; śarma — peace; na — not; alabhata — obtained; ātmanah — of the mind.

TRANSLATION

Although Indra searched vigorously with the assistance of the other demigods, he could not find Brihaspati. Then Indra thought, "Alas, my spiritual master has become dissatisfied with me, and now I have no means of achieving good fortune." Although Indra was surrounded by demigods, he could not find peace of mind.
