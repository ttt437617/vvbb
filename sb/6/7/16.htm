Canto 6: Prescribed Duties for MankindChapter 7: Indra Offends His Spiritual Master, Brihaspati.

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 6.7.16

evam cintayatas tasya

maghono bhagavān grihāt

brihaspatir gato 'drishtām

gatim adhyātma-māyayā

SYNONYMS

evam — thus; cintayatah — while thinking very seriously; tasya — he; maghonah — Indra; bhagavān — the most powerful; grihāt — from his home; brihaspatih — Brihaspati; gatah — went; adrishtām — invisible; gatim — to a state; adhyātma — due to being highly elevated in spiritual consciousness; māyayā — by his potency.

TRANSLATION

While Indra, the King of the demigods, thought in this way and repented in his own assembly, Brihaspati, the most powerful spiritual master, understood his mind. Thus he became invisible to Indra and left home, for Brihaspati was spiritually more powerful than King Indra.
