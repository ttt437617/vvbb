Canto 6: Prescribed Duties for MankindChapter 8: The Nārāyana-kavaca Shield

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 6.8.3

śrī-bādarāyanir uvāca

vritah purohitas tvāshtro

mahendrāyānupricchate

nārāyanākhyam varmāha

tad ihaika-manāh śrinu

SYNONYMS

śrī-bādarāyanih uvāca — Śrī Śukadeva Gosvāmī said; vritah — the chosen; purohitah — priest; tvāshtrah — the son of Tvashtā; mahendrāya — unto King Indra; anupricchate — after he (Indra) inquired; nārāyana-ākhyam — named Nārāyana-kavaca; varma — defensive armor made of a mantra; āha — he said; tat — that; iha — this; eka-manāh — with great attention; śrinu — hear from me.

TRANSLATION

Śrī Śukadeva Gosvāmī said: King Indra, the leader of the demigods, inquired about the armor known as Nārāyana-kavaca from Viśvarūpa, who was engaged by the demigods as their priest. Please hear Viśvarūpa's reply with great attention.
