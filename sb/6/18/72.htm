Canto 6: Prescribed Duties for MankindChapter 18: Diti Vows to Kill King Indra

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 6.18.72

kritto me saptadhā garbha

āsan sapta kumārakāh

te 'pi caikaikaśo vriknāh

saptadhā nāpi mamrire

SYNONYMS

krittah — cut; me — by me; sapta-dhā — into seven; garbhah — the embryo; āsan — there came to be; sapta — seven; kumārakāh — babies; te — they; api — although; ca — also; eka-ekaśah — each one; vriknāh — cut; sapta-dhā — into seven; na — not; api — still; mamrire — died.

TRANSLATION

First I cut the child in the womb into seven pieces, which became seven children. Then I cut each of the children into seven pieces again. By the grace of the Supreme Lord, however, none of them died.
