Canto 6: Prescribed Duties for MankindChapter 18: Diti Vows to Kill King Indra

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 6.18.60

ekadā sā tu sandhyāyām

ucchishtā vrata-karśitā

asprishta-vāry-adhautāńghrih

sushvāpa vidhi-mohitā

SYNONYMS

ekadā — once; sā — she; tu — but; sandhyāyām — during the evening twilight; ucchishtā — just after eating; vrata — from the vow; karśitā — weak and thin; asprishta — not touched; vāri — water; adhauta — not washed; ańghrih — her feet; sushvāpa — went to sleep; vidhi — by fate; mohitā — bewildered.

TRANSLATION

Having grown weak and thin because of strictly following the principles of the vow, Diti once unfortunately neglected to wash her mouth, hands and feet after eating and went to sleep during the evening twilight.
