Canto 6: Prescribed Duties for MankindChapter 18: Diti Vows to Kill King Indra

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 6.18.58

evam tasyā vrata-sthāyā

vrata-cchidram harir nripa

prepsuh paryacaraj jihmo

mriga-heva mrigākritih

SYNONYMS

evam — thus; tasyāh — of her; vrata-sthāyāh — who was faithfully discharging her vow; vrata-chidram — a fault in the execution of the vow; harih — Indra; nripa — O King; prepsuh — desiring to find; paryacarat — served; jihmah — deceitful; mriga-hā — a hunter; iva — like; mriga-ākritih — in the form of a deer.

TRANSLATION

O King Parīkshit, as the hunter of a deer becomes like a deer by covering his body with deerskin and serving the deer, so Indra, although at heart the enemy of the sons of Diti, became outwardly friendly and served Diti in a faithful way. Indra's purpose was to cheat Diti as soon as he could find some fault in the way she discharged the vows of the ritualistic ceremony. However, he wanted to be undetected, and therefore he served her very carefully.
