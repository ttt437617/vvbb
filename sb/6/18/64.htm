Canto 6: Prescribed Duties for MankindChapter 18: Diti Vows to Kill King Indra

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 6.18.64

mā bhaishta bhrātaro mahyam

yūyam ity āha kauśikah

ananya-bhāvān pārshadān

ātmano marutām ganān

SYNONYMS

mā bhaishta — do not fear; bhrātarah — brothers; mahyam — my; yūyam — you; iti — thus; āha — said; kauśikah — Indra; ananya-bhāvān — devoted; pārshadān — followers; ātmanah — his; marutām ganān — the Maruts.

TRANSLATION

When Indra saw that actually they were his devoted followers, he said to them: If you are all my brothers, you have nothing more to fear from me.
