Canto 6: Prescribed Duties for MankindChapter 18: Diti Vows to Kill King Indra

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 6.18.18

bāna ārādhya giriśam

lebhe tad-gana-mukhyatām

yat-pārśve bhagavān āste

hy adyāpi pura-pālakah

SYNONYMS

bānah — Bāna; ārādhya — having worshiped; giriśam — Lord Śiva; lebhe — obtained; tat — of him (Lord Śiva); gana-mukhyatām — the platform of being one of the chief associates; yat-pārśve — beside whom; bhagavān — Lord Śiva; āste — remains; hi — because of which; adya — now; api — even; pura-pālakah — the protector of the capital.

TRANSLATION

Since King Bāna was a great worshiper of Lord Śiva, he became one of Lord Śiva's most celebrated associates. Even now, Lord Śiva protects King Bāna's capital and always stands beside him.
