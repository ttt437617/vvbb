Canto 6: Prescribed Duties for MankindChapter 18: Diti Vows to Kill King Indra

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 6.18.14

śiro 'harad yasya hariś

cakrena pibato 'mritam

samhrādasya kritir bhāryā-

sūta pañcajanam tatah

SYNONYMS

śirah — the head; aharat — cut off; yasya — of whom; harih — Hari; cakrena — with the disc; pibatah — drinking; amritam — nectar; samhrādasya — of Samhlāda; kritih — Kriti; bhāryā — the wife; asūta — gave birth to; pañcajanam — Pañcajana; tatah — from him.

TRANSLATION

While Rāhu, in disguise, was drinking nectar among the demigods, the Supreme Personality of Godhead severed his head. The wife of Samhlāda was named Kriti. By union with Samhlāda, Kriti gave birth to a son named Pañcajana.
