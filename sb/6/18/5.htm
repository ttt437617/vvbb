Canto 6: Prescribed Duties for MankindChapter 18: Diti Vows to Kill King Indra

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 6.18.5

vālmīkiś ca mahā-yogī

valmīkād abhavat kila

agastyaś ca vasishthaś ca

mitrā-varunayor rishī

SYNONYMS

vālmīkih — Vālmīki; ca — and; mahā-yogī — the great mystic; valmīkāt — from an anthill; abhavat — took birth; kila — indeed; agastyah — Agastya; ca — and; vasishthah — Vasishtha; ca — also; mitrā-varunayoh — of Mitra and Varuna; rishī — the two sages.

TRANSLATION

By the semen of Varuna, the great mystic Vālmīki took birth from an anthill. Bhrigu and Vālmīki were specific sons of Varuna, whereas Agastya and Vasishtha Rishis were the common sons of Varuna and Mitra, the tenth son of Aditi.
