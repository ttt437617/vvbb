Canto 6: Prescribed Duties for MankindChapter 18: Diti Vows to Kill King Indra

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 6.18.24

kadā nu bhrātri-hantāram

indriyārāmam ulbanam

aklinna-hridayam pāpam

ghātayitvā śaye sukham

SYNONYMS

kadā — when; nu — indeed; bhrātri-hantāram — the killer of the brothers; indriya-ārāmam — very fond of sense gratification; ulbanam — cruel; aklinna-hridayam — hard hearted; pāpam — sinful; ghātayitvā — having caused to be killed; śaye — shall I rest; sukham — happily.

TRANSLATION

Lord Indra, who is very much fond of sense gratification, has killed the two brothers Hiranyāksha and Hiranyakaśipu by means of Lord Vishnu. Therefore Indra is cruel, hardhearted and sinful. When will I, having killed him, rest with a pacified mind?
