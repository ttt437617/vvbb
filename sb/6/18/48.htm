Canto 6: Prescribed Duties for MankindChapter 18: Diti Vows to Kill King Indra

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 6.18.48

nāpsu snāyān na kupyeta

na sambhāsheta durjanaih

na vasītādhauta-vāsah

srajam ca vidhritām kvacit

SYNONYMS

na — not; apsu — in water; snāyāt — must bathe; na kupyeta — must not become angry; na sambhāsheta — must not speak; durjanaih — with wicked persons; na vasīta — must not wear; adhauta-vāsah — unwashed clothes; srajam — flower garland; ca — and; vidhritām — which was already worn; kvacit — ever.

TRANSLATION

Kaśyapa Muni continued: My dear gentle wife, never enter the water while bathing, never be angry, and do not even speak or associate with wicked people. Never wear clothes that have not been properly washed, and do not put on a garland that has already been worn.
