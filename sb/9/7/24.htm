Canto 9: LiberationChapter 7: The Descendants of King Māndhātā

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 9.7.24

satyam sāram dhritim drishtvā

sabhāryasya ca bhūpateh

viśvāmitro bhriśam prīto

dadāv avihatām gatim

SYNONYMS

satyam — truthfulness; sāram — firmness; dhritim — forbearance; drishtvā — by seeing; sa-bhāryasya — with his wife; ca — and; bhūpateh — of Mahārāja Hariścandra; viśvāmitrah — the great sage Viśvāmitra; bhriśam — very much; prītah — being pleased; dadau — gave him; avihatām gatim — imperishable knowledge.

TRANSLATION

The great sage Viśvāmitra saw that Mahārāja Hariścandra, along with his wife, was truthful, forbearing and concerned with the essence. Thus he gave them imperishable knowledge for fulfillment of the human mission.
