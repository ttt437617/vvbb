Canto 9: LiberationChapter 19: King Yayāti Achieves Liberation

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 9.19.9

so 'pi cānugatah strainah

kripanas tām prasāditum

kurvann idavidā-kāram

nāśaknot pathi sandhitum

SYNONYMS

sah — that he-goat; api — also; ca — also; anugatah — following the she-goat; strainah — hen pecked; kripanah — very poor; tām — her; prasāditum — to satisfy; kurvan — making; idavidā-kāram — an utterance in the language of the goats; na — not; aśaknot — was able; pathi — on the road; sandhitum — to satisfy.

TRANSLATION

Being very sorry, the he-goat, who was subservient to his wife, followed the she-goat on the road and tried his best to flatter her, but he could not pacify her.
