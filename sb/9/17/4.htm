Canto 9: LiberationChapter 17: The Dynasties of the Sons of Purūravā

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 9.17.4

kāśyasya kāśis tat-putro

rāshtro dīrghatamah-pitā

dhanvantarir dīrghatamasa

āyur-veda-pravartakah

yajña-bhug vāsudevāmśah

smrita-mātrārti-nāśanah

SYNONYMS

kāśyasya — of Kāśya; kāśih — Kāśi; tat-putrah — his son; rāshtrah — Rāshtra; dīrghatamah-pitā — he became the father of Dīrghatama; dhanvantarih — Dhanvantari; dīrghatamasah — from Dīrghatama; āyuh-veda-pravartakah — the inaugurator of medical science, Āyur Veda; yajña-bhuk — the enjoyer of the results of sacrifice; vāsudeva-amśah — incarnation of Lord Vāsudeva; smrita-mātra — if he is remembered; ārti-nāśanah — it immediately vanquishes all kinds of disease.

TRANSLATION

The son of Kāśya was Kāśi, and his son was Rāshtra, the father of Dīrghatama. Dīrghatama had a son named Dhanvantari, who was the inaugurator of the medical science and an incarnation of Lord Vāsudeva, the enjoyer of the results of sacrifices. One who remembers the name of Dhanvantari can be released from all disease.
