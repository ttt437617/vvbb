Canto 9: LiberationChapter 17: The Dynasties of the Sons of Purūravā

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 9.17.17

sahadevas tato hīno

jayasenas tu tat-sutah

sańkritis tasya ca jayah

kshatra-dharmā mahā-rathah

kshatravriddhānvayā bhūpā

ime śrinv atha nāhushān

SYNONYMS

sahadevah — Sahadeva; tatah — from Sahadeva; hīnah — a son named Hīna; jayasenah — Jayasena; tu — also; tat-sutah — the son of Hīna; sańkritih — Sańkriti; tasya — of Sańkriti; ca — also; jayah — a son named Jaya; kshatra-dharmā — expert in the duties of a kshatriya; mahā-rathah — a greatly powerful fighter; kshatravriddha-anvayāh — in the dynasty of Kshatravriddha; bhūpāh — kings; ime — all these; śrinu — hear from me; atha — now; nāhushān — the descendants of Nahusha.

TRANSLATION

From Haryabala came a son named Sahadeva, and from Sahadeva came Hīna. The son of Hīna was Jayasena, and the son of Jayasena was Sańkriti. The son of Sańkriti was the powerful and expert fighter named Jaya. These kings were the members of the Kshatravriddha dynasty. Now let me describe to you the dynasty of Nahusha.

Thus end the Bhaktivedanta purports of the Ninth Canto, Seventeenth Chapter, of the Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam, entitled "The Dynasties of the Sons of Purūravā."
