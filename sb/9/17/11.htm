Canto 9: LiberationChapter 17: The Dynasties of the Sons of Purūravā

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 9.17.11

tad-gotram brahmavij jajñe

śrinu vamśam anenasah

śuddhas tatah śucis tasmāc

citrakrid dharmasārathih

SYNONYMS

tat-gotram — the descendant of Akriya; brahmavit — Brahmavit; jajñe — took birth; śrinu — just hear from me; vamśam — descendants; anenasah — of Anenā; śuddhah — a son known as Śuddha; tatah — from him; śucih — Śuci; tasmāt — from him; citrakrit — Citrakrit; dharma-sārathih — Dharmasārathi.

TRANSLATION

The son of Akriya was known as Brahmavit, O King. Now hear about the descendants of Anenā. From Anenā came a son named Śuddha, and his son was Śuci. The son of Śuci was Dharmasārathi, also called Citrakrit.
