Canto 9: LiberationChapter 17: The Dynasties of the Sons of Purūravā

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 9.17.15

gurunā hūyamāne 'gnau

balabhit tanayān rajeh

avadhīd bhramśitān mārgān

na kaścid avaśeshitah

SYNONYMS

gurunā — by the spiritual master (Brihaspati); hūyamāne agnau — while oblations were being offered in the fire of sacrifice; balabhit — Indra; tanayān — the sons; rajeh — of Rajī; avadhīt — killed; bhramśitān — fallen; mārgāt — from the moral principles; na — not; kaścit — anyone; avaśeshitah — remained alive.

TRANSLATION

Thereafter, Brihaspati, the spiritual master of the demigods, offered oblations in the fire so that the sons of Rajī would fall from moral principles. When they fell, Lord Indra killed them easily because of their degradation. Not a single one of them remained alive.
