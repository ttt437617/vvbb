Canto 9: LiberationChapter 2: The Dynasties of the Sons of Manu

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 9.2.7

vyāghro 'pi vrikna-śravano

nistrimśāgrāhatas tatah

niścakrāma bhriśam bhīto

raktam pathi samutsrijan

SYNONYMS

vyāghrah — the tiger; api — also; vrikna-śravanah — its ear being cut off; nistrimśa-agra-āhatah — because of being cut by the tip of the sword; tatah — thereafter; niścakrāma — fled (from that place); bhriśam — very much; bhītah — being afraid; raktam — blood; pathi — on the road; samutsrijan — discharging.

TRANSLATION

Because the tiger's ear had been cut by the edge of the sword, the tiger was very afraid, and it fled from that place, while bleeding on the street.
