Canto 9: LiberationChapter 2: The Dynasties of the Sons of Manu

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 9.2.16

karūshān mānavād āsan

kārūshāh kshatra-jātayah

uttarā-patha-goptāro

brahmanyā dharma-vatsalāh

SYNONYMS

karūshāt — from Karūsha; mānavāt — from the son of Manu; āsan — there was; kārūshāh — called the Kārūshas; kshatra-jātayah — a group of kshatriyas; uttarā — northern; patha — of the direction; goptārah — kings; brahmanyāh — celebrated protectors of the brahminical culture; dharma-vatsalāh — extremely religious.

TRANSLATION

From Karūsha, another son of Manu, came the Kārūsha dynasty, a family of kshatriyas. The Kārūsha kshatriyas were the kings of the northern direction. They were celebrated protectors of brahminical culture and were all firmly religious.
