Canto 9: LiberationChapter 2: The Dynasties of the Sons of Manu

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 9.2.32

yasyām utpādayām āsa

viśravā dhanadam sutam

prādāya vidyām paramām

rishir yogeśvarah pituh

SYNONYMS

yasyām — in whom (Ilavilā); utpādayām āsa — gave birth; viśravāh — Viśravā; dhana-dam — Kuvera, or one who gives money; sutam — to a son; prādāya — after receiving; vidyām — absolute knowledge; paramām — supreme; rishih — the great saintly person; yoga-īśvarah — master of mystic yoga; pituh — from his father.

TRANSLATION

After the great saint Viśravā, the master of mystic yoga, received absolute knowledge from his father, he begot in the womb of Ilavilā the greatly celebrated son known as Kuvera, the giver of money.
