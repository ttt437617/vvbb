Canto 9: LiberationChapter 2: The Dynasties of the Sons of Manu

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 9.2.22

tato brahma-kulam jātam

āgniveśyāyanam nripa

narishyantānvayah prokto

dishta-vamśam atah śrinu

SYNONYMS

tatah — from Agniveśya; brahma-kulam — a dynasty of brāhmanas; jātam — was generated; āgniveśyāyanam — known as Āgniveśyāyana; nripa — O King Parīkshit; narishyanta — of Narishyanta; anvayah — descendants; proktah — have been explained; dishta-vamśam — the dynasty of Dishta; atah — hereafter; śrinu — hear.

TRANSLATION

O King, from Agniveśya came a brahminical dynasty known as Āgniveśyāyana. Now that I have described the descendants of Narishyanta, let me describe the descendants of Dishta. Please hear from me.
