Canto 9: LiberationChapter 2: The Dynasties of the Sons of Manu

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 9.2.19

citraseno narishyantād

rikshas tasya suto 'bhavat

tasya mīdhvāms tatah pūrna

indrasenas tu tat-sutah

SYNONYMS

citrasenah — one named Citrasena; narishyantāt — from Narishyanta, another son of Manu; rikshah — Riksha; tasya — of Citrasena; sutah — the son; abhavat — became; tasya — of him (Riksha); mīdhvān — Mīdhvān; tatah — from him (Mīdhvān); pūrnah — Pūrna; indrasenah — Indrasena; tu — but; tat-sutah — the son of him (Pūrna).

TRANSLATION

From Narishyanta came a son named Citrasena and from him a son named Riksha. From Riksha came Mīdhvān, from Mīdhvān came Pūrna, and from Pūrna came Indrasena.
