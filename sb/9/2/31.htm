Canto 9: LiberationChapter 2: The Dynasties of the Sons of Manu

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 9.2.31

tam bheje 'lambushā devī

bhajanīya-gunālayam

varāpsarā yatah putrāh

kanyā celavilābhavat

SYNONYMS

tam — him (Trinabindu); bheje — accepted as husband; alambushā — the girl Alambushā; devī — goddess; bhajanīya — worthy of accepting; guna-ālayam — the reservoir of all good qualities; vara-apsarāh — the best of the Apsarās; yatah — from whom (Trinabindu); putrāh — some sons; kanyā — a daughter; ca — and; ilavilā — named Ilavilā; abhavat — was born.

TRANSLATION

The best of the Apsarās, the highly qualified girl named Alambushā, accepted the similarly qualified Trinabindu as her husband. She gave birth to a few sons and a daughter known as Ilavilā.
