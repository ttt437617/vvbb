Canto 9: LiberationChapter 2: The Dynasties of the Sons of Manu

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 9.2.27

maruttasya yathā yajño

na tathānyo 'sti kaścana

sarvam hiranmayam tv āsīd

yat kiñcic cāsya śobhanam

SYNONYMS

maruttasya — of Marutta; yathā — as; yajñah — performance of sacrifice; na — not; tathā — like that; anyah — any other; asti — there is; kaścana — anything; sarvam — everything; hiran-mayam — made of gold; tu — indeed; āsīt — there was; yat kiñcit — whatever he had; ca — and; asya — of Marutta; śobhanam — extremely beautiful.

TRANSLATION

The sacrificial paraphernalia of King Marutta was extremely beautiful, for everything was made of gold. Indeed, no other sacrifice could compare to his.
