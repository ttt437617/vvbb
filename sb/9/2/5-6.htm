Canto 9: LiberationChapter 2: The Dynasties of the Sons of Manu

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 9.2.5-6

ekām jagrāha balavān

sā cukrośa bhayāturā

tasyās tu kranditam śrutvā

prishadhro 'nusasāra ha

khadgam ādāya tarasā

pralīnodu-gane niśi

ajānann acchinod babhroh

śirah śārdūla-śańkayā

SYNONYMS

ekām — one of the cows; jagrāha — seized; balavān — the strong tiger; sā — that cow; cukrośa — began to cry; bhaya-āturā — in distress and fear; tasyāh — of her; tu — but; kranditam — the screaming; śrutvā — hearing; prishadhrah — Prishadhra; anusasāra ha — followed; khadgam — sword; ādāya — taking; tarasā — very hastily; pralīna-udu-gane — when the stars were covered by clouds; niśi — at night; ajānan — without knowledge; acchinot — cut off; babhroh — of the cow; śirah — the head; śārdūla-śańkayā — mistaking it for the head of the tiger.

TRANSLATION

When the very strong tiger seized the cow, the cow screamed in distress and fear, and Prishadhra, hearing the screaming, immediately followed the sound. He took up his sword, but because the stars were covered by clouds, he mistook the cow for the tiger and mistakenly cut off the cows' head with great force.
