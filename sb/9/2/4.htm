Canto 9: LiberationChapter 2: The Dynasties of the Sons of Manu

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 9.2.4

ekadā prāviśad goshtham

śārdūlo niśi varshati

śayānā gāva utthāya

bhītās tā babhramur vraje

SYNONYMS

ekadā — once upon a time; prāviśat — entered; goshtham — the land of the cowshed; śārdūlah — a tiger; niśi — at night; varshati — while it was raining; śayānāh — lying down; gāvah — cows; utthāya — getting up; bhītāh — fearing; tāh — all of them; babhramuh — scattered here and there; vraje — in the land surrounding the cowshed.

TRANSLATION

Once at night, while it was raining, a tiger entered the land of the cowshed. Upon seeing the tiger, all the cows, who were lying down, got up in fear and scattered here and there on the land.
