Canto 9: LiberationChapter 8: The Sons of Sagara Meet Lord Kapiladeva

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 9.8.3

vriddham tam pañcatām prāptam

mahishy anumarishyatī

aurvena jānatātmānam

prajāvantam nivāritā

SYNONYMS

vriddham — when he was old; tam — him; pañcatām — death; prāptam — who had obtained; mahishī — the queen; anumarishyatī — who wanted to die with him and become satī; aurvena — by the great sage Aurva; jānatā — understanding that; ātmānam — the body of the queen; prajā-vantam — bore a son within the womb; nivāritā — was forbidden.

TRANSLATION

Bāhuka died when he was old, and one of his wives wanted to die with him, following the satī rite. At that time, however, Aurva Muni, knowing her to be pregnant, forbade her to die.
