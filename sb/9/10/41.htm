Canto 9: LiberationChapter 10: The Pastimes of the Supreme Lord, Rāmacandra

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 9.10.41

dhunvanta uttarāsańgān

patim vīkshya cirāgatam

uttarāh kosalā mālyaih

kiranto nanritur mudā

SYNONYMS

dhunvantah — waving; uttara-āsańgān — the upper cloths covering the body; patim — the Lord; vīkshya — seeing; cira-āgatam — returned after many years of banishment; uttarāh kosalāh — the citizens of Ayodhyā; mālyaih kirantah — offering Him garlands; nanrituh — began to dance; mudā — in great jubilation.

TRANSLATION

The citizens of Ayodhyā, upon seeing their King return after a long absence, offered Him flower garlands, waved their upper cloths, and danced in great jubilation.
