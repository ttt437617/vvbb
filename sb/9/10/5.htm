Canto 9: LiberationChapter 10: The Pastimes of the Supreme Lord, Rāmacandra

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 9.10.5

viśvāmitrādhvare yena

mārīcādyā niśā-carāh

paśyato lakshmanasyaiva

hatā nairrita-puńgavāh

SYNONYMS

viśvāmitra-adhvare — in the sacrificial arena of the great sage Viśvāmitra; yena — by whom (Lord Rāmacandra); mārīca-ādyāh — headed by Mārīca; niśā-carāh — the uncivilized persons wandering at night in the darkness of ignorance; paśyatah lakshmanasya — being seen by Lakshmana; eva — indeed; hatāh — were killed; nairrita-puńgavāh — the great chiefs of the Rākshasas.

TRANSLATION

In the arena of the sacrifice performed by Viśvāmitra, Lord Rāmacandra, the King of Ayodhyā, killed many demons, Rākshasas and uncivilized men who wandered at night in the mode of darkness. May Lord Rāmacandra, who killed these demons in the presence of Lakshmana, be kind enough to give us protection.
