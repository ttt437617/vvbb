Canto 9: LiberationChapter 20: The Dynasty of Pūru

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 9.20.29

bharatasya mahat karma

na pūrve nāpare nripāh

naivāpur naiva prāpsyanti

bāhubhyām tridivam yathā

SYNONYMS

bharatasya — of Mahārāja Bharata, the son of Mahārāja Dushmanta; mahat — very great, exalted; karma — activities; na — neither; pūrve — previously; na — nor; apare — after his time; nripāh — kings as a class; na — neither; eva — certainly; āpuh — attained; na — nor; eva — certainly; prāpsyanti — will get; bāhubhyām — by the strength of his arms; tri-divam — the heavenly planets; yathā — as.

TRANSLATION

As one cannot approach the heavenly planets simply by the strength of his arms (for who can touch the heavenly planets with his hands?), one cannot imitate the wonderful activities of Mahārāja Bharata. No one could perform such activities in the past, nor will anyone be able to do so in the future.
