Canto 9: LiberationChapter 20: The Dynasty of Pūru

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 9.20.19

tam duratyaya-vikrāntam

ādāya pramadottamā

harer amśāmśa-sambhūtam

bhartur antikam āgamat

SYNONYMS

tam — him; duratyaya-vikrāntam — whose strength was insurmountable; ādāya — taking with her; pramadā-uttamā — the best of women, Śakuntalā; hareh — of God; amśa-amśa-sambhūtam — a partial plenary incarnation; bhartuh antikam — unto her husband; āgamat — approached.

TRANSLATION

Śakuntalā, the best of beautiful women, along with her son, whose strength was insurmountable and who was a partial expansion of the Supreme Godhead, approached her husband, Dushmanta.
