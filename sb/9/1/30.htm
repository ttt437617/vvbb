Canto 9: LiberationChapter 1: King Sudyumna Becomes a Woman

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 9.1.30

tān vilokyāmbikā devī

vivāsā vrīditā bhriśam

bhartur ańkāt samutthāya

nīvīm āśv atha paryadhāt

SYNONYMS

tān — all the saintly persons; vilokya — seeing them; ambikā — mother Durgā; devī — the goddess; vivāsā — because she was naked; vrīditā — ashamed; bhriśam — highly; bhartuh — of her husband; ańkāt — from the lap; samutthāya — getting up; nīvīm — breast; āśu atha — very quickly; paryadhāt — covered with cloth.

TRANSLATION

When the goddess Ambikā saw the great saintly persons, she was very much ashamed because at that time she was naked. She immediately got up from the lap of her husband and tried to cover her breast.
