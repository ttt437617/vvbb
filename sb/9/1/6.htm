Canto 9: LiberationChapter 1: King Sudyumna Becomes a Woman

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 9.1.6

śrī-sūta uvāca

evam parīkshitā rājñā

sadasi brahma-vādinām

prishtah provāca bhagavāñ

chukah parama-dharma-vit

SYNONYMS

śrī-sūtah uvāca — Śrī Sūta Gosvāmī said; evam — in this way; parīkshitā — by Mahārāja Parīkshit; rājñā — by the King; sadasi — in the assembly; brahma-vādinām — of all the great saintly experts in Vedic knowledge; prishtah — having been asked; provāca — answered; bhagavān — the most powerful; śukah — Śuka Gosvāmī; parama-dharma-vit — the most learned scholar in religious principles.

TRANSLATION

Sūta Gosvāmī said: When Śukadeva Gosvāmī, the greatest knower of religious principles, was thus requested by Mahārāja Parīkshit in the assembly of all the scholars learned in Vedic knowledge, he then proceeded to speak.
