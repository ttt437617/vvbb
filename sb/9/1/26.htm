Canto 9: LiberationChapter 1: King Sudyumna Becomes a Woman

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 9.1.26

tasmin pravishta evāsau

sudyumnah para-vīra-hā

apaśyat striyam ātmānam

aśvam ca vadavām nripa

SYNONYMS

tasmin — in that forest; pravishtah — having entered; eva — indeed; asau — he; sudyumnah — Prince Sudyumna; para-vīra-hā — who could very well subdue his enemies; apaśyat — observed; striyam — female; ātmānam — himself; aśvam ca — and his horse; vadavām — a mare; nripa — O King Parīkshit.

TRANSLATION

O King Parīkshit, as soon as Sudyumna, who was expert in subduing enemies, entered the forest, he saw himself transformed into a female and his horse transformed into a mare.
