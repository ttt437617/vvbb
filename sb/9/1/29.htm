Canto 9: LiberationChapter 1: King Sudyumna Becomes a Woman

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 9.1.29

śrī-śuka uvāca

ekadā giriśam drashtum

rishayas tatra suvratāh

diśo vitimirābhāsāh

kurvantah samupāgaman

SYNONYMS

śrī-śukah uvāca — Śrī Śukadeva Gosvāmī said; ekadā — once upon a time; giriśam — Lord Śiva; drashtum — to see; rishayah — very saintly persons; tatra — in that forest; su-vratāh — highly elevated in spiritual power; diśah — all directions; vitimira-ābhāsāh — having been cleared of all darkness whatsoever; kurvantah — doing so; samupāgaman — arrived.

TRANSLATION

Śukadeva Gosvāmī answered: Great saintly persons who strictly observed the spiritual rules and regulations and whose own effulgence dissipated all the darkness of all directions once came to see Lord Śiva in that forest.
