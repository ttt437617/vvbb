Canto 9: LiberationChapter 1: King Sudyumna Becomes a Woman

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 9.1.28

śrī-rājovāca

katham evam guno deśah

kena vā bhagavan kritah

praśnam enam samācakshva

param kautūhalam hi nah

SYNONYMS

śrī-rājā uvāca — Mahārāja Parīkshit said; katham — how; evam — this; gunah — quality; deśah — the country; kena — why; vā — either; bhagavan — O most powerful; kritah — it was so done; praśnam — question; enam — this; samācakshva — just deliberate; param — very much; kautūhalam — eagerness; hi — indeed; nah — our.

TRANSLATION

Mahārāja Parīkshit said: O most powerful brāhmana, why was this place so empowered, and who made it so powerful? Kindly answer this question, for I am very eager to hear about this.
