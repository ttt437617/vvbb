Canto 9: LiberationChapter 1: King Sudyumna Becomes a Woman

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 9.1.37

sa tasya tām daśām drishtvā

kripayā bhriśa-pīditah

sudyumnasyāśayan pumstvam

upādhāvata śańkaram

SYNONYMS

sah — he, Vasishtha; tasya — of Sudyumna; tām — that; daśām — condition; drishtvā — seeing; kripayā — out of mercy; bhriśa-pīditah — being very much aggrieved; sudyumnasya — of Sudyumna; āśayan — desiring; pumstvam — the maleness; upādhāvata — began to worship; śańkaram — Lord Śiva.

TRANSLATION

Upon seeing Sudyumna's deplorable condition, Vasishtha was very much aggrieved. Desiring for Sudyumna to regain his maleness, Vasishtha again began to worship Lord Śańkara [Śiva].
