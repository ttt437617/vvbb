Canto 9: LiberationChapter 16: Lord Paraśurāma Destroys the World's Ruling Class

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 9.16.4

kālātyayam tam vilokya

muneh śāpa-viśańkitā

āgatya kalaśam tasthau

purodhāya kritāñjalih

SYNONYMS

kāla-atyayam — passing the time; tam — that; vilokya — observing; muneh — of the great sage Jamadagni; śāpa-viśańkitā — being afraid of the curse; āgatya — returning; kalaśam — the waterpot; tasthau — stood; purodhāya — putting in front of the sage; krita-añjalih — with folded hands.

TRANSLATION

Later, understanding that the time for offering the sacrifice had passed, Renukā feared a curse from her husband. Therefore when she returned she simply put the waterpot before him and stood there with folded hands.
