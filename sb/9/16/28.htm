Canto 9: LiberationChapter 16: Lord Paraśurāma Destroys the World's Ruling Class

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 9.16.28

gādher abhūn mahā-tejāh

samiddha iva pāvakah

tapasā kshātram utsrijya

yo lebhe brahma-varcasam

SYNONYMS

gādheh — from Mahārāja Gādhi; abhūt — was born; mahā-tejāh — very powerful; samiddhah — inflamed; iva — like; pāvakah — fire; tapasā — by austerities and penances; kshātram — the position of a kshatriya; utsrijya — giving up; yah — one who (Viśvāmitra); lebhe — achieved; brahma-varcasam — the quality of a brāhmana.

TRANSLATION

Viśvāmitra, the son of Mahārāja Gādhi, was as powerful as the flames of fire. From the position of a kshatriya, he achieved the position of a powerful brāhmana by undergoing penances and austerities.

PURPORT

Now, having narrated the history of Lord Paraśurāma, Śukadeva Gosvāmī begins the history of Viśvāmitra. From the history of Paraśurāma we can understand that although Paraśurāma belonged to the brahminical group, he circumstantially had to work as a kshatriya. Later, after finishing his work as a kshatriya, he again became a brāhmana and returned to Mahendra-parvata. Similarly, we can see that although Viśvāmitra was born in a kshatriya family, by austerities and penances he achieved the position of a brāhmana. These histories confirm the statements in śāstra that a brāhmana may become a kshatriya, a kshatriya may become a brāhmana or vaiśya, and a vaiśya may become a brāhmana, by achieving the required qualities. One's status does not depend upon birth. As confirmed in Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam (7.11.35) by Nārada:

yasya yal lakshanam proktam

pumso varnābhivyañjakam

yad anyatrāpi driśyeta

tat tenaiva vinirdiśet

"If one shows the symptoms of being a brāhmana, kshatriya, vaiśya or śūdra, even if he has appeared in a different class, he should be accepted according to those symptoms of classification." To know who is a brāhmana and who is a kshatriya, one must consider a man's quality and work. If all the unqualified śūdras become so-called brāhmanas and kshatriyas, social order will be impossible to maintain. Thus there will be discrepancies, human society will turn into a society of animals, and the situation all over the world will be hellish.
