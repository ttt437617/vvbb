Canto 9: LiberationChapter 16: Lord Paraśurāma Destroys the World's Ruling Class

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 9.16.17

gatvā māhishmatīm rāmo

brahma-ghna-vihata-śriyam

teshām sa śīrshabhī rājan

madhye cakre mahā-girim

SYNONYMS

gatvā — going; māhishmatīm — to the place known as Māhishmatī; rāmah — Lord Paraśurāma; brahma-ghna — because of the killing of a brāhmana; vihata-śriyam — doomed, bereft of all opulences; teshām — of all of them (the sons of Kārtavīryārjuna and the other kshatriya inhabitants); sah — he, Lord Paraśurāma; śīrshabhih — by the heads cut off from their bodies; rājan — O Mahārāja Parīkshit; madhye — within the jurisdiction of Māhishmatī; cakre — made; mahā-girim — a great mountain.

TRANSLATION

O King, Lord Paraśurāma then went to Māhishmatī, which was already doomed by the sinful killing of a brāhmana. In the midst of that city he made a mountain of heads, severed from the bodies of the sons of Kārtavīryārjuna.
