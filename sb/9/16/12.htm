Canto 9: LiberationChapter 16: Lord Paraśurāma Destroys the World's Ruling Class

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 9.16.12

yācyamānāh kripanayā

rāma-mātrātidārunāh

prasahya śira utkritya

ninyus te kshatra-bandhavah

SYNONYMS

yācyamānāh — being begged for the life of her husband; kripanayā — by the poor unprotected woman; rāma-mātrā — by the mother of Lord Paraśurāma; ati-dārunāh — very cruel; prasahya — by force; śirah — the head of Jamadagni; utkritya — having separated; ninyuh — took away; te — the sons of Kārtavīryārjuna; kshatra-bandhavah — not kshatriyas, but the most abominable sons of kshatriyas.

TRANSLATION

With pitiable prayers, Renukā, the mother of Paraśurāma and wife of Jamadagni, begged for the life of her husband. But the sons of Kārtavīryārjuna, being devoid of the qualities of kshatriyas, were so cruel that despite her prayers they forcibly cut off his head and took it away.
