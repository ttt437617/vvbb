Canto 9: LiberationChapter 16: Lord Paraśurāma Destroys the World's Ruling Class

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 9.16.31

yo vai hariścandra-makhe

vikrītah purushah paśuh

stutvā devān prajeśādīn

mumuce pāśa-bandhanāt

SYNONYMS

yah — he who (Śunahśepha); vai — indeed; hariścandra-makhe — in the sacrifice performed by King Hariścandra; vikrītah — was sold; purushah — man; paśuh — sacrificial animal; stutvā — offering prayers; devān — to the demigods; prajā-īśa-ādīn — headed by Lord Brahmā; mumuce — was released; pāśa-bandhanāt — from being bound with ropes like an animal.

TRANSLATION

Śunahśepha's father sold Śunahśepha to be sacrificed as a man-animal in the yajña of King Hariścandra. When Śunahśepha was brought into the sacrificial arena, he prayed to the demigods for release and was released by their mercy.

PURPORT

Here is a description of Śunahśepha. When Hariścandra was to sacrifice his son Rohita, Rohita arranged to save his own life by purchasing Śunahśepha from Śunahśepha's father to be sacrificed in the yajña. Śunahśepha was sold to Mahārāja Hariścandra because he was the middle son, between the oldest and the youngest. It appears that the sacrifice of a man as an animal in yajña has been practiced for a very long time.
