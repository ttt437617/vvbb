Canto 9: LiberationChapter 16: Lord Paraśurāma Destroys the World's Ruling Class

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 9.16.29

viśvāmitrasya caivāsan

putrā eka-śatam nripa

madhyamas tu madhucchandā

madhucchandasa eva te

SYNONYMS

viśvāmitrasya — of Viśvāmitra; ca — also; eva — indeed; āsan — there were; putrāh — sons; eka-śatam — 101; nripa — O King Parīkshit; madhyamah — the middle one; tu — indeed; madhucchandāh — known as Madhucchandā; madhucchandasah — named the Madhucchandās; eva — indeed; te — all of them.

TRANSLATION

O King Parīkshit, Viśvāmitra had 101 sons, of whom the middle one was known as Madhucchandā. In relation to him, all the other sons were celebrated as the Madhucchandās.

PURPORT

In this connection, Śrīla Viśvanātha Cakravartī Thākura quotes this statement from the Vedas: tasya ha viśvāmitrasyaika-śatam putrā āsuh pañcāśad eva jyāyāmso madhucchandasah pañcāśat kanīyāmsah. "Viśvāmitra had 101 sons. Fifty were older than Madhucchandā and fifty younger."
