Canto 9: LiberationChapter 16: Lord Paraśurāma Destroys the World's Ruling Class

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 9.16.10

ekadāśramato rāme

sabhrātari vanam gate

vairam sishādhayishavo

labdha-cchidrā upāgaman

SYNONYMS

ekadā — once upon a time; āśramatah — from the āśrama of Jamadagni; rāme — when Lord Paraśurāma; sa-bhrātari — with his brothers; vanam — into the forest; gate — having gone; vairam — revenge for past enmity; sishādhayishavah — desiring to fulfill; labdha-chidrāh — taking the opportunity; upāgaman — they came near the residence of Jamadagni.

TRANSLATION

Once when Paraśurāma left the āśrama for the forest with Vasumān and his other brothers, the sons of Kārtavīryārjuna took the opportunity to approach Jamadagni's residence to seek vengeance for their grudge.
