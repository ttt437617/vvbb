Canto 9: LiberationChapter 5: Durvāsā Muni's Life Spared

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 9.5.8

yadā visrishtas tvam anañjanena vai

balam pravishto 'jita daitya-dānavam

bāhūdarorv-ańghri-śirodharāni

vriścann ajasram pradhane virājase

SYNONYMS

yadā — when; visrishtah — sent; tvam — your good self; anañjanena — by the transcendental Supreme Personality of Godhead; vai — indeed; balam — the soldiers; pravishtah — entering among; ajita — O indefatigable and unconquerable one; daitya-dānavam — of the Daityas and Dānavas, the demons; bāhu — arms; udara — bellies; ūru — thighs; ańghri — legs; śirah-dharāni — necks; vriścan — separating; ajasram — incessantly; pradhane — in the battlefield; virājase — you stay.

TRANSLATION

O indefatigable one, when you are sent by the Supreme Personality of Godhead to enter among the soldiers of the Daityas and the Dānavas, you stay on the battlefield and unendingly separate their arms, bellies, thighs, legs and heads.
