Canto 9: LiberationChapter 5: Durvāsā Muni's Life Spared

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 9.5.23

samvatsaro 'tyagāt tāvad

yāvatā nāgato gatah

munis tad-darśanākāńksho

rājāb-bhaksho babhūva ha

SYNONYMS

samvatsarah — one complete year; atyagāt — passed; tāvat — as long as; yāvatā — so long; na — not; āgatah — returned; gatah — Durvāsā Muni, who had left that place; munih — the great sage; tat-darśana-ākāńkshah — desiring to see him again; rājā — the King; ap-bhakshah — taking only water; babhūva — remained; ha — indeed.

TRANSLATION

Durvāsā Muni had left the place of Mahārāja Ambarīsha, and as long as he had not returned — for one complete year — the King had fasted, maintaining himself simply by drinking water.
