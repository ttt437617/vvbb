Canto 9: LiberationChapter 11: Lord Rāmacandra Rules the World

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 9.11.28

pūgaih savrintai rambhābhih

pattikābhih suvāsasām

ādarśair amśukaih sragbhih

krita-kautuka-toranām

SYNONYMS

pūgaih — by trees of betel nut; sa-vrintaih — with bunches of flowers and fruits; rambhābhih — with banana trees; pattikābhih — with flags; su-vāsasām — decorated with colorful cloth; ādarśaih — with mirrors; amśukaih — with cloths; sragbhih — with garlands; krita-kautuka — made auspicious; toranām — possessing reception gates.

TRANSLATION

Wherever Lord Rāmacandra visited, auspicious welcome gates were constructed, with banana trees and betel nut trees, full of flowers and fruits. The gates were decorated with various flags made of colorful cloth and with tapestries, mirrors and garlands.
