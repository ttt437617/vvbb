Canto 9: LiberationChapter 11: Lord Rāmacandra Rules the World

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 9.11.24

śrī-rājovāca

katham sa bhagavān rāmo

bhrātrin vā svayam ātmanah

tasmin vā te 'nvavartanta

prajāh paurāś ca īśvare

SYNONYMS

śrī-rājā uvāca — Mahārāja Parīkshit inquired; katham — how; sah — He, the Lord; bhagavān — the Supreme Personality of Godhead; rāmah — Lord Rāmacandra; bhrātrin — unto the brothers (Lakshmana, Bharata and Śatrughna); vā — either; svayam — personally; ātmanah — expansions of His person; tasmin — unto the Lord; vā — either; te — they (all the inhabitants and the brothers); anvavartanta — behaved; prajāh — all the inhabitants; paurāh — the citizens; ca — and; īśvare — unto the Supreme Lord.

TRANSLATION

Mahārāja Parīkshit inquired from Śukadeva Gosvāmī: How did the Lord conduct Himself, and how did He behave in relationship with His brothers, who were expansions of His own self? And how did His brothers and the inhabitants of Ayodhyā treat Him?
