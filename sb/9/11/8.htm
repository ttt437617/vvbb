Canto 9: LiberationChapter 11: Lord Rāmacandra Rules the World

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 9.11.8

kadācil loka-jijñāsur

gūdho rātryām alakshitah

caran vāco 'śrinod rāmo

bhāryām uddiśya kasyacit

SYNONYMS

kadācit — once upon a time; loka-jijñāsuh — desiring to know about the public; gūdhah — hiding Himself by a disguise; rātryām — at night; alakshitah — without being identified by anyone else; caran — walking; vācah — speaking; aśrinot — heard; rāmah — Lord Rāmacandra; bhāryām — unto His wife; uddiśya — indicating; kasyacit — of someone.

TRANSLATION

Śukadeva Gosvāmī continued: Once while Lord Rāmacandra was walking at night incognito, hiding Himself by a disguise to find out the people's opinion of Himself, He heard a man speaking unfavorably about His wife, Sītādevī.
