Canto 9: LiberationChapter 11: Lord Rāmacandra Rules the World

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 9.11.29

tam upeyus tatra tatra

paurā arhana-pānayah

āśisho yuyujur deva

pāhīmām prāk tvayoddhritām

SYNONYMS

tam — unto Him, Lord Rāmacandra; upeyuh — approached; tatra tatra — wherever He visited; paurāh — the inhabitants of the neighborhood; arhana-pānayah — carrying paraphernalia to worship the Lord; āśishah — blessings from the Lord; yuyujuh — came down; deva — O my Lord; pāhi — just maintain; imām — this land; prāk — as before; tvayā — by You; uddhritām — rescued (from the bottom of the sea in Your incarnation as Varāha).

TRANSLATION

Wherever Lord Rāmacandra visited, the people approached Him with paraphernalia of worship and begged the Lord's blessings. "O Lord," they said, "as You rescued the earth from the bottom of the sea in Your incarnation as a boar, may You now maintain it. Thus we beg Your blessings."
