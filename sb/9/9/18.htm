Canto 9: LiberationChapter 9: The Dynasty of Amśumān

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 9.9.18

tatah sudāsas tat-putro

damayantī-patir nripah

āhur mitrasaham yam vai

kalmāshāńghrim uta kvacit

vasishtha-śāpād raksho 'bhūd

anapatyah sva-karmanā

SYNONYMS

tatah — from Sarvakāma; sudāsah — Sudāsa was born; tat-putrah — the son of Sudāsa; damayantī-patih — the husband of Damayantī; nripah — he became king; āhuh — it is said; mitrasaham — Mitrasaha; yam vai — also; kalmāshāńghrim — by Kalmāshapāda; uta — known; kvacit — sometimes; vasishtha-śāpāt — being cursed by Vasishtha; rakshah — a man-eater; abhūt — became; anapatyah — without any son; sva-karmanā — by his own sinful act.

TRANSLATION

Sarvakāma had a son named Sudāsa, whose son, known as Saudāsa, was the husband of Damayantī. Saudāsa is sometimes known as Mitrasaha or Kalmāshapāda. Because of his own misdeed, Mitrasaha was sonless and was cursed by Vasishtha to become a man-eater [Rākshasa].
