Canto 9: LiberationChapter 14: King Purūravā Enchanted by Urvaśī

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 9.14.33

sa tām vīkshya kurukshetre

sarasvatyām ca tat-sakhīh

pañca prahrishta-vadanah

prāha sūktam purūravāh

SYNONYMS

sah — he, Purūravā; tām — Urvaśī; vīkshya — observing; kurukshetre — at the place known as Kurukshetra; sarasvatyām — on the bank of the Sarasvatī; ca — also; tat-sakhīh — her companions; pañca — five; prahrishta-vadanah — being very happy and smiling; prāha — said; sūktam — sweet words; purūravāh — King Purūravā.

TRANSLATION

Once during his travels all over the world, Purūravā saw Urvaśī, accompanied by five companions, on the bank of the Sarasvatī at Kurukshetra. With jubilation in his face, he then spoke to her in sweet words as follows.
