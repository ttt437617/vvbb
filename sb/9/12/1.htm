Canto 9: LiberationChapter 12: The Dynasty of Kuśa, the Son of Lord Rāmacandra

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 9.12.1

śrī-śuka uvāca

kuśasya cātithis tasmān

nishadhas tat-suto nabhah

pundarīko 'tha tat-putrah

kshemadhanvābhavat tatah

SYNONYMS

śrī-śukah uvāca — Śrī Śukadeva Gosvāmī said; kuśasya — of Kuśa, the son of Lord Rāmacandra; ca — also; atithih — Atithi; tasmāt — from him; nishadhah — Nishadha; tat-sutah — his son; nabhah — Nabha; pundarīkah — Pundarīka; atha — thereafter; tat-putrah — his son; kshemadhanvā — Kshemadhanvā; abhavat — became; tatah — thereafter.

TRANSLATION

Śukadeva Gosvāmī said: The son of Rāmacandra was Kuśa, the son of Kuśa was Atithi, the son of Atithi was Nishadha, and the son of Nishadha was Nabha. The son of Nabha was Pundarīka, and from Pundarīka came a son named Kshemadhanvā.
