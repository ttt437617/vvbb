Canto 9: LiberationChapter 15: Paraśurāma, the Lord's Warrior Incarnation

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 9.15.14

yam āhur vāsudevāmśam

haihayānām kulāntakam

trih-sapta-kritvo ya imām

cakre nihkshatriyām mahīm

SYNONYMS

yam — whom (Paraśurāma); āhuh — all the learned scholars say; vāsudeva-amśam — an incarnation of Vāsudeva, the Supreme Personality of Godhead; haihayānām — of the Haihayas; kula-antakam — the annihilator of the dynasty; trih-sapta-kritvah — twenty-one times; yah — who (Paraśurāma); imām — this; cakre — made; nihkshatriyām — devoid of kshatriyas; mahīm — the earth.

TRANSLATION

Learned scholars accept this Paraśurāma as the celebrated incarnation of Vāsudeva who annihilated the dynasty of Kārtavīrya. Paraśurāma killed all the kshatriyas on earth twenty-one times.
