Canto 9: LiberationChapter 15: Paraśurāma, the Lord's Warrior Incarnation

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 9.15.28

ghoram ādāya paraśum

satūnam varma kārmukam

anvadhāvata durmarsho

mrigendra iva yūthapam

SYNONYMS

ghoram — extremely fierce; ādāya — taking in hand; paraśum — a chopper; sa-tūnam — along with a quiver; varma — a shield; kārmukam — a bow; anvadhāvata — followed; durmarshah — Lord Paraśurāma, being exceedingly angry; mrigendrah — a lion; iva — like; yūthapam — (goes to attack) an elephant.

TRANSLATION

Taking up his fierce chopper, his shield, his bow and a quiver of arrows, Lord Paraśurāma, exceedingly angry, chased Kārtavīryārjuna just as a lion chases an elephant.
