Canto 9: LiberationChapter 15: Paraśurāma, the Lord's Warrior Incarnation

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 9.15.4

jahnos tu purus tasyātha

balākaś cātmajo 'jakah

tatah kuśah kuśasyāpi

kuśāmbus tanayo vasuh

kuśanābhaś ca catvāro

gādhir āsīt kuśāmbujah

SYNONYMS

jahnoh — of Jahnu; tu — indeed; puruh — a son named Puru; tasya — of Puru; atha — thereafter; balākah — a son named Balāka; ca — and; ātmajah — Balāka's son; ajakah — of the name Ajaka; tatah — thereafter; kuśah — Kuśa; kuśasya — of Kuśa; api — then; kuśāmbuh — Kuśāmbu; tanayah — Tanaya; vasuh — Vasu; kuśanābhah — Kuśanābha; ca — and; catvārah — four (sons); gādhih — Gādhi; āsīt — there was; kuśāmbujah — the son of Kuśāmbu.

TRANSLATION

The son of Jahnu was Puru, the son of Puru was Balāka, the son of Balāka was Ajaka, and the son of Ajaka was Kuśa. Kuśa had four sons, named Kuśāmbu, Tanaya, Vasu and Kuśanābha. The son of Kuśāmbu was Gādhi.
