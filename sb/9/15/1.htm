Canto 9: LiberationChapter 15: Paraśurāma, the Lord's Warrior Incarnation

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 9.15.1

śrī-bādarāyanir uvāca

ailasya corvaśī-garbhāt

shad āsann ātmajā nripa

āyuh śrutāyuh satyāyū

rayo 'tha vijayo jayah

SYNONYMS

śrī-bādarāyanih uvāca — Śrī Śukadeva Gosvāmī said; ailasya — of Purūravā; ca — also; urvaśī-garbhāt — from the womb of Urvaśī; shat — six; āsan — there were; ātmajāh — sons; nripa — O King Parīkshit; āyuh — Āyu; śrutāyuh — Śrutāyu; satyāyuh — Satyāyu; rayah — Raya; atha — as well as; vijayah — Vijaya; jayah — Jaya.

TRANSLATION

Śukadeva Gosvāmī continued: O King Parīkshit, from the womb of Urvaśī, six sons were generated by Purūravā. Their names were Āyu, Śrutāyu, Satyāyu, Raya, Vijaya and Jaya.
