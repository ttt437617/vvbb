Canto 9: LiberationChapter 21: The Dynasty of Bharata;

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 9.21.36

tad drishtvā kripayāgrihnāc

chāntanur mrigayām caran

kripah kumārah kanyā ca

drona-patny abhavat kripī

SYNONYMS

tat — those twin male and female babies; drishtvā — seeing; kripayā — out of compassion; agrihnāt — took; śāntanuh — King Śāntanu; mrigayām — while hunting in the forest; caran — wandering in that way; kripah — Kripa; kumārah — the male child; kanyā — the female child; ca — also; drona-patnī — the wife of Dronācārya; abhavat — became; kripī — named Kripī.

TRANSLATION

While Mahārāja Śāntanu was on a hunting excursion, he saw the male and female children lying in the forest, and out of compassion he took them home. Consequently, the male child was known as Kripa, and the female child was named Kripī. Kripī later became the wife of Dronācārya.

Thus end the Bhaktivedanta purports of the Ninth Canto, Twenty-first Chapter, of the Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam, entitled "The Dynasty of Bharata."
