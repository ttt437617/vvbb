Canto 9: LiberationChapter 21: The Dynasty of Bharata;

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 9.21.11

tasya tām karunām vācam

niśamya vipula-śramām

kripayā bhriśa-santapta

idam āhāmritam vacah

SYNONYMS

tasya — of him (the candāla); tām — those; karunām — pitiable; vācam — words; niśamya — hearing; vipula — very much; śramām — fatigued; kripayā — out of compassion; bhriśa-santaptah — very much aggrieved; idam — these; āha — spoke; amritam — very sweet; vacah — words.

TRANSLATION

Aggrieved at hearing the pitiable words of the poor fatigued candāla, Mahārāja Rantideva spoke the following nectarean words.

PURPORT

Mahārāja Rantideva's words were like amrita, or nectar, and therefore, aside from rendering bodily service to an aggrieved person, by his words alone the King could save the life of anyone who might hear him.
