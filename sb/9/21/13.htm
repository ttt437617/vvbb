Canto 9: LiberationChapter 21: The Dynasty of Bharata;

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 9.21.13

kshut-trit-śramo gātra-paribhramaś ca

dainyam klamah śoka-vishāda-mohāh

sarve nivrittāh kripanasya jantor

jijīvishor jīva-jalārpanān me

SYNONYMS

kshut — from hunger; trit — and thirst; śramah — fatigue; gātra-paribhramah — trembling of the body; ca — also; dainyam — poverty; klamah — distress; śoka — lamentation; vishāda — moroseness; mohāh — and bewilderment; sarve — all of them; nivrittāh — finished; kripanasya — of the poor; jantoh — living entity (the candāla); jijīvishoh — desiring to live; jīva — maintaining life; jala — water; arpanāt — by delivering; me — mine.

TRANSLATION

By offering my water to maintain the life of this poor candāla, who is struggling to live, I have been freed from all hunger, thirst, fatigue, trembling of the body, moroseness, distress, lamentation and illusion.
