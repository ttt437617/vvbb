Canto 9: LiberationChapter 21: The Dynasty of Bharata;

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 9.21.8

yāte śūdre tam anyo 'gād

atithih śvabhir āvritah

rājan me dīyatām annam

saganāya bubhukshate

SYNONYMS

yāte — when he went away; śūdre — the śūdra guest; tam — unto the King; anyah — another; agāt — arrived there; atithih — guest; śvabhih āvritah — accompanied by dogs; rājan — O King; me — unto me; dīyatām — deliver; annam — eatables; sa-ganāya — with my company of dogs; bubhukshate — hankering for food.

TRANSLATION

When the śūdra went away, another guest arrived, surrounded by dogs, and said, "O King, I and my company of dogs are very hungry. Please give us something to eat."
