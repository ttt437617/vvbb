Canto 9: LiberationChapter 21: The Dynasty of Bharata;

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 9.21.14

iti prabhāshya pānīyam

mriyamānah pipāsayā

pulkasāyādadād dhīro

nisarga-karuno nripah

SYNONYMS

iti — thus; prabhāshya — giving his statement; pānīyam — drinking water; mriyamānah — although on the verge of death; pipāsayā — because of thirst; pulkasāya — unto the low-class candāla; adadāt — delivered; dhīrah — sober; nisarga-karunah — by nature very kind; nripah — the King.

TRANSLATION

Having spoken thus, King Rantideva, although on the verge of death because of thirst, gave his own portion of water to the candāla without hesitation, for the King was naturally very kind and sober.
