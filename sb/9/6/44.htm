Canto 9: LiberationChapter 6: The Downfall of Saubhari Muni

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 9.6.44

tāsām kalir abhūd bhūyāms

tad-arthe 'pohya sauhridam

mamānurūpo nāyam va

iti tad-gata-cetasām

SYNONYMS

tāsām — of all the princesses; kalih — disagreement and quarrel; abhūt — there was; bhūyān — very much; tat-arthe — for the sake of Saubhari Muni; apohya — giving up; sauhridam — a good relationship; mama — mine; anurūpah — the fit person; na — not; ayam — this; vah — yours; iti — in this way; tat-gata-cetasām — being attracted by him.

TRANSLATION

Thereafter, the princesses, being attracted by Saubhari Muni, gave up their sisterly relationship and quarreled among themselves, each one of them contending, "This man is just suitable for me, and not for you." In this way there ensued a great disagreement.
