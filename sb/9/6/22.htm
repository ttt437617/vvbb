Canto 9: LiberationChapter 6: The Downfall of Saubhari Muni

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 9.6.22

yah priyārtham utańkasya

dhundhu-nāmāsuram balī

sutānām eka-vimśatyā

sahasrair ahanad vritah

SYNONYMS

yah — he who; priya-artham — for the satisfaction; utańkasya — of the great sage Utańka; dhundhu-nāma — of the name Dhundhu; asuram — a demon; balī — very powerful (Kuvalayāśva); sutānām — of sons; eka-vimśatyā — by twenty-one; sahasraih — thousands; ahanat — killed; vritah — surrounded.

TRANSLATION

To satisfy the sage Utańka, the greatly powerful Kuvalayāśva killed a demon named Dhundhu. He did this with the assistance of his twenty-one thousand sons.
