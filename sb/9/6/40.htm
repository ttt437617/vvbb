Canto 9: LiberationChapter 6: The Downfall of Saubhari Muni

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 9.6.39-40

yamunāntar-jale magnas

tapyamānah param tapah

nirvritim mīna-rājasya

drishtvā maithuna-dharminah

jāta-spriho nripam viprah

kanyām ekām ayācata

so 'py āha grihyatām brahman

kāmam kanyā svayamvare

SYNONYMS

yamunā-antah-jale — in the deep water of the River Yamunā; magnah — merged completely; tapyamānah — executing austerities; param — uncommon; tapah — austerity; nirvritim — pleasure; mīna-rājasya — of a big fish; drishtvā — seeing; maithuna-dharminah — engaged in sexual affairs; jāta-sprihah — became sexually inclined; nripam — unto the King (Māndhātā); viprah — the brāhmana (Saubhari Rishi); kanyām ekām — one daughter; ayācata — begged for; sah — he, the King; api — also; āha — said; grihyatām — you can take; brahman — O brāhmana; kāmam — as she desires; kanyā — daughter; svayamvare — a personal selection.

TRANSLATION

Saubhari Rishi was engaged in austerity, deep in the water of the River Yamunā, when he saw a pair of fish engaged in sexual affairs. Thus he perceived the pleasure of sex life, and induced by this desire he went to King Māndhātā and begged for one of the King's daughters. In response to this request, the King said, "O brāhmana, any of my daughters may accept any husband according to her personal selection."

PURPORT

This is the beginning of the story of Saubhari Rishi. According to Viśvanātha Cakravartī Thākura, Māndhātā was the king of Mathurā, and Saubhari Rishi was engaged in austerity while submerged deep within the River Yamunā. When the rishi felt sexual desire, he emerged from the water and went to King Māndhātā to ask that one of the King's daughters become his wife.
