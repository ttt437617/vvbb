Canto 9: LiberationChapter 6: The Downfall of Saubhari Muni

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 9.6.4

kshuvatas tu manor jajñe

ikshvākur ghrānatah sutah

tasya putra-śata-jyeshthā

vikukshi-nimi-dandakāh

SYNONYMS

kshuvatah — while sneezing; tu — but; manoh — of Manu; jajñe — was born; ikshvākuh — by the name Ikshvāku; ghrānatah — from the nostrils; sutah — the son; tasya — of Ikshvāku; putra-śata — one hundred sons; jyeshthāh — prominent; vikukshi — of the name Vikukshi; nimi — by the name Nimi; dandakāh — by the name Dandakā.

TRANSLATION

The son of Manu was Ikshvāku. When Manu was sneezing, Ikshvāku was born from Manu's nostrils. King Ikshvāku had one hundred sons, of whom Vikukshi, Nimi and Dandakā were the most prominent.

PURPORT

According to Śrīdhara Svāmī, although the Bhāgavatam (9.1.11-12) has previously included Ikshvāku among the ten sons begotten by Manu in his wife Śraddhā, this was a generalization. It is here specifically explained that Ikshvāku was born simply from the sneezing of Manu.
