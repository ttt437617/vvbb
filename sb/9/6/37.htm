Canto 9: LiberationChapter 6: The Downfall of Saubhari Muni

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 9.6.37

yāvat sūrya udeti sma

yāvac ca pratitishthati

tat sarvam yauvanāśvasya

māndhātuh kshetram ucyate

SYNONYMS

yāvat — as long as; sūryah — the sun; udeti — has risen on the horizon; sma — in the past; yāvat — as long as; ca — also; pratitishthati — continues to stay; tat — all those things mentioned above; sarvam — everything; yauvanāśvasya — of the son of Yuvanāśva; māndhātuh — called Māndhātā; kshetram — location; ucyate — is said to be.

TRANSLATION

All places, from where the sun rises on the horizon, shining brilliantly, to where the sun sets, are known as the possession of the celebrated Māndhātā, the son of Yuvanāśva.
