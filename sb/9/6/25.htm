Canto 9: LiberationChapter 6: The Downfall of Saubhari Muni

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 9.6.25

bahulāśvo nikumbhasya

kriśāśvo 'thāsya senajit

yuvanāśvo 'bhavat tasya

so 'napatyo vanam gatah

SYNONYMS

bahulāśvah — of the name Bahulāśva; nikumbhasya — of Nikumbha; kriśāśvah — of the name Kriśāśva; atha — thereafter; asya — of Kriśāśva; senajit — Senajit; yuvanāśvah — of the name Yuvanāśva; abhavat — was born; tasya — of Senajit; sah — he; anapatyah — without any sons; vanam gatah — retired to the forest as a vānaprastha.

TRANSLATION

The son of Nikumbha was Bahulāśva, the son of Bahulāśva was Kriśāśva, the son of Kriśāśva was Senajit, and the son of Senajit was Yuvanāśva. Yuvanāśva had no sons, and thus he retired from family life and went to the forest.
