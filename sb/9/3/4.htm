Canto 9: LiberationChapter 3: The Marriage of Sukanyā and Cyavana Muni

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 9.3.4

te daiva-coditā bālā

jyotishī kantakena vai

avidhyan mugdha-bhāvena

susrāvāsrik tato bahih

SYNONYMS

te — those two; daiva-coditā — as if impelled by providence; bālā — that young daughter; jyotishī — two glowworms within the hole of the earthworm; kantakena — with a thorn; vai — indeed; avidhyat — pierced; mugdha-bhāvena — as if without knowledge; susrāva — came out; asrik — blood; tatah — from there; bahih — outside.

TRANSLATION

As if induced by providence, the girl ignorantly pierced those two glowworms with a thorn, and when they were pierced, blood began to ooze out of them.
