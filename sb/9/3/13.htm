Canto 9: LiberationChapter 3: The Marriage of Sukanyā and Cyavana Muni

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 9.3.13

bādham ity ūcatur vipram

abhinandya bhishaktamau

nimajjatām bhavān asmin

hrade siddha-vinirmite

SYNONYMS

bādham — yes, we shall act; iti — thus; ūcatuh — they both replied, accepting the proposal of Cyavana; vipram — unto the brāhmana (Cyavana Muni); abhinandya — congratulating him; bhishak-tamau — the two great physicians, the Aśvinī-kumāras; nimajjatām — just dive; bhavān — yourself; asmin — in this; hrade — lake; siddha-vinirmite — which is especially meant for all kinds of perfection.

TRANSLATION

The great physicians, the Aśvinī-kumāras, very gladly accepted Cyavana Muni's proposal. Thus they told the brāhmana, "Just dive into this lake of successful life." [One who bathes in this lake has his desires fulfilled.]
