Canto 9: LiberationChapter 3: The Marriage of Sukanyā and Cyavana Muni

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 9.3.36

sutām dattvānavadyāńgīm

balāya bala-śāline

badary-ākhyam gato rājā

taptum nārāyanāśramam

SYNONYMS

sutām — his daughter; dattvā — after delivering; anavadya-ańgīm — having a perfect body; balāya — unto Lord Baladeva; bala-śāline — unto the most powerful, the supreme powerful; badarī-ākhyam — named Badarikāśrama; gatah — he went; rājā — the King; taptum — to perform austerities; nārāyana-āśramam — to the place of Nara-Nārāyana.

TRANSLATION

Thereafter, the King gave his most beautiful daughter in charity to the supremely powerful Baladeva and then retired from worldly life and went to Badarikāśrama to please Nara-Nārāyana.

Thus end the Bhaktivedanta purports of the Ninth Canto, Third Chapter, of the Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam, entitled "The Marriage of Sukanyā and Cyavana Muni."
