Canto 9: LiberationChapter 4: Ambarīsha Mahārāja Offended by Durvāsā Muni

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 9.4.37

pratinandya sa tām yācñām

kartum āvaśyakam gatah

nimamajja brihad dhyāyan

kālindī-salile śubhe

SYNONYMS

pratinandya — gladly accepting; sah — Durvāsā Muni; tām — that; yācñām — request; kartum — to perform; āvaśyakam — the necessary ritualistic ceremonies; gatah — went; nimamajja — dipped his body in the water; brihat — the Supreme Brahman; dhyāyan — meditating on; kālindī — of the Yamunā; salile — in the water; śubhe — very auspicious.

TRANSLATION

Durvāsā Muni gladly accepted the request of Mahārāja Ambarīsha, but to perform the regulative ritualistic ceremonies he went to the River Yamunā. There he dipped into the water of the auspicious Yamunā and meditated upon the impersonal Brahman.
