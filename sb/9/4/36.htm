Canto 9: LiberationChapter 4: Ambarīsha Mahārāja Offended by Durvāsā Muni

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 9.4.36

tam ānarcātithim bhūpah

pratyutthānāsanārhanaih

yayāce 'bhyavahārāya

pāda-mūlam upāgatah

SYNONYMS

tam — unto him (Durvāsā); ānarca — worshiped; atithim — although an uninvited guest; bhūpah — the King (Ambarīsha); pratyutthāna — by standing up; āsana — by offering a seat; arhanaih — and by paraphernalia for worship; yayāce — requested; abhyavahārāya — for eating; pāda-mūlam — at the root of his feet; upāgatah — fell down.

TRANSLATION

After standing up to receive Durvāsā Muni, King Ambarīsha offered him a seat and paraphernalia of worship. Then, sitting at his feet, the King requested the great sage to eat.
