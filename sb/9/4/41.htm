Canto 9: LiberationChapter 4: Ambarīsha Mahārāja Offended by Durvāsā Muni

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 9.4.41

ity apah prāśya rājarshiś

cintayan manasācyutam

pratyacashta kuru-śreshtha

dvijāgamanam eva sah

SYNONYMS

iti — thus; apah — water; prāśya — after drinking; rājarshih — the great King Ambarīsha; cintayan — meditating upon; manasā — by the mind; acyutam — the Supreme Personality of Godhead; pratyacashta — began to wait; kuru-śreshtha — O best of the Kuru kings; dvija-āgamanam — the return of Durvāsā Muni, the great mystic brāhmana; eva — indeed; sah — the King.

TRANSLATION

O best of the Kuru dynasty, after he drank some water, King Ambarīsha, meditating upon the Supreme Personality of Godhead within his heart, waited for the return of the great mystic Durvāsā Muni.
