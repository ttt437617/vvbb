Canto 9: LiberationChapter 18: King Yayāti Regains His Youth

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 9.18.46

sapta-dvīpa-patih samyak

pitrivat pālayan prajāh

yathopajosham vishayāñ

jujushe 'vyāhatendriyah

SYNONYMS

sapta-dvīpa-patih — the master of the entire world, consisting of seven islands; samyak — completely; pitri-vat — exactly like a father; pālayan — ruling; prajāh — the subjects; yathā-upajosham — as much as he wanted; vishayān — material happiness; jujushe — enjoyed; avyāhata — without being disturbed; indriyah — his senses.

TRANSLATION

Thereafter, King Yayāti became the ruler of the entire world, consisting of seven islands, and ruled the citizens exactly like a father. Because he had taken the youth of his son, his senses were unimpaired, and he enjoyed as much material happiness as he desired.
