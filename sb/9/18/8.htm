Canto 9: LiberationChapter 18: King Yayāti Regains His Youth

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 9.18.8

tā jalāśayam āsādya

kanyāh kamala-locanāh

tīre nyasya dukūlāni

vijahruh siñcatīr mithah

SYNONYMS

tāh — they; jala-āśayam — to the lakeside; āsādya — coming; kanyāh — all the girls; kamala-locanāh — with eyes like lotus petals; tīre — on the bank; nyasya — giving up; dukūlāni — their dresses; vijahruh — began to sport; siñcatīh — throwing water; mithah — on one another.

TRANSLATION

When the young, lotus-eyed girls came to the bank of a reservoir of water, they wanted to enjoy by bathing. Thus they left their clothing on the bank and began sporting, throwing water on one another.
