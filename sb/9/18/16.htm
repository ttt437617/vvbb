Canto 9: LiberationChapter 18: King Yayāti Regains His Youth

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 9.18.16

ātma-vrittam avijñāya

katthase bahu bhikshuki

kim na pratīkshase 'smākam

grihān balibhujo yathā

SYNONYMS

ātma-vrittam — one's own position; avijñāya — without understanding; katthase — you are talking madly; bahu — so much; bhikshuki — beggar; kim — whether; na — not; pratīkshase — you wait; asmākam — our; grihān — at the house; balibhujah — crows; yathā — like.

TRANSLATION

You beggar, since you don't understand your position, why should you unnecessarily talk so much? Don't all of you wait at our house, depending on us for your livelihood like crows?

PURPORT

Crows have no independent life; they fully depend on the remnants of foodstuffs thrown by householders into the garbage tank. Therefore, because a brāhmana depends on his disciples, when Śarmishthā was heavily rebuked by Devayānī she charged Devayānī with belonging to a family of crowlike beggars. It is the nature of women to fight verbally at even a slight provocation. As we see from this incident, this has been their nature for a long, long time.
