Canto 9: LiberationChapter 18: King Yayāti Regains His Youth

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 9.18.31

vilokyauśanasīm rājañ

charmishthā suprajām kvacit

tam eva vavre rahasi

sakhyāh patim ritau satī

SYNONYMS

vilokya — by seeing; auśanasīm — Devayānī, the daughter of Śukrācārya; rājan — O King Parīkshit; śarmishthā — the daughter of Vrishaparvā; su-prajām — possessing nice children; kvacit — at some time; tam — him (King Yayāti); eva — indeed; vavre — requested; rahasi — in a secluded place; sakhyāh — of her friend; patim — the husband; ritau — at the appropriate time; satī — being in that position.

TRANSLATION

O King Parīkshit, upon seeing Devayānī with a nice son, Śarmishthā once approached King Yayāti at the appropriate time for conception. In a secluded place, she requested the King, the husband of her friend Devayānī, to enable her to have a son also.
