Canto 9: LiberationChapter 18: King Yayāti Regains His Youth

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 9.18.9

vīkshya vrajantam giriśam

saha devyā vrisha-sthitam

sahasottīrya vāsāmsi

paryadhur vrīditāh striyah

SYNONYMS

vīkshya — seeing; vrajantam — passing by; giriśam — Lord Śiva; saha — with; devyā — Pārvatī, the wife of Lord Śiva; vrisha-sthitam — seated upon his bull; sahasā — quickly; uttīrya — getting out of the water; vāsāmsi — garments; paryadhuh — put on the body; vrīditāh — being ashamed; striyah — the young girls.

TRANSLATION

While sporting in the water, the girls suddenly saw Lord Śiva passing by, seated on the back of his bull with his wife, Pārvatī. Ashamed because they were naked, the girls quickly got out of the water and covered themselves with their garments.
