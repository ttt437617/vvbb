Canto 9: LiberationChapter 18: King Yayāti Regains His Youth

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 9.18.15

evam kshipantīm śarmishthā

guru-putrīm abhāshata

rushā śvasanty urańgīva

dharshitā dashta-dacchadā

SYNONYMS

evam — thus; kshipantīm — chastising; śarmishthā — the daughter of Vrishaparvā; guru-putrīm — unto the daughter of the guru, Śukrācārya; abhāshata — said; rushā — being very angry; śvasantī — breathing very heavily; urańgī iva — like a serpent; dharshitā — offended, trampled; dashta-dat-chadā — biting her lip with her teeth.

TRANSLATION

Śukadeva Gosvāmī said: When thus rebuked in cruel words, Śarmishthā was very angry. Breathing heavily like a serpent and biting her lower lip with her teeth, she spoke to the daughter of Śukrācārya as follows.
