Canto 9: LiberationChapter 18: King Yayāti Regains His Youth

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 9.18.30

nāhushāya sutām dattvā

saha śarmishthayośanā

tam āha rājañ charmishthām

ādhās talpe na karhicit

SYNONYMS

nāhushāya — unto King Yayāti, the descendant of Nahusha; sutām — his daughter; dattvā — giving in marriage; saha — with; śarmishthayā — Śarmishthā, the daughter of Vrishaparvā and servant of Devayānī; uśanā — Śukrācārya; tam — unto him (King Yayāti); āha — said; rājan — my dear King; śarmishthām — Śarmishthā, the daughter of Vrishaparvā; ādhāh — allow; talpe — on your bed; na — not; karhicit — at any time.

TRANSLATION

When Śukrācārya gave Devayānī in marriage to Yayāti, he had Śarmishthā go with her, but he warned the King, "My dear King, never allow this girl Śarmishthā to lie with you in your bed."
