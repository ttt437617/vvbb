Canto 9: LiberationChapter 18: King Yayāti Regains His Youth

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 9.18.4

catasrishv ādiśad dikshu

bhrātrin bhrātā yavīyasah

krita-dāro jugoporvīm

kāvyasya vrishaparvanah

SYNONYMS

catasrishu — over the four; ādiśat — allowed to rule; dikshu — directions; bhrātrin — four brothers; bhrātā — Yayāti; yavīyasah — young; krita-dārah — married; jugopa — ruled; ūrvīm — the world; kāvyasya — the daughter of Śukrācārya; vrishaparvanah — the daughter of Vrishaparvā.

TRANSLATION

King Yayāti had four younger brothers, whom he allowed to rule the four directions. Yayāti himself married Devayānī, the daughter of Śukrācārya, and Śarmishthā, the daughter of Vrishaparvā, and ruled the entire earth.
