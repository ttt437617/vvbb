Canto 9: LiberationChapter 23: The Dynasties of the Sons of Yayāti

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 9.23.6

cakruh sva-nāmnā vishayān

shad imān prācyakāmś ca te

khalapāno 'ńgato jajñe

tasmād divirathas tatah

SYNONYMS

cakruh — they created; sva-nāmnā — by their own names; vishayān — different states; shat — six; imān — all these; prācyakān ca — on the eastern side (of India); te — those (six kings); khalapānah — Khalapāna; ańgatah — from King Ańga; jajñe — took birth; tasmāt — from him (Khalapāna); divirathah — Diviratha; tatah — thereafter.

TRANSLATION

These six sons, headed by Ańga, later became kings of six states in the eastern side of India. These states were known according to the names of their respective kings. From Ańga came a son named Khalapāna, and from Khalapāna came Diviratha.
