Canto 9: LiberationChapter 23: The Dynasties of the Sons of Yayāti

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 9.23.29

teshām jyeshtho vītihotro

vrishnih putro madhoh smritah

tasya putra-śatam tv āsīd

vrishni-jyeshtham yatah kulam

SYNONYMS

teshām — of all of them; jyeshthah — the eldest son; vītihotrah — a son named Vītihotra; vrishnih — Vrishni; putrah — the son; madhoh — of Madhu; smritah — was well known; tasya — of him (Vrishni); putra-śatam — one hundred sons; tu — indeed; āsīt — there were; vrishni — Vrishni; jyeshtham — the eldest; yatah — from him; kulam — the dynasty.

TRANSLATION

Of the sons of Tālajańgha, Vītihotra was the eldest. The son of Vītihotra named Madhu had a celebrated son named Vrishni. Madhu had one hundred sons, of whom Vrishni was the eldest. The dynasties known as Yādava, Mādhava and Vrishni had their origin from Yadu, Madhu and Vrishni.
