Canto 9: LiberationChapter 23: The Dynasties of the Sons of Yayāti

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 9.23.28

jayadhvajāt tālajańghas

tasya putra-śatam tv abhūt

kshatram yat tālajańghākhyam

aurva-tejopasamhritam

SYNONYMS

jayadhvajāt — of Jayadhvaja; tālajańghah — a son named Tālajańgha; tasya — of him (Tālajańgha); putra-śatam — one hundred sons; tu — indeed; abhūt — were born; kshatram — a dynasty of kshatriyas; yat — which; tālajańgha-ākhyam — were known as the Tālajańghas; aurva-tejah — being very powerful; upasamhritam — were killed by Mahārāja Sagara.

TRANSLATION

Jayadhvaja had a son named Tālajańgha, who had one hundred sons. All the kshatriyas in that dynasty, known as Tālajańgha, were annihilated by the great power received by Mahārāja Sagara from Aurva Rishi.
