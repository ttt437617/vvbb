Canto 3: The Status QuoChapter 1: Questions by Vidura

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 3.1.2

yad vā ayam mantra-krid vo

bhagavān akhileśvarah

pauravendra-griham hitvā

praviveśātmasāt kritam

SYNONYMS

yat — the house; vai — what else is there to say; ayam — Śrī Krishna; mantra-krit — minister; vah — you people; bhagavān — the Personality of Godhead; akhila-īśvarah — the Lord of everything; pauravendra — Duryodhana; griham — house; hitvā — giving up; praviveśa — entered; ātmasāt — identify with oneself; kritam — so accepted.

TRANSLATION

What else is there to say about the residential house of the Pāndavas? Śrī Krishna, the Lord of everything, acted as your minister. He used to enter that house as if it were His own, and He did not take any care of Duryodhana's house.

PURPORT

According to the Gaudīya acintya-bhedābheda-tattva philosophy, anything which satisfies the senses of the Supreme Lord, Śrī Krishna, is also Śrī Krishna. For example, Śrī Vrindāvana-dhāma is nondifferent from Śrī Krishna (tad-dhāma vrindāvanam) because at Vrindāvana the Lord enjoys the transcendental bliss of His internal potency. Similarly, the house of the Pāndavas was also the source of transcendental bliss for the Lord. It is mentioned here that the Lord identified the house with His own Self. Thus the house of the Pāndavas was as good as Vrindāvana, and Vidura should not have given up that place of transcendental bliss. Therefore the reason for his quitting the house was not exactly family misunderstanding; rather, Vidura took the opportunity to meet Rishi Maitreya and discuss transcendental knowledge. For a saintly person like Vidura, any disturbance due to worldly affairs is insignificant. Such disturbances, however, are sometimes favorable for higher realization, and therefore Vidura took advantage of a family misunderstanding in order to meet Maitreya Rishi.
