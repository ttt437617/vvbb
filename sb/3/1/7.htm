Canto 3: The Status QuoChapter 1: Questions by Vidura

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 3.1.7

yadā sabhāyām kuru-deva-devyāh

keśābhimarśam suta-karma garhyam

na vārayām āsa nripah snushāyāh

svāsrair harantyāh kuca-kuńkumāni

SYNONYMS

yadā — when; sabhāyām — the assembly; kuru-deva-devyāh — of Draupadī, the wife of godly Yudhishthira; keśa-abhimarśam — insult by grabbing her hair; suta-karma — action taken by his son; garhyam — which was abominable; na — did not; vārayām āsa — forbid; nripah — the King; snushāyāh — of his daughter-in-law; svāsraih — by her tears; harantyāh — of she who was washing; kuca-kuńkumāni — red dust on her breast.

TRANSLATION

The King did not forbid his son Duhśāsana's abominable action of grabbing the hair of Draupadī, the wife of the godly King Yudhishthira, even though her tears washed the red dust on her breast.
