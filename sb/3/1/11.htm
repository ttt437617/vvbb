Canto 3: The Status QuoChapter 1: Questions by Vidura

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 3.1.11

ajāta-śatroh pratiyaccha dāyam

titikshato durvishaham tavāgah

sahānujo yatra vrikodarāhih

śvasan rushā yat tvam alam bibheshi

SYNONYMS

ajāta-śatroh — of Yudhishthira, who has no enemy; pratiyaccha — return; dāyam — legitimate share; titikshatah — of he who is so forbearing; durvishaham — unbearable; tava — your; āgah — offense; saha — along with; anujah — younger brothers; yatra — wherein; vrikodara — Bhīma; ahih — revenging snake; śvasan — breathing heavily; rushā — in anger; yat — whom; tvam — you; alam — verily; bibheshi — do fear.

TRANSLATION

[Vidura said:] You must now return the legitimate share to Yudhishthira, who has no enemies and who has been forbearing through untold sufferings due to your offenses. He is waiting with his younger brothers, among whom is the revengeful Bhīma, breathing heavily like a snake. Surely you are afraid of him.
