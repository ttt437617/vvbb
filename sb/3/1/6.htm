Canto 3: The Status QuoChapter 1: Questions by Vidura

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 3.1.6

śrī-śuka uvāca

yadā tu rājā sva-sutān asādhūn

pushnan na dharmena vinashta-drishtih

bhrātur yavishthasya sutān vibandhūn

praveśya lākshā-bhavane dadāha

SYNONYMS

śrī-śukah uvāca — Śrī Śukadeva Gosvāmī said; yadā — when; tu — but; rājā — King Dhritarāshtra; sva-sutān — his own sons; asādhūn — dishonest; pushnan — nourishing; na — never; dharmena — on the right path; vinashta-drishtih — one who has lost his insight; bhrātuh — of his brother; yavishthasya — younger; sutān — sons; vibandhūn — having no guardian (father); praveśya — made to enter; lākshā — lacquer; bhavane — in the house; dadāha — set on fire.

TRANSLATION

Śrī Śukadeva Gosvāmī said: King Dhritarāshtra became blind under the influence of impious desires to nourish his dishonest sons, and thus he set fire to the lacquer house to burn his fatherless nephews, the Pāndavas.

PURPORT

Dhritarāshtra was blind from birth, but his blindness in committing impious activities to support his dishonest sons was a greater blindness than his physical lack of eyesight. The physical lack of sight does not bar one from spiritual progress. But when one is blind spiritually, even though physically fit, that blindness is dangerously detrimental to the progressive path of human life.
