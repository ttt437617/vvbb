Canto 3: The Status QuoChapter 1: Questions by Vidura

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 3.1.39

yamāv utasvit tanayau prithāyāh

pārthair vritau pakshmabhir akshinīva

remāta uddāya mridhe sva-riktham

parāt suparnāv iva vajri-vaktrāt

SYNONYMS

yamau — twins (Nakula and Sahadeva); utasvit — whether; tanayau — sons; prithāyāh — of Prithā; pārthaih — by the sons of Prithā; vritau — protected; pakshmabhih — by shields; akshinī — of the eyes; iva — like; remāte — playing carelessly; uddāya — snatching; mridhe — in the fight; sva-riktham — own property; parāt — from the enemy Duryodhana; suparnau — Garuda, the carrier of Lord Vishnu; iva — like; vajri-vaktrāt — from the mouth of Indra.

TRANSLATION

Are the twin brothers who are protected by their brothers doing well? Just as the eye is always protected by the eyelid, they are protected by the sons of Prithā, who snatched back their rightful kingdom from the hands of their enemy Duryodhana, just as Garuda snatched nectar from the mouth of Indra, the thunderbolt carrier.

PURPORT

Indra, the King of heaven, carries a thunderbolt in his hand and is very strong, yet Garuda, the carrier of Lord Vishnu, was able to snatch nectar from his mouth. Similarly, Duryodhana was as strong as the King of heaven, and still the sons of Prithā, the Pāndavas, were able to snatch away their kingdom from Duryodhana. Both Garuda and the Pārthas are pet devotees of the Lord, and thus it was possible for them to face such strong enemies.

Vidura's inquiry was about the youngest brothers of the Pāndavas, namely Nakula and Sahadeva. These twin brothers were sons of Mādrī, the stepmother of the other Pāndavas. But although they were stepbrothers, because Kuntī took charge of them after the departure of Mādrī with her husband Mahārāja Pāndu, Nakula and Sahadeva were as good as the other three Pāndavas, Yudhishthira, Bhīma and Arjuna. The five brothers are known in the world as regular brothers. The three elder Pāndavas took care of the younger brothers, just as the eyelid takes care of the eye. Vidura was anxious to know whether, after winning back their own kingdom from the hands of Duryodhana, the younger brothers were still living happily under the care of the elder brothers.
