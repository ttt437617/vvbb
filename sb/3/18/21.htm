Canto 3: The Status QuoChapter 18: The Battle Between Lord Boar and the Demon Hiranyāksha

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 3.18.21

āsanna-śaundīram apeta-sādhvasam

krita-pratīkāram ahārya-vikramam

vilakshya daityam bhagavān sahasra-nīr

jagāda nārāyanam ādi-sūkaram

SYNONYMS

āsanna — attained; śaundīram — power; apeta — devoid of; sādhvasam — fear; krita — making; pratīkāram — opposition; ahārya — unopposable; vikramam — having power; vilakshya — having seen; daityam — the demon; bhagavān — the worshipful Brahmā; sahasra-nīh — the leader of thousands of sages; jagāda — addressed; nārāyanam — Lord Nārāyana; ādi — the original; sūkaram — having the form of a boar.

TRANSLATION

After arriving at the place of combat, Brahmā, the leader of thousands of sages and transcendentalists, saw the demon, who had attained such unprecedented power that no one could fight with him. Brahmā then addressed Nārāyana, who was assuming the form of a boar for the first time.
