Canto 3: The Status QuoChapter 18: The Battle Between Lord Boar and the Demon Hiranyāksha

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 3.18.26

eshā ghoratamā sandhyā

loka-cchambat-karī prabho

upasarpati sarvātman

surānām jayam āvaha

SYNONYMS

eshā — this; ghora-tamā — darkest; sandhyā — evening time; loka — the world; chambat-karī — destroying; prabho — O Lord; upasarpati — is approaching; sarva-ātman — O Soul of all souls; surānām — to the demigods; jayam — victory; āvaha — bring.

TRANSLATION

My Lord, the darkest evening, which covers the world, is fast approaching. Since You are the Soul of all souls, kindly kill him and win victory for the demigods.
