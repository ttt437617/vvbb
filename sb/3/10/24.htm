Canto 3: The Status QuoChapter 10: Divisions of the Creation

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 3.10.24

śvā srigālo vriko vyāghro

mārjārah śaśa-śallakau

simhah kapir gajah kūrmo

godhā ca makarādayah

SYNONYMS

śvā — dog; srigālah — jackal; vrikah — fox; vyāghrah — tiger; mārjārah — cat; śaśa — rabbit; śallakau — sajāru (with thorns on the body); simhah — lion; kapih — monkey; gajah — elephant; kūrmah — tortoise; godhā — gosāpa (snake with four legs); ca — also; makara-ādayah — the alligator and others.

TRANSLATION

The dog, jackal, tiger, fox, cat, rabbit, sajāru, lion, monkey, elephant, tortoise, alligator, gosāpa, etc., all have five nails in their claws. They are known as pañca-nakhas, or animals having five nails.
