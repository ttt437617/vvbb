Canto 3: The Status QuoChapter 10: Divisions of the Creation

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 3.10.19

saptamo mukhya-sargas tu

shad-vidhas tasthushām ca yah

vanaspaty-oshadhi-latā-

tvaksārā vīrudho drumāh

SYNONYMS

saptamah — the seventh; mukhya — principle; sargah — creation; tu — indeed; shat-vidhah — six kinds of; tasthushām — of those who do not move; ca — also; yah — those; vanaspati — fruit trees without flowers; oshadhi — trees and plants existing until the fruit is ripe; latā — creepers; tvaksārāh — pipe plants; vīrudhah — creepers without support; drumāh — trees with flowers and fruits.

TRANSLATION

The seventh creation is that of the immovable entities, which are of six kinds: the fruit trees without flowers, trees and plants which exist until the fruit is ripe, creepers, pipe plants, creepers which have no support, and trees with flowers and fruits.
