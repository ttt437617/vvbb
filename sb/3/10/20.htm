Canto 3: The Status QuoChapter 10: Divisions of the Creation

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 3.10.20

utsrotasas tamah-prāyā

antah-sparśā viśeshinah

SYNONYMS

utsrotasah — they seek their subsistence upwards; tamah-prāyāh — almost unconscious; antah-sparśāh — slightly feeling within; viśeshinah — with varieties of manifestation.

TRANSLATION

All the immovable trees and plants seek their subsistence upwards. They are almost unconscious but have feelings of pain within. They are manifested in variegatedness.
