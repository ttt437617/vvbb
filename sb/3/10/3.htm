Canto 3: The Status QuoChapter 10: Divisions of the Creation

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 3.10.3

sūta uvāca

evam sañcoditas tena

kshattrā kaushāravir munih

prītah pratyāha tān praśnān

hridi-sthān atha bhārgava

SYNONYMS

sūtah uvāca — Śrī Sūta Gosvāmī said; evam — thus; sañcoditah — being enlivened; tena — by him; kshattrā — by Vidura; kaushāravih — the son of Kushāra; munih — great sage; prītah — being pleased; pratyāha — replied; tān — those; praśnān — questions; hridi-sthān — from the core of his heart; atha — thus; bhārgava — O son of Bhrigu.

TRANSLATION

Sūta Gosvāmī said: O son of Bhrigu, the great sage Maitreya Muni, thus hearing from Vidura, felt very much enlivened. Everything was in his heart, and thus he began to reply to the questions one after another.

PURPORT

The phrase sūta uvāca ("Sūta Gosvāmī said") appears to indicate a break in the discourse between Mahārāja Parīkshit and Śukadeva Gosvāmī. While Śukadeva Gosvāmī was speaking to Mahārāja Parīkshit, Sūta Gosvāmī was only one member of a large audience. But Sūta Gosvāmī was speaking to the sages of Naimishāranya, headed by the sage Śaunaka, a descendant of Śukadeva Gosvāmī. This, however, does not make any substantial difference in the topics under discussion.
