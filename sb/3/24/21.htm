Canto 3: The Status QuoChapter 24: The Renunciation of Kardama Muni

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 3.24.21

gate śata-dhritau kshattah

kardamas tena coditah

yathoditam sva-duhitrih

prādād viśva-srijām tatah

SYNONYMS

gate — after he departed; śata-dhritau — Lord Brahmā; kshattah — O Vidura; kardamah — Kardama Muni; tena — by him; coditah — ordered; yathā-uditam — as told; sva-duhitrih — his own daughters; prādāt — handed over; viśva-srijām — to the creators of the world's population; tatah — thereafter.

TRANSLATION

O Vidura, after the departure of Brahmā, Kardama Muni, having been ordered by Brahmā, handed over his nine daughters, as instructed, to the nine great sages who created the population of the world.
