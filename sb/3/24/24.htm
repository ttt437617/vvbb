Canto 3: The Status QuoChapter 24: The Renunciation of Kardama Muni

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 3.24.24

atharvane 'dadāc chāntim

yayā yajño vitanyate

viprarshabhān kritodvāhān

sadārān samalālayat

SYNONYMS

atharvane — to Atharvā; adadāt — he gave away; śāntim — Śānti; yayā — by whom; yajñah — sacrifice; vitanyate — is performed; vipra-rishabhān — the foremost brāhmanas; krita-udvāhān — married; sa-dārān — with their wives; samalālayat — maintained them.

TRANSLATION

He delivered Śānti to Atharvā. Because of Śānti, sacrificial ceremonies are well performed. Thus he got the foremost brāhmanas married, and he maintained them along with their wives.
