Canto 3: The Status QuoChapter 33: Activities of Kapila

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 3.33.16

payah-phena-nibhāh śayyā

dāntā rukma-paricchadāh

āsanāni ca haimāni

susparśāstaranāni ca

SYNONYMS

payah — of milk; phena — the foam; nibhāh — resembling; śayyāh — beds; dāntāh — made of ivory; rukma — golden; paricchadāh — with covers; āsanāni — chairs and benches; ca — and; haimāni — made of gold; su-sparśa — soft to the touch; āstaranāni — cushions; ca — and.

TRANSLATION

The opulence of the household of Kardama Muni is described herein. The bedsheets and mattresses were all as white as the foam of milk, the chairs and benches were made of ivory and were covered by cloths of lace with golden filigree, and the couches were made of gold and had very soft pillows.
