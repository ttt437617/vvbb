Canto 3: The Status QuoChapter 11: Calculation of Time, from the Atom

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 3.11.3

evam kālo 'py anumitah

saukshmye sthaulye ca sattama

samsthāna-bhuktyā bhagavān

avyakto vyakta-bhug vibhuh

SYNONYMS

evam — thus; kālah — time; api — also; anumitah — measured; saukshmye — in the subtle; sthaulye — in the gross forms; ca — also; sattama — O best; samsthāna — combinations of the atoms; bhuktyā — by the motion; bhagavān — the Supreme Personality of Godhead; avyaktah — unmanifested; vyakta-bhuk — controlling all physical movement; vibhuh — the great potential.

TRANSLATION

One can estimate time by measuring the movement of the atomic combination of bodies. Time is the potency of the almighty Personality of Godhead, Hari, who controls all physical movement although He is not visible in the physical world.
