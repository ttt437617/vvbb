Canto 3: The Status QuoChapter 11: Calculation of Time, from the Atom

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 3.11.16

vidura uvāca

pitri-deva-manushyānām

āyuh param idam smritam

pareshām gatim ācakshva

ye syuh kalpād bahir vidah

SYNONYMS

vidurah uvāca — Vidura said; pitri — the Pitā planets; deva — the heavenly planets; manushyānām — and that of the human beings; āyuh — duration of life; param — final; idam — in their own measurement; smritam — calculated; pareshām — of the superior living entities; gatim — duration of life; ācakshva — kindly calculate; ye — all those who; syuh — are; kalpāt — from the millennium; bahih — outside; vidah — greatly learned.

TRANSLATION

Vidura said: I now understand the life durations of the residents of the Pitā planets and heavenly planets as well as that of the human beings. Now kindly inform me of the durations of life of those greatly learned living entities who are beyond the range of a kalpa.

PURPORT

The partial dissolution of the universe that takes place at the end of Brahmā's day does not affect all the planetary systems. The planets of highly learned living entities like the sages Sanaka and Bhrigu are not affected by the dissolutions of the millenniums. All the planets are of different types, and each is controlled by a different kāla-cakra, or schedule of eternal time. The time of the earth planet is not applicable to other, more elevated planets. Therefore, Vidura herein inquires about the duration of life on other planets.
