Canto 3: The Status QuoChapter 8: Manifestation of Brahmā from Garbhodakaśāyī Vishnu

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 3.8.13

tasyārtha-sūkshmābhinivishta-drishter

antar-gato 'rtho rajasā tanīyān

gunena kālānugatena viddhah

sūshyams tadābhidyata nābhi-deśāt

SYNONYMS

tasya — His; artha — subject; sūkshma — subtle; abhinivishta-drishteh — of one whose attention was fixed; antah-gatah — internal; arthah — purpose; rajasā — by the mode of passion of material nature; tanīyān — very subtle; gunena — by the qualities; kāla-anugatena — in due course of time; viddhah — agitated; sūshyan — generating; tadā — then; abhidyata — pierced through; nābhi-deśāt — from the abdomen.

TRANSLATION

The subtle subject matter of creation, on which the Lord's attention was fixed, was agitated by the material mode of passion, and thus the subtle form of creation pierced through His abdomen.
