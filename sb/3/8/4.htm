Canto 3: The Status QuoChapter 8: Manifestation of Brahmā from Garbhodakaśāyī Vishnu

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 3.8.4

svam eva dhishnyam bahu mānayantam

yad vāsudevābhidham āmananti

pratyag-dhritākshāmbuja-kośam īshad

unmīlayantam vibudhodayāya

SYNONYMS

svam — Himself; eva — thus; dhishnyam — situated; bahu — greatly; mānayantam — esteemed; yat — that which; vāsudeva — Lord Vāsudeva; abhidham — by the name; āmananti — acknowledge; pratyak-dhrita-aksha — eyes settled for introspection; ambuja-kośam — lotuslike eye; īshat — slightly; unmīlayantam — opened; vibudha — of the greatly learned sages; udayāya — for the sake of advancement.

TRANSLATION

At that time Lord Sańkarshana was meditating upon His Supreme Lord, whom the learned esteem as Lord Vāsudeva, but for the sake of the advancement of the great learned sages He slightly opened His lotus like eyes and began to speak.
