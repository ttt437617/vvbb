Canto 3: The Status QuoChapter 8: Manifestation of Brahmā from Garbhodakaśāyī Vishnu

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 3.8.23

mrināla-gaurāyata-śesha-bhoga-

paryańka ekam purusham śayānam

phanātapatrāyuta-mūrdha-ratna-

dyubhir hata-dhvānta-yugānta-toye

SYNONYMS

mrināla — lotus flower; gaura — white all over; āyata — gigantic; śesha-bhoga — body of Śesha-nāga; paryańke — on the bed; ekam — alone; purusham — the Supreme Person; śayānam — was lying; phana-ātapatra — umbrella of a serpent hood; āyuta — bedecked with; mūrdha — head; ratna — jewels; dyubhih — by the rays; hata-dhvānta — darkness dissipated; yuga-anta — devastation; toye — in the water.

TRANSLATION

Brahmā could see that on the water there was a gigantic lotuslike white bedstead, the body of Śesha-nāga, on which the Personality of Godhead was lying alone. The whole atmosphere was illuminated by the rays of the jewels bedecking the hood of Śesha-nāga, and that illumination dissipated all the darkness of those regions.
