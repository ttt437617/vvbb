Canto 3: The Status QuoChapter 19: The Killing of the Demon Hiranyāksha

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 3.19.6

tam vyagra-cakram diti-putrādhamena

sva-pārshada-mukhyena vishajjamānam

citrā vāco 'tad-vidām khe-carānām

tatra smāsan svasti te 'mum jahīti

SYNONYMS

tam — unto the Personality of Godhead; vyagra — revolving; cakram — whose discus; diti-putra — son of Diti; adhamena — vile; sva-pārshada — of His associates; mukhyena — with the chief; vishajjamānam — playing; citrāh — various; vācah — expressions; a-tat-vidām — of those who did not know; khe-carānām — flying in the sky; tatra — there; sma āsan — occurred; svasti — fortune; te — unto You; amum — him; jahi — please kill; iti — thus.

TRANSLATION

As the discus began to revolve in the Lord's hands and the Lord contended at close quarters with the chief of His Vaikuntha attendants, who had been born as Hiranyāksha, a vile son of Diti, there issued from every direction strange expressions uttered by those who were witnessing from airplanes. They had no knowledge of the Lord's reality, and they cried, "May victory attend You! Pray dispatch him. Play no more with him."
