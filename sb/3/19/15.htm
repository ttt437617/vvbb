Canto 3: The Status QuoChapter 19: The Killing of the Demon Hiranyāksha

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 3.19.15

vrikne sva-śūle bahudhārinā hareh

pratyetya vistīrnam uro vibhūtimat

pravriddha-roshah sa kathora-mushtinā

nadan prahrityāntaradhīyatāsurah

SYNONYMS

vrikne — when cut; sva-śūle — his trident; bahudhā — to many pieces; arinā — by the Sudarśana cakra; hareh — of the Supreme Personality of Godhead; pratyetya — after advancing toward; vistīrnam — broad; urah — chest; vibhūti-mat — the abode of the goddess of fortune; pravriddha — having been increased; roshah — anger; sah — Hiranyāksha; kathora — hard; mushtinā — with his fist; nadan — roaring; prahritya — after striking; antaradhīyata — disappeared; asurah — the demon.

TRANSLATION

The demon was enraged when his trident was cut to pieces by the discus of the Personality of Godhead. He therefore advanced toward the Lord and, roaring aloud, struck his hard fist against the Lord's broad chest, which bore the mark of Śrīvatsa. Then he went out of sight.

PURPORT

Śrīvatsa is a curl of white hair on the chest of the Lord which is a special sign of His being the Supreme Personality of Godhead. In Vaikunthaloka or in Goloka Vrindāvana, the inhabitants are exactly of the same form as the Personality of Godhead, but by this Śrīvatsa mark on the chest of the Lord He is distinguished from all others.
