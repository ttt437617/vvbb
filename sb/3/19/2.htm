Canto 3: The Status QuoChapter 19: The Killing of the Demon Hiranyāksha

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 3.19.2

tatah sapatnam mukhataś

carantam akuto-bhayam

jaghānotpatya gadayā

hanāv asuram akshajah

SYNONYMS

tatah — then; sapatnam — enemy; mukhatah — in front of Him; carantam — stalking; akutah-bhayam — fearlessly; jaghāna — struck; utpatya — after springing up; gadayā — with His mace; hanau — at the chin; asuram — the demon; aksha-jah — the Lord, who was born from the nostril of Brahmā.

TRANSLATION

The Lord, who had appeared from the nostril of Brahmā, sprang and aimed His mace at the chin of His enemy, the Hiranyāksha demon, who was stalking fearlessly before Him.
