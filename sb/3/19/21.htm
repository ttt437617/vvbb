Canto 3: The Status QuoChapter 19: The Killing of the Demon Hiranyāksha

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 3.19.21

bahubhir yaksha-rakshobhih

patty-aśva-ratha-kuñjaraih

ātatāyibhir utsrishtā

himsrā vāco 'tivaiśasāh

SYNONYMS

bahubhih — by many; yaksha-rakshobhih — Yakshas and Rākshasas; patti — marching on foot; aśva — on horses; ratha — on chariots; kuñjaraih — or on elephants; ātatāyibhih — ruffians; utsrishtāh — were uttered; himsrāh — cruel; vācah — words; ati-vaiśasāh — murderous.

TRANSLATION

Cruel and savage slogans were uttered by hosts of ruffian Yakshas and Rākshasas, who all either marched on foot or rode on horses, elephants or chariots.
