Canto 3: The Status QuoChapter 19: The Killing of the Demon Hiranyāksha

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 3.19.25

tam mushtibhir vinighnantam

vajra-sārair adhokshajah

karena karna-mūle 'han

yathā tvāshtram marut-patih

SYNONYMS

tam — Hiranyāksha; mushtibhih — with his fists; vinighnantam — striking; vajra-sāraih — as hard as a thunderbolt; adhokshajah — Lord Adhokshaja; karena — with the hand; karna-mūle — at the root of the ear; ahan — struck; yathā — as; tvāshtram — the demon Vritra (son of Tvashtā); marut-patih — Indra (lord of the Maruts).

TRANSLATION

The demon now began to strike the Lord with his hard fists, but Lord Adhokshaja slapped him in the root of the ear, even as Indra, the lord of the Maruts, hit the demon Vritra.

PURPORT

The Lord is explained here to be adhokshaja, beyond the reach of all material calculation. Akshaja means "the measurement of our senses," and adhokshaja means "that which is beyond the measurement of our senses."
