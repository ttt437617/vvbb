Canto 3: The Status QuoChapter 32: Entanglement in Fruitive Activities

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 3.32.17

rajasā kuntha-manasah

kāmātmāno 'jitendriyāh

pitrin yajanty anudinam

griheshv abhiratāśayāh

SYNONYMS

rajasā — by the mode of passion; kuntha — full of anxieties; manasah — their minds; kāma-ātmānah — aspiring for sense gratification; ajita — uncontrolled; indriyāh — their senses; pitrin — the forefathers; yajanti — they worship; anudinam — every day; griheshu — in home life; abhirata — engaged; āśayāh — their minds.

TRANSLATION

Such persons, impelled by the mode of passion, are full of anxieties and always aspire for sense gratification due to uncontrolled senses. They worship the forefathers and are busy day and night improving the economic condition of their family, social or national life.
