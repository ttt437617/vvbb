Canto 3: The Status QuoChapter 23: Devahūti's Lamentation

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 3.23.20

hamsa-pārāvata-vrātais

tatra tatra nikūjitam

kritrimān manyamānaih svān

adhiruhyādhiruhya ca

SYNONYMS

hamsa — of swans; pārāvata — of pigeons; vrātaih — with multitudes; tatra tatra — here and there; nikūjitam — vibrated; kritrimān — artificial; manyamānaih — thinking; svān — belonging to their own kind; adhiruhya adhiruhya — rising repeatedly; ca — and.

TRANSLATION

Here and there in that palace were multitudes of live swans and pigeons, as well as artificial swans and pigeons so lifelike that the real swans rose above them again and again, thinking them live birds like themselves. Thus the palace vibrated with the sounds of these birds.
