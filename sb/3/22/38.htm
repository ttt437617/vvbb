Canto 3: The Status QuoChapter 22: The Marriage of Kardama Muni and Devahūti

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 3.22.38

yah prishto munibhih prāha

dharmān nānā-vidhāñ chubhān

nrinām varnāśramānām ca

sarva-bhūta-hitah sadā

SYNONYMS

yah — who; prishtah — being questioned; munibhih — by the sages; prāha — spoke; dharmān — the duties; nānā-vidhān — many varieties; śubhān — auspicious; nrinām — of human society; varna-āśramānām — of the varnas and āśramas; ca — and; sarva-bhūta — for all living beings; hitah — who does welfare; sadā — always.

TRANSLATION

In reply to questions asked by certain sages, he [Svāyambhuva Manu], out of compassion for all living entities, taught the diverse sacred duties of men in general and the different varnas and āśramas.
