Canto 3: The Status QuoChapter 22: The Marriage of Kardama Muni and Devahūti

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 3.22.1

maitreya uvāca

evam āvishkritāśesha-

guna-karmodayo munim

savrīda iva tam samrād

upāratam uvāca ha

SYNONYMS

maitreyah — the great sage Maitreya; uvāca — said; evam — thus; āvishkrita — having been described; aśesha — all; guna — of the virtues; karma — of the activities; udayah — the greatness; munim — the great sage; sa-vrīdah — feeling modest; iva — as though; tam — him (Kardama); samrāt — Emperor Manu; upāratam — silent; uvāca ha — addressed.

TRANSLATION

Śrī Maitreya said: After describing the greatness of the Emperor's manifold qualities and activities, the sage became silent, and the Emperor, feeling modesty, addressed him as follows.
