Canto 3: The Status QuoChapter 17: Victory of Hiranyāksha Over All the Directions of the Universe

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 3.17.22

mano-vīrya-varotsiktam

asrinyam akuto-bhayam

bhītā nililyire devās

tārkshya-trastā ivāhayah

SYNONYMS

manah-vīrya — by mental and bodily strength; vara — by the boon; utsiktam — proud; asrinyam — not able to be checked; akutah-bhayam — fearing no one; bhītāh — frightened; nililyire — hid themselves; devāh — the demigods; tārkshya — Garuda; trastāh — frightened of; iva — like; ahayah — snakes.

TRANSLATION

His mental and bodily strength as well as the boon conferred upon him had made him proud. He feared death at the hands of no one, and there was no checking him. The gods, therefore, were seized with fear at his very sight, and they hid themselves even as snakes hide themselves for fear of Garuda.

PURPORT

The asuras are generally strongly built, as described here, and therefore their mental condition is very sound, and their prowess is also extraordinary. Hiranyāksha and Hiranyakaśipu, having received the boon that they would not be killed by any other living entity within this universe, were almost immortal, and thus they were completely fearless.
