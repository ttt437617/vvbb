Canto 3: The Status QuoChapter 17: Victory of Hiranyāksha Over All the Directions of the Universe

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 3.17.30

paśyāmi nānyam purushāt purātanād

yah samyuge tvām rana-mārga-kovidam

ārādhayishyaty asurarshabhehi tam

manasvino yam grinate bhavādriśāh

SYNONYMS

paśyāmi — I see; na — not; anyam — other; purushāt — than the person; purātanāt — most ancient; yah — who; samyuge — in battle; tvām — to you; rana-mārga — in the tactics of war; kovidam — very much skilled; ārādhayishyati — will give satisfaction; asura-rishabha — O chief of the asuras; ihi — approach; tam — Him; manasvinah — heroes; yam — whom; grinate — praise; bhavādriśāh — like you.

TRANSLATION

You are so skilled in war that I do not see anyone else but the most ancient person, Lord Vishnu, who can give satisfaction in battle to you. Therefore, O chief of the asuras, approach Him, whom even heroes like you mention with praise.

PURPORT

Aggressive materialistic warriors are actually punished by the Supreme Lord for their policy of unnecessarily disturbing world peace. Therefore Varuna advised Hiranyāksha that the right course to satisfy his fighting spirit would be to seek to fight with Vishnu.
