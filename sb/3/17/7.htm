Canto 3: The Status QuoChapter 17: Victory of Hiranyāksha Over All the Directions of the Universe

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 3.17.7

cukrośa vimanā vārdhir

udūrmih kshubhitodarah

sodapānāś ca saritaś

cukshubhuh śushka-pańkajāh

SYNONYMS

cukrośa — wailed aloud; vimanāh — stricken with sorrow; vārdhih — the ocean; udūrmih — high waves; kshubhita — agitated; udarah — the creatures inside; sa-udapānāh — with the drinking water of the lakes and the wells; ca — and; saritah — the rivers; cukshubhuh — were agitated; śushka — withered; pańkajāh — lotus flowers.

TRANSLATION

The ocean with its high waves wailed aloud as if stricken with sorrow, and there was a commotion among the creatures inhabiting the ocean. The rivers and lakes were also agitated, and lotuses withered.
