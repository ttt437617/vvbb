Canto 3: The Status QuoChapter 17: Victory of Hiranyāksha Over All the Directions of the Universe

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 3.17.12

rudanto rāsabha-trastā

nīdād udapatan khagāh

ghoshe 'ranye ca paśavah

śakrin-mūtram akurvata

SYNONYMS

rudantah — shrieking; rāsabha — by the asses; trastāh — frightened; nīdāt — from the nest; udapatan — flew up; khagāh — birds; ghoshe — in the cowshed; aranye — in the woods; ca — and; paśavah — the cattle; śakrit — dung; mūtram — urine; akurvata — passed.

TRANSLATION

Frightened by the braying of the asses, birds flew shrieking from their nests, while cattle in the cowsheds as well as in the woods passed dung and urine.
