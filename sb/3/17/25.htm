Canto 3: The Status QuoChapter 17: Victory of Hiranyāksha Over All the Directions of the Universe

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 3.17.25

tasmin pravishte varunasya sainikā

yādo-ganāh sanna-dhiyah sasādhvasāh

ahanyamānā api tasya varcasā

pradharshitā dūrataram pradudruvuh

SYNONYMS

tasmin pravishte — when he entered the ocean; varunasya — of Varuna; sainikāh — the defenders; yādah-ganāh — the aquatic animals; sanna-dhiyah — depressed; sa-sādhvasāh — with fear; ahanyamānāh — not being hit; api — even; tasya — his; varcasā — by splendor; pradharshitāh — stricken; dūra-taram — far away; pradudruvuh — they ran fast.

TRANSLATION

On his entering the ocean, the aquatic animals who formed the host of Varuna were stricken with fear and ran far away. Thus Hiranyāksha showed his splendor without dealing a blow.

PURPORT

Materialistic demons sometimes appear to be very powerful and are seen to establish their supremacy throughout the world. Here also it appears that Hiranyāksha, by his demoniac strength, actually established his supremacy throughout the universe, and the demigods were afraid of his uncommon power. Not only were the demigods in space afraid of the demons Hiranyakaśipu and Hiranyāksha, but so also were the aquatic animals within the sea.
