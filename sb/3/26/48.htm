Canto 3: The Status QuoChapter 26: Fundamental Principles of Material Nature

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 3.26.48

tejo-guna-viśesho 'rtho

yasya tac cakshur ucyate

ambho-guna-viśesho 'rtho

yasya tad rasanam viduh

bhūmer guna-viśesho 'rtho

yasya sa ghrāna ucyate

SYNONYMS

tejah-guna-viśeshah — the distinctive characteristic of fire (form); arthah — object of perception; yasya — whose; tat — that; cakshuh — the sense of sight; ucyate — is called; ambhah-guna-viśeshah — the distinctive characteristic of water (taste); arthah — object of perception; yasya — whose; tat — that; rasanam — the sense of taste; viduh — they know; bhūmeh guna-viśeshah — the distinctive characteristic of earth (odor); arthah — object of perception; yasya — whose; sah — that; ghrānah — the sense of smell; ucyate — is called.

TRANSLATION

The sense whose object of perception is form, the distinctive characteristic of fire, is the sense of sight. The sense whose object of perception is taste, the distinctive characteristic of water, is known as the sense of taste. Finally, the sense whose object of perception is odor, the distinctive characteristic of earth, is called the sense of smell.
