Canto 3: The Status QuoChapter 26: Fundamental Principles of Material Nature

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 3.26.66

gudam mrityur apānena

nodatishthat tadā virāt

hastāv indro balenaiva

nodatishthat tadā virāt

SYNONYMS

gudam — His anus; mrityuh — the god of death; apānena — with the organ of defecation; na — not; udatishthat — did arise; tadā — even then; virāt — the virāt-purusha; hastau — the two hands; indrah — Lord Indra; balena — with their power to grasp and drop things; eva — indeed; na — not; udatishthat — did arise; tadā — even then; virāt — the virāt-purusha.

TRANSLATION

The god of death entered His anus with the organ of defecation, but the virāt-purusha could not be spurred to activity. The god Indra entered the hands with their power of grasping and dropping things, but the virāt-purusha would not get up even then.
