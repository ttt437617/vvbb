Canto 3: The Status QuoChapter 26: Fundamental Principles of Material Nature

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 3.26.25

sahasra-śirasam sākshād

yam anantam pracakshate

sańkarshanākhyam purusham

bhūtendriya-manomayam

SYNONYMS

sahasra-śirasam — with a thousand heads; sākshāt — directly; yam — whom; anantam — Ananta; pracakshate — they all; sańkarshana-ākhyam — Sańkarshana by name; purusham — the Supreme Personality of Godhead; bhūta — the gross elements; indriya — the senses; manah-mayam — consisting of the mind.

TRANSLATION

The threefold ahańkāra, the source of the gross elements, the senses and the mind, is identical with them because it is their cause. It is known by the name of Sańkarshana, who is directly Lord Ananta with a thousand heads.
