Canto 3: The Status QuoChapter 26: Fundamental Principles of Material Nature

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 3.26.63

vahnir vācā mukham bheje

nodatishthat tadā virāt

ghrānena nāsike vāyur

nodatishthat tadā virāt

SYNONYMS

vahnih — the god of fire; vācā — with the organ of speech; mukham — the mouth; bheje — entered; na — not; udatishthat — did arise; tadā — then; virāt — the virāt-purusha; ghrānena — with the olfactory sense; nāsike — into His two nostrils; vāyuh — the god of the winds; na — not; udatishthat — did arise; tadā — then; virāt — the virāt-purusha.

TRANSLATION

The god of fire entered His mouth with the organ of speech, but the virāt-purusha could not be aroused. Then the god of wind entered His nostrils with the sense of smell, but still the virāt-purusha refused to be awakened.
