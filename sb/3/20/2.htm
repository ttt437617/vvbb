Canto 3: The Status QuoChapter 20: Conversation Between Maitreya and Vidura

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 3.20.2

kshattā mahā-bhāgavatah

krishnasyaikāntikah suhrit

yas tatyājāgrajam krishne

sāpatyam aghavān iti

SYNONYMS

kshattā — Vidura; mahā-bhāgavatah — a great devotee of the Lord; krishnasya — of Lord Krishna; ekāntikah — unalloyed devotee; suhrit — intimate friend; yah — he who; tatyāja — abandoned; agra-jam — his elder brother (King Dhritarāshtra); krishne — toward Krishna; sa-apatyam — along with his one hundred sons; agha-vān — offender; iti — thus.

TRANSLATION

Śaunaka Rishi inquired about Vidura, who was a great devotee and friend of Lord Krishna and who gave up the company of his elder brother because the latter, along with his sons, played tricks against the desires of the Lord.

PURPORT

The incident referred to here is that Vidura left the protection of his elder brother Dhritarāshtra, went traveling everywhere to sacred places and met Maitreya at Hardwar. Śaunaka Rishi here inquires about the topics of the conversation between Maitreya Rishi and Vidura. Vidura's qualification was that he was not only a friend of the Lord but also a great devotee. When Krishna tried to stop the war and mitigate the misunderstanding between the cousin-brothers, they refused to accept His counsel; therefore Kshattā, or Vidura, was unsatisfied with them, and he left the palace. As a devotee, Vidura showed by example that anywhere that Krishna is not honored is a place unfit for human habitation. A devotee may be tolerant regarding his own interests, but he should not be tolerant when there is misbehavior toward the Lord or the Lord's devotee. Here the word aghavān is very significant, for it indicates that the Kauravas, Dhritarāshtra's sons, lost the war because of being sinful in disobeying the instructions of Krishna.
