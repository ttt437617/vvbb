Canto 3: The Status QuoChapter 20: Conversation Between Maitreya and Vidura

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 3.20.27

tvam ekah kila lokānām

klishtānām kleśa-nāśanah

tvam ekah kleśadas teshām

anāsanna-padām tava

SYNONYMS

tvam — You; ekah — alone; kila — indeed; lokānām — of the people; klishtānām — afflicted with miseries; kleśa — the distresses; nāśanah — relieving; tvam ekah — You alone; kleśa-dah — inflicting distress; teshām — on those; anāsanna — not taken shelter; padām — feet; tava — Your.

TRANSLATION

My Lord, You are the only one capable of ending the affliction of the distressed and inflicting agony on those who never resort to Your feet.

PURPORT

The words kleśadas teshām anāsanna-padām tava indicate that the Lord has two concerns. The first is to give protection to persons who take shelter of His lotus feet, and the second is to give trouble to those who are always demoniac and who are inimical toward the Lord. Māyā's function is to give afflictions to the nondevotees. Here Brahmā said, "You are the protector of the surrendered souls; therefore I surrender unto Your lotus feet. Please give me protection from these demons."
