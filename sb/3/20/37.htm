Canto 3: The Status QuoChapter 20: Conversation Between Maitreya and Vidura

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 3.20.37

iti sāyantanīm sandhyām

asurāh pramadāyatīm

pralobhayantīm jagrihur

matvā mūdha-dhiyah striyam

SYNONYMS

iti — in this way; sāyantanīm — the evening; sandhyām — twilight; asurāh — the demons; pramadāyatīm — behaving like a wanton woman; pralobhayantīm — alluring; jagrihuh — seized; matvā — thinking to be; mūdha-dhiyah — unintelligent; striyam — a woman.

TRANSLATION

The asuras, clouded in their understanding, took the evening twilight to be a beautiful woman showing herself in her alluring form, and they seized her.

PURPORT

The asuras are described here as mūdha-dhiyah, meaning that they are captivated by ignorance, just like the ass. The demons were captivated by the false, glaring beauty of this material form, and thus they embraced her.
