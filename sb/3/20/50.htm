Canto 3: The Status QuoChapter 20: Conversation Between Maitreya and Vidura

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 3.20.50

tebhyah so 'srijat svīyam

puram purusham ātmavān

tān drishtvā ye purā srishtāh

praśaśamsuh prajāpatim

SYNONYMS

tebhyah — to them; sah — Lord Brahmā; asrijat — gave; svīyam — his own; puram — body; purusham — human; ātma-vān — self-possessed; tān — them; drishtvā — on seeing; ye — those who; purā — earlier; srishtāh — were created (the demigods, Gandharvas, etc., who were created earlier); praśaśamsuh — applauded; prajāpatim — Brahmā (the lord of created beings).

TRANSLATION

The self-possessed creator gave them his own human form. On seeing the Manus, those who had been created earlier — the demigods, the Gandharvas and so on — applauded Brahmā, the lord of the universe.
