Canto 3: The Status QuoChapter 20: Conversation Between Maitreya and Vidura

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 3.20.20

kshut-tridbhyām upasrishtās te

tam jagdhum abhidudruvuh

mā rakshatainam jakshadhvam

ity ūcuh kshut-trid-arditāh

SYNONYMS

kshut-tridbhyām — by hunger and thirst; upasrishtāh — were overcome; te — the demons (Yakshas and Rākshasas); tam — Lord Brahmā; jagdhum — to eat; abhidudruvuh — ran toward; mā — do not; rakshata — spare; enam — him; jakshadhvam — eat; iti — thus; ūcuh — said; kshut-trit-arditāh — afflicted by hunger and thirst.

TRANSLATION

Overpowered by hunger and thirst, they ran to devour Brahmā from all sides and cried, "Spare him not! Eat him up!"

PURPORT

The representatives of the Yakshas and Rākshasas still exist in some countries of the world. It is understood that such uncivilized men take pleasure in killing their own grandfathers and holding a "love feast" by roasting the bodies.
