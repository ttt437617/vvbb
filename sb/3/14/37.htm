Canto 3: The Status QuoChapter 14: Pregnancy of Diti in the Evening

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 3.14.37

maitreya uvāca

sva-sargasyāśisham lokyām

āśāsānām pravepatīm

nivritta-sandhyā-niyamo

bhāryām āha prajāpatih

SYNONYMS

maitreyah uvāca — the great sage Maitreya said; sva-sargasya — of her own children; āśisham — welfare; lokyām — in the world; āśāsānām — desiring; pravepatīm — while trembling; nivritta — averted from; sandhyā-niyamah — the rules and regulations of evening; bhāryām — unto the wife; āha — said; prajāpatih — the progenitor.

TRANSLATION

Maitreya said: The great sage Kaśyapa thus addressed his wife, who was trembling because of fear that her husband was offended. She understood that he had been dissuaded from his daily duties of offering evening prayers, yet she desired the welfare of her children in the world.
