Canto 3: The Status QuoChapter 13: The Appearance of Lord Varāha

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 3.13.22

drishto 'ńgushtha-śiro-mātrah

kshanād ganda-śilā-samah

api svid bhagavān esha

yajño me khedayan manah

SYNONYMS

drishtah — just seen; ańgushtha — thumb; śirah — tip; mātrah — only; kshanāt — immediately; ganda-śilā — large stone; samah — like; api svit — whether; bhagavān — the Personality of Godhead; eshah — this; yajñah — Vishnu; me — my; khedayan — perturbing; manah — mind.

TRANSLATION

First of all this boar was seen no bigger than the tip of a thumb, and within a moment He was as large as a stone. My mind is perturbed. Is He the Supreme Personality of Godhead, Vishnu?

PURPORT

Since Brahmā is the supermost person in the universe and he had never before experienced such a form, he could guess that the wonderful appearance of the boar was an incarnation of Vishnu. The uncommon features symptomatic of the incarnation of Godhead can bewilder even the mind of Brahmā.
