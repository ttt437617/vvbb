Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 1.13.36

vimrijyāśrūni pānibhyām

vishtabhyātmānam ātmanā

ajāta-śatrum pratyūce

prabhoh pādāv anusmaran

SYNONYMS

vimrijya — smearing; aśrūni — tears of the eyes; pānibhyām — with his hands; vishtabhya — situated; ātmānam — the mind; ātmanā — by intelligence; ajāta-śatrum — unto Mahārāja Yudhishthira; pratyūce — began to reply; prabhoh — of his master; pādau — feet; anusmaran — thinking after.

TRANSLATION

First he slowly pacified his mind by intelligence, and wiping away his tears and thinking of the feet of his master, Dhritarāshtra, he began to reply to Mahārāja Yudhishthira.
