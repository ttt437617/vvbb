Canto 1: CreationChapter 11: Lord Krishna's Entrance into Dvārakā

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 1.11.29

tāh putram ańkam āropya

sneha-snuta-payodharāh

harsha-vihvalitātmānah

sishicur netrajair jalaih

SYNONYMS

tāh — all of them; putram — the son; ańkam — the lap; āropya — having placed on; sneha-snuta — moistened by affection; payodharāh — breasts filled up; harsha — delight; vihvalita-ātmānah — overwhelmed by; sishicuh — wet; netrajaih — from the eyes; jalaih — water.

TRANSLATION

The mothers, after embracing their son, sat Him on their laps. Due to pure affection, milk sprang from their breasts. They were overwhelmed with delight, and the tears from their eyes wetted the Lord.

PURPORT

When Lord Krishna was at Vrindāvana even the cows would become moistened by affection towards Him, and He would draw milk from the nipples of every affectionate living being, so what to speak of the stepmothers who were already as good as His own mother.
