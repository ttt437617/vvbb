Canto 1: CreationChapter 15: The Pāndavas Retire Timely

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 1.15.46

te sādhu-krita-sarvārthā

jñātvātyantikam ātmanah

manasā dhārayām āsur

vaikuntha-caranāmbujam

SYNONYMS

te — all of them; sādhu-krita — having performed everything worthy of a saint; sarva-arthāh — that which includes everything worthy; jñātvā — knowing it well; ātyantikam — the ultimate; ātmanah — of the living being; manasā — within the mind; dhārayām āsuh — sustained; vaikuntha — the Lord of the spiritual sky; carana-ambujam — the lotus feet.

TRANSLATION

They all had performed all the principles of religion and as a result rightly decided that the lotus feet of the Lord Śrī Krishna are the supreme goal of all. Therefore they meditated upon His feet without interruption.

PURPORT

In the Bhagavad-gītā (7.28) the Lord says that only those who have done pious deeds in previous lives and have become freed from the results of all impious acts can concentrate upon the lotus feet of the Supreme Lord Śrī Krishna. The Pāndavas, not only in this life but also in their previous lives, had always performed the supreme pious work, and thus they are ever free from all the reactions of impious work. It is quite reasonable, therefore, that they concentrated their minds upon the lotus feet of the Supreme Lord Śrī Krishna. According to Śrī Viśvanātha Cakravartī, dharma, artha, kāma and moksha principles are accepted by persons who are not free from the results of impious action. Such persons affected with the contaminations of the above four principles cannot at once accept the lotus feet of the Lord in the spiritual sky. The Vaikuntha world is situated far beyond the material sky. The material sky is under the management of Durgā Devī, or the material energy of the Lord, but the Vaikuntha world is managed by the personal energy of the Lord.
