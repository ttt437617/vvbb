Canto 1: CreationChapter 16: How Parīkshit Received the Age of Kali

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 1.16.36

tayor evam kathayatoh

prithivī-dharmayos tadā

parīkshin nāma rājarshih

prāptah prācīm sarasvatīm

SYNONYMS

tayoh — between them; evam — thus; kathayatoh — engaged in conversation; prithivī — earth; dharmayoh — and the personality of religion; tadā — at that time; parīkshit — King Parīkshit; nāma — of the name; rāja-rishih — a saint amongst kings; prāptah — arrived; prācīm — flowing towards the east; sarasvatīm — River Sarasvatī.

TRANSLATION

While the earth and the personality of religion were thus engaged in conversation, the saintly King Parīkshit reached the shore of the Sarasvatī River, which flowed towards the east.

Thus end the Bhaktivedanta purports of the First Canto, Sixteenth Chapter, of the Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam, entitled "How Parīkshit Received the Age of Kali."
