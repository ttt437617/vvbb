Canto 1: CreationChapter 18: Mahārāja Parīkshit Cursed by a Brāhmana Boy

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 1.18.36

ity uktvā rosha-tāmrāksho

vayasyān rishi-bālakah

kauśiky-āpa upaspriśya

vāg-vajram visasarja ha

SYNONYMS

iti — thus; uktvā — saying; rosha-tāmra-akshah — with red-hot eyes due to being angry; vayasyān — unto the playmates; rishi-bālakah — the son of a rishi; kauśikī — the River Kauśika; āpah — water; upaspriśya — by touching; vāk — words; vajram — thunderbolt; visasarja — threw; ha — in the past.

TRANSLATION

The son of the rishi, his eyes red-hot with anger, touched the water of the River Kauśika while speaking to his playmates and discharged the following thunderbolt of words.

PURPORT

The circumstances under which Mahārāja Parīkshit was cursed were simply childish, as it appears from this verse. Śrińgi was showing his impudency amongst his playmates, who were innocent. Any sane man would have prevented him from doing such great harm to all human society. By killing a king like Mahārāja Parīkshit, just to make a show of acquired brahminical power, the inexperienced son of a brāhmana committed a great mistake.
