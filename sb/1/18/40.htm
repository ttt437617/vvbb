Canto 1: CreationChapter 18: Mahārāja Parīkshit Cursed by a Brāhmana Boy

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 1.18.40

visrijya tam ca papraccha

vatsa kasmād dhi rodishi

kena vā te 'pakritam

ity uktah sa nyavedayat

SYNONYMS

visrijya — throwing aside; tam — that; ca — also; papraccha — asked; vatsa — my dear son; kasmāt — what for; hi — certainly; rodishi — crying; kena — by whom; vā — otherwise; te — they; apakritam — misbehaved; iti — thus; uktah — being asked; sah — the boy; nyavedayat — informed of everything.

TRANSLATION

He threw the dead snake aside and asked his son why he was crying, whether anyone had done him harm. On hearing this, the son explained to him what had happened.

PURPORT

The father did not take the dead snake on his neck very seriously. He simply threw it away. Actually there was nothing seriously wrong in Mahārāja Parīkshit's act, but the foolish son took it very seriously, and being influenced by Kali he cursed the King and thus ended a chapter of happy history.
