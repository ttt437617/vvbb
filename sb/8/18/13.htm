Canto 8: Withdrawal of the Cosmic CreationsChapter 18: Lord Vāmanadeva, the Dwarf Incarnation

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 8.18.13

tam vatum vāmanam drishtvā

modamānā maharshayah

karmāni kārayām āsuh

puraskritya prajāpatim

SYNONYMS

tam — Him; vatum — the brahmacārī; vāmanam — dwarf; drishtvā — seeing; modamānāh — in a happy mood; mahā-rishayah — the great saintly persons; karmāni — ritualistic ceremonies; kārayām āsuh — performed; puraskritya — keeping in front; prajāpatim — Kaśyapa Muni, the Prajāpati.

TRANSLATION

When the great sages saw the Lord as the brahmacārī-dwarf Vāmana, they were certainly very pleased. Thus they placed before them Kaśyapa Muni, the Prajāpati, and performed all the ritualistic ceremonies, such as the birthday ceremony.

PURPORT

According to Vedic civilization, when a child is born in the family of a brāhmana, the birthday ceremony, known as jāta-karma, is first performed, and then other ceremonies are also gradually performed. But when this vāmana-rūpa appeared in the form of a vatu, or brahmacārī, His sacred thread ceremony was also performed immediately.
