Canto 8: Withdrawal of the Cosmic CreationsChapter 18: Lord Vāmanadeva, the Dwarf Incarnation

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 8.18.22

te ritvijo yajamānah sadasyā

hata-tvisho vāmana-tejasā nripa

sūryah kilāyāty uta vā vibhāvasuh

sanat-kumāro 'tha didrikshayā kratoh

SYNONYMS

te — all of them; ritvijah — the priests; yajamānah — as well as Bali Mahārāja, who had engaged them in performing the yajña; sadasyāh — all the members of the assembly; hata-tvishah — decreased in their bodily effulgence; vāmana-tejasā — by the brilliant effulgence of Lord Vāmana; nripa — O King; sūryah — the sun; kila — whether; āyāti — is coming; uta vā — either; vibhāvasuh — the fire-god; sanat-kumārah — the Kumāra known as Sanat-kumāra; atha — either; didrikshayā — with a desire to observe; kratoh — the sacrificial ceremony.

TRANSLATION

O King, because of Vāmanadeva's bright effulgence, the priests, along with Bali Mahārāja and all the members of the assembly, were robbed of their splendor. Thus they began to ask one another whether the sun-god himself, Sanat-kumāra or the fire-god had personally come to see the sacrificial ceremony.
