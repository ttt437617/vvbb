Canto 8: Withdrawal of the Cosmic CreationsChapter 18: Lord Vāmanadeva, the Dwarf Incarnation

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 8.18.17

tasmā ity upanītāya

yaksha-rāt pātrikām adāt

bhikshām bhagavatī sākshād

umādād ambikā satī

SYNONYMS

tasmai — unto Him (Lord Vāmanadeva); iti — in this way; upanītāya — who had undergone His sacred thread ceremony; yaksha-rāt — Kuvera, the treasurer of heaven and King of the Yakshas; pātrikām — a pot for begging alms; adāt — delivered; bhikshām — alms; bhagavatī — mother Bhavānī, the wife of Lord Śiva; sākshāt — directly; umā — Umā; adāt — gave; ambikā — the mother of the universe; satī — the chaste.

TRANSLATION

When Vāmanadeva had thus been given the sacred thread, Kuvera, King of the Yakshas, gave Him a pot for begging alms, and mother Bhagavatī, the wife of Lord Śiva and most chaste mother of the entire universe, gave Him His first alms.
