Canto 8: Withdrawal of the Cosmic CreationsChapter 7: Lord Śiva Saves the Universe by Drinking Poison

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 8.7.45

niśamya karma tac chambhor

deva-devasya mīdhushah

prajā dākshāyanī brahmā

vaikunthaś ca śaśamsire

SYNONYMS

niśamya — after hearing; karma — the act; tat — that; śambhoh — of Lord Śiva; deva-devasya — who is worshipable even for the demigods; mīdhushah — he who bestows great benedictions upon people in general; prajāh — the people in general; dākshāyanī — Bhavānī, the daughter of Daksha; brahmā — Lord Brahmā; vaikunthah ca — Lord Vishnu also; śaśamsire — praised very much.

TRANSLATION

Upon hearing of this act, everyone, including Bhavānī [the daughter of Mahārāja Daksha], Lord Brahmā, Lord Vishnu, and the people in general, very highly praised this deed performed by Lord Śiva, who is worshiped by the demigods and who bestows benedictions upon the people.
