Canto 8: Withdrawal of the Cosmic CreationsChapter 8: The Churning of the Milk Ocean

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 8.8.3

tata uccaihśravā nāma

hayo 'bhūc candra-pāndurah

tasmin balih sprihām cakre

nendra īśvara-śikshayā

SYNONYMS

tatah — thereafter; uccaihśravāh nāma — by the name Uccaihśravā; hayah — a horse; abhūt — was generated; candra-pāndurah — being as white as the moon; tasmin — unto it; balih — Mahārāja Bali; sprihām cakre — desired to possess; na — not; indrah — the King of the demigods; īśvara-śikshayā — by the previous advice of the Lord.

TRANSLATION

Thereafter, a horse named Uccaihśravā, which was as white as the moon, was generated. Bali Mahārāja desired to possess this horse, and Indra, the King of heaven, did not protest, for he had previously been so advised by the Supreme Personality of Godhead.
