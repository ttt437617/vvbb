Canto 8: Withdrawal of the Cosmic CreationsChapter 8: The Churning of the Milk Ocean

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 8.8.12

rishayah kalpayām cakrur

ābhishekam yathā-vidhi

jagur bhadrāni gandharvā

natyaś ca nanritur jaguh

SYNONYMS

rishayah — the great sages; kalpayām cakruh — executed; ābhishekam — the abhisheka ceremony, which is required during the installation of the Deity; yathā-vidhi — as directed in the authorized scriptures; jaguh — chanted Vedic mantras; bhadrāni — all good fortune; gandharvāh — and the inhabitants of Gandharvaloka; natyah — the women who were professional dancers; ca — also; nanrituh — very nicely danced on the occasion; jaguh — and sang authorized songs prescribed in the Vedas.

TRANSLATION

The great sages performed the bathing ceremony of the goddess of fortune as directed in the authorized scriptures, the Gandharvas chanted all-auspicious Vedic mantras, and the professional women dancers very nicely danced and sang authorized songs prescribed in the Vedas.
