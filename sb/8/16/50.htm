Canto 8: Withdrawal of the Cosmic CreationsChapter 16: Executing the Payo-vrata Process of Worship

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 8.16.50

trayodaśyām atho vishnoh

snapanam pañcakair vibhoh

kārayec chāstra-drishtena

vidhinā vidhi-kovidaih

SYNONYMS

trayodaśyām — on the thirteenth day of the moon; atho — thereafter; vishnoh — of Lord Vishnu; snapanam — bathing; pañcakaih — by pañcāmrita, five substances; vibhoh — the Lord; kārayet — one should execute; śāstra-drishtena — enjoined in the scripture; vidhinā — under regulative principles; vidhi-kovidaih — assisted by the priests who know the regulative principles.

TRANSLATION

Thereafter, following the directions of the śāstra with help from brāhmanas who know the śāstra, on the thirteenth day of the moon one should bathe Lord Vishnu with five substances [milk, yogurt, ghee, sugar and honey].
