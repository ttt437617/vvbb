Canto 8: Withdrawal of the Cosmic CreationsChapter 16: Executing the Payo-vrata Process of Worship

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 8.16.26

sinīvālyām mridālipya

snāyāt kroda-vidīrnayā

yadi labhyeta vai srotasy

etam mantram udīrayet

SYNONYMS

sinīvālyām — on the dark-moon day; mridā — with dirt; ālipya — smearing the body; snāyāt — one should bathe; kroda-vidīrnayā — dug up by the tusk of a boar; yadi — if; labhyeta — it is available; vai — indeed; srotasi — in a flowing river; etam mantram — this mantra; udīrayet — one should chant.

TRANSLATION

If dirt dug up by a boar is available, on the day of the dark moon one should smear this dirt on his body and then bathe in a flowing river. While bathing, one should chant the following mantra.
