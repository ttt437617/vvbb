Canto 8: Withdrawal of the Cosmic CreationsChapter 6: The Demigods and Demons Declare a Truce

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 8.6.1

śrī-śuka uvāca

evam stutah sura-ganair

bhagavān harir īśvarah

teshām āvirabhūd rājan

sahasrārkodaya-dyutih

SYNONYMS

śrī-śukah uvāca — Śrī Śukadeva Gosvāmī said; evam — in this way; stutah — being worshiped by prayers; sura-ganaih — by the demigods; bhagavān — the Supreme Personality of Godhead; harih — the vanquisher of all inauspiciousness; īśvarah — the supreme controller; teshām — in front of Lord Brahmā and all the demigods; āvirabhūt — appeared there; rājan — O King (Parīkshit); sahasra — of thousands; arka — of suns; udaya — like the rising; dyutih — His effulgence.

TRANSLATION

Śrī Śukadeva Gosvāmī said: O King Parīkshit, the Supreme Personality of Godhead, Hari, being thus worshiped with prayers by the demigods and Lord Brahmā, appeared before them. His bodily effulgence resembled the simultaneous rising of thousands of suns.
