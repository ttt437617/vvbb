Canto 8: Withdrawal of the Cosmic CreationsChapter 6: The Demigods and Demons Declare a Truce

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 8.6.30

mahendrah ślakshnayā vācā

sāntvayitvā mahā-matih

abhyabhāshata tat sarvam

śikshitam purushottamāt

SYNONYMS

mahā-indrah — the King of heaven, Indra; ślakshnayā — very mild; vācā — by words; sāntvayitvā — pleasing Bali Mahārāja very much; mahā-matih — the most intelligent person; abhyabhāshata — addressed; tat — that; sarvam — everything; śikshitam — that was learned; purusha-uttamāt — from Lord Vishnu.

TRANSLATION

After pleasing Bali Mahārāja with mild words, Lord Indra, the King of the demigods, who was most intelligent, very politely submitted all the proposals he had learned from the Supreme Personality of Godhead, Lord Vishnu.
