Canto 8: Withdrawal of the Cosmic CreationsChapter 6: The Demigods and Demons Declare a Truce

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 8.6.16

śrī-śuka uvāca

evam viriñcādibhir īditas tad

vijñāya teshām hridayam yathaiva

jagāda jīmūta-gabhīrayā girā

baddhāñjalīn samvrita-sarva-kārakān

SYNONYMS

śrī-śukah uvāca — Śrī Śukadeva Gosvāmī said; evam — thus; viriñca-ādibhih — by all the demigods, headed by Lord Brahmā; īditah — being worshiped; tat vijñāya — understanding the expectation; teshām — of all of them; hridayam — the core of the heart; yathā — as; eva — indeed; jagāda — replied; jīmūta-gabhīrayā — like the sound of clouds; girā — by words; baddha-añjalīn — unto the demigods, who stood with folded hands; samvrita — restrained; sarva — all; kārakān — senses.

TRANSLATION

Śukadeva Gosvāmī continued: When the Lord was thus offered prayers by the demigods, headed by Lord Brahmā, He understood the purpose for which they had approached Him. Therefore, in a deep voice that resembled the rumbling of clouds, the Lord replied to the demigods, who all stood there attentively with folded hands.
