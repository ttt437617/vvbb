Canto 8: Withdrawal of the Cosmic CreationsChapter 21: Bali Mahārāja Arrested by the Lord

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 8.21.30

yāvat tapaty asau gobhir

yāvad induh sahodubhih

yāvad varshati parjanyas

tāvatī bhūr iyam tava

SYNONYMS

yāvat — as far as; tapati — is shining; asau — the sun; gobhih — by the sunshine; yāvat — as long or as far as; induh — the moon; saha-udubhih — with the luminaries or stars; yāvat — as far as; varshati — are pouring rain; parjanyah — the clouds; tāvatī — to that much distance; bhūh — land; iyam — this; tava — in your possession.

TRANSLATION

As far as the sun and moon shine with the stars and as far as the clouds pour rain, all the land throughout the universe is in your possession.
