Canto 8: Withdrawal of the Cosmic CreationsChapter 11: King Indra Annihilates the Demons

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 8.11.10

śrī-śuka uvāca

ity ākshipya vibhum vīro

nārācair vīra-mardanah

ākarna-pūrnairahanad

ākshepair āha tam punah

SYNONYMS

śrī-śukah uvāca — Śrī Śukadeva Gosvāmī said; iti — thus; ākshipya — chastising; vibhum — unto King Indra; vīrah — the valiant Bali Mahārāja; nārācaih — by the arrows named nārācas; vīra-mardanah — Bali Mahārāja, who could subdue even great heros; ākarna-pūrnaih — drawn up to his ear; ahanat — attacked; ākshepaih — by words of chastisement; āha — said; tam — unto him; punah — again.

TRANSLATION

Śukadeva Gosvāmī said: After thus rebuking Indra, King of heaven, with sharp words, Bali Mahārāja, who could subdue any other hero, drew back to his ear the arrows known as nārācas and attacked Indra with these arrows. Then he again chastised Indra with strong words.
