Canto 8: Withdrawal of the Cosmic CreationsChapter 11: King Indra Annihilates the Demons

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 8.11.37

iti śakram vishīdantam

āha vāg aśarīrinī

nāyam śushkair atho nārdrair

vadham arhati dānavah

SYNONYMS

iti — in this way; śakram — unto Indra; vishīdantam — lamenting; āha — spoke; vāk — a voice; aśarīrinī — without any body, or from the sky; na — not; ayam — this; śushkaih — by anything dry; atho — also; na — nor; ārdraih — by anything moist; vadham — annihilation; arhati — is befitting; dānavah — this demon (Namuci).

TRANSLATION

Śukadeva Gosvāmī continued: While the morose Indra was lamenting in this way, an ominous, unembodied voice said from the sky, "This demon Namuci is not to be annihilated by anything dry or moist."
