Canto 8: Withdrawal of the Cosmic CreationsChapter 13: Description of Future Manus

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 8.13.9

tritīyām vadavām eke

tāsām samjñā-sutās trayah

yamo yamī śrāddhadevaś

chāyāyāś ca sutāñ chrinu

SYNONYMS

tritīyām — the third wife; vadavām — Vadavā; eke — some people; tāsām — of all three wives; samjñā-sutāh trayah — three issues of Samjñā; yamah — one son named Yama; yamī — Yamī, a daughter; śrāddhadevah — Śrāddhadeva, another son; chāyāyāh — of Chāyā; ca — and; sutān — the sons; śrinu — just hear about.

TRANSLATION

It is said that the sun-god had a third wife, named Vadavā. Of the three wives, the wife named Samjñā had three children — Yama, Yamī and Śrāddhadeva. Now let me describe the children of Chāyā.
