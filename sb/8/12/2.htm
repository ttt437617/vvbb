Canto 8: Withdrawal of the Cosmic CreationsChapter 12: The Mohinī-mūrti Incarnation Bewilders Lord Śiva

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 8.12.1-2

śrī-bādarāyanir uvāca

vrisha-dhvajo niśamyedam

yoshid-rūpena dānavān

mohayitvā sura-ganān

harih somam apāyayat

vrisham āruhya giriśah

sarva-bhūta-ganair vritah

saha devyā yayau drashtum

yatrāste madhusūdanah

SYNONYMS

śrī-bādarāyanih uvāca — Śrī Śukadeva Gosvāmī said; vrisha-dhvajah — Lord Śiva, who is carried by a bull; niśamya — hearing; idam — this (news); yoshit-rūpena — by assuming the form of a woman; dānavān — the demons; mohayitvā — enchanting; sura-ganān — unto the demigods; harih — the Supreme Personality of Godhead; somam — nectar; apāyayat — caused to drink; vrisham — the bull; āruhya — mounting; giriśah — Lord Śiva; sarva — all; bhūta-ganaih — by the ghosts; vritah — surrounded; saha devyā — with Umā; yayau — went; drashtum — to see; yatra — where; āste — stays; madhusūdanah — Lord Vishnu.

TRANSLATION

Śukadeva Gosvāmī said: The Supreme Personality of Godhead, Hari, in the form of a woman, captivated the demons and enabled the demigods to drink the nectar. After hearing of these pastimes, Lord Śiva, who is carried by a bull, went to the place where Madhusūdana, the Lord, resides. Accompanied by his wife, Umā, and surrounded by his companions, the ghosts, Lord Śiva went there to see the Lord's form as a woman.
