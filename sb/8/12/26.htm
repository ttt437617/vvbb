Canto 8: Withdrawal of the Cosmic CreationsChapter 12: The Mohinī-mūrti Incarnation Bewilders Lord Śiva

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 8.12.26

sā tam āyāntam ālokya

vivastrā vrīditā bhriśam

nilīyamānā vriksheshu

hasantī nānvatishthata

SYNONYMS

sā — that woman; tam — Lord Śiva; āyāntam — who was coming near; ālokya — seeing; vivastrā — She was naked; vrīditā — very bashful; bhriśam — so much; nilīyamānā — was hiding; vriksheshu — among the trees; hasantī — smiling; na — not; anvatishthata — stood in one place.

TRANSLATION

The beautiful woman was already naked, and when She saw Lord Śiva coming toward Her, She became extremely bashful. Thus She kept smiling, but She hid Herself among the trees and did not stand in one place.
