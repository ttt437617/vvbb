Canto 8: Withdrawal of the Cosmic CreationsChapter 15: Bali Mahārāja Conquers the Heavenly Planets

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 8.15.13

hamsa-sārasa-cakrāhva-

kārandava-kulākulāh

nalinyo yatra krīdanti

pramadāh sura-sevitāh

SYNONYMS

hamsa — of swans; sārasa — cranes; cakrāhva — birds known as cakravākas; kārandava — and water fowl; kula — by groups; ākulāh — congested; nalinyah — lotus flowers; yatra — where; krīdanti — enjoyed sporting; pramadāh — beautiful women; sura-sevitāh — protected by the demigods.

TRANSLATION

Beautiful women protected by the demigods sported in the gardens, which had lotus ponds full of swans, cranes, cakravākas and ducks.
