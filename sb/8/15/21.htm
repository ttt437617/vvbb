Canto 8: Withdrawal of the Cosmic CreationsChapter 15: Bali Mahārāja Conquers the Heavenly Planets

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 8.15.21

mridańga-śańkhānaka-dundubhi-svanaih

satāla-vīnā-murajeshta-venubhih

nrityaih savādyair upadeva-gītakair

manoramām sva-prabhayā jita-prabhām

SYNONYMS

mridańga — of drums; śańkha — conchshells; ānaka-dundubhi — and kettledrums; svanaih — by the sounds; sa-tāla — in perfect tune; vīnā — a stringed instrument; muraja — a kind of drum; ishta-venubhih — accompanied by the very nice sound of the flute; nrityaih — with dancing; sa-vādyaih — with concert instruments; upadeva-gītakaih — with singing by the secondary demigods like the Gandharvas; manoramām — beautiful and pleasing; sva-prabhayā — by its own brilliance; jita-prabhām — the personification of beauty was conquered.

TRANSLATION

The city was filled with the sounds of mridańgas, conchshells, kettledrums, flutes and well-tuned stringed instruments all playing in concert. There was constant dancing and the Gandharvas sang. The combined beauty of Indrapurī defeated beauty personified.
