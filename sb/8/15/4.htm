Canto 8: Withdrawal of the Cosmic CreationsChapter 15: Bali Mahārāja Conquers the Heavenly Planets

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 8.15.4

tam brāhmanā bhrigavah prīyamānā

ayājayan viśvajitā tri-nākam

jigīshamānam vidhinābhishicya

mahābhishekena mahānubhāvāh

SYNONYMS

tam — upon him (Bali Mahārāja); brāhmanāh — all the brāhmanas; bhrigavah — the descendants of Bhrigu Muni; prīyamānāh — being very pleased; ayājayan — engaged him in performing a sacrifice; viśvajitā — known as Viśvajit; tri-nākam — the heavenly planets; jigīshamānam — desiring to conquer; vidhinā — according to regulative principles; abhishicya — after purifying; mahā-abhishekena — by bathing him in a great abhisheka ceremony; mahā-anubhāvāh — the exalted brāhmanas.

TRANSLATION

The brāhmana descendants of Bhrigu Muni were very pleased with Bali Mahārāja, who desired to conquer the kingdom of Indra. Therefore, after purifying him and properly bathing him according to regulative principles, they engaged him in performing the yajña known as Viśvajit.
