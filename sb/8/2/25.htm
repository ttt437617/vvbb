Canto 8: Withdrawal of the Cosmic CreationsChapter 2: The Elephant Gajendra's Crisis

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 8.2.25

vigāhya tasminn amritāmbu nirmalam

hemāravindotpala-renu-rūshitam

papau nikāmam nija-pushkaroddhritam

ātmānam adbhih snapayan gata-klamah

SYNONYMS

vigāhya — entering; tasmin — into the lake; amrita-ambu — water as pure as nectar; nirmalam — crystal clear; hema — very cold; aravinda-utpala — from the lilies and lotuses; renu — with the dust; rūshitam — which was mixed; papau — he drank; nikāmam — until fully satisfied; nija — own; pushkara-uddhritam — drawing with his trunk; ātmānam — himself; adbhih — with water; snapayan — bathing thoroughly; gata-klamah — was relieved of all fatigue.

TRANSLATION

The King of the elephants entered the lake, bathed thoroughly and was relieved of his fatigue. Then, with the aid of his trunk, he drank the cold, clear, nectarean water, which was mixed with the dust of lotus flowers and water lilies, until he was fully satisfied.
