Canto 8: Withdrawal of the Cosmic CreationsChapter 2: The Elephant Gajendra's Crisis

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 8.2.22

vrikā varāhā mahisharksha-śalyā

gopuccha-śālāvrika-markatāś ca

anyatra kshudrā harināh śaśādayaś

caranty abhītā yad-anugrahena

SYNONYMS

vrikāh — foxes; varāhāh — boars; mahisha — buffalos; riksha — bears; śalyāh — porcupines; gopuccha — a type of deer; śālāvrika — wolves; markatāh — monkeys; ca — and; anyatra — elsewhere; kshudrāh — small animals; harināh — deer; śaśa-ādayah — rabbits and others; caranti — roaming (in the forest); abhītāh — without fear; yat-anugrahena — by the mercy of that elephant.

TRANSLATION

By the mercy of this elephant, animals like the foxes, wolves, buffalos, bears, boars, gopucchas, porcupines, monkeys, rabbits, the other deer and many other small animals loitered elsewhere in the forest. They were not afraid of him.

PURPORT

All the animals were practically controlled by this elephant, yet although they could move without fear, because of respect they did not stand before him.
