Canto 8: Withdrawal of the Cosmic CreationsChapter 2: The Elephant Gajendra's Crisis

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 8.2.26

sa pushkarenoddhrita-śīkarāmbubhir

nipāyayan samsnapayan yathā grihī

ghrinī karenuh karabhāmś ca durmado

nācashta kricchram kripano 'ja-māyayā

SYNONYMS

sah — he (the leader of the elephants); pushkarena — with his trunk; uddhrita — by drawing out; śīkara-ambubhih — and sprinkling the water; nipāyayan — causing them to drink; samsnapayan — and bathing them; yathā — as; grihī — a householder; ghrinī — always kind (to the members of his family); karenuh — to his wives, the female elephants; karabhān — to the children; ca — as well as; durmadah — who is too attached to the members of his family; na — not; ācashta — considered; kricchram — hardship; kripanah — being without spiritual knowledge; aja-māyayā — because of the influence of the external, illusory energy of the Supreme Personality of Godhead.

TRANSLATION

Like a human being who lacks spiritual knowledge and is too attached to the members of his family, the elephant, being illusioned by the external energy of Krishna, had his wives and children bathe and drink the water. Indeed, he raised water from the lake with his trunk and sprayed it over them. He did not mind the hard labor involved in this endeavor.
