Canto 8: Withdrawal of the Cosmic CreationsChapter 2: The Elephant Gajendra's Crisis

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 8.2.1

śrī-śuka uvāca

āsīd girivaro rājams

trikūta iti viśrutah

kshīrodenāvritah śrīmān

yojanāyutam ucchritah

SYNONYMS

śrī-śukah uvāca — Śrī Śukadeva Gosvāmī said; āsīt — there was; girivarah — a very big mountain; rājan — O King; tri-kūtah — Trikūta; iti — thus; viśrutah — celebrated; kshīra-udena — by the ocean of milk; āvritah — surrounded; śrīmān — very beautiful; yojana — a measurement of eight miles; ayutam — ten thousand; ucchritah — very high.

TRANSLATION

Śukadeva Gosvāmī said: My dear King, there is a very large mountain called Trikūta. It is ten thousand yojanas [eighty thousand miles] high. Being surrounded by the ocean of milk, it is very beautifully situated.
