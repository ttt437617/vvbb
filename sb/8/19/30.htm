Canto 8: Withdrawal of the Cosmic CreationsChapter 19: Lord Vāmanadeva Begs Charity from Bali Mahārāja

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 8.19.30

śrī-śukra uvāca

esha vairocane sākshād

bhagavān vishnur avyayah

kaśyapād aditer jāto

devānām kārya-sādhakah

SYNONYMS

śrī-śukrah uvāca — Śukrācārya said; eshah — this (boy in the form of a dwarf); vairocane — O son of Virocana; sākshāt — directly; bhagavān — the Supreme Personality of Godhead; vishnuh — Lord Vishnu; avyayah — without deterioration; kaśyapāt — from His father, Kaśyapa; aditeh — in the womb of His mother, Aditi; jātah — was born; devānām — of the demigods; kārya-sādhakah — working in the interest.

TRANSLATION

Śukrācārya said: O son of Virocana, this brahmacārī in the form of a dwarf is directly the imperishable Supreme Personality of Godhead, Vishnu. Accepting Kaśyapa Muni as His father and Aditi as His mother, He has now appeared in order to fulfill the interests of the demigods.
