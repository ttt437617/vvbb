Canto 8: Withdrawal of the Cosmic CreationsChapter 19: Lord Vāmanadeva Begs Charity from Bali Mahārāja

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 8.19.11

sa tan-niketam parimriśya śūnyam

apaśyamānah kupito nanāda

kshmām dyām diśah kham vivarān samudrān

vishnum vicinvan na dadarśa vīrah

SYNONYMS

sah — that Hiranyakaśipu; tat-niketam — the residence of Lord Vishnu; parimriśya — searching; śūnyam — vacant; apaśyamānah — not seeing Lord Vishnu; kupitah — being very angry; nanāda — cried very loudly; kshmām — on the surface of the earth; dyām — in outer space; diśah — in all directions; kham — in the sky; vivarān — in all the caves; samudrān — all the oceans; vishnum — Lord Vishnu; vicinvan — searching for; na — not; dadarśa — did see; vīrah — although he was very powerful.

TRANSLATION

Upon seeing that the residence of Lord Vishnu was vacant, Hiranyakaśipu began searching for Lord Vishnu everywhere. Angry at not seeing Him, Hiranyakaśipu screamed loudly and searched the entire universe, including the surface of the earth, the higher planetary systems, all directions and all the caves and oceans. But Hiranyakaśipu, the greatest hero, did not see Vishnu anywhere.
