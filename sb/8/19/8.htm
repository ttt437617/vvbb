Canto 8: Withdrawal of the Cosmic CreationsChapter 19: Lord Vāmanadeva Begs Charity from Bali Mahārāja

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 8.19.8

tam āyāntam samālokya

śūla-pānim kritāntavat

cintayām āsa kāla-jño

vishnur māyāvinām varah

SYNONYMS

tam — him (Hiranyakaśipu); āyāntam — coming forward; samālokya — observing minutely; śūla-pānim — with a trident in his hand; kritānta-vat — just like death personified; cintayām āsa — thought; kāla-jñah — who knows the progress of time; vishnuh — Lord Vishnu; māyāvinām — of all kinds of mystics; varah — the chief.

TRANSLATION

Seeing Hiranyakaśipu coming forward bearing a trident in his hand like personified death, Lord Vishnu, the best of all mystics and the knower of the progress of time, thought as follows.
