Canto 8: Withdrawal of the Cosmic CreationsChapter 19: Lord Vāmanadeva Begs Charity from Bali Mahārāja

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 8.19.29

vishnave kshmām pradāsyantam

uśanā asureśvaram

jānamś cikīrshitam vishnoh

śishyam prāha vidām varah

SYNONYMS

vishnave — unto Lord Vishnu (Vāmanadeva); kshmām — the land; pradāsyantam — who was ready to deliver; uśanāh — Śukrācārya; asura-īśvaram — unto the King of the demons (Bali Mahārāja); jānan — knowing well; cikīrshitam — what was the plan; vishnoh — of Lord Vishnu; śishyam — unto his disciple; prāha — said; vidām varah — the best of the knowers of everything.

TRANSLATION

Understanding Lord Vishnu's purpose, Śukrācārya, the best of the learned, immediately spoke as follows to his disciple, who was about to offer everything to Lord Vāmanadeva.
