Canto 8: Withdrawal of the Cosmic CreationsChapter 19: Lord Vāmanadeva Begs Charity from Bali Mahārāja

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 8.19.26

yadricchā-lābha-tushtasya

tejo viprasya vardhate

tat praśāmyaty asantoshād

ambhasevāśuśukshanih

SYNONYMS

yadricchā-lābha-tushtasya — who is satisfied by things obtained by the grace of God; tejah — the brilliant effulgence; viprasya — of a brāhmana; vardhate — increases; tat — that (effulgence); praśāmyati — is diminished; asantoshāt — because of dissatisfaction; ambhasā — by pouring of water; iva — as; āśuśukshanih — a fire.

TRANSLATION

A brāhmana who is satisfied with whatever is providentially obtained is increasingly enlightened with spiritual power, but the spiritual potency of a dissatisfied brāhmana decreases, as fire diminishes in potency when water is sprinkled upon it.
