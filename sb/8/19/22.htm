Canto 8: Withdrawal of the Cosmic CreationsChapter 19: Lord Vāmanadeva Begs Charity from Bali Mahārāja

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 8.19.22

tribhih kramair asantushto

dvīpenāpi na pūryate

nava-varsha-sametena

sapta-dvīpa-varecchayā

SYNONYMS

tribhih — three; kramaih — by steps; asantushtah — one who is dissatisfied; dvīpena — by a complete island; api — although; na pūryate — cannot be satisfied; nava-varsha-sametena — even by possessing nine varshas; sapta-dvīpa-vara-icchayā — by the desire to take possession of seven islands.

TRANSLATION

If I were not satisfied with three paces of land, then surely I would not be satisfied even with possessing one of the seven islands, consisting of nine varshas. Even if I possessed one island, I would hope to get others.
