Canto 8: Withdrawal of the Cosmic CreationsChapter 24: Matsya, the Lord's Fish Incarnation

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 8.24.39

ittham ādiśya rājānam

harir antaradhīyata

so 'nvavaikshata tam kālam

yam hrishīkeśa ādiśat

SYNONYMS

ittham — as aforementioned; ādiśya — instructing; rājānam — the King (Satyavrata); harih — the Supreme Personality of Godhead; antaradhīyata — disappeared from that place; sah — he (the King); anvavaikshata — began to wait for; tam kālam — that time; yam — which; hrishīka-īśah — Lord Hrishīkeśa, the master of all the senses; ādiśat — instructed.

TRANSLATION

After thus instructing the King, the Supreme Personality of Godhead immediately disappeared. Then King Satyavrata began to wait for that time of which the Lord had instructed.
