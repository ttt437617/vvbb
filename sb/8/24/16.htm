Canto 8: Withdrawal of the Cosmic CreationsChapter 24: Matsya, the Lord's Fish Incarnation

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 8.24.16

tasyā dīnataram vākyam

āśrutya sa mahīpatih

kalaśāpsu nidhāyainām

dayālur ninya āśramam

SYNONYMS

tasyāh — of the fish; dīna-taram — pitiable; vākyam — words; āśrutya — hearing; sah — that; mahī-patih — the King; kalaśa-apsu — in the water contained in the water jug; nidhāya — taking; enām — the fish; dayāluh — merciful; ninye — brought; āśramam — to his residence.

TRANSLATION

The merciful King, being moved by the pitiable words of the fish, placed the fish in a water jug and brought Him to his own residence.
