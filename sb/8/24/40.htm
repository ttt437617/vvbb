Canto 8: Withdrawal of the Cosmic CreationsChapter 24: Matsya, the Lord's Fish Incarnation

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 8.24.40

āstīrya darbhān prāk-kūlān

rājarshih prāg-udań-mukhah

nishasāda hareh pādau

cintayan matsya-rūpinah

SYNONYMS

āstīrya — spreading; darbhān — kuśa grass; prāk-kūlān — the upper portion facing east; rāja-rishih — Satyavrata, the saintly King; prāk-udak-mukhah — looking toward the northeast (īśāna); nishasāda — sat down; hareh — of the Supreme Personality of Godhead; pādau — upon the lotus feet; cintayan — meditating; matsya-rūpinah — who had assumed the form of a fish.

TRANSLATION

After spreading kuśa with its tips pointing east, the saintly King, himself facing the northeast, sat down on the grass and began to meditate upon the Supreme Personality of Godhead, Vishnu, who had assumed the form of a fish.
