Canto 8: Withdrawal of the Cosmic CreationsChapter 24: Matsya, the Lord's Fish Incarnation

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 8.24.6

uccāvaceshu bhūteshu

caran vāyur iveśvarah

noccāvacatvam bhajate

nirgunatvād dhiyo gunaih

SYNONYMS

ucca-avaceshu — having higher or lower bodily forms; bhūteshu — among the living entities; caran — behaving; vāyuh iva — exactly like the air; īśvarah — the Supreme Lord; na — not; ucca-avacatvam — the quality of higher or lower grades of life; bhajate — accepts; nirgunatvāt — because of being transcendental, above all material qualities; dhiyah — generally; gunaih — by the modes of material nature.

TRANSLATION

Like the air passing through different types of atmosphere, the Supreme Personality of Godhead, although appearing sometimes as a human being and sometimes as a lower animal, is always transcendental. Because He is above the material modes of nature, He is unaffected by higher and lower forms.

PURPORT

The Supreme Personality of Godhead is the master of the material nature (mayādhyakshena prakritih sūyate sacarācaram [Bg. 9.10]). Therefore, being the supreme controller of the laws of nature, the Lord cannot be under their influence. An example given in this regard is that although the wind blows through many places, the air is not affected by the qualities of these places. Although the air sometimes carries the odor of a filthy place, the air has nothing to do with such a place. Similarly, the Supreme Personality of Godhead, being all-good and all-auspicious, is never affected by the material qualities like an ordinary living entity. purushah prakriti-stho hi bhuńkte prakritijān gunān (Bg. 13.21). When the living entity is in the material nature, he is affected by its qualities. The Supreme Personality of Godhead, however, is not affected. Disrespectfully, one who does not know this considers the Supreme Personality of Godhead an ordinary living being (avajānanti mām mūdhāh). Param bhāvam ajānantah: such a conclusion is reached by the unintelligent because they are unaware of the transcendental qualities of the Lord.
