Canto 8: Withdrawal of the Cosmic CreationsChapter 24: Matsya, the Lord's Fish Incarnation

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 8.24.13

satyavrato 'ñjali-gatām

saha toyena bhārata

utsasarja nadī-toye

śapharīm dravideśvarah

SYNONYMS

satyavratah — King Satyavrata; añjali-gatām — in the water held in the palms of the King; saha — with; toyena — water; bhārata — O King Parīkshit; utsasarja — threw; nadī-toye — in the water of the river; śapharīm — that small fish; dravida-īśvarah — Satyavrata, the King of Dravida.

TRANSLATION

Satyavrata, the King of Dravidadeśa, threw the fish into the water of the river along with the water in his palm, O King Parīkshit, descendant of Bharata.
