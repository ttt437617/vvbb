Canto 8: Withdrawal of the Cosmic CreationsChapter 24: Matsya, the Lord's Fish Incarnation

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 8.24.44

so 'nudhyātas tato rājñā

prādurāsīn mahārnave

eka-śrińga-dharo matsyo

haimo niyuta-yojanah

SYNONYMS

sah — the Lord; anudhyātah — being meditated upon; tatah — thereafter (hearing the words of the saintly brāhmanas); rājñā — by the King; prādurāsīt — appeared (before him); mahā-arnave — in the great ocean of inundation; eka-śrińga-dharah — with one horn; matsyah — a big fish; haimah — made of gold; niyuta-yojanah — eight million miles long.

TRANSLATION

Then, while the King constantly meditated upon the Supreme Personality of Godhead, a large golden fish appeared in the ocean of inundation. The fish had one horn and was eight million miles long.
