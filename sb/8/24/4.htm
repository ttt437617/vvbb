Canto 8: Withdrawal of the Cosmic CreationsChapter 24: Matsya, the Lord's Fish Incarnation

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 8.24.4

śrī-sūta uvāca

ity ukto vishnu-rātena

bhagavān bādarāyanih

uvāca caritam vishnor

matsya-rūpena yat kritam

SYNONYMS

śrī-sūtah uvāca — Śrī Sūta Gosvāmī said; iti uktah — thus being questioned; vishnu-rātena — by Mahārāja Parīkshit, known as Vishnurāta; bhagavān — the most powerful; bādarāyanih — the son of Vyāsadeva, Śukadeva Gosvāmī; uvāca — said; caritam — the pastimes; vishnoh — of Lord Vishnu; matsya-rūpena — by Him in the form of a fish; yat — whatever; kritam — was done.

TRANSLATION

Sūta Gosvāmī said: When Parīkshit Mahārāja thus inquired from Śukadeva Gosvāmī, that most powerful saintly person began describing the pastimes of the Lord's incarnation as a fish.
