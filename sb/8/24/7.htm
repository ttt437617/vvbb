Canto 8: Withdrawal of the Cosmic CreationsChapter 24: Matsya, the Lord's Fish Incarnation

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 8.24.7

āsīd atīta-kalpānte

brāhmo naimittiko layah

samudropaplutās tatra

lokā bhūr-ādayo nripa

SYNONYMS

āsīt — there was; atīta — past; kalpa-ante — at the end of the kalpa; brāhmah — of Lord Brahmā's day; naimittikah — because of that; layah — inundation; samudra — in the ocean; upaplutāh — were inundated; tatra — there; lokāh — all the planets; bhūh-ādayah — Bhūh, Bhuvah and Svah, the three lokas; nripa — O King.

TRANSLATION

O King Parīkshit, at the end of the past millennium, at the end of Brahmā's day, because Lord Brahmā sleeps during the night, annihilation took place, and the three worlds were covered by the water of the ocean.
