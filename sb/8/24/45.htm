Canto 8: Withdrawal of the Cosmic CreationsChapter 24: Matsya, the Lord's Fish Incarnation

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 8.24.45

nibadhya nāvam tac-chrińge

yathokto harinā purā

varatrenāhinā tushtas

tushtāva madhusūdanam

SYNONYMS

nibadhya — anchoring; nāvam — the boat; tat-śrińge — onto the horn of the big fish; yathā-uktah — as advised; harinā — by the Supreme Personality of Godhead; purā — before; varatrena — used as a rope; ahinā — by the great serpent (of the name Vāsuki); tushtah — being pleased; tushtāva — he satisfied; madhusūdanam — the Supreme Lord, the killer of Madhu.

TRANSLATION

Following the instructions formerly given by the Supreme Personality of Godhead, the King anchored the boat to the fish's horn, using the serpent Vāsuki as a rope. Thus being satisfied, he began offering prayers to the Lord.
