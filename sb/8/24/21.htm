Canto 8: Withdrawal of the Cosmic CreationsChapter 24: Matsya, the Lord's Fish Incarnation

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 8.24.21

tata ādāya sā rājñā

kshiptā rājan sarovare

tad āvrityātmanā so 'yam

mahā-mīno 'nvavardhata

SYNONYMS

tatah — from there; ādāya — taking away; sā — the fish; rājñā — by the King; kshiptā — being thrown; rājan — O King (Mahārāja Parīkshit); sarovare — in a lake; tat — that; āvritya — covering; ātmanā — by the body; sah — the fish; ayam — this; mahā-mīnah — gigantic fish; anvavardhata — immediately developed.

TRANSLATION

O Mahārāja Parīkshit, the King took the fish from the well and threw Him in a lake, but the fish then assumed a gigantic form exceeding the extent of the water.
