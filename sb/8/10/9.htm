Canto 8: Withdrawal of the Cosmic CreationsChapter 10: The Battle Between the Demigods and the Demons

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 8.10.9

ushtraih kecid ibhaih kecid

apare yuyudhuh kharaih

kecid gaura-mukhair rikshair

dvīpibhir haribhir bhatāh

SYNONYMS

ushtraih — on the backs of camels; kecit — some persons; ibhaih — on the backs of elephants; kecit — some persons; apare — others; yuyudhuh — engaged in fighting; kharaih — on the backs of asses; kecit — some persons; gaura-mukhaih — on white-faced monkeys; rikshaih — on red-faced monkeys; dvīpibhih — on the backs of tigers; haribhih — on the backs of lions; bhatāh — all the soldiers engaged in this way.

TRANSLATION

Some soldiers fought on the backs of camels, some on the backs of elephants, some on asses, some on white-faced and red-faced monkeys, some on tigers and some on lions. In this way, they all engaged in fighting.
