Canto 8: Withdrawal of the Cosmic CreationsChapter 10: The Battle Between the Demigods and the Demons

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 8.10.39

śirobhir uddhūta-kirīta-kundalaih

samrambha-drigbhih paridashta-dacchadaih

mahā-bhujaih sābharanaih sahāyudhaih

sā prāstritā bhūh karabhorubhir babhau

SYNONYMS

śirobhih — by the heads; uddhūta — separated, scattered from; kirīta — having their helmets; kundalaih — and earrings; samrambha-drigbhih — eyes staring in anger (although the heads were severed from their bodies); paridashta — having been bitten by the teeth; dacchadaih — the lips; mahā-bhujaih — with big arms; sa-ābharanaih — decorated with ornaments; saha-āyudhaih — and with weapons in their hands, although the hands were severed; sā — that battlefield; prāstritā — scattered; bhūh — the warfield; karabha-ūrubhih — and with thighs and legs resembling the trunks of elephants; babhau — it so became.

TRANSLATION

In the course of the battle, the warfield became strewn with the severed heads of heroes, their eyes still staring and their teeth still pressed against their lips in anger. Helmets and earrings were scattered from these severed heads. Similarly, many arms, decorated with ornaments and clutching various weapons, were strewn here and there, as were many legs and thighs, which resembled the trunks of elephants.
