Canto 8: Withdrawal of the Cosmic CreationsChapter 10: The Battle Between the Demigods and the Demons

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 8.10.1

śrī-śuka uvāca

iti dānava-daiteyā

nāvindann amritam nripa

yuktāh karmani yattāś ca

vāsudeva-parāńmukhāh

SYNONYMS

śrī-śukah uvāca — Śrī Śukadeva Gosvāmī said; iti — thus; dānava-daiteyāh — the asuras and the demons; na — not; avindan — achieved (the desired result); amritam — nectar; nripa — O King; yuktāh — all being combined; karmani — in the churning; yattāh — engaged with full attention and effort; ca — and; vāsudeva — of the Supreme Personality of Godhead, Krishna; parāńmukhāh — because of being nondevotees.

TRANSLATION

Śukadeva Gosvāmī said: O King, the demons and Daityas all engaged with full attention and effort in churning the ocean, but because they were not devotees of Vāsudeva, the Supreme Personality of Godhead, Krishna, they were not able to drink the nectar.
