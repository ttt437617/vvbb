Canto 8: Withdrawal of the Cosmic CreationsChapter 10: The Battle Between the Demigods and the Demons

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 8.10.50

srishto daityena sumahān

vahnih śvasana-sārathih

sāmvartaka ivātyugro

vibudha-dhvajinīm adhāk

SYNONYMS

srishtah — created; daityena — by the demon (Bali Mahārāja); su-mahān — very great, devastating; vahnih — a fire; śvasana-sārathih — being carried by the blasting wind; sāmvartakah — the fire named Sāmvartaka, which appears during the time of dissolution; iva — just like; ati — very much; ugrah — terrible; vibudha — of the demigods; dhvajinīm — the soldiers; adhāk — burned to ashes.

TRANSLATION

A great devastating fire created by Bali Mahārāja began burning all the soldiers of the demigods. This fire, accompanied by blasting winds, seemed as terrible as the Sāmvartaka fire, which appears at the time of dissolution.
