Canto 8: Withdrawal of the Cosmic CreationsChapter 1: The Manus, Administrators of the Universe

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 8.1.31

śrī-rājovāca

bādarāyana etat te

śrotum icchāmahe vayam

harir yathā gaja-patim

grāha-grastam amūmucat

SYNONYMS

śrī-rājā uvāca — King Parīkshit said; bādarāyane — O son of Bādarāyana (Vyāsadeva); etat — this; te — from you; śrotum icchāmahe — desire to hear; vayam — we; harih — the Lord Hari; yathā — the manner in which; gaja-patim — the King of the elephants; grāha-grastam — when attacked by the crocodile; amūmucat — delivered.

TRANSLATION

King Parīkshit said: My lord, Bādarāyani, we wish to hear from you in detail how the King of the elephants, when attacked by a crocodile, was delivered by Hari.
