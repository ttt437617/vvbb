Canto 8: Withdrawal of the Cosmic CreationsChapter 1: The Manus, Administrators of the Universe

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 8.1.29

devā vaidhritayo nāma

vidhrites tanayā nripa

nashtāh kālena yair vedā

vidhritāh svena tejasā

SYNONYMS

devāh — the demigods; vaidhritayah — the Vaidhritis; nāma — by the name; vidhriteh — of Vidhriti; tanayāh — who were the sons; nripa — O King; nashtāh — were lost; kālena — by the influence of time; yaih — by whom; vedāh — the Vedas; vidhritāh — were protected; svena — by their own; tejasā — power.

TRANSLATION

O King, in the Tāmasa manvantara the sons of Vidhriti, who were known as the Vaidhritis, also became demigods. Since in course of time the Vedic authority was lost, these demigods, by their own powers, protected the Vedic authority.

PURPORT

In the Tāmasa manvantara there were two kinds of demigods, and one of them was known as the Vaidhritis. The duty of the demigods is to protect the authority of the Vedas. The word devatā refers to one who carries the authority of the Vedas, whereas Rākshasas are those who defy the Vedic authority. If the authority of the Vedas is lost, the entire universe becomes chaotic. Therefore, it is the duty of the demigods, as well as kings and aides of governments, to give full protection to the Vedic authority; otherwise human society will be in a chaotic condition in which there cannot be peace or prosperity.
