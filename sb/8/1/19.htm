Canto 8: Withdrawal of the Cosmic CreationsChapter 1: The Manus, Administrators of the Universe

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 8.1.19

svārocisho dvitīyas tu

manur agneh suto 'bhavat

dyumat-sushena-rocishmat

pramukhās tasya cātmajāh

SYNONYMS

svārocishah — Svārocisha; dvitīyah — the second; tu — indeed; manuh — Manu; agneh — of Agni; sutah — the son; abhavat — became; dyumat — Dyumat; sushena — Sushena; rocishmat — Rocishmat; pramukhāh — beginning with them; tasya — of him (Svārocisha); ca — also; ātma-jāh — sons.

TRANSLATION

The son of Agni named Svārocisha became the second Manu. His several sons were headed by Dyumat, Sushena and Rocishmat.

PURPORT

manvantaram manur devā

manu-putrāh sureśvarah

rishayo 'mśāvatāraś ca

hareh shad vidham ucyate

There are many incarnations of the Supreme Personality of Godhead. Manu, the manu-putrāh (the sons of Manu), the king of the heavenly planets, and the seven great sages are all partial incarnations of the Supreme Lord. Manu himself, his sons Priyavrata and Uttānapāda, the demigods created by Daksha, and the rishis like Marīci were all partial incarnations of the Lord during the reign of Svāyambhuva Manu. During that time, the incarnation of the Lord as Yajña took charge of ruling the heavenly planets. The next Manu was Svārocisha. The Manus and the sages and demigods are further described in the following eleven verses.
