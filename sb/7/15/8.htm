Canto 7: The Science of GodChapter 15: Instructions for Civilized Human Beings

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 7.15.8

naitādriśah paro dharmo

nrinām sad-dharmam icchatām

nyāso dandasya bhūteshu

mano-vāk-kāyajasya yah

SYNONYMS

na — never; etādriśah — like this; parah — a supreme or superior; dharmah — religion; nrinām — of persons; sat-dharmam — superior religion; icchatām — being desirous of; nyāsah — giving up; dandasya — causing trouble because of envy; bhūteshu — unto the living entities; manah — in terms of the mind; vāk — words; kāya-jasya — and body; yah — which.

TRANSLATION

Persons who want to advance in superior religion are advised to give up all envy of other living entities, whether in relationship to the body, words or mind. There is no religion superior to this.
