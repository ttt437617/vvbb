Canto 7: The Science of GodChapter 12: The Perfect Society: Four Spiritual Classes

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 7.12.4

mekhalājina-vāsāmsi

jatā-danda-kamandalūn

bibhriyād upavītam ca

darbha-pānir yathoditam

SYNONYMS

mekhalā — a belt made of straw; ajina-vāsāmsi — garments made of deerskin; jatā — matted hair; danda — a rod; kamandalūn — and a waterpot known as a kamandalu; bibhriyāt — he (the brahmacārī) should regularly carry or wear; upavītam ca — and a sacred thread; darbha-pānih — taking purified kuśa in his hand; yathā uditam — as recommended in the śāstras.

TRANSLATION

Carrying pure kuśa grass in his hand, the brahmacārī should dress regularly with a belt of straw and with deerskin garments. He should wear matted hair, carry a rod and waterpot and be decorated with a sacred thread, as recommended in the śāstras.
