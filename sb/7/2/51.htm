Canto 7: The Science of GodChapter 2: Hiranyakaśipu, King of the Demons

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 7.2.51

kulińga-mithunam tatra

vicarat samadriśyata

tayoh kulińgī sahasā

lubdhakena pralobhitā

SYNONYMS

kulińga-mithunam — a pair of (male and female) birds known as kulińga; tatra — there (where the hunter was hunting); vicarat — wandering; samadriśyata — he saw; tayoh — of the pair; kulińgī — the female bird; sahasā — suddenly; lubdhakena — by the hunter; pralobhitā — allured.

TRANSLATION

While wandering in the forest, the hunter saw a pair of kulińga birds. Of the two, the female was captivated by the hunter's lure.
