Canto 7: The Science of GodChapter 2: Hiranyakaśipu, King of the Demons

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 7.2.54

kāmam nayatu mām devah

kim ardhenātmano hi me

dīnena jīvatā duhkham

anena vidhurāyushā

SYNONYMS

kāmam — as He likes; nayatu — let Him take away; mām — me; devah — the Supreme Lord; kim — what use; ardhena — with half; ātmanah — of the body; hi — indeed; me — my; dīnena — poor; jīvatā — living; duhkham — in suffering; anena — this; vidhura-āyushā — having a lifetime full of affliction.

TRANSLATION

If unkind Providence takes away my wife, who is half my body, why should He not take me also? What is the use of my living with half of my body, bereaved by loss of my wife? What shall I gain in this way?
