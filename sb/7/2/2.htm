Canto 7: The Science of GodChapter 2: Hiranyakaśipu, King of the Demons

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 7.2.2

āha cedam rushā pūrnah

sandashta-daśana-cchadah

kopojjvaladbhyām cakshurbhyām

nirīkshan dhūmram ambaram

SYNONYMS

āha — said; ca — and; idam — this; rushā — with anger; pūrnah — full; sandashta — bitten; daśana-chadah — whose lips; kopa-ujjvaladbhyām — blazing with anger; cakshurbhyām — with eyes; nirīkshan — looking over; dhūmram — smoky; ambaram — the sky.

TRANSLATION

Filled with rage and biting his lips, Hiranyakaśipu gazed at the sky with eyes that blazed in anger, making the whole sky smoky. Thus he began to speak.

PURPORT

As usual, the demon is envious of the Supreme Personality of Godhead and inimical toward Him. These were Hiranyakaśipu's external bodily features as he considered how to kill Lord Vishnu and devastate His kingdom, Vaikunthaloka.
