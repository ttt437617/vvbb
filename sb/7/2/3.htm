Canto 7: The Science of GodChapter 2: Hiranyakaśipu, King of the Demons

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 7.2.3

karāla-damshtrogra-drishtyā

dushprekshya-bhrukutī-mukhah

śūlam udyamya sadasi

dānavān idam abravīt

SYNONYMS

karāla-damshtra — with terrible teeth; ugra-drishtyā — and fierce glance; dushprekshya — horrible to see; bhru-kutī — with frowning eyebrows; mukhah — whose face; śūlam — trident; udyamya — raising; sadasi — in the assembly; dānavān — to the demons; idam — this; abravīt — spoke.

TRANSLATION

Exhibiting his terrible teeth, fierce glance and frowning eyebrows, terrible to see, he took up his weapon, a trident, and thus began speaking to his associates, the assembled demons.
