Canto 7: The Science of GodChapter 7: What Prahlāda Learned in the Womb

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 7.7.12

tato me mātaram rishih

samānīya nijāśrame

āśvāsyehoshyatām vatse

yāvat te bhartur āgamah

SYNONYMS

tatah — thereafter; me — my; mātaram — mother; rishih — the great saint Nārada Rishi; samānīya — bringing; nija-āśrame — to his own āśrama; āśvāsya — giving her assurance; iha — here; ushyatām — stay; vatse — my dear child; yāvat — until; te — your; bhartuh — of the husband; āgamah — the coming.

TRANSLATION

Prahlāda Mahārāja continued: The great saint Nārada Muni brought my mother to his āśrama and assured her of all protection, saying, "My dear child, please remain at my āśrama until the arrival of your husband."
