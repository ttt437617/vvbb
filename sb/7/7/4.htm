Canto 7: The Science of GodChapter 7: What Prahlāda Learned in the Womb

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 7.7.4-5

teshām atibalodyogam

niśamyāsura-yūthapāh

vadhyamānāh surair bhītā

dudruvuh sarvato diśam

kalatra-putra-vittāptān

grihān paśu-paricchadān

nāvekshyamānās tvaritāh

sarve prāna-parīpsavah

SYNONYMS

teshām — of the demigods, headed by King Indra; atibala-udyogam — the great exertion and strength; niśamya — hearing of; asura-yūthapāh — the great leaders of the demons; vadhyamānāh — being killed one after another; suraih — by the demigods; bhītāh — afraid; dudruvuh — ran away; sarvatah — in all; diśam — directions; kalatra — wives; putra-vitta — children and wealth; āptān — relatives; grihān — homes; paśu-paricchadān — animals and paraphernalia of household life; na — not; avekshyamānāh — seeing to; tvaritāh — very hasty; sarve — all of them; prāna-parīpsavah — very much desiring to live.

TRANSLATION

When the great leaders of the demons, who were being killed one after another, saw the unprecedented exertion of the demigods in fighting, they began to flee, scattering themselves in all directions. Simply to protect their lives, they hastily fled from their homes, wives, children, animals and household paraphernalia. Paying no heed to all these, the demons simply fled.
