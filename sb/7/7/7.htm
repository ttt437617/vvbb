Canto 7: The Science of GodChapter 7: What Prahlāda Learned in the Womb

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 7.7.7

nīyamānām bhayodvignām

rudatīm kurarīm iva

yadricchayāgatas tatra

devarshir dadriśe pathi

SYNONYMS

nīyamānām — being taken away; bhaya-udvignām — disturbed and full of fear; rudatīm — crying; kurarīm iva — like a kurarī (osprey); yadricchayā — by chance; āgatah — arrived; tatra — on the spot; deva-rishih — the great saint Nārada; dadriśe — he saw; pathi — on the road.

TRANSLATION

As she was being led away, crying in fear like a kurarī captured by a vulture, the great sage Nārada, who at that time had no engagement, appeared on the scene and saw her in that condition.
