Canto 7: The Science of GodChapter 11: The Perfect Society: Four Social Classes

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 7.11.1

śrī-śuka uvāca

śrutvehitam sādhu sabhā-sabhājitam

mahattamāgranya urukramātmanah

yudhishthiro daitya-pater mudānvitah

papraccha bhūyas tanayam svayambhuvah

SYNONYMS

śrī-śukah uvāca — Śrī Śukadeva Gosvāmī said; śrutvā — hearing; īhitam — the narration; sādhu sabhā-sabhājitam — which is discussed in assemblies of great devotees like Lord Brahmā and Lord Śiva; mahat-tama-agranyah — the best of the saintly persons (Yudhishthira); urukrama-ātmanah — of he (Prahlāda Mahārāja) whose mind is always engaged upon the Supreme Personality of Godhead, who always acts uncommonly; yudhishthirah — King Yudhishthira; daitya-pateh — of the master of the demons; mudā-anvitah — in a pleasing mood; papraccha — inquired; bhūyah — again; tanayam — unto the son; svayambhuvah — of Lord Brahmā.

TRANSLATION

Śukadeva Gosvāmī continued: After hearing about the activities and character of Prahlāda Mahārāja, which are adored and discussed among great personalities like Lord Brahmā and Lord Śiva, Yudhishthira Mahārāja, the most respectful king among exalted personalities, again inquired from the great saint Nārada Muni in a mood of great pleasure.
