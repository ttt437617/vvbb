Canto 7: The Science of GodChapter 11: The Perfect Society: Four Social Classes

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 7.11.22

śauryam vīryam dhritis tejas

tyāgaś cātmajayah kshamā

brahmanyatā prasādaś ca

satyam ca kshatra-lakshanam

SYNONYMS

śauryam — power in battle; vīryam — being unconquerable; dhritih — patience (even in reverses, a kshatriya is very grave); tejah — ability to defeat others; tyāgah — giving charity; ca — and; ātma-jayah — not being overwhelmed by bodily necessities; kshamā — forgiveness; brahmanyatā — faithfulness to the brahminical principles; prasādah — jolliness in any condition of life; ca — and; satyam ca — and truthfulness; kshatra-lakshanam — these are the symptoms of a kshatriya.

TRANSLATION

To be influential in battle, unconquerable, patient, challenging and charitable, to control the bodily necessities, to be forgiving, to be attached to the brahminical nature and to be always jolly and truthful — these are the symptoms of the kshatriya.
