Canto 7: The Science of GodChapter 11: The Perfect Society: Four Social Classes

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 7.11.15

vaiśyas tu vārtā-vrittih syān

nityam brahma-kulānugah

śūdrasya dvija-śuśrūshā

vrittiś ca svāmino bhavet

SYNONYMS

vaiśyah — the mercantile community; tu — indeed; vārtā-vrittih — engaged in agriculture, cow protection, and trade; syāt — must be; nityam — always; brahma-kula-anugah — following the directions of the brāhmanas; śūdrasya — of the fourth-grade persons, the workers; dvija-śuśrūshā — the service of the three higher sections (the brāhmanas, kshatriyas and vaiśyas); vrittih — means of livelihood; ca — and; svāminah — of the master; bhavet — he must be.

TRANSLATION

The mercantile community should always follow the directions of the brāhmanas and engage in such occupational duties as agriculture, trade, and protection of cows. For the śūdras the only duty is to accept a master from a higher social order and engage in his service.
