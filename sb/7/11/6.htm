Canto 7: The Science of GodChapter 11: The Perfect Society: Four Social Classes

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 7.11.6

yo 'vatīryātmano 'mśena

dākshāyanyām tu dharmatah

lokānām svastaye 'dhyāste

tapo badarikāśrame

SYNONYMS

yah — He who (Lord Nārāyana); avatīrya — adventing; ātmanah — of Himself; amśena — with a part (Nara); dākshāyanyām — in the womb of Dākshāyanī, the daughter of Mahārāja Daksha; tu — indeed; dharmatah — from Dharma Mahārāja; lokānām — of all people; svastaye — for the benefit of; adhyāste — executes; tapah — austerity; badarikāśrame — in the place known as Badarikāśrama.

TRANSLATION

Lord Nārāyana, along with His partial manifestation Nara, appeared in this world through the daughter of Daksha Mahārāja known as Mūrti. He was begotten by Dharma Mahārāja for the benefit of all living entities. Even now, He is still engaged in executing great austerities near the place known as Badarikāśrama.
