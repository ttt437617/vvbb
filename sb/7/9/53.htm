Canto 7: The Science of GodChapter 9: Prahlāda Pacifies Lord Nrisimhadeva with Prayers

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 7.9.53

mām aprīnata āyushman

darśanam durlabham hi me

drishtvā mām na punar jantur

ātmānam taptum arhati

SYNONYMS

mām — Me; aprīnatah — not pleasing; āyushman — O long-living Prahlāda; darśanam — seeing; durlabham — very rare; hi — indeed; me — of Me; drishtvā — after seeing; mām — Me; na — not; punah — again; jantuh — the living entity; ātmānam — for himself; taptum — to lament; arhati — deserves.

TRANSLATION

My dear Prahlāda, may you live a long time. One cannot appreciate or understand Me without pleasing Me, but one who has seen or pleased Me has nothing more for which to lament for his own satisfaction.

PURPORT

One cannot be happy under any circumstances unless one pleases the Supreme Personality of Godhead, but one who has learned how to please the Supreme Lord need no longer lament for his material condition.
