Canto 7: The Science of GodChapter 4: Hiranyakaśipu Terrorizes the Universe

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 7.4.45

putrān vipratikūlān svān

pitarah putra-vatsalāh

upālabhante śikshārtham

naivāgham aparo yathā

SYNONYMS

putrān — sons; vipratikūlān — who act against the will of the father; svān — their own; pitarah — fathers; putra-vatsalāh — being very affectionate to the children; upālabhante — chastise; śiksha-artham — to teach them lessons; na — not; eva — indeed; agham — punishment; aparah — an enemy; yathā — like.

TRANSLATION

A father and mother are always affectionate to their children. When the children are disobedient the parents chastise them, not due to enmity but only for the child's instruction and welfare. How did Hiranyakaśipu, the father of Prahlāda Mahārāja, chastise such a noble son? This is what I am eager to know.
