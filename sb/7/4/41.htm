Canto 7: The Science of GodChapter 4: Hiranyakaśipu Terrorizes the Universe

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 7.4.41

kvacid utpulakas tūshnīm

āste samsparśa-nirvritah

aspanda-pranayānanda-

salilāmīlitekshanah

SYNONYMS

kvacit — sometimes; utpulakah — with the hairs of his body standing on end; tūshnīm — completely silent; āste — remains; samsparśa-nirvritah — feeling great joy by contact with the Lord; aspanda — steady; pranaya-ānanda — due to transcendental bliss from a relationship of love; salila — filled with tears; āmīlita — half-closed; īkshanah — whose eyes.

TRANSLATION

Sometimes, feeling the touch of the Lord's lotus hands, he became spiritually jubilant and remained silent, his hairs standing on end and tears gliding down from his half-closed eyes because of his love for the Lord.

PURPORT

When a devotee feels separation from the Lord, he becomes eager to see where the Lord is, and sometimes when he feels pangs of separation, tears flow incessantly from his half-closed eyes. As stated by Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu in His Śikshāshtaka, yugāyitam nimeshena cakshushā prāvrishāyitam. The words cakshushā prāvrishāyitam refer to tears falling incessantly from the devotee's eyes. These symptoms, which appear in pure devotional ecstasy, were visible in the body of Prahlāda Mahārāja.
