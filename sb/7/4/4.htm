Canto 7: The Science of GodChapter 4: Hiranyakaśipu Terrorizes the Universe

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 7.4.4

evam labdha-varo daityo

bibhrad dhemamayam vapuh

bhagavaty akarod dvesham

bhrātur vadham anusmaran

SYNONYMS

evam — thus; labdha-varah — having obtained his desired boon; daityah — Hiranyakaśipu; bibhrat — acquiring; hema-mayam — possessing the luster of gold; vapuh — a body; bhagavati — unto Lord Vishnu, the Supreme Personality of Godhead; akarot — maintained; dvesham — envy; bhrātuh vadham — the killing of his brother; anusmaran — always thinking of.

TRANSLATION

The demon Hiranyakaśipu, having thus been blessed by Lord Brahmā and having acquired a lustrous golden body, continued to remember the death of his brother and therefore be envious of Lord Vishnu.

PURPORT

A demoniac person, in spite of acquiring all the opulences possible to obtain in this universe, continues to be envious of the Supreme Personality of Godhead.
