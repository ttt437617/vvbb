Canto 7: The Science of GodChapter 5: Prahlāda Mahārāja, the Saintly Son of Hiranyakaśipu

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 7.5.25

niśamyaitat suta-vaco

hiranyakaśipus tadā

guru-putram uvācedam

rushā prasphuritādharah

SYNONYMS

niśamya — hearing; etat — this; suta-vacah — speech from his son; hiranyakaśipuh — Hiranyakaśipu; tadā — at that time; guru-putram — unto the son of Śukrācārya, his spiritual master; uvāca — spoke; idam — this; rushā — with anger; prasphurita — trembling; adharah — whose lips.

TRANSLATION

After hearing these words of devotional service from the mouth of his son Prahlāda, Hiranyakaśipu was extremely angry. His lips trembling, he spoke as follows to Shanda the son of his guru, Śukrācārya.
