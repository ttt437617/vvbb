Canto 7: The Science of GodChapter 5: Prahlāda Mahārāja, the Saintly Son of Hiranyakaśipu

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 7.5.49

jitam tvayaikena jagat-trayam bhruvor

vijrimbhana-trasta-samasta-dhishnyapam

na tasya cintyam tava nātha cakshvahe

na vai śiśūnām guna-doshayoh padam

SYNONYMS

jitam — conquered; tvayā — by you; ekena — alone; jagat-trayam — the three worlds; bhruvoh — of the eyebrows; vijrimbhana — by the expanding; trasta — become afraid; samasta — all; dhishnyapam — the chief persons in every planet; na — not; tasya — from him; cintyam — to be anxious; tava — of you; nātha — O master; cakshvahe — we find; na — nor; vai — indeed; śiśūnām — of children; guna-doshayoh — of a good quality or fault; padam — the subject matter.

TRANSLATION

O lord, we know that when you simply move your eyebrows, all the commanders of the various planets are most afraid. Without the help of any assistant, you have conquered all the three worlds. Therefore, we do not find any reason for you to be morose and full of anxiety. As for Prahlāda, he is nothing but a child and cannot be a cause of anxiety. After all, his bad or good qualities have no value.
