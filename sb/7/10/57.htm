Canto 7: The Science of GodChapter 10: Prahlāda, the Best Among Exalted Devotees

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 7.10.57

athānugrihya bhagavān

mā bhaishteti surān vibhuh

śaram dhanushi sandhāya

pureshv astram vyamuñcata

SYNONYMS

atha — thereafter; anugrihya — just to show them favor; bhagavān — the most powerful; mā — do not; bhaishta — be afraid; iti — thus; surān — unto the demigods; vibhuh — Lord Śiva; śaram — arrows; dhanushi — on the bow; sandhāya — fixing; pureshu — at those three residences occupied by the demons; astram — weapons; vyamuñcata — released.

TRANSLATION

The most powerful and able Lord Śiva reassured them and said, "Do not be afraid." He then fixed his arrows to his bow and released them toward the three residences occupied by the demons.
