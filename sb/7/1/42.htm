Canto 7: The Science of GodChapter 1: The Supreme Lord Is Equal to Everyone

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 7.1.42

hiranyakaśipuh putram

prahlādam keśava-priyam

jighāmsur akaron nānā

yātanā mrityu-hetave

SYNONYMS

hiranyakaśipuh — Hiranyakaśipu; putram — son; prahlādam — Prahlāda Mahārāja; keśava-priyam — the beloved devotee of Keśava; jighāmsuh — desirous of killing; akarot — enacted; nānā — various; yātanāh — tortures; mrityu — death; hetave — to cause.

TRANSLATION

Desiring to kill his son Prahlāda, who was a great devotee of Lord Vishnu, Hiranyakaśipu tortured him in many ways.
