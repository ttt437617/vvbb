Canto 7: The Science of GodChapter 1: The Supreme Lord Is Equal to Everyone

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 7.1.7

sattvam rajas tama iti

prakriter nātmano gunāh

na teshām yugapad rājan

hrāsa ullāsa eva vā

SYNONYMS

sattvam — the mode of goodness; rajah — the mode of passion; tamah — the mode of ignorance; iti — thus; prakriteh — of material nature; na — not; ātmanah — of the spirit soul; gunāh — qualities; na — not; teshām — of them; yugapat — simultaneously; rājan — O King; hrāsah — diminution; ullāsah — prominence; eva — certainly; vā — or.

TRANSLATION

My dear King Parīkshit, the material qualities — sattva-guna, rajo-guna and tamo-guna — all belong to the material world and do not even touch the Supreme Personality of Godhead. These three gunas cannot act by increasing or decreasing simultaneously.

PURPORT

The original position of the Supreme Personality of Godhead is one of equality. There is no question of His being influenced by sattva-guna, rujo-guna or tamo-guna, for these material qualities cannot touch the Supreme Lord. The Lord is therefore called the supreme īśvara. Īśvarah paramah krishnah: [Bs. 5.1] He is the supreme controller. He controls the material qualities (daivī hy eshā guna-mayī mama māyā). Mayādhyakshena prakritih sūyate: [Bg. 9.10] material nature (prakriti) works under His order. How, then, can He be under the qualities of prakriti? Krishna is never influenced by the material qualities. Therefore there is no question of partiality in the Supreme Personality of Godhead.
