Canto 7: The Science of GodChapter 1: The Supreme Lord Is Equal to Everyone

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 7.1.44

tatas tau rākshasau jātau

keśinyām viśravah-sutau

rāvanah kumbhakarnaś ca

sarva-lokopatāpanau

SYNONYMS

tatah — thereafter; tau — the two doorkeepers (Jaya and Vijaya); rākshasau — demons; jātau — born; keśinyām — in the womb of Keśinī; viśravah-sutau — the sons of Viśravā; rāvanah — Rāvana; kumbhakarnah — Kumbhakarna; ca — and; sarva-loka — to all people; upatāpanau — giving misery.

TRANSLATION

Thereafter the same Jaya and Vijaya, the two doorkeepers of Lord Vishnu, took birth as Rāvana and Kumbhakarna, begotten by Viśravā in the womb of Keśinī. They were extremely troublesome to all the people of the universe.
