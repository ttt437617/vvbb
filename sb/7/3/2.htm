Canto 7: The Science of GodChapter 3: Hiranyakaśipu's Plan to Become Immortal

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 7.3.2

sa tepe mandara-dronyām

tapah parama-dārunam

ūrdhva-bāhur nabho-drishtih

pādāńgushthāśritāvanih

SYNONYMS

sah — he (Hiranyakaśipu); tepe — performed; mandara-dronyām — in a valley of Mandara Hill; tapah — austerity; parama — most; dārunam — difficult; ūrdhva — raising; bāhuh — arms; nabhah — toward the sky; drishtih — his vision; pāda-ańgushtha — with the big toes of his feet; āśrita — resting on; avanih — the ground.

TRANSLATION

In the valley of Mandara Hill, Hiranyakaśipu began performing his austerities by standing with his toes on the ground, keeping his arms upward and looking toward the sky. This position was extremely difficult, but he accepted it as a means to attain perfection.
