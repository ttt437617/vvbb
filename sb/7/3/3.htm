Canto 7: The Science of GodChapter 3: Hiranyakaśipu's Plan to Become Immortal

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 7.3.3

jatā-dīdhitibhī reje

samvartārka ivāmśubhih

tasmims tapas tapyamāne

devāh sthānāni bhejire

SYNONYMS

jatā-dīdhitibhih — by the effulgence of the hair on his head; reje — was shining; samvarta-arkah — the sun at the time of destruction; iva — like; amśubhih — by the beams; tasmin — when he (Hiranyakaśipu); tapah — austerities; tapyamāne — was engaged in; devāh — all the demigods who were wandering throughout the universe to see Hiranyakaśipu's demoniac activities; sthānāni — to their own places; bhejire — returned.

TRANSLATION

From the hair on Hiranyakaśipu's head there emanated an effulgent light as brilliant and intolerable as the rays of the sun at the time of dissolution. Seeing the performance of such austere penances, the demigods, who had been wandering throughout the planets, now returned to their respective homes.
