Canto 5: The Creative ImpetusChapter 20: Studying the Structure of the Universe

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 5.20.35

yāvan mānasottara-mervor antaram tāvatī bhūmih kāñcany anyādarśa-talopamā yasyām prahitah padārtho na kathañcit punah pratyupalabhyate tasmāt sarva-sattva-parihritāsīt

SYNONYMS

yāvat — as much as; mānasottara-mervoh antaram — the land between Mānasottara and Meru (beginning from the middle of Mount Sumeru); tāvatī — that much; bhūmih — land; kāñcanī — made of gold; anyā — another; ādarśa-tala-upamā — whose surface is just like the surface of a mirror; yasyām — on which; prahitah — dropped; padārthah — a thing; na — not; kathañcit — in any way; punah — again; pratyupalabhyate — is found; tasmāt — therefore; sarva-sattva — by all living entities; parihritā — abandoned; āsīt — was.

TRANSLATION

Beyond the ocean of sweet water is a tract of land as broad as the area between the middle of Mount Sumeru and the boundary of Mānasottara Mountain. In that tract of land there are many living beings. Beyond it, extending to Lokāloka Mountain, is another land, which is made of gold. Because of its golden surface, it reflects light like the surface of a mirror, and any physical article that falls on that land can never be perceived again. All living entities, therefore, have abandoned that golden land.
