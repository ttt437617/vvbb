Canto 5: The Creative ImpetusChapter 22: The Orbits of the Planets

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 5.22.6

atha ca yāvatārdhena nabho-vīthyām pracarati tam kālam ayanam ācakshate

SYNONYMS

atha — now; ca — also; yāvatā — by as long as; ardhena — half; nabhah-vīthyām — in outer space; pracarati — the sun moves; tam — that; kālam — time; ayanam — ayana; ācakshate — is said.

TRANSLATION

Thus the time the sun takes to rotate through half of outer space is called an ayana, or its period of movement [in the north or in the south].
