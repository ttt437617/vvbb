Canto 5: The Creative ImpetusChapter 6: The Activities of Lord Rishabhadeva

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 5.6.5

kāmo manyur mado lobhah

śoka-moha-bhayādayah

karma-bandhaś ca yan-mūlah

svīkuryāt ko nu tad budhah

SYNONYMS

kāmah — lust; manyuh — anger; madah — pride; lobhah — greed; śoka — lamentation; moha — illusion; bhaya — fear; ādayah — all these together; karma-bandhah — bondage to fruitive activities; ca — and; yat-mūlah — the origin of which; svīkuryāt — would accept; kah — who; nu — indeed; tat — that mind; budhah — if one is learned.

TRANSLATION

The mind is the root cause of lust, anger, pride, greed, lamentation, illusion and fear. Combined, these constitute bondage to fruitive activity. What learned man would put faith in the mind?

PURPORT

The mind is the original cause of material bondage. It is followed by many enemies, such as anger, pride, greed, lamentation, illusion and fear. The best way to control the mind is to engage it always in Krishna consciousness (sa vai manah krishna-padāravindayoh). Since the followers of the mind bring about material bondage, we should be very careful not to trust the mind.
