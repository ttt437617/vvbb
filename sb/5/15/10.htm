Canto 5: The Creative ImpetusChapter 15: The Glories of the Descendants of King Priyavrata

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 5.15.10

yam abhyashiñcan parayā mudā satīh

satyāśisho daksha-kanyāh saridbhih

yasya prajānām duduhe dharāśisho

nirāśisho guna-vatsa-snutodhāh

SYNONYMS

yam — whom; abhyashiñcan — bathed; parayā — with great; mudā — satisfaction; satīh — all chaste and devoted to their husbands; satya — true; āśishah — whose blessings; daksha-kanyāh — the daughters of King Daksha; saridbhih — with sanctified water; yasya — whose; prajānām — of the citizens; duduhe — fulfilled; dharā — the planet earth; āśishah — of all desires; nirāśishah — although personally having no desire; guna-vatsa-snuta-udhāh — earth becoming like a cow whose udders flowed upon seeing Gaya's qualities in ruling over the citizens.

TRANSLATION

All the chaste and honest daughters of Mahārāja Daksha, such as Śraddhā, Maitrī and Dayā, whose blessings were always effective, bathed Mahārāja Gaya with sanctified water. Indeed, they were very satisfied with Mahārāja Gaya. The planet earth personified came as a cow, and, as though she saw her calf, she delivered milk profusely when she saw all the good qualities of Mahārāja Gaya. In other words, Mahārāja Gaya was able to derive all benefits from the earth and thus satisfy the desires of his citizens. However, he personally had no desire.

PURPORT

The earth over which Mahārāja Gaya ruled is compared to a cow. The good qualities whereby he maintained and ruled the citizens are compared to the calf. A cow delivers milk in the presence of her calf; similarly the cow, or earth, fulfilled the desires of Mahārāja Gaya, who was able to utilize all the resources of the earth to benefit his citizens. This was possible because he was bathed in sanctified water by the honest daughters of Daksha. Unless a king or ruler is blessed by authorities, he cannot rule the citizens very satisfactorily. Through the good qualities of the ruler, the citizens become very happy and well qualified.
