Canto 5: The Creative ImpetusChapter 3: Rishabhadeva's Appearance in the Womb of Merudevī, the Wife of King Nābhi

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 5.3.18

tata āgnīdhrīye 'mśa-kalayāvatarishyāmy ātma-tulyam anupalabhamānah

SYNONYMS

tatah — therefore; āgnīdhrīye — in the wife of Nābhi, the son of Āgnīdhra; amśa-kalayā — by an expansion of My personal form; avatarishyāmi — I shall advent Myself; ātma-tulyam — My equal; anupalabhamānah — not finding.

TRANSLATION

Since I cannot find anyone equal to Me, I shall personally expand Myself into a plenary portion and thus advent Myself in the womb of Merudevī, the wife of Mahārāja Nābhi, the son of Āgnīdhra.

PURPORT

This is an example of the omnipotence of the Supreme Personality of Godhead. Although He is one without a second, He expands Himself by svāmśa, His personal expansion, and sometimes by vibhinnāmśa, or His separated expansion. Lord Vishnu herein agrees to send His personal expansion as the son of Merudevī, the wife of Mahārāja Nābhi, who is the son of Āgnīdhra. The ritvijah, the priests, knew that God is one, yet they prayed for the Supreme Lord to become the son of Mahārāja Nābhi to let the world know that the Absolute Truth, the Supreme Personality of Godhead, is one without a second. When He incarnates, He expands Himself in different potencies.
