Canto 5: The Creative ImpetusChapter 21: The Movements of the Sun

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 5.21.5

yadā vriścikādishu pañcasu vartate tadāho-rātrāni viparyayāni bhavanti

SYNONYMS

yadā — when; vriścika-ādishu — headed by Vriścika (Scorpio); pañcasu — five; vartate — remains; tadā — at that time; ahah-rātrāni — the days and nights; viparyayāni — the opposite (the duration of the day decreases, and that of night increases); bhavanti — are.

TRANSLATION

When the sun passes through the five signs beginning with Vriścika [Scorpio], the duration of the days decreases [until Capricorn], and then gradually it increases month after month, until day and night become equal [in Aries].
