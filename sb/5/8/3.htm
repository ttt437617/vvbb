Canto 5: The Creative ImpetusChapter 8: A Description of the Character of Bharata Mahārāja

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 5.8.3

tayā pepīyamāna udake tāvad evāvidūrena nadato mriga-pater unnādo loka-bhayańkara udapatat

SYNONYMS

tayā — by the doe; pepīyamāne — being drunk with great satisfaction; udake — the water; tāvat eva — exactly at that time; avidūrena — very near; nadatah — roaring; mriga-pateh — of one lion; unnādah — the tumultuous sound; loka-bhayam-kara — very fearful to all living entities; udapatat — arose.

TRANSLATION

While the doe was drinking with great satisfaction, a lion, which was very close, roared very loudly. This was frightful to every living entity, and it was heard by the doe.
