Canto 11: General HistoryChapter 1: The Curse Upon the Yadu Dynasty

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 11.1.22

kaścin matsyo 'grasīl loham

cūrnāni taralais tatah

uhyamānāni velāyām

lagnāny āsan kilairakāh

SYNONYMS

kaścit — a certain; matsyah — fish; agrasīt — swallowed; loham — the iron; cūrnāni — the bits of powder; taralaih — by the waves; tatah — from that place; uhyamānāni — being carried; velāyām — on the shore; lagnāni — becoming stuck; āsan — they became; kilu — indeed; erakāh — a particular species of grass with long, sharp-edged blades.

TRANSLATION

A certain fish swallowed the iron lump, and the bits of iron, carried back to the shore by the waves, implanted themselves there and grew into tall, sharp canes.
