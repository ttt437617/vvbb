Canto 11: General HistoryChapter 24: The Philosophy of Sāńkhya

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 11.24.16

anur brihat kriśah sthūlo

yo yo bhāvah prasidhyati

sarvo 'py ubhaya-samyuktah

prakrityā purushena ca

SYNONYMS

anuh — small; brihat — great; kriśah — thin; sthūlah — stout; yah yah — whatever; bhāvah — manifestation; prasidhyati — is established; sarvah — all; api — indeed; ubhaya — by both; samyuktah — conjoined; prakrityā — by nature; purushena — by the enjoying spirit soul; ca — and.

TRANSLATION

Whatever features visibly exist within this world — small or great, thin or stout — certainly contain both the material nature and its enjoyer, the spirit soul.
