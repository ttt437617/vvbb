Canto 11: General HistoryChapter 9: Detachment from All that Is Material

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 11.9.8

ubhayor apy abhūd ghosho

hy avaghnantyāh sva-śańkhayoh

tatrāpy ekam nirabhidad

ekasmān nābhavad dhvanih

SYNONYMS

ubhayoh — from the two (on each hand); api — still; abhūt — there was; ghoshah — noise; hi — indeed; avaghnantyāh — of her who was husking the rice; sva-śańkhayoh — from each set of two shell ornaments; tatra — therein; api — indeed; ekam — one only; nirabhidat — she separated; ekasmāt — from that one ornament; na — not; abhavat — there was; dhvanih — a sound.

TRANSLATION

Thereafter, as the young girl continued to husk the rice, the two bracelets on each wrist continued to collide and make noise. Therefore she took one bracelet off each arm, and with only one left on each wrist there was no more noise.
