Canto 11: General HistoryChapter 21: Lord Krishna's Explanation of the Vedic Path

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 11.21.41

gāyatry ushnig anushtup ca

brihatī pańktir eva ca

trishtub jagaty aticchando

hy atyashty-atijagad-virāt

SYNONYMS

gāyatrī ushnik anushtup ca — known as Gāyatrī, Ushnik and Anushtup; brihatī pańktih — Brihatī and Pańkti; eva ca — also; trishtup jagatī aticchandah — Trishtup, Jagatī and Aticchanda; hi — indeed; atyashti-atijagat-virāt — Atyashti, Atijagatī and Ativirāt.

TRANSLATION

The Vedic meters are Gāyatrī, Ushnik, Anushtup, Brihatī, Pańkti, Trishtup, Jagatī, Aticchanda, Atyashti, Atijagatī and Ativirāt.

PURPORT

The Gāyatrī meter has twenty-four syllables, the Ushnik twenty-eight, the Anushtup thirty-two, and so on, each meter having four more syllables than the previous one. Vedic sound is called brihatī, or most expansive, and thus it is not possible for ordinary living entities to understand all the technical details in this matter.
