Canto 11: General HistoryChapter 23: The Song of the Avantī Brāhmana.

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 11.23.42

dvija uvāca

nāyam jano me sukha-duhkha-hetur

na devatātmā graha-karma-kālāh

manah param kāranam āmananti

samsāra-cakram parivartayed yat

SYNONYMS

dvijah uvāca — the brāhmana said; na — not; ayam — these; janah — people; me — my; sukha — of happiness; duhkha — and distress; hetuh — the cause; na — nor; devatā — the demigods; ātmā — my own body; graha — the controlling planets; karma — my past work; kālāh — or time; manah — the mind; param — rather only; kāranam — the cause; āmananti — is called by standard authorities; samsāra — of material life; cakram — the cycle; parivartayet — causes to rotate; yat — which.

TRANSLATION

The brāhmana said: These people are not the cause of my happiness and distress. Neither are the demigods, my own body, the planets, my past work, or time. Rather, it is the mind alone that causes happiness and distress and perpetuates the rotation of material life.
