Canto 11: General HistoryChapter 23: The Song of the Avantī Brāhmana.

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 11.23.21

arthenālpīyasā hy ete

samrabdhā dīpta-manyavah

tyajanty āśu spridho ghnanti

sahasotsrijya sauhridam

SYNONYMS

arthena — by wealth; alpīyasā — insignificant; hi — even; ete — they; samrabdhāh — agitated; dīpta — inflamed; manyavah — their anger; tyajanti — they give up; āśu — very quickly; spridhah — becoming quarrelsome; ghnanti — they destroy; sahasā — quickly; utsrijya — rejecting; sauhridam — goodwill.

TRANSLATION

For even a small amount of money these relatives and friends become very agitated and their anger is inflamed. Acting as rivals, they quickly give up all sentiments of goodwill and will reject one at a moment's notice, even to the point of committing murder.
