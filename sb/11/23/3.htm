Canto 11: General HistoryChapter 23: The Song of the Avantī Brāhmana.

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 11.23.3

na tathā tapyate viddhah

pumān bānais tu marma-gaih

yathā tudanti marma-sthā

hy asatām parusheshavah

SYNONYMS

na — not; tathā — in the same way; tapyate — is caused pain; viddhah — pierced; pumān — a person; bānaih — by arrows; tu — however; marma-gaih — going to the heart; yathā — as; tudanti — prick; marma-sthāh — attaching within the heart; hi — indeed; asatām — of evil persons; parusha — harsh (words); ishavah — the arrows.

TRANSLATION

Sharp arrows which pierce one's chest and reach the heart do not cause as much suffering as the arrows of harsh, insulting words that become lodged within the heart when spoken by uncivilized men.
