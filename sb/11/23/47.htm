Canto 11: General HistoryChapter 23: The Song of the Avantī Brāhmana.

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 11.23.47

mano-vaśe 'nye hy abhavan sma devā

manaś ca nānyasya vaśam sameti

bhīshmo hi devah sahasah sahīyān

yuñjyād vaśe tam sa hi deva-devah

SYNONYMS

manah — of the mind; vaśe — under the control; anye — others; hi — indeed; abhavan — have become; sma — in the past; devāh — the senses (represented by their presiding deities); manah — the mind; ca — and; na — never; anyasya — of another; vaśam — under the control; sameti — comes; bhīshmah — fearsome; hi — indeed; devah — the godlike power; sahasah — than the strongest; sahīyān — stronger; yuñjyāt — can fix; vaśe — under control; tam — that mind; sah — such a person; hi — indeed; deva-devah — the master of all the senses.

TRANSLATION

All the senses have been under the control of the mind since time immemorial, and the mind himself never comes under the sway of any other. He is stronger than the strongest, and his godlike power is fearsome. Therefore, anyone who can bring the mind under control becomes the master of all the senses.
