Canto 11: General HistoryChapter 23: The Song of the Avantī Brāhmana.

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 11.23.9

tasyaivam yaksha-vittasya

cyutasyobhaya-lokatah

dharma-kāma-vihīnasya

cukrudhuh pañca-bhāginah

SYNONYMS

tasya — at him; evam — in this way; yaksha-vittasya — who simply kept his wealth without spending it, like the Yakshas, who guard the treasury of Kuvera; cyutasya — who was deprived; ubhaya — of both; lokatah — worlds (this life and the next); dharma — religiosity; kāma — and sense gratification; vihīnasya — lacking; cakrudhuh — they became angry; pañca-bhāginah — the deities of the five prescribed household sacrifices.

TRANSLATION

In this way the presiding deities of the five family sacrifices became angry at the briāhmana, who, being niggardly, guarded his wealth like a Yaksha, who had no good destination either in this world or the next, and who was totally deprived of religiosity and sense enjoyment.
