Canto 11: General HistoryChapter 29: Bhakti-yoga

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 11.29.40

namo 'stu te mahā-yogin

prapannam anuśādhi mām

yathā tvac-caranāmbhoje

ratih syād anapāyinī

SYNONYMS

namah astu — let me offer my obeisances; te — unto You; mahā-yogin — O greatest of mystics; prapannam — who am surrendered; anuśādhi — please instruct; mām — me; yathā — how; tvat — Your; carana-ambhoje — at the lotus feet; ratih — transcendental attraction; syāt — may be; anapāyinī — undeviating.

TRANSLATION

Obeisances unto You, O greatest of yogīs. Please instruct me, who am surrendered unto You, how I may have undeviating attachment to Your lotus feet.
