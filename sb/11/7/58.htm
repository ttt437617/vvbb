Canto 11: General HistoryChapter 7: Lord Krishna Instructs Uddhava

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 11.7.58

teshu kāle vyajāyanta

racitāvayavā hareh

śaktibhir durvibhāvyābhih

komalāńga-tanūruhāh

SYNONYMS

teshu — from those eggs; kāle — in time; vyajāyanta — were born; racita — produced; avayavāh — (children) whose limbs; hareh — of the Supreme Lord, Hari; śaktibhih — by the potencies; durvibhāvyābhih — which are inconceivable; komala — tender; ańga — whose limbs; tanūruhāh — and feathers.

TRANSLATION

When the time was ripe, baby pigeons, with tender limbs and feathers created by the inconceivable potencies of the Lord, were born from those eggs.
