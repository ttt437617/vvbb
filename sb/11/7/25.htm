Canto 11: General HistoryChapter 7: Lord Krishna Instructs Uddhava

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 11.7.25

avadhūtam dviyam kañcic

carantam akuto-bhayam

kavim nirīkshya tarunam

yaduh papraccha dharma-vit

SYNONYMS

avadhūtam — the mendicant; dvijam — a brāhmana; kañcit — a certain; carantam — wandering; akutah-bhayam — without fear for any reason; kavim — learned; nirīkshya — observing; tarunam — young; yaduh — King Yadu; papraccha — inquired; dharma-vit — expert in religious principles.

TRANSLATION

Mahārāja Yadu once observed a certain brāhmana avadhūta, who appeared to be quite young and learned, wandering about fearlessly. Being himself most learned in spiritual science, the King took the opportunity and inquired from him as follows.
