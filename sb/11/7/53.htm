Canto 11: General HistoryChapter 7: Lord Krishna Instructs Uddhava

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 11.7.53

kapotah kaścanāranye

krita-nīdo vanaspatau

kapotyā bhāryayā sārdham

uvāsa katicit samāh

SYNONYMS

kapotah — pigeon; kaścana — a certain; aranye — in the forest; krita-nīdah — having built his nest; vanaspatau — in a tree; kapotyā — with a female pigeon; bhāryayā — his wife; sa-ardham — as his companion; uvāsa — he dwelled; katicit — for some; samāh — years.

TRANSLATION

There once was a pigeon who lived in the forest along with his wife. He had built a nest within a tree and lived there for several years in her company.
