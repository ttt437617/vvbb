Canto 11: General HistoryChapter 7: Lord Krishna Instructs Uddhava

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 11.7.73

evam kutumby aśāntātmā

dvandvārāmah patatri-vat

pushnan kutumbam kripanah

sānubandho 'vasīdati

SYNONYMS

evam — thus; kutumbī — a family man; aśānta — unpeaceful; ātmā — his soul; dvandva — in material dualities (like male and female); ārāmah — taking his pleasure; patatri-vat — like this bird; pushnan — maintaining; kutumbam — his family; kripanah — the miser; sa-anubandhah — with his relatives; avasīdati — must suffer greatly.

TRANSLATION

In this way, one who is too attached to family life becomes disturbed at heart. Like the pigeon, he tries to find pleasure in mundane sex attraction. Busily engaged in maintaining his own family, the miserly person is fated to suffer greatly, along with all his family members.
