Canto 11: General HistoryChapter 7: Lord Krishna Instructs Uddhava

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 11.7.57

kapotī prathamam garbham

grihnantī kāla āgate

andāni sushuve nīde

sta-patyuh sannidhau satī

SYNONYMS

kapotī — the female pigeon; prathamam — her first; garbham — pregnancy; grihnantī — carrying; kāle — when the time (for delivery); āgate — had come; andāni — eggs; sushuve — she delivered; nīde — in the nest; sva-patyuh — of her husband; sannidhau — in the presence; satī — the chaste.

TRANSLATION

Then the female pigeon experienced her first pregnancy. When the time arrived, the chaste lady delivered a number of eggs within the nest in the presence of her husband.
