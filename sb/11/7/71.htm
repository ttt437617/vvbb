Canto 11: General HistoryChapter 7: Lord Krishna Instructs Uddhava

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 11.7.71

tāms tathaivāvritān śigbhir

mrityu-grastān viceshtatah

svayam ca kripanah śikshu

paśyann apy abudho 'patat

SYNONYMS

tān — them; tathā — also; eva — indeed; āvritān — surrounded; śigbhih — by the net; mrityu — by death; grastān — seized; viceshtatah — stunned; svayam — himself; ca — also; kripanah — wretched; śikshu — within the net; paśyan — while watching; api — even; abudhah — unintelligent; apatat — he fell.

TRANSLATION

As the father pigeon wretchedly stared at his poor children trapped in the net and on the verge of death, pathetically struggling to free themselves, his mind went blank, and thus he himself fell into the hunter's net.
