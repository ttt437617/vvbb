Canto 11: General HistoryChapter 7: Lord Krishna Instructs Uddhava

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 11.7.64

kapotaś ca kapotī ca

prajā-poshe sadotsukau

gatau poshanam ādāya

sva-nīdam upajagmatuh

SYNONYMS

kapotah — the pigeon; ca — and; kapotī — the she-pigeon; ca — and; prajā — of their children; poshe — in the matter of maintaining; sadā — always; utsukau — eagerly engaged; gatau — having gone; poshanam — food; ādāya — bringing; sva — their own; nīdam — to the nest; upajagmatuh — they approached.

TRANSLATION

The pigeon and his wife were always anxious for the maintenance of their children, and they were wandering in the forest for that purpose. Having obtained proper food, they now returned to their nest.
