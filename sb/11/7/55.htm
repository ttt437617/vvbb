Canto 11: General HistoryChapter 7: Lord Krishna Instructs Uddhava

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 11.7.55

śayyāsanātana-sthāna

vārtā-krīdāśanādikam

mithunī-bhūya viśrabdhau

ceratur vana-rājishu

SYNONYMS

śayyā — resting; āsana — sitting; atana — walking; sthāna — standing; vārtā — conversing; krīdā — playing; aśana — eating; ādikam — and so on; mithunī-bhūya — together as a couple; viśrabdhau — trusting; ceratuh — they performed; vana — of the forest; rājishu — among the groves of trees.

TRANSLATION

Naively trusting in the future, they carried out their acts of resting, sitting, walking, standing, conversing, playing, eating and so forth as a loving couple among the trees of the forest.
