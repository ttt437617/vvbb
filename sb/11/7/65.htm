Canto 11: General HistoryChapter 7: Lord Krishna Instructs Uddhava

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 11.7.65

kapotī svātmajān vīkshya

bālakān jāla-samvritān

tān abhyadhāvat krośantī

krośato bhriśa-duhkhitā

SYNONYMS

kapotī — the female pigeon; sva-ātma-jān — her own offspring; vīkshya — seeing; bālakān — the children; jāla — by the nest; samvritān — surrounded; tān — toward them; abhyadhāvat — she ran; krośantī — calling out; krośatah — toward them who were also crying; bhriśa — extremely; duhkhitā — distressed.

TRANSLATION

When the lady pigeon caught sight of her own children trapped within the hunter's net, she was overwhelmed with anguish, and crying out, she rushed toward them as they cried out to her in return.
