Canto 11: General HistoryChapter 7: Lord Krishna Instructs Uddhava

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 11.7.74

yah prāpya mānusham lokam

mukti-dvāram apāvritam

griheshu khaga-vat saktas

tam ārūdha-cyutam viduh

SYNONYMS

yah — one who; prāpya — having achieved; mānusham lokam — the human form of life; mukti — of liberation; dvāram — the door; apāvritam — wide open; griheshu — in family affairs; khaga-vat — like the bird of this story; saktah — attached; tam — him; ārūdha — having climbed high; cyutam — then falling; viduh — they consider.

TRANSLATION

The doors of liberation are opened wide to one who has achieved human life. But if a human being simply devotes himself to family life like the foolish bird in this story, then he is to be considered as one who has climbed to a high place only to trip and fall down.

Thus end the purports of the humble servants of His Divine Grace A. C. Bhaktivedanta Swami Prabhupāda to the Eleventh Canto, Seventh Chapter, of the Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam, entitled "Lord Krishna Instructs Uddhava."
