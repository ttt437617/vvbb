Canto 11: General HistoryChapter 26: The Aila-gītā

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 11.26.33

annam hi prāninām prāna

ārtānām śaranam tv aham

dharmo vittam nrinām pretya

santo 'rvāg bibhyato 'ranam

SYNONYMS

annam — food; hi — indeed; prāninām — of living entities; prānah — the very life; ārtānām — of those who are distressed; śaranam — the shelter; tu — and; aham — I; dharmah — religion; vittam — the wealth; nrinām — of men; pretya — when they have passed away from this world; santah — the devotees; arvāk — of going downward; bibhyatah — for those who are fearful; aranam — the refuge.

TRANSLATION

Just as food is the life of all creatures, just as I am the ultimate shelter for the distressed, and just as religion is the wealth of those who are passing away from this world, so My devotees are the only refuge of persons fearful of falling into a miserable condition of life.

PURPORT

Those fearful of being dragged down by material lust and anger should take shelter of the lotus feet of the Lord's devotees, who can engage one safely in the Lord's loving service.
