Canto 11: General HistoryChapter 26: The Aila-gītā

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 11.26.10

sa-paricchadam ātmānam

hitvā trinam iveśvaram

yāntīm striyam cānvagamam

nagna unmatta-vad rudan

SYNONYMS

sa-paricchadam — along with my kingdom and all paraphernalia; ātmānam — myself; hitvā — abandoning; trinam — a blade of grass; iva — as if; īśvaram — the powerful lord; yāntīm — going away; striyam — the woman; ca — and; anvagaman — I followed; nagnah — naked; unmatta-vat — like a madman; rudan — crying out.

TRANSLATION

Although I was a powerful lord with great opulence, that woman gave me up as if I were no more than an insignificant blade of grass. And still, naked and without shame, I followed her, crying out to her like a madman.
