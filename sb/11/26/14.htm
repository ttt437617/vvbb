Canto 11: General HistoryChapter 26: The Aila-gītā

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 11.26.14

sevato varsha-pūgān me

urvaśyā adharāsavam

na tripyaty ātma-bhūh kāmo

vahnir āhutibhir yathā

SYNONYMS

sevatah — who was serving; varsha-pūgān — for many years; me — my; urvaśyāh — of Urvaśī; adhara — of the lips; āsavam — the nectar; na tripyati — never became satisfied; ātma-bhūh — born from the mind; kāmah — the lust; vahnih — fire; āhutibhih — by oblations; yathā — just as.

TRANSLATION

Even after I had served the so-called nectar of the lips of Urvaśī for many years, my lusty desires kept rising again and again within my heart and were never satisfied, just like a fire that can never be extinguished by the oblations of ghee poured into its flames.
