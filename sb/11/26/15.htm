Canto 11: General HistoryChapter 26: The Aila-gītā

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 11.26.15

pumścalyāpahritam cittam

ko nv anyo mocitum prabhuh

ātmārāmeśvaram rite

bhagavantam adhokshajam

SYNONYMS

pumścalya — by a prostitute; apahritam — stolen; cittam — the intelligence; kah — who; nu — indeed; anyah — other person; mocitum — to free; prabhuh — is capable; ātma-ārāma — of the self-satisfied sages; īśvaram — the Lord; rite — except for; bhagavantam — the Supreme Personality of Godhead; adhokshajam — who lies beyond the purview of material senses.

TRANSLATION

Who but the Supreme Personality of Godhead, who lies beyond material perception and is the Lord of self-satisfied sages, can possibly save my consciousness, which has been stolen by a prostitute?
