Canto 11: General HistoryChapter 26: The Aila-gītā

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 11.26.11

kutas tasyānubhāvah syāt

teja īśatvam eva vā

yo 'nvagaccham striyam yāntīm

khara-vat pāda-tāditah

SYNONYMS

kutah — where; tasya — of that person (myself); anubhāvah — the influence; syāt — is; tejah — strength; īśatvam — sovereignty; eva — indeed; vā — or; yah — who; anvagaccham — ran after; striyam — this woman; yāntīm — while going away; khara-vat — just like an ass; pāda — by the foot; tāditah — punished.

TRANSLATION

Where are my so-called great influence, power and sovereignty? Just like an ass being kicked in the face by his she-ass, I ran after that woman, who had already given me up.
