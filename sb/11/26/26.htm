Canto 11: General HistoryChapter 26: The Aila-gītā

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 11.26.26

tato duhsańgam utsrijya

satsu sajjeta buddhimān

santa evāsya chindanti

mano-vyāsańgam uktibhih

SYNONYMS

tatah — therefore; duhsańgam — bad association; utsrijya — throwing away; satsu — to saintly devotees; sajjeta — he should become attached; buddhi-man — one who is intelligent; santah — saintly persons; eva — only; asya — his; chindanti — cut off; manah — of the mind; vyāsańgam — excessive attachment; uktibhih — by their words.

TRANSLATION

An intelligent person should therefore reject all bad association and instead take up the association of saintly devotees, whose words cut off the excessive attachment of one's mind.
