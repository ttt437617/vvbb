Canto 11: General HistoryChapter 28: Jñāna-yoga

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 11.28.43

yogam nishevato nityam

kāyaś cet kalpatām iyāt

tac chraddadhyān na matimān

yogam utsrijya mat-parah

SYNONYMS

yogam — the practice of yoga; nishevatah — of one executing; nityam — regularly; kāyah — the material body; cet — even if; kalpatām — fitness; iyāt — attains; tat — in that; śraddadhyāt — take faith; na — does not; mati-mān — who is intelligent; yogam — the system of mystic yoga; utsrijya — giving up; mat-parah — the devotee dedicated to Me.

TRANSLATION

Although the physical body may be improved by various processes of yoga, an intelligent person who has dedicated his life to Me does not place his faith in the prospect of perfecting his physical body through yoga, and in fact he gives up such procedures.

PURPORT

A devotee of the Lord keeps his body fit by eating nourishing Krishna prasādam, by maintaining a clean and regulated life, free from unnecessary anxiety, and by chanting and dancing before the Deity of the Lord. When a devotee is sick, he accepts medical treatment by normal methods, but beyond this there is no need to absorb one's mind in the physical body in the name of so-called yoga practice. Ultimately one must accept the destiny that has been ordained by the Lord.
