Canto 11: General HistoryChapter 28: Jñāna-yoga

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 11.28.39

yoga-dhāranayā kāmścid

āsanair dhāranānvitaih

tapo-mantraushadhaih kāmścid

upasargān vinirdahet

SYNONYMS

yoga-dhāranayā — by yogic meditation; kāmścit — some disturbances; āsanaih — by prescribed postures; dhāranā-anvitaih — together with meditation on controlled breathing; tapah — by special austerities; mantra — magical chants; aushadhaih — and medicinal herbs; kāmścit — some; upasargān — obstructions; vinirdahet — can be eradicated.

TRANSLATION

Some of these obstructions may be counteracted by yogic meditation or by sitting postures, practiced together with concentration on controlled breathing, and others may be counteracted by special austerities, mantras or medicinal herbs.
