Canto 11: General HistoryChapter 14: Lord Krishna Explains the Yoga System to Śrī Uddhava

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 11.14.45

evam samāhita-matir

mām evātmānam ātmani

vicashte mayi sarvātman

jyotir jyotishi samyutam

SYNONYMS

evam — thus; samāhita — completely fixed; matih — consciousness; mām — Me; eva — indeed; ātmānam — the individual soul; ātmani — within the individual soul; vicashte — sees; mayi — in Me; sarva-ātman — in the Supreme Personality of Godhead; jyotih — the sunrays; jyotishi — within the sun; samyutam — united.

TRANSLATION

One who has completely fixed his mind on Me should see Me within his own soul and should see the individual soul within Me, the Supreme Personality of Godhead. Thus, he sees the individual souls united with the Supreme Soul, just as one sees the sun's rays completely united with the sun.

PURPORT

In the spiritual world everything is naturally effulgent because that is the nature of spirit. Thus when one sees the individual soul as part and parcel of the Supreme Lord, the experience can be compared to seeing the sun's rays emanating from the sun. The Supreme Lord is within the living entity, and simultaneously the living entity is within the Lord. But in both cases the Supreme Lord, and not the living entity, is the maintainer and controller. How happy everyone could be by taking to Krishna consciousness and finding the Supreme Lord, Krishna, within everything and everything within Krishna. Liberated life in Krishna consciousness is so pleasurable that the greatest misfortune is to be without such consciousness. Śrī Krishna is kindly explaining in many different ways the supremacy of Krishna consciousness, and fortunate persons will understand the sincere message of the Lord.
