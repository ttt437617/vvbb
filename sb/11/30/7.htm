Canto 11: General HistoryChapter 30: The Disappearance of the Yadu Dynasty

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 11.30.7

tatrābhishicya śucaya

uposhya su-samāhitāh

devatāh pūjayishyāmah

snapanālepanārhanaih

SYNONYMS

tatra — there; abhishicya — taking bath; śucayah — cleansed; uposhya — fasting; su-samāhitāh — fixing our minds; devatāh — the demigods; pūjayishyāmah — we shall worship; snapana — by bathing; ālepana — smearing with sandalwood; arhanaih — and by various offerings.

TRANSLATION

There we should bathe for purification, fast, and fix our minds in meditation. We should then worship the demigods by bathing their images, anointing them with sandalwood pulp, and presenting them various offerings.
