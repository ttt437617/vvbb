Canto 11: General HistoryChapter 30: The Disappearance of the Yadu Dynasty

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 11.30.8

brāhmanāms tu mahā-bhāgān

krita-svastyayanā vayam

go-bhū-hiranya-vāsobhir

gajāśva-ratha-veśmabhih

SYNONYMS

brāhmanān — the brāhmanas; tu — and; mahā-bhāgān — greatly fortunate; krita — having performed; svasti-ayanāh — the ceremonies for good fortune; vayam — we; go — with cows; bhū — land; hiranya — gold; vāsobhih — and clothing; gaja — with elephants; aśva — horses; ratha — chariots; veśmabhih — and houses.

TRANSLATION

After performing the expiatory rituals with the help of greatly fortunate brāhmanas, we will worship those brāhmanas by offering them cows, land, gold, clothing, elephants, horses, chariots and dwelling places.
