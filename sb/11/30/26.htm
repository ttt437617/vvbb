Canto 11: General HistoryChapter 30: The Disappearance of the Yadu Dynasty

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 11.30.26

rāmah samudra-velāyām

yogam āsthāya paurusham

tatyāja lokam mānushyam

samyojyātmānam ātmani

SYNONYMS

rāmah — Lord Balarāma; samudra — of the ocean; velāyām — on the shore; yogam — meditation; āsthāya — resorting to; paurusham — upon the Supreme Personality of Godhead; tatyāja — He gave up; lokam — the world; mānushyam — human; samyojya — merging; ātmānam — Himself; ātmani — within Himself.

TRANSLATION

Lord Balarāma then sat down on the shore of the ocean and fixed Himself in meditation upon the Supreme Personality of Godhead. Merging Himself within Himself, He gave up this mortal world.
