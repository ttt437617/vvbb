Canto 11: General HistoryChapter 30: The Disappearance of the Yadu Dynasty

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 11.30.21

tā vajra-kalpā hy abhavan

parighā mushtinā bhritāh

jaghnur dvishas taih krishnena

vāryamānās tu tam ca te

SYNONYMS

tāh — those stalks; vajra-kalpāh — as strong as thunderbolts; hi — indeed; abhavan — became; parighāh — iron staffs; mushtinā — with their fists; bhritāh — held; jaghnuh — they attacked; dvishah — their enemies; taih — with these; krishnena — by Lord Krishna; vāryamānāh — being checked; tu — although; tam — Him; ca — as well; te — they.

TRANSLATION

As soon as they took these cane stalks in their fists, the stalks changed into iron rods as hard as thunderbolts. With these weapons the warriors began attacking one another again and again, and when Lord Krishna tried to stop them they attacked Him as well.
