Canto 11: General HistoryChapter 30: The Disappearance of the Yadu Dynasty

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 11.30.46

gaccha dvāravatīm sūta

jñātīnām nidhanam mithah

sańkarshanasya niryānam

bandhubhyo brūhi mad-daśām

SYNONYMS

gaccha — just go; dvāravatīm — to Dvārakā; sūta — O chariot driver; jñātīnām — of their close relatives; nidhanam — the destruction; mithah — mutual; sańkarshanasya — of Lord Balarāma; niryānam — the passing away; bandhubhyah — to Our family members; brūhi — speak; mat-daśām — My condition.

TRANSLATION

O driver, go to Dvārakā and tell Our family members how their loved ones destroyed one another. Also tell them of the disappearance of Lord Sańkarshana and of My present condition.

PURPORT

Lord Krishna sent His chariot driverless back to Vaikuntha, along with the horses and weapons, because the chariot driver, Dāruka, had some final service to do on earth.
