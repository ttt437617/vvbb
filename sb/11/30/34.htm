Canto 11: General HistoryChapter 30: The Disappearance of the Yadu Dynasty

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 11.30.34

catur-bhujam tam purusham

drishtvā sa krita-kilbishah

bhītah papāta śirasā

pādayor asura-dvishah

SYNONYMS

catuh-bhujam — four-armed; tam — that; purusham — personality; drishtvā — seeing; sah — he; krita-kilbishah — having committed an offense; bhītah — afraid; papāta — fell; śirasā — with his head; pādayoh — at the feet; asura-dvishah — of the Supreme Lord, the enemy of the demons.

TRANSLATION

Then, seeing that four-armed personality, the hunter became terrified of the offense he had committed, and he fell down, placing his head upon the feet of the enemy of the demons.
