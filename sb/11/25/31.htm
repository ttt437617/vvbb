Canto 11: General HistoryChapter 25: The Three Modes of Nature and Beyond

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 11.25.31

sarve guna-mayā bhāvāh

purushāvyakta-dhishthitāh

drishtam śrutam anudhyātam

buddhyā vā purusharshabha

SYNONYMS

sarve — all; guna-mayah — composed of the modes of nature; bhāvah — states of existence; purusha — by the enjoying soul; avyakta — and subtle nature; dhishthitāh — established and maintained; drishtam — seen; śrutam — heard; anudhyātam — conceived; buddhyā — by the intelligence; vā — or; purusha-rishabha — O best among men.

TRANSLATION

O best of human beings, all states of material being are related to the interaction of the enjoying soul and material nature. Whether seen, heard of or only conceived within the mind, they are without exception constituted of the modes of nature.
