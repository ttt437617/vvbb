Canto 11: General HistoryChapter 25: The Three Modes of Nature and Beyond

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 11.25.22

sattve pralīnāh svar yānti

nara-lokam rajo-layāh

tamo-layās tu nirayam

yānti mām eva nirgunāh

SYNONYMS

sattve — in the mode of goodness; pralīnāh — those who die; svah — to heaven; yānti — they go; nara-lokam — to the world of human beings; rajah-layāh — those who die in the mode of passion; tamah-layāh — those who die in the mode of ignorance; tu — and; nirayam — to hell; yānti — they go; mām — to Me; eva — however; nirgunāh — those who are free from all the modes.

TRANSLATION

Those who leave this world in the mode of goodness go to the heavenly planets, those who pass away in the mode of passion remain in the world of human beings, and those dying in the mode of ignorance must go to hell. But those who are free from the influence of all modes of nature come to Me.
