Canto 11: General HistoryChapter 25: The Three Modes of Nature and Beyond

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 11.25.8

pravritti-lakshane nishthā

pumān yarhi grihāśrame

sva-dharme cānu tishtheta

gunānām samitir hi sā

SYNONYMS

pravritti — of the path of material enjoyment; lakshane — in that which is the symptom; nishthā — dedication; pumān — a person; yarhi — when; griha-āśrame — in family life; sva-dharme — in prescribed duties; ca — and; anu — later; tishtheta — he stands; gunānām — of the modes of nature; samitih — the combination; hi — indeed; sā — this.

TRANSLATION

When a man desires sense gratification, being attached to family life, and when he consequently becomes established in religious and occupational duties, the combination of the modes of nature is manifest.

PURPORT

According to Śrīla Śrīdhara Svāmī, religious duties performed for promotion to heaven are understood to be in the mode of passion, those performed to enjoy ordinary family life are in the mode of ignorance, and those performed selflessly to fulfill one's occupational duty in the varnāśrama system are in the mode of goodness. The Lord has thus explained how mundane religiosity is manifest within the modes of nature.
