Canto 11: General HistoryChapter 16: The Lord's Opulence

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 11.16.19

nāgendrānām ananto 'ham

mrigendrah śrińgi-damshtrinām

āśramānām aham turyo

varnānām prathamo 'nagha

SYNONYMS

nāga-indrānām — among the best of many-hooded snakes; anantah — Anantadeva; aham — I am; mriga-indrah — the lion; śrińgi-damshtrinām — among animals with sharp horns and teeth; āśramānām — among the four social orders of life; aham — I am; turyah — the fourth, sannyāsa; varnānām — among the four occupational orders; prathamah — the first, the brāhmanas; anagha — O sinless one.

TRANSLATION

O sinless Uddhava, among the best of snakes I am Anantadeva, and among those animals with sharp horns and teeth I am the lion. Among the social orders I am the fourth, or the renounced order of life, and among the occupational divisions I am the first, the brāhmanas.
