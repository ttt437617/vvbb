Canto 11: General HistoryChapter 16: The Lord's Opulence

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 11.16.40

tejah śrīh kīrtir aiśvaryam

hrīs tyāgah saubhagam bhagah

vīryam titikshā vijñānam

yatra yatra sa me 'mśakah

SYNONYMS

tejah — power; śrīh — beautiful, valuable things; kīrtih — fame; aiśvaryam — opulence; hrīh — humility; tyāgah — renunciation; saubhagam — that which pleases the mind and senses; bhagah — good fortune; vīryam — strength; titikshā — tolerance; vijñānam — spiritual knowledge; yatra yatra — wherever; sah — this; me — My; amśakah — expansion.

TRANSLATION

Whatever power, beauty, fame, opulence, humility, renunciation, mental pleasure, fortune, strength, tolerance or spiritual knowledge there may be is simply an expansion of My opulence.

PURPORT

Although the Lord stated in the previous verse that His opulences are innumerable, the Lord here gives a specific resume and demonstration of His opulences.
