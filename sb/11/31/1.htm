Canto 11: General HistoryChapter 31: The Disappearance of Lord Śrī Krishna

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 11.31.1

śrī-śuka uvāca

atha tatrāgamad brahmā

bhavānyā ca samam bhavah

mahendra-pramukhā devā

munayah sa-prajeśvarāh

SYNONYMS

śrī-śukah uvāca — Śukadeva Gosvāmī said; atha — then; tatra — there; āgamat — came; brahmā — Lord Brahmā; bhavānyā — his consort, Bhavānī; ca — and; samam — along with; bhavah — Lord Śiva; mahā-indra-pramukhāh — led by Lord Indra; devāh — the demigods; munayah — the sages; sa — with; prajā-īśvarāh — the progenitors of the universe's population.

TRANSLATION

Śukadeva Gosvāmī said: Then Lord Brahmā arrived at Prabhāsa along with Lord Śiva and his consort, the sages, the Prajāpatis and all the demigods, headed by Indra.
