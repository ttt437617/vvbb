Canto 11: General HistoryChapter 31: The Disappearance of Lord Śrī Krishna

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 11.31.8

devādayo brahma-mukhyā

na viśantam sva-dhāmani

avijñāta-gatim krishnam

dadriśuś cāti-vismitāh

SYNONYMS

deva-ādayah — the demigods and others; brahma-mukhyāh — headed by Brahmā; na — not; viśantam — entering; sva-dhāmani — His own abode; avijñāta — unknown; gatim — His movements; krishnam — Lord Krishna; dadriśuh — they saw; ca — and; ati-vismitāh — very amazed.

TRANSLATION

Most of the demigods and other higher beings led by Brahmā could not see Lord Krishna as He was entering His own abode, since He did not reveal His movements. But some of them did catch sight of Him, and they were extremely amazed.
