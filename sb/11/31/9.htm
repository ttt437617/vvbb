Canto 11: General HistoryChapter 31: The Disappearance of Lord Śrī Krishna

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 11.31.9

saudāmanyā yathāklāśe

yāntyā hitvābhra-mandalam

gatir na lakshyate martyais

tathā krishnasya daivataih

SYNONYMS

saudāmanyāh — of lightning; yathā — just as; ākāśe — in the sky; yāntyāh — which is traveling; hitvā — having left; abhra-mandalam — the clouds; gatih — the movement; na lakshyate — cannot be ascertained; martyaih — by mortals; tathā — similarly; krishnasya — of Lord Krishna; daivataih — by the demigods.

TRANSLATION

Just as ordinary men cannot ascertain the path of a lightning bolt as it leaves a cloud, the demigods could not trace out the movements of Lord Krishna as He returned to His abode.

PURPORT

The sudden movements of a lightning bolt are seen by the demigods but not by human beings. Similarly, the sudden departure of Lord Krishna could be understood by the Lord's intimate associates in the spiritual sky but not by the demigods.
