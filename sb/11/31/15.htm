Canto 11: General HistoryChapter 31: The Disappearance of Lord Śrī Krishna

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 11.31.15

dāruko dvārakām etya

vasudevograsenayoh

patitvā caranāv asrair

nyashiñcat krishna-vicyutah

SYNONYMS

dārukah — Dāruka; dvārakām — in Dvārakā; etya — arriving; vasudeva-ugrasenayoh — of Vasudeva and Ugrasena; patitvā — falling down; caranau — at the feet; asraih — with his tears; nyashiñcat — drenched; krishna-vicyutah — deprived of Lord Krishna.

TRANSLATION

As soon as Dāruka reached Dvārakā, he threw himself at the feet of Vasudeva and Ugrasena and drenched their feet with his tears, lamenting the loss of Lord Krishna.
