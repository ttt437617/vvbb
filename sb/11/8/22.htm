Canto 11: General HistoryChapter 8: The Story of Pińgalā

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 11.8.22

pińgalā nāma veśyāsīd

videha-nagare purā

tasyā me śikshitam kiñcin

nibodha nripa-nandana

SYNONYMS

pińgalā nāma — of the name Pińgalā; veśyā — a prostitute; āsīt — there was; videha-nagare — in the city named Videha; purā — in the past; tasyāh — from her; me — by me; śikshitam — what was learned; kiñcit — something; nibodha — now you learn; nripa-nandana — O son of kings.

TRANSLATION

O son of kings, previously in the city of Videha there dwelled a prostitute named Pińgalā. Now please hear what I have learned from that lady.
