Canto 11: General HistoryChapter 27: Lord Krishna's Instructions on the Process of Deity Worship

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 11.27.33

pādyam ācamanīyam ca

gandham sumanaso 'kshatān

dhūpa-dīpopahāryāni

dadyān me śraddhayārcakah

SYNONYMS

pādyam — water for washing the feet; ācamanīyam — water for washing the mouth; ca — and; gandham — fragrances; sumanasah — flowers; akshatān — unbroken grains; dhūpa — incense; dīpa — lamps; upahāryāni — such items of paraphernalia; dadyāt — he should present; me — to Me; śraddhayā — with faith; arcakah — the worshiper.

TRANSLATION

The worshiper should faithfully present Me with water for washing My feet and mouth, fragrant oils, flowers and unbroken grains, along with incense, lamps and other offerings.
