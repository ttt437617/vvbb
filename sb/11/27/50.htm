Canto 11: General HistoryChapter 27: Lord Krishna's Instructions on the Process of Deity Worship

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 11.27.50

mad-arcām sampratishthāpya

mandiram kārayed dridham

pushpodyānāni ramyāni

pūjā-yātrotsavāśritān

SYNONYMS

mat-arcām — My Deity form; sampratishthāpya — properly establishing; mandiram — a temple; kārayet — he should construct; dridham — strong; pushpa-udyānāni — flower gardens; ramyāni — beautiful; pūjā — for regular, daily worship; yātrā — special festivals; utsava — and yearly holidays; āśritān — set aside.

TRANSLATION

The devotee should more fully establish My Deity by solidly constructing a temple, along with beautiful gardens. These gardens should be set aside to provide flowers for the regular daily worship, special Deity processions and holiday observances.

PURPORT

Wealthy, pious persons should be engaged in constructing temples and gardens for the pleasure of the Deity. The word dridham indicates that the most solid means of construction should be employed.
