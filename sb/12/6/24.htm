Canto 12: The Age of DeteriorationChapter 6: Mahārāja Parīkshit Passes Away

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 12.6.24

naisha tvayā manushyendra

vadham arhati sarpa-rāt

anena pītam amritam

atha vā ajarāmarah

SYNONYMS

na — not; eshah — this snake-bird; tvayā — by you; manushya-indra — O great ruler of men; vadham — murder; arhati — deserves; sarpa-rāt — the king of snakes; anena — by him; pītam — has been drunk; amritam — the nectar of the demigods; atha — therefore; vai — certainly; ajara — free from the effects of old age; amarah — virtually immortal.

TRANSLATION

O King among men, it is not fitting that this king of snakes meet death at your hands, for he has drunk the nectar of the immortal demigods. Consequently he is not subject to the ordinary symptoms of old age and death.
