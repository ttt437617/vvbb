Canto 12: The Age of DeteriorationChapter 6: Mahārāja Parīkshit Passes Away

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 12.6.57

śākalyas tat-sutah svām tu

pañcadhā vyasya samhitām

vātsya-mudgala-śālīya-

gokhalya-śiśireshv adhāt

SYNONYMS

śākalyah — Śākalya; tat-sutah — the son of Māndūkeya; svām — his own; tu — and; pañcadhā — in five parts; vyasya — dividing; samhitām — the collection; vātsya-mudgala-śālīya — to Vātsya, Mudgala and Śālīya; gokhalya-śiśireshu — and to Gokhalya and Śiśira; adhāt — gave.

TRANSLATION

The son of Māndūkeya, named Śākalya, divided his own collection into five, entrusting one subdivision each to Vātsya, Mudgala, Śālīya, Gokhalya and Śiśira.
