Canto 12: The Age of DeteriorationChapter 6: Mahārāja Parīkshit Passes Away

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 12.6.21

tac chrutvājuhuvur viprāh

sahendram takshakam makhe

takshakāśu patasveha

sahendrena marutvatā

SYNONYMS

tat — that; śrutvā — hearing; ājuhuvuh — they performed the ritual of offering oblation; viprāh — the brāhmana priests; saha — along with; indram — King Indra; takshakam — the snake-bird Takshaka; makhe — into the sacrificial fire; takshaka — O Takshaka; āśu — quickly; patasva — you should fall; iha — here; saha indrena — together with Indra; marut-vatā — who is accompanied by all the demigods.

TRANSLATION

Hearing this, the priests then chanted this mantra for offering Takshaka together with Indra as an oblation into the sacrificial fire: O Takshaka, fall immediately into this fire, together with Indra and his entire host of demigods!
