Canto 12: The Age of DeteriorationChapter 6: Mahārāja Parīkshit Passes Away

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 12.6.75

jaimineh sama-gasyāsīt

sumantus tanayo munih

sutvāms tu tat-sutas tābhyām

ekaikām prāha samhitām

SYNONYMS

jaimineh — of Jaimini; sama-gasya — the singer of the Sāma Veda; āsīt — there was; sumantuh — Sumantu; tanayah — the son; munih — the sage (Jaimini); sutvān — Sutvān; tu — and; tat-sutah — the son of Sumantu; tābhyām — to each of them; eka-ekām — one of each of the two parts; prāha — he spoke; samhitām — collection.

TRANSLATION

Jaimini Rishi, the authority of the Sāma Veda, had a son named Sumantu, and the son of Sumantu was Sutvān. The sage Jaimini spoke to each of them a different part of the Sāma-veda-samhitā.
