Canto 12: The Age of DeteriorationChapter 6: Mahārāja Parīkshit Passes Away

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 12.6.5

bhagavams takshakādibhyo

mrityubhyo na bibhemy aham

pravishto brahma nirvānam

abhayam darśitam tvayā

SYNONYMS

bhagavan — my lord; takshaka — from the snake-bird Takshaka; ādibhyah — or other living entities; mrityubhyah — from repeated deaths; na bibhemi — do not fear; aham — I; pravishtah — having entered; brahma — the Absolute Truth; nirvānam — exclusive of everything material; abhayam — fearlessness; darśitam — shown; tvayā — by you.

TRANSLATION

My lord, I now have no fear of Takshaka or any other living being, or even of repeated deaths, because I have absorbed myself in that purely spiritual Absolute Truth, which you have revealed and which destroys all fear.
