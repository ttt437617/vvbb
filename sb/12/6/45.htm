Canto 12: The Age of DeteriorationChapter 6: Mahārāja Parīkshit Passes Away

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 12.6.45

putrān adhyāpayat tāms tu

brahmarshīn brahma-kovidān

te tu dharmopadeshtārah

sva-putrebhyah samādiśan

SYNONYMS

putrān — to his sons; adhyāpayat — he taught; tān — those Vedas; tu — and; brahma-rishīn — to the great sages among the brāhmanas; brahma — in the art of Vedic recitation; kovidān — who were very expert; te — they; tu — moreover; dharma — in religious rituals; upadeshtārah — instructors; sva-putrebhyah — to their own sons; samādiśan — imparted.

TRANSLATION

Brahmā taught these Vedas to his sons, who were great sages among the brāhmanas and experts in the art of Vedic recitation. They in turn took the role of ācāryas and imparted the Vedas to their own sons.
