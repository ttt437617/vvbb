Canto 12: The Age of DeteriorationChapter 6: Mahārāja Parīkshit Passes Away

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 12.6.78

udīcyāh sāma-gāh śishyā

āsan pañca-śatāni vai

paushyañjy-āvantyayoś cāpi

tāmś ca prācyān pracakshate

SYNONYMS

udīcyāh — belonging to the north; sāma-gāh — the singer of the Sāma Veda; śishyāh — the disciples; āsan — there were; pañca-śatāni — five hundred; vai — indeed; paushyañji-āvantyayoh — of Paushyañji and Āvantya; ca — and; api — indeed; tān — they; ca — also; prācyān — easterners; pracakshate — are called.

TRANSLATION

The five hundred disciples of Paushyañji and Āvantya became known as the northern singers of the Sāma Veda, and in later times some of them also became known as eastern singers.
