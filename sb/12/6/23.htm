Canto 12: The Age of DeteriorationChapter 6: Mahārāja Parīkshit Passes Away

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 12.6.23

tam patantam vimānena

saha-takshakam ambarāt

vilokyāńgirasah prāha

rājānam tam brihaspatih

SYNONYMS

tam — him; patantam — falling; vimānena — in his airplane; saha-takshakam — with Takshaka; ambarāt — from the sky; vilokya — observing; āńgirasah — the son of Ańgirā; prāha — spoke; rājānam — to the King (Janamejaya); tam — to him; brihaspatih — Brihaspati.

TRANSLATION

Brihaspati, the son of Ańgirā Muni, seeing Indra falling from the sky in his airplane along with Takshaka, approached King Janamejaya and spoke to him as follows.
