Canto 12: The Age of DeteriorationChapter 6: Mahārāja Parīkshit Passes Away

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 12.6.51

tāsām sa caturah śishyān

upāhūya mahā-matih

ekaikām samhitām brahmann

ekaikasmai dadau vibhuh

SYNONYMS

tāsām — of those four collections; sah — he; caturah — four; śishyān — disciples; upāhūya — calling near; mahā-matih — the powerfully intelligent sage; eka-ekām — one by one; samhitām — a collection; brahman — O brāhmana; eka-ekasmai — to each of them; dadau — he gave; vibhuh — the powerful Vyāsadeva.

TRANSLATION

The most powerful and intelligent Vyāsadeva called four of his disciples, O brāhmana, and entrusted to each of them one of these four samhitās.
