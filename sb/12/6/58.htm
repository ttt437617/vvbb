Canto 12: The Age of DeteriorationChapter 6: Mahārāja Parīkshit Passes Away

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 12.6.58

jātūkarnyaś ca tac-chishyah

sa-niruktām sva-samhitām

balāka-paila-jābāla-

virajebhyo dadau munih

SYNONYMS

jātūkarnyah — Jātūkarnya; ca — and; tat-śishyah — the disciple of Śākalya; sa-niruktām — along with a glossary explaining obscure terms; sva-samhitām — the collection he received; balāka-paila-jābāla-virajebhyah — to Balāka, Paila, Jābāla and Viraja; dadau — passed down; munih — the sage.

TRANSLATION

The sage Jātūkarnya was also a disciple of Śākalya, and after dividing the samhitā he received from Śākalya into three parts, he added a fourth section, a Vedic glossary. He taught one of these parts to each of four disciples — Balāka, the second Paila, Jābāla and Viraja.
