Canto 12: The Age of DeteriorationChapter 6: Mahārāja Parīkshit Passes Away

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 12.6.33

ta etad adhigacchanti

vishnor yat paramam padam

aham mameti daurjanyam

na yeshām deha-geha-jam

SYNONYMS

te — they; etat — this; adhigacchanti — come to know; vishnoh — of Lord Vishnu; yat — which; paramam — the supreme; padam — personal situation; aham — I; mama — my; iti — thus; daurjanyam — the depravity; na — is not; yeshām — for whom; deha — the body; geha — and home; jam — based upon.

TRANSLATION

Such devotees come to understand the supreme transcendental situation of the Personality of Godhead, Lord Vishnu, because they are no longer polluted by the concepts of "I" and "my," which are based on body and home.
