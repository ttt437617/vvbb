Canto 12: The Age of DeteriorationChapter 9: Mārkandeya Rishi Sees the Illusory Potency of the Lord

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 12.9.30

himālayam pushpavahām ca tām nadīm

nijāśramam yatra rishī apaśyata

viśvam vipaśyañ chvasitāc chiśor vai

bahir nirasto nyapatal layābdhau

SYNONYMS

himālayam — the Himālaya Mountains; pushpa-vahām — Pushpabhadrā; ca — and; tām — that; nadīm — river; nija-āśramam — his own hermitage; yatra — where; rishī — the two sages, Nara-Nārāyana; apaśyata — he saw; viśvam — the universe; vipaśyan — while observing; śvasitāt — by the breath; śiśoh — of the infant; vai — indeed; bahih — outside; nirastah — expelled; nyapatat — he fell; laya-abdhau — into the ocean of dissolution.

TRANSLATION

He saw before him the Himālaya Mountains, the Pushpabhadrā River, and his own hermitage, where he had had the audience of the sages Nara-Nārāyana. Then, as Mārkandeya beheld the entire universe, the infant exhaled, expelling the sage from His body and casting him back into the ocean of dissolution.
