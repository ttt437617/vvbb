Canto 12: The Age of DeteriorationChapter 9: Mārkandeya Rishi Sees the Illusory Potency of the Lord

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 12.9.10

tasyaikadā bhrigu-śreshtha

pushpabhadrā-tate muneh

upāsīnasya sandhyāyām

brahman vāyur abhūn mahān

SYNONYMS

tasya — while he; ekadā — one day; bhrigu-śreshtha — O best of the descendants of Bhrigu; pushpabhadrā-tate — on the bank of the river Pushpabhadrā; muneh — the sage; upāsīnasya — was performing worship; sandhyāyām — at the juncture of the day; brahman — O brāhmana; vāyuh — a wind; abhūt — arose; mahān — great.

TRANSLATION

O brāhmana Śaunaka, best of the Bhrigus, one day while Mārkandeya was performing his evening worship on the bank of the Pushpabhadrā, a great wind suddenly arose.
