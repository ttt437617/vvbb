Canto 12: The Age of DeteriorationChapter 9: Mārkandeya Rishi Sees the Illusory Potency of the Lord

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 12.9.12

tato vyadriśyanta catuh samudrāh

samantatah kshmā-talam āgrasantah

samīra-vegormibhir ugra-nakra-

mahā-bhayāvarta-gabhīra-ghoshāh

SYNONYMS

tatah — then; vyadriśyanta — appeared; catuh samudrah — the four oceans; samantatah — on all sides; kshmā-talam — the surface of the earth; āgrasantah — swallowing up; samīra — of the wind; vega — impelled by the force; ūrmibhih — with their waves; ugra — terrible; nakra — with sea monsters; mahā-bhaya — very fearful; āvarta — with whirlpools; gabhīra — grave; ghoshāh — with sounds.

TRANSLATION

Then the four great oceans appeared on all sides, swallowing up the surface of the earth with their wind-tossed waves. In these oceans were terrible sea monsters, fearful whirlpools and ominous rumblings.
