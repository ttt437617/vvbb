Canto 12: The Age of DeteriorationChapter 5: Śukadeva Gosvāmī's Final Instructions to Mahārāja Parīkshit

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 12.5.5

ghate bhinne ghatākāśa

ākāśah syād yathā purā

evam dehe mrite jīvo

brahma sampadyate punah

SYNONYMS

ghate — a pot; bhinne — when it is broken; ghāta-ākāśah — the sky within the pot; ākāśah — sky; syāt — remains; yathā — as; purā — previously; evam — similarly; dehe — the body; mrite — when it is given up, in the liberated condition; jīvah — the individual soul; brahma — his spiritual status; sampadyate — attains; punah — once again.

TRANSLATION

When a pot is broken, the portion of sky within the pot remains as the element sky, just as before. In the same way, when the gross and subtle bodies die, the living entity within resumes his spiritual identity.
