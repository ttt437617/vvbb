Canto 12: The Age of DeteriorationChapter 5: Śukadeva Gosvāmī's Final Instructions to Mahārāja Parīkshit

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 12.5.13

etat te kathitam tāta

yad ātmā prishtavān nripa

harer viśvātmanaś ceshtām

kim bhūyah śrotum icchasi

SYNONYMS

etat — this; te — to you; kathitam — narrated; tāta — my dear Parīkshit; yat — which; ātmā — you; prishtavān — inquired; nripa — O King; hareh — of the Supreme Personality of Godhead; viśva-ātmanah — of the Soul of the universe; ceshtām — the pastimes; kim — what; bhūyah — further; śrotum — to hear; icchasi — do you wish.

TRANSLATION

Beloved King Parīkshit, I have narrated to you the topics you originally inquired about — the pastimes of Lord Hari, the Supreme Soul of the universe. Now, what more do you wish to hear?

PURPORT

In his commentary on this text, Śrīla Jīva Gosvāmī has elaborately demonstrated, by citing many Bhāgavatam verses, the exalted devotional position of King Parīkshit, who was fully determined to fix his mind upon Lord Krishna and go back home, back to Godhead.

Thus end the purports of the humble servants of His Divine Grace A. C. Bhaktivedanta Swami Prabhupāda to the Twelfth Canto, Fifth Chapter, of the Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam, entitled "Śukadeva Gosvāmī's Final Instructions to Mahārāja Parīkshit."
