Canto 12: The Age of DeteriorationChapter 8: Mārkandeya's Prayers to Nara-Nārāyana Rishi

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 12.8.13

ittham brihad-vrata-dharas

tapah-svādhyāya-samyamaih

dadhyāv adhokshajam yogī

dhvasta-kleśāntarātmanā

SYNONYMS

ittham — in this manner; brihat-vrata-dharah — maintaining the vow of celibacy, brahmacarya; tapah-svādhyāya-samyamaih — by his austerities, studies of the Vedas and regulative principles; dadhyau — he meditated; adhokshajam — upon the transcendental Lord; yogī — the yogī; dhvasta — destroyed; kleśa — all troubles; antah-ātmana — with his introspective mind.

TRANSLATION

In this way the devotional mystic Mārkandeya maintained rigid celibacy through penance, study of the Vedas and self-discipline. With his mind thus free of all disturbances, he turned it inward and meditated on the Supreme Personality of Godhead, who lies beyond the material senses.
