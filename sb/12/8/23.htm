Canto 12: The Age of DeteriorationChapter 8: Mārkandeya's Prayers to Nara-Nārāyana Rishi

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 12.8.23

hutvāgnim samupāsīnam

dadriśuh śakra-kińkarāh

mīlitāksham durādharsham

mūrtimantam ivānalam

SYNONYMS

hutvā — having offered oblations; agnim — to the sacrificial fire; samupāsīnam — sitting in yogic meditation; dadriśuh — they saw; śakra — of Indra; kińkarāh — the servants; mīlita — closed; aksham — his eyes; durādharsham — invincible; mūrti-mantam — personified; iva — as if; analam — fire.

TRANSLATION

These servants of Indra found the sage sitting in meditation, having just offered his prescribed oblations into the sacrificial fire. His eyes closed in trance, he seemed invincible, like fire personified.
