Canto 12: The Age of DeteriorationChapter 8: Mārkandeya's Prayers to Nara-Nārāyana Rishi

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 12.8.15

etat purandaro jñātvā

saptame 'smin kilāntare

tapo-viśańkito brahmann

ārebhe tad-vighātanam

SYNONYMS

etat — this; purandarah — Lord Indra; jñātvā — learning; saptame — in the seventh; asmin — this; kila — indeed; antare — reign of Manu; tapah — of the austerities; viśańkitah — becoming fearful; brahman — O brāhmana Śaunaka; ārebhe — he set into motion; tat — of that austeritv; vighātanam — obstruction.

TRANSLATION

O brāhmana, during the seventh reign of Manu, the current age, Lord Indra came to know of Mārkandeya's austerities and became fearful of his growing mystic potency. Thus he tried to impede the sage's penance.
