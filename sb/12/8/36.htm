Canto 12: The Age of DeteriorationChapter 8: Mārkandeya's Prayers to Nara-Nārāyana Rishi

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 12.8.36

sa tat-sandarśanānanda-

nirvritātmendriyāśayah

hrishta-romāśru-pūrnāksho

na sehe tāv udīkshitum

SYNONYMS

sah — he, Mārkandeya; tat — of Them; sandarśana — because of seeing; ānanda — by the ecstasy; nirvrita — pleased; ātma — whose body; indriya — senses; āśayah — and mind; hrishta — standing on end; romā — his bodily hairs; aśru — with tears; pūrna — filled; akshah — his eyes; na sehe — he was unable; tau — upon them; udīkshitum — to glance.

TRANSLATION

The ecstasy of seeing Them completely satisfied Mārkandeya's body, mind and senses and caused the hairs on his body to stand on end and his eyes to fill with tears. Overwhelmed, Mārkandeya found it difficult to look at Them.
