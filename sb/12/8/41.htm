Canto 12: The Age of DeteriorationChapter 8: Mārkandeya's Prayers to Nara-Nārāyana Rishi

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 12.8.41

mūrtī ime bhagavato bhagavams tri-lokyāh

kshemāya tāpa-viramāya ca mrityu-jityai

nānā bibharshy avitum anya-tanūr yathedam

srishtvā punar grasasi sarvam ivornanābhih

SYNONYMS

mūrtī — the two personal forms; ime — these; bhagavatah — of the Supreme Personality of Godhead; bhagavan — O Lord; tri-lokyāh — of all the three worlds; kshemāya — for the ultimate benefit; tāpa — of material misery; viramāya — for the cessation; ca — and; mrityu — of death; jityai — for the conquest; nānā — various; bibharshi — You manifest; avitum — for the purpose of protecting; anya — other; tanūh — transcendental bodies; yathā — just as; idam — this universe; srishtvā — having created; punah — once again; grasasi — You swallow up; sarvam — entirely; iva — just like; ūrna-nābhih — a spider.

TRANSLATION

O Supreme Personality of Godhead, these two personal forms of Yours have appeared to bestow the ultimate benefit for the three worlds — the cessation of material misery and the conquest of death. My Lord, although You create this universe and then assume many transcendental forms to protect it, You also swallow it up, just like a spider who spins and later withdraws its web.
