Canto 12: The Age of DeteriorationChapter 8: Mārkandeya's Prayers to Nara-Nārāyana Rishi

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 12.8.35

te vai bhagavato rūpe

nara-nārāyanāv rishī

drishtvotthāyādarenoccair

nanāmāńgena danda-vat

SYNONYMS

te — They; vai — indeed; bhagavatah — of the Personality of Godhead; rūpe — the personal manifestations; nara-nārāyanau — Nara and Nārāyana; rishī — the two sages; drishtvā — seeing; utthāya — standing up; ādarena — with respect; uccaih — great; nanāma — bowed down; ańgena — with his entire body; danda-vat — just like a stick.

TRANSLATION

These two sages, Nara and Nārāyana, were the direct personal forms of the Supreme Lord. When Mārkandeya Rishi saw Them, he immediately stood up and then with great respect offered Them obeisances by falling down flat on the ground like a stick.
