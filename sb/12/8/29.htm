Canto 12: The Age of DeteriorationChapter 8: Mārkandeya's Prayers to Nara-Nārāyana Rishi

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 12.8.29

ta ittham apakurvanto

munes tat-tejasā mune

dahyamānā nivavrituh

prabodhyāhim ivārbhakāh

SYNONYMS

te — they; ittham — in this way; apakurvantah — trying to do harm; muneh — to the sage; tat — his; tejasā — by the potency; mune — O sage (Śaunaka); dahyamānāh — feeling burned; nivavrituh — they desisted; prabodhya — having awakened; ahim — a snake; iva — as if; arbhakāh — children.

TRANSLATION

O learned Śaunaka, while Cupid and his followers tried to harm the sage, they felt themselves being burned alive by his potency. Thus they stopped their mischief, just like children who have aroused a sleeping snake.
