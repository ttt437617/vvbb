Canto 12: The Age of DeteriorationChapter 8: Mārkandeya's Prayers to Nara-Nārāyana Rishi

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 12.8.26-27

krīdantyāh puñjikasthalyāh

kandukaih stana-gauravāt

bhriśam udvigna-madhyāyāh

keśa-visramsita-srajah

itas tato bhramad-drishteś

calantyā anu kandukam

vāyur jahāra tad-vāsah

sūkshmam trutita-mekhalam

SYNONYMS

krīdantyāh — who was playing; puñjikasthalyāh — of the Apsarā named Puñjikasthalī; kandukaih — with a number of balls; stana — of her breasts; gauravāt — because of the great weight; bhriśam — very much; udvigna — overburdened; madhyāyāh — whose waist; keśa — from her hair; visramsita — falling; srajah — the flower garland; itah tatah — here and there; bhramat — wandering; drishteh — whose eyes; calantyāh — who was running about; anu kandukam — after her ball; vāyuh — the wind; jahāra — stole away; tat-vāsah — her garment; sūkshmam — fine; trutita — loosened; mekhalam — the belt.

TRANSLATION

The Apsarā Puñjikasthalī made a show of playing with a number of toy balls. Her waist seemed weighed down by her heavy breasts, and the wreath of flowers in her hair became disheveled. As she ran about after the balls, glancing here and there, the belt of her thin garment loosened, and suddenly the wind blew her clothes away.
