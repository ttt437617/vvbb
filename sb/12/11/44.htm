Canto 12: The Age of DeteriorationChapter 11: Summary Description of the Mahāpurusha

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 12.11.44

vishnur aśvataro rambhā

sūryavarcāś ca satyajit

viśvāmitro makhāpeta

ūrja-māsam nayanty amī

SYNONYMS

vishnuh aśvatarah rambhā — Vishnu, Aśvatara and Rambhā; sūryavarcāh — Sūryavarcā; ca — and; satyajit — Satyajit; viśvāmītrah makhāpetah — Viśvāmitra and Makhāpeta; ūrja-māsam — the month of Ūrja (Kārttika); nayanti — rule; amī — these.

TRANSLATION

Vishnu as the sun-god, Aśvatara as the Nāga, Rambhā as the Apsarā, Sūryavarcā as the Gandharva, Satyajit as the Yaksha, Viśvāmitra as the sage and Makhāpeta as the Rākshasa rule the month of Ūrja.

PURPORT

All these sun-gods and their associates are mentioned in divisions in the Kūrma Purāna, as follows:
