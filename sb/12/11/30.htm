Canto 12: The Age of DeteriorationChapter 11: Summary Description of the Mahāpurusha

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 12.11.30

eka eva hi lokānām

sūrya ātmādi-krid dharih

sarva-veda-kriyā-mūlam

rishibhir bahudhoditah

SYNONYMS

ekah — one; eva — only; hi — indeed; lokānām — of the worlds; sūryah — the sun; ātmā — their soul; ādi-krit — the original creator; harih — the Personality of Godhead, Hari; sarva-veda — in all the Vedas; kriyā — of the ritualistic activities; mūlam — the basis; rishibhih — by the sages; bahudhā — variously; uditah — designated.

TRANSLATION

The sun-god, being nondifferent from Lord Hari, is the one soul of all the worlds and their original creator. He is the source of all the ritualistic activities prescribed in the Vedas and has been given many names by the Vedic sages.
