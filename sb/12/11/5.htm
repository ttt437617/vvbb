Canto 12: The Age of DeteriorationChapter 11: Summary Description of the Mahāpurusha

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 12.11.5

māyādyair navabhis tattvaih

sa vikāra-mayo virāt

nirmito driśyate yatra

sa-citke bhuvana-trayam

SYNONYMS

māyā-ādyaih — beginning with the unmanifest stage of nature; navabhih — with the nine; tattvaih — elements; sah — that; vikāra-mayah — also comprising the transformations (of the eleven senses and the five gross elements); virāt — the universal form of the Lord; nirmitah — constructed; driśyate — are seen; yatra — in which; sa-citke — being conscious; bhuvana-trayam — the three planetary systems.

TRANSLATION

The universal form [virāt] of the Personality of Godhead includes the nine basic elements of creation, starting with the unmanifest nature, and their subsequent transformations. Once this universal form is instilled with consciousness, the three planetary systems become visible within it.

PURPORT

The nine basic elements of creation are prakriti, sūtra, mahat-tattva, false ego, and the five subtle perceptions. The transformations are the eleven senses and the five gross material elements.
