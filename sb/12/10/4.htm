Canto 12: The Age of DeteriorationChapter 10: Lord Śiva and Umā Glorify Mārkandeya Rishi

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 12.10.4

athomā tam rishim vīkshya

giriśam samabhāshata

paśyemam bhagavan vipram

nibhritātmendriyāśayam

SYNONYMS

atha — then; umā — Umā; tam — that; rishim — sage; vīkshya — seeing; giriśam — to Lord Śiva; samabhāshata — spoke; paśya — just see; imam — this; bhagavan — my lord; vipram — learned brāhmana; nibhrita — motionless; ātma-indriya-āśayam — his body, senses and mind.

TRANSLATION

Goddess Umā, seeing the sage, addressed Lord Giriśa: My lord, just see this learned brāhmana, his body, mind and senses motionless in trance.
