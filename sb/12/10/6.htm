Canto 12: The Age of DeteriorationChapter 10: Lord Śiva and Umā Glorify Mārkandeya Rishi

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 12.10.6

śrī-bhagavān uvāca

naivecchaty āśishah kvāpi

brahmarshir moksham apy uta

bhaktim parām bhagavati

labdhavān purushe 'vyaye

SYNONYMS

śrī-bhagavān uvāca — the powerful lord said; na — not; eva — indeed; icchati — desires; āśishah — benedictions; kva api — in any realm; brahma-rishih — the saintly brāhmana; moksham — liberation; api uta — even; bhaktim — devotional service; parām — transcendental; bhagavati — for the Supreme Lord; labdhavān — he has achieved; purushe — for the Personality of Godhead; avyaye — who is inexhaustible.

TRANSLATION

Lord Śiva replied: Surely this saintly brāhmana does not desire any benediction, not even liberation itself, for he has attained pure devotional service unto the inexhaustible Personality of Godhead.

PURPORT

The words naivecchaty āśishah kvāpi indicate that Mārkandeya Rishi was uninterested in any reward available on any planet within the universe. Nor did he want liberation, for he had achieved the Supreme Lord Himself.
