Canto 12: The Age of DeteriorationChapter 10: Lord Śiva and Umā Glorify Mārkandeya Rishi

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 12.10.5

nibhritoda-jhasha-vrāto

vātāpāye yathārnavah

kurv asya tapasah sākshāt

samsiddhim siddhi-do bhavān

SYNONYMS

nibhrita — stationary; uda — water; jhasha-vrātah — and schools of fish; vāta — of the wind; apāye — upon the ceasing; yathā — just as; arnavah — the ocean; kuru — please make; asya — his; tapasah — of the austerities; sākshāt — manifest; samsiddhim — perfection; siddhi-dah — the bestower of perfection; bhavān — you.

TRANSLATION

He is as calm as the waters of the ocean when the wind has ceased and the fish remain still. Therefore, my lord, since you bestow perfection on the performers of austerity, please award this sage the perfection that is obviously due him.
