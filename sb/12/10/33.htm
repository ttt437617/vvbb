Canto 12: The Age of DeteriorationChapter 10: Lord Śiva and Umā Glorify Mārkandeya Rishi

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 12.10.33

kam vrine nu param bhūman

varam tvad vara-darśanāt

yad-darśanāt pūrna-kāmah

satya-kāmah pumān bhavet

SYNONYMS

kam — what; vrine — shall I choose; nu — indeed; param — other; bhūman — O all-pervading lord; varam — benediction; tvat — from you; vara-darśanāt — the sight of whom is itself the highest benediction; yat — of whom; darśanāt — from the seeing; pūrna-kāmah — full in all desires; satya-kāmah — able to achieve anything desired; pumān — a person; bhavet — becomes.

TRANSLATION

O all-pervading lord, since I have received the benediction of seeing you, what other benediction can I ask for? Simply by seeing you, a person fulfills all his desires and can achieve anything imaginable.
