Canto 12: The Age of DeteriorationChapter 4: The Four Categories of Universal Annihilation

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 12.4.4

esha naimittikah proktah

pralayo yatra viśva-srik

śete 'nantāsano viśvam

ātmasāt-kritya cātma-bhūh

SYNONYMS

eshah — this; naimittikah — occasional; proktah — is said; pralayah — annihilation; yatra — in which; viśva-srik — the creator of the universe, the Supreme Lord, Nārāyana; śete — lies down; ananta-āsanah — upon the snake-bed of Ananta Śesha; viśvam — the universe; ātma-sāt-kritya — absorbing within Himself; ca — also; ātma-bhūh — Lord Brahmā.

TRANSLATION

This is called the naimittika, or occasional, annihilation, during which the original creator, Lord Nārāyana, lies down upon the bed of Ananta Śesha and absorbs the entire universe within Himself while Lord Brahmā sleeps.
