Canto 12: The Age of DeteriorationChapter 2: The Symptoms of Kali-yuga

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 12.2.24

yadā candraś ca sūryaś ca

tathā tishya-brihaspatī

eka-rāśau sameshyanti

bhavishyati tadā kritam

SYNONYMS

yadā — when; candrah — the moon; ca — and; sūryah — the sun; ca — and; tathā — also; tishya — the asterism Tishyā (more commonly known as Pushyā, extending from 3° 20´ to 16° 40´ Cancer); brihaspatī — and the planet Jupiter; eka-rāśau — in the same constellation (Cancer); sameshyanti — will enter simultaneously; bhavishyati — will be; tadā — then; kritam — Satya-yuga.

TRANSLATION

When the moon, the sun and Brihaspatī are together in the constellation Karkata, and all three enter simultaneously into the lunar mansion Pushyā — at that exact moment the age of Satya, or Krita, will begin.
