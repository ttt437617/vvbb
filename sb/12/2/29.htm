Canto 12: The Age of DeteriorationChapter 2: The Symptoms of Kali-yuga

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 12.2.29

vishnor bhagavato bhānuh

krishnākhyo 'sau divam gatah

tadāviśat kalir lokam

pāpe yad ramate janah

SYNONYMS

vishnoh — of Vishnu; bhagavatah — the Supreme Personality of Godhead; bhānuh — the sun; krishna-ākhyah — known as Krishna; asau — He; divam — to the spiritual sky; gatah — having returned; tadā — then; aviśat — entered; kalih — the age of Kali; lokam — this world; pāpe — in sin; yat — in which age; ramate — take pleasure; janah — the people.

TRANSLATION

The Supreme Lord, Vishnu, is brilliant like the sun and is known as Krishna. When He returned to the spiritual sky, Kali entered this world, and people then began to take pleasure in sinful activities.
