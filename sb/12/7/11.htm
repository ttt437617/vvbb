Canto 12: The Age of DeteriorationChapter 7: The Purānic Literatures

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 12.7.11

avyākrita-guna-kshobhān

mahatas tri-vrito 'hamah

bhūta-sūkshmendriyārthānām

sambhavah sarga ucyate

SYNONYMS

avyākrita — of the unmanifest stage of nature; guna-kshobhāt — by the agitation of the modes; mahatah — from the basic mahat-tattva; tri-vritah — threefold; ahamah — from the false ego; bhūta-sūkshma — of the subtle forms of perception; indriya — of the senses; arthānām — and the objects of sense perception; sambhavah — the generation; sargah — creation; ucyate — is called.

TRANSLATION

From the agitation of the original modes within the unmanifest material nature, the mahat-tattva arises. From the mahat-tattva comes the element false ego, which divides into three aspects. This threefold false ego further manifests as the subtle forms of perception, as the senses and as the gross sense objects. The generation of all these is called creation.
