Canto 12: The Age of DeteriorationChapter 7: The Purānic Literatures

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 12.7.7

kaśyapo 'ham ca sāvarnī

rāma-śishyo 'kritavranah

adhīmahi vyāsa-śishyāc

catvāro mūla-samhitāh

SYNONYMS

kaśyapah — Kaśyapa; aham — I; ca — and; sāvarnih — Sāvarni; rāma-śishyah — a disciple of Rāma; akritvranah — namely Akritavrana; adhīmahi — we have assimilated; vyāsa-śishyāt — from the disciple of Vyāsa (Romaharshana); catvārah — four; mūla-samhitāh — basic collections.

TRANSLATION

Romaharshana, a disciple of Vedavyāsa, divided the Purānas into four basic compilations. The sage Kaśyapa and I, along with Sāvarni and Akritavrana, a disciple of Rāma, learned these four divisions.
