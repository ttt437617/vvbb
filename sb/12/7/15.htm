Canto 12: The Age of DeteriorationChapter 7: The Purānic Literatures

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 12.7.15

manvantaram manur devā

manu-putrāh sureśvarāh

rshayo 'mśāvatārāś ca

hareh shad-vidham ucyate

SYNONYMS

manu-antaram — the reign of each Manu; manuh — the Manu; devāh — the demigods; manu-putrāh — the sons of Manu; sura-īśvarāh — the different Indras; rishayah — the chief sages; amśa-avatārāh — the incarnations of portions of the Supreme Lord; ca — and; hareh — of Lord Hari; shat-vidham — sixfold; ucyate — is said.

TRANSLATION

In each reign of Manu, six types of personalities appear as manifestations of Lord Hari: the ruling Manu, the chief demigods, the sons of Manu, Indra, the great sages and the partial incarnations of the Supreme Personality of Godhead.
