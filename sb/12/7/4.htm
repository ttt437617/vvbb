Canto 12: The Age of DeteriorationChapter 7: The Purānic Literatures

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 12.7.4

nakshatrakalpah śāntiś ca

kaśyapāńgirasādayah

ete ātharvanācāryāh

śrinu paurānikān mune

SYNONYMS

nakshatrakalpah — Nakshatrakalpa; śāntih — Śāntikalpa; ca — also; kaśyapa-āńgirasa-ādayah — Kaśyapa, Āńgirasa and others; ete — these; ātharvana-ācāryāh — spiritual masters of the Atharva Veda; śrinu — now hear; paurānikān — the authorities of the Purānas; mune — O sage, Śaunaka.

TRANSLATION

Nakshatrakalpa, Śāntikalpa, Kaśyapa, Āńgirasa and others were also among the ācāryas of the Atharva Veda. Now, O sage, listen as I name the authorities on Purānic literature.
