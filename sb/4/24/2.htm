Canto 4: Creation of the Fourth OrderChapter 24: Chanting the Song Sung by Lord Śiva

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 4.24.2

haryakshāyādiśat prācīm

dhūmrakeśāya dakshinām

pratīcīm vrika-samjñāya

turyām dravinase vibhuh

SYNONYMS

haryakshāya — unto Haryaksha; adiśat — delivered; prācīm — eastern; dhūmrakeśāya — unto Dhūmrakeśa; dakshinām — the southern side; pratīcīm — the western side; vrika-samjñāya — unto his brother whose name was Vrika; turyām — the northern side; dravinase — unto another brother of his named Dravina; vibhuh — the master.

TRANSLATION

Mahārāja Vijitāśva offered the eastern part of the world to his brother Haryaksha, the southern part to Dhūmrakeśa, the western part to Vrika and the northern part to Dravina.
