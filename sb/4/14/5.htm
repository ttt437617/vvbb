Canto 4: Creation of the Fourth OrderChapter 14: The Story of King Vena

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 4.14.5

evam madāndha utsikto

nirańkuśa iva dvipah

paryatan ratham āsthāya

kampayann iva rodasī

SYNONYMS

evam — thus; mada-andhah — being blind with power; utsiktah — proud; nirańkuśah — uncontrolled; iva — like; dvipah — an elephant; paryatan — traveling; ratham — a chariot; āsthāya — having mounted; kampayan — causing to tremble; iva — indeed; rodasī — the sky and earth.

TRANSLATION

When he became overly blind due to his opulences, King Vena mounted a chariot and, like an uncontrolled elephant, began to travel through the kingdom, causing the sky and earth to tremble wherever he went.
