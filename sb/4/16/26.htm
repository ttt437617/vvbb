Canto 4: Creation of the Fourth OrderChapter 16: Praise of King Prithu by the Professional Reciters

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 4.16.26

tatra tatra giras tās tā

iti viśruta-vikramah

śroshyaty ātmāśritā gāthāh

prithuh prithu-parākramah

SYNONYMS

tatra tatra — here and there; girah — words; tāh tāh — many, various; iti — thus; viśruta-vikramah — he whose chivalrous activities are widely reputed; śroshyati — will hear; ātma-āśritāh — about himself; gāthāh — songs, narrations; prithuh — King Prithu; prithu-parākramah — distinctly powerful.

TRANSLATION

In this way when the chivalrous activities of King Prithu come to be known to the people in general, King Prithu will always hear about himself and his uniquely powerful activities.

PURPORT

To artificially advertise oneself and thus enjoy a so-called reputation is a kind of conceit. Prithu Mahārāja was famous amongst the people because of his chivalrous activities. He did not have to advertise himself artificially. One's factual reputation cannot be covered.
