Canto 4: Creation of the Fourth OrderChapter 15: King Prithu's Appearance and Coronation

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 4.15.1

maitreya uvāca

atha tasya punar viprair

aputrasya mahīpateh

bāhubhyām mathyamānābhyām

mithunam samapadyata

SYNONYMS

maitreyah uvāca — Maitreya continued to speak; atha — thus; tasya — his; punah — again; vipraih — by the brāhmanas; aputrasya — without a son; mahīpateh — of the King; bāhubhyām — from the arms; mathyamānābhyām — being churned; mithunam — a couple; samapadyata — took birth.

TRANSLATION

The great sage Maitreya continued: My dear Vidura, thus the brāhmanas and the great sages again churned the two arms of King Vena's dead body. As a result a male and female couple came out of his arms.
