Canto 4: Creation of the Fourth OrderChapter 21: Instructions by Mahārāja Prithu

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 4.21.3

savrindaih kadalī-stambhaih

pūga-potaih parishkritam

taru-pallava-mālābhih

sarvatah samalańkritam

SYNONYMS

sa-vrindaih — along with fruits and flowers; kadalī-stambhaih — by the pillars of banana trees; pūga-potaih — by collections of young animals and by processions of elephants; parishkritam — very nicely cleansed; taru — young plants; pallava — new leaves of mango trees; mālābhih — by garlands; sarvatah — everywhere; samalańkritam — nicely decorated.

TRANSLATION

At the street crossings there were bunches of fruits and flowers, as well as pillars of banana trees and betel nut branches. All these combined decorations everywhere looked very attractive.
