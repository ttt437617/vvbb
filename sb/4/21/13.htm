Canto 4: Creation of the Fourth OrderChapter 21: Instructions by Mahārāja Prithu

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 4.21.13

ekadāsīn mahā-satra-

dīkshā tatra divaukasām

samājo brahmarshīnām ca

rājarshīnām ca sattama

SYNONYMS

ekadā — once upon a time; āsīt — took a vow; mahā-satra — great sacrifice; dīkshā — initiation; tatra — in that function; diva-okasām — of the demigods; samājah — assembly; brahma-rishīnām — of great saintly brāhmanas; ca — also; rāja-rishīnām — of great saintly kings; ca — also; sat-tama — the greatest of devotees.

TRANSLATION

Once upon a time King Prithu initiated the performance of a very great sacrifice in which great saintly sages, brāhmanas, demigods from higher planetary systems and great saintly kings known as rājarshis all assembled together.

PURPORT

In this verse the most significant point is that although King Prithu's residential quarters were in India, between the rivers Ganges and Yamunā, the demigods also participated in the great sacrifice he performed. This indicates that formerly the demigods used to come to this planet. Similarly, great personalities like Arjuna, Yudhishthira and many others used to visit higher planetary systems. Thus there was interplanetary communication via suitable airplanes and space vehicles.
