Canto 4: Creation of the Fourth OrderChapter 21: Instructions by Mahārāja Prithu

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 4.21.45

maitreya uvāca

iti bruvānam nripatim

pitri-deva-dvijātayah

tushtuvur hrishta-manasah

sādhu-vādena sādhavah

SYNONYMS

maitreyah uvāca — the great sage Maitreya continued to speak; iti — thus; bruvānam — while speaking; nri-patim — the King; pitri — the denizens of Pitriloka; deva — the demigods; dvi-jātayah — and the twice-born (the brāhmanas and the Vaishnavas); tushtuvuh — satisfied; hrishta-manasah — greatly pacified in mind; sādhu-vādena — by expressing congratulations; sādhavah — all the saintly persons present.

TRANSLATION

The great sage Maitreya said: After hearing King Prithu speak so nicely, all the demigods, the denizens of Pitriloka, the brāhmanas and the saintly persons present at the meeting congratulated him by expressing their good will.

PURPORT

When a person speaks very nicely at a meeting, he is congratulated by the audience, who express their good will with the words sādhu, sādhu. This is called sādhu-vāda. All the saintly persons, Pitās (denizens of Pitriloka) and demigods who were present at the meeting and heard Prithu Mahārāja expressed their good will with the words sādhu, sādhu. They all accepted the good mission of Prithu Mahārāja, and they were fully satisfied.
