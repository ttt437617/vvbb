Canto 4: Creation of the Fourth OrderChapter 21: Instructions by Mahārāja Prithu

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 4.21.20

cāru citra-padam ślakshnam

mrishtam gūdham aviklavam

sarveshām upakārārtham

tadā anuvadann iva

SYNONYMS

cāru — beautiful; citra-padam — flowery; ślakshnam — very clear; mrishtam — very great; gūdham — meaningful; aviklavam — without any doubt; sarveshām — for all; upakāra-artham — just to benefit them; tadā — at that time; anuvadan — began to repeat; iva — like.

TRANSLATION

Mahārāja Prithu's speech was very beautiful, full of metaphorical language, clearly understandable and very pleasing to hear. His words were all grave and certain. It appears that when he spoke, he expressed his personal realization of the Absolute Truth in order to benefit all who were present.

PURPORT

Mahārāja Prithu was beautiful in his external bodily features, and his speech was also very glorious in all respects. His words, which were nicely composed in highly metaphorical ornamental language, were pleasing to hear and were not only mellow but also very clearly understandable and without doubt or ambiguity.
