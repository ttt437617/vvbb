Canto 4: Creation of the Fourth OrderChapter 17: Mahārāja Prithu Becomes Angry at the Earth

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 4.17.12

maitreya uvāca

prithuh prajānām karunam

niśamya paridevitam

dīrgham dadhyau kuruśreshtha

nimittam so 'nvapadyata

SYNONYMS

maitreyah uvāca — the great saint Maitreya said; prithuh — King Prithu; prajānām — of the citizens; karunam — pitiable condition; niśamya — hearing; paridevitam — lamentation; dīrgham — for a long time; dadhyau — contemplated; kuru-śreshtha — O Vidura; nimittam — the cause; sah — he; anvapadyata — found out.

TRANSLATION

After hearing this lamentation and seeing the pitiable condition of the citizens, King Prithu contemplated this matter for a long time to see if he could find out the underlying causes.
