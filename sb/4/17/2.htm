Canto 4: Creation of the Fourth OrderChapter 17: Mahārāja Prithu Becomes Angry at the Earth

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 4.17.2

brāhmana-pramukhān varnān

bhrityāmātya-purodhasah

paurāñ jāna-padān śrenīh

prakritīh samapūjayat

SYNONYMS

brāhmana-pramukhān — unto the leaders of the brāhmana community; varnān — to the other castes; bhritya — servants; amātya — ministers; purodhasah — to the priests; paurān — to the citizens; jāna-padān — to his countrymen; śrenīh — to different communities; prakritīh — to the admirers; samapūjayat — he gave proper respects.

TRANSLATION

King Prithu thus satisfied and offered all respect to all the leaders of the brāhmanas and other castes, to his servants, to his ministers and to the priests, citizens, general countrymen, people from other communities, admirers and others, and thus they all became happy.
