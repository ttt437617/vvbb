Canto 4: Creation of the Fourth OrderChapter 1: Genealogical Table of the Daughters of Manu

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 4.1.46-47

ta ete munayah kshattar

lokān sargair abhāvayan

esha kardama-dauhitra-

santānah kathitas tava

śrinvatah śraddadhānasya

sadyah pāpa-harah parah

prasūtim mānavīm daksha

upayeme hy ajātmajah

SYNONYMS

te — they; ete — all; munayah — great sages; kshattah — O Vidura; lokān — the three worlds; sargaih — with their descendants; abhāvayan — filled; eshah — this; kardama — of the sage Kardama; dauhitra — grandsons; santānah — offspring; kathitah — already spoken; tava — unto you; śrinvatah — hearing; śraddadhānasya — of the faithful; sadyah — immediately; pāpa-harah — reducing all sinful activities; parah — great; prasūtim — Prasūti; mānavīm — daughter of Manu; dakshah — King Daksha; upayeme — married; hi — certainly; aja-ātmajah — son of Brahmā.

TRANSLATION

My dear Vidura, the population of the universe was thus increased by the descendants of these sages and the daughters of Kardama. Anyone who hears the descriptions of this dynasty with faith will be relieved from all sinful reactions. Another of Manu's daughters, known as Prasūti, married the son of Brahmā named Daksha.
