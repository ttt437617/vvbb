Canto 4: Creation of the Fourth OrderChapter 1: Genealogical Table of the Daughters of Manu

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 4.1.29

maitreya uvāca

iti tasya vacah śrutvā

trayas te vibudharshabhāh

pratyāhuh ślakshnayā vācā

prahasya tam rishim prabho

SYNONYMS

maitreyah uvāca — the sage Maitreya said; iti — thus; tasya — his; vacah — words; śrutvā — after hearing; trayah te — all three; vibudha — demigods; rishabhāh — chiefs; pratyāhuh — replied; ślakshnayā — gentle; vācā — voices; prahasya — smiling; tam — unto him; rishim — the great sage; prabho — O mighty one.

TRANSLATION

The great sage Maitreya continued: Upon hearing Atri Muni speak in that way, the three great deities smiled, and they replied in the following sweet words.
