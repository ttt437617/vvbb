Canto 4: Creation of the Fourth OrderChapter 13: Description of the Descendants of Dhruva Mahārāja

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 4.13.33

tathā sva-bhāgadheyāni

grahīshyanti divaukasah

yad yajña-purushah sākshād

apatyāya harir vritah

SYNONYMS

tathā — thereupon; sva-bhāga-dheyāni — their shares in the sacrifice; grahīshyanti — will accept; diva-okasah — all the demigods; yat — because; yajña-purushah — the enjoyer of all sacrifices; sākshāt — directly; apatyāya — for the purpose of a son; harih — the Supreme Personality of Godhead; vritah — is invited.

TRANSLATION

When Hari, the supreme enjoyer of all sacrifices, is invited to fulfill your desire for a son, all the demigods will come with Him and take their shares in the sacrifice.

PURPORT

Whenever a sacrifice is performed, it is meant for satisfying Lord Vishnu, the enjoyer of the fruits of all sacrifices; and when Lord Vishnu agrees to come to a sacrificial arena, all the demigods naturally follow their master, and their shares are offered in such sacrifices. The conclusion is that the sacrifices performed are meant for Lord Vishnu, not for the demigods.
