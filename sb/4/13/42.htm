Canto 4: Creation of the Fourth OrderChapter 13: Description of the Descendants of Dhruva Mahārāja

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 4.13.42

tam vicakshya khalam putram

śāsanair vividhair nripah

yadā na śāsitum kalpo

bhriśam āsīt sudurmanāh

SYNONYMS

tam — him; vicakshya — observing; khalam — cruel; putram — son; śāsanaih — by punishments; vividhaih — different kinds of; nripah — the King; yadā — when; na — not; śāsitum — to bring under control; kalpah — was able; bhriśam — greatly; āsīt — became; su-durmanāh — aggrieved.

TRANSLATION

After seeing the cruel and merciless behavior of his son, Vena, King Ańga punished him in different ways to reform him, but was unable to bring him to the path of gentleness. He thus became greatly aggrieved.
