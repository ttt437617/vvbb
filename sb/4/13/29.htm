Canto 4: Creation of the Fourth OrderChapter 13: Description of the Descendants of Dhruva Mahārāja

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 4.13.29

maitreya uvāca

ańgo dvija-vacah śrutvā

yajamānah sudurmanāh

tat prashtum vyasrijad vācam

sadasyāms tad-anujñayā

SYNONYMS

maitreyah uvāca — the great sage Maitreya answered; ańgah — King Ańga; dvija-vacah — the brāhmanas' words; śrutvā — after hearing; yajamānah — the performer of the sacrifice; sudurmanāh — very much aggrieved in mind; tat — about that; prashtum — in order to inquire; vyasrijat vācam — he spoke; sadasyān — to the priests; tat — their; anujñayā — taking permission.

TRANSLATION

Maitreya explained that King Ańga, after hearing the statements of the priests, was greatly aggrieved. At that time he took permission from the priests to break his silence and inquired from all the priests who were present in the sacrificial arena.
