Canto 4: Creation of the Fourth OrderChapter 23: Mahārāja Prithu's Going Back Home

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 4.23.38

vaicitravīryābhihitam

mahan-māhātmya-sūcakam

asmin kritam atimartyam

pārthavīm gatim āpnuyāt

SYNONYMS

vaicitravīrya — O son of Vicitravīrya (Vidura); abhihitam — explained; mahat — great; māhātmya — greatness; sūcakam — awakening; asmin — in this; kritam — performed; ati-martyam — uncommon; pārthavīm — in connection with Prithu Mahārāja; gatim — advancement, destination; āpnuyāt — one should achieve.

TRANSLATION

The great sage Maitreya continued: My dear Vidura, I have as far as possible spoken the narrations about Prithu Mahārāja, which enrich one's devotional attitude. Whoever takes advantage of these benefits also goes back home, back to Godhead, like Mahārāja Prithu.

PURPORT

The word śrāvayet, mentioned in a previous verse, indicates that one should not only read for himself, but should also induce others to read and hear. That is called preaching. Caitanya Mahāprabhu recommended this practice: yāre dekha, tāre kaha 'krishna'-upadeśa (Cc. Madhya 7.128). "Whomever you meet, simply talk with him about the instructions given by Krishna or tell him of narrations about Krishna." Prithu Mahārāja's history of devotional service is as potent as narrations about the activities of the Supreme Personality of Godhead. One should not make distinctions between the pastimes of the Lord and the activities of Prithu Mahārāja, and whenever it is possible a devotee should attempt to induce others to hear about Prithu Mahārāja. One should not only read of his pastimes for one's own benefit but should induce others to read and hear about them also. In this way everyone can be benefited.
