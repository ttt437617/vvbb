Canto 4: Creation of the Fourth OrderChapter 23: Mahārāja Prithu's Going Back Home

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 4.23.23

vilokyānugatām sādhvīm

prithum vīra-varam patim

tushtuvur varadā devair

deva-patnyah sahasraśah

SYNONYMS

vilokya — by observing; anugatām — dying after the husband; sādhvīm — the chaste woman; prithum — of King Prithu; vīra-varam — the great warrior; patim — husband; tushtuvuh — offered prayers; vara-dāh — able to give benediction; devaih — by the demigods; deva-patnyah — the wives of the demigods; sahasraśah — in thousands.

TRANSLATION

After observing this brave act performed by the chaste wife Arci, the wife of the great King Prithu, many thousands of the wives of the demigods, along with their husbands, offered prayers to the Queen, for they were very much satisfied.
