Canto 4: Creation of the Fourth OrderChapter 23: Mahārāja Prithu's Going Back Home

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 4.23.35

dhanyam yaśasyam āyushyam

svargyam kali-malāpaham

dharmārtha-kāma-mokshānām

samyak siddhim abhīpsubhih

śraddhayaitad anuśrāvyam

caturnām kāranam param

SYNONYMS

dhanyam — the source of riches; yaśasyam — the source of reputation; āyushyam — the source of an increased span of life; svargyam — the source of elevation to the heavenly planets; kali — of the age of Kali; mala-apaham — decreasing the contamination; dharma — religion; artha — economic development; kāma — sense gratification; mokshānām — of liberation; samyak — completely; siddhim — perfection; abhīpsubhih — by those desiring; śraddhayā — with great respect; etat — this narration; anuśrāvyam — must one hear; caturnām — of the four; kāranam — cause; param — ultimate.

TRANSLATION

By hearing the narration of Prithu Mahārāja, one can become great, increase his duration of life, gain promotion to the heavenly planets and counteract the contaminations of this age of Kali. In addition, one can promote the causes of religion, economic development, sense gratification and liberation. Therefore from all sides it is advisable for a materialistic person who is interested in such things to read and hear the narrations of the life and character of Prithu Mahārāja.

PURPORT

By reading and hearing the narrations of the life and character of Prithu Mahārāja, one naturally becomes a devotee, and as soon as one becomes a devotee, his material desires automatically become fulfilled. Therefore it is recommended in Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam (2.3.10):

akāmah sarva-kāmo vā

moksha-kāma udāra-dhīh

tīvrena bhakti-yogena

yajeta purusham param

If a person wants to return home, back to Godhead, or wants to become a pure devotee (akāma), or wants some material prosperity (sakāma or sarva-kāma), or wants to merge into the existence of the Supreme Brahman effulgence (moksha-kāma), he is recommended to take to the path of devotional service and hear and chant of Lord Vishnu or of His devotee. This is the sum and substance of all Vedic literatures. Vedaiś ca sarvair aham eva vedyah (Bg. 15.15). The purpose of Vedic knowledge is to understand Krishna and His devotees. Whenever we speak of Krishna, we refer to His devotees also, for He is not alone. He is never nirviśesha or śūnya, without variety, or zero. Krishna is full of variety, and as soon as Krishna is present, there cannot be any question of void.
