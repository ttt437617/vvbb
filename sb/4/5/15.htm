Canto 4: Creation of the Fourth OrderChapter 5: Frustration of the Sacrifice of Daksha

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 4.5.15

rurujur yajña-pātrāni

tathaike 'gnīn anāśayan

kundeshv amūtrayan kecid

bibhidur vedi-mekhalāh

SYNONYMS

rurujuh — broke; yajña-pātrāni — the pots used in the sacrifice; tathā — so; eke — some; agnīn — the sacrificial fires; anāśayan — extinguished; kundeshu — on the sacrificial arenas; amūtrayan — passed urine; kecit — some; bibhiduh — tore down; vedi-mekhalāh — the boundary lines of the sacrificial arena.

TRANSLATION

They broke all the pots made for use in the sacrifice, and some of them began to extinguish the sacrificial fire. Some tore down the boundary line of the sacrificial arena, and some passed urine on the arena.
