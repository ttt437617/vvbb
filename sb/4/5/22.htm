Canto 4: Creation of the Fourth OrderChapter 5: Frustration of the Sacrifice of Daksha

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 4.5.22

ākramyorasi dakshasya

śita-dhārena hetinā

chindann api tad uddhartum

nāśaknot tryambakas tadā

SYNONYMS

ākramya — having sat; urasi — on the chest; dakshasya — of Daksha; śita-dhārena — having a sharp blade; hetinā — with a weapon; chindan — cutting; api — even though; tat — that (head); uddhartum — to separate; na aśaknot — was not able; tri-ambakah — Vīrabhadra (who had three eyes); tadā — after this.

TRANSLATION

Then Vīrabhadra, the giantlike personality, sat on the chest of Daksha and tried to separate his head from his body with sharp weapons, but was unsuccessful.
