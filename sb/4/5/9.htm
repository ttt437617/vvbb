Canto 4: Creation of the Fourth OrderChapter 5: Frustration of the Sacrifice of Daksha

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 4.5.9

prasūti-miśrāh striya udvigna-cittā

ūcur vipāko vrijinasyaiva tasya

yat paśyantīnām duhitrinām prajeśah

sutām satīm avadadhyāv anāgām

SYNONYMS

prasūti-miśrāh — headed by Prasūti; striyah — the women; udvigna-cittāh — being very anxious; ūcuh — said; vipākah — the resultant danger; vrijinasya — of the sinful activity; eva — indeed; tasya — his (Daksha's); yat — because; paśyantīnām — who were looking on; duhitrinām — of her sisters; prajeśah — the lord of the created beings (Daksha); sutām — his daughter; satīm — Satī; avadadhyau — insulted; anāgām — completely innocent.

TRANSLATION

Prasūti, the wife of Daksha, along with the other women assembled, became very anxious and said: This danger has been created by Daksha because of the death of Satī, who, even though completely innocent, quit her body as her sisters looked on.

PURPORT

Prasūti, being a softhearted woman, could immediately understand that the imminent danger approaching was due to the impious activity of hardhearted Prajāpati Daksha. He was so cruel that he would not save her youngest daughter, Satī, from the act of committing suicide in the presence of her sisters. Satī's mother could understand how much Satī had been pained by the insult of her father. Satī had been present along with the other daughters, and Daksha had purposely received all of them but her because she happened to be the wife of Lord Śiva. This consideration convinced the wife of Daksha of the danger which was now ahead, and thus she knew that Daksha must be prepared to die for his heinous act.
