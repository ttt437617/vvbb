Canto 4: Creation of the Fourth OrderChapter 5: Frustration of the Sacrifice of Daksha

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 4.5.25

sādhu-vādas tadā teshām

karma tat tasya paśyatām

bhūta-preta-piśācānām

anyeshām tad-viparyayah

SYNONYMS

sādhu-vādah — joyful exclamation; tadā — at that time; teshām — of those (followers of Lord Śiva); karma — action; tat — that; tasya — of him (Vīrabhadra); paśyatām — seeing; bhūta-preta-piśācānām — of the bhūtas (ghosts), pretas and piśācas; anyeshām — of the others (in the party of Daksha); tat-viparyayah — the opposite of that (an exclamation of grief).

TRANSLATION

Upon seeing the action of Vīrabhadra, the party of Lord Śiva was pleased and cried out joyfully, and all the bhūtas, ghosts and demons that had come made a tumultuous sound. On the other hand, the brāhmanas in charge of the sacrifice cried out in grief at the death of Daksha.
