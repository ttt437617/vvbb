Canto 4: Creation of the Fourth OrderChapter 5: Frustration of the Sacrifice of Daksha

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 4.5.26

juhāvaitac chiras tasmin

dakshināgnāv amarshitah

tad-deva-yajanam dagdhvā

prātishthad guhyakālayam

SYNONYMS

juhāva — sacrificed as an oblation; etat — that; śirah — head; tasmin — in that; dakshina-agnau — in the sacrificial fire on the southern side; amarshitah — Vīrabhadra, being greatly angry; tat — of Daksha; deva-yajanam — the arrangements for the sacrifice to the demigods; dagdhvā — having set fire; prātishthat — departed; guhyaka-ālayam — to the abode of the Guhyakas (Kailāsa).

TRANSLATION

Vīrabhadra then took the head and with great anger threw it into the southern side of the sacrificial fire, offering it as an oblation. In this way the followers of Lord Śiva devastated all the arrangements for sacrifice. After setting fire to the whole arena, they departed for their master's abode, Kailāsa.

Thus end the Bhaktivedanta purports of the Fourth Canto, Fifth Chapter, of the Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam, entitled "Frustration of the Sacrifice of Daksha."
