Canto 4: Creation of the Fourth OrderChapter 2: Daksha Curses Lord Śiva

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 4.2.23

buddhyā parābhidhyāyinyā

vismritātma-gatih paśuh

strī-kāmah so 'stv atitarām

daksho basta-mukho 'cirāt

SYNONYMS

buddhyā — by intelligence; para-abhidhyāyinyā — by accepting the body as the self; vismrita-ātma-gatih — having forgotten the knowledge of Vishnu; paśuh — an animal; strī-kāmah — attached to sex life; sah — he (Daksha); astu — let; atitarām — excessive; dakshah — Daksha; basta-mukhah — the face of a goat; acirāt — in a very short time.

TRANSLATION

Daksha has accepted the body as all in all. Therefore, since he has forgotten the vishnu-pāda, or vishnu-gati, and is attached to sex life only, within a short time he will have the face of a goat.
