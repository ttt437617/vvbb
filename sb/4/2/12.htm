Canto 4: Creation of the Fourth OrderChapter 2: Daksha Curses Lord Śiva

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 4.2.12

grihītvā mriga-śāvākshyāh

pānim markata-locanah

pratyutthānābhivādārhe

vācāpy akrita nocitam

SYNONYMS

grihītvā — taking; mriga-śāva — like a deer cub; akshyāh — of her who has eyes; pānim — the hand; markata — of a monkey; locanah — he who has the eyes; pratyutthāna — of rising from one's seat; abhivāda — the honor; arhe — to me, who deserves; vācā — with sweet words; api — even; akrita na — he did not do; ucitam — honor.

TRANSLATION

He has eyes like a monkey's, yet he has married my daughter, whose eyes are just like those of a deer cub. Nevertheless he did not stand up to receive me, nor did he think it fit to welcome me with sweet words.
