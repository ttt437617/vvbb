Canto 4: Creation of the Fourth OrderChapter 2: Daksha Curses Lord Śiva

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 4.2.6

udatishthan sadasyās te

sva-dhishnyebhyah sahāgnayah

rite viriñcām śarvam ca

tad-bhāsākshipta-cetasah

SYNONYMS

udatishthan — stood up; sadasyāh — the members of the assembly; te — they; sva-dhishnyebhyah — from their own seats; saha-agnayah — along with the fire-gods; rite — except for; viriñcām — Brahmā; śarvam — Śiva; ca — and; tat — his (Daksha's); bhāsa — by the luster; ākshipta — are influenced; cetasah — those whose minds.

TRANSLATION

Influenced by his personal bodily luster, all the fire-gods and other participants in that great assembly, with the exceptions of Lord Brahmā and Lord Śiva, gave up their own sitting places and stood in respect for Daksha.
