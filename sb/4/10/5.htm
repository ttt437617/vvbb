Canto 4: Creation of the Fourth OrderChapter 10: Dhruva Mahārāja's Fight With the Yakshas

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 4.10.5

gatvodīcīm diśam rājā

rudrānucara-sevitām

dadarśa himavad-dronyām

purīm guhyaka-sańkulām

SYNONYMS

gatvā — going; udīcīm — northern; diśam — direction; rājā — King Dhruva; rudra-anucara — by followers of Rudra, Lord Śiva; sevitām — inhabited; dadarśa — saw; himavat — Himalayan; dronyām — in a valley; purīm — a city; guhyaka — ghostly persons; sańkulām — full of.

TRANSLATION

Dhruva Mahārāja went to the northern direction of the Himalayan range. In a valley he saw a city full of ghostly persons who were followers of Lord Śiva.

PURPORT

In this verse it is stated that the Yakshas are more or less devotees of Lord Śiva. By this indication the Yakshas may be taken to be the Himalayan tribes like the Tibetans.
