Canto 4: Creation of the Fourth OrderChapter 10: Dhruva Mahārāja's Fight With the Yakshas

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 4.10.8

sa tān āpatato vīra

ugra-dhanvā mahā-rathah

ekaikam yugapat sarvān

ahan bānais tribhis tribhih

SYNONYMS

sah — Dhruva Mahārāja; tān — all of them; āpatatah — falling upon him; vīrah — hero; ugra-dhanvā — powerful bowman; mahā-rathah — who could fight with many chariots; eka-ekam — one after another; yugapat — simultaneously; sarvān — all of them; ahan — killed; bānaih — by arrows; tribhih tribhih — by threes.

TRANSLATION

Dhruva Mahārāja, who was a great charioteer and certainly a great bowman also, immediately began to kill them by simultaneously discharging arrows three at a time.
