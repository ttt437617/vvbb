Canto 4: Creation of the Fourth OrderChapter 10: Dhruva Mahārāja's Fight With the Yakshas

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 4.10.20

hatāvaśishtā itare ranājirād

raksho-ganāh kshatriya-varya-sāyakaih

prāyo vivriknāvayavā vidudruvur

mrigendra-vikrīdita-yūthapā iva

SYNONYMS

hata-avaśishtāh — the remaining soldiers who were not killed; itare — others; rana-ajirāt — from the battlefield; rakshah-ganāh — the Yakshas; kshatriya-varya — of the greatest of the kshatriyas, or warriors; sāyakaih — by the arrows; prāyah — mostly; vivrikna — cut to pieces; avayavāh — their bodily limbs; vidudruvuh — fled; mrigendra — by a lion; vikrīdita — being defeated; yūthapāh — elephants; iva — like.

TRANSLATION

The remaining Yakshas who somehow or other were not killed had their limbs cut to pieces by the arrows of the great warrior Dhruva Mahārāja. Thus they began to flee, just as elephants flee when defeated by a lion.
