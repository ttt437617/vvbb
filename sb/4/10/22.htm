Canto 4: Creation of the Fourth OrderChapter 10: Dhruva Mahārāja's Fight With the Yakshas

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 4.10.22

iti bruvamś citra-rathah sva-sārathim

yattah pareshām pratiyoga-śańkitah

śuśrāva śabdam jaladher iveritam

nabhasvato dikshu rajo 'nvadriśyata

SYNONYMS

iti — thus; bruvan — talking; citra-rathah — Dhruva Mahārāja, whose chariot was very beautiful; sva-sārathim — to his charioteer; yattah — being on guard; pareshām — from his enemies; pratiyoga — counterattack; śańkitah — being apprehensive; śuśrāva — heard; śabdam — sound; jaladheh — from the ocean; iva — as if; īritam — resounded; nabhasvatah — because of wind; dikshu — in all directions; rajah — dust; anu — then; adriśyata — was perceived.

TRANSLATION

In the meantime, while Dhruva Mahārāja, doubtful of his mystic enemies, was talking with his charioteer, they heard a tremendous sound, as if the whole ocean were there, and they found that from the sky a great dust storm was coming over them from all directions.
