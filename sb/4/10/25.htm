Canto 4: Creation of the Fourth OrderChapter 10: Dhruva Mahārāja's Fight With the Yakshas

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 4.10.25

tatah khe 'driśyata girir

nipetuh sarvato-diśam

gadā-parigha-nistrimśa-

musalāh sāśma-varshinah

SYNONYMS

tatah — thereafter; khe — in the sky; adriśyata — was visible; girih — a mountain; nipetuh — fell down; sarvatah-diśam — from all directions; gadā — clubs; parigha — iron bludgeons; nistrimśa — swords; musalāh — maces; sa-aśma — great pieces of stone; varshinah — with a shower of.

TRANSLATION

Next, a great mountain was visible in the sky, and from all directions hailstones fell, along with lances, clubs, swords, iron bludgeons and great pieces of stone.
