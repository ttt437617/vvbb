Canto 4: Creation of the Fourth OrderChapter 10: Dhruva Mahārāja's Fight With the Yakshas

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 4.10.15

nadatsu yātudhāneshu

jaya-kāśishv atho mridhe

udatishthad rathas tasya

nīhārād iva bhāskarah

SYNONYMS

nadatsu — while exclaiming; yātudhāneshu — the ghostly Yakshas; jaya-kāśishu — proclaiming victory; atho — then; mridhe — in the fighting; udatishthat — appeared; rathah — the chariot; tasya — of Dhruva Mahārāja; nīhārāt — from the mist; iva — like; bhāskarah — the sun.

TRANSLATION

The Yakshas, being temporarily victorious, exclaimed that they had conquered Dhruva Mahārāja. But in the meantime Dhruva's chariot suddenly appeared, just as the sun suddenly appears from within foggy mist.

PURPORT

Here Dhruva Mahārāja is compared to the sun and the great assembly of the Yakshas to foggy mist. Fog is insignificant in comparison with the sun. Although the sun is sometimes seen to be covered by fog, in fact the sun cannot be covered by anything. Our eyes may be covered by a cloud, but the sun is never covered. By this comparison to the sun, the greatness of Dhruva Mahārāja in all circumstances is affirmed.
