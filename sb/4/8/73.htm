Canto 4: Creation of the Fourth OrderChapter 8: Dhruva Mahārāja Leaves Home for the Forest

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 4.8.73

dvitīyam ca tathā māsam

shashthe shashthe 'rbhako dine

trina-parnādibhih śīrnaih

kritānno 'bhyarcayan vibhum

SYNONYMS

dvitīyam — the next month; ca — also; tathā — as mentioned above; māsam — month; shashthe shashthe — every sixth day; arbhakah — the innocent boy; dine — on days; trina-parna-ādibhih — by grasses and leaves; śīrnaih — which were dry; krita-annah — made for his food; abhyarcayan — and thus continued his method of worship; vibhum — for the Supreme Personality of Godhead.

TRANSLATION

In the second month Dhruva Mahārāja ate only every six days, and for his eatables he took dry grass and leaves. Thus he continued his worship.
