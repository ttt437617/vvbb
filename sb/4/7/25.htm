Canto 4: Creation of the Fourth OrderChapter 7: The Sacrifice Performed by Daksha

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 4.7.25

daksho grihītārhana-sādanottamam

yajñeśvaram viśva-srijām param gurum

sunanda-nandādy-anugair vritam mudā

grinan prapede prayatah kritāñjalih

SYNONYMS

dakshah — Daksha; grihīta — accepted; arhana — rightful; sādana-uttamam — sacrificial vessel; yajña-īśvaram — unto the master of all sacrifices; viśva-srijām — of all the Prajāpatis; param — the supreme; gurum — preceptor; sunanda-nanda-ādi-anugaih — by associates like Sunanda and Nanda; vritam — surrounded; mudā — with great pleasure; grinan — offering respectful prayers; prapede — took shelter; prayatah — having a subdued mind; krita-añjalih — with folded hands.

TRANSLATION

When Lord Vishnu accepted the oblations offered in the sacrifice, Daksha, the Prajāpati, began with great pleasure to offer respectful prayers unto Him. The Supreme Personality of Godhead is actually the master of all sacrifices and preceptor of all the Prajāpatis, and He is served even by such personalities as Nanda and Sunanda.
