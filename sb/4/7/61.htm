Canto 4: Creation of the Fourth OrderChapter 7: The Sacrifice Performed by Daksha

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 4.7.61

idam pavitram param īśa-ceshtitam

yaśasyam āyushyam aghaugha-marshanam

yo nityadākarnya naro 'nukīrtayed

dhunoty agham kaurava bhakti-bhāvatah

SYNONYMS

idam — this; pavitram — pure; param — supreme; īśa-ceshtitam — pastime of the Supreme Lord; yaśasyam — fame; āyushyam — long duration of life; agha-ogha-marshanam — destroying sins; yah — who; nityadā — always; ākarnya — after hearing; narah — a person; anukīrtayet — should narrate; dhunoti — clears off; agham — material contamination; kaurava — O descendant of Kuru; bhakti-bhāvatah — with faith and devotion.

TRANSLATION

The great sage Maitreya concluded: If one hears and again narrates, with faith and devotion, this story of the Daksha yajña as it was conducted by the Supreme Personality of Godhead, Vishnu, then certainly one is cleared of all contamination of material existence, O son of Kuru.

Thus end the Bhaktivedanta purports of the Fourth Canto, Seventh Chapter, of the Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam, entitled "The Sacrifice performed by Daksha."
