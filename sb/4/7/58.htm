Canto 4: Creation of the Fourth OrderChapter 7: The Sacrifice Performed by Daksha

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 4.7.58

evam dākshāyanī hitvā

satī pūrva-kalevaram

jajñe himavatah kshetre

menāyām iti śuśruma

SYNONYMS

evam — thus; dākshāyanī — the daughter of Daksha; hitvā — after giving up; satī — Satī; pūrva-kalevaram — her former body; jajñe — was born; himavatah — of the Himalayas; kshetre — in the wife; menāyām — in Menā; iti — thus; śuśruma — I have heard.

TRANSLATION

Maitreya said: I have heard that after giving up the body she had received from Daksha, Dākshāyanī (his daughter) took her birth in the kingdom of the Himalayas. She was born as the daughter of Menā. I heard this from authoritative sources.

PURPORT

Menā is also known as Menakā and is the wife of the king of the Himalayas.
