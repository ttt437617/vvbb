Canto 4: Creation of the Fourth OrderChapter 7: The Sacrifice Performed by Daksha

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 4.7.12

kricchrāt samstabhya ca manah

prema-vihvalitah sudhīh

śaśamsa nirvyalīkena

bhāveneśam prajāpatih

SYNONYMS

kricchrāt — with great endeavor; samstabhya — pacifying; ca — also; manah — mind; prema-vihvalitah — bewildered by love and affection; su-dhīh — one who has come to his real senses; śaśamsa — praised; nirvyalīkena — without duplicity, or with great love; bhāvena — in feeling; īśam — to Lord Śiva; prajāpatih — King Daksha.

TRANSLATION

At this time, King Daksha, afflicted by love and affection, was very much awakened to his real senses. With great endeavor, he pacified his mind, checked his feelings, and with pure consciousness began to offer prayers to Lord Śiva.
