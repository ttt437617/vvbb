Canto 4: Creation of the Fourth OrderChapter 6: Brahmā Satisfies Lord Śiva

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 4.6.12

mayūra-kekābhirutam

madāndhāli-vimūrcchitam

plāvitai rakta-kanthānām

kūjitaiś ca patattrinām

SYNONYMS

mayūra — peacocks; kekā — with the cries; abhirutam — resounding; mada — by intoxication; andha — blinded; ali — by the bees; vimūrcchitam — resounded; plāvitaih — with the singing; rakta-kanthānām — of the cuckoos; kūjitaih — with the whispering; ca — and; patattrinām — of other birds.

TRANSLATION

On Kailāsa Hill there is always the rhythmical sound of the peacocks' sweet vibrations and the bees' humming. Cuckoos are always singing, and other birds whisper amongst themselves.
