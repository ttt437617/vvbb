Canto 4: Creation of the Fourth OrderChapter 6: Brahmā Satisfies Lord Śiva

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 4.6.19-20

kumudotpala-kahlāra-

śatapatra-vanarddhibhih

nalinīshu kalam kūjat-

khaga-vrindopaśobhitam

mrigaih śākhāmrigaih krodair

mrigendrair riksha-śalyakaih

gavayaih śarabhair vyāghrai

rurubhir mahishādibhih

SYNONYMS

kumuda — kumuda; utpala — utpala; kahlāra — kahlāra; śatapatra — lotuses; vana — forest; riddhibhih — being covered with; nalinīshu — in the lakes; kalam — very sweetly; kūjat — whispering; khaga — of birds; vrinda — groups; upaśobhitam — decorated with; mrigaih — with deer; śākhā-mrigaih — with monkeys; krodaih — with boars; mriga-indraih — with lions; riksha-śalyakaih — with rikshas and śalyakas; gavayaih — with forest cows; śarabhaih — with forest asses; vyāghraih — with tigers; rurubhih — with small deer; mahisha-ādibhih — with buffalo, etc.

TRANSLATION

There are different kinds of lotus flowers, such as kumuda, utpala and śatapatra. The forest appears to be a decorated garden, and the small lakes are full of various kinds of birds who whisper very sweetly. There are many kinds of other animals also, like deer, monkeys, boars, lions, rikshas, śalyakas, forest cows, forest asses, tigers, small deer, buffalo and many other animals, who are fully enjoying their lives.
