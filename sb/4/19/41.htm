Canto 4: Creation of the Fourth OrderChapter 19: King Prithu's One Hundred Horse Sacrifices

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 4.19.41

viprāh satyāśishas tushtāh

śraddhayā labdha-dakshināh

āśisho yuyujuh kshattar

ādi-rājāya sat-kritāh

SYNONYMS

viprāh — all the brāhmanas; satya — true; āśishah — whose benedictions; tushtāh — being very satisfied; śraddhayā — with great respect; labdha-dakshināh — who obtained rewards; āśishah — benedictions; yuyujuh — offered; kshattah — O Vidura; ādi-rājāya — upon the original king; sat-kritāh — being honored.

TRANSLATION

With great respect, the original king, Prithu, offered all kinds of rewards to the brāhmanas present at the sacrifice. Since all these brāhmanas were very much satisfied, they gave their heartfelt blessings to the King.
