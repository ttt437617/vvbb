Canto 4: Creation of the Fourth OrderChapter 19: King Prithu's One Hundred Horse Sacrifices

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 4.19.42

tvayāhūtā mahā-bāho

sarva eva samāgatāh

pūjitā dāna-mānābhyām

pitri-devarshi-mānavāh

SYNONYMS

tvayā — by you; āhūtāh — were invited; mahā-bāho — O great mighty-armed one; sarve — all; eva — certainly; samāgatāh — assembled; pūjitāh — were honored; dāna — by charity; mānābhyām — and by respect; pitri — the inhabitants of Pitriloka; deva — demigods; rishi — great sages; mānavāh — as well as common men.

TRANSLATION

All the great sages and brāhmanas said: O mighty King, by your invitation all classes of living entities have attended this assembly. They have come from Pitriloka and the heavenly planets, and great sages as well as common men have attended this meeting. Now all of them are very much satisfied by your dealings and your charity towards them.

Thus end the Bhaktivedanta purports of the Fourth Canto, Nineteenth Chapter, of the Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam, entitled "King Prithu's One Hundred Horse Sacrifices."
