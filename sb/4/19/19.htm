Canto 4: Creation of the Fourth OrderChapter 19: King Prithu's One Hundred Horse Sacrifices

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 4.19.19

upasrijya tamas tīvram

jahārāśvam punar harih

cashāla-yūpataś channo

hiranya-raśanam vibhuh

SYNONYMS

upasrijya — creating; tamah — darkness; tīvram — dense; jahāra — took away; aśvam — the horse; punah — again; harih — King Indra; cashāla-yūpatah — from the wooden instrument where the animals were sacrificed; channah — being covered; hiranya-raśanam — tied with a gold chain; vibhuh — very powerful.

TRANSLATION

My dear Vidura, Indra, being the King of heaven and very powerful, immediately brought a dense darkness upon the sacrificial arena. Covering the whole scene in this way, he again took away the horse, which was chained with golden shackles near the wooden instrument where animals were sacrificed.
