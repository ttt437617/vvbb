Canto 4: Creation of the Fourth OrderChapter 19: King Prithu's One Hundred Horse Sacrifices

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 4.19.14

tam tādriśākritim vīkshya

mene dharmam śarīrinam

jatilam bhasmanācchannam

tasmai bānam na muñcati

SYNONYMS

tam — him; tādriśa-ākritim — in such dress; vīkshya — after seeing; mene — considered; dharmam — pious or religious; śarīrinam — having a body; jatilam — having knotted hair; bhasmanā — by ashes; ācchannam — smeared all over the body; tasmai — unto him; bānam — arrow; na — not; muñcati — he did release.

TRANSLATION

King Indra was fraudulently dressed as a sannyāsī, having knotted his hair on his head and smeared ashes all over his body. Upon seeing such dress, the son of King Prithu considered Indra a religious man and pious sannyāsī. Therefore he did not release his arrows.
