Canto 4: Creation of the Fourth OrderChapter 19: King Prithu's One Hundred Horse Sacrifices

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 4.19.16

evam vainya-sutah proktas

tvaramānam vihāyasā

anvadravad abhikruddho

rāvanam gridhra-rād iva

SYNONYMS

evam — thus; vainya-sutah — the son of King Prithu; proktah — being ordered; tvaramānam — Indra, who was moving hastily; vihāyasā — in the sky; anvadravat — began to chase; abhikruddhah — being very angry; rāvanam — Rāvana; gridhra-rāt — the king of vultures; iva — like.

TRANSLATION

Being thus informed, the grandson of King Vena immediately began to follow Indra, who was fleeing through the sky in great haste. He was very angry with him, and he chased him just as the king of the vultures chased Rāvana.
