Canto 4: Creation of the Fourth OrderChapter 19: King Prithu's One Hundred Horse Sacrifices

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 4.19.6

kapilo nārado datto

yogeśāh sanakādayah

tam anvīyur bhāgavatā

ye ca tat-sevanotsukāh

SYNONYMS

kapilah — Kapila Muni; nāradah — the great sage Nārada; dattah — Dattātreya; yoga-īśāh — the masters of mystic power; sanaka-ādayah — headed by Sanaka; tam — Lord Vishnu; anvīyuh — followed; bhāgavatāh — great devotees; ye — all those who; ca — also; tat-sevana-utsukāh — always eager to serve the Lord.

TRANSLATION

Great devotees, who were always engaged in the service of the Supreme Personality of Godhead, as well as the great sages named Kapila, Nārada and Dattātreya, and masters of mystic powers, headed by Sanaka Kumāra, all attended the great sacrifice with Lord Vishnu.
