Canto 4: Creation of the Fourth OrderChapter 19: King Prithu's One Hundred Horse Sacrifices

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 4.19.1

maitreya uvāca

athādīkshata rājā tu

hayamedha-śatena sah

brahmāvarte manoh kshetre

yatra prācī sarasvatī

SYNONYMS

maitreyah uvāca — the sage Maitreya said; atha — thereafter; adīkshata — took initiation; rājā — the King; tu — then; haya — horse; medha — sacrifices; śatena — to perform one hundred; sah — he; brahmāvarte — known as Brahmāvarta; manoh — of Svāyambhuva Manu; kshetre — in the land; yatra — where; prācī — eastern; sarasvatī — the river named Sarasvatī.

TRANSLATION

The great sage Maitreya continued: My dear Vidura, King Prithu initiated the performance of one hundred horse sacrifices at the spot where the River Sarasvatī flows towards the east. This piece of land is known as Brahmāvarta, and it was controlled by Svāyambhuva Manu.
