Canto 4: Creation of the Fourth OrderChapter 19: King Prithu's One Hundred Horse Sacrifices

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 4.19.13

atrinā codito hantum

prithu-putro mahā-rathah

anvadhāvata sańkruddhas

tishtha tishtheti cābravīt

SYNONYMS

atrinā — by the great sage Atri; coditah — being encouraged; hantum — to kill; prithu-putrah — the son of King Prithu; mahā-rathah — a great hero; anvadhāvata — followed; sańkruddhah — being very angry; tishtha tishtha — just wait, just wait; iti — thus; ca — also; abravīt — he said.

TRANSLATION

When the son of King Prithu was informed by Atri of King Indra's trick, he immediately became very angry and followed Indra to kill him, calling, "Wait! Wait!"

PURPORT

The words tishtha tishtha are used by a kshatriya when he challenges his enemy. When fighting, a kshatriya cannot flee from the battlefield. However, when a kshatriya out of cowardice flees from the battlefield, showing his back to his enemy, he is challenged with the words tishtha tishtha. A real kshatriya does not kill his enemy from behind, nor does a real kshatriya turn his back on the battlefield. According to kshatriya principle and spirit, one either attains victory or dies on the battlefield. Although King Indra was very exalted, being the King of heaven, he became degraded due to his stealing the horse intended for sacrifice. Therefore he fled without observing the kshatriya principles, and the son of Prithu had to challenge him with the words tishtha tishtha.
