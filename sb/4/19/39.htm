Canto 4: Creation of the Fourth OrderChapter 19: King Prithu's One Hundred Horse Sacrifices

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 4.19.39

maitreya uvāca

ittham sa loka-gurunā

samādishto viśāmpatih

tathā ca kritvā vātsalyam

maghonāpi ca sandadhe

SYNONYMS

maitreyah uvāca — the great sage Maitreya continued to speak; ittham — thus; sah — King Prithu; loka-gurunā — by the original teacher of all people, Lord Brahmā; samādishtah — being advised; viśām-patih — the king, master of the people; tathā — in that way; ca — also; kritvā — having done; vātsalyam — affection; maghonā — with Indra; api — even; ca — also; sandadhe — concluded peace.

TRANSLATION

The great sage Maitreya continued: When King Prithu was thus advised by the supreme teacher, Lord Brahmā, he abandoned his eagerness to perform yajñas and with great affection concluded a peace with King Indra.
