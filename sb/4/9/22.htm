Canto 4: Creation of the Fourth OrderChapter 9: Dhruva Mahārāja Returns Home

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 4.9.22

prasthite tu vanam pitrā

dattvā gām dharma-samśrayah

shat-trimśad-varsha-sāhasram

rakshitāvyāhatendriyah

SYNONYMS

prasthite — after departure; tu — but; vanam — to the forest; pitrā — by your father; dattvā — awarding; gām — the whole world; dharma-samśrayah — under the protection of piety; shat-trimśat — thirty-six; varsha — years; sāhasram — one thousand; rakshitā — you will rule; avyāhata — without decay; indriyah — the power of the senses.

TRANSLATION

After your father goes to the forest and awards you the rule of his kingdom, you will rule continuously the entire world for thirty-six thousand years, and all your senses will continue to be as strong as they are now. You will never become old.

PURPORT

In the Satya-yuga people generally lived for one hundred thousand years. Dhruva Mahārāja's ruling the world for thirty-six thousand years was quite possible in those days.
