Canto 4: Creation of the Fourth OrderChapter 12: Dhruva Mahārāja Goes Back to Godhead

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 4.12.2

dhanada uvāca

bho bhoh kshatriya-dāyāda

paritushto 'smi te 'nagha

yat tvam pitāmahādeśād

vairam dustyajam atyajah

SYNONYMS

dhana-dah uvāca — the master of the treasury (Kuvera) said; bhoh bhoh — O; kshatriya-dāyāda — O son of a kshatriya; paritushtah — very glad; asmi — I am; te — with you; anagha — O sinless one; yat — because; tvam — you; pitāmaha — of your grandfather; ādeśāt — under the instruction; vairam — enmity; dustyajam — difficult to avoid; atyajah — have given up.

TRANSLATION

The master of the treasury, Kuvera, said: O sinless son of a kshatriya, I am very glad to know that under the instruction of your grandfather you have given up your enmity, although it is very difficult to avoid. I am very pleased with you.
