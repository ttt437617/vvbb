Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 60: Lord Krishna Teases Queen Rukminī.

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.60.16

vaidarbhy etad avijñāya

tvayādīrgha-samīkshayā

vritā vayam gunair hīnā

bhikshubhih ślāghitā mudhā

SYNONYMS

vaidarbhi — O princess of Vidarbha; etat — this; avijñāya — not knowing; tvayā — by you; adīrgha-samīkshayā — without long-range vision; vritāh — chosen; vayam — We; gunaih — of good qualities; hīnāh — devoid; bhikshubhih — by beggars; ślāghitāh — praised; mudhā — out of their bewilderment.

TRANSLATION

O Vaidarbhī, not being farsighted, you didn't realize this, and therefore you chose Us as your husband, even though We have no good qualities and are glorified only by deluded beggars.
