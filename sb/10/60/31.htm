Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 60: Lord Krishna Teases Queen Rukminī.

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.60.31

ayam hi paramo lābho

griheshu griha-medhinām

yan narmair īyate yāmah

priyayā bhīru bhāmini

SYNONYMS

ayam — this; hi — indeed; paramah — the greatest; lābhah — gain; griheshu — in family life; griha-medhinām — for worldly householders; yat — which; narmaih — with joking words; īyate — is spent; yāmah — time; priyayā — with one's beloved; bhīru — O timid one; bhāmini — O temperamental one.

TRANSLATION

The greatest pleasure worldly householders can enjoy at home is to spend time joking with their beloved wives, My dear timid and temperamental one.

PURPORT

The word bhāmini indicates an angry, passionate, temperamental woman. Since lovely Rukminī did not become angry despite all provocation, the Lord is still speaking facetiously.
