Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 60: Lord Krishna Teases Queen Rukminī.

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.60.20

udāsīnā vayam nūnam

na stry-apatyārtha-kāmukāh

ātma-labdhyāsmahe pūrnā

gehayor jyotir-akriyāh

SYNONYMS

udāsīnāh — indifferent; vayam — We; nūnam — indeed; na — not; strī — for wives; apatya — children; artha — and wealth; kāmukāh — hankering; ātma-labdhyā — by being self-satisfied; āsmahe — We remain; pūrnāh — complete; gehayoh — to body and home; jyotih — like a fire; akriyāh — engaged in no activity.

TRANSLATION

We care nothing for wives, children and wealth. Always satisfied within Ourselves, We do not work for body and home, but like a light, We merely witness.
