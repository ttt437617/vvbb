Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 60: Lord Krishna Teases Queen Rukminī.

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.60.24

tasyāh su-duhkha-bhaya-śoka-vinashta-buddher

hastāc chlathad-valayato vyajanam papāta

dehaś ca viklava-dhiyah sahasaiva muhyan

rambheva vāyu-vihato pravikīrya keśān

SYNONYMS

tashyāh — her; su-duhkha — by the great unhappiness; bhaya — fear; śoka — and remorse; vinashta — spoiled; buddheh — whose intelligence; hastāt — from the hand; ślathat — slipping; valayatah — whose bangles; vyajanam — the fan; papāta — fell; dehah — her body; ca — also; viklava — disrupted; dhiyah — whose mind; sahasā eva — suddenly; muhyan — fainting; rambhā — a plantain tree; iva — as if; vāyu — by the wind; vihatah — blown down; pravikīrya — scattering; keśān — her hair.

TRANSLATION

Rukminī's mind was overwhelmed with unhappiness, fear and grief. Her bangles slipped from her hand, and her fan fell to the ground. In her bewilderment she suddenly fainted, her hair scattering all about as her body fell to the ground like a plantain tree blown over by the wind.

PURPORT

Shocked by Lord Krishna's words, Rukminī could not understand that the Lord was only teasing, and thus she displayed these ecstatic symptoms of grief, which Śrīla Viśvanātha Cakravartī characterizes as sāttvika ecstasies ranging from "becoming stunned" to "dissolution."
