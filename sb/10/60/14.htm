Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 60: Lord Krishna Teases Queen Rukminī.

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.60.14

nishkiñcanā vayam śaśvan

nishkiñcana-jana-priyāh

tasmā tprāyena na hy ādhyā

mām bhajanti su-madhyame

SYNONYMS

nishkiñcanāh — having no possessions; vayam — We; śaśvat — always; nishkiñcana-jana — to those who have no possessions; priyāh — very dear; tasmāt — therefore; prāyena — usually; na — not; hi — indeed; ādhyāh — the rich; mām — Me; bhajanti — worship; su-madhyame — O fine-waisted one.

TRANSLATION

We have no material possessions, and We are dear to those who similarly have nothing. Therefore, O slender one, the wealthy hardly ever worship Me.

PURPORT

Like the Lord, His devotees are uninterested in material sense gratification, being awakened to the superior pleasure of Krishna consciousness. Those who are intoxicated by material wealth cannot appreciate the supreme wealth of the kingdom of God.
