Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 60: Lord Krishna Teases Queen Rukminī.

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.60.41

yad-vāñchayā nripa-śikhāmanayo 'nga-vainya-

jāyanta-nāhusha-gayādaya aikya-patyam

rājyam visrijya viviśur vanam ambujāksha

sīdanti te 'nupadavīm ta ihāsthitāh kim

SYNONYMS

yat — for whom; vāñchayā — out of desire; nripa — of kings; śikhāmanayah — the crown jewels; ańga-vainya-jāyanta-nāhusha-gaya-ādayah — Ańga (the father of Vena), Vainya (Prithu, the son of Vena), Jāyanta (Bharata), Nāhusha (Yayāti), Gaya and others; aikya — exclusive; patyam — having sovereignty; rājyam — their kingdoms; visrijya — abandoning; viviśuh — entered; vanam — the forest; ambuja-aksha — O lotus-eyed one; sīdanti — suffer frustration; te — Your; anupadavīm — on the path; te — they; iha — in this world; āsthitāh — fixed; kim — whether.

TRANSLATION

Wanting Your association, the best of kings — Ańga, Vainya, Jāyanta, Nāhusha, Gaya and others — abandoned their absolute sovereignty and entered the forest to seek You out. How could those kings suffer frustration in this world, O lotus-eyed one?

PURPORT

Here Queen Rukminī refutes the ideas put forth by Lord Krishna in Text 13. In fact Śrīmatī Rukminī-devī repeats Lord Krishna's own words. The Lord said, āsthitāh padavīm su-bhru prāyah sīdanti yoshitah: "Women who follow My path generally suffer." Here Rukminī-devī says, sīdanti te 'nupadavīm ta ihāsthitāh kim: "Why should those fixed on Your path suffer in this world?" She gives the example of many great kings who renounced their powerful sovereignty to enter the forest, where they performed austerities and worshiped the Lord, intensely desiring His transcendental association. Thus, according to Śrīla Viśvanātha Cakravartī, Śrīmatī Rukminī-devī here intends to tell Śrī Krishna, "You have said that I, a king's daughter, am unintelligent and frustrated because I married You. But how can You accuse all these great enlightened kings of being unintelligent? They were the wisest of men, yet they gave up everything to follow You and were certainly not frustrated by the result. Indeed, they achieved the perfection of Your association."
