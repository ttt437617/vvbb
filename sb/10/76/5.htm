Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 76: The Battle Between Śālva and the Vrishnis

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.76.5

samvatsarānte bhagavān

āśu-tosha umā-patih

varena cchandayām āsa

śālvam śaranam āgatam

SYNONYMS

samvatsara — of a year; ante — at the end; bhagavān — the great lord; āśu-toshah — he who is quickly pleased; umā-patih — the master of Umā; varena — with a benediction; chandayām āsa — had him choose; śālvam — Śālva; śaranam — for shelter; āgatam — approached.

TRANSLATION

The great Lord Umāpati is known as "he who is quickly pleased," yet only at the end of a year did he gratify Śālva, who had approached him for shelter, by offering him a choice of benedictions.

PURPORT

Śālva worshiped Lord Śiva, who is famous as Āśutosha, "one who is quickly satisfied." And yet Lord Śiva did not come to Śālva for an entire year because, being bhagavān, a great, all-knowing personality, he understood that any benediction given to Lord Krishna's enemy would be fruitless. Still, as stated by the words śaranam āgatam, Śālva had come to Lord Śiva for shelter, and thus to maintain the standard principle that a worshiper receives a benediction, Lord Śiva offered one to Śālva.
