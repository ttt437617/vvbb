Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 76: The Battle Between Śālva and the Vrishnis

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.76.4

iti mūdhah pratijñāya

devam paśu-patim prabhum

ārādhayām āsa nripah

pāmśu-mushtim sakrid grasan

SYNONYMS

iti — with these words; mūdhah — the fool; pratijñāya — having vowed; devam — the lord; paśu-patim — Śiva, the protector of animallike men; prabhum — his master; āradhayām āsa — worshiped; nripah — the King; pāmśu — of dust; mushtim — a handful; sakrit — once (daily); grasan — eating.

TRANSLATION

Having thus made his vow, the foolish King proceeded to worship Lord Paśupati [Śiva] as his deity by eating a handful of dust each day, and nothing more.
