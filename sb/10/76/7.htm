Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 76: The Battle Between Śālva and the Vrishnis

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.76.7

tatheti giriśādishto

mayah para-puram-jayah

puram nirmāya śālvāya

prādāt saubham ayas-mayam

SYNONYMS

tathā — so be it; iti — having thus said; giri-śa — by Lord Śiva; ādishtah — ordered; mayah — Maya Dānava; para — of the enemy; puram — the cities; jayah — who conquers; puram — a city; nirmāya — constructing; śālvāya — to Śālva; prādāt — he gave; saubham — named Saubha; ayah — of iron; mayam — made.

TRANSLATION

Lord Śiva said, "So be it." On his order, Maya Dānava, who conquers his enemies' cities, constructed a flying iron city named Saubha and presented it to Śālva.
