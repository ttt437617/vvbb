Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 76: The Battle Between Śālva and the Vrishnis

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.76.30

kim nu vakshye 'bhisańgamya

pitarau rāma-keśavau

yuddhāt samyag apakrāntah

prishtas tatrātmanah kshamam

SYNONYMS

kim — what; nu — then; vakshye — will I say; abhisańgamya — meeting; pitarau — with My fathers; rāma-keśavau — Balarāma and Krishna; yuddhāt — from the battle; samyak — altogether; apakrāntah — fled; prishtah — questioned; tatra — in that case; ātmanah — for My self; kshamam — fitting.

TRANSLATION

"What will I say to My fathers, Rāma and Keśava, when I return to Them after having simply fled the battle? What can I tell Them that will befit My honor?

PURPORT

Śrī Pradyumna here uses the word pitarau, "fathers," loosely. Lord Balarāma was of course His uncle.
