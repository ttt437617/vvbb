Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 30: The Gopīs Search for Krishna

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.30.13

pricchatemā latā bāhūn

apy āślishtā vanaspateh

nūnam tat-karaja-sprishtā

bibhraty utpulakāny aho

SYNONYMS

pricchata — just ask; imāh — from these; latāh — creepers; bāhūn — the arms (branches); api — even though; āślishtāh — embracing; vanaspateh — of' the tree; nūnam — certainly; tat — of Him, Krishna; kara-ja — by the fingernails; sprishtāh — touched; bibhrati — they are bearing; utpulakāni — joyful eruptions on the skin; aho — just see.

TRANSLATION

Let us ask these creepers about Krishna. Even though they are embracing the arms of their husband, this tree, they certainly must have been touched by Krishna's fingernails, since out of joy they are manifesting eruptions on their skin.

PURPORT

The gopīs reasoned that the creepers would not show signs of rapture merely by physical contact with their husband, a tree. Thus the gopīs concluded that although the creepers were embracing the strong limbs of their husband, they must have been touched by Lord Krishna as He moved through the forest.
