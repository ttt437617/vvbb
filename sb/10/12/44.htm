Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 12: The Killing of the Demon Aghāsura

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.12.44

śrī-sūta uvāca

ittham sma prishtah sa tu bādarāyanis

tat-smāritānanta-hritākhilendriyah

kricchrāt punar labdha-bahir-driśih śanaih

pratyāha tam bhāgavatottamottama

SYNONYMS

śrī-sūtah uvāca — Śrī Sūta Gosvāmī said; ittham — in this way; sma — in the past; prishtah — being inquired from; sah — he; tu — indeed; bādarāyanih — Śukadeva Gosvāmī; tat — by him (Śukadeva Gosvāmī); smārita-ananta — as soon as Lord Krishna was remembered; hrita — lost in ecstasy; akhila-indriyah — all actions of the external senses; kricchrāt — with great difficulty; punah — again; labdha-bahih-driśih — having revived his external sensory perception; śanaih — slowly; pratyāha — replied; tam — unto Mahārāja Parīkshit; bhāgavata-uttama-uttama — O great saintly person, greatest of all devotees (Śaunaka).

TRANSLATION

Sūta Gosvāmī said: O Śaunaka, greatest of saints and devotees, when Mahārāja Parīkshit inquired from Śukadeva Gosvāmī in this way, Śukadeva Gosvāmī, immediately remembering subject matters about Krishna within the core of his heart, externally lost contact with the actions of his senses. Thereafter, with great difficulty, he revived his external sensory perception and began to speak to Mahārāja Parīkshit about krishna-kathā.

Thus end the Bhaktivedanta purports of the Tenth Canto, Twelfth Chapter, of the Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam, entitled, "The Killing of the Demon Aghāsura."
