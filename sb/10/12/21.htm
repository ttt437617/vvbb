Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 12: The Killing of the Demon Aghāsura

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.12.21

pratispardhete srikkabhyām

savyāsavye nagodare

tuńga-śrińgālayo 'py etās

tad-damshtrābhiś ca paśyata

SYNONYMS

pratispardhete — just resembling; srikkabhyām — with the corners of the mouth; savya-asavye — left and right; naga-udare — caves of a mountain; tuńga-śrińga-ālayah — the high mountain peaks; api — although it is so; etāh tat-damshtrābhih — they resemble the teeth of the animal; ca — and; paśyata — just see.

TRANSLATION

On the left and right, the two depressions resembling mountain caves are the corners of its mouth, and the high mountain peaks are its teeth.
