Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 12: The Killing of the Demon Aghāsura

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.12.36

rājann ājagaram carma

śushkam vrindāvane 'dbhutam

vrajaukasām bahu-titham

babhūvākrīda-gahvaram

SYNONYMS

rājan — O Mahārāja Parīkshit; ājagaram carma — the dry body of Aghāsura, which remained only a big skin; śushkam — when it completely dried up; vrindāvane adbhutam — like a wonderful museum piece in Vrindāvana; vraja-okasām — for the inhabitants of Vrajabhūmi, Vrindāvana; bahu-titham — for many days, or for a long time; babhūva — became; ākrīda — sporting place; gahvaram — a cave.

TRANSLATION

O King Parīkshit, when the python-shaped body of Aghāsura dried up into merely a big skin, it became a wonderful place for the inhabitants of Vrindāvana to visit, and it remained so for a long, long time.
