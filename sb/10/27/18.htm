Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 27: Lord Indra and Mother Surabhi Offer Prayers

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.27.18

athāha surabhih krishnam

abhivandya manasvinī

sva-santānair upāmantrya

gopa-rūpinam īśvaram

SYNONYMS

atha — then; āha — spoke; surabhih — the mother of the cows, Surabhi; krishnam — to Krishna; abhivandya — offering respects; manasvinī — peaceful in mind; sva-santānaih — together with her progeny, the cows; upāmantrya — begging for His attention; gopa-rūpinam — appearing as a cowherd boy; īśvaram — the Supreme Lord.

TRANSLATION

Mother Surabhi, along with her progeny, the cows, then offered her obeisances to Lord Krishna. Respectfully requesting His attention, the gentle lady addressed the Supreme Personality of Godhead, who was present before her as a cowherd boy.

PURPORT

The statement here that the heavenly cow Surabhi approached Lord Krishna along with her progeny (sva-santānaih) is a reference to the transcendental cows who play with Lord Krishna in Vrindāvana. Although Lord Krishna's cows are transcendental, the heavenly cow Surabhi affectionately saw them, as indeed Lord Krishna Himself did, as related to her. Since Lord Krishna was appearing in the form of a cowherd boy, the whole situation was quite congenial, and Surabhi took the opportunity to offer the following prayers.
