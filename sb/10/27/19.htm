Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 27: Lord Indra and Mother Surabhi Offer Prayers

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.27.19

surabhir uvāca

krishna krishna mahā-yogin

viśvātman viśva-sambhava

bhavatā loka-nāthena

sa-nāthā vayam acyuta

SYNONYMS

surabhih uvāca — Surabhi said; krishna krishna — O Krishna, Krishna; mahā yogin — O greatest of mystics; viśva-ātman — O Soul of the universe; viśva-sambhava — O origin of the universe; bhavatā — by You; loka nāthena — the master of the world; sa-nāthāh — having a master; vayam — we; acyuta — O infallible one.

TRANSLATION

Mother Surabhi said: O Krishna, Krishna, greatest of mystics! O Soul and origin of the universe! You are the master of the world, and by Your grace, O infallible Lord, we have You as our master.

PURPORT

Śrīla Viśvanātha Cakravartī Thākura points out here that mother Surabhi is feeling great ecstasy as she repeats the words "Krishna, Krishna." Krishna lifted Govardhana Hill by His mystic power and thus protected the cows of Vrindāvana, whereas her so-called master, Indra, had tried to kill them. Thus Surabhi now clearly understands that it is not the demigods but rather the Supreme God, Krishna Himself, who is her real master forever.
