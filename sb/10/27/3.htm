Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 27: Lord Indra and Mother Surabhi Offer Prayers

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.27.3

drishta-śrutānubhāvo 'sya

krishnasyāmita-tejasah

nashta-tri-lokeśa-mada

idam āha kritāñjalih

SYNONYMS

drishta — seen; śruta — heard; anubhāvah — the power; asya — of this; krishnasya — Lord Krishna; amita — immeasurable; tejasah — whose potencies; nashta — destroyed; tri-loka — of the three worlds; īśa — of being the lord; madah — his intoxication; idam — these words; āha — spoke; krita-añjalih — joining his palms in supplication.

TRANSLATION

Indra had now heard of and seen the transcendental power of omnipotent Krishna, and his false pride in being the lord of the three worlds was thus defeated. Holding his hands together in supplication, he addressed the Lord as follows.
