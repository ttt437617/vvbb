Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 27: Lord Indra and Mother Surabhi Offer Prayers

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.27.25

tam tushtuvur deva-nikāya-ketavo

hy avākiramś cādbhuta-pushpa-vrishtibhih

lokāh parām nirvritim āpnuvams trayo

gāvas tadā gām anayan payo-drutām

SYNONYMS

tam — Him; tushtuvuh — praised; deva-nikāya — of all the demigods; ketavah — the most eminent; hi — indeed; avākiran — they covered Him; ca — and; adbhuta — amazing; pushpa — of flowers; vrishtibhih — with showers; lokāh — the worlds; parām — supreme; nirvritim — satisfaction; āpnuvan — experienced; trayah — three; gāvah — the cows; tadā — then; gām — the earth; anayan — brought; payah — with their milk; drutām — to saturation.

TRANSLATION

The most eminent demigods chanted the praises of the Lord and scattered wonderful showers of flowers all around Him. All three worlds felt supreme satisfaction, and the cows drenched the surface of the earth with their milk.

PURPORT

The word ketavah means, literally, "banners." The leading demigods are the emblems, or banners, of the demigod race, and they took the lead in glorifying the Lord and covering Him with an amazing shower of multicolored, fragrant flowers.
