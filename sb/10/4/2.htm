Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 4: The Atrocities of King Kamsa

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.4.2

te tu tūrnam upavrajya

devakyā garbha-janma tat

ācakhyur bhoja-rājāya

yad udvignah pratīkshate

SYNONYMS

te — all the watchmen; tu — indeed; tūrnam — very quickly; upavrajya — going before (the King); devakyāh — of Devakī; garbha-janma — the deliverance from the womb; tat — that (child); ācakhyuh — submitted; bhoja-rājāya — unto the King of the Bhojas, Kamsa; yat — of whom; udvignah — with great anxiety; pratīkshate — was waiting (for the child's birth).

TRANSLATION

Thereafter, all the watchmen very quickly approached King Kamsa, the ruler of the Bhoja dynasty, and submitted the news of the birth of Devakī's child. Kamsa, who had awaited this news very anxiously, immediately took action.

PURPORT

Kamsa was very anxiously waiting because of the prophecy that the eighth child of Devakī would kill him. This time, naturally, he was awake and waiting, and when the watchmen approached him, he immediately took action to kill the child.
