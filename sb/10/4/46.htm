Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 4: The Atrocities of King Kamsa

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.4.46

āyuh śriyam yaśo dharmam

lokān āśisha eva ca

hanti śreyāmsi sarvāni

pumso mahad-atikramah

SYNONYMS

āyuh — the duration of life; śriyam — beauty; yaśah — fame; dharmam — religion; lokān — elevation to higher planets; āśishah — blessings; eva — indeed; ca — also; hanti — destroys; śreyāmsi — benedictions; sarvāni — all; pumsah — of a person; mahat-atikramah — trespassing against great personalities.

TRANSLATION

My dear King, when a man persecutes great souls, all his benedictions of longevity, beauty, fame, religion, blessings and promotion to higher planets will be destroyed.

Thus end the Bhaktivedanta purports of the Tenth Canto, Fourth Chapter, of the Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam, entitled "The Atrocities of King Kamsa."
