Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 73: Lord Krishna Blesses the Liberated Kings

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.73.17

śrī-śuka uvāca

samstūyamāno bhagavān

rājabhir mukta-bandhanaih

tān āha karunas tāta

śaranyah ślakshnayā girā

SYNONYMS

śrī-śukah uvāca — Śukadeva Gosvāmī said; samstūyamānah — being nicely praised; bhagavān — the Supreme Lord; rājabhih — by the kings; mukta — freed; bandhanaih — from their bondage; tān — to them; āha — He spoke; karunah — merciful; tāta — my dear (King Parīkshit); śaranyah — the giver of shelter; ślakshnayā — with gentle; girā — words.

TRANSLATION

Śukadeva Gosvāmī said: Thus the kings, now freed from bondage, glorified the Supreme Lord. Then, my dear Parīkshit, that merciful bestower of shelter spoke to them in a gentle voice.
