Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 80: The Brāhmana Sudāmā Visits Lord Krishna in Dvārakā

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.80.42

tushto 'ham bho dvija-śreshthāh

satyāh santu manorathāh

chandāmsy ayāta-yāmāni

bhavantv iha paratra ca

SYNONYMS

tushtah — satisfied; aham — I am; bho — my dear ones; dvija — of brāhmanas; śreshthāh — O best; satyāh — fulfilled; santu — may they be; manah-rathāh — your desires; chandāmsi — Vedic mantras; ayāta-yāmāni — never growing old; bhavantu — may they be; iha — in this world; paratra — in the next world; ca — and.

TRANSLATION

You boys are first-class brāhmanas, and I am satisfied with you. May all your desires be fulfilled, and may the Vedic mantras you have learned never lose their meaning for you, in this world or the next.

PURPORT

Cooked food left sitting for three hours is called yāta-yāma, indicating that it has lost its taste, and similarly if a devotee does not remain fixed in Krishna consciousness, the transcendental knowledge that once inspired him on the spiritual path will lose its "taste," or meaning, for him. Thus Sāndīpani Muni blesses his disciples that the Vedic mantras, which reveal the Absolute Truth, will never lose their meaning for them but will remain ever fresh in their minds.
