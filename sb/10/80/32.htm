Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 80: The Brāhmana Sudāmā Visits Lord Krishna in Dvārakā

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.80.32

sa vai sat-karmanām sākshād

dvijāter iha sambhavah

ādyo 'ńga yatrāśraminām

yathāham jñāna-do guruh

SYNONYMS

sah — he; vai — indeed; sat — sanctified; karmanām — of duties; sākshāt — directly; dvi-jāteh — of one who has been twice born; iha — in this material life; sambhavah — birth; ādyah — first; ańga — My dear friend; yatra — through whom; āśraminām — for the members of all the spiritual orders of society; yathā — as; aham — Myself; jñāna — of divine knowledge; dah — the bestower; guruh — spiritual master.

TRANSLATION

My dear friend, he who gives a person his physical birth is his first spiritual master, and he who initiates him as a twice-born brāhmana and engages him in religious duties is indeed more directly his spiritual master. But the person who bestows transcendental knowledge upon the members of all the spiritual orders of society is one's ultimate spiritual master. Indeed, he is as good as My own self.
