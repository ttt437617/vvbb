Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 80: The Brāhmana Sudāmā Visits Lord Krishna in Dvārakā

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.80.2

ko nu śrutvāsakrid brahmann

uttamahśloka-sat-kathāh

virameta viśesha-jño

vishannah kāma-mārganaih

SYNONYMS

kah — who; nu — indeed; śrutvā — having heard; asakrit — repeatedly; brahman — O brāhmana; uttamah-śloka — of Lord Krishna; sat — transcendental; kathāh — topics; virameta — can desist; viśesha — the essence (of life); jñah — who knows; vishannah — disgusted; kāma — for material desire; mārganaih — with seeking.

TRANSLATION

O brāhmana, how could anyone who knows the essence of life and is disgusted with endeavoring for sense gratification give up the transcendental topics of Lord Uttamahśloka after hearing them repeatedly?

PURPORT

Śrīla Viśvanātha Cakravartī comments herein that we see many persons who, even after hearing the topics of the Lord repeatedly, give up their spiritual dedication. The ācārya replies that the word viśesha-jña is therefore significant here. Those who have actually understood the essence of life do not give up Krishna consciousness. A further qualification is to be vishannah kāma-mārganaih, disgusted with material sense gratification. These two qualities are complementary. One who has experienced the real taste of Krishna consciousness automatically becomes disgusted with the inferior taste of material pleasure. Such a genuine hearer of the topics of Krishna cannot give up hearing about the Lord's fascinating pastimes.
