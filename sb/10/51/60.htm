Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 51: The Deliverance of Mucukunda

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.51.60

yuñjānānām abhaktānām

prānāyāmādibhir manah

akshīna-vāsanam rājan

driśyate punar utthitam

SYNONYMS

yuñjānānām — who are engaging themselves; abhaktānām — of nondevotees; prānāyāma — with prānāyāma (yogic breath control); ādibhih — and other practices; manah — the minds; akshīna — not eliminated; vāsanam — the last traces of whose material desire; rājan — O King (Mucukunda); driśyate — is seen; punah — again; utthitam — waking (to thoughts of sense gratification).

TRANSLATION

The minds of nondevotees who engage in such practices as prānāyama are not fully cleansed of material desires. Thus, O King, material desires are again seen to arise in their minds.
