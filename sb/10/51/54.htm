Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 51: The Deliverance of Mucukunda

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.51.54

manye mamānugraha īśa te krito

rājyānubandhāpagamo yadricchayā

yah prārthyate sādhubhir eka-caryayā

vanam vivikshadbhir akhanda-bhūmi-paih

SYNONYMS

manye — I think; mama — to me; anugrahah — mercy; īśa — O Lord; te — by You; kritah — done; rājya — to kingdom; anubandha — of attachment; apagamah — the removal; yadricchayā — spontaneous; yah — which; prārthyate — is prayed for; sādhubhih — saintly; eka-caryayā — in solitude; vanam — the forest; vivikshadbhih — who desire to enter; akhanda — unlimited; bhūmi — of lands; paih — by rulers.

TRANSLATION

My Lord, I think You have shown me mercy, since my attachment to my kingdom has spontaneously ceased. Such freedom is prayed for by saintly rulers of vast empires who desire to enter the forest for a life of solitude.
