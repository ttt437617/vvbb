Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 51: The Deliverance of Mucukunda

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.51.41

kālanemir hatah kamsah

pralambādyāś ca sad-dvishah

ayam ca yavano dagdho

rājams te tigma-cakshushā

SYNONYMS

kālanemih — the demon Kālanemi; hatah — killed; kamsah — Kamsa; pralamba — Pralamba; ādyāh — and others; ca — also; sat — of those who are pious; dvishah — envious; ayam — this; ca — and; yavanah — barbarian; dagdhah — burned; rājan — O King; te — your; tigma — sharp; cakshushā — by the glance.

TRANSLATION

I have killed Kālanemi, reborn as Kamsa, as well as Pralamba and other enemies of the pious. And now, O King, this barbarian has been burnt to ashes by your piercing glance.
