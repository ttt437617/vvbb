Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 51: The Deliverance of Mucukunda

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.51.57

ciram iha vrijinārtas tapyamāno 'nutāpair

avitrisha-shad-amitro 'labdha-śāntih kathañcit

śarana-da samupetas tvat-padābjam parātman

abhayam ritam aśokam pāhi māpannam īśa

SYNONYMS

ciram — for a long time; iha — in this world; vrijina — by disturbances; ārtah — distressed; tapyamānah — tormented; anutāpaih — with remorse; avitrisha — unsatiated; shat — six; amitrah — whose enemies (the five senses and the mind); alabdha — not attaining; śāntih — peace; kathañcit — by some means; śarana — of shelter; da — O bestower; samupetah — who have approached; tvat — Your; pada-abjam — lotus feet; para-ātman — O Supreme Soul; abhayam — fearless; ritam — the truth; aśokam — free from sorrow; pāhi — please protect; mā — me; āpannam — who am confronted with dangers; īśa — O Lord.

TRANSLATION

For so long I have been pained by troubles in this world and have been burning with lamentation. My six enemies are never satiated, and I can find no peace. Therefore, O giver of shelter, O Supreme Soul, please protect me. O Lord, in the midst of danger I have by good fortune approached Your lotus feet, which are the truth and which thus make one fearless and free of sorrow.
