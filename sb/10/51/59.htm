Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 51: The Deliverance of Mucukunda

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.51.59

pralobhito varair yat tvam

apramādāya viddhi tat

na dhīr ekānta-bhaktānām

āśīrbhir bhidyate kvacit

SYNONYMS

pralobhitah — enticed; varaih — with benedictions; yat — which fact; tvam — you; apramādāya — for (showing your) freedom from bewilderment; viddhi — please know; tat — that; na — not; dhīh — the intelligence; ekānta — exclusive; bhaktānām — of devotees; āśīrbhih — by blessings; bhidyate — is diverted; kvacit — ever.

TRANSLATION

Understand that I enticed you with benedictions just to prove that you would not be deceived. The intelligence of My unalloyed devotees is never diverted by material blessings.
