Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 51: The Deliverance of Mucukunda

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.51.10

nanv asau dūram ānīya

śete mām iha sādhu-vat

iti matvācyutam mūdhas

tam padā samatādayat

SYNONYMS

nanu — is it so; asau — He; dūram — a long distance; ānīya — bringing; śete — is lying down; mām — me; iha — here; sādhu-vat — like a saintly person; iti — so; matvā — thinking (him); acyutam — (to be) Lord Krishna; mūdhah — deluded; tam — him; padā — with his foot; samatādayat — struck with full force.

TRANSLATION

"So, after leading me such a long distance, now He is lying here like some saint!" Thus thinking the sleeping man to be Lord Krishna, the deluded fool kicked him with all his strength.
