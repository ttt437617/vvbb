Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 13: The Stealing of the Boys and Calves by Brahmā

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.13.63

utthāyotthāya krishnasya

cirasya pādayoh patan

āste mahitvam prāg-drishtam

smritvā smritvā punah punah

SYNONYMS

utthāya utthāya — rising repeatedly; krishnasya — of Lord Krishna; cirasya — for a long time; pādayoh — at the lotus feet; patan — falling down; āste — remained; mahitvam — the greatness; prāk-drishtam — which he had previously seen; smritvā smritvā — remembering and remembering; punah punah — again and again.

TRANSLATION

Rising and falling again and again at the lotus feet of Lord Krishna for a long time, Lord Brahmā remembered over and over the Lord's greatness he had just seen.

PURPORT

As stated in one prayer,

śrutim apare smritim itare

bhāratam anye bhajantu bhava-bhītāh

aham iha nandam vande

yasyālīnde param brahma

"Let others study the Vedas, smriti and Mahābhārata, fearing material existence, but I shall worship Nanda Mahārāja, in whose courtyard is crawling the Supreme Brahman. Nanda Mahārāja is so great that the Parabrahman is crawling in his yard, and therefore I shall worship him." (Padyāvalī 126)

Brahmā was falling down in ecstasy. Because of the presence of the Supreme Personality of Godhead, who exactly resembled a human child, Brahmā was naturally astonished. Therefore with a faltering voice he offered prayers, understanding that here was the Supreme Person.
