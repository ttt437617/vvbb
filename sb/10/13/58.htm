Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 13: The Stealing of the Boys and Calves by Brahmā

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.13.58

tato 'rvāk pratilabdhākshah

kah paretavad utthitah

kricchrād unmīlya vai drishtīr

ācashtedam sahātmanā

SYNONYMS

tatah — then; arvāk — externally; pratilabdha-akshah — having revived his consciousness; kah — Lord Brahmā; pareta-vat — just like a dead man; utthitah — stood up; kricchrāt — with great difficulty; unmīlya — opening up; vai — indeed; drishtīh — his eyes; ācashta — he saw; idam — this universe; saha-ātmanā — along with himself.

TRANSLATION

Lord Brahmā's external consciousness then revived, and he stood up, just like a dead man coming back to life. Opening his eyes with great difficulty, he saw the universe, along with himself.

PURPORT

We actually do not die. At death, we are merely kept inert for some time, just as during sleep. At night we sleep, and all our activities stop, but as soon as we arise, our memory immediately returns, and we think, "Oh, where am I? What do I have to do?" This is called suptotthita-nyāya. Suppose we die. "Die" means that we become inert for some time and then again begin our activities. This takes place life after life, according to our karma, or activities, and svabhāva, or nature by association. Now, in the human life, if we prepare ourselves by beginning the activity of our spiritual life, we return to our real life and attain perfection. Otherwise, according to karma, svabhāva, prakriti and so on, our varieties of life and activity continue, and so also do our birth and death. As explained by Bhaktivinoda Thākura, māyāra vaśe, yāccha bhese', khāccha hābudubu bhāi: "My dear brothers, why are you being washed away by the waves of māyā?" One should come to the spiritual platform, and then one's activities will be permanent. Krita-punya-puñjāh: this stage is attained after one accumulates the results of pious activities for many, many lives. Janma-koti-sukritair na labhyate (Cc. Madhya 8.70). The Krishna consciousness movement wants to stop koti-janma, repeated birth and death. In one birth, one should rectify everything and come to permanent life. This is Krishna consciousness.
