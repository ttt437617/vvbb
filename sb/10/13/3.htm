Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 13: The Stealing of the Boys and Calves by Brahmā

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.13.3

śrinushvāvahito rājann

api guhyam vadāmi te

brūyuh snigdhasya śishyasya

guravo guhyam apy uta

SYNONYMS

śrinusva — please hear; avahitah — with great attention; rājan — O King (Mahārāja Parīkshit); api — although; guhyam — very confidential (because ordinary men cannot understand the activities of Krishna); vadāmi — I shall explain; te — unto you; brūyuh — explain; snigdhasya — submissive; śishyasya — of a disciple; guravah — spiritual masters; guhyam — very confidential; api uta — even so.

TRANSLATION

O King, kindly hear me with great attention. Although the activities of the Supreme Lord are very confidential, no ordinary man being able to understand them, I shall speak about them to you, for spiritual masters explain to a submissive disciple even subject matters that are very confidential and difficult to understand.
