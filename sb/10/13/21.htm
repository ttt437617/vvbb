Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 13: The Stealing of the Boys and Calves by Brahmā

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.13.21

tat-tad-vatsān prithań nītvā

tat-tad-goshthe niveśya sah

tat-tad-ātmābhavad rājams

tat-tat-sadma pravishtavān

SYNONYMS

tat-tat-vatsān — the calves, which belonged to different cows; prithak — separately; nītvā — bringing; tat-tat-goshthe — to their respective cow sheds; niveśya — entering; sah — Krishna; tat-tat-ātmā — as originally different individual souls; abhavat — He expanded Himself in that way; rājan — O King Parīkshit; tat-tat-sadma — their respective houses; pravishtavān — entered (Krishna thus entered everywhere).

TRANSLATION

O Mahārāja Parīkshit, Krishna, who had divided Himself as different calves and also as different cowherd boys, entered different cow sheds as the calves and then different homes as different boys.

PURPORT

Krishna had many, many friends, of whom Śrīdāmā, Sudāmā and Subala were prominent. Thus Krishna Himself became Śrīdāmā, Sudāmā and Subala and entered their respective houses with their respective calves.
