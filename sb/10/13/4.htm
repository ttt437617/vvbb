Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 13: The Stealing of the Boys and Calves by Brahmā

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.13.4

tathāgha-vadanān mrityo

rakshitvā vatsa-pālakān

sarit-pulinam ānīya

bhagavān idam abravīt

SYNONYMS

tathā — thereafter; agha-vadanāt — from the mouth of Aghāsura; mrityoh — death personified; rakshitvā — after saving; vatsa-pālakān — all the cowherd boys and calves; sarit-pulinam — to the bank of the river; ānīya — bringing them; bhagavān — the Supreme Personality of Godhead, Krishna; idam — these words; abravīt — spoke.

TRANSLATION

Then, after saving the boys and calves from the mouth of Aghāsura, who was death personified, Lord Krishna, the Supreme Personality of Godhead, brought them all to the bank of the river and spoke the following words.
