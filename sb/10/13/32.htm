Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 13: The Stealing of the Boys and Calves by Brahmā

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.13.32

gopās tad-rodhanāyāsa-

maughya-lajjoru-manyunā

durgādhva-kricchrato 'bhyetya

go-vatsair dadriśuh sutān

SYNONYMS

gopāh — the cowherd men; tat-rodhana-āyāsa — of their attempt to stop the cows from going to their calves; maughya — on account of the frustration; lajjā — being ashamed; uru-manyunā — and at the same time becoming very angry; durga-adhva-kricchratah — although they passed the very rough way with great difficulty; abhyetya — after reaching there; go-vatsaih — along with the calves; dadriśuh — saw; sutān — their respective sons.

TRANSLATION

The cowherd men, having been unable to check the cows from going to their calves, felt simultaneously ashamed and angry. They crossed the rough road with great difficulty, but when they came down and saw their own sons, they were overwhelmed by great affection.

PURPORT

Everyone was increasing in affection for Krishna. When the cowherd men coming down from the hill saw their own sons, who were no one else than Krishna, their affection increased.
