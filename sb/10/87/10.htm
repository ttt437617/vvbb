Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 87: The Prayers of the Personified Vedas

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.87.10

śvetadvīpam gatavati

tvayi drashtum tad-īśvaram

brahma-vādah su-samvrittah

śrutayo yatra śerate

tatra hāyam abhūt praśnas

tvam mām yam anupricchasi

SYNONYMS

śvetadvīpam — to Śvetadvīpa; gatavati — having gone; tvayi — you (Nārada); drashtum — to see; tat — its; īśvaram — Lord (Aniruddha); brahma — into the nature of the Supreme; vādah — a symposium; su — enthusiastically; samvrittah — ensued; śrutayah — the Vedas; yatra — in whom (Lord Aniruddha, also known as Kshīrodakaśāyī Vishnu); śerate — lay down to rest; tatra — about Him; ha — indeed; ayam — this; abhūt — arose; praśnah — question; tvam — you; mām — of Me; yam — which; anupricchasi — again are asking.

TRANSLATION

At that time you happened to be visiting the Lord on Śvetadvīpa — that Supreme Lord in whom the Vedas lie down to rest during the period of universal annihilation. A lively discussion arose among the sages on Janaloka as to the nature of the Supreme Absolute Truth. Indeed, the same question arose then that you are asking Me now.
