Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 87: The Prayers of the Personified Vedas

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.87.49

ity etad varnitam rājan

yan nah praśnah kritas tvayā

yathā brahmany anirdeśye

nīrigune 'pi manaś caret

SYNONYMS

iti — thus; etat — this; varnitam — related; rājan — O King (Parīkshit); yat — which; nah — to us; praśnah — question; kritah — made; tvayā — by you; yathā — how; brahmani — in the Absolute Truth; anirdeśye — which cannot be described in words; nirgune — which has no material qualities; api — even; manah — the mind; caret — moves.

TRANSLATION

Thus I have replied to the question You asked me, O King, concerning how the mind can have access to the Absolute Truth, which is indescribable by material words and devoid of material qualities.
