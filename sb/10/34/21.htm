Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 34: Nanda Mahārāja Saved and Śańkhacūda Slain

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.34.21

upagīyamānau lalitam

strī-janair baddha-sauhridaih

sv-alańkritānuliptāńgau

sragvinau virajo-'mbarau

SYNONYMS

upagīyamānau — Their glories being sung; lalitam — charmingly; strī-janaih — by the womenfolk; baddha — bound; sauhridaih — in affection for Them; su-alańkrita — finely decorated; anulipta — and anointed with (sandalwood pulp); ańgau — whose limbs; srak-vinau — wearing flower garlands; virajah — spotless; ambarau — whose garments.

TRANSLATION

Krishna and Balarāma wore flower garlands and spotless garments, and Their limbs were beautifully decorated and anointed. The women sang Their glories in a charming way, bound to Them by affection.
