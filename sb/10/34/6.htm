Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 34: Nanda Mahārāja Saved and Śańkhacūda Slain

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.34.6

sa cukrośāhinā grastah

krishna krishna mahān ayam

sarpo mām grasate tāta

prapannam parimocaya

SYNONYMS

sah — he, Nanda Mahārāja; cukrośa — shouted; ahinā — by the snake; grastah — seized; krishna krishna — O Krishna, Krishna; mahān — large; ayam — this; sarpah — serpent; mām — me; grasate — is swallowing; tāta — my dear boy; prapannam — who is surrendered; parimocaya — please deliver.

TRANSLATION

In the clutches of the snake, Nanda Mahārāja cried out, "Krishna, Krishna, my dear boy! This huge serpent is swallowing me! Please save me, who am surrendered to You!"
