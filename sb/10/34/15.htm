Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 34: Nanda Mahārāja Saved and Śańkhacūda Slain

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.34.15

tam tvāham bhava-bhītānām

prapannānām bhayāpaham

āpricche śāpa-nirmuktah

pāda-sparśād amīva-han

SYNONYMS

tam — that same person; tvā — You; aham — I; bhava — of material existence; bhītānām — for those who are afraid; prapannānām — who are surrendered; bhaya — of fear; apaham — the remover; āpricche — I request permission; śāpa — from the curse; nirmuktah — freed; pāda-sparśāt — by the touch of Your foot; amīva — of all distress; han — O destroyer.

TRANSLATION

My Lord, You destroy all fear for those who, fearing this material world, take shelter of You. By the touch of Your feet I am now freed from the curse of the sages. O destroyer of distress, please let me return to my planet.

PURPORT

According to the ācāryas, the word āpricche indicates that Sudarśana humbly requested the Lord for permission to return to his abode, where he might take up his duties again, certainly in a chastened state of mind.
