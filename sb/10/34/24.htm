Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 34: Nanda Mahārāja Saved and Śańkhacūda Slain

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.34.24

gopyas tad-gītam ākarnya

mūrcchitā nāvidan nripa

sramsad-dukūlam ātmānam

srasta-keśa-srajam tatah

SYNONYMS

gopyah — the gopīs; tat — of Them; gītam — the singing; ākarnya — hearing; mūrcchitāh — stunned; na avidan — were not aware of; nripa — my dear King; sramsat — slipping; dukūlam — the fine cloth of their garments; ātmānam — themselves; srasta — disheveled; keśa — their hair; srajam — the garlands; tatah — (slipping) from that.

TRANSLATION

The gopīs became stunned when they heard that song. Forgetting themselves, O King, they did not notice that their fine garments were becoming loose and their hair and garlands disheveled.
