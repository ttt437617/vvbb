Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 34: Nanda Mahārāja Saved and Śańkhacūda Slain

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.34.22

niśā-mukham mānayantāv

uditodupa-tārakam

mallikā-gandha-mattāli-

jushtam kumuda-vāyunā

SYNONYMS

niśā-mukham — the beginning of night; mānayantau — the two of Them honoring; udita — having risen; udupa — the moon; tārakam — and stars; mallikā — of the jasmine flowers; gandha — by the fragrance; matta — intoxicated; ali — by the bees; jushtam — liked; kumuda — from the lotuses; vāyunā — with the breeze.

TRANSLATION

The two Lords praised the nightfall, signaled by the rising of the moon and the appearance of stars, a lotus-scented breeze and bees intoxicated by the fragrance of jasmine flowers.
