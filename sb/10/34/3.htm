Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 34: Nanda Mahārāja Saved and Śańkhacūda Slain

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.34.3

gāvo hiranyam vāsāmsi

madhu madhv-annam ādritāh

brāhmanebhyo daduh sarve

devo nah prīyatām iti

SYNONYMS

gāvah — cows; hiranyam — gold; vāsāmsi — clothing; madhu — sweet-tasting; madhu — mixed with honey; annam — grains; ādritāh — respectfully; brāhmanebhyah — to the brāhmanas; daduh — they gave; sarve — all of them; devah — the lord; nah — with us; prīyatām — may be pleased; iti — thus praying.

TRANSLATION

The cowherd men gave the brāhmanas gifts of cows, gold, clothing and cooked grains mixed with honey. Then the cowherds prayed, "May the lord be pleased with us."
