Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 75: Duryodhana Humiliated

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.75.22

atha rājāhate kshaume

paridhāya sv-alańkritah

ritvik-sadasya-viprādīn

ānarcābharanāmbaraih

SYNONYMS

atha — next; rājā — the King; ahate — unused; kshaume — a pair of silken garments; paridhāya — putting on; su-alańkritah — nicely ornamented; ritvik — the priests; sadasya — the officiating members of the assembly; vipra — the brāhmanas; ādīn — and others; ānarca — he worshiped; ābharana — with ornaments; ambaraih — and clothing.

TRANSLATION

Next the King put on new silken garments and adorned himself with fine jewelry. He then honored the priests, assembly officials, learned brāhmanas and other guests by presenting them with ornaments and clothing.

PURPORT

Śrīla Prabhupāda writes, "The King not only dressed himself and decorated himself, but he also gave clothing and ornaments to all the priests and to the others who had participated in the yajñas. In this way he worshiped them all."
