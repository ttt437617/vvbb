Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 44: The Killing of Kamsa

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.44.20

bhagavad-gātra-nishpātair

vajra-nīshpesha-nishthuraih

cānūro bhajyamānāńgo

muhur glānim avāpa ha

SYNONYMS

bhagavat — of the Supreme Lord; gātra — by the limbs; nishpātaih — due to the blows; vajra — of lightning; nishpesha — like a crushing stroke; nishthuraih — hard; cānūrah — Cānūra; bhajyamāna — being broken; ańgah — his entire body; muhuh — more and more; glānim — pain and fatigue; avāpa ha — felt.

TRANSLATION

The harsh blows from the Supreme Lord's limbs fell like crushing lightning bolts upon Cānūra, breaking every part of his body and causing him more and more pain and fatigue.

PURPORT

Cānūra's elbows, arms, knees and other limbs were all weakening.
