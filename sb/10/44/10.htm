Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 44: The Killing of Kamsa

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.44.10

na sabhām praviśet prājñah

sabhya-doshān anusmaran

abruvan vibruvann ajño

narah kilbisham aśnute

SYNONYMS

na — not; sabhām — an assembly; praviśet — should enter; prājñah — the wise person; sabhya — of the assembly members; doshān — sinful discrepancies; anusmaran — keeping in mind; abruvan — not speaking; vibruvan — speaking wrongly; ajñah — ignorant (or pretending to be so); narah — a man; kilbisham — sin; aśnute — incurs.

TRANSLATION

A wise person should not enter an assembly if he knows the participants there are committing acts of impropriety. And if, having entered such an assembly, he fails to speak the truth, speaks falsely or pleads ignorance, he will certainly incur sin.
