Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 44: The Killing of Kamsa

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.44.12

kim na paśyata rāmasya

mukham ātāmra-locanam

mushtikam prati sāmarsham

hāsa-samrambha-śobhitam

SYNONYMS

kim — why; na paśyata — do you not see; rāmasya — of Lord Balarāma; mukham — the face; ātāmra — like copper; locanam — with eyes; mushtikam — Mushtika; prati — toward; sa-amarsham — with anger; hāsa — by His laughter; samrambha — and His absorption; śobhitam — beautified.

TRANSLATION

Don't you see the face of Lord Balarāma, with its eyes copper-red from His anger toward Mushtika and its beauty enhanced by His laughter and His absorption in the fight?
