Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 44: The Killing of Kamsa

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.44.21

sa śyena-vega utpatya

mushtī-kritya karāv ubhau

bhagavantam vāsudevam

kruddho vakshasy abādhata

SYNONYMS

sah — he, Cānūra; śyena — of a hawk; vegah — with the speed; utpatya — falling upon Him; mushtī — into fists; kritya — making; karau — his hands; ubhau — both; bhagavantam — the Supreme Lord; vāsudevam — Krishna; kruddhah — angry; vakshasi — upon His chest; abādhata — struck.

TRANSLATION

Furious, Cānūra attacked Lord Vāsudeva with the speed of a hawk and struck His chest with both fists.

PURPORT

It appears that Cānūra, realizing he was being defeated, became furious and made a final attempt to defeat Lord Krishna. The demon certainly had the spirit of a good fighter, but if he hoped for victory, he was certainly in the wrong place at the wrong time with the wrong person.
