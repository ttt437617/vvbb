Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 44: The Killing of Kamsa

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.44.2

hastābhyām hastayor baddhvā

padbhyām eva ca pādayoh

vicakarshatur anyonyam

prasahya vijigīshayā

SYNONYMS

hastābhyām — with their hands; hastayoh — by the hands; baddhvā — seizing; padbhyām — with their legs; eva ca — also; pādayoh — by the legs; vicakarshatuh — they (Krishna paired with Cānūra, and Balarāma with Mushtika) dragged; anyonyam — each other; prasahya — with force; vijigīshayā — with desire for victory.

TRANSLATION

Seizing each other's hands and locking legs with each other, the opponents struggled powerfully, eager for victory.
