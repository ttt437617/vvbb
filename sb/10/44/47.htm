Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 44: The Killing of Kamsa

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.44.47

anāgasām tvam bhūtānām

kritavān droham ulbanam

tenemām bho daśām nīto

bhūta-dhruk ko labheta śam

SYNONYMS

anāgasām — sinless; tvam — you; bhūtānām — against creatures; kritavān — have committed; droham — violence; ulbanam — terrible; tena — by that; imām — to this; bho — O dear one; daśām — condition; nītah — brought; bhūta — to living beings; dhruk — causing harm; kah — who; labheta — can achieve; śam — happiness.

TRANSLATION

O dear one, you have been brought to this state because of the terrible violence you committed against innocent creatures. How can one who harms others attain happiness?

PURPORT

Having expressed their sentimental grief, the ladies now speak practical wisdom. They are beginning to see things realistically because their minds were purified by the agony of the recent events and by the association of Lord Krishna.
