Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 44: The Killing of Kamsa

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.44.26

tatah kūtam anuprāptam

rāmah praharatām varah

avadhīl līlayā rājan

sāvajñam vāma-mushtinā

SYNONYMS

tatah — then; kūtam — the demonic wrestler Kūta; anuprāptam — appearing on the scene; rāmah — Lord Balarāma; praharatām — of fighters; varah — the best; avadhīt — killed; līlayā — playfully; rājan — O King, Parīkshit; sa-avajñam — neglectfully; vāma — left; mushtinā — with His fist.

TRANSLATION

Confronted next by the wrestler Kūta, Lord Balarāma, the best of fighters, playfully and nonchalantly killed him with His left fist, O King.
