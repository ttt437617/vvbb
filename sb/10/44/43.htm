Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 44: The Killing of Kamsa

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.44.43

teshām striyo mahā-rāja

suhrin-marana-duhkhitāh

tatrābhīyur vinighnantyah

śīrshāny aśru-vilocanāh

SYNONYMS

teshām — of them (Kamsa and his brothers); striyah — the wives; mahārāja — O King (Parīkshit); suhrit — of their well-wishers (their husbands); marana — because of the death; duhkhitāh — sorrowful; tatra — that place; abhīyuh — approached; vinighnantyah — beating; śīrshāni — their heads; aśru — with tears; vilocanāh — their eyes.

TRANSLATION

My dear King, the wives of Kamsa and his brothers, aggrieved by the death of their well-wishing husbands, came forward with tearful eyes, beating their heads.
