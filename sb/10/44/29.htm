Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 44: The Killing of Kamsa

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.44.29

gopān vayasyān ākrishya

taih samsrijya vijahratuh

vādyamāneshu tūryeshu

valgantau ruta-nūpurau

SYNONYMS

gopān — the cowherd boys; vayasyān — Their young friends; ākrishya — gathering together; taih — with them; samsrijya — joining up; vijahratuh — They sported; vādyamāneshu — while they played; tūryeshu — the musical instruments; valgantau — the two of Them dancing about; ruta — resounding; nūpurau — Their ankle bells.

TRANSLATION

Krishna and Balarāma then called Their young cowherd boyfriends to join Them, and in their company the Lords danced about and sported, Their ankle bells resounding as musical instruments played.

PURPORT

Nowadays we see that in championship boxing matches, as soon as there is a victory, all the friends and relatives of the victorious boxer rush into the ring to congratulate him, and often the champion will dance about in great happiness. Exactly in this mood, Krishna and Balarāma danced about, celebrating Their victory with Their friends and relatives.
