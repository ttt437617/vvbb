Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 44: The Killing of Kamsa

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.44.5

utthāpanair unnayanaiś

cālanaih sthāpanair api

parasparam jigīshantāv

apacakratur ātmanah

SYNONYMS

utthāpanaih — with lifting up; unnayanaih — carrying; cālanaih — pushing away; sthāpanaih — holding stationary; api — also; parasparam — each other; jigīshantau — wanting victory; apacakratuh — they harmed; ātmanah — (even) themselves.

TRANSLATION

Forcefully lifting and carrying each other, pushing each other away and holding each other down, the fighters hurt even their own bodies in their great eagerness for victory.

PURPORT

Śrīla Jīva Gosvāmī explains that although Krishna and Balarāma did not, of course, harm Themselves, it appeared that way to Cānūra, Mushtika and others of mundane vision. In other words, the Lords were fully absorbed in the pastime of being wrestlers.
