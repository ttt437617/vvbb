Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 21: The Gopīs Glorify the Song of Krishna's Flute

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.21.3

tad vraja-striya āśrutya

venu-gītam smarodayam

kāścit paroksham krishnasya

sva-sakhībhyo 'nvavarnayan

SYNONYMS

tat — that; vraja-striyah — the ladies in the cowherd village; āśrutya — hearing; venu-gītam — the song of the flute; smara-udayam — which gives rise to the influence of Cupid; kāścit — some of them; paroksham — privately; krishnasya — about Krishna; sva-sakhībhyah — to their intimate companions; anvavarnayan — described.

TRANSLATION

When the young ladies in the cowherd village of Vraja heard the song of Krishna's flute, which arouses the influence of Cupid, some of them privately began describing Krishna's qualities to their intimate friends.
