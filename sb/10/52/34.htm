Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 52: Rukminī's Message to Lord Krishna

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.52.34

kaccid vah kuśalam brahman

rājato yasya hi prajāh

sukham vasanti vishaye

pālyamānāh sa me priyah

SYNONYMS

kaccit — whether; vah — your; kuśalam — well-being; brahman — O brāhmana; rājatah — from the King; yasya — whose; hi — indeed; prajāh — subjects; sukham — happily; vasanti — reside; vishaye — in the state; pālyamānāh — being protected; sah — he; me — to Me; priyah — dear.

TRANSLATION

O brāhmana, is your King attending to your welfare? Indeed, that king in whose country the citizens are happy and protected is very dear to Me.
