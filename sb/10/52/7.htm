Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 52: Rukminī's Message to Lord Krishna

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.52.7

vilokya vega-rabhasam

ripu-sainyasya mādhavau

manushya-ceshtām āpannau

rājan dudruvatur drutam

SYNONYMS

vilokya — seeing; vega — of the waves; rabhasam — the fierceness; ripu — enemy; sainyasya — of the armies; mādhavau — the two Mādhavas (Krishna and Balarāma); manushya — humanlike; ceshtām — behavior; āpannau — assuming; rājan — O King (Parīkshit); dudruvatuh — ran away; drutam — quickly.

TRANSLATION

O King, seeing the fierce waves of the enemy's army, the two Mādhavas, imitating human behavior, ran swiftly away.
