Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 52: Rukminī's Message to Lord Krishna

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.52.31

santushto yarhi varteta

brāhmano yena kenacit

ahīyamānah svad dharmāt

sa hy asyākhila-kāma-dhuk

SYNONYMS

santushtah — satisfied; yarhi — when; varteta — carries on; brāhmanah — a brāhmana; yena kenacit — with whatever; ahīyamānah — not falling short; svāt — of his own; dharmāt — religious duty; sah — those religious principles; hi — indeed; asya — for him; akhila — of everything; kāma-dhuk — the mystic cow, milked for fulfillment of any desire.

TRANSLATION

When a brāhmana is satisfied with whatever comes his way and does not fall away from his religious duties, those very religious principles become his desire cow, fulfilling all his wishes.
