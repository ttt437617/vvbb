Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 52: Rukminī's Message to Lord Krishna

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.52.20

brahman krishna-kathāh punyā

mādhvīr loka-malāpahāh

ko nu tripyeta śrinvānah

śruta-jño nitya-nūtanāh

SYNONYMS

brahman — O brāhmana; krishna-kathāh — topics of Krishna; punyāh — pious; mādhvīh — sweet; loka — of the world; mala — the contamination; apahāh — which remove; kah — who; nu — at all; tripyeta — would become satiated; śrinvānah — hearing; śruta — what is heard; jñah — who can understand; nitya — always; nūtanāh — novel.

TRANSLATION

What experienced listener, O brāhmana, could ever grow satiated while listening to the pious, charming and ever-fresh topics of Lord Krishna, which cleanse away the world's contamination?
