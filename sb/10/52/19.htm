Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 52: Rukminī's Message to Lord Krishna

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.52.19

bhagavan śrotum icchāmi

krishnasyāmita-tejasah

yathā māgadha-śālvādīn

jitvā kanyām upāharat

SYNONYMS

bhagavan — O lord (Śukadeva Gosvāmī); śrotum — to hear; icchāmi — I wish; krishnasya — about Krishna; amita — immeasurable; tejasah — whose potency; yathā — how; māgadha-śālva-ādīn — such kings as Jarāsandha and Śālva; jitvā — defeating; kanyām — the bride; upāharat — He took away.

TRANSLATION

My lord, I wish to hear how the immeasurably powerful Lord Krishna took away His bride while defeating such kings as Māgadha and Sālva.

PURPORT

We should not think that Śrī Krishna was actually afraid of Jarāsandha. In the very next chapter we will find that Śrī Krishna easily defeats Jarāsandha and his soldiers. Thus we should never doubt the supreme prowess of Lord Krishna.
