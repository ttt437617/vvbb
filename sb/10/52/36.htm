Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 52: Rukminī's Message to Lord Krishna

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.52.36

evam samprishta-sampraśno

brāhmanah parameshthinā

līlā-grihīta-dehena

tasmai sarvam avarnayat

SYNONYMS

evam — thus; samprishta — asked; sampraśnah — questions; brāhmanah — the brāhmana; parameshthinā — by the Supreme Personality of Godhead; līla — as His pastime; grihīta — who assumes; dehena — His bodies; tasmai — to Him; sarvam — everything; avarnayat — he related.

TRANSLATION

Thus questioned by the Supreme Personality of Godhead, who incarnates to perform His pastimes, the brāhmana told Him everything.

PURPORT

The word grihīta may be translated as "grasped or caught," and thus, exactly as in English, may also mean "to perceive or understand something." Therefore Lord Krishna's transcendental body is perceived, understood, or in other words, grasped by the devotees when the Lord comes to exhibit His transcendental pastimes. These pastimes are not whimsical, but are a part of the complex program, structured and executed by the Lord Himself, for awakening the conditioned souls to their natural love and devotion for Him and bringing them back to Godhead.
