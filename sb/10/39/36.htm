Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 39: Akrūra's vision

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.39.36

yāvad ālakshyate ketur

yāvad renū rathasya ca

anuprasthāpitātmāno

lekhyānīvopalakshitāh

SYNONYMS

yāvat — as long as; ālakshyate — was visible; ketuh — the flag; yāvat — as long as; renuh — the dust; rathasya — of the chariot; ca — and; anuprasthāpita — sending after; ātmānah — their minds; lekhyāni — painted figures; iva — like; upalakshitāh — they appeared.

TRANSLATION

Sending their minds aher Krishna, the gopīs stood as motionless as figures in a painting. They remained there as long as the flag atop the chariot was visible, and even until they could no longer see the dust raised by the chariot wheels.
