Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 39: Akrūra's vision

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.39.23

sukham prabhātā rajanīyam āśishah

satyā babhūvuh pura-yoshitām dhruvam

yāh sampravishtasya mukham vrajas-pateh

pāsyanty apāńgotkalita-smitāsavam

SYNONYMS

sukham — happy; prabhātā — its dawn; rajanī — the night; iyam — this; āśishah — the hopes; satyāh — true; babhūvuh — have become; pura — of the city; yoshitām — of the women; dhruvam — certainly; yāh — who; sampravishtasya — of Him who has entered (Mathurā); mukham — the face; vrajah-pateh — of the master of Vraja; pāsyanti — they will drink; apāńga — upon the corners of His eyes; utkalita — expanded; smita — a smile; āsavam — nectar.

TRANSLATION

The dawn following this night will certainly be auspicious for the women of Mathurā. All their hopes will now be fulfilled, for as the Lord of Vraja enters their city, they will be able to drink from His face the nectar of the smile emanating from the corners of His eyes.
