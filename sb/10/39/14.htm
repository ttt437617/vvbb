Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 39: Akrūra's vision

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.39.14

kāścit tat-krita-hrit-tāpa

śvāsa-mlāna-mukha-śriyah

sramsad-dukūla-valaya

keśa-granthyaś ca kāścana

SYNONYMS

kāścit — some of them; tat — by that (hearing); krita — created; hrit — in their hearts; tāpa — from the torment; śvāsa — by the sighing; mlāna — made pale; mukha — of their faces; śriyah — the luster; sramsat — loosening; dukūla — their dresses; valaya — bracelets; keśa — in their hair; granthyah — the knots; ca — and; kāścana — others.

TRANSLATION

Some gopīs felt so pained at heart that their faces turned pale from their heavy breathing. Others were so anguished that their dresses, bracelets and braids became loose.
