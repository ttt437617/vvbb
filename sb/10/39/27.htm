Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 39: Akrūra's vision

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.39.27

anārdra-dhīr esha samāsthito ratham

tam anv amī ca tvarayanti durmadāh

gopā anobhih sthavirair upekshitam

daivam ca no 'dya pratikūlam īhate

SYNONYMS

anārdra-dhīh — hard-hearted; eshah — this (Krishna); samāsthitah — having mounted; ratham — the chariot; tam — Him; anu — following; amī — these; ca — and; tvarayanti — hurry; durmadāh — befooled; gopāh — cowherds; anobhih — in their bullock carts; sthaviraih — by the elders; upekshitam — disregarded; daivam — fate; ca — and; nah — with us; adya — today; pratikūlam — unfavorably; īhate — is acting.

TRANSLATION

Hard-hearted Krishna has already mounted the chariot, and now the foolish cowherds are hurrying after Him in their bullock carts. Even the elders are saying nothing to stop Him. Today fate is working against us.

PURPORT

Śrīla Śrīdhara Svāmī reveals what the gopīs thought: "These foolish cowherd men and elders are not even trying to stop Krishna. Don't they realize they are committing suicide? They are helping Krishna go to Mathurā, but they will have to come back to Vrindāvana and will certainly die in His absence. The whole world has become nonsensical."
