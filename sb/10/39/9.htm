Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 39: Akrūra's vision

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.39.9

yat-sandeśo yad-artham vā

dūtah sampreshitah svayam

yad uktam nāradenāsya

sva-janmānakadundubheh

SYNONYMS

yat — having which; sandeśah — message; yat — which; artham — purpose; vā — and; dūtah — as a messenger; sampreshitah — sent; svayam — himself (Akrūra); yat — what; uktam — was spoken; nāradena — by Nārada; asya — to him (Kamsa); sva — His (Krishna's); janma — birth; ānakadundubheh — from Vasudeva.

TRANSLATION

Akrūra relayed the message he had been sent to deliver. He also described Kamsa's real intentions and how Nārada had informed Kamsa that Krishna had been born as the son of Vasudeva.
