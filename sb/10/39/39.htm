Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 39: Akrūra's vision

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.39.39

tatropaspriśya pānīyam

pītvā mrishtam mani-prabham

vriksha-shandam upavrajya

sa-rāmo ratham āviśat

SYNONYMS

tatra — there; upaspriśya — touching the water; pānīyam — in His hand; pītvā — drinking; mrishtam — sweet; mani — like jewels; prabham — effulgent; vriksha — of trees; shandam — a grove; upavrajya — moving up to; sa-rāmah — with Balarāma; ratham — the chariot; āviśat — He mounted.

TRANSLATION

The river's sweet water was more effulgent than brilliant jewels. After Lord Krishna had touched it for purification, He drank some from His hand. Then He had the chariot moved near a grove of trees and climbed back on, along with Balarāma.
