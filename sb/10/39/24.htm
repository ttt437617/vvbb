Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 39: Akrūra's vision

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.39.24

tāsām mukundo madhu-mañju-bhāshitair

grihīta-cittah para-vān manasvy api

katham punar nah pratiyāsyate 'balā

grāmyāh salajja-smita-vibhramair bhraman

SYNONYMS

tāsām — their; mukundah — Krishna; madhu — like honey; mañju — sweet; bhāshitaih — by the words; grihīta — seized; cittah — whose mind; paravān — subservient; manasvī — intelligent; api — although; katham — how; punah — again; nah — to us; pratiyāsyate — will He return; abalāh — O girls; grāmyāh — who are rustic; sa-lajja — shyly; smita — smiling; vibhramaih — by their enchantments; bhraman — becoming bewildered.

TRANSLATION

O gopīs, although our Mukunda is intelligent and very obedient to His parents, once He has fallen under the spell of the honey-sweet words of the women of Mathurā and been enchanted by their alluring, shy smiles, how will He ever return to us unsophisticated village girls?
