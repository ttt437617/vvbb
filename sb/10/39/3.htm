Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 39: Akrūra's vision

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.39.3

sāyantanāśanam kritvā

bhagavān devakī-sutah

suhritsu vrittam kamsasya

papracchānyac cikīrshitam

SYNONYMS

sāyantana — of the evening; aśanam — the dining; kritvā — having done; bhagavān — the Supreme Lord; devakī-sutah — the son of Devakī; suhritsu — toward His well-wishing relatives and friends; vrittam — about the behavior; kamsasya — of Kamsa; papraccha — He inquired; anyat — other; cikīrshitam — intentions.

TRANSLATION

After the evening meal, Lord Krishna, the son of Devakī, asked Akrūra how Kamsa was treating their dear relatives and friends and what the King was planning to do.
