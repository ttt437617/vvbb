Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 39: Akrūra's vision

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.39.33

gopās tam anvasajjanta

nandādyāh śakatais tatah

ādāyopāyanam bhūri

kumbhān go-rasa-sambhritān

SYNONYMS

gopāh — the cowherd men; tam — Him; anvasajjanta — followed; nanda-ādyāh — headed by Nanda; śakataih — in their wagons; tatah — then; ādāya — having taken; upāyanam — offerings; bhūri — abundant; kumbhān — clay pots; go-rasa — with milk products; sambhritān — filled.

TRANSLATION

Led by Nanda Mahārāja, the cowherd men followed behind Lord Krishna in their wagons. The men brought along many offerings for the King, including clay pots filled with ghee and other milk products.
