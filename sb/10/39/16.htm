Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 39: Akrūra's vision

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.39.16

smarantyaś cāparāh śaurer

anurāga-smiteritāh

hridi-spriśaś citra-padā

girah sammumuhuh striyah

SYNONYMS

smarantyah — remembering; ca — and; aparāh — others; śaureh — of Krishna; anurāga — affectionate; smita — by His smile; īritāh — sent; hridi — the heart; spriśah — touching; citra — wonderful; padāh — with phrases; girah — the speech; sammumuhuh — fainted; striyah — women.

TRANSLATION

And still other young women fainted simply by remembering the words of Lord Śauri [Krishna]. These words, decorated with wonderful phrases and expressed with affectionate smiles, would deeply touch the young girls' hearts.
