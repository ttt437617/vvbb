Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 39: Akrūra's vision

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.39.21

krūras tvam akrūra-samākhyayā sma naś

cakshur hi dattam harase batājña-vat

yenaika-deśe 'khila-sarga-saushthavam

tvadīyam adrākshma vayam madhu-dvishah

SYNONYMS

krūrah — cruel; tvam — you (are); akrūra-samākhyayā — by the name Akrūra (which means "not cruel"); sma — certainly; nah — our; cakshuh — eyes; hi — indeed; dattam — given; harase — you are taking; bata — alas; ajña — a fool; vat — just like; yena — with which (eyes); eka — in one; deśe — place; akhila — of all; sarga — the creation; saushthavam — the perfection; tvadīyam — your; adrākshma — have seen; vayam — we; madhudvishah — of Lord Krishna, enemy of the demon Madhu.

TRANSLATION

O Providence, though you come here with the name Akrūra, you are indeed cruel, for like a fool you are taking away what you once gave us — those eyes with which we have seen, even in one feature of Lord Madhudvisha's form, the perfection of your entire creation.

PURPORT

The gopīs did not care to see anything but Krishna; therefore if Krishna left Vrindāvana, their eyes would have no function. Thus Krishna's departure was blinding these poor girls, and in their distress they berated Akrūra, whose name means "not cruel," as cruel indeed.
