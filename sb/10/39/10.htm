Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 39: Akrūra's vision

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.39.10

śrutvākrūra-vacah krishno

balaś ca para-vīra-hā

prahasya nandam pitaram

rājñā dishtam vijajñatuh

SYNONYMS

śrutvā — hearing; akrūra-vacah — Akrūra's words; krishnah — Lord Krishna; balah — Lord Balarāma; ca — and; para-vīra — of opposing heroes; hā — the destroyer; prahasya — laughing; nandam — to Nanda Mahārāja; pitaram — Their father; rājñā — by the King; dishtam — the order given; vijajñatuh — They informed.

TRANSLATION

Lord Krishna and Lord Balarāma, the vanquisher of heroic opponents, laughed when They heard Akrūra's words. The Lords then informed Their father, Nanda Mahārāja, of King Kamsa's orders.
