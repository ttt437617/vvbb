Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 6: The Killing of the Demon Pūtanā

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.6.5-6

tām keśa-bandha-vyatishakta-mallikām

brihan-nitamba-stana-kricchra-madhyamām

suvāsasam kalpita-karna-bhūshana-

tvishollasat-kuntala-manditānanām

valgu-smitāpāńga-visarga-vīkshitair

mano harantīm vanitām vrajaukasām

amamsatāmbhoja-karena rūpinīm

gopyah śriyam drashtum ivāgatām patim

SYNONYMS

tām — her; keśa-bandha-vyatishakta-mallikām — whose arrangement of hair was decorated with a garland of mallikā flowers; brihat — very, very big; nitamba-stana — by her hips and firm breasts; kricchra-madhyamām — whose slim waist was overburdened; su-vāsasam — nicely painted or very attractively dressed; kalpita-karna-bhūshana — of the earrings arranged on her ears; tvishā — by the brilliance; ullasat — very attractive; kuntala-mandita-ānanām — whose beautiful face was surrounded by black hair; valgu-smita-apāńga-visarga-vīkshitaih — by her casting her smiling glance on everyone very attractively; manah harantīm — everyone's attention was attracted (by her); vanitām — an especially attractive woman; vraja-okasām — of the inhabitants of Gokula; amamsata — thought; ambhoja — holding a lotus flower; karena — with her hand; rūpinīm — very beautiful; gopyah — the gopī inhabitants of Gokula; śriyam — the goddess of fortune; drashtum — to see; iva — as if; āgatām — had come; patim — her husband.

TRANSLATION

Her hips were full, her breasts were large and firm, seeming to overburden her slim waist, and she was dressed very nicely. Her hair, adorned with a garland of mallikā flowers, was scattered about her beautiful face. Her earrings were brilliant, and as she smiled very attractively, glancing upon everyone, her beauty drew the attention of all the inhabitants of Vraja, especially the men. When the gopīs saw her, they thought that the beautiful goddess of fortune, holding a lotus flower in her hand, had come to see her husband, Krishna.
