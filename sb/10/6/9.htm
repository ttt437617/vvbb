Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 6: The Killing of the Demon Pūtanā

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.6.9

tām tīkshna-cittām ativāma-ceshtitām

vīkshyāntarā kosha-paricchadāsivat

vara-striyam tat-prabhayā ca dharshite

nirīkshyamāne jananī hy atishthatām

SYNONYMS

tām — that (Pūtanā Rākshasī); tīkshna-cittām — having a very fierce heart for killing the child; ati-vāma-ceshtitām — although she was trying to treat the child better than a mother; vīkshya antarā — seeing her within the room; kosha-paricchada-asi-vat — like a sharp sword within a soft sheath; vara-striyam — the very beautiful woman; tat-prabhayā — by her influence; ca — also; dharshite — being overwhelmed; nirīkshyamāne — were seeing; jananī — the two mothers; hi — indeed; atishthatām — they remained silent, without prohibiting.

TRANSLATION

Pūtanā Rākshasī's heart was fierce and cruel, but she looked like a very affectionate mother. Thus she resembled a sharp sword in a soft sheath. Although seeing her within the room, Yaśodā and Rohinī, overwhelmed by her beauty, did not stop her, but remained silent because she treated the child like a mother.

PURPORT

Although Pūtanā was an outsider and although she personified fierce death because the determination within her heart was to kill the child, when she directly came and placed the child on her lap to offer the child her breast to suck, the mothers were so captivated by her beauty that they did not prohibit her. Sometimes a beautiful woman is dangerous because everyone, being captivated by external beauty (māyā-mohita), is unable to understand what is in her mind. Those who are captivated by the beauty of the external energy are called māyā-mohita. Mohitam nābhijānāti mām ebhyah param avyayam (Bg. 7.13). Na te viduh svārtha-gatim hi vishnum durāśayā ye bahir-artha-māninah (Bhāg. 7.5.31). Here, of course, the two mothers Rohinī and Yaśodā were not māyā-mohita, deluded by the external energy, but to develop the pastimes of the Lord, they were captivated by yogamāyā. Such māyā-moha is the action of yogamāyā.
