Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 6: The Killing of the Demon Pūtanā

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.6.2

kamsena prahitā ghorā

pūtanā bāla-ghātinī

śiśūmś cacāra nighnantī

pura-grāma-vrajādishu

SYNONYMS

kamsena — by King Kamsa; prahitā — engaged previously; ghorā — very fierce; pūtanā — by the name Pūtanā; bāla-ghātinī — a Rākshasī who killed; śiśūn — small babies; cacāra — wandered; nighnantī — killing; pura-grāma-vraja-ādishu — in towns, cities and villages here and there.

TRANSLATION

While Nanda Mahārāja was returning to Gokula, the same fierce Pūtanā whom Kamsa had previously engaged to kill babies was wandering about in the towns, cities and villages, doing her nefarious duty.
