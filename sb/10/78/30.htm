Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 78: The Killing of Dantavakra, Vidūratha and Romaharshana

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.78.30

asya brahmāsanam dattam

asmābhir yadu-nandana

āyuś cātmāklamam tāvad

yāvat satram samāpyate

SYNONYMS

asya — his; brahma-āsanam — the spiritual master's seat; dattam — given; asmābhih — by us; yadu-nandana — O darling of the Yadus; āyuh — long life; ca — and; ātma — bodily; aklamam — freedom from trouble; tāvat — for that long; yāvat — until; satram — the sacrifice; samāpyate — is completed.

TRANSLATION

"O favorite of the Yadus, we gave him the seat of the spiritual master and promised him long life and freedom from physical pain for as long as this sacrifice continues.

PURPORT

Although Romaharshana was not a brāhmana, having been born of a mixed marriage, he was invested with that status by the assembled sages and thus given the brahmāsana, the seat of the chief officiating priest.
