Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 78: The Killing of Dantavakra, Vidūratha and Romaharshana

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.78.10

tatah sūkshmataram jyotih

krishnam āviśad adbhutam

paśyatām sarva-bhūtānām

yathā caidya-vadhe nripa

SYNONYMS

tatah — then; sūkshma-taram — very subtle; jyotih — a light; krishnam — Lord Krishna; āviśat — entered; adbhutam — amazing; paśyatām — as they watched; sarva — all; bhūtānām — living beings; yathā — just as; caidya-vadhe — when Śiśupāla was killed; nripa — 0 King (Parīkshit).

TRANSLATION

A most subtle and wondrous spark of light then [rose from the demon's body and] entered Lord Krishna while everyone looked on, O King, just as when Śiśupāla was killed.
