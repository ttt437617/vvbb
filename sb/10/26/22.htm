Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 26: Wonderful Krishna

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.26.22

tasmān nanda kumāro 'yam

nārāyana-samo gunaih

śriyā kīrtyānubhāvena

tat-karmasu na vismayah

SYNONYMS

tasmāt — therefore; nanda — O Nanda Mahārāja; kumārah — child; ayam — this; nārāyana-samah — is as good as Nārāyana; gunaih — by His qualities; śriyā — by His opulence; kīrtyā — especially by His name and fame; anubhāvena — and by His influence; tat — His; karmasu — concerning the activities; na — there is no; vismayah — surprise.

TRANSLATION

Therefore, O Nanda Mahārāja, this child of yours is as good as Nārāyana. In His transcendental qualities, opulence, name, fame and influence, He is exactly like Nārāyana. Thus you should not be astonished hy His activities.

PURPORT

Nanda here reports to the cowherd men the concluding remarks of Garga Muni, who spoke at the secret birth ceremony of Lord Krishna.
