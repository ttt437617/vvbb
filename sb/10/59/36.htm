Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 59: The Killing of the Demon Naraka

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.59.36

tāh prāhinod dvāravatīm

su-mrishta-virajo-'mbarāh

nara-yānair mahā-kośān

rathāśvān dravinam mahāt

SYNONYMS

tāh — them; prāhinot — He sent; dvāravatīm — to Dvārakā; su-mrishta — well cleaned; virajah — spotless; ambarāh — with clothes; nara-yānaih — by human conveyances (palanquins); mahā — great; kośān — treasures; ratha — chariots; aśvān — and horses; dravinam — wealth; mahat — extensive.

TRANSLATION

The Lord had the princesses arrayed in clean, spotless garments and then sent them in palanquins to Dvārakā, together with great treasures of chariots, horses and other valuables.
