Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 59: The Killing of the Demon Naraka

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.59.37

airāvata-kulebhāmś ca

catur-dantāms tarasvinah

pāndurāmś ca catuh-shashtim

prerayām āsa keśavah

SYNONYMS

airāvata — of Airāvata, Lord Indra's carrier; kula — from the family; ibhān — elephants; ca — also; catuh — four; dantān — having tusks; tarasvinah — swift; pāndurān — white; ca — and; catuh-shashtim — sixty-four; prerayām āsa — dispatched; keśavah — Lord Krishna.

TRANSLATION

Lord Krishna also dispatched sixty-four swift white elephants, descendants of Airāvata, who each sported four tusks.
