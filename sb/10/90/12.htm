Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 90: Summary of Lord Krishna's Glories

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.90.12

natānām nartakīnām ca

gīta-vādyopajīvinām

krīdālańkāra-vāsāmsi

krishno 'dāt tasya ca striyah

SYNONYMS

natānām — to the male performers; nartakīnām — the female performers; ca — and; gīta — by singing; vādya — and playing musical instruments; upajīvinām — who earned their livelihoods; krīdā — from His sports; alańkāra — the ornaments; vāsāmsi — and garments; krishnah — Lord Krishna; adāt — gave; tasya — His; ca — and; striyah — wives.

TRANSLATION

Afterward, Lord Krishna and His wives would give the ornaments and clothing they had worn during their water sports to the male and female performers, who earned their livelihood from singing and from playing instrumental music.
