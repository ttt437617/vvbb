Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 90: Summary of Lord Krishna's Glories

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.90.13

krishnasyaivam viharato

gaty-ālāpekshita-smitaih

narma-kshveli-parishvańgaih

strīnām kila hritā dhiyah

SYNONYMS

krishnasya — of Lord Krishna; evam — thus; viharatah — who was sporting; gati — by the movements; ālāpa — conversing; īkshita — glancing; smitaih — and smiling; narma — by the jokes; kshveli — playful exchanges; parishvańgaih — and embraces; strīnām — of the wives; kila — indeed; hritāh — stolen; dhiyah — the hearts.

TRANSLATION

In this way Lord Krishna would sport with His queens, totally captivating their hearts with His gestures, talks, glances and smiles, and also with His jokes, playful exchanges and embraces.
