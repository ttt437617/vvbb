Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 7: The Killing of the Demon Trināvarta

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.7.3

athānyad api krishnasya

tokācaritam adbhutam

mānusham lokam āsādya

taj-jātim anurundhatah

SYNONYMS

atha — also; anyat api — other pastimes also; krishnasya — of child Krishna; toka-ācaritam adbhutam — they are also wonderful childhood pastimes; mānusham — as if playing as a human child; lokam āsādya — appearing on this planet earth in human society; tat-jātim — exactly like a human child; anurundhatah — who was imitating.

TRANSLATION

Please describe other pastimes of Krishna, the Supreme Personality, who appeared on this planet earth, imitating a human child and performing wonderful activities like killing Pūtanā.

PURPORT

Mahārāja Parīkshit requested Śukadeva Gosvāmī to narrate other childhood pastimes exhibited by Krishna while playing as a human child. The Supreme Personality of Godhead incarnates at different times in different planets and universes, and according to the nature of those places, He exhibits His unlimited potency. That a child sitting on the lap of his mother was able to kill the gigantic Pūtanā is extremely wonderful for the inhabitants of this planet, but on other planets the inhabitants are more advanced, and therefore the pastimes the Lord performs there are still more wonderful. Krishna's appearance on this planet like a human being makes us more fortunate than the demigods in the higher planets, and therefore Mahārāja Parīkshit was very much interested in hearing about Him.
