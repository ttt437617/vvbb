Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 7: The Killing of the Demon Trināvarta

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.7.24

iti khara-pavana-cakra-pāmśu-varshe

suta-padavīm abalāvilakshya mātā

atikarunam anusmaranty aśocad

bhuvi patitā mrita-vatsakā yathā gauh

SYNONYMS

iti — thus; khara — very strong; pavana-cakra — by a whirlwind; pāmśu-varshe — when there were showers of dust and small dust particles; suta-padavīm — the place of her son; abalā — the innocent woman; avilakshya — not seeing; mātā — because of being His mother; ati-karunam — very pitifully; anusmarantī — she was thinking of her son; aśocat — lamented extraordinarily; bhuvi — on the ground; patitā — fell down; mrita-vatsakā — who has lost her calf; yathā — like; gauh — a cow.

TRANSLATION

Because of the dust storm stirred up by the strong whirlwind, mother Yaśodā could find no trace of her son, nor could she understand why. Thus she fell down on the ground like a cow who has lost her calf and began to lament very pitifully.
