Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 7: The Killing of the Demon Trināvarta

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.7.21

gokulam sarvam āvrinvan

mushnamś cakshūmshi renubhih

īrayan sumahā-ghora-

śabdena pradiśo diśah

SYNONYMS

gokulam — the whole tract of land known as Gokula; sarvam — everywhere; āvrinvan — covering; mushnan — taking away; cakshūmshi — the power of vision; renubhih — by particles of dust; īrayan — was vibrating; su-mahā-ghora — very fierce and heavy; śabdena — with a sound; pradiśah diśah — entered everywhere, in all directions.

TRANSLATION

Covering the whole land of Gokula with particles of dust, that demon, acting as a strong whirlwind, covered everyone's vision and began vibrating everywhere with a greatly fearful sound.

PURPORT

Trināvartāsura assumed the form of a whirlwind and covered with a dust storm the whole tract of land known as Gokula, so that no one could see even the nearest thing.
