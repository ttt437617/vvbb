Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 84: The Sages' Teachings at Kurukshetra

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.84.31

sannikarsho 'tra martyānām

anādarana-kāranam

gāńgam hitvā yathānyāmbhas

tatratyo yāti śuddhaye

SYNONYMS

sannikarshah — proximity; atra — here (in this world); martyānām — for mortals; anādarana — of disregard; kāranam — a cause; gāńgam — (the water) of the Ganges; hitvā — leaving; yathā — as; anya — other; ambhah — to water; tatratyah — one who lives near it; yāti — goes; śuddhaye — for purification.

TRANSLATION

In this world familiarity breeds contempt. For example, one who lives on the banks of the Ganges might travel to some other body of water to be purified.
