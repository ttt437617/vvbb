Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 84: The Sages' Teachings at Kurukshetra

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.84.40

tvam tv adya mukto dvābhyām vai

rishi-pitror mahā-mate

yajñair devarnam unmucya

nirrino 'śarano bhava

SYNONYMS

tvam — you; tu — but; adya — now; muktah — freed; dvābhyām — from two (of the debts); vai — certainly; rishi — to the sages; pitroh — and to the forefathers; mahā-mate — O generous one; yajñaih — by Vedic sacrifices; deva — to the demigods; rinam — from the debt; unmucya — relieving yourself; nirrinah — without debt; aśaranah — without material shelter; bhava — become.

TRANSLATION

But you, O magnanimous soul, are already free from two of your debts — those to the sages and the forefathers. Now absolve yourself of your debt to the demigods by executing Vedic sacrifices, and in this way free yourself completely of debt and renounce all material shelter.
