Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 84: The Sages' Teachings at Kurukshetra

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.84.27

śrī-śuka uvāca

ity anujñāpya dāśārham

dhritarāshtram yudhishthiram

rājarshe svāśramān gantum

munayo dadhire manah

SYNONYMS

śrī-śukah uvāca — Śukadeva Gosvāmī said; iti — thus speaking; anujñāpya — taking permission to leave; dāśārham — of Lord Krishna, the descendant of Mahārāja Daśārha; dhritarāshtram — of Dhritarāshtra; yudhishthiram — of Yudhishthira; rāja — among kings; rishe — O sage; sva — their own; āśramān — to the hermitages; gantum — to going; munayah — the sages; dadhire — turned; manah — their minds.

TRANSLATION

Śukadeva Gosvāmī said: Having thus spoken, O wise king, the sages then took leave of Lord Dāśārha, Dhritarāshtra and Yudhishthira and prepared to depart for their āśramas.
