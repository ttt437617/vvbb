Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 84: The Sages' Teachings at Kurukshetra

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.84.59

nandas tu saha gopālair

brihatyā pūjayārcitah

krishna-rāmograsenādyair

nyavātsīd bandhu-vatsalah

SYNONYMS

nandah — Nanda Mahārāja; tu — and; saha — together with; gopālaih — the cowherds; brihatyā — especially opulent; pūjayā — with worship; arcitah — honored; krishna-rāma-ugrasena-ādyaih — by Krishna, Balarāma, Ugrasena and the others; nyavātsīt — stayed; bandhu — to his relatives; vatsalah — affectionate.

TRANSLATION

Nanda Mahārāja showed his affection for his relatives, the Yadus, by remaining with them a little longer, together with his cowherds. During his stay, Krishna, Balarāma, Ugrasena and the others honored him with especially opulent worship.
