Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 84: The Sages' Teachings at Kurukshetra

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.84.49

tasyartvijo mahā-rāja

ratna-kauśeya-vāsasah

sa-sadasyā virejus te

yathā vritra-hano 'dhvare

SYNONYMS

tasya — his; ritvijah — priests; mahā-rāja — O great king (Parīkshit); ratna — with jewels; kauśeya — silk; vāsasah — and garments; sa — along with; sadasyāh — the officiating members of the assembly; virejuh — appeared effulgent; te — they; yathā — as if; vritra-hanah — of Lord Indra, the killer of Vritra; adhvare — in the sacrifice.

TRANSLATION

My dear Mahārāja Parīkshit, Vasudeva's priests and the officiating members of the assembly, dressed in silk dhotīs and jeweled ornaments, looked so effulgent that they seemed to be standing in the sacrificial arena of Indra, the killer of Vritra.
