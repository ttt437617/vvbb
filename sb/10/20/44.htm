Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 20: The Rainy Season and Autumn in Vrindāvana

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.20.44

akhanda-mandalo vyomni

rarājodu-ganaih śaśī

yathā yadu-patih krishno

vrishni-cakrāvrito bhuvi

SYNONYMS

akhanda — unbroken; mandalah — its sphere; vyomni — in the sky; rarāja — shone forth; udu-ganaih — along with the stars; śaśī — the moon; yathā — as; yadu-patih — the master of the Yadu dynasty; krishnah — Lord Krishna; vrishni-cakra — by the circle of Vrishnis; āvritah — surrounded; bhuvi — upon the earth.

TRANSLATION

The full moon shone in the sky, surrounded by stars, just as Śrī Krishna, the Lord of the Yadu dynasty, shone brilliantly on the earth, surrounded by all the Vrishnis.

PURPORT

Śrīla Sanātana Gosvāmī explains that in Vrindāvana the full moon is eternally risen, and this full moon is like the full manifestation of the Absolute Truth, Śrī Krishna. When He was manifest on earth, Lord Krishna was surrounded by prominent members of the Vrishni dynasty such as Nanda, Upananda, Vasudeva and Akrūra.
