Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 20: The Rainy Season and Autumn in Vrindāvana

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.20.32

evam nivasatos tasmin

rāma-keśavayor vraje

śarat samabhavad vyabhrā

svacchāmbv-aparushānilā

SYNONYMS

evam — in this manner; nivasatoh — while the two of Them were dwelling; tasmin — in that; rāma-keśavayoh — Lord Rāma and Lord Keśava; vraje — in Vrindāvana; śarat — the fall season; samabhavat — became fully manifest; vyabhrā — free from clouds in the sky; svaccha-ambu — in which the water was clear; aparusha-anilā — and the wind was gentle.

TRANSLATION

While Lord Rāma and Lord Keśava were thus dwelling in Vrindāvana, the fall season arrived, when the sky is cloudless, the water clear and the wind gentle.
