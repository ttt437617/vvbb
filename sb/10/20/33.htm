Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 20: The Rainy Season and Autumn in Vrindāvana

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.20.33

śaradā nīrajotpattyā

nīrāni prakritim yayuh

bhrashtānām iva cetāmsi

punar yoga-nishevayā

SYNONYMS

śaradā — by the effect of the autumn season; nīraja — the lotus flowers; utpattyā — which regenerates; nīrāni — the bodies of water; prakritim — to their natural state (of cleanliness); yayuh — returned; bhrashtānām — of those who are fallen; iva — just as; cetāmsi — the minds; punah — once again; yoga — of devotional service; nishevayā — by practice.

TRANSLATION

The autumn season, which regenerated the lotus flowers, also restored the various bodies of water to their original purity, just as the process of devotional service purifies the minds of the fallen yogīs when they return to it.
