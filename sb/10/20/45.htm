Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 20: The Rainy Season and Autumn in Vrindāvana

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.20.45

āślishya sama-śītoshnam

prasūna-vana-mārutam

janās tāpam jahur gopyo

na krishna-hrita-cetasah

SYNONYMS

āślishya — embracing; sama — equal; śīta-ushnam — between cold and heat; prasūna-vana — of the forest of flowers; mārutam — the wind; janāh — the people in general; tāpam — suffering; jahuh — were able to abandon; gopyah — the gopīs; na — not; krishna — by Lord Krishna; hrita — stolen; cetasah — whose hearts.

TRANSLATION

Except for the gopīs, whose hearts had been stolen by Krishna, the people could forget their suffering by embracing the wind coming from the flower-filled forest. This wind was neither hot nor cold.
