Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 20: The Rainy Season and Autumn in Vrindāvana

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.20.16

mārgā babhūvuh sandigdhās

trinaiś channā hy asamskritāh

nābhyasyamānāh śrutayo

dvijaih kālena cāhatāh

SYNONYMS

mārgāh — the roads; babhūvuh — became; sandigdhāh — obscured; trinaih — by grass; channāh — covered over; hi — indeed; asamskritāh — not cleansed; na abhyasyamānāh — not being studied; śrutayah — the scriptures; dvijaih — by the brāhmanas; kālena — by the effects of time; ca — and; āhatāh — corrupted.

TRANSLATION

During the rainy season the roads, not being cleansed, became covered with grass and debris and were thus difficult to make out. These roads were like religious scriptures that brāhmanas no longer study and that thus become corrupted and covered over with the passage of time.
