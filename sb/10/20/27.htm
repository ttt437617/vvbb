Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 20: The Rainy Season and Autumn in Vrindāvana

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.20.27

vanaukasah pramuditā

vana-rājīr madhu-cyutah

jala-dhārā girer nādād

āsannā dadriśe guhāh

SYNONYMS

vana-okasah — the aborigine girls of the forest; pramuditāh — joyful; vana-rājīh — the trees of the forest; madhu-cyutah — dripping sweet sap; jala-dhārāh — waterfalls; gireh — on the mountains; nādāt — from their resounding; āsannāh — nearby; dadriśe — He observed; guhāh — caves.

TRANSLATION

The Lord saw the joyful aborigine girls of the forest, the trees dripping sweet sap, and the mountain waterfalls, whose resounding indicated that there were caves nearby.
