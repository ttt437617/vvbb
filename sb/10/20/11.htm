Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 20: The Rainy Season and Autumn in Vrindāvana

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.20.11

haritā haribhih śashpair

indragopaiś ca lohitā

ucchilīndhra-krita-cchāyā

nrinām śrīr iva bhūr abhūt

SYNONYMS

haritāh — greenish; haribhih — which is green; śashpaih — because of the newly grown grass; indragopaih — because of the indragopa insects; ca — and; lohitā — reddish; ucchilīndhra — by the mushrooms; krita — afforded; chāyā — shelter; nrinām — of men; śrīh — the opulence; iva — just as; bhūh — the earth; abhūt — became.

TRANSLATION

The newly grown grass made the earth emerald green, the indragopa insects added a reddish hue, and white mushrooms added further color and circles of shade. Thus the earth appeared like a person who has suddenly become rich.

PURPORT

Śrīla Śrīdhara Svāmī comments that the word nrinām indicates men of the royal order. Thus the colorful display of dark green fields decorated with bright red insects and white mushroom umbrellas can be compared to a royal parade displaying the military strength of a king.
