Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 20: The Rainy Season and Autumn in Vrindāvana

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.20.37

naivāvidan kshīyamānam

jalam gādha-jale-carāh

yathāyur anv-aham kshayyam

narā mūdhāh kutumbinah

SYNONYMS

na — not; eva — indeed; avidan — appreciated; kshīyamānam — diminishing; jalam — the water; gādha-jale — in shallow water; carāh — those who move; yathā — as; āyuh — their life span; anu-aham — every day; kshayyam — diminishing; narāh — men; mūdhāh — foolish; kutumbinah — living with family members.

TRANSLATION

The fish swimming in the increasingly shallow water did not at all understand that the water was diminishing, just as foolish family men cannot see how the time they have left to live is diminishing with every passing day.

PURPORT

After the rainy season the water gradually goes down, but stupid fish do not understand this; thus they are often stranded on the lakeshores and riverbanks. Similarly, those infatuated with family life do not understand that the remainder of their life is constantly decreasing; thus they fail to perfect their Krishna consciousness and are stranded in the cycle of birth and death.
