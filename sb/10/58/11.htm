Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 58: Krishna Marries Five Princesses

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.58.11

yudhishthira uvāca

kim na ācaritam śreyo

na vedāham adhīśvara

yogeśvarānām durdarśo

yan no drishtah ku-medhasām

SYNONYMS

yudhishthirah uvāca — Yudhishthira said; kim — what; nah — by us; ācaritam — performed; śreyah — pious work; na veda — do not know; aham — I; adhīśvara — O supreme controller; yoga — of mystic yoga; īśvarānām — by the masters; durdarśah — rarely seen; yat — that; nah — by us; drishtah — seen; ku-medhasam — who are unintelligent.

TRANSLATION

King Yudhishthira said: O supreme controller, I do not know what pious deeds we fools have done so that we can see You, whom the masters of yogic perfection rarely see.
