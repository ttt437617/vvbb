Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 58: Krishna Marries Five Princesses

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.58.42

kintv asmābhih kritah pūrvam

samayah sātvatarshabha

pumsām vīrya-parīkshārtham

kanyā-vara-parīpsayā

SYNONYMS

kintu — but; asmābhih — by us (her family); kritah — made; pūrvam — previously; samayah — a stipulation; sātvata-rishabha — O chief of the Sātvatas; pumsām — of the men (who came as suitors); vīrya — the prowess; parīkshā — of testing; artham — for the purpose; kanyā — for my daughter; vara — the husband; parīpsayā — with the desire of finding.

TRANSLATION

But to ascertain the proper husband for my daughter, O chief of the Sātvatas, we previously set a condition to test the prowess of her suitors.

PURPORT

According to Śrīla Viśvanātha Cakravartī, the King's actual purpose in setting up the test was to obtain Śrī Krishna as his son-in-law, since only He could subdue the bulls. Without such a test it would have been difficult for Nagnajit to refuse the many apparently qualified princes and kings who came to ask for his daughter's hand in marriage.
