Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 58: Krishna Marries Five Princesses

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.58.54

tān asyatah śara-vrātān

bandhu-priya-krid arjunah

gāndīvī kālayām āsa

simhah kshudra-mrigān iva

SYNONYMS

tān — them; asyatah — throwing; śara — of arrows; vrātān — multitudes; bandhu — his friend (Lord Krishna); priya — to please; krit — acting; arjunah — Arjuna; gandīvī — the possessor of the bow Gāndīva; kālayām āsa — drove them away; simhah — a lion; kshudra — insignificant; mrigān — animals; iva — as.

TRANSLATION

Arjuna, wielder of the Gāndīva bow, was always eager to please his friend Krishna, and thus he drove back those opponents, who were shooting torrents of arrows at the Lord. He did this just as a lion drives away insignificant animals.
