Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 58: Krishna Marries Five Princesses

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.58.25

bhagavāms tatra nivasan

svānām priya-cikīrshayā

agnaye khāndavam dātum

arjunasyāsa sārathih

SYNONYMS

bhagavān — the Supreme Lord; tatra — there; nivasan — residing; svānām — for His own (devotees); priya — pleasure; cikīrshayā — desiring to give; agnaye — to Agni, the demigod of fire; khāndavam — the Khāndava forest; dātum — in order to give; arjunasya — of Arjuna; āsa — He became; sārathih — the chariot driver.

TRANSLATION

The Supreme Lord stayed in that city for some time to please His devotees. On one occasion, Śrī Krishna wanted to give the Khāndava forest as a gift to Agni, and so the Lord became Arjuna's charioteer.

PURPORT

Śrīla Jīva Gosvāmī explains the sequence of events that occurred during Lord Krishna's stay with the Pāndavas. He states that first the Khāndava forest was burned, then Kālindī was found, then the city was constructed, and then the assembly hall was presented to the Pāndavas.
