Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 58: Krishna Marries Five Princesses

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.58.5

paramāsana āsīnam

krishnā krishnam aninditā

navodhā vrīditā kiñcic

chanair etyābhyavandata

SYNONYMS

parama — exalted; āsane — on a seat; āsīnam — sitting; krishnā — Draupadī; krishnam — Krishna; aninditā — blameless; nava — newly; ūdhā — married; vrīditā — shy; kiñcit — somewhat; śanaih — slowly; etya — approaching; abhyavandata — offered her obeisances.

TRANSLATION

Faultless Draupadī, the Pāndavas' newly married wife, slowly and somewhat timidly approached Lord Krishna, who sat on an exalted seat, and offered Him her obeisances.

PURPORT

Śrīmatī Draupadī was so devoted to Krishna that she herself was called Krishnā, which is the feminine form of the name, and Arjuna was also called Krishna because of his devotion to the Lord. Similarly, the devotees of the modern Krishna consciousness movement are often called "the Krishnas." So it appears that the custom of addressing Krishna's devotees by His name has a long history.
