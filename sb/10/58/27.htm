Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 58: Krishna Marries Five Princesses

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.58.27

mayaś ca mocito vahneh

sabhām sakhya upāharat

yasmin duryodhanasyāsīj

jala-sthala-driśi-bhramah

SYNONYMS

mayah — the demon named Maya; ca — and; mocitah — delivered; vahneh — from the fire; sabhām — an assembly hall; sakhye — to his friend, Arjuna; upāharat — presented; yasmin — in which; duryodhanasya — of Duryodhana; āsīt — there was; jala — of water; sthala — and dry ground; driśi — in seeing; bhramah — confusion.

TRANSLATION

When the demon Maya was saved from the fire by his friend Arjuna, Maya presented him with an assembly hall, in which Duryodhana would later mistake water for a solid floor.
