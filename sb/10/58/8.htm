Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 58: Krishna Marries Five Princesses

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.58.8

tam āha prema-vaiklavya-

ruddha-kanthāśru-locanā

smarantī tān bahūn kleśān

kleśāpāyātma-darśanam

SYNONYMS

tam — to Him; āha — she said; prema — of love; vaiklavya — due to the distress; ruddha — choking; kanthā — whose throat; aśru — (filled) with tears; locanā — whose eyes; smarantī — remembering; tān — those; bahūn — many; kleśān — pains; kleśa — of pain; apāya — for the dispelling; ātma — Himself; darśanam — who shows.

TRANSLATION

So overcome by love that her throat choked up and her eyes filled with tears, Queen Kuntī remembered the many troubles she and her sons had endured. Thus she addressed Lord Krishna, who appears before His devotees to drive away their distress
