Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 58: Krishna Marries Five Princesses

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.58.4

yudhishthirasya bhīmasya

kritvā pādābhivandanam

phālgunam parirabhyātha

yamābhyām cābhivanditah

SYNONYMS

yudhishthirasya bhīmasya — to Yudhishthira and Bhīma; kritvā — after offering; pāda — at their feet; abhivandanam — obeisances; phālgunam — Arjuna; parirabhya — firmly embracing; atha — then; yamābhyām — by the twin brothers, Nakula and Sahadeva; ca — and; abhivanditah — greeted respectfully.

TRANSLATION

After the Lord bowed down at the feet of Yudhishthira and Bhīma and firmly embraced Arjuna, He accepted obeisances from the twin brothers, Nakula and Sahadeva.

PURPORT

Externally Krishna was the Pāndavas' cousin, and their relationship was just like that between cousin-brothers. Because Yudhishthira and Bhīma were externally senior to Krishna, the Lord bowed down at their feet, whereas He embraced His peer Arjuna and accepted obeisances from the younger brothers, Nakula and Sahadeva. Sometimes inexperienced devotees think it is sinful to honor or bow down to an elder brother in Krishna consciousness. But from Lord Krishna's example here we may conclude that offering all respects to a senior brother in Krishna consciousness is not sinful.
