Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 58: Krishna Marries Five Princesses

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.58.24

yadaiva krishnah sandishtah

pārthānām paramādbutam

kārayām āsa nagaram

vicitram viśvakarmanā

SYNONYMS

yadā eva — when; krishnah — Lord Krishna; sandishtah — requested; pārthānām — for the sons of Prithā; parama — most; adbhutam — amazing; kārayām āsa — He had constructed; nagaram — a city; vicitram — full of variety; viśvakarmanā — by Viśvakarmā, the architect of the demigods.

TRANSLATION

[Describing a previous incident, Śukadeva Gosvāmī said:] Upon the request of the Pāndavas, Lord Krishna had Viśvakarmā build them a most wonderful and amazing city.

PURPORT

Śrīla Viśvanātha Cakravartī mentions that this city was constructed before the burning of the Khāndava forest and hence before the Lord found His bride Kālindī.
