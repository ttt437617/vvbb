Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 58: Krishna Marries Five Princesses

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.58.12

iti vai vārshikān māsān

rājñā so 'bhyarthitah sukham

janayan nayanānandam

indraprasthaukasām vibhuh

SYNONYMS

iti — thus; vai — indeed; vārshikān — of the rainy season; māsān — the months; rājñā — by the King; sah — He; abhyarthitah — invited; sukham — happily; janayan — generating; nayana — for the eyes; ānandam — bliss; indraprastha-okasām — of the residents of Indraprastha; vibhuh — the almighty Lord.

TRANSLATION

Requested by the King to stay with them, the almighty Lord remained happily in Indraprastha during the months of the rainy season, giving joy to the eyes of the city's residents.

PURPORT

If possible, the readers of the Bhāgavatam should try to correctly chant the Sanskrit verses, which are exquisitely poetic.
