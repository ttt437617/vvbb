Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 58: Krishna Marries Five Princesses

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.58.33

na tām śekur nripā vodhum

ajitvā sapta-go-vrishān

tīkshna-śrińgān su-durdharshān

vīrya-gandhāsahān khalān

SYNONYMS

na — not; tām — her; śekuh — were able; nripāh — kings; vodhum — to marry; ajitvā — without defeating; sapta — seven; go-vrishān — bulls; tīkshna — sharp; śrińgān — whose horns; su — very; durdharshān — uncontrollable; vīrya — of warriors; gandha — the smell; asahān — not tolerating; khalān — vicious.

TRANSLATION

The kings who came as suitors were not allowed to marry her unless they could subdue seven sharp-horned bulls. These bulls were extremely vicious and uncontrollable, and they could not tolerate even the smell of warriors.
