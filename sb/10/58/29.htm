Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 58: Krishna Marries Five Princesses

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.58.29

athopayeme kālindīm

su-punya-rtv-riksha ūrjite

vitanvan paramānandam

svānām parama-mańgalah

SYNONYMS

atha — then; upayeme — He married; kālindīm — Kālindī; su — very; punya — auspicious; ritu — the season; rikshe — and the lunar asterism; ūrjite — (on a day) when the configuration of the sun and other heavenly bodies was good; vitanvan — spreading; parama — the greatest; ānandam — pleasure; svānām — for His devotees; parama — supremely; mańgalah — auspicious.

TRANSLATION

The supremely auspicious Lord then married Kālindī on a day when the season, the lunar asterism and the configurations of the sun and other heavenly bodies were all propitious. In this way He brought the greatest pleasure to His devotees.
