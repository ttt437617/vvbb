Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 65: Lord Balarāma Visits Vrindāvana

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.65.7

kaccin no bāndhavā rāma

sarve kuśalam āsate

kaccit smaratha no rāma

yūyam dāra-sutānvitāh

SYNONYMS

kaccit — whether; nah — our; bāndhavāh — relatives; rāma — O Balarāma; sarve — all; kuśalam — well; āsate — are; kaccit — whether; smaratha — remember; nah — us; rāma — O Rāma; yūyam — all of you; dāra — with wives; suta — and children; anvitāh — together.

TRANSLATION

[The cowherds said:] O Rāma, are all our relatives doing well? And Rāma, do all of you, with your wives and children, still remember us?
