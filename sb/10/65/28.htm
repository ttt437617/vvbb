Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 65: Lord Balarāma Visits Vrindāvana

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.65.28

rāma rāma mahā-bāho

na jāne tava vikramam

yasyaikāmśena vidhritā

jagatī jagatah pate

SYNONYMS

rāma rāma — O Rāma, Rāma; mahā-bāho — O mighty-armed one; na jāne — I do not appreciate; tava — Your; vikramam — prowess; yasya — whose; eka — one; amśena — by a portion; vidhritā — is sustained; jagatī — the earth; jagatah — of the universe; pate — O master.

TRANSLATION

[Goddess Yamunā said:] Rāma, Rāma, O mighty-armed one! I know nothing of Your prowess. With a single portion of Yourself You hold up the earth, O Lord of the universe.

PURPORT

The phrase ekāmśena ("with a single portion") refers to the Lord's expansion as Śesha. This is confirmed by the ācāryas.
