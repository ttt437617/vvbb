Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 65: Lord Balarāma Visits Vrindāvana

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.65.21

upagīyamāno gandharvair

vanitā-śobhi-mandale

reme karenu-yūtheśo

māhendra iva vāranah

SYNONYMS

upagīyamānah — being praised in song; gandharvaih — by Gandharvas; vanitā — by young women; śobhi — beautified; mandale — in the circle; reme — He enjoyed; karenu — of she-elephants; yūtha — of a herd; īśah — the master; māhā-indrah — of Lord Indra; iva — just like; vāranah — the elephant (named Airāvata).

TRANSLATION

As the Gandharvas sang His glories, Lord Balarāma enjoyed within the brilliant circle of young women. He appeared just like Indra's elephant, the lordly Airāvata, enjoying in the company of she-elephants.
