Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 65: Lord Balarāma Visits Vrindāvana

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.65.30

tato vyamuñcad yamunām

yācito bhagavān balah

vijagāha jalam strībhih

karenubhir ivebha-rāt

SYNONYMS

tatah — then; vyamuñcat — released; yamunām — the Yamunā; yācitah — begged; bhagavān — the Supreme Lord; balah — Balarāma; vijagāha — He submerged Himself; jalam — in the water; strībhih — with the women; karenubhih — with his she-elephants; iva — as; ibha — of elephants; rāt — the king.

TRANSLATION

[Śukadeva Gosvāmī continued:] Thereupon Lord Balarāma released the Yamunā and, like the king of the elephants with his entourage of she-elephants, entered the river's water with His female companions.
