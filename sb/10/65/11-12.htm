Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 65: Lord Balarāma Visits Vrindāvana

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.65.11-12

mātaram pitaram bhrātrin

patīn putrān svasrin api

yad-arthe jahima dāśārha

dustyajān sva-janān prabho

tā nah sadyah parityajya

gatah sañchinna-sauhridah

katham nu tādriśam strībhir

na śraddhīyeta bhāshitam

SYNONYMS

mātaram — mother; pitaram — father; bhrātrin — brothers; patīn — husbands; putrān — children; svasrin — sisters; api — also; yat — of whom; arthe — for the sake; jahima — we abandoned; dāśārha — O descendant of Dāśārha; dustyajān — difficult to give up; sva-janān — own people; prabho — O Lord; tāh — these women; nah — ourselves; sadyah — suddenly; parityajya — rejecting; gatah — gone away; sañchinna — having broken off; sauhridah — friendship; katham — how; nu — indeed; tādriśam — such; strībhih — by women; na śraddhīyeta — would not be trusted; bhāshītam — words spoken.

TRANSLATION

"For Krishna's sake, O descendant of Dāśārha, we abandoned our mothers, fathers, brothers, husbands, children and sisters, even though these family relations are difficult to give up. But now, O Lord, that same Krishna has suddenly abandoned us and gone away, breaking off all affectionate ties with us. And yet how could any woman fail to trust His promises?
