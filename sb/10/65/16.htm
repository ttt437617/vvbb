Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 65: Lord Balarāma Visits Vrindāvana

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.65.16

sańkarshanas tāh krishnasya

sandeśair hridayam-gamaih

sāntvayām āsa bhagavān

nānānunaya-kovidah

SYNONYMS

sańkarshanah — Lord Balarāma, the supreme attractor; tāh — them; krishnasya — of Lord Krishna; sandeśaih — by the confidential messages; hridayam — the heart; gamaih — touching; sāntvayām āsa — consoled; bhagavān — the Supreme Lord; nānā — of various kinds; anunaya — in conciliation; kovidah — expert.

TRANSLATION

The Supreme Lord Balarāma, the attractor of all, being expert at various kinds of conciliation, consoled the gopīs by relaying to them the confidential messages Lord Krishna had sent with Him. These messages deeply touched the gopīs' hearts.

PURPORT

Śrīla Jīva Gosvāmī quotes the following verse from Śrī Vishnu Purāna (5.24.20), which describes the messages Lord Balarāma brought from Krishna for the gopīs:

sandeśaih sāma-madhuraih

prema-garbhair agarvitaih

rāmenāśvāsitā gopyah

krishnasyāti-manoharaih

"Lord Balarāma consoled the gopīs by giving them Lord Krishna's most charming messages, which expressed sweet conciliation, which were inspired by His pure love for them, and which were without a tinge of pride." Śrīla Jīva Gosvāmī also comments that the use of the name Sańkarshana here implies that Balarāma attracted Lord Krishna to appear in His mind and in this way showed Śrī Krishna to the gopīs. Thus Balarāma consoled Śrī Krishna's beloved girlfriends.

Śrīla Viśvanātha Cakravartī comments that Lord Krishna sent various messages. Some instructed the gopīs in transcendental knowledge, others were conciliatory, and still others revealed the Lord's power. Besides its given meaning, the word hridayam-gamaih also indicates that these messages were confidential.
