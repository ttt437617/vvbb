Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 65: Lord Balarāma Visits Vrindāvana

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.65.33

adyāpi driśyate rājan

yamunākrishta-vartmanā

balasyānanta-vīryasya

vīryam sūcayatīva hi

SYNONYMS

adya — today; api — even; driśyate — is seen; rājan — O King (Parīkshit); yamunā — the Yamunā River; ākrishta — pulled; vartmanā — whose currents; balasya — of Lord Balarāma; ananta — unlimited; vīryasya — whose potency; vīryam — the prowess; sūcayatī — indicating; iva — as; hi — indeed.

TRANSLATION

Even today, O King, one can see how the Yamunā flows through the many channels created when it was dragged by the unlimitedly powerful Lord Balarāma. Thus she demonstrates His prowess.
