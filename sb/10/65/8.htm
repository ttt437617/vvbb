Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 65: Lord Balarāma Visits Vrindāvana

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.65.8

dishtyā kamso hatah pāpo

dishtyā muktāh suhrij-janāh

nihatya nirjitya ripūn

dishtyā durgam samāśrītāh

SYNONYMS

dishtyā — by good fortune; kamsah — Kamsa; hatah — killed; pāpah — sinful; dishtyā — by good fortune; muktāh — freed; suhrit-janāh — dear relatives; nihatya — killing; nirjitya — conquering; ripūn — enemies; dishtyā — by good fortune; durgam — a fortress; samāśrītāh — taken shelter of.

TRANSLATION

It is our great fortune that sinful Kamsa has been killed and our dear relatives freed. And it is also our good fortune that our relatives have killed and defeated their enemies and found complete security in a great fortress.
