Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 10: Deliverance of the Yamala-arjuna Trees

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.10.32

grihyamānais tvam agrāhyo

vikāraih prākritair gunaih

ko nv ihārhati vijñātum

prāk siddham guna-samvritah

SYNONYMS

grihyamānaih — accepting the body made of material nature as existing at the present moment because of being visible; tvam — You; agrāhyah — not confined in a body made of material nature; vikāraih — agitated by the mind; prākritaih gunaih — by the material modes of nature (sattva-guna, rajo-guna and tamo-guna); kah — who is there; nu — after that; iha — in this material world; arhati — who deserves; vijñātum — to know; prāk siddham — that which existed before the creation; guna-samvritah — because of being covered by the material qualities.

TRANSLATION

O Lord, You exist before the creation. Therefore, who, trapped by a body of material qualities in this material world, can understand You?

PURPORT

As it is said:

atah śrī-krishna-nāmādi

na bhaved grāhyam indriyaih

sevonmukhe hi jihvādau

svayam eva sphuraty adah

[BRS. 1.2.234]

Krishna's name, attributes and form are Absolute Truth, existing before the creation. Therefore, how can those who are created — that is, those entrapped in bodies created of material elements — understand Krishna perfectly? This is not possible. But, sevonmukhe hi jihvādau svayam eva sphuraty adah: Krishna reveals Himself to those engaged in devotional service. This is also confirmed in Bhagavad-gītā (18.15) by the Lord Himself: bhaktyā mām abhijānāti [Bg. 18.55]. Even the descriptions of Krishna in Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam are sometimes misunderstood by less intelligent men with a poor fund of knowledge. Therefore, the best course by which to know Him is to engage oneself in pure devotional activities. The more one advances in devotional activities, the more one can understand Him as He is. If from the material platform one could understand Krishna, then, since Krishna is everything (sarvam khalv idam brahma), one could understand Krishna by seeing anything within this material world. But that is not possible.

mayā tatam idam sarvam

jagad avyakta-mūrtinā

mat-sthāni sarva-bhūtāni

na cāham teshv avasthitah

(Bg. 9.4)

Everything is resting on Krishna, and everything is Krishna, but this is not to be realized by persons on the material platform.
