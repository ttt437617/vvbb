Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 81: The Lord Blesses Sudāmā Brāhmana

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.81.26

pati-vratā patim drishtvā

premotkanthāśru-locanā

mīlitākshy anamad buddhyā

manasā parishasvaje

SYNONYMS

pati-vratā — devoted to her husband; patim — her husband; drishtvā — seeing; prema — of love; utkantha — with the eagerness; aśru — tearful; locanā — whose eyes; mīlita — holding closed; akshī — her eyes; anamat — she bowed down; buddhyā — with thoughtful reflection; manasā — with her heart; parishasvaje — she embraced.

TRANSLATION

When the chaste lady saw her husband, her eyes filled with tears of love and eagerness. As she held her eyes closed, she solemnly bowed down to him, and in her heart she embraced him.
