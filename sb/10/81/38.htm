Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 81: The Lord Blesses Sudāmā Brāhmana

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.81.38

ittham vyavasito buddhyā

bhakto 'tīva janārdane

vishayān jāyayā tyakshyan

bubhuje nāti-lampatah

SYNONYMS

ittham — in this way; vyavasitah — fixing his determination; buddhyā — with intelligence; bhaktah — devoted; atīva — absolutely; janārdane — to Lord Krishna, the shelter of all living beings; vishayān — the objects of sense gratification; jāyayā — with his wife; tyakshyan — wanting to renounce; bubhuje — he enjoyed; na — not; ati — avaricious.

TRANSLATION

[Śukadeva Gosvāmī continued:] Thus firmly fixing his determination by means of his spiritual intelligence, Sudāmā remained absolutely devoted to Lord Krishna, the shelter of all living beings. Free from avarice, he enjoyed, together with his wife, the sense pleasures that had been bestowed upon him, always with the idea of eventually renouncing all sense gratification.
