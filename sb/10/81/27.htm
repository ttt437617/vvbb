Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 81: The Lord Blesses Sudāmā Brāhmana

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.81.27

patnīm vīkshya visphurantīm

devīm vaimānikīm iva

dāsīnām nishka-kanthīnām

madhye bhāntīm sa vismitah

SYNONYMS

patnīm — his wife; vīkshya — seeing; visphurantīm — appearing effulgent; devīm — a demigoddess; vaimānikīm — come in a heavenly airplane; iva — as if; dāsīnām — of maidservants; nishka — lockets; kanthīnām — on whose necks; madhye — in the midst; bhāntīm — shining; sah — he; vismitah — amazed.

TRANSLATION

Sudāmā was amazed to see his wife. Shining forth in the midst of maidservants adorned with jeweled lockets, she looked as effulgent as a demigoddess in her celestial airplane.

PURPORT

Śrīla Viśvanātha Cakravartī explains that up to now the Supreme Lord had kept the brāhmana in his wretched state so that his wife could recognize him.
