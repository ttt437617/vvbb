Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 83: Draupadī Meets the Queens of Krishna

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.83.5

śrī-rishir uvāca

ity uttamah-śloka-śikhā-manim janeshv

abhishtuvatsv andhaka-kaurava-striyah

sametya govinda-kathā mitho 'grinams

tri-loka-gītāh śrinu varnayāmi te

SYNONYMS

śrī-rishih uvāca — the great sage, Śukadeva, said; iti — thus; uttamah-śloka — of great personalities who are glorified in choice poetry; śikhā-manim — the crest jewel (Lord Krishna); janeshu — His devotees; abhishtuvatsu — while they were glorifying; andhaka-kaurava — of the Andhaka and Kaurava clans; striyah — the women; sametya — meeting; govinda-kathāh — topics of Lord Govinda; mithah — among one another; agrinan — spoke; tri — three; loka — in the worlds; gītāh — sung; śrinu — please hear; varnayāmi — I will describe; te — to you (Parīkshit Mahārāja).

TRANSLATION

The great sage Śukadeva Gosvāmī said: As Yudhishthira and the others were thus praising Lord Krishna, the crest jewel of all sublimely glorified personalities, the women of the Andhaka and Kaurava clans met with one another and began discussing topics about Govinda that are sung throughout the three worlds. Please listen as I relate these to you.
