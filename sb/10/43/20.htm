Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 43: Krishna Kills the Elephant Kuvalayāpīda

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.43.20

nirīkshya tāv uttama-pūrushau janā

mañca-sthitā nāgara-rāshtrakā nripa

praharsha-vegotkalitekshanānanāh

papur na triptā nayanais tad-ānanam

SYNONYMS

nirīkshya — seeing; tau — both of Them; uttama-pūrushau — the Supreme Personalities; janāh — the people; mañca — in the viewing galleries; sthitāh — sitting; nāgara — the city-dwellers; rāshtrakāh — and the people from outlying districts; nripa — O King; praharsha — of their joy; vega — by the force; utkalita — made to expand widely; īkshana — their eyes; ānanāh — and faces; papuh — they drank; na — not; triptāh — satiated; nayanaih — with their eyes; tat — of Them; ānanam — the faces.

TRANSLATION

O King, as the citizens of the city and the people from outlying districts gazed upon those two Supreme Personalities from their seats in the galleries, the force of the people's happiness caused their eyes to open wide and their faces to blossom. They drank in the vision of the Lords' faces without becoming satiated.
