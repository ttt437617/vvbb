Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 43: Krishna Kills the Elephant Kuvalayāpīda

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.43.26-27

gāvah sa-pālā etena

dāvāgneh parimocitāh

kāliyo damitah sarpa

indraś ca vimadah kritah

saptāham eka-hastena

dhrito 'dri-pravaro 'munā

varsha-vātāśanibhyaś ca

paritrātam ca gokulam

SYNONYMS

gāvah — the cows; sa — together with; pālāh — their tenders; etena — by Him; dāva-agneh — from the forest fire; parimocitāh — saved; kāliyah — Kāliya; damitah — subdued; sarpah — the serpent; indrah — Indra; ca — and; vimadah — prideless; kritah — made; sapta-aham — for seven days; eka-hastena — with one hand; dhritah — held; adri — of mountains; pravarah — the most eminent; amunā — by Him; varsha — from rain; vāta — wind; aśanibhyah — and hail; ca — also; paritrātam — delivered; ca — and; gokulam — the residents of Gokula.

TRANSLATION

He saved the cows and the cowherds from a forest fire and subdued the serpent Kāliya. He removed Lord Indra's false pride by holding up the best of mountains with one hand for an entire week, thus protecting the inhabitants of Gokula from rain, wind and hail.
