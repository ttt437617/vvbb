Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 43: Krishna Kills the Elephant Kuvalayāpīda

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.43.18

hatam kuvalayāpīdam

drishtvā tāv api durjayau

kamso manasy api tadā

bhriśam udvivije nripa

SYNONYMS

hatam — killed; kuvalayāpīdam — the elephant Kuvalayāpīda; drishtvā — seeing; tau — the two of Them, Krishna and Balarāma; api — and; durjayau — invincible; kamsah — King Kamsa; manasi — in his mind; api — indeed; tadā — then; bhriśam — exceedingly; udvivije — became anxious; nripa — O King (Parīkshit).

TRANSLATION

When Kamsa saw that Kuvalayāpīda was dead and the two brothers were invincible, he was overwhelmed with anxiety, O King.
