Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 43: Krishna Kills the Elephant Kuvalayāpīda

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.43.19

tau rejatū rańga-gatau mahā-bhujau

vicitra-veshābharana-srag-ambarau

yathā natāv uttama-vesha-dhārinau

manah kshipantau prabhayā nirīkshatām

SYNONYMS

tau — the two of Them; rejatuh — shone; rańga-gatau — present in the arena; mahā-bhujau — the mighty-armed Lords; vicitra — variegated; vesha — whose style of dress; ābharana — ornaments; srak — garlands; ambarau — and garments; yathā — like; natau — two actors; uttama — excellent; vesha — costumes; dhārinau — wearing; manah — the minds; kshipantau — striking; prabhayā — with Their effulgences; nirīkshatām — of those who looked on.

TRANSLATION

Arrayed with variegated ornaments, garlands and garments, just like a pair of excellently costumed actors, the two mighty-armed Lords shone splendidly in the arena. Indeed, They overpowered the minds of all onlookers with Their effulgences.
