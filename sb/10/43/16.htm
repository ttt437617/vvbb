Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 43: Krishna Kills the Elephant Kuvalayāpīda

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.43.16

vritau gopaih katipayair

baladeva-janārdanau

rańgam viviśatū rājan

gaja-danta-varāyudhau

SYNONYMS

vritau — surrounded; gopaih — by cowherd boys; katipayaih — several; baladeva-janārdanau — Balarāma and Krishna; rańgam — the arena; viviśatuh — entered; rājan — O King (Parīkshit); gaja-danta — the elephant's tusks; vara — chosen; āyudhau — whose weapons.

TRANSLATION

My dear King, Lord Baladeva and Lord Janārdana, each carrying one of the elephant's tusks as His chosen weapon, entered the arena with several cowherd boys.
