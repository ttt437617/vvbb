Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 43: Krishna Kills the Elephant Kuvalayāpīda

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.43.40

tasmād bhavadbhyām balibhir

yoddhavyam nānayo 'tra vai

mayi vikrama vārshneya

balena saha mushtikah

SYNONYMS

tasmāt — therefore; bhavadbhyām — You two; balibhih — with those who are strong; yoddhavyam — should fight; na — there is not; anayah — injustice; atra — in this; vai — certainly; mayi — to me; vikrama — (show) Your prowess; vārshneya — O descendant of Vrishni; balena saha — with Balarāma; mushtikah — Mushtika (should fight).

TRANSLATION

Therefore You two should fight powerful wrestlers. There's certainly nothing unfair about that. You, O descendant of Vrishni, can show Your prowess against me, and Balarāma can fight with Mushtika.

Thus end the purports of the humble servants of His Divine Grace A. C. Bhaktivedanta Swami Prabhupāda to the Tenth Canto, Forty-third Chapter, of the Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam, entitled "Krishna Kills the Elephant Kuvalayāpīda."
