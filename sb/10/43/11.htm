Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 43: Krishna Kills the Elephant Kuvalayāpīda

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.43.11

sa dhāvan kriīdayā bhūmau

patitvā sahasotthitah

tam matvā patitam kruddho

dantābhyām so 'hanat kshitim

SYNONYMS

sah — He; dhāvan — running; krīdayā — playfully; bhūmau — on the ground; patitvā — falling; sahasā — suddenly; utthitah — getting up; tam — Him; matvā — thinking; patitam — fallen; kruddhah — angry; dantābhyām — with his tusks; sah — he, Kuvalayāpīda; ahanat — struck; kshitim — the earth.

TRANSLATION

As Krishna dodged about, He playfully fell on the ground and quickly got up again. The raging elephant, thinking Krishna was down, tried to gore Him with his tusks but struck the earth instead.
