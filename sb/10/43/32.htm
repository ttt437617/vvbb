Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 43: Krishna Kills the Elephant Kuvalayāpīda

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.43.32

he nanda-sūno he rāma

bhavantau vīra-sammatau

niyuddha-kuśalau śrutvā

rājñāhūtau didrikshunā

SYNONYMS

he nanda-sūno — O son of Nanda; he rāma — O Rāma; bhavantau — You two; vīra — by heroes; sammatau — are well respected; niyuddha — in wrestling; kuśalau — skillful; śrutvā — hearing; rājñā — by the King; āhūtau — called for; didrikshunā — who wanted to see.

TRANSLATION

[Cānūra said:l O son of Nanda, O Rāma, You two are well respected by courageous men and are both skillful at wrestling. Having heard of Your prowess, the King has called You here, wanting to see for himself.
