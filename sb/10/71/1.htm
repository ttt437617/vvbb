Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 71: The Lord Travels to Indraprastha

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.71.1

śrī-śuka uvāca

ity udīritam ākarnya

devarisher uddhavo 'bravīt

sabhyānām matam ājñāya

krishnasya ca mahā-matih

SYNONYMS

śrī-śukah uvāca — Śukadeva Gosvāmī said; iti — thus; udīritam — that which was stated; ākarnya — hearing; deva-risheh — by Nārada, the sage of the demigods; uddhavah — Uddhava; abravīt — spoke; sabhyānām — of the members of the royal assembly; matam — the opinion; ājñāya — understanding; krishnasya — of Lord Krishna; ca — and; mahā-matih — great-minded.

TRANSLATION

Śukadeva Gosvāmī said: Having thus heard the statements of Devarshi Nārada, and understanding the opinions of both the assembly and Lord Krishna, the great-minded Uddhava began to speak.
