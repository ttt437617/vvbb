Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 71: The Lord Travels to Indraprastha

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.71.26

dorbhyām parishvajya ramāmalālayam

mukunda-gātram nri-patir hatāśubhah

lebhe parām nirvritim aśru-locano

hrishyat-tanur vismrita-loka-vibhramah

SYNONYMS

dorbhyām — with his arms; parishvajya — embracing; ramā — of the goddess of fortune; amala — faultless; alayam — the abode; mukunda — of Lord Krishna; gātram — the body; nri-patih — the king; hata — destroyed; aśubhah — all of whose bad fortune; lebhe — achieved; parām — the highest; nirvritim — joy; aśru — tears; locanah — in whose eyes; hrishyat — exhilarated; tanuh — whose body; vismrita — forgetting; loka — of the mundane realm; vibhramah — the illusory affairs.

TRANSLATION

The eternal form of Lord Krishna is the everlasting residence of the goddess of fortune. As soon as King Yudhishthira embraced Him, the King became free of all the contamination of material existence. He immediately felt transcendental bliss and merged in an ocean of happiness. There were tears in his eyes, and his body shook due to ecstasy. He completely forgot that he was living in this material world.

PURPORT

The above translation is taken from Śrīla Prabhupāda's Krishna.
