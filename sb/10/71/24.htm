Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 71: The Lord Travels to Indraprastha

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.71.24

gīta-vāditra-ghoshena

brahma-ghoshena bhūyasā

abhyayāt sa hrishīkeśam

prānāh prānam ivādritah

SYNONYMS

gīta — of song; vāditra — and instrumental music; ghoshena — with the sound; brahma — of the Vedas; ghoshena — with the sound; bhūyasā — abundant; abhyayāt — went forth; sah — he; hrishīkeśam — to Lord Krishna; prānāh — the senses; prānam — consciousness, or the air of life; iva — as; ādritah — reverential.

TRANSLATION

As songs and musical instruments resounded along with the loud vibration of Vedic hymns, the King went forth with great reverence to meet Lord Hrishīkeśa, just as the senses go forth to meet the consciousness of life.

PURPORT

Lord Krishna is here described as Hrishīkeśa, the Lord of the senses, and King Yudhishthira's rushing to the Lord is compared to the senses eagerly joining the consciousness of life. Without consciousness, the senses are useless; indeed, the senses function through consciousness. Similarly, when the individual souls are bereft of Krishna consciousness, love of God, they enter into a useless and illusory struggle called material existence. Pure devotees like King Yudhishthira are never bereft of the Lord's association, for they keep Him always within their heart, and yet they feel special ecstasy when they see the Lord after long separation, as described here.
