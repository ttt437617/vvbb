Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 71: The Lord Travels to Indraprastha

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.71.39

govindam griham ānīya

deva-deveśam ādritah

pūjāyām nāvidat krityam

pramodopahato nripah

SYNONYMS

govindam — Lord Krishna; griham — to His quarters; ānīya — bringing; deva — of all gods; deva-īśam — the Supreme God and controller; ādritah — reverential; pūjāyām — in the ritual worship; na avidat — did not know; krityam — the details of performance; pramoda — by his great joy; upahatah — overwhelmed; nripah — the King.

TRANSLATION

King Yudhishthira respectfully brought Lord Govinda, the Supreme God of gods, to his personal quarters. The King was so overcome with joy that he could not remember all the rituals of worship.

PURPORT

Śrīla Prabhupāda writes, "As he brought Krishna within the palace, King Yudhishthira became so confused in his jubilation that he practically forgot what he was to do at that time in order to receive Krishna and worship Him properly."
