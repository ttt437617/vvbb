Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 71: The Lord Travels to Indraprastha

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.71.7

brahma-vesha-dharo gatvā

tam bhiksheta vrikodarah

hanishyati na sandeho

dvai-rathe tava sannidhau

SYNONYMS

brahma — of a brāhmana; vesha — the dress; dharah — wearing; gatvā — going; tam — to him, Jarāsandha; bhiksheta — should beg; vrika-udarah — Bhīma; hanishyati — he will kill him; na — no; sandehah — doubt; dvai-rathe — in one-on-one chariot combat; tava — Your; sannidhau — in the presence.

TRANSLATION

Bhīma should go to him disguised as a brāhmana and beg charity. Thus he will obtain single combat with Jarāsandha, and in Your presence Bhīma will no doubt kill him.

PURPORT

The idea is that Bhīma should beg as charity a one-to-one fight with Jarāsandha.
