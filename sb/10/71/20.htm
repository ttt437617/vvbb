Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 71: The Lord Travels to Indraprastha

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.71.20

ity uktah prasthito dūto

yathā-vad avadan nripān

te 'pi sandarśanam śaureh

pratyaikshan yan mumukshavah

SYNONYMS

iti — thus; uktah — addressed; prasthitah — departed; dūtah — the messenger; yathā-vat — accurately; avadat — he told; nripān — the kings; te — they; api — and; sandarśanam — the audience; śaureh — of Lord Krishna; pratyaikshan — awaited; yat — because; mumukshavah — being eager for liberation.

TRANSLATION

Thus addressed, the messenger departed and accurately relayed the Lord's message to the kings. Eager for freedom, they then waited expectantly for their meeting with Lord Krishna.

PURPORT

The great Vaishnava scholar Śrīla Jīva Gosvāmī comments here that by force of circumstances the kings began focusing their attention on Lord Krishna alone.
