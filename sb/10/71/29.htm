Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 71: The Lord Travels to Indraprastha

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.71.29

sūta-māgadha-gandharvā

vandinaś copamantrinah

mridańga-śańkha-pataha

vīnā-panava-gomukhaih

brāhmanāś cāravindāksham

tushtuvur nanritur jaguh

SYNONYMS

sūta — bards; māgadha — chroniclers; gandharvāh — demigods known for their singing; vandinah — eulogists; ca — and; upamantrinah — jesters; mridańga — with mridańga drums; śańkha — conchshells; pataha — kettledrums; vīnā — vīnās; panava — a smaller drum; gomukhaih — and gomukha horns; brāhmanāh — brāhmanas; ca — as well; aravinda-aksham — the lotus-eyed Lord; tushtuvuh — glorified with hymns; nanrituh — danced; jaguh — -sang.

TRANSLATION

Sūtas, Māgadhas, Gandharvas, Vandīs, jesters and brāhmanas all glorified the lotus-eyed Lord — some reciting prayers, some dancing and singing — as mridańgas, conchshells, kettledrums, vīnās, panavas and gomukhas resounded.
