Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 71: The Lord Travels to Indraprastha

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.71.18

atho munir yadu-patinā sabhājitah

pranamya tam hridi vidadhad vihāyasā

niśamya tad-vyavasitam āhritārhano

mukunda-sandaraśana-nirvritendriyah

SYNONYMS

atha u — and then; munih — the sage (Nārada); yadu-patinā — by Krishna, the Lord of the Yadus; sabhājitah — honored; pranamya — bowing down; tam — to Him; hridi — in his heart; vidadhat — placing Him; vihāyasā — through the sky; niśamya — having heard; tat — His; vyavasitam — affirmed intention; āhrita — having accepted; arhanah — worship; mukunda — of Lord Krishna; sandaraśana — by the meeting; nirvrita — peaceful; indriyah — whose senses.

TRANSLATION

Honored by Śrī Krishna, the chief of the Yadus, Nārada Muni bowed down to the Lord. All of Nārada's senses were satisfied by his meeting with Lord Krishna. Thus, having heard the decision of the Lord and having been worshiped by Him, Nārada placed Him firmly within his heart and departed through the sky.
