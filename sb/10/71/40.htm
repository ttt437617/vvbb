Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 71: The Lord Travels to Indraprastha

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.71.40

pitri-svasur guru-strīnām

krishnaś cakre 'bhivādanam

svayam ca krishnayā rājan

bhaginyā cābhivanditah

SYNONYMS

pitri — His father's; svasuh — of the sister (Kuntī); guru — of His elders; strīnām — and of the wives; krishnah — Lord Krishna; cakre — performed; abhivādanam — offering of obeisances; svayam — Himself; ca — and; krishnayā — by Krishnā (Draupadī); rājan — 0 King (Parīkshit); bhaginyā — by His sister (Subhadrā); ca — also; abhivanditah — bowed down to.

TRANSLATION

Lord Krishna bowed down to His aunt and the wives of His elders, O King, and then Draupadī and the Lord's sister bowed down to Him.

PURPORT

Śrīla Prabhupāda writes, "Lord Krishna delightfully offered His respectful obeisances to Kuntī and other elderly ladies of the palace. His younger sister, Subhadrā, was also standing there with Draupadī, and both offered their respectful obeisances unto the lotus feet of the Lord."
