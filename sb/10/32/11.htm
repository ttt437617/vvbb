Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 32: The Reunion

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.32.11-12

tāh samādāya kālindyā

nirviśya pulinam vibhuh

vikasat-kunda-mandāra

surabhy-anila-shatpadam

śarac-candrāmśu-sandoha-

dhvasta-doshā-tamah śivam

krishnāyā hasta-taralā

cita-komala-vālukam

SYNONYMS

tāh — those gopīs; samādāya — taking; kālindyāh — of the Yamunā; nirviśya — entering upon; pulinam — the bank; vibhuh — the almighty Supreme Lord; vikasat — blooming; kunda-mandāra — of kunda and mandāra flowers; surabhi — fragrant; anila — with the breeze; sat-padam — with bees; śarat — autumnal; candra — of the moon; amśu — of the rays; sandoha — by the abundance; dhvasta — dispelled; doshā — of the night; tamah — the darkness; śivam — auspicious; krishnāyāh — of the river Yamunā; hasta — like hands; tarala — by her waves; ācita — collected; komala — soft; vālukam — sand.

TRANSLATION

The almighty Lord then took the gopīs with Him to the bank of the Kālindī, who with the hands of her waves had scattered piles of soft sand upon the shore. In that auspicious place the breeze, bearing the fragrance of blooming kunda and mandāra flowers, attracted many bees, and the abundant rays of the autumn moon dispelled the darkness of night.
