Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 32: The Reunion

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.32.5

kācid añjalināgrihnāt

tanvī tāmbūla-carvitam

ekā tad-ańghri-kamalam

santaptā stanayor adhāt

SYNONYMS

kācit — one; añjalinā — with joined hands; agrihnāt — took; tanvī — slender; tāmbūla — of betel nut; carvitam — His chewed remnants; ekā — one; tat — His; ańghri — foot; kamalam — lotus; santaptā — burning; stanayoh — on her breasts; adhāt — placed.

TRANSLATION

A slender gopī respectfully took in her joined hands the betel nut He had chewed, and another gopī, burning with desire, put His lotus feet on her breasts.
