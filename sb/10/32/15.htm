Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 32: The Reunion

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.32.15

sabhājayitvā tam anańga-dīpanam

sahāsa-līlekshana-vibhrama-bhruvā

samsparśanenāńka-kritāńghri-hastayoh

samstutya īshat kupitā babhāshire

SYNONYMS

sabhājayitvā — honoring; tam — Him; anańga — of lusty desires; dīpanam — the inciter; sa-hāsa — smiling; līlā — playful; īkshana — with glances; vibhrama — sporting; bhruvā — with their eyebrows; samsparśanena — with touching; ańka — upon their laps; krita — placed; ańghri — of His feet; hastayoh — and hands; samstutya — offering praise; īshat — somewhat; kupitāh — angry; babhāshire — they spoke.

TRANSLATION

Śrī Krishna had awakened romantic desires within the gopīs, and they honored Him by glancing at Him with playful smiles, gesturing amorously with their eyebrows, and massaging His hands and feet as they held them in their laps. Even while worshiping Him, however, they felt somewhat angry, and thus they addressed Him as follows.
