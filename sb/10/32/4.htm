Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 32: The Reunion

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.32.4

kācit karāmbujam śaurer

jagrihe 'ñjalinā mudā

kācid dadhāra tad-bāhum

amse candana-bhūshitam

SYNONYMS

kācit — one of them; kara-ambujam — the lotus hand; śaureh — of Lord Krishna; jagrihe — seized; añjalinā — in her folded palms; mudā — with joy; kācit — another; dadhāra — put; tat-bāhum — His arm; amse — on her shoulder; candana — with sandalwood paste; bhūshitam — adorned.

TRANSLATION

One gopī joyfully took Krishna's hand between her folded palms, and another placed His arm, anointed with sandalwood paste, on her shoulder.
