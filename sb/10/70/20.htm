Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 70: Lord Krishna's Daily Activities

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.70.20

mridańga-vīnā-muraja-

venu-tāla-dara-svanaih

nanritur jagus tushtuvuś ca

sūta-māgadha-vandinah

SYNONYMS

mridańga — of mridańga drums; vīnā — vīnās; muraja — and of murajas, another kind of drum; venu — of flutes; tāla — cymbals; dara — and conchshells; svanaih — with the sounds; nanrituh — they danced; jaguh — sang; tushtuvuh — offered praise; ca — and; sūta — bards; māgadha — reciters of history; vandinah — and panegyrists.

TRANSLATION

These performers would dance and sing to the sounds of mridańgas, vīnās, murajas, flutes, cymbals and conchshells, while professional poets, chroniclers and panegyrists would recite the Lord's glories.
