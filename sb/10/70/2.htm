Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 70: Lord Krishna's Daily Activities

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.70.2

vayāmsy aroruvan krishnam

bodhayantīva vandinah

gāyatsv alishv anidrāni

mandāra-vana-vāyubhih

SYNONYMS

vayāmsi — birds; aroruvan — sounded loudly; krishnam — Lord Krishna; bodhayanti — waking; iva — as if; vandinah — bards; gāyatsu — as they sang; alishu — bees; anidrāni — aroused from sleep; mandāra — of pārijāta trees; vana — from the garden; vāyubhih — by the breeze.

TRANSLATION

The bees' buzzing, caused by the fragrant breeze from the parijāta garden, roused the birds from sleep. And when the birds began to sing loudly, they woke Lord Krishna like court poets reciting His glories.
