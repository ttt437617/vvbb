Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 70: Lord Krishna's Daily Activities

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.70.3

muhūrtam tam tu vaidarbhī

nāmrishyad ati-śobhanam

parirambhana-viśleshāt

priya-bāhv-antaram gatā

SYNONYMS

muhūrtam — time of the day; tam — that; tu — but; vaidarbhī — Queen Rukminī; na amrishyat — did not like; ati — very; śobhanam — auspicious; parirambhana — of His embrace; viśleshāt — because of the loss; priya — of her beloved; bāhu — the arms; antaram — between; gatā — situated.

TRANSLATION

Lying in her beloved's arms, Queen Vaidarbhī did not like this most auspicious hour, for it meant she would lose His embrace.

PURPORT

Śrīla Śrīdhara Svāmī explains that the reaction of Queen Vaidarbhī, Rukminī-devī, shows the attitude of all the queens.
