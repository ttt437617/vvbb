Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 70: Lord Krishna's Daily Activities

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.70.1

śrī-śuka uvāca

athoshasy upavrittāyām

kukkutān kūjato 'śapan

grihīta-kanthyah patibhir

mādhavyo virahāturāh

SYNONYMS

śrī-śukah uvāca — Śukadeva Gosvāmī said; atha — then; ushasi — the dawn; upavrittāyām — as it was approaching; kukkutān — the roosters; kūjatah — who were crowing; aśapan — cursed; grihīta — being held; kanthyah — whose necks; patibhih — by their husbands (Lord Krishna in His multiple manifestations); mādhavyah — the wives of Lord Krishna; viraha — over separation; āturāh — agitated.

TRANSLATION

Śukadeva Gosvāmī said: As dawn approached, the wives of Lord Mādhava, each embraced around the neck by her husband, cursed the crowing roosters. The ladies were disturbed that now they would be separated from Him.

PURPORT

This description of Lord Krishna's daily activities starts with the crowing of the rooster. Lord Krishna's wives knew that the Lord would dutifully get up and perform His prescribed morning rituals, and thus they were agitated at their coming separation from Him and cursed the roosters.
