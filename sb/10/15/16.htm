Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 15: The Killing of Dhenuka, the Ass Demon

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.15.16

kvacit pallava-talpeshu

niyuddha-śrama-karśitah

vriksha-mūlāśrayah śete

gopotsańgopabarhanah

SYNONYMS

kvacit — sometimes; pallava — made from new twigs and buds; talpeshu — upon beds; niyuddha — from the fighting; śrama — by fatigue; karśitah — worn out; vriksha — of a tree; mūla — at the base; āśrayah — taking shelter; śete — He lay down; gopa-utsańga — the lap of a cowherd boy; upabarhanah — as His pillow.

TRANSLATION

Sometimes Lord Krishna grew tired from fighting and lay down at the base of a tree, resting upon a bed made of soft twigs and buds and using the lap of a cowherd friend as His pillow.

PURPORT

The word pallava-talpeshu implies that Lord Krishna expanded Himself into many forms and lay down upon the many beds of twigs, leaves and flowers hastily constructed by His enthusiastic cowherd friends.
