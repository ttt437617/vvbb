Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 15: The Killing of Dhenuka, the Ass Demon

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.15.45

gatādhvāna-śramau tatra

majjanonmardanādibhih

nīvīm vasitvā rucirām

divya-srag-gandha-manditau

SYNONYMS

gata — gone; adhvāna-śramau — whose weariness from being upon the road; tatra — there (in Their home); majjana — by bathing; unmardana — massaging; ādibhih — and so on; nīvīm — in undergarments; vasitvā — being dressed; rucirām — charming; divya — transcendental; srak — with garlands; gandha — and fragrances; manditau — decorated.

TRANSLATION

By being bathed and massaged, the two young Lords were relieved of the weariness caused by walking on the country roads. Then They were dressed in attractive robes and decorated with transcendental garlands and fragrances.
