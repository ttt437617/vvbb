Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 15: The Killing of Dhenuka, the Ass Demon

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.15.15

nrityato gāyatah kvāpi

valgato yudhyato mithah

grihīta-hastau gopālān

hasantau praśaśamsatuh

SYNONYMS

nrityatah — who were dancing; gāyatah — singing; kva api — sometimes; valgatah — moving about; yudhyatah — fighting; mithah — with one another; grihīta-hastau — holding Their hands together; gopālān — the cowherd boys; hasantau — laughing; praśaśamsatuh — They offered praise.

TRANSLATION

Sometimes, as the cowherd boys danced, sang, moved about and playfully fought with each other, Krishna and Balarāma, standing nearby hand in hand, would glorify Their friends' activities and laugh.
