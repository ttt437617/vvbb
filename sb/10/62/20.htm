Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 62: The Meeting of Ūshā and Aniruddha

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.62.20

citralekhā tam ājñāya

pautram krishnasya yoginī

yayau vihāyasā rājan

dvārakām krishna-pālitām

SYNONYMS

citralekhā — Citralekhā; tam — Him; ājñāya — recognizing; pautram — as the grandson; krishnasya — of Lord Krishna; yoginī — female mystic; yayau — she went; vihāyasā — by the mystic skyways; rājan — O King; dvārakām — to Dvārakā; krishna-pālitām — protected by Krishna.

TRANSLATION

Citralekhā, endowed with mystic powers, recognized Him as Krishna's grandson [Aniruddha]. My dear King, she then traveled by the mystic skyway to Dvārakā, the city under Lord Krishna's protection.
