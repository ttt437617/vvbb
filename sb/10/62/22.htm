Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 62: The Meeting of Ūshā and Aniruddha

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.62.22

sā ca tam sundara-varam

vilokya muditānanā

dushprekshye sva-grihe pumbhī

reme prādyumninā samam

SYNONYMS

sā — she; ca — and; tam — Him; sundara-varam — the most beautiful man; vilokya — beholding; mudita — joyful; ānanā — her face; dushprekshye — which was not to be seen; sva — in her own; grihe — quarters; pumbhih — by men; reme — she enjoyed; pradyumninā samam — together with the son of Pradyumna.

TRANSLATION

When Ūshā beheld Him, the most beautiful of men, her face lit up with joy. She took the son of Pradyumna to her private quarters, which men were forbidden even to see, and there enjoyed with Him.
