Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 88: Lord Śiva Saved from Vrikāsura

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.88.34

yady asatyam vacah śambhoh

kathañcid dānavarshabha

tadainam jahy asad-vācam

na yad vaktānritam punah

SYNONYMS

yadi — if; asatyam — untrue; vacah — the words; śambhoh — of Lord Śiva; kathañcit — in any way; dānava-rishabha — O best of the demons; tadā — then; enam — him; jahi — please kill; asat — untrue; vācam — whose words; na — not; yat — so that; vaktā — he may speak; anritam — what is false; punah — again.

TRANSLATION

If the words of Lord Śambhu prove untrue in any way, O best of the demons, then kill the liar so he may never lie again.

PURPORT

Lord Śiva may have the power to revive himself even after being killed, but at least he will be dissuaded from lying again.
