Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 88: Lord Śiva Saved from Vrikāsura

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.88.37

mumucuh pushpa-varshāni

hate pāpe vrikāsure

devarshi-pitri-gandharvā

mocitah sańkatāc chivah

SYNONYMS

mumucuh — they released; pushpa — of flowers; varshāni — rain; hate — having been killed; pāpe — the sinful; vrika-asure — demon Vrika; deva-rishi — the celestial sages; pitri — departed forefathers; gandharvāh — and singers of heaven; mocitah — freed; sańkatāt — from danger; śivah — Lord Śiva.

TRANSLATION

The celestial sages, Pitās and Gandharvas rained down flowers to celebrate the killing of sinful Vrikāsura. Now Lord Śiva was out of danger.
