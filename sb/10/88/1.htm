Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 88: Lord Śiva Saved from Vrikāsura

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.88.1

śrī-rājovāca

devāsura-manushyesu

ye bhajanty aśivam śivam

prāyas te dhanino bhojā

na tu lakshmyāh patim harim

SYNONYMS

śrī-rājā uvāca — the King (Parīkshit) said; deva — among demigods; asura — demons; manushyeshu — and humans; ye — who; bhajanti — worship; aśivam — austere; śivam — Lord Śiva; prāyah — usually; te — they; dhaninah — rich; bhojāh — enjoyers of sense gratification; na — not; tu — however; lakshmyāh — of the goddess of fortune; patim — the husband; harim — Lord Hari.

TRANSLATION

King Parīkshit said: Those demigods, demons and humans who worship Lord Śiva, a strict renunciant, usually enjoy wealth and sense gratification, while the worshipers of the Supreme Lord Hari, the husband of the goddess of fortune, do not.
