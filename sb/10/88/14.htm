Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 88: Lord Śiva Saved from Vrikāsura

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.88.14

vriko nāmāsurah putrah

śakuneh pathi nāradam

drishtvāśu-tosham papraccha

deveshu trishu durmatih

SYNONYMS

vrikah — Vrika; nāma — by name; asurah — a demon; putrah — a son; śakuneh — of Śakuni; pathi — on the road; nāradam — the sage Nārada; drishtvā — seeing; āśu — quickly; tosham — pleased; papraccha — he asked about; deveshu — among the lords; trishu — three; durmatih — wicked.

TRANSLATION

The demon named Vrika, a son of Śakuni's, once met Nārada on the road. The wicked fellow asked him which of the three chief gods could be pleased most quickly.
