Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 88: Lord Śiva Saved from Vrikāsura

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.88.20

tam āha cāńgālam alam vrinīshva me

yathābhikāmam vitarāmi te varam

prīyeya toyena nrinām prapadyatām

aho tvayātmā bhriśam ardyate vrithā

SYNONYMS

tam — to him; āha — he (Lord Śiva) said; ca — and; ańga — my dear; alam alam — enough, enough; vrinīshva — please choose a benediction; me — from me; yathā — howsoever; abhikāmam — you desire; vitarāmi — I will bestow; te — to you; varam — your chosen boon; prīyeya — I become pleased; toyena — with water; nrinām — from persons; prapadyatām — who approach me for shelter; aho — ah; tvayā — by you; ātmā — your body; bhriśam — excessively; ardyate — tormented; vrithā — in vain.

TRANSLATION

Lord Śiva said to him: My friend, please stop, stop! Ask from me whatever you want, and I will bestow that boon upon you. Alas, you have subjected your body to great torment for no reason, since I am pleased with a simple offering of water from those who approach me for shelter.
