Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 89: Krishna and Arjuna Retrieve a Brāhmana's Sons

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.89.25

evam dvitīyam viprarshis

tritīyam tv evam eva ca

visrijya sa nripa-dvāri

tām gāthām samagāyata

SYNONYMS

evam — in the same manner; dvitīyam — a second time; vipra-rishih — the wise brāhmana; tritīyam — a third time; tu — and; evam eva ca — just the same way; visrijya — leaving (his dead son); sah — he; nripa-dvāri — at the King's door; tām — the same; gāthām — song; samagāyata — he sang.

TRANSLATION

The wise brāhmana suffered the same tragedy with his second and third child. Each time, he left the body of his dead son at the King's door and sang the same song of lamentation.
