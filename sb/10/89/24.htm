Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 89: Krishna and Arjuna Retrieve a Brāhmana's Sons

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.89.24

himsā-vihāram nripatim

duhśīlam ajitendriyam

prajā bhajantyah sīdanti

daridrā nitya-duhkhitāh

SYNONYMS

himsā — violence; vihāram — whose sport; nri-patim — this King; duhśīlam — wicked; ajita — unconquered; indriyam — whose senses; prajāh — the citizens; bhajantyah — serving; sīdanti — suffer distress; daridrāh — poverty-stricken; nitya — always; duhkhitāh — unhappy.

TRANSLATION

Citizens serving such a wicked king, who takes pleasure in violence and cannot control his senses, are doomed to suffer poverty and constant misery.
