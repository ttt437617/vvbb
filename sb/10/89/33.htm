Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 89: Krishna and Arjuna Retrieve a Brāhmana's Sons

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.89.33

māvamamsthā mama brahman

vīryam tryambaka-toshanam

mrityum vijitya pradhane

āneshye te prajāh prabho

SYNONYMS

mā avamamsthāh — do not belittle; mama — my; brahman — O brāhmana; vīryam — prowess; tri-ambaka — Lord Śiva; toshanam — which satisfied; mrityum — death personified; vijitya — defeating; pradhane — in battle; āneshye — I will bring back; te — your; prajāh — children; prabho — O master.

TRANSLATION

Do not minimize my ability, which was good enough to satisfy Lord Śiva, O brāhmana. I will bring back your sons, dear master, even if I have to defeat Death himself in battle.
