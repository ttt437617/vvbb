Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 57: Satrājit Murdered, the Jewel Returned

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.57.2

bhīshmam kripam sa viduram

gāndhārīm dronam eva ca

tulya-duhkhau ca sańgamya

hā kashtam iti hocatuh

SYNONYMS

bhīshmam — Bhīshma; kripam — Ācārya Kripa; sa-viduram — and also Vidura; gāndhārīm — Gāndhārī, the wife of Dhritarāshtra; dronam — Ācārya Drona; eva ca — as well; tulya — equally; duhkhau — sorrowful; ca — and; sańgamya — meeting with; hā — alas; kashtam — how painful; iti — thus; ha ūcatuh — They spoke.

TRANSLATION

The two Lords met with Bhīshma, Kripa, Vidura, Gāndhārī and Drona. Showing sorrow equal to theirs, They cried out, "Alas, how painful this is!"

PURPORT

Śrīla Śrīdhara Svāmī points out that those who were involved in the assassination attempt were of course not at all sorry to hear of the Pāndavas' death. The persons specifically mentioned here, however — Bhīshma, Kripa, Vidura, Gāndhārī and Drona — were actually unhappy to hear of the supposed tragedy.
