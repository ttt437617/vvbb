Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 57: Satrājit Murdered, the Jewel Returned

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.57.25

tam drishtvā sahasotthāya

maithilah prīta-mānasah

arhayām āsa vidhi-vad

arhanīyam samarhanaih

SYNONYMS

tam — Him, Lord Balarāma; drishtvā — seeing; sahasā — immediately; utthāya — rising; maithilah — the King of Mithilā; prīta-mānasah — feeling affection; arhayām āsa — he honored Him; vidhi-vat — in accordance with scriptural injunctions; arhanīyam — worshipable; samarhanaih — with elaborate paraphernalia of worship.

TRANSLATION

The King of Mithilā immediately rose from his seat when he saw Lord Balarāma approaching. With great love the King honored the supremely worshipable Lord by offering Him elaborate worship, as stipulated by scriptural injunctions.
