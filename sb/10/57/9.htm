Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 57: Satrājit Murdered, the Jewel Returned

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.57.9

tad ākarnyeśvarau rājann

anusritya nri-lokatām

aho nah paramam kashtam

ity asrākshau vilepatuh

SYNONYMS

tat — that; ākarnya — hearing; īśvarau — the two Lords; rājan — O King (Parīkshit); anusritya — imitating; nri-lokatām — the way of human society; aho — alas; nah — for Us; paramam — the greatest; kashtam — distress; iti — thus; asra — tearful; akshau — whose eyes; vilepatuh — They both lamented.

TRANSLATION

When Lord Krishna and Lord Balarāma heard this news, O King, They exclaimed, "Alas! This is the greatest tragedy for Us! "Thus imitating the ways of human society, They lamented, Their eyes brimming with tears.
