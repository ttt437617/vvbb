Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 57: Satrājit Murdered, the Jewel Returned

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.57.8

taila-dronyām mritam prāsya

jagāma gajasāhvayam

krishnāya viditārthāya

taptācakhyau pitur vadham

SYNONYMS

taila — of oil; dronyām — in a large vessel; mritam — the corpse; prāsya — putting; jagāma — she went; gaja-sāhvayam — to Hastināpura, the Kuru capital; krishnāya — to Krishna; vidita-arthāya — who already was aware of the situation; taptā — sorrowful; ācakhyau — she related; pituh — of her father; vadham — the killing.

TRANSLATION

Queen Satyabhāmā put her father's corpse in a large vat of oil and went to Hastināpura, where she sorrowfully told Lord Krishna, who was already aware of the situation, about her father's murder.
