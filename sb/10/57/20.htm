Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 57: Satrājit Murdered, the Jewel Returned

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.57.20

mithilāyām upavane

visrijya patitam hayam

padbhyām adhāvat santrastah

krishno 'py anvadravad rushā

SYNONYMS

mithilāyām — at Mithilā; upavane — in a suburban garden; visrijya — abandoning; patitam — fallen; hayam — his horse; padbhyām — on foot; adhāvat — he ran; santrastah — terrified; krishnah — Lord Krishna; api — also; anvadravat — ran after; rushā — furiously.

TRANSLATION

In a garden on the outskirts of Mithilā, the horse Śatadhanvā was riding collapsed. Terrified, he abandoned the horse and began to flee on foot, with Krishna in angry pursuit.
