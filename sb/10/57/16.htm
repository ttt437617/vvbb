Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 57: Satrājit Murdered, the Jewel Returned

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.57.16

yah sapta-hāyanah śailam

utpātyaikena pāninā

dadhāra līlayā bāla

ucchilīndhram ivārbhakah

SYNONYMS

yah — who; sapta — seven; hāyanah — years of age; śailam — a mountain; utpātya — uprooting; ekena — with a single; pāninā — hand; dadhāra — held up; līlayā — as play; bālah — a mere child; ucchilīndhram — a mushroom; iva — as; arbhakah — a boy.

TRANSLATION

"As a child of seven, Krishna uprooted an entire mountain and held it aloft as easily as a young boy picks up a mushroom.
