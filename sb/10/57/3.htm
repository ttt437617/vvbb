Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 57: Satrājit Murdered, the Jewel Returned

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.57.3

labdhvaitad antaram rājan

śatadhanvānam ūcatuh

akrūra-kritavarmānau

manih kasmān na grihyate

SYNONYMS

labdhvā — achieving; etat — this; antaram — opportunity; rājan — O King (Parīkshit); śatadhanvānam — to Śatadhanvā; ūcatuh — said; akrūra-kritavarmānau — Akrūra and Kritavarmā; manih — the jewel; kasmāt — why; na grihyate — should not be taken.

TRANSLATION

Taking advantage of this opportunity, O King, Akrūra and Kritavarmā went to Śatadhanvā and said, "Why not take the Syamantaka jewel?

PURPORT

Akrūra and Kritavarmā reasoned that since Krishna and Balarāma were absent from Dvārakā, Satrājit could be killed and the jewel stolen. Śrīla Śrīdhara Svāmī mentions that these two must have flattered Śatadhanvā telling him, "You are much braver than we are; so you kill him."
