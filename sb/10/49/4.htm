Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 49: Akrūra's Mission in Hastināpura

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.49.4

uvāsa katicin māsān

rājño vritta-vivitsayā

dushprajasyālpa-sārasya

khala-cchandānuvartinah

SYNONYMS

uvāsa — resided; katicit — some; māsān — months; rājñah — of the King (Dhritarāshtra); vritta — the activity; vivitsayā — with the desire of finding out; dushprajasya — whose sons were wicked; alpa — weak; sārasya — whose determination; khala — of mischievous persons (like Karna); chanda — the desires; anuvartinah — who tended to follow.

TRANSLATION

He remained in Hastināpura for several months to scrutinize the conduct of the weak-willed King, who had bad sons and who was inclined to give in to the whims of mischievous advisers.
