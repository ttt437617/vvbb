Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 49: Akrūra's Mission in Hastināpura

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.49.30

śrī-śuka uvāca

ity abhipretya nripater

abhiprāyam sa yādavah

suhridbhih samanujñātah

punar yadu-purīm agāt

SYNONYMS

śrī-śukah uvāca — Śukadeva Gosvāmī said; iti — thus; abhipretya — ascertaining; nripateh — of the King; abhiprāyam — the mentality; sah — he; yādavah — Akrūra, the descendant of King Yadu; suhridbhih — by his well-wishers; samanujñātah — given permission to leave; punah — again; yadu-purīm — to the city of the Yadu dynasty; agāt — went.

TRANSLATION

Śukadeva Gosvāmī said: Having thus apprised himself of the King's attitude, Akrūra, the descendant of Yadu, took permission from his well-wishing relatives and friends and returned to the capital of the Yādavas.
