Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 49: Akrūra's Mission in Hastināpura

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.49.18

dharmena pālayann urvīm

prajāh śīlena rañjayan

vartamānah samah sveshu

śreyah kīrtim avāpsyasi

SYNONYMS

dharmena — religiously; pālayan — protecting; urvīm — the earth; prajāh — the citizens; śīlena — by good character; rañjayan — delighting; vartamānah — remaining; samah — equally disposed; sveshu — to your relatives; śreyah — perfection; kīrtim — glory; avāpsyasi — you will achieve.

TRANSLATION

By religiously protecting the earth, delighting your subjects with your noble character, and treating all your relatives equally, you will surely achieve success and glory.

PURPORT

Akrūra told Dhritarāshtra that even though he had usurped the throne, if he now ruled according to the principles of dharma and behaved properly, he could be successful.
