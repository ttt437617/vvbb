Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 49: Akrūra's Mission in Hastināpura

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.49.26

dhritarāshtra uvāca

yathā vadati kalyānīm

vācam dāna-pate bhavān

tathānayā na tripyāmi

martyah prāpya yathāmritam

SYNONYMS

dhritarāshtrah uvāca — Dhritarāshtra said; yathā — as; vadati — speak; kalyānīm — auspicious; vācam — words; dāna — of charity; pate — O master; bhavān — you; tathā — so; anayā — by this; na tripyāmi — I am not satiated; martyah — a mortal; prāpya — attaining; yathā — as if; amritam — the nectar of immortality.

TRANSLATION

Dhritarāshtra said: O master of charity, I can never be satiated while hearing your auspicious words. Indeed, I am like a mortal who has obtained the nectar of the gods.

PURPORT

In the opinion of Śrīla Viśvanātha Cakravartī, Dhritarāshtra was in fact proud and felt he already knew everything Akrūra was speaking, but to maintain diplomatic gravity he spoke as a saintly gentleman.
