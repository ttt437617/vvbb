Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 67: Lord Balarāma Slays Dvivida Gorilla

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.67.7

purushān yoshito driptah

kshmābhrid-dronī-guhāsu sah

nikshipya cāpyadhāc chailaih

peśashkārīva kītakam

SYNONYMS

purushān — men; yoshitah — and women; driptah — audacious; kshmā-bhrit — of a mountain; dronī — within a valley; guhāsu — inside caves; sah — he; nikshipya — casting; ca — and; apyadhāt — sealed; śailaih — with large stones; peśashkārī — a wasp; iva — as; kītakam — a small insect.

TRANSLATION

Just as a wasp imprisons smaller insects, he arrogantly threw both men and women into caves in a mountain valley and sealed the caves shut with boulders.
