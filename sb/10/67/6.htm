Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 67: Lord Balarāma Slays Dvivida Gorilla

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.67.6

āśramān rishi-mukhyānām

kritvā bhagna-vanaspatīn

adūshayac chakrin-mūtrair

agnīn vaitānikān khalah

SYNONYMS

āśramān — the spiritual communities; rishi — of sages; mukhyānām — exalted; kritva — making; bhagna — broken; vanaspatīn — whose trees; adūshayat — he contaminated; śakrit — with stool; mūtraih — and urine; agnīn — the fires; vaitānikān — sacrificial; khalah — wicked.

TRANSLATION

The wicked ape tore down the trees in the hermitages of exalted sages and contaminated their sacrificial fires with his feces and urine.
