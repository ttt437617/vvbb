Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 67: Lord Balarāma Slays Dvivida Gorilla

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.67.12

tasya dhārshtyam kaper vīkshya

tarunyo jāti-cāpalāh

hāsya-priyā vijahasur

baladeva-parigrahāh

SYNONYMS

tasya — of him; dhārshtyam — the impudence; kapeh — of the ape; vīkshya — seeing; tarunyah — the young women; jāti — by nature; cāpalāh — not serious; hāsya-priyāh — fond of laughter; vijahasuh — laughed loudly; baladeva-parigrahāh — the consorts of Lord Baladeva.

TRANSLATION

When Lord Baladeva's consorts saw the ape's impudence, they began to laugh. They were, after all, young girls who were fond of joking and prone to silliness.
