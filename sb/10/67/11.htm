Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 67: Lord Balarāma Slays Dvivida Gorilla

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.67.11

dushtah śākhā-mrigah śākhām

ārūdhah kampayan drumān

cakre kilakilā-śabdam

ātmānam sampradarśayan

SYNONYMS

dushtah — mischievous; śākhā-mrigah — the ape ("the animal who lives on branches"); śākhām — a branch; ārūdhah — having climbed; kampayan — shaking; drumān — trees; cakre — he made; kilakilā-śabdam — the sound kilakilā; ātmānam — himself; sampradarśayan — showing.

TRANSLATION

The mischievous ape climbed a tree branch and then revealed his presence by shaking the trees and making the sound kilakilā.

PURPORT

The word śākhā-mriga indicates that the ape Dvivida, like ordinary apes, was naturally inclined to climb trees. Śrīla Prabhupāda writes, "This gorilla by the name Dvivida could climb up on the trees and jump from one branch to another. Sometimes he would jerk the branches, creating a particular type of sound — kilakilā — so that Lord Balarāma was greatly distracted from the pleasing atmosphere."
