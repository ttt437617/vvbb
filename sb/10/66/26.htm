Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 66: Paundraka, the False Vasudeva

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.66.26

rājñah kāśī-pater jñātvā

mahishyah putra-bāndhavāh

paurāś ca hā hatā rājan

nātha nātheti prārudan

SYNONYMS

rājñah — of the King; kāśī-pateh — the lord of Kāśī; jñātvā — recognizing; mahishyah — his queens; putra — his sons; bāndhavāh — and other relatives; paurāh — the citizens of the city; ca — and; hā — alas; hatāh — (we are) killed; rājan — O King (Parīkshit); nātha nātha — O master, master; iti — thus; prārudan — they cried out loud.

TRANSLATION

My dear King, when they recognized it as the head of their King — the lord of Kāśi — his queens, sons and other relatives, along with all the citizens of the city, began to cry pitifully: "Alas, we are killed! O my lord, my lord!"
