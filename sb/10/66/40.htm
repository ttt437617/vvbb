Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 66: Paundraka, the False Vasudeva

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.66.40

krityānalah pratihatah sa rathānga-pāner

astraujasā sa nripa bhagna-mukho nivrittah

vārānasīm parisametya sudakshinam tam

sartvig-janam samadahat sva-krito 'bhicārah

SYNONYMS

krityā — produced by mystic power; analah — the fire; pratihatah — frustrated; sah — he; ratha-ańga-pāneh — of Lord Krishna, Who holds the Sudarśana disc in His hand; astra — of the weapon; ojasā — by the power; sah — he; nripa — O King; bhagna-mukhah — turning away; nivrittah — having desisted; vārānasīm — the city of Vārānasī; parisametya — approaching on all sides; sudakshinam — Sudakshina; tam — him; sa — together with; ritvik-janam — his priests; samadahat — burned to death; sva — by himself (Sudakshina); kritah — created; abhicārah — meant for doing violence.

TRANSLATION

Frustrated by the power of Lord Krishna's weapon, O King, the fiery creature produced by black magic turned his face away and retreated. Created for violence, the demon then returned to Vārānasī, where he surrounded the city and then burned Sudakshina and his priests to death, even though Sudakshina was his creator.

PURPORT

Śrīla Prabhupāda comments as follows: "Having failed to set fire to Dvārakā, [the fiery demon] went back to Vārānasī, the kingdom of Kāśirāja. As a result of his return, all the priests who had helped instruct the black art of mantras, along with their employer, Sudakshina, were burned into ashes by the glaring effulgence of the fiery demon. According to the methods of black-art mantras instructed in the tantra, if the mantra fails to kill the enemy, then, because it must kill someone, it kills the original creator. Sudakshina was the originator, and the priests assisted him; therefore all of them were burned to ashes. This is the way of the demons: the demons create something to kill God, but by the same weapon the demons themselves are killed."
