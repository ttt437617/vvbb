Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 66: Paundraka, the False Vasudeva

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.66.34

padbhyām tāla-pramānābhyām

kampayann avanī-talam

so 'bhyadhāvad vrito bhūtair

dvārakām pradahan diśah

SYNONYMS

padbhyām — with his legs; tāla — of palm trees; pramānābhyām — whose measure; kampayan — shaking; avanī — of the earth; talam — the surface; sah — he; abhyadhāvat — ran; vritah — accompanied; bhūtaih — by ghostly spirits; dvārakām — toward Dvārakā; pradahan — burning up; diśah — the directions.

TRANSLATION

On legs as tall as palm trees, the monster raced toward Dvārakā in the company of ghostly spirits, shaking the ground and burning the world in all directions.
