Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 66: Paundraka, the False Vasudeva

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.66.30-31

dakshināgnim paricara

brāhmanaih samam ritvijam

abhicāra-vidhānena

sa cāgnih pramathair vritah

sādhayishyati sańkalpam

abrahmanye prayojitah

ity ādishtas tathā cakre

krishnāyābhicaran vratī

SYNONYMS

dakshina-agnim — to the Dakshina fire; paricara — you should render service; brāhmanaih — brāhmanas; samam — together with; ritvijam — the original priest; abhicāra-vidhānena — with the ritual known as abhicāra (meant for killing or otherwise harming an enemy); sah — that; ca — and; agnih — fire; pramathaih — by the Pramathas (powerful mystics who are in Lord Śiva's retinue and who assume many different forms); vritah — surrounded; sādhayishyati — it will accomplish; sańkalpam — your intention; abrahmanye — against one who is inimical to brāhmanas; prayojitah — utilized; iti — so; ādishtah — instructed; tathā — in that way; cakre — he did; krishnāya — against Lord Krishna; abhicaran — intending to do harm; vratī — observing the required vows.

TRANSLATION

Lord Śiva told him, "Accompanied by brāhmanas, serve the Dakshināgni fire — the original priest — following the injunctions of the abhicāra ritual. Then the Dakshināgni fire, together with many Pramathas, will fulfill your desire if you direct it against someone inimical to the brāhmanas." So instructed, Sudakshina strictly observed the ritualistic vows and invoked the abhicāra against Lord Krishna.

PURPORT

It is clearly stated here that the powerful Dakshināgni fire could be directed only against someone unfavorable to brahminical culture. Lord Krishna, however, is most favorable to the brāhmanas and in fact maintains the brahminical culture. Lord Śiva thus knew that if Sudakshina attempted to direct the power of this ritual against Lord Krishna, Sudakshina himself would perish.
