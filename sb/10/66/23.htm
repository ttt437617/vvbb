Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 66: Paundraka, the False Vasudeva

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.66.23

evam matsarinam hatvā

paundrakam sa-sakham harih

dvārakām āviśat siddhair

gīyamāna-kathāmritah

SYNONYMS

evam — thus; matsarinam — envious; hatvā — killing; paundrakam — Paundraka; sa — together with; sakham — his friend; harih — Lord Krishna; dvārakām — Dvārakā; āviśat — He entered; siddhaih — by the mystics of heaven; gīyamāna — being sung; kathā — narrations about Him; amritah — nectarean.

TRANSLATION

Having thus killed envious Paundraka and his ally, Lord Krishna returned to Dvārakā. As He entered the city, the Siddhas of heaven chanted His immortal, nectarean glories.
