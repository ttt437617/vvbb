Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 66: Paundraka, the False Vasudeva

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.66.21

iti kshiptvā śitair bānair

virathī-kritya paundrakam

śiro 'vriścad rathāńgena

vajrenendro yathā gireh

SYNONYMS

iti — with these words; kshiptvā — deriding; śitaih — sharp; bānaih — with His arrows; virathī — chariotless; kritya — making; paundrakam — Paundraka; śirah — his head; avriścat — He cut off; ratha-ańgena — with His Sudarśana disc; vajrena — with his thunderbolt weapon; indrah — Lord Indra; yathā — as; gireh — of a mountain.

TRANSLATION

Having thus derided Paundraka, Lord Krishna destroyed his chariot with His sharp arrows. The Lord then cut off his head with the Sudarśana disc, just as Lord Indra lops off a mountain peak with his thunderbolt weapon.
