Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 66: Paundraka, the False Vasudeva

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.66.43

ya enam śrāvayen martya

uttamah-śloka-vikramam

samāhito vā śrinuyāt

sarva-pāpaih pramucyate

SYNONYMS

yah — one who; enam — this; śrāvayet — causes others to hear; martyah — a mortal human; uttamah-śloka — of Lord Krishna, who is praised in the best transcendental verses; vikramam — the heroic pastime; samāhitah — with concentration; vā — or; śrinuyāt — hears; sarva — from all; pāpaih — sins; pramucyate — becomes released.

TRANSLATION

Any mortal who recounts this heroic pastime of Lord Uttamah-śloka's, or who simply hears it attentively, will become freed from all sins.

Thus end the purports of the humble servants of His Divine Grace A. C. Bhaktivedanta Swami Prabhupāda to the Tenth Canto, Sixty-sixth Chapter, of the Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam, entitled "Paundraka, the False Vāsudeva."
