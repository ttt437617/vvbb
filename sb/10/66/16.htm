Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 66: Paundraka, the False Vasudeva

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.66.16

śulair gadābhih parighaih

śakty-rishti-prāsa-tomaraih

asibhih pattiśair bānaih

prāharann arayo harim

SYNONYMS

śūlaih — with tridents; gadābhih — clubs; parighaih — and bludgeons; śakti — pikes; rishti — a kind of sword; prāsa — long, barbed darts; tomaraih — and lances; asibhih — with swords; pattiśaih — with axes; bānaih — and with arrows; prāharan — attacked; arayah — the enemies; harim — Lord Krishna.

TRANSLATION

The enemies of Lord Hari attacked Him with tridents, clubs, bludgeons, pikes, rishtis, barbed darts, lances, swords, axes and arrows.
