Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 72: The Slaying of the Demon Jarāsandha

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.72.41

sañcintyārī-vadhopāyam

bhīmasyāmogha-darśanah

darśayām āsa vitapam

pātayann iva samjñayā

SYNONYMS

sañcintya — having thought; ari — their enemy; vadha — for killing; upāyam — about the means; bhīmasya — to Bhīma; amogha-darśanah — the Supreme Lord, whose vision is infallible; darśayām āsa — showed; vitapam — a tree branch; pātayan — tearing apart; iva — as if; samjñayā — as a sign.

TRANSLATION

Having determined how to kill the enemy, that Lord of infallible vision made a sign to Bhīma by tearing in half a small branch of a tree.
