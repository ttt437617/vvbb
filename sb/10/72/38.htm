Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 72: The Slaying of the Demon Jarāsandha

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.72.38

ittham tayoh prahatayor gadayor nri-vīrau

kruddhau sva-mushtibhir ayah-sparaśair apishtām

śabdas tayoh praharator ibhayor ivāsīn

nirghāta-vajra-parushas tala-tādanotthah

SYNONYMS

ittham — in this manner; tayoh — their; prahatayoh — being ruined; gadayoh — the clubs; nr — among human beings; vīrau — the two great heroes; kruddhau — angry; sva — their own; mushtibhih — with the fists; ayah — like iron; sparaśaih — whose touch; apishtām — they battered; śabdah — the sound; tayoh — of them; praharatoh — striking; ibhayoh — of two elephants; iva — as; āsīt — became; nirghāta — crashing; vajra — like thunder; parushah — harsh; tala — of their palms; tādana — by the hitting; utthah — raised.

TRANSLATION

Their clubs thus ruined, those great heroes among men angrily pummeled each other with their iron-hard fists. As they slapped each other, the sound resembled the crash of elephants colliding or harsh thunderclaps.
