Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 72: The Slaying of the Demon Jarāsandha

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.72.43

ekam pādam padākramya

dorbhyām anyam pragrihya sah

gudatah pātayām āsa

śākham iva mahā-gajah

SYNONYMS

ekam — one; pādam — leg; padā — with his foot; ākramya — standing on top of; dorbhyām — with his two hands; anyam — the other; pragrihya — taking hold of; sah — he; gudatah — beginning from the anus; pātayām āsa — tore him asunder; śākhām — a tree branch; iva — as; mahā — great; gajah — an elephant.

TRANSLATION

Bhīma pressed down on one leg with his foot while grabbing Jarāsandha's other leg in his hands, and just as a great elephant might break the branch of a tree, Bhīma tore Jarāsandha apart from the anus upward.
