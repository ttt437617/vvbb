Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 72: The Slaying of the Demon Jarāsandha

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.72.15

śrutvājitam jarāsandham

nripater dhyāyato harih

āhopāyam tam evādya

uddhavo yam uvāca ha

SYNONYMS

śrutvā — hearing; ajitam — unconquered; jarāsandham — Jarāsandha; nripateh — the King; dhyāyatah — as he pondered; harih — Lord Krishna; āha — told; upāyam — the means; tam — to him; eva — indeed; ādyah — the original person; uddhavah — Uddhava; yam — which; uvāca ha — had spoken.

TRANSLATION

When King Yudhishthira heard that Jarāsandha remained undefeated, he set to pondering, and then the primeval Lord, Hari, told him the means Uddhava had described for defeating Jarāsandha.
