Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 47: The Song of the Bee

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.47.52

he nātha he ramā-nātha

vraja-nāthārti-nāśana

magnam uddhara govinda

gokulam vrijinārnavāt

SYNONYMS

he nātha — O master; he ramā-nātha — O master of the goddess of fortune; vraja-nātha — O master of the cowherd village; ārti — of suffering; nāśana — O destroyer; magnam — submerged; uddhara — uplift; govinda — O Govinda; gokulam — Gokula; vrijina — of distress; arnavāt — from the ocean.

TRANSLATION

O master, O master of the goddess of fortune, O master of Vraja ! O destroyer of all suffering, Govinda, please lift Your Gokula out of the ocean of distress in which it is drowning!

PURPORT

Śrīla Viśvanātha Cakravartī presents the following insight into this scene: Someone might propose to the gopīs, "Why don't you just go somewhere else? Leave Vrindāvana, and then you won't have to see these rivers, mountains and forests. Cover your eyes with your garments, use your intelligence to lead your minds to some other thought, and thus forget Krishna." The gopīs answer this suggestion in the previous verse by stating, "We no longer possess our intelligence, for Krishna has taken it away by His supreme beauty and charm."

Now in the present verse the feelings of the gopīs become so strong that they disregard Uddhava and, turning toward Mathurā, address Krishna Himself with humble cries. They address Krishna as Vrajanātha because in the past young Krishna performed many inconceivable pastimes to protect His beloved village people, such as lifting Govardhana Hill and destroying many monstrous demons. In this heartrending verse, the gopīs cry out to Krishna to remember the wonderful, sweet relationship they once enjoyed together as innocent village people. Indeed, Śrī Krishna would lovingly take care of His father's cows, and the gopīs appealed to Him to remember these duties and return so He could resume them.
