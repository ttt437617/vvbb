Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 17: The History of Kāliya

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.17.4

visha-vīrya-madāvishtah

kādraveyas tu kāliyah

kadarthī-kritya garudam

svayam tam bubhuje balim

SYNONYMS

visha — because of his poison; vīrya — and his strength; mada — in intoxication; āvishtah — absorbed; kādraveyah — the son of Kadru; tu — on the other hand; kāliyah — Kāliya; kadarthī-kritya — disregarding; garudam — Garuda; svayam — himself; tam — that; bubhuje — ate; balim — the offering.

TRANSLATION

Although all the other serpents were dutifully making offerings to Garuda, one serpent — the arrogant Kāliya, son of Kadru — would eat all these offerings before Garuda could claim them. Thus Kāliya directly defied the carrier of Lord Vishnu.
