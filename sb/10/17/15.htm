Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 17: The History of Kāliya

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.17.15

yaśodā rohinī nando

gopyo gopāś ca kaurava

krishnam sametya labdhehā

āsan śushkā nagā api

SYNONYMS

yaśodā rohinī nandah — Yaśodā, Rohinī and Nanda Mahārāja; gopyah — the cowherd ladies; gopāh — the cowherd men; ca — and; kaurava — O Parīkshit, descendant of Kuru; krishnam — Lord Krishna; sametya — meeting; labdha — having regained; īhāh — their conscious functions; āsan — they became; śushkāh — dried up; nagāh — the trees; api — even.

TRANSLATION

Having regained their vital functions, Yaśodā, Rohinī, Nanda and all the other cowherd women and men went up to Krishna. O descendant of Kuru, even the dried-up trees came back to life.
