Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 17: The History of Kāliya

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.17.7

tam tārkshya-putrah sa nirasya manyumān

pracanda-vego madhusūdanāsanah

pakshena savyena hiranya-rocishā

jaghāna kadru-sutam ugra-vikramah

SYNONYMS

tam — him, Kāliya; tārkshya-putrah — the son of Kaśyapa; sah — he, Garuda; nirasya — warding off; manyu-mān — full of anger; pracanda-vegah — moving with terrible swiftness; madhusūdana-āsanah — the carrier of Lord Madhusūdana, Krishna; pakshena — with his wing; savyena — left; hiranya — like gold; rocishā — the effulgence of which; jaghāna — he struck; kadru-sutam — the son of Kadru (Kāliya); ugra — mighty; vikramah — his prowess.

TRANSLATION

The angry son of Tārkshya moved with overwhelming speed in repelling Kāliya's attack. That terribly powerful carrier of Lord Madhusūdana struck the son of Kadru with his left wing, which shone like gold.
