Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 17: The History of Kāliya

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.17.12

tat kāliyah param veda

nānyah kaścana lelihah

avātsīd garudād bhītah

krishnena ca vivāsitah

SYNONYMS

tam — that; kāliyah — Kāliya; param — only; veda — knew; na — not; anyah — other; kaścana — any; lelihah — serpent; avātsīt — he dwelt; garudāt — of Garuda; bhītah — afraid; krishnena — by Krishna; ca — and; vivāsitah — expelled.

TRANSLATION

Of all the serpents, only Kāliya came to know of this affair, and in fear of Garuda he took up residence in that Yamunā lake. Later Lord Krishna drove him out.
