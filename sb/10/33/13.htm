Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 33: The Rāsa Dance

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.33.13

nrityatī gāyatī kācit

kūjan nūpura-mekhalā

pārśva-sthācyuta-hastābjam

śrāntādhāt stanayoh śivam

SYNONYMS

nrityatī — dancing; gāyatī — singing; kācit — a certain gopī; kūjan — murmuring; nūpura — her ankle bells; mekhalā — and her belt; pārśva-stha — standing at her side; acyuta — of Lord Krishna; hasta-abjam — the lotus hand; śrāntā — feeling tired; adhāt — placed; stanayoh — upon her breasts; śivam — pleasing.

TRANSLATION

Another gopī became fatigued as she danced and sang, the bells on her ankles and waist tinkling. So she placed upon her breasts the comforting lotus hand of Lord Acyuta, who was standing by her side.
