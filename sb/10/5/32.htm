Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 5: The Meeting of Nanda Mahārāja and Vasudeva

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.5.32

śrī-śuka uvāca

iti nandādayo gopāh

proktās te śaurinā yayuh

anobhir anadud-yuktais

tam anujñāpya gokulam

SYNONYMS

śrī-śukah uvāca — Śrī Śukadeva Gosvāmī said; iti — thus; nanda-ādayah — Nanda Mahārāja and his companions; gopāh — the cowherd men; proktāh — being advised; te — they; śaurinā — by Vasudeva; yayuh — started from that place; anobhih — by the bullock carts; anadut-yuktaih — yoked with oxen; tam anujñāpya — taking permission from Vasudeva; gokulam — for Gokula.

TRANSLATION

Śukadeva Gosvāmī said: After Vasudeva advised Nanda Mahārāja in this way, Nanda Mahārāja and his associates, the cowherd men, took permission from Vasudeva, yoked their bulls to the bullock carts, and started riding for Gokula.

Thus end the Bhaktivedanta purports to the Tenth Canto, Fifth Chapter, of the Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam, entitled "The Meeting of Nanda Mahārāja and Vasudeva."
