Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 5: The Meeting of Nanda Mahārāja and Vasudeva

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.5.28

pumsas tri-vargo vihitah

suhrido hy anubhāvitah

na teshu kliśyamāneshu

tri-vargo 'rthāya kalpate

SYNONYMS

pumsah — of a person; tri-vargah — the three aims of life (religion, economic development and sense gratification); vihitah — enjoined according to Vedic ritualistic ceremonies; suhridah — toward relatives and friends; hi — indeed; anubhāvitah — when they are properly in line; na — not; teshu — in them; kliśyamāneshu — if they are actually in any difficulty; tri-vargah — these three aims of life; arthāya — for any purpose; kalpate — does become so.

TRANSLATION

When one's friends and relatives are properly situated, one's religion, economic development and sense gratification, as described in the Vedic literatures, are beneficial. Otherwise, if one's friends and relatives are in distress, these three cannot offer any happiness.

PURPORT

Vasudeva regretfully informed Nanda Mahārāja that although he had his wife and children, he could not properly discharge his duty of maintaining them and was therefore unhappy.
