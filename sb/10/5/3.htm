Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 5: The Meeting of Nanda Mahārāja and Vasudeva

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.5.3

dhenūnām niyute prādād

viprebhyah samalańkrite

tilādrīn sapta ratnaugha-

śātakaumbhāmbarāvritān

SYNONYMS

dhenūnām — of milk-giving cows; niyute — two million; prādāt — gave in charity; viprebhyah — unto the brāhmanas; samalańkrite — completely decorated; tila-adrīn — hills of grain; sapta — seven; ratna-ogha-śāta-kaumbha-ambara-āvritān — covered with jewels and cloth embroidered with gold.

TRANSLATION

Nanda Mahārāja gave two million cows, completely decorated with cloth and jewels, in charity to the brāhmanas. He also gave them seven hills of grain, covered with jewels and with cloth decorated with golden embroidery.
