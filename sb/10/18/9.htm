Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 18: Lord Balarāma Slays the Demon Pralamba

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.18.9

pravāla-barha-stabaka-

srag-dhātu-krita-bhūshanāh

rāma-krishnādayo gopā

nanritur yuyudhur jaguh

SYNONYMS

pravāla — newly grown leaves; barha — peacock feathers; stabaka — bunches of small flowers; srak — garlands; dhātu — and colored minerals; krita-bhūshanāh — wearing as their ornaments; rāma-krishna-ādayah — headed by Lord Balarāma and Lord Krishna; gopāh — the cowherd boys; nanrituh — danced; yuyudhuh — fought; jaguh — sang.

TRANSLATION

Decorating themselves with newly grown leaves, along with peacock feathers, garlands, clusters of flower buds, and colored minerals, Balarāma, Krishna and Their cowherd friends danced, wrestled and sang.
