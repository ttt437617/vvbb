Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 18: Lord Balarāma Slays the Demon Pralamba

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.18.3

sa ca vrindāvana-gunair

vasanta iva lakshitah

yatrāste bhagavān sākshād

rāmena saha keśavah

SYNONYMS

sah — this (hot season); ca — nevertheless; vrindāvana — of Śrī Vrindāvana; gunaih — by the transcendental qualities; vasantah — springtime; iva — as if; lakshitah — manifesting symptoms; yatra — in which (Vrindāvana); āste — remains; bhagavān — the Supreme Personality of Godhead; sākshāt — personally; rāmena saha — together with Lord Balarāma; keśavah — Lord Śrī Krishna.

TRANSLATION

Nevertheless, because the Supreme Personality of Godhead was personally staying in Vrindāvana along with Balarāma, summer manifested the qualities of spring. Such are the features of the land of Vrindāvana.
