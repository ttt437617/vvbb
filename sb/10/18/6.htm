Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 18: Lord Balarāma Slays the Demon Pralamba

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.18.6

agādha-toya-hradinī-tatormibhir

dravat-purīshyāh pulinaih samantatah

na yatra candāmśu-karā visholbanā

bhuvo rasam śādvalitam ca grihnate

SYNONYMS

agādha — very deep; toya — whose water; hradinī — of the rivers; tata — upon the shores; ūrmibhih — by the waves; dravat — liquefied; purīshyāh — whose mud; pulinaih — by the sandy banks; samantatah — on all sides; na — not; yatra — upon which; canda — of the sun; amśu-karāh — the rays; visha — like poison; ulbanāh — fierce; bhuvah — of the earth; rasam — the juice; śādvalitam — the greenness; ca — and; grihnate — take away.

TRANSLATION

With their flowing waves the deep rivers drenched their banks, making them damp and muddy. Thus the rays of the sun, which were as fierce as poison, could not evaporate the earth's sap or parch its green grass.
