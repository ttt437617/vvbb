Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 18: Lord Balarāma Slays the Demon Pralamba

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.18.15

kvacic ca dardura-plāvair

vividhair upahāsakaih

kadācit syandolikayā

karhicin nripa-ceshtayā

SYNONYMS

kvacit — sometimes; ca — and; dardura — like frogs; plāvaih — with jumping; vividhaih — various; upahāsakaih — with jokes; kadācit — sometimes; syandolikayā — with riding in swings; karhicit — and sometimes; nripa-ceshtayā — with pretending to be kings.

TRANSLATION

They would sometimes jump around like frogs, sometimes play various jokes, sometimes ride in swings and sometimes imitate monarchs.

PURPORT

Śrīla Viśvanātha Cakravartī Thākura explains the word nripa-ceshtayā as follows: In Vrindāvana there was a particular place on the riverbank where people who wanted to cross the Yamunā would pay a small tax. At times the cowherd boys would assemble in this area and prevent the young girls of Vrindāvana from crossing the river, insisting that they had to pay a customs duty first. Such activities were full of joking and laughter.
