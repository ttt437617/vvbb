Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 18: Lord Balarāma Slays the Demon Pralamba

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.18.16

evam tau loka-siddhābhih

krīdābhiś ceratur vane

nady-adri-droni-kuñjeshu

kānaneshu sarahsu ca

SYNONYMS

evam — in this way; tau — the two of Them, Krishna and Balarāma; loka-siddhābhih — which are well known in human society; krīdābhih — with games; ceratuh — They wandered; vane — in the forest; nadī — among the rivers; adri — mountains; droni — valleys; kuñjeshu — and groves; kānaneshu — in the smaller forests; sarahsu — along the lakes; ca — and.

TRANSLATION

In this way Krishna and Balarāma played all sorts of well-known games as They wandered among the rivers, hills, valleys, bushes, trees and lakes of Vrindāvana.
