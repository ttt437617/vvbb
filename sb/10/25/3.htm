Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 25: Lord Krishna Lifts Govardhana Hill

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.25.3

aho śrī-mada-māhātmyam

gopānām kānanaukasām

krishnam martyam upāśritya

ye cakrur deva-helanam

SYNONYMS

aho — just see; śrī — because of opulence; mada — of intoxication; māhātmyam — the great extent; gopānām — of the cowherds; kānana — in the forest; okasām — who dwell; krishnam — Krishna; martyam — an ordinary human; upāśritya — taking shelter of; ye — who; cakruh — have committed; deva — against the demigods; helanam — offense.

TRANSLATION

[Indra said:] Just see how these cowherd men living in the forest have become so greatly intoxicated by their prosperity! They have surrendered to an ordinary human being, Krishna, and thus they have offended the gods.

PURPORT

Of course, Indra was really saying that the cowherd men had offended him, Indra, by taking shelter of Krishna, whom Indra considered to be martya, a mortal. This was certainly a gross miscalculation on Indra's part.
