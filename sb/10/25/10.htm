Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 25: Lord Krishna Lifts Govardhana Hill

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.25.10

sthūnā-sthūlā varsha-dhārā

muñcatsv abhreshv abhīkshnaśah

jalaughaih plāvyamānā bhūr

nādriśyata natonnatam

SYNONYMS

sthūnā — like columns; sthūlāh — massive; varsa-dhārāh — downpours of rain; muñcatsu — releasing; abhreshu — the clouds; abhīkshnaśah — incessantly; jala-oghaih — by the flood of water; plāvyamānā — being submerged; bhūh — the earth; na adriśyata — could not be seen; nataunnatam — low or high.

TRANSLATION

As the clouds released torrents of rain as thick as massive columns, the earth was submerged in the flood, and high ground could no longer be distinguished from low.
