Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 36: The Slaying of Arista, the Bull Demon

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.36.19

nivārito nāradena

tat-sutau mrityum ātmanah

jñātvā loha-mayaih pāśair

babandha saha bhāryayā

SYNONYMS

nivāritah — checked; nāradena — by Nārada; tat-sutau — his two sons; mrityum — death; ātmanah — his own; jñātvā — understanding; loha-mayaih — made of iron; pāśaih — with shackles; babandha — he bound up (Vasudeva); saha — together with; bhāryayā — his wife.

TRANSLATION

But Nārada restrained Kamsa by reminding him that it was the two sons of Vasudeva who would cause his death. Kamsa then had Vasudeva and his wife shackled in iron chains.

PURPORT

Kamsa realized that there was no use in killing Vasudeva, since it was Vasudeva's sons, Krishna and Balarāma, who were to kill him. According to the ācāryas, Nārada also advised Kamsa that if he killed Vasudeva the two young boys might flee and that it was therefore better not to kill him. Rather, Nārada recommended, Kamsa should bring Krishna and Balarāma to Kamsa's capital city, Mathurā.

Śrīla Viśvanātha Cakravartī points out that Nārada did not act inimically toward the great devotees Vasudeva and Devakī when he revealed this information to Kamsa. In fact, as explained in the Eleventh Canto, Vasudeva was grateful to Nārada because he was arranging for Kamsa's death at Krishna's hands, and further arranging for Krishna to come and live in Mathurā, where His loving father could associate with Him.
