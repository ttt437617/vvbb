Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 36: The Slaying of Arista, the Bull Demon

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.36.20

pratiyāte tu devarshau

kamsa ābhāshya keśinam

preshayām āsa hanyetām

bhavatā rāma-keśavau

SYNONYMS

pratiyāte — having left; tu — then; deva-rishau — the sage among the demigods; kamsah — King Kamsa; ābhāshya — addressing; keśinam — the demon Keśī; preshayām āsa — he sent him; hanyetām — the two should be killed; bhavatā — by you; rāma-keśavau — Balarāma and Krishna.

TRANSLATION

After Nārada left, King Kamsa summoned Keśī and ordered him, "Go kill Rāma and Krishna."

PURPORT

Before having Krishna and Balarāma brought to Mathurā, Kamsa tried sending one more demon to Vrindāvana.
