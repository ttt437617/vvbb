Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 36: The Slaying of Arista, the Bull Demon

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.36.15

evam kukudminam hatvā

stūyamānah dvijātibhih

viveśa goshtham sa-balo

gopīnām nayanotsavah

SYNONYMS

evam — thus; kukudminam — the humped (bull demon); hatvā — killing; stūyamānah — being praised; dvijātibhih — by the brāhmanas; viveśa — He entered; goshtham — the cowherd village; sa-balah — together with Lord Balarāma; gopīnām — of the gopīs; nayana — for the eyes; utsavah — who is a festival.

TRANSLATION

Having thus killed the bull demon Arishta, He who is a festival for the gopīs' eyes entered the cowherd village with Balarāma.

PURPORT

This verse exemplifies the sublime contrast of spiritual qualities within Śrī Krishna. In one four-line verse we simultaneously learn that Lord Krishna killed a powerful and wicked demon and that His boyish beauty gave festive pleasure to His young girlfriends. Lord Krishna is as hard as a thunderbolt or as soft as a rose, depending on our attitude toward Him. The demon Arishta wanted to kill Krishna and all His friends, so the Lord beat him into a wet rag and killed him. The gopīs, however, loved Krishna, and thus the Lord boyishly reciprocated their conjugal feelings.
