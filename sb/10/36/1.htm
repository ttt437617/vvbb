Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 36: The Slaying of Arista, the Bull Demon

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.36.1

śrī bādarāyanir uvāca

atha tarhy āgato goshtham

arishto vrishabhāsurah

mahīm mahā-kakut-kāyah

kampayan khura-vikshatām

SYNONYMS

śrī bādarāyanih uvāca — Śrī Śukadeva Gosvāmī said; atha — next; tarhi — then; āgatah — came; goshtham — to the cowherd village; arishtah — named Arishta; vrishabha-asurah — the bull demon; mahīm — the earth; mahā — great; kakut — having a hump; kāyah — whose body; kampayan — making tremble; khura — by his hooves; vikshatām — torn.

TRANSLATION

Śukadeva Goswāmī said: The demon Arishta then came to the cowherd village. Appearing in the form of a bull with a large hump, he made the earth tremble as he tore it apart with his hooves.

PURPORT

According to the Śrī Vishnu Purāna, Arishtāsura entered Krishna's village at twilight, as the Lord prepared to dance with the gopīs:

prodoshārdhe kadācit tu

rāsāsakte janārdane

trāsayan sa-mado goshtham

arishtah sampāgatah

"Once, midway through the period of dusk, when Lord Janārdana was eager to perform the rāsa dance, Arishtāsura madly entered the cowherd village, terrifying everyone."
