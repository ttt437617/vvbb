Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 36: The Slaying of Arista, the Bull Demon

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.36.22-23

bho bho niśamyatām etad

vīra-cānūra-mushtikau

nanda-vraje kilāsāte

sutāv ānakadundubheh

rāma-krishnau tato mahyam

mrityuh kila nidarśitah

bhavadbhyām iha samprāptau

hanyetām malla-līlayā

SYNONYMS

bhoh bhoh — my dear (advisers); niśamyatām — please listen; etat — to this; vīra — O heroes; cānūra-mushtikau — Cānūra and Mushtika; nanda-vraje — in the cowherd village of Nanda; kila — indeed; āsāte — are living; sutau — the two sons; ānakadundubheh — of Vasudeva; rāma-krishnau — Rāma and Krishna; tatah — from Them; mahyam — my; mrityuh — death; kila — indeed; nidarśitah — has been indicated; bhavadbhyām — by you two; iha — here; samprāptau — brought; hanyetām — They should be killed; malla — of wrestling; līlayā — on the pretext of the sport.

TRANSLATION

My dear heroic Cānūra and Mushtika, please hear this. Rāma and Krishna, the sons of Ānakadundubhi [Vasudeva], are living in Nanda's cowherd village. It has been predicted that these two boys will be the cause of my death. When They are brought here, kill Them on the pretext of engaging Them in a wrestling match.
