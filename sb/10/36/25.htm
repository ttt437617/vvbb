Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 36: The Slaying of Arista, the Bull Demon

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.36.25

mahāmātra tvayā bhadra

rańga-dvāry upanīyatām

dvipah kuvalayāpīdo

jahi tena mamāhitau

SYNONYMS

mahā-mātra — O elephant-keeper; tvayā — by you; bhadra — my good man; rańga — of the arena; dvāri — to the doorway; upanīyatām — should be brought; dvipah — the elephant; kuvalayāpīdah — named Kuvalayāpīda; jahi — destroy; tena — with that (elephant); mama — my; ahitau — enemies.

TRANSLATION

You, elephant-keeper, my good man, should position the elephant Kuvalayāpīda at the entrance to the wrestling arena and have him kill my two enemies.
