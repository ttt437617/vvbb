Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 36: The Slaying of Arista, the Bull Demon

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.36.13

tam āpatantam sa nigrihya śrińgayoh

padā samākramya nipātya bhū-tale

nishpīdayām āsa yathārdram ambaram

kritvā vishānena jaghāna so 'patat

SYNONYMS

tam — him; āpatantam — attacking; sah — He; nigrihya — seizing; śrińgayoh — by the horns; padā — with His foot; samākramya — treading; nipātya — making him fall; bhū-tale — onto the ground; nishpīdayām āsa — He beat him; yathā — like; ardram — wet; ambaram — a garment; kritvā — making; vishānena — with his horn; jaghāna — struck; sah — he; apatat — fell.

TRANSLATION

As Arishta attacked, Lord Krishna seized him by the horns and knocked him to the ground with His foot. The Lord then thrashed him as if he were a wet cloth, and finally He yanked out one of the demon's horns and struck him with it until he lay prostrate.
