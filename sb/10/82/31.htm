Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 82: Krishna and Balarāma Meet the Inhabitants of Vrindāvana

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.82.31

śrī-śuka uvāca

nandas tatra yadūn prāptān

jñātvā krishna-purogamān

tatrāgamad vrito gopair

anah-sthārthair didrikshayā

SYNONYMS

śrī-śukah uvāca — Śukadeva Gosvāmī said; nandah — Nanda Mahārāja; tatra — there; yadūn — the Yadus; prāptān — arrived; jñātvā — finding out; krishna — Lord Krishna; purah-gamān — keeping in front; tatra — there; agamat — he went; vritah — accompanied; gopaih — by the cowherds; anah — on their wagons; stha — placed; arthaih — whose possessions; didrikshayā — wanting to see.

TRANSLATION

Śukadeva Gosvāmī said: When Nanda Mahārāja learned that the Yadus had arrived, led by Krishna, he immediately went to see them. The cowherds accompanied him, their various possessions loaded on their wagons.

PURPORT

The cowherds of Vraja were planning to stay at Kurukshetra for some days, so they came equipped with adequate provisions, especially milk products and other foods for the pleasure of Krishna and Balarāma.
