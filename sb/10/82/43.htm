Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 82: Krishna and Balarāma Meet the Inhabitants of Vrindāvana

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.82.43

vāyur yathā ghanānīkam

trinam tūlam rajāmsi ca

samyojyākshipate bhūyas

tathā bhūtāni bhūta-krit

SYNONYMS

vāyuh — the wind; yathā — as; ghana — of clouds; anīkam — groups; trinam — grass; tūlam — cotton; rajāmsi — dust; ca — and; samyojya — bringing together; ākshipate — throws apart; bhūyah — once again; tathā — so; bhūtāni — living beings; bhūta — of living beings; krit — the creator.

TRANSLATION

Just as the wind brings together masses of clouds, blades of grass, wisps of cotton and particles of dust, only to scatter them all again, so the creator deals with His created beings in the same way.
