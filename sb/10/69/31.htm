Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 69: Nārada Muni Visits Lord Krishna's Palaces in Dvārakā

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.69.31

kurvantam vigraham kaiścit

sandhim cānyatra keśavam

kutrāpi saha rāmena

cintayantam satām śivam

SYNONYMS

kurvantam — making; vigraham — war; kaiścit — with certain persons; sandhim — reconciliation; ca — and; anyatra — elsewhere; keśavam — Lord Krishna; kutra api — somewhere; saha — together; rāmena — with Lord Balarāma; cintayantam — thinking; satām — of the saintly; śivam — the welfare.

TRANSLATION

In one place He was planning battles in consultation with some of His advisers, and in another place He was making peace. Somewhere Lord Keśava and Lord Balarāma were together pondering the welfare of the pious.
