Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 69: Nārada Muni Visits Lord Krishna's Palaces in Dvārakā

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.69.23

tatrāpy acashta govindam

lālayantam sutān śiśūn

tato 'nyasmin grihe 'paśyan

majjanāya kritodyamam

SYNONYMS

tatra — there; api — and; acashta — he saw; govindam — Lord Krishna; lālayantam — coddling; sutān — His children; śiśūn — infant; tatah — then; anyasmin — in another; grihe — palace; apaśyat — he saw (Him); majjanāya — for taking bath; krita-udyamam — preparing.

TRANSLATION

This time Nāradajī saw that Lord Krishna was engaged as an affectionate father petting His small children. From there he entered another palace and saw Lord Krishna preparing to lake His bath.

PURPORT

This translation is from Śrīla Prabhupāda's Krishna, The Supreme Personality of Godhead.

Śrīla Viśvanātha Cakravartī comments that in virtually all the palaces Nārada visited, Lord Krishna worshiped and honored him.
