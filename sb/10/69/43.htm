Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 69: Nārada Muni Visits Lord Krishna's Palaces in Dvārakā

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.69.43

ity artha-kāma-dharmeshu

krishnena śraddhitātmanā

samyak sabhājitah prītas

tam evānusmaran yayau

SYNONYMS

iti — thus; artha — with items of utility for economic development; kāma — of sense gratification; dharmeshu — and of religiosity; krishnena — by Lord Krishna; śraddhita — faithful; ātmanā — whose heart; samyak — thoroughly; sabhājitah — honored; prītah — pleased; tam — Him; eva — indeed; anusmaran — always remembering; yayau — he went.

TRANSLATION

Lord Krishna greatly honored Nārada, faithfully presenting him with gifts related to economic prosperity, sense gratification and religious duties. Thus fully satisfied, the sage departed, constantly remembering the Lord.

PURPORT

As Śrīla Prabhupāda points out in Krishna, the phrase artha-kāma-dharmeshu indicates that Lord Krishna was behaving like an ordinary householder deeply concerned with economic development, sense gratification and religious duties. Nārada could understand the Lord's purpose, and he was most pleased by Śrī Krishna's exemplary behavior. Thus fully enlivened in his pure Krishna consciousness, he departed.
