Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 69: Nārada Muni Visits Lord Krishna's Palaces in Dvārakā

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.69.19

tato 'nyad āviśad geham

krishna-patnyāh sa nāradah

yogeśvareśvarasyāńga

yoga-māyā-vivitsayā

SYNONYMS

tatah — then; anyat — another; āviśat — entered; geham — residence; krishna-patnyāh — of a wife of Lord Krishna; sah — he; nāradah — Nārada Muni; yoga-īśvara — of the masters of mystic power; īśvarasya — of the supreme master; ańga — my dear King; yoga-māyā — the spiritual power of bewilderment; vivitsayā — with the desire of knowing.

TRANSLATION

Nārada then entered the palace of another of Lord Krishna's wives, my dear King. He was eager to witness the spiritual potency possessed by the master of all masters of mystic power.
