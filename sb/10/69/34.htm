Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 69: Nārada Muni Visits Lord Krishna's Palaces in Dvārakā

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.69.34

yajantam sakalān devān

kvāpi kratubhir ūrjitaih

pūrtayantam kvacid dharmam

kūrpārāma-mathādibhih

SYNONYMS

yajantam — worshiping; sakalān — all; devān — the demigods; kva api — somewhere; kratubhih — with sacrifices; ūrjitaih — full-blown; pūrtayantam — fulfilling by civil service; kvacit — somewhere; dharmam — religious obligation; kūrpa — with wells; ārāma — public parks; matha — monasteries; ādibhih — and so on.

TRANSLATION

Somewhere He was worshiping all the demigods with elaborate sacrifices, and elsewhere He was fulfilling His religious obligations by doing public welfare work, such as the construction of wells, public parks and monasteries.
