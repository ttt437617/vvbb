Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 69: Nārada Muni Visits Lord Krishna's Palaces in Dvārakā

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.69.13

tasmin samāna-guna-rūpa-vayah-su-vesha-

dāsī-sahasra-yutayānusavam grihinyā

vipro dadarśa camara-vyajanena rukma-

dandena sātvata-patim parivījayantyā

SYNONYMS

tasmin — therein; samāna — equal; guna — whose personal qualities; rūpa — beauty; vayah — youth; su-vesha — and fine dress; dāsī — by maidservants; sahasra — a thousand; yutayā — joined; anusavam — at every moment; grihinyā — together with His wife; viprah — the learned brāhmana (Nārada); dadarśa — saw; camara — of yak-tail; vyajanena — with a fan; rukma — gold; dandena — whose handle; sātvata-patim — the Lord of the Sātvatas, Śrī Krishna; parivījayantyā — fanning.

TRANSLATION

In that palace the learned brāhmana saw the Lord of the Sātvatas, Śrī Krishna, together with His wife, who fanned Him with a gold-handled yak-tail fan. She personally served Him in this way, even though she was constantly attended by a thousand maidservants equal to her in personal character, beauty, youth and fine dress.
