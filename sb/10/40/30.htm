Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 40: The Prayers of Akrūra

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.40.30

namas te vāsudevāya

sarva-bhūta-kshayāya ca

hrishīkeśa namas tubhyam

prapannam pāhi mām prabho

SYNONYMS

namah — obeisances; te — to You; vāsudevāya — the son of Vasudeva; sarva — of all; bhūta — living beings; kshayāya — the residence; ca — and; hrishīka-īśa — O Lord of the mind and senses; namah — obeisances; tubhyam — to You; prapannam — who am surrendered; pāhi — please protect; mām — me; prabho — O master.

TRANSLATION

O son of Vasudeva, obeisances to You, within whom all living beings reside. O Lord of the mind and senses, again I offer You my obeisances. O master, please protect me, who am surrendered unto You.

Thus end the purports of the humble servants of His Divine Grace A. C. Bhaktivedanta Swami Prabhupāda to the Tenth Canto, Fortieth Chapter, of the Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam, entitled "The Prayers of Akrūra."
