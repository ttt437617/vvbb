Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 40: The Prayers of Akrūra

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.40.16

yāni yānīha rūpāni

krīdanārtham bibharshi hi

tair āmrishta-śuco lokā

mudā gāyanti te yaśah

SYNONYMS

yāni yāni — which various; iha — in this material world; rūpāni — forms; krīdana — of play; artham — for the sake; bibharshi — You manifest; hi — indeed; taih — by them; āmrishta — cleansed; śucah — of their unhappiness; lokāh — people; mudā — joyfully; gāyanti — sing; te — Your; yaśah — glories.

TRANSLATION

To enjoy Your pastimes You manifest Yourself in various forms in this material world, and these incarnations cleanse away all the unhappiness of those who joyfully chant Your glories.
