Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 68: The Marriage of Sāmba

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.68.21

ugrasenah kshiteśeśo

yad va ājñāpayat prabhuh

tad avyagra-dhiyah śrutvā

kurudhvam avilambitam

SYNONYMS

ugrasenah — King Ugrasena; kshita — of the earth; īśa — of rulers; īśah — the ruler; yat — what; vah — of you; ājñāpayat — has demanded; prabhuh — our master; tat — that; avyagra-dhiyah — with undivided attention; śrutvā — hearing; kurudhvam — you should do; avilambitam — without delay.

TRANSLATION

[Lord Balarāma said:] King Ugrasena is our master and the ruler of kings. With undivided attention you should hear what he has ordered you to do, and then you should do it at once.
