Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 68: The Marriage of Sāmba

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.68.29

śrī-bādarāyanir uvāca

janma-bandhu-śrīyonnaddha-

madās te bharatarshabha

āśrāvya rāmam durvācyam

asabhyāh puram āviśan

SYNONYMS

śrī-bādarāyanih uvāca — Śukadeva Gosvāmī said; janma — of birth; bandhu — and relationships; śrīyā — by the opulences; unnaddha — made great; madāh — whose intoxication; te — they; bharata-rishabha — O best of the descendants of Bharata; āśrāvya — making hear; rāmam — Lord Balarāma; durvācyam — their harsh words; asabhyāh — rude men; puram — the city; āviśan — entered.

TRANSLATION

Śrī Bādarāyani said: O best of the Bhāratas, after the arrogant Kurus, thoroughly puffed up by the opulence of their high birth and relations, had spoken these harsh words to Lord Balarāma, they turned and went back to their city.
