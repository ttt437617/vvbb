Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 68: The Marriage of Sāmba

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.68.25

ete yaunena sambaddhāh

saha-śayyāsanāśanāh

vrishnayas tulyatām nītā

asmad-datta-nripāsanāh

SYNONYMS

ete — these; yaunena — by marital relation; sambaddhāh — connected; saha — sharing; śayyā — beds; āsana — seats; aśanāh — and meals; vrishnayah — the Vrishnis; tulyatām — to equality; nītāh — brought; asmat — by us; datta — given; nripa-āsanāh — whose thrones.

TRANSLATION

It is because these Vrishnis are bound to us by marital ties that we have granted them equality, allowing them to share our beds, seats and meals. Indeed, it is we who have given them their royal thrones.
