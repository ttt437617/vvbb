Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 41: Krishna and Balarāma Enter Mathurā

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.41.14

avanijyāńghri-yugalam

āsīt ślokyo balir mahān

aiśvaryam atulam lebhe

gatim caikāntinām tu yā

SYNONYMS

avanijya — bathing; ańghri-yugalam — the two feet; āsīt — became; ślokyah — glorious; balih — King Bali; mahān — the great; aiśvaryam — power; atulam — unequaled; lebhe — he achieved; gatīm — the destination; ca — and; ekāntinām — of the unalloyed devotees of the Lord; tu — indeed; yā — which.

TRANSLATION

By bathing Your feet, the exalted Bali Mahārāja attained not only glorious fame and unequaled power but also the final destination of pure devotees.
