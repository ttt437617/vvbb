Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 41: Krishna and Balarāma Enter Mathurā

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.41.49

ity abhipretya rājendra

sudāmā prīta-mānasah

śastaih su-gandhaih kusumair

mālā viracitā dadau

SYNONYMS

iti — thus speaking; abhipretya — understanding Their intention; rāja-indra — O best of kings (Parīkshit); sudāmā — Sudāmā; prīta-mānasah — pleased at heart; śastaih — fresh; su-gandhaih — and fragrant; kusumaih — with flowers; malah — garlands; viracitāh — made; dadau — he gave.

TRANSLATION

[Śukadeva Gosvāmī continued:] O best of kings, having spoken these words, Sudāmā could understand what Krishna and Balarāma wanted. Thus with great pleasure he presented Them with garlands of fresh, fragrant flowers.
