Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 41: Krishna and Balarāma Enter Mathurā

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.41.13

punīhi pāda-rajasā

grihān no griha-medhinām

yac-chaucenānutripyanti

pitarah sāgnayah surāh

SYNONYMS

punīhi — please purify; pāda — of Your feet; rajasā — with the dust; grihān — the home; nah — of us; griha-medhinām — who are attached to household ritual duties; yat — by which; śaucena — purification; anutripyanti — will become satisfied; pitarah — my forefathers; sa — together with; agnayah — the sacrificial fires; surāh — and the demigods.

TRANSLATION

I am simply an ordinary householder attached to ritual sacrifices, so please purify my home with the dust of Your lotus feet. By that act of purification, my forefathers, the sacrificial fires and the demigods will all become satisfied.
