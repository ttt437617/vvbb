Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 41: Krishna and Balarāma Enter Mathurā

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.41.17

śrī-bhagavan uvāca

āyāsye bhavato geham

aham arya-samanvitah

yadu-cakra-druham hatvā

vitarishye suhrit-priyam

SYNONYMS

śrī-bhagavān uvāca — the Supreme Lord said; āyāsye — will come; bhavatah — to your; geham — house; aham — I; ārya — by My elder (brother, Balarāma); samanvitah — accompanied; yadu-cakra — of the circle of Yadus; druham — the enemy (Kamsa); hatvā — killing; vitarishye — I will grant; suhrit — to My well-wishers; priyam — satisfaction.

TRANSLATION

The Supreme Lord said: I will come to Your house with My elder brother, but first I must satisfy My friends and well-wishers by killing the enemy of the Yadu clan.

PURPORT

Akrūra glorified Krishna in Text 16 as yadūttama, "the best of the Yadus." Śrī Krishna here confirms this by saying, in effect, "Since I am the best of the Yadus, I must kill the enemy of the Yadus, Kamsa, and then I will come to your house."
