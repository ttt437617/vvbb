Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 41: Krishna and Balarāma Enter Mathurā

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.41.41

nānā-lakshana-veshābhyām

krishna-rāmau virejatuh

sv-alańkritau bāla-gajau

parvanīva sitetarau

SYNONYMS

nānā — various; lakshana — having fine qualities; veshābhyām — with Their individual clothes; krishna-rāmau — Krishna and Balarāma; virejatuh — appeared resplendent; su-alańkritau — nicely decorated; bāla — young; gajau — elephants; parvani — during a festival; iva — as if; sita — white; itarau — and the opposite (black).

TRANSLATION

Krishna and Balarāma looked resplendent, each in His own unique, wonderfully ornamented outfit. They resembled a pair of young elephants, one white and the other black, decorated for a festive occasion.
