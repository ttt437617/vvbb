Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 41: Krishna and Balarāma Enter Mathurā

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.41.39

vasitvātma-priye vastre

krishnah sańkarshanas tathā

śeshāny ādatta gopebhyo

visrijya bhuvi kānicit

SYNONYMS

vasitvā — dressing Himself; ātma-priye — which He liked; vastre — in a pair of garments; krishnah — Krishna; sańkarshanah — Balarāma; tathā — also; śeshāni — the rest; ādatta — He gave; gopebhyah — to the cowherd boys; visrijya — throwing away; bhuvi — on the ground; kānicit — several.

TRANSLATION

Krishna and Balarāma put on pairs of garments that especially pleased Them, and then Krishna distributed the remaining clothes among the cowherd boys, leaving some scattered on the ground.
