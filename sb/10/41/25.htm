Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 41: Krishna and Balarāma Enter Mathurā

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.41.25

kāścid viparyag-dhrita-vastra-bhūshanā

vismritya caikam yugaleshv athāparāh

kritaika-patra-śravanaika-nūpurā

nāńktvā dvitīyam tv aparāś ca locanam

SYNONYMS

kaścit — some of them; viparyak — backwards; dhrita — putting on; vastra — their clothes; bhūshanah — and ornaments; vismritya — forgetting; ca — and; ekam — one; yugaleshu — of the pairs; atha — and; aparāh — others; krita — placing; eka — only one; patra — earring; śravana — on their ears; eka — or one; nūpurāh — set of ankle bells; na ańktvā — not anointing; dvitīyam — the second; tu — but; aparāh — other ladies; ca — and; locanam — an eye.

TRANSLATION

Some of the ladies put their clothes and ornaments on backwards, others forgot one of their earrings or ankle bells, and others applied makeup to one eye but not the other.

PURPORT

The ladies were very eager to see Krishna, and in their haste and excitement they forgot themselves.
