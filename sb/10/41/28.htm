Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 41: Krishna and Balarāma Enter Mathurā

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.41.28

drishtvā muhuh śrutam anudruta-cetasas tam tat-prekshanotsmita-sudhokshana-labdha-mānāh

ānanda-mūrtim upaguhya driśātma-labdham

hrishyat-tvaco jahur anantam arindamādhim

SYNONYMS

drishtvā — seeing; muhuh — repeatedly; śrutam — heard about; anudruta — melted; cetasah — whose hearts; tam — Him; tat — His; prekshana — of the glances; ut-smita — and the broad smiles; sudhā — by the nectar; ukshana — from the sprinkling; labdha — receiving; mānāh — honor; ānanda — of ecstasy; mūrtim — the personal form; upaguhya — embracing; driśā — through their eyes; ātma — within themselves; labdham — gained; hrishyat — erupting; tvacah — their skin; jahuh — they gave up; anantam — unlimited; arim-dama — O subduer of enemies (Parīkshit); ādhim — mental distress.

TRANSLATION

The ladies of Mathurā had repeatedly heard about Krishna, and thus as soon as they saw Him their hearts melted. They felt honored that He was sprinkling upon them the nectar of His glances and broad smiles. Taking Him into their hearts through their eyes, they embraced Him, the embodiment of all ecstasy, and as their bodily hairs stood on end, O subduer of enemies, they forgot the unlimited distress caused by His absence.
