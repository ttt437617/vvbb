Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 41: Krishna and Balarāma Enter Mathurā

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.41.44

tayor āsanam ānīya

pādyam cārghyārhanādibhih

pūjām sānugayoś cakre

srak-tāmbūlānulepanaih

SYNONYMS

tayoh — for Them; āsanam — seats; ānīya — bringing; pādyam — water to wash the feet; ca — and; arghya — with water to wash the hands; arhana — presents; ādibhih — and so on; pūjām — worship; sa-anugayoh — of the two, together with Their companions; cakre — he performed; srak — with garlands; tāmbūla — betel-nut preparation (pān); anulepanaih — and sandalwood paste.

TRANSLATION

After offering Them seats and bathing Their feet, Sudāmā worshiped Them and Their companions with arghya, garlands, pān, sandalwood paste and other presentations.
