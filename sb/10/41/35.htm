Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 41: Krishna and Balarāma Enter Mathurā

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.41.35

īdriśāny eva vāsāmsī

nityam giri-vane-carah

paridhatta kim udvrittā

rāja-dravyāny abhīpsatha

SYNONYMS

īdriśāni — of this sort; eva — indeed; vāsāmsi — garments; nityam — always; giri — on the mountains; vane — and in the forests; carāh — those who travel; paridhatta — would put on; kim — whether; udvrittāh — impudent; rāja — the King's; dravyāni — things; abhīpsatha — You want.

TRANSLATION

[The washerman said:] You impudent boys! You're accustomed to roaming the mountains and forests, and yet You would dare put on such clothes as these! These are the King's possessions You're asking for!
