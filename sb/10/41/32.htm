Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 41: Krishna and Balarāma Enter Mathurā

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.41.32

rajakam kañcid āyāntam

rańga-kāram gadāgrajah

drishtvāyācata vāsāmsi

dhautāny aty-uttamāni ca

SYNONYMS

rajakam — washerman; kañcit — a certain; āyāntam — approaching; rańga-kāram — engaged in dyeing; gada-agrajah — Lord Śrī Krishna, the elder brother of Gada; drishtvā — seeing; ayācata — requested; vāsāmsi — garments; dhautāni — cleaned; ati-uttamāni — first class; ca — and.

TRANSLATION

Seeing a washerman approaching who had been dyeing some clothes, Krishna asked him for the finest laundered garments he had.
