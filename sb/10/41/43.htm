Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 41: Krishna and Balarāma Enter Mathurā

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.41.43

tatah sudāmno bhavanam

mālā-kārasya jagmatuh

tau drishtvā sa samutthāya

nanāma śirasā bhuvi

SYNONYMS

tatah — then; sudāmnah — of Sudāmā; bhavanam — to the home; mālā-kārasya — of the garland-maker; jagmatuh — the two of Them went; tau — Them; drishtvā — seeing; sah — he; samutthāya — standing up; nanāma — bowed down; śirasā — with his head; bhuvi — on the ground.

TRANSLATION

The two Lords then went to the house of the garland-maker Sudāmā. When Sudāmā saw Them he at once stood up and then bowed down, placing his head on the ground.
