Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 41: Krishna and Balarāma Enter Mathurā

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.41.38

tasyānujīvinah sarve

vāsah-kośān visrijya vai

dudruvuh sarvato mārgam

vāsāmsi jagrihe 'cyutah

SYNONYMS

tasya — his; anujīvinah — employees; sarve — all; vāsah — of clothes; kośān — the bundles; visrijya — leaving behind; vai — indeed; dadruvuh — they fled; sarvatah — in all directions; mārgam — down the road; vāsāmsi — garments; jagrihe — took; acyutah — Lord Krishna.

TRANSLATION

The washerman's assistants all dropped their bundles of clothes and fled down the road, scattering in all directions. Lord Krishna then took the clothes.
