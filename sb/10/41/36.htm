Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 41: Krishna and Balarāma Enter Mathurā

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.41.36

yātāśu bāliśā maivam

prārthyam yadi jijīvīshā

badhnanti ghnanti lumpanti

driptam rāja-kulāni vai

SYNONYMS

yāta — go; āśu — quickly; bāliśah — fools; mā — do not; evam — like this; prārthyam — beg; yadi — if; jijīvishā — You have the desire to live; badhnanti — they tie up; ghnanti — kill; lumpanti — and loot (his house); driptam — one who is bold; rāja-kulāni — the King's men; vai — indeed.

TRANSLATION

Fools, get out of here quickly! Don't beg like this if You want to stay alive. When someone is too bold, the King's men arrest him and kill him and take all his property.
