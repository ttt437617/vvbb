Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 41: Krishna and Balarāma Enter Mathurā

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.41.48

tāv ajñāpayatam bhrityam

kim aham karavāni vām

pumso 'ty-anugraho hy esha

bhavadbhir yan niyujyate

SYNONYMS

tau — They; ājñāpayatam — should please order; bhrityam — Their servant; kim — what; aham — I; karavāni — should do; vām — for You; pumsah — for any person; ati — extreme; anugrahah — mercy; hi — indeed; eshah — this; bhavadbhih — by You; yat — in which; niyujyate — he is engaged.

TRANSLATION

Please order me, Your servant, to do whatever You wish. To be engaged by You in some service is certainly a great blessing for anyone.
