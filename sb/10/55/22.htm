Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 55: The History of Pradyumna

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.55.22

bādhyamāno 'stra-varshena

raukmineyo mahā-rathah

sattvātmikām mahā-vidyām

sarva-māyopamardinīm

SYNONYMS

bādhyamānah — harassed; astra — of weapons; varshena — by the rain; raukmineyah — Pradyumna, son of Rukminī; mahā-rathah — the powerful warrior; sattva-ātmikām — produced of the mode of goodness; mahā-vidyām — (He utilized) the mystic knowledge called Mahā-māyā; sarva — all; mayā — magic; upamardinīm — which overcomes.

TRANSLATION

Harassed by this rain of weapons, Lord Raukmineya, the greatly powerful warrior, made use of the mystic science called Mahā-māyā, which was created from the mode of goodness and which could defeat all other mystic power.
