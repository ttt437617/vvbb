Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 55: The History of Pradyumna

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.55.39

nashtam pradyumnam āyātam

ākarnya dvārakaukasah

aho mrita ivāyāto

bālo dishtyeti hābruvan

SYNONYMS

nashtam — lost; pradyumnam — Pradyumna; āyātam — returned; ākarnya — hearing; dvārakā-okasah — the residents of Dvārakā; aho — ah; mritah — dead; iva — as if; āyātah — come back; bālah — the child; dishtyā — by the favor of providence; iti — thus; ha — indeed; abruvan — they spoke.

TRANSLATION

Hearing that lost Pradyumna had come home, the residents of Dvārakā declared, "Ah, providence has allowed this child to return as if from death!"
