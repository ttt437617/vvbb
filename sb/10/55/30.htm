Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 55: The History of Pradyumna

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.55.30

atha tatrāsitāpāńgī

vaidarbhī valgu-bhāshinī

asmarat sva-sutam nashtam

sneha-snuta-payodharā

SYNONYMS

atha — then; tatra — there; asita — black; apāńgī — the corners of whose eyes; vaidarbhī — Queen Rukminī; valgu — sweet; bhāshinī — whose speaking; asmarat — remembered; sva-sutam — her son; nashtam — lost; sneha — out of love; snuta — having become moist; payah-dharā — whose breasts.

TRANSLATION

Seeing Pradyumna, sweet-voiced, dark-eyed Rukminī remembered her lost son, and her breasts became moist out of affection.
