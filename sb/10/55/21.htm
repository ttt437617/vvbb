Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 55: The History of Pradyumna

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.55.21

sa ca māyām samāśritya

daiteyīm maya-darśitam

mumuce 'stra-mayam varsham

kārshnau vaihāyaso 'surah

SYNONYMS

sah — he, Śambara; ca — and; māyām — magic; samāśritya — resorting to; daiteyīm — demoniac; maya — by Maya Dānava; darśitam — shown; mumuce — he released; astra-mayam — of weapons; varsham — a rainfall; kārshnau — upon the son of Krishna; vaihāyasah — standing in the sky; asurah — the demon.

TRANSLATION

Resorting to the black magic of the Daityas taught to him by Maya Dānava, Śambara suddenly appeared in the sky and released a downpour of weapons upon Krishna's son.
