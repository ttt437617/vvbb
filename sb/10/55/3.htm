Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 55: The History of Pradyumna

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.55.3

tam śambarah kāma-rūpī

hritvā tokam anirdaśam

sa viditvātmanah śatrum

prāsyodanvaty agād griham

SYNONYMS

tam — Him; śambarah — the demon Śambara; kāma — as he desired; rūpī — assuming forms; hritvā — stealing; tokam — the child; anih-daśam — not yet ten days old; sah — he (Śambara); viditvā — recognizing; ātmanah — his own; śatrum — enemy; prāsya — throwing; udanvati — into the sea; agāt — went; griham — to his home.

TRANSLATION

The demon Śambara, who could assume any form he desired, kidnapped the infant before He was even ten days old. Understanding Pradyumna to be his enemy, Śambara threw Him into the sea and then returned home.

PURPORT

Śrīla Viśvanātha Cakravartī points out that according to the Vishnu Purāna, Pradyumna was kidnapped on the sixth day after His birth.
