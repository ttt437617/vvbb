Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 55: The History of Pradyumna

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.55.29

avadhārya śanair īshad

vailakshanyena yoshitah

upajagmuh pramuditāh

sa-strī ratnam su-vismitāh

SYNONYMS

avadhārya — realizing; śanaih — gradually; īshat — slight; vailakshanyena — by the difference of appearance; yoshitah — the ladies; upajagmuh — they came near; pramuditāh — delighted; sa — together with; strī — of women; ratnam — the jewel; su-vismitah — very surprised.

TRANSLATION

Gradually, from the slight differences between His appearance and Krishna's, the ladies realized He was not the Lord. Delighted and astonished, they approached Pradyumna and His consort, who was a jewel among women.
