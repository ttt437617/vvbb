Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 55: The History of Pradyumna

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.55.37

tac chrutvā mahad āścaryam

krishnāntah-pura-yoshitah

abhyanandan bahūn abdān

nashtam mritam ivāgatam

SYNONYMS

tat — that; śrutvā — hearing; mahat — great; āścaryam — wonder; krishna-antah-pura — of Lord Krishna's personal residence; yoshitah — the women; abhyanandan — they greeted; bahūn — for many; abdān — years; nashtam — lost; mritam — someone dead; iva — as if; āgatam — returned.

TRANSLATION

When the women of Lord Krishna's palace heard this most amazing account, they joyfully greeted Pradyumna, who had been lost for many years but who had now returned as if from the dead.
