Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 55: The History of Pradyumna

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.55.36

vijñātārtho 'pi bhagavāms

tūshnīm āsa janārdanah

nārado 'kathayat sarvam

śambarāharanādikam

SYNONYMS

vijñāta — understanding fully; arthah — the matter; api — even though; bhagavān — the Supreme Personality of Godhead; tūshnīm — silent; āsa — remained; janārdanah — Krishna; nāradah — Nārada Muni; akathayat — recounted; sarvam — everything; śambara — by Śambara; āharana — the kidnapping; ādikam — beginning with.

TRANSLATION

Although Lord Janārdana knew perfectly well what had transpired, He remained silent. The sage Nārada, however, explained everything, beginning with Śambara's kidnapping of the child.
