Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 1: The Advent of Lord Krishna: Introduction

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.1.10

vraje vasan kim akaron

madhupuryām ca keśavah

bhrātaram cāvadhīt kamsam

mātur addhātad-arhanam

SYNONYMS

vraje — at Vrindāvana; vasan — while residing; kim akarot — what did He do; madhupuryām — in Mathurā; ca — and; keśavah — Krishna, the killer of Keśī; bhrātaram — the brother; ca — and; avadhīt — killed; kamsam — Kamsa; mātuh — of His mother; addhā — directly; a-tat-arhanam — which was not at all sanctioned by the śāstras.

TRANSLATION

Lord Krishna lived both in Vrindāvana and in Mathurā. What did He do there? Why did He kill Kamsa, His mother's brother? Such killing is not at all sanctioned in the śāstras.

PURPORT

One's maternal uncle, the brother of one's mother, is on the level of one's father. When a maternal uncle has no son, his nephew legally inherits his property. Therefore, why did Krishna directly kill Kamsa, the brother of His mother? Mahārāja Parīkshit was very much inquisitive about the facts in this regard.
