Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 46: Uddhava Visits Vrindāvana

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.46.20

dāvāgner vāta-varshāc ca

vrisha-sarpāc ca rakshitāh

duratyayebhyo mrityubhyah

krishnena su-mahātmanā

SYNONYMS

dāva-agneh — from the forest fire; vāta — from the wind; varshāt — and rain; ca — also; vrisha — from the bull; sarpāt — from the serpent; ca — and; rakshitāh — protected; duratyayebhyah — insurmountable; mrityubhyah — from mortal dangers; krishnena — by Krishna; su-mahā-ātmanā — the very great soul.

TRANSLATION

We were saved from the forest fire, the wind and rain, the bull and serpent demons — from all such insurmountable, deadly dangers — by that very great soul, Krishna.
