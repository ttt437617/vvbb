Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 46: Uddhava Visits Vrindāvana

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.46.19

apy āyāsyati govindah

sva-janān sakrid īkshitum

tarhi drakshyāma tad-vaktram

su-nasam su-smitekshanam

SYNONYMS

api — whether; āyāsyati — will come back; govindah — Krishna; sva-janān — His relatives; sakrit — once; īkshitum — to see; tarhi — then; drakshyāma — we may glance upon; tat — His; vaktram — face; su-nasam — with beautiful nose; su — beautiful; smita — smile; īkshanam — and eyes.

TRANSLATION

Will Govinda return even once to see His family? If He ever does, we may then glance upon His beautiful face, with its beautiful eyes, nose and smile.

PURPORT

Now that Krishna had become a prince in the great city of Mathurā, Nanda had lost hope that He would come back to live in the simple cowherd village of Vrindāvana. But he hoped against hope that Krishna would come back at least once to visit the simple cowherd folk who had raised Him from birth.
