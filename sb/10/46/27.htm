Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 46: Uddhava Visits Vrindāvana

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.46.27

śrī-śuka uvāca

iti samsmritya samsmritya

nandah krishnānurakta-dhīh

aty-utkantho 'bhavat tūshnīm

prema-prasara-vihvalah

SYNONYMS

śrī-śukah uvāca — Śukadeva Gosvāmī said; iti — thus; samsmritya samsmritya — intensely and repeatedly remembering; nandah — Nanda Mahārāja; krishna — to Krishna; anurakta — completely attracted; dhīh — whose mind; ati — extremely; utkanthah — anxious; abhavat — he became; tūshnīm — silent; prema — of his pure love; prasara — by the force; vihvalah — overcome.

TRANSLATION

Śukadeva Gosvāmī said: Thus intensely remembering Krishna again and again, Nanda Mahārāja, his mind completely attached to the Lord, felt extreme anxiety and fell silent, overcome by the strength of his love.
