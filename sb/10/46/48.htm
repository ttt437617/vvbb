Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 46: Uddhava Visits Vrindāvana

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.46.48

akrūra āgatah kim vā

yah kamsasyārtha-sādhakah

yena nīto madhu-purīm

krishnah kamala-locanah

SYNONYMS

akrūrah — Akrūra; āgatah — has come; kim vā — perhaps; yah — who; kamsasya — of King Kamsa; artha — of the purpose; sādhakah — the executor; yena — by whom; nītah — brought; madhu-purīm — to Mathurā City; krishnah — Krishna; kamala — lotuslike; locanah — whose eyes.

TRANSLATION

"Perhaps Akrūra has returned — he who fulfilled Kamsa's desire by taking lotus-eyed Krishna to Mathurā.

PURPORT

The gopīs angrily spoke this statement.
