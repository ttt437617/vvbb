Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 46: Uddhava Visits Vrindāvana

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.46.17

dishtyā kamso hatah pāpah

sānugah svena pāpmanā

sādhūnām dharma-śīlānām

yadūnām dveshti yah sadā

SYNONYMS

dishtyā — by good fortune; kamsah — King Kamsa; hatah — has been killed; pāpah — the sinful; sa — along with; anugah — his followers (brothers); svena — because of his own; pāpmanā — sinfulness; sādhūnām — saintly; dharma-śīlānām — always righteous in their behavior; yadūnām — the Yadus; dveshti — hated; yah — who; sadā — always.

TRANSLATION

Fortunately, because of his own sins, the sinful Kamsa has been killed, along with all his brothers. He always hated the saintly and righteous Yadus.
