Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 46: Uddhava Visits Vrindāvana

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.46.45

tā dīpa-dīptair manibhir virejū

rajjūr vikarshad-bhuja-kańkana-srajah

calan-nitamba-stana-hāra-kundala-

tvishat-kapolāruna-kuńkumānanāh

SYNONYMS

tāh — those women; dīpa — by the lamps; dīptaih — illumined; manibhih — with jewels; virejuh — shone; rajjūh — the (churning) ropes; vikarshat — pulling; bhuja — upon their arms; kańkana — of bangles; srajah — wearing rows; calan — moving; nitamba — their hips; stana — breasts; hāra — and necklaces; kundala — due to their earrings; tvishat — glowing; kapola — their cheeks; aruna — reddish; kuńkuma — with kuńkuma powder; ānanāh — their faces.

TRANSLATION

As they pulled on the churning ropes with their bangled arms, the women of Vraja shone with the splendor of their jewels, which reflected the lamps' light. Their hips, breasts and necklaces moved about, and their faces, anointed with reddish kuńkuma, glowed radiantly with the luster of their earrings reflecting from their cheeks.
