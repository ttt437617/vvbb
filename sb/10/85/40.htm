Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 85: Lord Krishna Instructs Vasudeva and Retrieves Devakī's Sons

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.85.40

darśanam vām hi bhūtānām

dushprāpam cāpy adurlabham

rajas-tamah-svabhāvānām

yan nah prāptau yadricchayā

SYNONYMS

darśanam — the vision; vām — of You two; hi — indeed; bhūtānām — for living beings in general; dushprāpam — rarely achieved; ca api — yet still; adurlabham — not difficult to obtain; rajah — in passion; tamah — and ignorance; svabhāvānām — for those whose natures; yat — in that; nah — by us; prāptau — obtained; yadricchayā — causelessly.

TRANSLATION

Seeing You Lords is a rare achievement for most living beings. But even persons like us, situated in the modes of passion and ignorance, can easily see You when You reveal Yourself by Your own sweet will.

PURPORT

By ascribing to himself the degraded status of a demoniac birth, Bali Mahārāja denied any spiritual qualification for being visited by Krishna and Balarāma. What to speak of demons like himself, Bali thought, even advanced renunciants on the paths of jñāna and yoga fail to please the Lord when they do not give up their pride and envy.
