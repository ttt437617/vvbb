Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 85: Lord Krishna Instructs Vasudeva and Retrieves Devakī's Sons

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.85.52

ity uktvā tān samādāya

indrasenena pūjitau

punar dvāravatīm etya

mātuh putrān ayacchatām

SYNONYMS

iti — thus; uktvā — speaking; tān — them; samādāya — taking; indrasenena — by Bali Mahārāja; pūjitau — both honored; punah — once more; dvāravatīm — to Dvārakā; etya — going; mātuh — of Their mother; putrān — the sons; ayacchatām — They presented.

TRANSLATION

[Śukadeva Gosvāmī continued:] After saying this, Lord Krishna and Lord Balarāma, having been duly worshiped by Bali Mahārāja, took the six sons and returned to Dvārakā, where They presented them to Their mother.
