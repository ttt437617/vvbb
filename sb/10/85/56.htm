Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 85: Lord Krishna Instructs Vasudeva and Retrieves Devakī's Sons

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.85.55-56

pītvāmritam payas tasyāh

pīta-śesham gadā-bhritah

nārāyanāńga-samsparśa-

pratilabdhātma-darśanāh

te namaskritya govindam

devakīm pitaram balam

mishatām sarva-bhūtānām

yayur dhāma divaukasām

SYNONYMS

pītvā — having drunk; amritam — nectarean; payah — milk; tasyāh — her; pīta — of what had been drunk; śesham — the remnants; gadā-bhritah — of Krishna, the wielder of the club; nārāyana — of the Supreme Lord, Nārāyana (Krishna); ańga — of the body; samsparśa — by the touch; pratilabdha — regained; ātma — of their original selves (as demigods); darśanāh — the perception; te — they; namaskritya — bowing down; govindam — to Lord Krishna; devakīm — to Devakī; pitaram — to their father; balam — and to Lord Balarāma; mishatām — as they looked on; sarva — all; bhūtānām — the people; yayuh — they went; dhāma — to the abode; diva-okasām — of the demigods.

TRANSLATION

By drinking her nectarean milk, the remnants of what Krishna Himself had previously drunk, the six sons touched the transcendental body of the Lord, Nārāyana, and this contact awakened them to their original identities. They bowed down to Govinda, Devakī, their father and Balarāma, and then, as everyone looked on, they left for the abode of the demigods.

PURPORT

Lord Krishna remained as an infant with Devakī and Vasudeva for only a very short time. First the Lord appeared before them in His four-armed Vishnu form, and after hearing their prayers He changed Himself into an apparently ordinary infant for their pleasure. But to save Krishna from suffering His brothers' fate, Vasudeva at once removed Him from Kamsa's prison. Just before Vasudeva took Him away, mother Devakī suckled Krishna once so that He would not feel thirsty during the long trip to Nanda-vraja. This we learn from the commentary of Śrīla Viśvanātha Cakravartī Thākura.
