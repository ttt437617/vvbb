Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 85: Lord Krishna Instructs Vasudeva and Retrieves Devakī's Sons

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.85.57

tam drishtvā devakī devī

mritāgamana-nirgamam

mene su-vismitā māyām

krishnasya racitām nripa

SYNONYMS

tam — this; drishtvā — seeing; devakī — Devakī; devī — divine; mrita — of the dead (sons); āgamana — the return; nirgamam — and departure; mene — she thought; su — very much; vismitā — amazed; māyām — magic; krishnasya — by Krishna; racitām — produced; nripa — O King (Parīkshit).

TRANSLATION

Seeing her sons return from death and then depart again, saintly Devakī was struck with wonder, O King. She concluded that this was all simply an illusion created by Krishna.
