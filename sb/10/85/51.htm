Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 85: Lord Krishna Instructs Vasudeva and Retrieves Devakī's Sons

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.85.51

smarodgīthah parishvańgah

patańgah kshudrabhrid ghrinī

shad ime mat-prasādena

punar yāsyanti sad-gatim

SYNONYMS

smara-udgīthah parishvańgah — Smara, Udgītha and Parishvańga; patańgah kshudrabhrit ghrinī — Patańga, Kshudrabhrit and Ghrinī; shat — six; ime — these; mat — My; prasādena — by the grace; punah — again; yāsyanti — will go; sat — of saintly persons; gatim — to the destination.

TRANSLATION

By My grace these six — Smara, Udgītha, Parishvańga, Patańga, Kshudrabhrit and Ghrinī — will return to the abode of pure saints.

PURPORT

These are the names the six children first had when they were sons of Marīci. The oldest, Smara, was called Kīrtimān when born again to Vasudeva, as recorded in the Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam (10.1.57),

kīrtimantam prathama-jam

kamsāyānakadundubhih

arpayām āsa kricchrena

so 'nritad ati-vihvalah

"Vasudeva was very much disturbed by the fear of becoming a liar by breaking his promise. Thus with great pain he delivered his firstborn son, named Kīrtimān, into the hands of Kamsa."
