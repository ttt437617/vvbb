Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 85: Lord Krishna Instructs Vasudeva and Retrieves Devakī's Sons

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.85.53

tān drishtvā bālakān devī

putra-sneha-snuta-stanī

parishvajyāńkam āropya

mūrdhny ajighrad abhīkshnaśah

SYNONYMS

tān — them; drishtvā — seeing; bālakān — the boys; devī — the goddess (Devakī); putra — for her sons; sneha — due to her affection; snuta — flowing; stanī — whose breasts; parishvajya — embracing; ańkam — on her lap; āropya — placing; mūrdhni — their heads; ajighrat — she smelled; abhīkshnaśah — repeatedly.

TRANSLATION

When she saw her lost children, Goddess Devakī felt such affection for them that milk flowed from her breasts. She embraced them and took them onto her lap, smelling their heads again and again.
