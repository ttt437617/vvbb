Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 85: Lord Krishna Instructs Vasudeva and Retrieves Devakī's Sons

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.85.59

śrī-sūta uvāca

ya idam anuśrinoti śrāvayed vā murāreś

caritam amrita-kīrter varnitam vyāsa-putraih

jagad-agha-bhid alam tad-bhakta-sat-karna-pūram

bhagavati krita-citto yāti tat-kshema-dhāma

SYNONYMS

śrī-sūtah uvāca — Śrī Sūta said (to the sages assembled at Naimishāranya, to whom he was repeating the conversation between Śukadeva Gosvāmī and Parīkshit Mahārāja); yah — whoever; idam — this; anuśrinoti — properly hears; śrāvayet — causes others to hear; vā — or; murāreh — of Lord Krishna, killer of the demon Mura; caritam — pastime; amrita — deathless; kīrteh — whose glories; varnitam — described; vyāsa-putraih — by the respected son of Vyāsadeva; jagat — of the universe; agha — the sins; bhit — which (pastime) destroys; alam — totally; tat — His; bhakta — for the devotees; sat — transcendental; karna-pūram — ornament for the ears; bhagavati — on the Supreme Lord; krita — fixing; cittah — his mind; yāti — he goes; tat — His; kshema — auspicious; dhāma — to the personal abode.

TRANSLATION

Śrī Sūta Gosvāmī said: This pastime enacted by Lord Murāri, whose fame is eternal, totally destroys the sins of the universe and serves as the transcendental ornament for His devotees' ears. Anyone who carefully hears or narrates this pastime, as recounted by the venerable son of Vyāsa, will be able to fix his mind in meditation on the Supreme Lord and attain to the all-auspicious kingdom of God.

PURPORT

According to Śrīla Śrīdhara Svāmī, hearing the wonderful events of Lord Krishna's life destroys sins in a manner that is perfect (alam) because it is easy. Anyone can easily participate in this hearing, and those who become devoted to Krishna always enjoy wearing on their ears the ornaments of topics concerning Him. Not only those who were present at the time of their occurrence, but also Śukadeva Gosvāmī, Sūta Gosvāmī, all who have heard since and everyone in the universe who will hear in the future — all are blessed by the continuous recital of Lord Krishna's transcendental glories.

Thus end the purports of the humble servants of His Divine Grace A. C. Bhaktivedanta Swami Prabhupāda to the Tenth Canto, Eighty-fifth Chapter, of the Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam, entitled "Lord Krishna Instructs Vasudeva and Retrieves Devakī's Sons."
