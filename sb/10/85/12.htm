Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 85: Lord Krishna Instructs Vasudeva and Retrieves Devakī's Sons

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.85.12

naśvareshv iha bhāveshu

tad asi tvam anaśvaram

yathā dravya-vikāreshu

dravya-mātram nirūpitam

SYNONYMS

naśvareshu — subject to destruction; iha — in this world; bhāveshu — among entities; tat — that; asi — are; tvam — You; anaśvaram — the indestructible; yathā — just as; dravya — of a substance; vikāreshu — among the transformations; dravya-mātram — the substance itself; nirūpitam — ascertained.

TRANSLATION

You are the one indestructible entity among all the destructible things of this world, like the underlying substance that is seen to remain unchanged while the things made from it undergo transformations.
