Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 85: Lord Krishna Instructs Vasudeva and Retrieves Devakī's Sons

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.85.46

śādhy asmān īśitavyeśa

nishpāpān kuru nah prabho

pumān yac chraddhayātishthamś

codanāyā vimucyate

SYNONYMS

śādhi — please order; asmān — us; īśitavya — of those who are subject to being controlled; īśa — O controller; nishpāpān — sinless; kuru — please make; nah — us; prabho — O master; pumān — a person; yat — which; śraddhayā — with faith; ātishthan — executing; codanāyāh — of scriptural regulation; vimucyate — becomes free.

TRANSLATION

O Lord of all subordinate creatures, please tell us what to do and thus free us of all sin. One who faithfully executes Your command, O master, is no longer obliged to follow the ordinary Vedic rites.

PURPORT

The ācāryas explain Bali's thoughts as follows. Reflecting on the possibility that his request for immediate deliverance may have been too bold, Bali Mahārāja considers that first he will need to become sufficiently purified. In any case, he thinks, Lord Krishna and Lord Balarāma must have come to him for some specific purpose; if he can receive the Lords' order and carry it out, that will be his best opportunity for purification. Indeed, as Bali states, a devotee acting under the Personality of Godhead's instruction need no longer follow the sacrificial injunctions and prohibitions of the Vedas.
