Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 56: The Syamantaka Jewel

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.56.24

āsīt tad ashtā-vimśāham

itaretara-mushtibhih

vajra-nishpesha-parushair

aviśramam ahar-niśam

SYNONYMS

āsīt — was; tat — that; ashtā-vimśa — twenty-eight; aham — days; itara-itara — with one another's; mushtibhih — fists; vajra — of lightning; nishpesha — like the blows; parushaih — hard; aviśramam — without pause; ahah-niśam — day and night.

TRANSLATION

The fight went on without rest for twenty-eight days, the two opponents striking each other with their fists, which fell like the cracking blows of lightning.

PURPORT

Śrīla Viśvanātha Cakravartī points out that the fight continued day and night without intermission.
