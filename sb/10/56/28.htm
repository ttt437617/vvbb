Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 56: The Syamantaka Jewel

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.56.28

yasyeshad-utkalita-rosha-katāksha-mokshair

vartmādiśat kshubhita-nakra-timińgalo 'bdhih

setuh kritah sva-yaśa ujjvalitā ca lańkā

rakshah-śirāmsi bhuvi petur ishu-kshatāni

SYNONYMS

yasya — whose; īshat — slightly; utkalita — manifested; rosha — from the anger; katā-aksha — of sidelong glances; mokshaih — because of the release; vartma — a way; ādiśat — showed; kshubhita — agitated; nakra — (in which) the crocodiles; timińgalah — and huge timińgila fish; abdhih — the ocean; setuh — a bridge; kritah — made; sva — His own; yaśah — fame; ujjvalitā — set ablaze; ca — and; lańkā — the city of Lańkā; rakshah — of the demon (Rāvana); śirāmsi — the heads; bhuvi — to the ground; petuh — fell; ishu — by whose arrows; kshatāni — cut off.

TRANSLATION

You are He who impelled the ocean to give way when His sidelong glances, slightly manifesting His anger, disturbed the crocodiles and timińgila fish within the watery depths. You are He who built a great bridge to establish His fame, who burned down the city of Lańkā, and whose arrows severed the heads of Rāvana, which then fell to the ground.
