Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 56: The Syamantaka Jewel

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.56.2

śrī-rājovāca

satrājitah kim akarod

brahman krishnasya kilbishah

syamantakah kutas tasya

kasmād dattā sutā hareh

SYNONYMS

śrī-rājā — the King (Parīkshit Mahārāja); uvāca — said; satrājitah — Satrājit; kim — what; akarot — committed; brahman — O brāhmana; krishnasya — against Lord Krishna; kilbishah — offense; syamantakah — the Syamantaka jewel; kutah — from where; tasya — his; kasmāt — why; dattā — given; sutā — his daughter; hareh — to Lord Hari.

TRANSLATION

Mahārāja Parīkshit inquired: O brāhmana, what did King Satrājit do to offend Lord Krishna? Where did he get the Syamantaka jewel, and why did he give his daughter to the Supreme Lord?
