Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 11: The Childhood Pastimes of Krishna

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.11.39-40

kvacid vādayato venum

kshepanaih kshipatah kvacit

kvacit pādaih kińkinībhih

kvacit kritrima-go-vrishaih

vrishāyamānau nardantau

yuyudhāte parasparam

anukritya rutair jantūmś

ceratuh prākritau yathā

SYNONYMS

kvacit — sometimes; vādayatah — blowing; venum — on the flute; kshepanaih — with a device of rope for throwing; kshipatah — throwing stones to get fruit; kvacit — sometimes; kvacit pādaih — sometimes with the legs; kińkinībhih — with the sound of ankle bells; kvacit — sometimes; kritrima-go-vrishaih — by becoming artificial cows and bulls; vrishāyamānau — imitating the animals; nardantau — roaring loudly; yuyudhāte — They both used to fight; parasparam — with one another; anukritya — imitating; rutaih — by resounding; jantūn — all the animals; ceratuh — They used to wander; prākritau — two ordinary human children; yathā — like.

TRANSLATION

Sometimes Krishna and Balarāma would play on Their flutes, sometimes They would throw ropes and stones devised for getting fruits from the trees, sometimes They would throw only stones, and sometimes, Their ankle bells tinkling, They would play football with fruits like bael and āmalakī. Sometimes They would cover Themselves with blankets and imitate cows and bulls and fight with one another, roaring loudly, and sometimes They would imitate the voices of the animals. In this way They enjoyed sporting, exactly like two ordinary human children.

PURPORT

Vrindāvana is full of peacocks. Kūjat-kokila-hamsa-sārasa-ganākīrne mayūrākule. The Vrindāvana forest is always full of cuckoos, ducks, swans, peacocks, cranes and also monkeys, bulls and cows. So Krishna and Balarāma used to imitate the sounds of these animals and enjoy sporting.
