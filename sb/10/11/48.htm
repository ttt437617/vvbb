Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 11: The Childhood Pastimes of Krishna

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.11.48

sa vai bako nāma mahān

asuro baka-rūpa-dhrik

āgatya sahasā krishnam

tīkshna-tundo 'grasad balī

SYNONYMS

sah — that creature; vai — indeed; bakah nāma — by the name Bakāsura; mahān asurah — a great, gigantic demon; baka-rūpa-dhrik — assumed the bodily shape of a big duck; āgatya — coming there; sahasā — all of a sudden; krishnam — Krishna; tīkshna-tundah — sharp beak; agrasat — swallowed; balī — very powerful.

TRANSLATION

That great-bodied demon was named Bakāsura. He had assumed the body of a duck with a very sharp beak. Having come there, he immediately swallowed Krishna.
