Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 11: The Childhood Pastimes of Krishna

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.11.41

kadācid yamunā-tīre

vatsāmś cārayatoh svakaih

vayasyaih krishna-balayor

jighāmsur daitya āgamat

SYNONYMS

kadācit — sometimes; yamunā-tīre — on the bank of the Yamunā; vatsān — the calves; cārayatoh — when They were tending; svakaih — Their own; vayasyaih — with other playmates; krishna-balayoh — both Krishna and Balarāma; jighāmsuh — desiring to kill Them; daityah — another demon; āgamat — reached there.

TRANSLATION

One day while Rāma and Krishna, along with Their playmates, were tending the calves on the bank of the River Yamunā, another demon arrived there, desiring to kill Them.
