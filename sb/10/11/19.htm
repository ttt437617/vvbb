Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 11: The Childhood Pastimes of Krishna

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.11.19

paśya paśya vayasyāms te

mātri-mrishtān svalańkritān

tvam ca snātah kritāhāro

viharasva svalańkritah

SYNONYMS

paśya paśya — just see, just see; vayasyān — boys of Your age; te — Your; mātri-mrishtān — cleansed by their mothers; su-alańkritān — decorated with nice ornaments; tvam ca — You also; snātah — after taking a bath; krita-āhārah — and eating Your lunch; viharasva — enjoy with them; su-alańkritah — fully decorated like them.

TRANSLATION

Just see how all Your playmates of Your own age have been cleansed and decorated with beautiful ornaments by their mothers. You should come here, and after You have taken Your bath, eaten Your lunch and been decorated with ornaments, You may play with Your friends again.

PURPORT

Generally young boys are competitive. If one friend has done something, another friend also wants to do something. Therefore mother Yaśodā pointed out how Krishna's playmates were decorated, so that Krishna might be induced to decorate Himself like them.
