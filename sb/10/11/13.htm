Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 11: The Childhood Pastimes of Krishna

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.11.13

nopeyātām yadāhūtau

krīdā-sańgena putrakau

yaśodām preshayām āsa

rohinī putra-vatsalām

SYNONYMS

na upeyātām — would not return home; yadā — when; āhūtau — They were called back from playing; krīdā-sańgena — because of so much attachment to playing with other boys; putrakau — the two sons (Krishna and Balarāma); yaśodām preshayām āsa — sent mother Yaśodā to call Them; rohinī — mother Rohinī; putra-vatsalām — because mother Yaśodā was a more affectionate mother to Krishna and Balarāma.

TRANSLATION

Because of being too attached to playing with the other boys, Krishna and Balarāma did not return upon being called by Rohinī. Therefore Rohinī sent mother Yaśodā to call Them back, because mother Yaśodā was more affectionate to Krishna and Balarāma.

PURPORT

Yaśodām preshayām āsa. These very words show that since Krishna and Balarāma did not care to return in response to the order of Rohinī, Rohinī thought that if Yaśodā called They would have to return, for Yaśodā was more affectionate to Krishna and Balarāma.
