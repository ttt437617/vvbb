Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 11: The Childhood Pastimes of Krishna

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.11.28

vanam vrindāvanam nāma

paśavyam nava-kānanam

gopa-gopī-gavām sevyam

punyādri-trina-vīrudham

SYNONYMS

vanam — another forest; vrindāvanam nāma — named Vrindāvana; paśavyam — a very suitable place for maintenance of the cows and other animals; nava-kānanam — there are many new gardenlike places; gopa-gopī-gavām — for all the cowherd men, the members of their families, and the cows; sevyam — a very happy, very suitable place; punya-adri — there are nice mountains; trina — plants; vīrudham — and creepers.

TRANSLATION

Between Nandeśvara and Mahāvana is a place named Vrindāvana. This place is very suitable because it is lush with grass, plants and creepers for the cows and other animals. It has nice gardens and tall mountains and is full of facilities for the happiness of all the gopas and gopīs and our animals.

PURPORT

Vrindāvana is situated between Nandeśvara and Mahāvana. Formerly the cowherd men had shifted to Mahāvana, but still there were disturbances. Therefore the cowherd men selected Vrindāvana, which was between the two villages, and decided to go there.
