Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 11: The Childhood Pastimes of Krishna

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.11.9

darśayams tad-vidām loka

ātmano bhritya-vaśyatām

vrajasyovāha vai harsham

bhagavān bāla-ceshtitaih

SYNONYMS

darśayan — exhibiting; tat-vidām — unto persons who can understand Krishna's activities; loke — throughout the whole world; ātmanah — of Himself; bhritya-vaśyatām — how He is agreeable to carrying out the orders of His servants, His devotees; vrajasya — of Vrajabhūmi; uvāha — executed; vai — indeed; harsham — pleasure; bhagavān — the Supreme Personality of Godhead; bāla-ceshtitaih — by His activities like those of a child trying to do so many things.

TRANSLATION

To pure devotees throughout the world who could understand His activities, the Supreme Personality of Godhead, Krishna, exhibited how much He can be subdued by His devotees, His servants. In this way He increased the pleasure of the Vrajavāsīs by His childhood activities.

PURPORT

That Krishna performed childhood activities to increase the pleasure of His devotees was another transcendental humor. He exhibited these activities not only to the inhabitants of Vrajabhūmi, but also to others, who were captivated by His external potency and opulence. Both the internal devotees, who were simply absorbed in love of Krishna, and the external devotees, who were captivated by His unlimited potency, were informed of Krishna's desire to be submissive to His servants.
