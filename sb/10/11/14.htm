Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 11: The Childhood Pastimes of Krishna

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.11.14

krīdantam sā sutam bālair

ativelam sahāgrajam

yaśodājohavīt krishnam

putra-sneha-snuta-stanī

SYNONYMS

krīdantam — engaged in playing; sā — mother Yaśodā; sutam — her son; bālaih — with the other boys; ati-velam — although it was too late; saha-agrajam — who was playing with His elder brother, Balarāma; yaśodā — mother Yaśodā; ajohavīt — called ("Krishna and Balarāma, come here!"); krishnam — unto Krishna; putra-sneha-snuta-stanī — while she was calling Them, milk flowed from her breast because of her ecstatic love and affection.

TRANSLATION

Krishna and Balarāma, being attached to Their play, were playing with the other boys although it was very late. Therefore mother Yaśodā called Them back for lunch. Because of her ecstatic love and affection for Krishna and Balarāma, milk flowed from her breasts.

PURPORT

The word ajohavīt means "calling them again and again." "Krishna and Balarāma," she called, "please come back. You are late for Your lunch. You have played sufficiently. Come back."
