Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 11: The Childhood Pastimes of Krishna

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.11.17

pratīkshate tvām dāśārha

bhokshyamāno vrajādhipah

ehy āvayoh priyam dhehi

sva-grihān yāta bālakāh

SYNONYMS

pratīkshate — is waiting; tvām — for both of You (Krishna and Balarāma); dāśārha — O Balarāma; bhokshyamānah — desiring to eat; vraja-adhipah — the King of Vraja, Nanda Mahārāja; ehi — come here; āvayoh — our; priyam — pleasure; dhehi — just consider; sva-grihān — to their respective homes; yāta — let them go; bālakāh — the other boys.

TRANSLATION

Nanda Mahārāja, the King of Vraja, is now waiting to eat. O my dear son Balarāma, he is waiting for You. Therefore, come back to please us. All the boys playing with You and Krishna should now go to their homes.

PURPORT

It appears that Nanda Mahārāja regularly took his food with his two sons, Krishna and Balarāma. Yaśodā told the other boys, "Now you should go to your homes." Father and son generally sit together, so mother Yaśodā requested Krishna and Balarāma to return, and she advised the other boys to go home so that their parents would not have to wait for them.
