Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 11: The Childhood Pastimes of Krishna

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.11.52

tadā bakārim sura-loka-vāsinah

samākiran nandana-mallikādibhih

samīdire cānaka-śańkha-samstavais

tad vīkshya gopāla-sutā visismire

SYNONYMS

tadā — at that time; baka-arim — unto the enemy of Bakāsura; sura-loka-vāsinah — the celestial denizens of the higher planets; samākiran — showered flowers; nandana-mallikā-ādibhih — with such flowers as mallikā, which are grown in Nandana-kānana; samīdire — also congratulated Him; ca — and; ānaka-śańkha-samstavaih — by celestial kettledrums and conchshells, accompanied with prayers; tat vīkshya — by seeing this; gopāla-sutāh — the cowherd boys; visismire — were struck with wonder.

TRANSLATION

At that time, the celestial denizens of the higher planetary system showered mallikā-pushpa, flowers grown in Nandana-kānana, upon Krishna, the enemy of Bakāsura. They also congratulated Him by sounding celestial kettledrums and conchshells and by offering prayers. Seeing this, the cowherd boys were struck with wonder.
