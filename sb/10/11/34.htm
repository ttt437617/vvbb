Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 11: The Childhood Pastimes of Krishna

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.11.34

tathā yaśodā-rohinyāv

ekam śakatam āsthite

rejatuh krishna-rāmābhyām

tat-kathā-śravanotsuke

SYNONYMS

tathā — as well as; yaśodā-rohinyau — both mother Yaśodā and mother Rohinī; ekam śakatam — on one bullock cart; āsthite — seated; rejatuh — very beautiful; krishna-rāmābhyām — Krishna and Balarāma, along with Their mothers; tat-kathā — of the pastimes of Krishna and Balarāma; śravana-utsuke — being situated in hearing with great transcendental pleasure.

TRANSLATION

Thus hearing about the pastimes of Krishna and Balarāma with great pleasure, mother Yaśodā and Rohinīdevī, so as not to be separated from Krishna and Balarāma for even a moment, got up with Them on one bullock cart. In this situation, they all looked very beautiful.

PURPORT

It appears that mother Yaśodā and Rohinī could not be separated from Krishna and Balarāma even for a moment. They used to pass their time either by taking care of Krishna and Balarāma or by chanting about Their pastimes. Thus mother Yaśodā and Rohinī looked very beautiful.
