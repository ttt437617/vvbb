Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 11: The Childhood Pastimes of Krishna

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.11.51

tam āpatantam sa nigrihya tundayor

dorbhyām bakam kamsa-sakham satām patih

paśyatsu bāleshu dadāra līlayā

mudāvaho vīranavad divaukasām

SYNONYMS

tam — unto Bakāsura; āpatantam — again endeavoring to attack Him; sah — Lord Krishna; nigrihya — capturing; tundayoh — by the beak; dorbhyām — with His arms; bakam — Bakāsura; kamsa-sakham — who was the friend and associate of Kamsa; satām patih — Lord Krishna, the master of the Vaishnavas; paśyatsu — while observing; bāleshu — all the cowherd boys; dadāra — bifurcated; līlayā — very easily; mudā-āvahah — this action was very much pleasing; vīrana-vat — like the grass called vīrana (as it is bifurcated); divaukasām — to all the denizens of heaven.

TRANSLATION

When Krishna, the leader of the Vaishnavas, saw that the demon Bakāsura, the friend of Kamsa, was endeavoring to attack Him, with His arms He captured the demon by the two halves of the beak, and in the presence of all the cowherd boys Krishna very easily bifurcated Him, as a child splits a blade of vīrana grass. By thus killing the demon, Krishna very much pleased the denizens of heaven.
