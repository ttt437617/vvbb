Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 11: The Childhood Pastimes of Krishna

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.11.24

muktah kathañcid rākshasyā

bāla-ghnyā bālako hy asau

harer anugrahān nūnam

anaś copari nāpatat

SYNONYMS

muktah — was delivered; kathañcit — somehow or other; rākshasyāh — from the hands of the Rākshasī Pūtanā; bāla-ghnyāh — who was determined to kill small children; bālakah — especially the child Krishna; hi — because; asau — He; hareh anugrahāt — by the mercy of the Supreme Personality of Godhead; nūnam — indeed; anah ca — and the handcart; upari — on top of the child; na — not; apatat — did fall down.

TRANSLATION

The child Krishna, simply by the mercy of the Supreme Personality of Godhead, was somehow or other rescued from the hands of the Rākshasī Pūtanā, who was determined to kill Him. Then, again by the mercy of the Supreme Godhead, the handcart missed falling upon the child.
