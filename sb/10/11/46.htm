Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 11: The Childhood Pastimes of Krishna

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.11.46

svam svam vatsa-kulam sarve

pāyayishyanta ekadā

gatvā jalāśayābhyāśam

pāyayitvā papur jalam

SYNONYMS

svam svam — own respective; vatsa-kulam — the group of calves; sarve — all the boys and Krishna and Balarāma; pāyayishyantah — desiring to have them drink water; ekadā — one day; gatvā — going; jala-āśaya-abhyāśam — near the water tank; pāyayitvā — after allowing the animals to drink water; papuh jalam — they also drank water.

TRANSLATION

One day all the boys, including Krishna and Balarāma, each boy taking his own group of calves, brought the calves to a reservoir of water, desiring to allow them to drink. After the animals drank water, the boys drank water there also.
