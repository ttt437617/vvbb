Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 11: The Childhood Pastimes of Krishna

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.11.6

ulūkhalam vikarshantam

dāmnā baddham svam ātmajam

vilokya nandah prahasad-

vadano vimumoca ha

SYNONYMS

ulūkhalam — the wooden mortar; vikarshantam — dragging; dāmnā — by the rope; baddham — bound; svam ātmajam — his own son Krishna; vilokya — by seeing; nandah — Mahārāja Nanda; prahasat-vadanah — whose face began to smile when he saw the wonderful child; vimumoca ha — released Him from the bonds.

TRANSLATION

When Nanda Mahārāja saw his own son bound with ropes to the wooden mortar and dragging it, he smiled and released Krishna from His bonds.

PURPORT

Nanda Mahārāja was surprised that Yaśodā, Krishna's mother, could have bound her beloved child in such a way. Krishna was exchanging love with her. How then could she have been so cruel as to bind Him to the wooden mortar? Nanda Mahārāja understood this exchange of love, and therefore he smiled and released Krishna. In other words, as Krishna, the Supreme Personality of Godhead, binds a living entity in fruitive activities, He binds mother Yaśodā and Nanda Mahārāja in parental affection. This is His pastime.
