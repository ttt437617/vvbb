Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 23: The Brāhmanas' Wives Blessed

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.23.45

nanu svārtha-vimūdhānām

pramattānām grihehayā

aho nah smārayām āsa

gopa-vākyaih satām gatih

SYNONYMS

nanu — indeed; sva-artha — about their own true benefit; vimūdhānām — who were bewildered; pramattānām — who were intoxicated; griha-īhayā — with their household endeavors; aho — ah; nah — us; smārayām āsa — He reminded about; gopa-vākyaih — by the words of cowherds; satām — of the transcendental souls; gatih — the ultimate destination .

TRANSLATION

Indeed, infatuated as we are with our household affairs, we have deviated completely from the real aim of our life. But now just see how the Lord, through the words of these simple cowherd boys, has reminded us of the ultimate destination of all true transcendentalists.
