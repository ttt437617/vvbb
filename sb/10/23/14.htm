Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 23: The Brāhmanas' Wives Blessed

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.23.14

mām jñāpayata patnībhyah

sa-sańkarshanam āgatam

dāsyanti kāmam annam vah

snigdhā mayy ushitā dhiyā

SYNONYMS

mām — Me; jñāpayata — please announce; patnībhyah — to the wives; sa-sańkarshanam — together with Lord Balarāma; āgatam — arrived; dāsyanti — they will give; kāmam — as much as you desire; annam — food; vah — to you; snigdhāh — affectionate; mayi — in Me; ushitāh — residing; dhiyā — with their intelligence.

TRANSLATION

[Lord Krishna said:] Tell the wives of the brāhmanas that I have come here with Lord Sańkarshana. They will certainly give you all the food you want, for they are most affectionate toward Me and, indeed, with their intelligence reside in Me alone.

PURPORT

While physically the wives of the brāhmanas remained at home, within their minds they resided in the Supreme Lord Krishna because of intense affection for Him. Śrīla Viśvanātha Cakravartī Thākura explains that the reason Lord Krishna did not have the cowherd boys tell the brāhmanas' wives He was hungry is that He knew this would severely distress these devoted ladies. Simply out of affection for Lord Krishna, however, the wives would be happy to give all the food requested of them. They would not heed their husbands' prohibitions, since they resided within the Lord through their transcendental intelligence.
