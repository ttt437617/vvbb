Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 23: The Brāhmanas' Wives Blessed

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.23.46

anyathā pūrna-kāmasya

kaivalyādy-aśishām pateh

īśitavyaih kim asmābhir

īśasyaitad vidambanam

SYNONYMS

anyathā — otherwise; pūrna-kāmasya — of Him whose every possible desire is fulfilled; kaivalya — of liberation; ādi — and others; āśishām — benedictions; pateh — the master; īśitavyaih — with those who are meant to be controlled; kim — what; asmābhih — with us; īśasya — of Him who is the absolute controller; etat — this; vidambanam — pretense.

TRANSLATION

Otherwise, why would the supreme controller — whose every desire is already fulfilled and who is the master of liberation and all other transcendental benedictions — enact this pretense with us, who are always to be controlled by Him?

PURPORT

Although Lord Krishna is the Absolute Truth, He humbly sent His cowherd boyfriends to beg food from the brāhmanas. In so doing, He exposed the brāhmanas' foolish arrogance and established the glories of His own transcendental beauty by attracting their very wives to surrender at His lotus feet.
