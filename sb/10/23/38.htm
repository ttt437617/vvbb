Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 23: The Brāhmanas' Wives Blessed

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.23.38

athānusmritya viprās te

anvatapyan kritāgasah

yad viśveśvarayor yācñām

ahanma nri-vidambayoh

SYNONYMS

atha — then; anusmritya — coming to their senses; viprāh — the brāhmanas; te — they; anvatapyan — felt great remorse; krita-agasah — having committed sinful offenses; yat — because; viśva-īśvarayoh — of the two Lords of the universe, Krishna and Balarāma; yācñām — the humble supplication; ahanma — we transgressed; nr-vidambayoh — of those who were deceptively appearing as human beings.

TRANSLATION

The brāhmanas then came to their senses and began to feel great remorse. They thought, "We have sinned, for we have denied the request of the two Lords of the universe, who deceptively appeared as ordinary human beings."

PURPORT

Lord Krishna and Lord Balarāma did not try to deceive the brāhmanas: They straightforwardly requested food from them. Rather, the brāhmanas deceived themselves, as indicated by the Sanskrit word nri-vidambayoh, which means that Krishna and Balarāma are bewildering for an ordinary human being who considers Them also to be human. Still, because the wives of the brāhmanas were great devotees of the Lord, the foolish brāhmanas received spiritual benefit and finally came to their senses.
