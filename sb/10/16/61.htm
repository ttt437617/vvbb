Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 16: Krishna Chastises the Serpent Kāliya

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.16.61

ya etat samsmaren martyas

tubhyam mad-anuśāsanam

kīrtayann ubhayoh sandhyor

na yushmad bhayam āpnuyāt

SYNONYMS

yah — who; etat — this; samsmaret — remembers; martyah — a mortal; tubhyam — to you; mat — My; anuśāsanam — command; kīrtayan — chanting; ubhayoh — at both; sandhyoh — junctures of the day; na — not; yushmat — from you; bhayam — fear; āpnuyāt — obtains.

TRANSLATION

If a mortal being attentively remembers My command to you — to leave Vrindāvana and go to the ocean — and narrates this account at sunrise and sunset, he will never be afraid of you.
