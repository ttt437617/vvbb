Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 16: Krishna Chastises the Serpent Kāliya

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.16.20

gopyo 'nurakta-manaso bhagavaty anante

tat-sauhrida-smita-viloka-girah smarantyah

graste 'hinā priyatame bhriśa-duhkha-taptāh

śūnyam priya-vyatihritam dadriśus tri-lokam

SYNONYMS

gopyah — the cowherd girls; anurakta-manasah — their minds very much attached to Him; bhagavati — the Supreme Personality of Godhead; anante — the unlimited one; tat — His; sauhrida — loving; smita — smiling; viloka — glances; girah — and words; smarantyah — remembering; graste — being seized; ahinā — by the serpent; priya-tame — their most dear; bhriśa — extremely; duhkha — by pain; taptāh — tormented; śūnyam — empty; priya-vyatihritam — deprived of their darling; dadriśuh — they saw; tri-lokam — all the three worlds (the entire universe).

TRANSLATION

When the young gopīs, whose minds were constantly attached to Krishna, the unlimited Supreme Lord, saw that He was now within the grips of the serpent, they remembered His loving friendship, His smiling glances and His talks with them. Burning with great sorrow, they saw the entire universe as void.
