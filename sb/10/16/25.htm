Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 16: Krishna Chastises the Serpent Kāliya

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.16.25

tam jihvayā dvi-śikhayā parilelihānam

dve srikvanī hy ati-karāla-vishāgni-drishtim

krīdann amum parisasāra yathā khagendro

babhrāma so 'py avasaram prasamīkshamānah

SYNONYMS

tam — him, Kāliya; jihvayā — with his tongue; dvi-śikhayā — having two points; parilelihānam — repeatedly licking; dve — his two; srikvanī — lips; hi — indeed; ati-karāla — most terrible; visha-agni — full of poisonous fire; drishtim — whose glance; krīdan — playing; amum — him; parisasāra — moved around; yathā — just as; khaga-indrah — the king of birds, Garuda; babhrāma — wandered around; sah — Kāliya; api — also; avasaram — the opportunity (to strike); prasamīkshamānah — carefully looking for.

TRANSLATION

Again and again Kāliya licked his lips with his bifurcated tongues as He stared at Krishna with a glance full of terrible, poisonous fire. But Krishna playfully circled around him, just as Garuda would play with a snake. In response, Kāliya also moved about, looking for an opportunity to bite the Lord.

PURPORT

Lord Krishna moved around the serpent so skillfully that Kāliya could find no opportunity to bite Him. Thus the snake was defeated by Śrī Krishna's transcendental agility.
