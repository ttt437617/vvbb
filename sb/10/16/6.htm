Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 16: Krishna Chastises the Serpent Kāliya

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.16.6

tam canda-vega-visha-vīryam avekshya tena

dushtām nadīm ca khala-samyamanāvatārah

krishnah kadambam adhiruhya tato 'ti-tuńgam

āsphotya gādha-raśano nyapatad vishode

SYNONYMS

tam — him, Kāliya; canda-vega — of fearsome power; visha — the poison; vīryam — whose strength; avekshya — seeing; tena — by him; dushtām — contaminated; nadīm — the river; ca — and; khala — the envious demons; samyamana — for subduing; avatārah — whose descent from the spiritual world; krishnah — Lord Krishna; kadambam — a kadamba tree; adhiruhya — climbing up on; tatah — from it; ati-tuńgam — very high; āsphotya — slapping His arms; gādha-raśanah — tying His belt firmly; nyapatat — He jumped; visha-ude — into the poisoned water.

TRANSLATION

Lord Krishna saw how the Kāliya serpent had polluted the Yamunā River with his terribly powerful poison. Since Krishna had descended from the spiritual world specifically to subdue envious demons, the Lord immediately climbed to the top of a very high kadamba tree and prepared Himself for battle. He tightened His belt, slapped His arms and then jumped into the poisonous water.

PURPORT

According to the ācāryas, Lord Krishna also tied back the locks of His hair as He prepared to do battle with Kāliya.
