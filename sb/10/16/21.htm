Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 16: Krishna Chastises the Serpent Kāliya

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.16.21

tāh krishna-mātaram apatyam anupravishtām

tulya-vyathāh samanugrihya śucah sravantyah

tās tā vraja-priya-kathāh kathayantya āsan

krishnānane 'rpita-driśo mritaka-pratīkāh

SYNONYMS

tāh — those ladies; krishna-mātaram — the mother of Krishna (Yaśodā); apatyam — upon her son; anupravishtām — fixing her vision; tulya — equally; vyathāh — pained; samanugrihya — holding back firmly; śucah — floods of sorrow; sravantyah — spilling forth; tāh tāh — each of them; vraja-priya — of the darling of Vraja; kathāh — topics; kathayantyah — speaking; āsan — they stood; krishna-ānane — unto the face of Lord Krishna; arpita — offered; driśah — their eyes; mritaka — corpses; pratīkāh — resembling.

TRANSLATION

Although the elder gopīs were feeling just as much distress as she and were pouring forth a flood of sorrowful tears, they had to forcibly hold back Krishna's mother, whose consciousness was totally absorbed in her son. Standing like corpses, with their eyes fixed upon His face, these gopīs each took turns recounting the pastimes of the darling of Vraja.
