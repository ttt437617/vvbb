Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 16: Krishna Chastises the Serpent Kāliya

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.16.7

sarpa-hradah purusha-sāra-nipāta-vega-

sańkshobhitoraga-vishocchvasitāmbu-rāśih

paryak pluto visha-kashāya-bibhīshanormir

dhāvan dhanuh-śatam ananta-balasya kim tat

SYNONYMS

sarpa-hradah — the serpent's lake; purusha-sāra — of the most exalted Supreme Personality of Godhead; nipāta-vega — by the force of the fall; sańkshobhita — completely agitated; uraga — of the snakes; visha-ucchvasita — breathed upon with the poison; ambu-rāśih — all of whose water; paryak — on all sides; plutah — flooding; visha-kashāya — because of the contamination of the poison; bibhīshana — fearsome; ūrmih — whose waves; dhāvan — flowing; dhanuh-śatam — the extent of one hundred bow-lengths; ananta-balasya — for Him whose strength is immeasurable; kim — what; tat — that.

TRANSLATION

When the Supreme Personality of Godhead landed in the serpent's lake, the snakes there became extremely agitated and began breathing heavily, further polluting it with volumes of poison. The force of the Lord's entrance into the lake caused it to overflow on all sides, and poisonous, fearsome waves flooded the surrounding lands up to a distance of one hundred bow-lengths. This is not at all amazing, however, for the Supreme Lord possesses infinite strength.
