Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 16: Krishna Chastises the Serpent Kāliya

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.16.28

yad yac chiro na namate 'ńga śataika-śīrshnas

tat tan mamarda khara-danda-dharo 'ńghri-pātaih

kshīnāyusho bhramata ulbanam āsyato 'sriń

nasto vaman parama-kaśmalam āpa nāgah

SYNONYMS

yat yat — whichever; śirah — heads; na namate — would not bow down; ańga — my dear King Parīkshit; śata-eka-śīrshnah — of him who had 101 heads; tat tat — those; mamarda — trampled down; khara — on those who are evil; danda — punishment; dharah — the Lord who exerts; ańghri-pātaih — with the blows of His feet; kshīna-āyushah — of Kāliya, whose life was becoming depleted; bhramatah — who was still moving about; ulbanam — terrible; āsyatah — from his mouths; asrik — blood; nastah — from his nostrils; vaman — vomiting; parama — extreme; kaśmalam — trouble; āpa — experienced; nāgah — the serpent.

TRANSLATION

My dear King, Kāliya had 101 prominent heads, and when one of them would not bow down, Lord Śrī Krishna, who inflicts punishment on cruel wrong-doers, would smash that stubborn head by striking it with His feet. Then, as Kāliya entered his death throes, he began wheeling his heads around and vomiting ghastly blood from his mouths and nostrils. The serpent thus experienced extreme pain and misery.
