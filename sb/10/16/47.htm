Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 16: Krishna Chastises the Serpent Kāliya

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.16.47

avyākrita-vihārāya

sarva-vyākrita-siddhaye

hrishīkeśa namas te 'stu

munaye mauna-śīline

SYNONYMS

avyākrita-vihārāya — to Him whose glories are unfathomable; sarva-vyākrita — the creation and manifestation of all things; siddhaye — to Him who can be understood as existent on the basis of; hrishīka-īśa — O motivator of the senses; namah — obeisances; te — unto You, astu — let there be; munaye — to the silent; mauna-śīline — to Him who acts in silence.

TRANSLATION

O Lord Hrishīkeśa, master of the senses, please let us offer our obeisances unto You, whose pastimes are inconceivably glorious. Your existence can be inferred from the necessity for a creator and revealer of all cosmic manifestations. But although Your devotees can understand You in this way, to the nondevotees You remain silent, absorbed in self-satisfaction.
