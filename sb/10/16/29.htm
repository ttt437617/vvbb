Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 16: Krishna Chastises the Serpent Kāliya

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.16.29

tasyākshibhir garalam udvamatah śirahsu

yad yat samunnamati nihśvasato rushoccaih

nrityan padānunamayan damayām babhūva

pushpaih prapūjita iveha pumān purānah

SYNONYMS

tasya — of him; akshibhih — from the eyes; garalam — poisonous waste; udvamatah — who was vomiting; śirahsu — among the heads; yat yat — whichever; samunnamati — would rise up; nihśvasatah — who was breathing; rushā — out of anger; uccaih — heavily; nrityan — while dancing; padā — with His foot; anunamayan — making bow down; damayām babhūva — He subdued; pushpaih — with flowers; prapūjitah — being worshiped; iva — indeed; iha — on this occasion; pumān — the Personality of Godhead; purānah — original.

TRANSLATION

Exuding poisonous waste from his eyes, Kāliya, would occasionally dare to raise up one of his heads, which would breathe heavily with anger. Then the Lord would dance on it and subdue it, forcing it to bow down with His foot. The demigods took each of these exhibitions as an opportunity to worship Him, the primeval Personality of Godhead, with showers of flowers.
