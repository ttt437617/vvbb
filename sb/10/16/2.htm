Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 16: Krishna Chastises the Serpent Kāliya

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.16.2

śrī-rājovāca

katham antar-jale 'gādhe

nyagrihnād bhagavān ahim

sa vai bahu-yugāvāsam

yathāsīd vipra kathyatām

SYNONYMS

śrī-rājā uvāca — King Parīkshit said; katham — how; antah-jale — within the water; agādhe — unfathomable; nyagrihnāt — subdued; bhagavān — the Supreme Personality of Godhead; ahim — the serpent; sah — he, Kāliya; vai — indeed; bahu-yuga — for many ages; āvāsam — having residence; yathā — how; āsīt — so became; vipra — O learned brāhmana; kathyatām — please explain.

TRANSLATION

King Parīkshit inquired: O learned sage, please explain how the Supreme Personality of Godhead chastised the serpent Kāliya within the unfathomable waters of the Yamunā, and how it was that Kāliya had been living there for so many ages.
