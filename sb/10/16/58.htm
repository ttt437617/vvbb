Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 16: Krishna Chastises the Serpent Kāliya

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.16.58

vayam ca tatra bhagavan

sarpā jāty-uru-manyavah

katham tyajāmas tvan-māyām

dustyajām mohitāh svayam

SYNONYMS

vayam — we; ca — and; tatra — within that material creation; bhagavan — O Supreme Personality of Godhead; sarpāh — serpents; jāti — by species; uru-manyavah — too much absorbed in anger; katham — how; tyajāmah — we can give up; tvat-māyām — Your illusory potency; dustyajām — which is impossible to give up; mohitāh — bewildered; svayam — on our own.

TRANSLATION

O Supreme Personality of Godhead, among all the species within Your material creation, we serpents are by nature always enraged. Being thus deluded by Your illusory energy, which is very difficult to give up, how can we possibly give it up on our own?

PURPORT

Kāliya is here indirectly begging for the Lord's mercy, realizing that on his own he can never become free from illusion and suffering. Only by surrendering to the Lord and obtaining His mercy can one be released from the painful conditions of material life.
