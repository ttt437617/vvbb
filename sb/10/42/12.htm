Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 42: The Breaking of the Sacrificial Bow

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.42.12

eshyāmi te griham su-bhru

pumsām ādhi-vikarśanam

sādhitārtho 'grihānām nah

pānthānām tvam parāyanam

SYNONYMS

eshyāmi — I will go; te — your; griham — to the house; su-bhru — O you who have beautiful eyebrows; pumsām — of men; ādhi — the mental distress; vikarśanam — which eradicates; sādhita — having accomplished; arthah — My purpose; agrihānām — who have no home; nah — for Us; pānthānām — who are traveling on the road; tvam — you; para — the best; ayanam — shelter.

TRANSLATION

[Lord Krishna said:] O lady with beautiful eyebrows, as soon as I fulfill My purpose I will certainly visit your house, where men can relieve their anxiety. Indeed, you are the best refuge for Us homeless travelers.

PURPORT

By the word agrihānām, Śrī Krishna indicated not only that He had no fixed residence but also that He was not yet married.
