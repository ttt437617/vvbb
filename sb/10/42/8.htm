Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 42: The Breaking of the Sacrificial Bow

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.42.8

sā tadarju-samānāńgī

brihac-chroni-payodharā

mukunda-sparśanāt sadyo

babhūva pramadottamā

SYNONYMS

sā — she; tadā — then; riju — straight; samāna — even; ańgī — her limbs; brihat — large; śroni — her hips; payah-dharā — and breasts; mukunda-sparśanāt — by the touch of Lord Mukunda; sadyah — suddenly; babhūva — became; pramadā — a woman; uttamā — most perfect.

TRANSLATION

Simply by Lord Mukunda's touch, Trivakrā was suddenly transformed into an exquisitely beautiful woman with straight, evenly proportioned limbs and large hips and breasts.
