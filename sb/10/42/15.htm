Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 42: The Breaking of the Sacrificial Bow

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.42.15

tatah paurān pricchamāno

dhanushah sthānam acyutah

tasmin pravishto dadriśe

dhanur aindram ivādbhutam

SYNONYMS

tatah — then; paurān — from the city residents; pricchamānah — inquiring about; dhanushah — of the bow; sthānam — the place; acyutah — the infallible Supreme Lord; tasmin — there; pravishtah — entering; dadriśe — He saw; dhanuh — the bow; aindram — that of Lord Indra; iva — like; adbhutam — amazing.

TRANSLATION

Lord Krishna then asked the local people where the arena was in which the bow sacrifice would take place. When He went there He saw the amazing bow, which resembled Lord Indra's.
