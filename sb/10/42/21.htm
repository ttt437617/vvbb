Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 42: The Breaking of the Sacrificial Bow

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.42.21

balam ca kamsa-prahitam

hatvā śālā-mukhāt tatah

nishkramya ceratur hrishtau

nirīkshya pura-sampadah

SYNONYMS

balam — an armed force; ca — and; kamsa-prahitam — sent by Kamsa; hatvā — having killed; śālā — of the sacrificial arena; mukhāt — by the gate; tatah — then; nishkramya — exciting; ceratuh — the two of Them walked along; hrishtau — happy; nirīkshya — observing; pura — of the city; sampadah — the riches.

TRANSLATION

After also killing a contingent of soldiers sent by Kamsa, Krishna and Balarāma left the sacrificial arena by its main gate and continued Their walk about the city, happily looking at the opulent sights.
