Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 42: The Breaking of the Sacrificial Bow

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.42.26-27

kamsas tu dhanusho bhańgam

rakshinām sva-balasya ca

vadham niśamya govinda-

rāma-vikrīditam param

dīrgha-prajāgaro bhīto

durnimittāni durmatih

bahūny acashtobhayathā

mrityor dautya-karāni ca

SYNONYMS

kamsah — King Kamsa; tu — but; dhanushah — of the bow; bhańgam — the breaking; rakshinām — of the guards; sva — his; balasya — of the army; ca — and; vadham — the killing; niśamya — hearing of; govinda-rāma — of Krishna and Balarāma; vikrīditam — the playing; param — merely; dīrgha — for a long time; prajāgarah — remaining awake; bhītah — afraid; durnimittāni — bad omens; durmatih — the wicked-minded; bahūni — many; acashta — saw; ubhayathā — in both states (sleep and wakefulness); mrityoh — of death; dautya-karāni — the messengers; ca — and.

TRANSLATION

Wicked King Kamsa, on the other hand, was terrified, having heard how Krishna and Balarāma had broken the bow and killed his guards and soldiers, all simply as a game. He remained awake for a long time, and both while awake and while dreaming he saw many bad omens, messengers of death.
