Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 42: The Breaking of the Sacrificial Bow

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.42.7

padbhyām ākramya prapade

dry-ańguly-uttāna-pāninā

pragrihya cibuke 'dhyātmam

udanīnamad acyutah

SYNONYMS

padbhyām — with both His feet; ākramya — pressing down; prapade — on her toes; dvi — having two; ańguli — fingers; uttāna — pointing upwards; pāninā — with His hands; pragrihya — taking hold of; cibuke — her chin; adhyātmam — her body; udanīnamat — He raised; acyutah — Lord Krishna.

TRANSLATION

Pressing down on her toes with both His feet, Lord Acyuta placed one upward-pointing finger of each hand under her chin and straightened up her body.
