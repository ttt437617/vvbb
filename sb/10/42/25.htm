Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 42: The Breaking of the Sacrificial Bow

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.42.25

avaniktāńghri-yugalau

bhuktvā kshīropasecanam

ūshatus tām sukham rātrim

jñātvā kamsa-cikīrshitam

SYNONYMS

avanikta — bathed; ańghri-yugalau — the two feet of each of Them; bhuktvā — eating; kshīra-upasecanam — boiled rice sprinkled with milk; ūshatuh — They stayed there; tām — for that; sukham — comfortably; rātrim — night; jñātvā — knowing; kamsa-cikīrshitam — what Kamsa intended to do.

TRANSLATION

After Krishna's and Balarāma's feet were bathed, the two Lords ate rice with milk. Then, although knowing what Kamsa intended to do, They spent the night there comfortably.
