Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 42: The Breaking of the Sacrificial Bow

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.42.10

ehi vīra griham yāmo

na tvām tyaktum ihotsahe

tvayonmathita-cittāyāh

prasīda purusharshabha

SYNONYMS

ehi — come; vīra — O hero; griham — to my house; yāmah — let us go; na — not; tvām — You; tyaktum — to leave; iha — here; utsahe — I can bear; tvayā — by You; unmathita — agitated; cittāyāh — on her whose mind; prasīda — please have mercy; purusha-rishabha — O best of men.

TRANSLATION

[Trivakrā said:] Come, O hero, let us go to my house. I cannot bear to leave You here. O best of males, please take pity on me, since You have agitated my mind.

PURPORT

Śrīla Viśvanātha Cakravartī supplies the following conversation:

Krishna: Is it for the purpose of dining that you're inviting Me to your house?

Trivakrā: I simply can't leave You here.

Krishna: But people here on the King's road will misconstrue what you're saying and laugh. Therefore please don't speak like this.

Trivakrā: I can't help being agitated. You made the mistake of touching me. It's not my fault.
