Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 42: The Breaking of the Sacrificial Bow

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.42.36

vādyamānesu tūryeshu

malla-tālottareshu ca

mallāh sv-alańkritāh driptāh

sopādhyāyāh samāsata

SYNONYMS

vādyamāneshu — as they were being played; tūryeshu — the musical instruments; malla — suitable for wrestling; tāla — with meters; uttareshu — prominent; ca — and; mallāh — the wrestlers; su-alańkritāh — well ornamented; driptāh — proud; sa-upādhyāyāh — together with their instructors; samāsata — came and sat down.

TRANSLATION

While the musical instruments loudly played in the rhythmic meters appropriate for wrestling matches, the lavishly ornamented wrestlers proudly entered the arena with their coaches and sat down.
