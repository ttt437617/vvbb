Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 3: The Birth of Lord Krishna

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.3.6

jaguh kinnara-gandharvās

tushtuvuh siddha-cāranāh

vidyādharyaś ca nanritur

apsarobhih samam mudā

SYNONYMS

jaguh — recited auspicious songs; kinnara-gandharvāh — the Kinnaras and Gandharvas, inhabitants of various planets in the heavenly planetary system; tushtuvuh — offered their respective prayers; siddha-cāranāh — the Siddhas and Cāranas, other inhabitants of the heavenly planets; vidyādharyah ca — and the Vidyādharīs, another group of inhabitants of the heavenly planets; nanrituh — danced in transcendental bliss; apsarobhih — the Apsarās, beautiful dancers in the heavenly kingdom; samam — along with; mudā — in great jubilation.

TRANSLATION

The Kinnaras and Gandharvas began to sing auspicious songs, the Siddhas and Cāranas offered auspicious prayers, and the Vidyādharīs, along with the Apsarās, began to dance in jubilation.
