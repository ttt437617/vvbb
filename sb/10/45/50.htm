Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 45: Krishna Rescues His Teacher's Son

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.45.50

samanandan prajāh sarvā

drishtvā rāma-janārdanau

apaśyantyo bahv ahāni

nashta-labdha-dhanā iva

SYNONYMS

samanandan — rejoiced; prajāh — the citizens; sarvāh — all; drishtvā — seeing; rāma-janārdanau — Balarāma and Krishna; apaśyantyah — not having seen; bahu — for many; ahāni — days; nashta — lost; labdha — and regained; dhanāh — those whose wealth; iva — like.

TRANSLATION

All the citizens rejoiced upon seeing Krishna and Balarāma, whom they had not seen for many days. The people felt just like those who have lost their wealth and then regained it.

Thus end the purports of the humble servants of His Divine Grace A. C. Bhaktivedanta Swami Prabhupāda to the Tenth Canto, Forty-fifth Chapter, of the Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam, entitled "Krishna Rescues His Teacher's Son."
