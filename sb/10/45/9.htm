Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 45: Krishna Rescues His Teacher's Son

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.45.9

tat kshantum arhathas tāta

mātar nau para-tantrayoh

akurvator vām śuśrūshām

klishtayor durhridā bhriśam

SYNONYMS

tat — that; kshantum — forgive; arhathah — you may please; tāta — O Father; mātah — 0 Mother; nau — on the part of Us; para-tantrayoh — who are under the control of others; akurvatoh — not executing; vām — your; śuśrūshām — service; klishtayoh — caused pain; durhridā — by the hardhearted (Kamsa); bhriśam — greatly.

TRANSLATION

Dear Father and Mother, please forgive Us for not serving you. We are not independent and have been greatly frustrated by cruel Kamsa.

PURPORT

According to Sanskrit grammar, the words para-tantrayoh and klishtayoh may also refer to Vasudeva and Devakī. Actually, Vasudeva and Devakī were under the control of Providence and were disturbed by the activities of Kamsa, whereas Śrī Krishna is always the absolute Personality of Godhead.
