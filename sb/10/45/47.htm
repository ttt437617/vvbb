Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 45: Krishna Rescues His Teacher's Son

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.45.47

śrī-gurur uvāca

samyak sampādito vatsa

bhavadbhyām guru-nishkrayah

ko nu yushmad-vidha-guroh

kāmānām avaśishyate

SYNONYMS

śrī-guruh uvāca — Their spiritual master, Sāndīpani Muni, said; samyak — completely; sampāditah — fulfilled; vatsa — my dear boy; bhavadbhyām — by You two; guru-nishkrayah — the remuneration of one's guru; kah — which; nu — indeed; yushmat-vidha — of persons like You; guroh — for the spiritual master; kāmānām — of his desires; avaśishyate — remains.

TRANSLATION

The spiritual master said: My dear boys, You two have completely fulfilled the disciple's obligation to reward his spiritual master. Indeed, with disciples like You, what further desires could a guru have?
