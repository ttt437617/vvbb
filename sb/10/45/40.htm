Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 45: Krishna Rescues His Teacher's Son

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.45.40

śrī-samudra uvāca

na cāhārsham aham deva

daityah pañcajano mahān

antar-jala-carah krishna

śańkha-rūpa-dharo 'surah

SYNONYMS

śrī-samudrah uvāca — the ocean personified said; na — not; ca — and; ahārsham — did take (him) away; aham — I; deva — O Lord; daityah — a descendant of Diti; pañcajanah — named Pañcajana; mahān — powerful; antah — within; jala — the water; carah — going; krishna — O Krishna; śańkha — of a conch; rūpa — the form; dharah — assuming; asurah — the demon.

TRANSLATION

The ocean replied: O Lord Krishna, it was not I who abducted him, but a demonic descendant of Diti named Pañcajana, who travels in the water in the form of a conch.

PURPORT

Clearly the demon Pañcajana was too powerful for the ocean to control; otherwise the ocean would have prevented such an unlawful act.
