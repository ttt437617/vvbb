Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 45: Krishna Rescues His Teacher's Son

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.45.6

yas tayor ātmajah kalpa

ātmanā ca dhanena ca

vrittim na dadyāt tam pretya

sva-māmsam khādayanti hi

SYNONYMS

yah — who; tayoh — of them; ātma-jah — a son; kalpah — capable; ātmanā — with his physical resources; ca — and; dhanena — with his wealth; ca — also; vrittim — a livelihood; na dadyāt — does not give; tam — him; pretya — after passing away; sva — his own; māmsam — flesh; khādayanti — they make eat; hi — indeed.

TRANSLATION

A son who, though able to do so, fails to provide for his parents with his physical resources and wealth is forced after his death to eat his own flesh.
