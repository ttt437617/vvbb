Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 45: Krishna Rescues His Teacher's Son

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.45.32

yathopasādya tau dāntau

gurau vrittim aninditām

grāhayantāv upetau sma

bhaktyā devam ivādritau

SYNONYMS

yathā — fittingly; upasādya — obtaining; tau — Them; dāntau — who were self-controlled; gurau — to one's spiritual master; vrittim — service; aninditām — irreproachable; grāhayantau — making others take to; upetau — approaching for service; sma — indeed; bhaktyā — with devotion; devam — the Supreme Lord; iva — as if; ādritau — respected (by the guru).

TRANSLATION

Sāndīpani thought very highly of these two self-controlled disciples, whom he had obtained so fortuitously. By serving him as devotedly as one would serve the Supreme Lord Himself, They showed others an irreproachable example of how to worship the spiritual master.
