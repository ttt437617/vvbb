Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 45: Krishna Rescues His Teacher's Son

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.45.19

tatra pravayaso 'py āsan

yuvāno 'ti-balaujasah

pibanto 'kshair mukundasya

mukhāmbuja-sudhām muhuh

SYNONYMS

tatra — there (in Mathurā); pravayasah — the most elderly; api — even; āsan — were; yuvānah — youthful; ati — having abundant; bala — strength; ojasah — and vitality; pibantah — drinking; akshaih — with their eyes; mukundasya — of Lord Krishna; mukha-ambuja — of the lotus face; sudhām — the nectar; muhuh — repeatedly.

TRANSLATION

Even the most elderly inhabitants of the city appeared youthful, full of strength and vitality, for with their eyes they constantly drank the elixir of Lord Mukunda's lotus face.
