Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 45: Krishna Rescues His Teacher's Son

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.45.49

gurunaivam anujñātau

rathenānila-ramhasā

āyātau sva-puram tāta

parjanya-ninadena vai

SYNONYMS

gurunā — by Their spiritual master; evam — in this way; anujñātau — given leave; rathena — in Their chariot; anila — like the wind; ramhasā — whose speed; āyātau — came; sva — to Their own; puram — city (Mathurā); tāta — my dear (King Parīkshit); parjanya — like a cloud; ninadena — whose reverberation; vai — indeed.

TRANSLATION

Thus receiving Their guru's permission to leave, the two Lords returned to Their city on Their chariot, which moved as swiftly as the wind and resounded like a cloud.
