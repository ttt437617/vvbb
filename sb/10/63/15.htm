Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 63: Lord Krishna Fights with Bānāsura

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.63.15

skandah pradyumna-bānaughair

ardyamānah samantatah

asrig vimuñcan gātrebhyah

śikhināpakramad ranāt

SYNONYMS

skandah — Kārtikeya; pradyumna-bāna — of Pradyumna's arrows; oghaih — by the torrents; ardyamānah — distressed; samantatah — on all sides; asrik — blood; vimuñcan — exuding; gātrebhyah — from his limbs; śikhinā — on his peacock carrier; apākramat — went away; ranāt — from the battlefield.

TRANSLATION

Lord Kārtikeya was distressed by the flood of Pradyumna's arrows raining down from all sides, and thus he fled the battlefield on his peacock as blood poured from his limbs.
