Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 37: The Killing of the Demons Keśi and Vyoma

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.37.14

dishtyā te nihato daityo

līlayāyam hayākritih

yasya heshita-santrastās

tyajanty animishā divam

SYNONYMS

dishtyā — by (our) good fortune; te — by You; nihatah — killed; daityah — demon; līlayā — as a game; ayam — this; haya-ākritih — having the form of a horse; yasya — whose; heshita — by the neighing; santrastāh — terrified; tyajanti — abandon; animishāh — the demigods; divam — heaven.

TRANSLATION

The horse demon was so terrifying that his neighing frightened the demigods into leaving their heavenly kingdom. But by our good fortune You have enjoyed the sport of killing him.
