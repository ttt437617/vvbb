Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 37: The Killing of the Demons Keśi and Vyoma

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.37.33

guhā-pidhānam nirbhidya

gopān nihsārya kricchratah

stūyamānah surair gopaih

praviveśa sva-gokulam

SYNONYMS

guhā — of the cave; pidhānam — the blockage; nirbhidya — breaking; gopān — the cowherd boys; nihsārya — leading out; kricchratah — from the dangerous place; stūyamānah — being praised; suraih — by the demigods; gopaih — and by the cowherd boys; praviveśa — he entered; sva — His own; gokulam — cowherd village.

TRANSLATION

Krishna then smashed the boulder blocking the cave's entrance and led the trapped cowherd boys to safety. Thereafter, as the demigods and cowherd boys sang His glories, He returned to His cowherd village, Gokula.

Thus end the purports of the humble servants of His Divine Grace A. C. Bhaktivedanta Swami Prabhupāda to the Tenth Canto, Thirty-seventh Chapter, of the Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam, entitled "The Killing of the Demons Keśī and Vyoma."
