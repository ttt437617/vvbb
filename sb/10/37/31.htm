Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 37: The Killing of the Demons Keśi and Vyoma

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.37.31

sa nijam rūpam āsthāya

girīndra-sadriśam balī

icchan vimoktum ātmānam

nāśaknod grahanāturah

SYNONYMS

sah — he, the demon; nijam — his original; rūpam — form; āsthāya — assuming; giri-indra — a kingly mountain; sadriśam — just like; balī — powerful; icchan — wanting; vimoktum — to free; ātmānam — himself; na aśaknot — he was not able; grahana — by being forcibly held; āturah — debilitated.

TRANSLATION

The demon changed into his original form, as big and powerful as a great mountain. But try as he might to free himself, he could not do so, having lost his strength from being held in the Lord's tight grip.
