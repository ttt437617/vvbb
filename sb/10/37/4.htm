Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 37: The Killing of the Demons Keśi and Vyoma

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.37.4

tad vañcayitvā tam adhokshajo rushā

pragrihya dorbhyām parividhya pādayoh

sāvajñam utsrijya dhanuh-śatāntare

yathoragam tārkshya-suto vyavasthitah

SYNONYMS

tat — that; vañcayitvā — avoiding; tam — him; adhokshajah — the transcendental Lord; rushā — angrily; pragrihya — seizing; dorbhyām — with His arms; parividhya — whirling around; pādayoh — by the legs; sa-avajñam — contemptuously; utsrijya — discarding; dhanuh — of bow-lengths; śata — one hundred; antare — to the distance; yathā — as; uragam — a snake; tārkshya — of Kardama Muni; sutah — the son (Garuda); vyavasthitah — standing.

TRANSLATION

But the transcendental Lord dodged Keśī's blow and then with His arms angrily seized the demon by the legs, whirled him around in the air and contemptuously threw him the distance of one hundred bow-lengths, just as Garuda might throw a snake. Lord Krishna then stood there.
