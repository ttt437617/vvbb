Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 37: The Killing of the Demons Keśi and Vyoma

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.37.29

giri-daryām vinikshipya

nītam nītam mahāsurah

śilayā pidadhe dvāram

catuh-pañcāvaśeshitāh

SYNONYMS

giri — of a mountain; daryām — in a cave; vinikshipya — throwing; nītam nītam — gradually bringing them; mahā-asurah — the great demon; śilayā — with a stone; pidadhe — he blocked; dvāram — the entrance; catuh-pañca — four or five; avaśeshitāh — remained.

TRANSLATION

Gradually the great demon abducted more and more of the cowherd boys and cast them into a mountain cave, which he sealed shut with a boulder. Finally only four or five boys acting as sheep remained in the game.
