Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 37: The Killing of the Demons Keśi and Vyoma

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.37.9

devarshir upasańgamya

bhāgavata-pravaro nripa

krishnam aklishta-karmānam

rahasy etad abhāshata

SYNONYMS

deva-rishih — the sage among the demigods (Nārada Muni); upasańgamya — approaching; bhāgavata — of devotees of the Lord; pravarah — the most exalted; nripa — O King (Parīkshit); krishnam — Lord Krishna; aklishta — without trouble; karmānam — whose activities; rahasi — in privacy; etat — this; abhāshata — said.

TRANSLATION

My dear King, thereafter Lord Krishna was approached in a solitary place by the great sage among the demigods, Nārada Muni. That most exalted devotee spoke as follows to the Lord, who effortlessly performs His pastimes.

PURPORT

After speaking with Kamsa, Nārada went to see Lord Krishna. The Lord's Vrindāvana pastimes were nearly completed, and Nārada wanted to see those He would enact in Mathurā.
