Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 37: The Killing of the Demons Keśi and Vyoma

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.37.7

samedhamānena sa krishna-bāhunā

niruddha-vāyuś caranāmś ca vikshipan

prasvinna-gātrah parivritta-locanah

papāta landam visrijan kshitau vyasuh

SYNONYMS

samedhamānena — expanding; sah — he; krishna-bāhunā — by Lord Krishna's arm; niruddha — stopped; vāyuh — his breathing; caranān — his legs; ca — and; vikshipan — throwing about; prasvinna — perspiring; gātrah — his body; parivritta — rolling; locanah — his eyes; papāta — he fell down; landam — feces; visrijan — excreting; kshitau — onto the ground; vyasuh — lifeless.

TRANSLATION

As Lord Krishna's expanding arm completely blocked Keśī's breathing, his legs kicked convulsively, his body became covered with sweat, and his eyes rolled around. The demon then passed stool and fell on the ground, dead.
