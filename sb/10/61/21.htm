Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 61: Lord Balarāma Slays Rukmī

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.61.21

anāgatam atītam ca

vartamānam atīndriyam

viprakrishtam vyavahitam

samyak paśyanti yoginah

SYNONYMS

anāgatam — not yet happened; atītam — past; ca — also; vartamānam — present; atīndriyam — beyond the purview of the senses; viprakrishtam — distant; vyavahitam — blocked by obstacles; samyak — perfectly; paśyanti — see; yoginah — mystic yogīs.

TRANSLATION

Mystic yogīs can perfectly see that which has not yet happened, as well as things in the past or present, beyond the senses, remote or blocked by physical obstacles.

PURPORT

Here King Parīkshit encourages Śukadeva Gosvāmī to explain why Rukmī gave his daughter to Lord Krishna's son Pradyumna. The King stresses that since great yogīs like Śukadeva Gosvāmī know everything, the sage must also know this and should explain it to the anxious King.
