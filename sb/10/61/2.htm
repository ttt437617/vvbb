Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 61: Lord Balarāma Slays Rukmī

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.61.2

grihād anapagam vīkshya

rāja-putryo 'cyutam sthitam

preshtham nyamamsata svam svam

na tat-tattva-vidah striyah

SYNONYMS

grihāt — from their palaces; anapagam — never going out; vīkshya — seeing; rāja-putryah — daughters of kings; acyutam — Lord Krishna; sthitam — remaining; preshtham — most dear; nyamamsata — they considered; svam svam — each their own; na — not; tat — about Him; tattva — the truth; vidah — knowing; striyah — the women.

TRANSLATION

Because each of these princesses saw that Lord Acyuta never left her palace, each thought herself the Lord's favorite. These women did not understand the full truth about Him.

PURPORT

Śrīla Viśvanātha Cakravartī Thākura notes that Lord Krishna would leave the palaces only with the permission of His wives, and thus each one considered herself His favorite.
