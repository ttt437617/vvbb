Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 61: Lord Balarāma Slays Rukmī

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.61.24

rukminyās tanayām rājan

kritavarma-suto balī

upayeme viśālākshīm

kanyām cārumatīm kila

SYNONYMS

rukminyāh — of Rukminī; tanayām — the daughter; rājan — O King; kritavarma-sutah — the son of Krtavarmā; balī — named Balī; upayeme — married; viśāla — broad; akshīm — whose eyes; kanyām — young, innocent girl; cārumatīm — named Cārumatī; kila — indeed.

TRANSLATION

O King, Balī, the son of Kritavarmā, married Rukminī's young daughter, large-eyed Cārumatī.

PURPORT

Śrīla Śrīdhara Svāmī explains that each of the Lord's queens had one daughter, and that this mention of Cārumatī's marriage is an indirect reference to the marriages of all these princesses.
