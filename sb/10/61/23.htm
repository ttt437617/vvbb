Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 61: Lord Balarāma Slays Rukmī

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.61.23

yady apy anusmaran vairam

rukmī krishnāvamānitah

vyatarad bhāgineyāya

sutām kurvan svasuh priyam

SYNONYMS

yadi api — although; anusmaran — always remembering; vairam — his enmity; rukmī — Rukmī; krishna — by Lord Krishna; avamānitah — insulted; vyatarat — granted; bhāgineyāya — to his sister's son; sutām — his daughter; kurvan — doing; svasuh — of his sister; priyam — the pleasing.

TRANSLATION

Though Rukmī always remembered his enmity toward Lord Krishna, who had insulted him, in order to please his sister he sanctioned his daughter's marriage to his nephew.

PURPORT

The answer to King Parīkshit's question is given here. Ultimately Rukmī approved his daughter's marriage to Pradyumna in order to please his sister, Rukminī.
