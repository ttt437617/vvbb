Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 61: Lord Balarāma Slays Rukmī

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.61.35

naivāksha-kovidā yūyam

gopālā vana-gocarāh

akshair dīvyanti rājāno

bānaiś ca na bhavādriśāh

SYNONYMS

na — not; eva — indeed; aksha — in playing at dice; kovidāh — expert; yūyam — You; gopālāh — cowherds; vana — in the forest; gocarāh — ranging about; akshaih — with dice; dīvyanti — play; rājānah — kings; bānaih — with arrows; ca — and; na — not; bhavādriśāh — the likes of You.

TRANSLATION

[Rukmī said:] You cowherds who wander about the forests know nothing about dice. Playing with dice and sporting with arrows are only for kings, not for the likes of You.
