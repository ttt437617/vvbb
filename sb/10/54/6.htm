Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 54: The Marriage of Krishna and Rukminī

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.54.6

teshām tad-vikramam vīrā

gada-sańkarshanādayah

amrishyamānā nārācair

jaghnur haya-gajān rathān

SYNONYMS

teshām — by them (the opposing kings); tat — that; vikramam — show of prowess; vīrāh — the heroes; gada — Gada, the younger brother of Lord Krishna; sańkarshana — Lord Balarāma; ādayah — and others; amrishyamānāh — not tolerating; nārācaih — with arrows made of iron; jaghnuh — they struck; haya — horses; gajān — elephants; rathān — and chariots.

TRANSLATION

The heroes of the Lord's army, headed by Gada and Sańkarshana, could not tolerate the aggression of the opposing kings. Thus with iron arrows they began to strike down the enemy's horses, elephants and chariots.
