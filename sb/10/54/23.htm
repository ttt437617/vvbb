Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 54: The Marriage of Krishna and Rukminī

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.54.23

vikatthamānah kumatir

īśvarasyāpramāna-vit

rathenaikena govindam

tishtha tishthety athāhvayat

SYNONYMS

vikatthamānah — boasting; ku-matih — foolish; īśvarasya — of the Supreme Lord; apramāna-vit — not knowing the dimensions; rathena ekena — with a single chariot; govindam — to Lord Krishna; tishtha tishtha — stand and fight; iti — so saying; atha — then; āhvayat — he called.

TRANSLATION

Boasting thus, foolish Rukmī, ignorant of the true extent of the Supreme Lord's power, approached Lord Govinda in his lone chariot and challenged Him, "Just stand and fight!"

PURPORT

It appears from these verses that though Rukmī set out with an entire military division, he personally rushed up to Lord Krishna to fight with Him.
