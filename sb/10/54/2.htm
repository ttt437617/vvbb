Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 54: The Marriage of Krishna and Rukminī

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.54.2

tān āpatata ālokya

yādavānīka-yūthapāh

tasthus tat-sammukhā rājan

visphūrjya sva-dhanūmshi te

SYNONYMS

tān — them; āpatatah — in pursuit; ālokya — seeing; yādava-anīka — of the Yādava army; yūtha-pah — the officers; tasthuh — stood; tat — them; sammukhāh — directly facing; rājan — O King (Parīkshit); visphūrjya — twanging; sva — their; dhanūmshi — bows; te — they.

TRANSLATION

The commanders of the Yādava army, seeing the enemy racing to attack, turned to face them and stood firm, O King, twanging their bows.
