Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 54: The Marriage of Krishna and Rukminī

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.54.43

ātma-moho nrinām eva

kalpate deva-māyayā

suhrid durhrid udāsīna

iti dehātma-māninām

SYNONYMS

ātma — about the self; mohah — the bewilderment; nrinām — of men; eva — only; kalpate — is effected; deva — of the Supreme Lord; māyayā — by the illusory, material energy; suhrit — a friend; durhrit — an enemy; udāsīnah — a neutral party; iti — thus thinking; deha — the body; ātma — as the self; māninām — for those who consider.

TRANSLATION

The Supreme Lord's Māyā makes men forget their real selves, and thus, taking the body for the self, they consider others to be friends, enemies or neutral parties.
