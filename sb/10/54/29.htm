Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 54: The Marriage of Krishna and Rukminī

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.54.29

parigham pattiśam śūlam

carmāsī śakti-tomarau

yad yad āyudham ādatta

tat sarvam so 'cchinad dharih

SYNONYMS

parigham — spiked iron bludgeon; pattiśam — three-pointed spear; śūlam — lance; carma-asī — shield and sword; śakti — pike; tomarau — javelin; yat yat — whatever; āyudham — weapon; ādatta — he took up; tat sarvam — all of them; sah — He; acchinat — broke; harih — Lord Krishna.

TRANSLATION

Iron bludgeon, three-pointed spear, sword and shield, pike, javelin — whatever weapon Rukmī picked up, Lord Hari smashed it to bits.
