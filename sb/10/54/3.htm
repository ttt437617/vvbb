Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 54: The Marriage of Krishna and Rukminī

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.54.3

aśva-prishthe gaja-skandhe

rathopasthe 'stra kovidāh

mumucuh śara-varshāni

meghā adrishv apo yathā

SYNONYMS

aśva-prishthe — on horseback; gaja — of elephants; skandhe — on the shoulders; ratha — of chariots; upasthe — on the seats; astra — of weapons; kovidāh — those expert in the use; mumucuh — released; śara — of arrows; varshāni — rains; meghāh — clouds; adrishu — upon mountains; apah — water; yathā — as.

TRANSLATION

Mounted on the backs of horses, the shoulders of elephants and the seats of chariots, the enemy kings, expert with weapons, rained down arrows upon the Yadus like clouds pouring rain on mountains.
