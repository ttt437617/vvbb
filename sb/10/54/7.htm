Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 54: The Marriage of Krishna and Rukminī

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.54.7

petuh śirāmsi rathinām

aśvinām gajinām bhuvi

sa-kundala-kirītāni

soshnīshāni ca kotiśah

SYNONYMS

petuh — fell; śirāmsi — the heads; rathinām — of those riding on chariots; aśvinām — of those riding horses; gajinām — of those riding elephants; bhuvi — to the ground; sa — with; kundala — earrings; kirītāni — and helmets; sa — with; ushnīshāni — turbans; ca — and; kotiśah — by the millions.

TRANSLATION

The heads of soldiers fighting on chariots, horses and elephants fell to the ground by the millions; some heads wore earrings and helmets, others turbans.
