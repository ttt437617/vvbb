Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 54: The Marriage of Krishna and Rukminī

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.54.11

bho bhoh purusha-śārdūla

daurmanasyam idam tyaja

na priyāpriyayo rājan

nishthā dehishu driśyate

SYNONYMS

bhoh bhoh — O sir; purusha — among men; śārdūla — O tiger; daurmana-syam — depressed state of mind; idam — this; tyaja — give up; na — no; priya — of the desirable; apriyayoh — or the undesirable; rājan — O King; nishthā — permanence; dehishu — among embodied beings; driśyate — is seen.

TRANSLATION

[Jarāsandha said:] Listen, Śiśupāla, O tiger among men, give up your depression. After all, embodied beings' happiness and unhappiness is never seen to be permanent, O King.
