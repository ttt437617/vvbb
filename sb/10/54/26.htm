Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 54: The Marriage of Krishna and Rukminī

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.54.26

yāvan na me hato bānaih

śayīthā muñca dārīkām

smayan krishno dhanuś chittvā

shadbhir vivyādha rukminam

SYNONYMS

yāvat — while; na — not; me — my; hatah — killed; bānaih — by the arrows; śayīthah — you lie down; muñca — release; dārīkām — the girl; smayan — smiling; krishnah — Lord Krishna; dhanuh — his bow; chittvā — breaking; shadbhih — with six (arrows); vivyādha — pierced; rukminam — Rukmī.

TRANSLATION

"Release the girl before You are struck dead by my arrows and made to lie down!" In response to this, Lord Krishna smiled, and with six arrows He struck Rukmī and broke his bow.

PURPORT

Śrīla Viśvanātha Cakravartī points out that in fact Lord Krishna was meant to lie down together with Rukminī on a beautiful bed of flowers, but out of shyness Rukmī did not directly mention this point.
