Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 54: The Marriage of Krishna and Rukminī

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.54.25

yatra yāsi svasāram me

mushitvā dhvāńksha-vad dhavih

harishye 'dya madam manda

māyinah kūta-yodhinah

SYNONYMS

yatra — wherever; yāsi — You go; svasāram — sister; me — my; mushitvā — having stolen; dhvāńksha-vat — like a crow; havih — the sacrificial butter; harishye — I will remove; adya — today; madam — Your false pride; manda — You fool; māyinah — of the deceiver; kūta — cheating; yodhinah — of the fighter.

TRANSLATION

"Wherever You go, carrying off my sister like a crow stealing sacrificial butter, I will follow. This very day I shall relieve You of Your false pride, You fool, You deceiver, You cheater in battle!

PURPORT

In his hysterical attack, Rukmī displays the very qualities he attributes to Śrī Krishna. Every living being is part and parcel of the Lord and belongs to the Lord. Therefore Rukmī was like a crow trying to steal the sacrificial offering meant for the enjoyment of the Lord.
