Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 54: The Marriage of Krishna and Rukminī

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.54.27

ashtabhiś caturo vāhān

dvābhyām sūtam dhvajam tribhih

sa cānyad dhanur ādhāya

krishnam vivyādha pañcabhih

SYNONYMS

ashtabhih — with eight (arrows); caturah — the four; vāhān — horses; dvābhyām — with two; sūtam — the chariot driver; dhvajam — the flagpole; tribhih — with three; sah — he, Rukmī; ca — and; anyat — another; dhanuh — bow; ādhāya — taking up; krishnam — Krishna; vivyādha — pierced; pañcabhih — with five.

TRANSLATION

The Lord struck Rukmī's four horses with eight arrows, his chariot driver with two, and the chariot's flag with three. Rukmī grabbed another bow and struck Lord Krishna with five arrows.
