Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 54: The Marriage of Krishna and Rukminī

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.54.49

tasmād ajñāna-jam śokam

ātma-śosha-vimohanam

tattva-jñānena nirhritya

sva-sthā bhava śuci-smite

SYNONYMS

tasmāt — therefore; ajñāna — out of ignorance; jam — born; śokam — the lamentation; ātma — yourself; śosha — drying up; vimohanam — and bewildering; tattva — of the truth; jñānena — with knowledge; nirhritya — dispelling; sva-sthā — reinstated in your natural mood; bhava — please be; śuci-smite — O you whose smile is pure.

TRANSLATION

Therefore, with transcendental knowledge dispel the grief that is weakening and confounding your mind. Please resume your natural mood, O princess of the pristine smile.

PURPORT

Lord Balarāma reminds Śrīmatī Rukminī that she is the eternal goddess of fortune performing pastimes with the Lord in this world and should thus give up her so-called grief.
