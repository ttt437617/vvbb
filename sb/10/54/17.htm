Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 54: The Marriage of Krishna and Rukminī

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.54.17

śrī-śuka uvāca

evam prabodhito mitraiś

caidyo 'gāt sānugah puram

hata-śeshāh punas te 'pi

yayuh svam svam puram nripāh

SYNONYMS

śrī-śukah uvāca — Śukadeva Gosvāmī said; evam — thus; prabodhitah — persuaded; mitraih — by his friends; caidyah — Śiśupāla; agāt — went; sa-anugah — with his followers; puram — to his city; hata — from the killed; śeshāh — who remained; punah — again; te — they; api — also; yayuh — went; svam svam — each to his own; puram — city; nripāh — kings.

TRANSLATION

Śukadeva Gosvāmī said: Thus persuaded by his friends, Śiśupāla took his followers and went back to his capital. The surviving warriors also returned to their respective cities.
