Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 54: The Marriage of Krishna and Rukminī

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.54.36

krishnāntikam upavrajya

dadriśus tatra rukminam

tathā-bhūtam hata-prāyam

drishtvā sańkarshano vibhuh

vimucya baddham karuno

bhagavān krishnam abravīt

SYNONYMS

krishna — of Krishna; antikam — the proximity; upavrajya — approaching; dadriśuh — they (the Yadu soldiers) saw; tatra — there; rukminam — Rukmī; tathā-bhūtam — in such a condition; hata — dead; prāyam — practically; drishtvā — seeing; sańkarshanah — Balarāma; vibhuh — the omnipotent; vimucya — releasing; baddham — the bound-up (Rukmī); karunah — compassionate; bhagavān — the Lord; krishnam — to Krishna; abravīt — said.

TRANSLATION

As the Yadus approached Lord Krishna, they saw Rukmī in this sorry condition, practically dying of shame. When the all-powerful Lord Balarāma saw Rukmī, He compassionately released him and spoke the following to Lord Krishna.
