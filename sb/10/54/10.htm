Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 54: The Marriage of Krishna and Rukminī

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.54.10

śiśupālam samabhyetya

hrita-dāram ivāturam

nashta-tvisham gatotsāham

śushyad-vadanam abruvan

SYNONYMS

śiśupālam — Śiśupāla; samabhyetya — approaching; hrita — stolen; dāram — whose wife; iva — as if; āturam — perturbed; nashta — lost; tvisham — whose color; gata — gone; utsāham — whose enthusiasm; śushyat — dried up; vadanam — whose face; abruvan — they addressed.

TRANSLATION

The kings approached Śiśupāla, who was disturbed like a man who has lost his wife. His complexion was drained of color, his enthusiasm was gone, and his face appeared dried up. The kings spoke to him as follows.
