Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 77: Lord Krishna Slays the Demon Śālva

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.77.3

caturbhiś caturo vāhān

sūtam ekena cāhanat

dvābhyam dhanuś ca ketum ca

śarenānyena vai śirah

SYNONYMS

caturbhih — with four (arrows); caturah — four; vāhān — carriers; sūtam — the driver; ekena — with one; ca — and; ahanat — He struck; dvābhyām — with two; dhanuh — the bow; ca — and; ketum — the flag; ca — and; śarena — with an arrow; anyena — another; vai — indeed; śirah — the head.

TRANSLATION

With four of these arrows He struck Dyumān's four horses, with one arrow, his driver, with two more arrows, his bow and chariot flag, and with the last arrow, Dyumān's head.
