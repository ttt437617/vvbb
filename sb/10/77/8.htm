Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 77: Lord Krishna Slays the Demon Śālva

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.77.8

āha cāham ihāyāta

ārya-miśrābhisańgatah

rājanyāś caidya-pakshīyā

nūnam hanyuh purīm mama

SYNONYMS

āha — He said; ca — and; aham — I; iha — to this place (Indraprastha); āyātah — having come; ārya — by My elder (brother Balarāma); miśra — the distinguished personality; abhisańgatah — accompanied; rājanyāh — kings; caidya-pakshīyāh — siding with Caidya (Śiśupāla); nūnam — certainly; hanyuh — must be attacking; purīm — city; mama — My.

TRANSLATION

The Lord said to Himself: Because I have come here with My respected elder brother, kings partial to Śiśupāla may well be attacking My capital city.
