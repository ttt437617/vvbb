Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 19: Swallowing the Forest Fire

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.19.2

ajā gāvo mahishyaś ca

nirviśantyo vanād vanam

īshīkātavīm nirviviśuh

krandantyo dāva-tarshitāh

SYNONYMS

ajāh — the goats; gāvah — the cows; mahishyah — the buffalo; ca — and; nirviśantyah — entering; vanāt — from one forest; vanam — to another forest; īshīkā-atavīm — a forest of canes; nirviviśuh — they entered; krandantyah — crying out; dāva — because of a forest fire; tarshitāh — thirsty .

TRANSLATION

Passing from one part of the great forest to another, the goats, cows and buffalo eventually entered an area overgrown with sharp canes. The heat of a nearby forest fire made them thirsty, and they cried out in distress.
