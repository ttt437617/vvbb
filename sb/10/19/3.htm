Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 19: Swallowing the Forest Fire

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.19.3

te 'paśyantah paśūn gopāh

krishna-rāmādayas tadā

jātānutāpā na vidur

vicinvanto gavām gatim

SYNONYMS

te — they; apaśyantah — not seeing; paśūn — the animals; gopāh — the cowherd boys; krishna-rāma-ādayah — led by Krishna and Rāma; tadā — then; jāta-anutāpāh — feeling remorse; na viduh — they did not know; vicinvantah — searching out; gavām — of the cows; gatim — the path.

TRANSLATION

Not seeing the cows before them, Krishna, Rāma and Their cowherd friends suddenly felt repentant for having neglected them. The boys searched all around, but could not discover where they had gone.
