Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 19: Swallowing the Forest Fire

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.19.8

tam āpatantam parito davāgnim

gopāś ca gāvah prasamīkshya bhītāh

ūcuś ca krishnam sa-balam prapannā

yathā harim mrityu-bhayārditā janāh

SYNONYMS

tam — that; āpatantam — setting upon them; paritah — on all sides; dava-agnim — the forest fire; gopāh — the cowherd boys; ca — and; gāvah — the cows; prasamīkshya — intensely watching; bhītāh — afraid; ūcuh — they addressed; ca — and; krishnam — Lord Krishna; sa-balam — and Lord Balarāma; prapannāh — taking shelter; yathā — as; harim — the Supreme Personality of Godhead; mrityu — of death; bhaya — by fear; arditāh — troubled; janāh — persons.

TRANSLATION

As the cows and cowherd boys stared at the forest fire attacking them on all sides, they became fearful. The boys then approached Krishna and Balarāma for shelter, just as those who are disturbed by fear of death approach the Supreme Personality of Godhead. The boys addressed Them as follows.
