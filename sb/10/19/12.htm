Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 19: Swallowing the Forest Fire

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.19.12

tatheti mīlitāksheshu

bhagavān agnim ulbanam

pītvā mukhena tān kricchrād

yogādhīśo vyamocayat

SYNONYMS

tathā — all right; iti — thus speaking; mīlita — closing; aksheshu — their eyes; bhagavān — the Supreme Lord; agnim — the fire; ulbanam — terrible; pītvā — drinking; mukhena — with His mouth; tān — them; kricchrāt — from the danger; yoga-adhīśah — the supreme controller of all mystic power; vyamocayat — delivered .

TRANSLATION

"All right," the boys replied, and immediately closed their eyes. Then the Supreme Lord, the master of all mystic power, opened His mouth and swallowed the terrible fire, saving His friends from danger.

PURPORT

The cowherd boys were suffering from extreme fatigue, hunger and thirst, and were about to be consumed by a horrible forest fire. All this is indicated here by the word kricchrāt.
