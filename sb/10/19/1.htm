Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 19: Swallowing the Forest Fire

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.19.1

śrī-śuka uvāca

krīdāsakteshu gopeshu

tad-gāvo dūra-cārinīh

svairam carantyo viviśus

trina-lobhena gahvaram

SYNONYMS

śrī-śukah uvāca — Śrī Śukadeva Gosvāmī said; krīdā — in their playing; āsakteshu — while they were completely absorbed; gopeshu — the cowherd boys; tat-gāvah — their cows; dūra-cārinīh — wandering far away; svairam — independently; carantyah — grazing; viviśuh — they entered; trina — for grass; lobhena — out of greed; gahvaram — a dense forest.

TRANSLATION

Śukadeva Gosvāmī said: While the cowherd boys were completely absorbed in playing, their cows wandered far away. They hungered for more grass, and with no one to watch them they entered a dense forest.
