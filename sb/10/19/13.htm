Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 19: Swallowing the Forest Fire

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.19.13

tataś ca te 'kshīny unmīlya

punar bhāndīram āpitāh

niśamya vismitā āsann

ātmānam gāś ca mocitāh

SYNONYMS

tatah — then; ca — and; te — they; akshīni — their eyes; unmīlya — opening; punah — again; bhāndīram — to Bhāndīra; āpitāh — brought; niśamya — seeing; vismitāh — amazed; āsan — they became; ātmānam — themselves; gāh — the cows; ca — and; mocitāh — saved.

TRANSLATION

The cowherd boys opened their eyes and were amazed to find not only that they and the cows had been saved from the terrible fire but that they had all been brought back to the Bhāndīra tree.
