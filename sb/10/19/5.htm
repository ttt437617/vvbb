Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 19: Swallowing the Forest Fire

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.19.5

muñjātavyām bhrashta-mārgam

krandamānam sva-godhanam

samprāpya trishitāh śrāntās

tatas te sannyavartayan

SYNONYMS

muñjā-atavyām — in the Muñjā forest; bhrashta-mārgam — who had lost their way; krandamānam — crying; sva — their own; go-dhanam — cows (and other animals); samprāpya — finding; trishitāh — who were thirsty; śrāntāh — and tired; tatah — then; te — they, the cowherd boys; sannyavartayan — turned them all back.

TRANSLATION

Within the Muñjā forest the cowherd boys finally found their valuable cows, who had lost their way and were crying. Then the boys, thirsty and tired, herded the cows onto the path back home.
