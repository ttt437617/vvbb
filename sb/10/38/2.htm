Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 38: Akrūra's Arrival in Vrindāvana

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.38.2

gacchan pathi mahā-bhāgo

bhagavaty ambujekshane

bhaktim parām upagata

evam etad acintayat

SYNONYMS

gacchan — traveling; pathi — along the road; mahā-bhāgah — the greatly fortunate; bhagavati — for the Supreme Personality of Godhead; ambuja-īkshane — the lotus-eyed Lord; bhaktim — devotion; parām — exceptional; upagatah — he experienced; evam — thus; etat — this (as follows); acintayat — he thought.

TRANSLATION

As he traveled on the road, the great soul Akrūra felt tremendous devotion for the lotus-eyed Personality of Godhead, and thus he began to consider as follows.
