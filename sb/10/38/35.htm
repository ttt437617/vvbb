Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 38: Akrūra's Arrival in Vrindāvana

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.38.35

bhagavad-darśanāhlāda-

bāshpa-paryākulekshanah

pulakacitāńga autkanthyāt

svākhyāne nāśakan nripa

SYNONYMS

bhagavat — the Supreme Personality of Godhead; darśana — because of seeing; āhlāda — due to the joy; bāshpa — with tears; paryākula — overflowing; īkshanah — whose eyes; pulaka — with eruptions; ācita — marked; ańgah — whose limbs; autkanthyāt — from eagerness; sva-ākhyāne — to announce himself; na aśakat — he was not able; nripa — O King.

TRANSLATION

The joy of seeing the Supreme Lord flooded Akrūra's eyes with tears and decorated his limbs with eruptions of ecstasy. He felt such eagerness that he could not speak to present himself, O King.
