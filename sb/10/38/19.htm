Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 38: Akrūra's Arrival in Vrindāvana

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.38.19

apy ańghri-mūle 'vahitam kritāñjalim

mām īkshitā sa-smitam ārdrayā driśā

sapady apadhvasta-samasta-kilbisho

vodhā mudam vīta-viśańka ūrjitām

SYNONYMS

api — and; ańghri — of His feet; mūle — at the base; avahitam — fixed; krita-añjalim — with joined palms; mām — me; īkshitā — will look upon; sasmitam — smiling; ārdrayā — with an affectionate; driśā — glance; sapadi — immediately; apadhvasta — eradicated; samasta — all; kilbishah — contamination; vodhā — I will achieve; mudam — happiness; vīta — freed; viśańkah — from doubt; ūrjitām — intense.

TRANSLATION

Thus He will cast His smiling, affectionate glance upon me as I remain fixed with joined palms, fallen in obeisances at His feet. Then all my contamination will at once be dispelled, and I will give up all doubts and feel the most intense bliss.
