Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 38: Akrūra's Arrival in Vrindāvana

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.38.24

śrī-śuka uvāca

iti sañcintayan krishnam

śvaphalka-tanayo 'dhvani

rathena gokulam prāptah

sūryaś cāsta-girim nripa

SYNONYMS

śrī-śukah uvāca — Śukadeva Gosvāmī said; iti — thus; sañcintayan — thinking deeply; krishnam — about Lord Krishna; śvaphalka-tanayah — Akrūra, the son of Śvaphalka; adhvani — on the road; rathena — by his chariot; gokulam — the village of Gokula; prāptah — reached; sūryah — the sun; ca — and; asta-girim — the mountain behind which the sun sets; nripa — O King (Parīkshit).

TRANSLATION

Śukadeva Gosvāmī continued: My dear King, while the son of Śvaphalka, traveling on the road, thus meditated deeply on Śrī Krishna, he reached Gokula as the sun was beginning to set.

PURPORT

Śrīla Śrīdhara Svāmī comments that although Akrūra did not even notice the road, being deeply absorbed in meditation on Lord Krishna, he still reached Gokula on his chariot.
