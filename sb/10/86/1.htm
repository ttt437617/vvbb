Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 86: Arjuna Kidnaps Subhadrā, and Krishna Blesses His Devotees

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.86.1

śrī-rājovāca

brahman veditum icchāmah

svasārām rāma-krishnayoh

yathopayeme vijayo

yā mamāsīt pitāmahī

SYNONYMS

śrī-rājā uvāca — the great King (Parīkshit) said; brahman — O brāhmana (Śukadeva); veditum — to know; icchāmah — we wish; svasāram — the sister; rāma-krishnayoh — of Balarāma and Krishna; yathā — how; upayeme — married; vijayah — Arjuna; yā — she who; mama — my; āsīt — was; pitāmahī — the grandmother.

TRANSLATION

King Parīkshit said: O brāhmana, we would like to learn how Arjuna married Lord Balarāma's and Lord Krishna's sister, who was my grandmother.

PURPORT

Parīkshit Mahārāja turns now to the topic of the marriage of Lord Krishna's sister, Subhadrā. In the opinion of Śrīla Śrīdhara Svāmī, King Parīkshit's question here follows from the previous narration because Arjuna's winning the hand of Subhadrā was just as difficult a feat as Lord Krishna's retrieving the sons of Devakī from the realm of the dead, since Lord Balarāma Himself opposed Subhadrā's marriage to Arjuna.
