Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 86: Arjuna Kidnaps Subhadrā, and Krishna Blesses His Devotees

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.86.56

carācaram idam viśvam

bhāvā ye cāsya hetavah

mad-rūpānīti cetasy

ādhatte vipro mad-īkshayā

SYNONYMS

cara — moving; acaram — and nonmoving; idam — this; viśvam — universe; bhāvāh — elemental categories; ye — which; ca — and; asya — its; hetavah — sources; mat — My; rūpāni — forms; iti — such a thought; cetasi — within his mind; ādhatte — maintains; viprah — a brāhmana; mat — of Me; īkshayā — by his perception.

TRANSLATION

Because he has realized Me, a brāhmana is firmly fixed in the knowledge that everything moving and nonmoving in the universe, and also the primary elements of its creation, are all manifest forms expanded from Me.
