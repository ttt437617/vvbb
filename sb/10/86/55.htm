Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 86: Arjuna Kidnaps Subhadrā, and Krishna Blesses His Devotees

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.86.55

dushprajñā aviditvaivam

avajānanty asūyavah

gurum mām vipram ātmānam

arcādāv ijya-drishtayah

SYNONYMS

dushprajñāh — those of corrupted intelligence; aviditvā — failing to understand; evam — in this way; avajānanti — neglect; asūyavah — and behave enviously toward; gurum — their spiritual master; mām — Me; vipram — the learned brāhmana; ātmānam — their own self; arcā-ādaū — in the visibly manifest Deity of the Lord; ijya — as being worshipable; drishtayah — whose vision.

TRANSLATION

Ignorant of this truth, foolish people neglect and enviously offend a learned brāhmana, who, being nondifferent from Me, is their spiritual master and very self. They consider worshipable only such obvious manifestations of divinity as My Deity form.
