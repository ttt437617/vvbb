Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 86: Arjuna Kidnaps Subhadrā, and Krishna Blesses His Devotees

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.86.23

drishtvā ta uttamah-ślokam

prīty-utphulānanāśayāh

kair dhritāñjalibhir nemuh

śruta-pūrvāms tathā munīn

SYNONYMS

drishtvā — seeing; te — they; uttamah-ślokam — Lord Krishna, who is praised in sublime poetry; prīti — with love; utphulla — broadly blossoming; ānana — their faces; āśayāh — and hearts; kaih — on their heads; dhrita — held; añjalibhih — with joined palms; nemuh — they bowed down; śruta — heard of; pūrvān — before; tathā — also; munīn — to the sages.

TRANSLATION

As soon as the people saw Lord Uttamahśloka, their faces and hearts blossomed with affection. Joining their palms above their heads, they bowed down to the Lord and to the sages accompanying Him, whom they had previously only heard about.
