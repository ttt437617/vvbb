Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 22: Krishna Steals the Garments of the Unmarried Gopīs

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.22.37

tatra gāh pāyayitvāpah

su-mrishtāh śītalāh śivāh

tato nripa svayam gopāh

kāmam svādu papur jalam

SYNONYMS

tatra — there; gāh — the cows; pāyayitvā — making drink; apah — the water; su-mrishtāh — very clear; śītalāh — cool; śivāh — wholesome; tatah — then; nripa — O King Parīkshit; svayam — themselves; gopāh — the cowherd boys; kāmam — freely; svādu — sweet-tasting; papuh — they drank; jalam — the water.

TRANSLATION

The cowherd boys let the cows drink the clear, cool and wholesome water of the Yamunā. O King Parīkshit, the cowherd boys themselves also drank that sweet water to their full satisfaction.
