Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 48: Krishna Pleases His Devotees

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.48.33

pitary uparate bālāh

saha mātrā su-duhkhitāh

ānītāh sva-puram rājñā

vasanta iti śuśruma

SYNONYMS

pitari — their father; uparate — when he passed away; bālāh — young boys; saha — together with; mātrā — their mother; su — very; duhkhitāh — distressed; ānītāh — brought; sva — to his own; puram — capital city; rājñā — by the King; vasante — they are residing; iti — thus; śuśruma — We have heard.

TRANSLATION

We have heard that when their father passed away, the young Pāndavas were brought with their anguished mother to the capital city by King Dhritarāshtra, and that they are now living there.
