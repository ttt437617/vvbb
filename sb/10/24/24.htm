Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 24: Worshiping Govardhana Hill

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.24.24

na nah purojanapadā

na grāmā na grihā vayam

vanaukasas tāta nityam

vana-śaila-nivāsinah

SYNONYMS

na — not; nah — for us; purah — the cities; jana-padāh — developed inhabited area; na — not; grāmāh — villages; na — not; grihāh — living in permanent homes; vayam — we; vana-okasah — dwelling in the forests; tāta — My dear father; nityam — always; vana — in the forests; śaila — and on the hills; nivāsinah — living.

TRANSLATION

My dear father, our home is not in the cities or towns or villages. Being forest dwellers, we always live in the forest and on the hills.

PURPORT

Lord Krishna here points out that the residents of Vrindāvana should recognize their relationship with Govardhana Hill and with the forests of Vrindāvana, and not worry about a distant demigod like Indra. Having concluded His argument, Lord Krishna makes a radical proposal in the following verse.
