Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 24: Worshiping Govardhana Hill

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.24.23

rajasā coditā meghā

varshanty ambūni sarvatah

prajās tair eva sidhyanti

mahendrah kim karishyati

SYNONYMS

rajasā — by passion; coditāh — impelled; meghāh — the clouds; varshanti — pour down; ambūni — their water; sarvatah — everywhere; prajāh — the population; taih — by that water; eva — simply; sidhyanti — maintain their existence; mahā-indrah — the great Indra; kim — what; karishyati — can do.

TRANSLATION

Impelled by the material mode of passion, the clouds pour down their rain everywhere, and by this rain all creatures gain their sustenance. What has the great Indra to do with this arrangement?

PURPORT

Lord Krishna continues His mechanistic explanation of existence, concluding mahendrah kim karishyati: "Who needs the great Indra, since the rain, sent by the clouds, which in turn are impelled by the mode of passion, is actually producing everyone's food?" The word sarvatah indicates that the clouds magnanimously send their rain even on the ocean, rocks and barren land, where there is no apparent necessity for such sweet water.
