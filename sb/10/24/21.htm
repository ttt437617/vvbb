Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 24: Worshiping Govardhana Hill

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.24.21

krishi-vānijya-go-rakshā

kusīdam tūryam ucyate

vārtā catur-vidhā tatra

vayam go-vrittayo 'niśam

SYNONYMS

krishi — farming; vānijya — commerce; go-rakshā — and protecting cows: kusīdam — banking; tūryam — the fourth; ucyate — is said; vārtā — the occupational duty; catuh-vidhā — fourfold; tatra — among these; vayam — we; go-vrittayah — engaged in protecting the cows; aniśam — without cessation.

TRANSLATION

The occupational duties of the vaiśya are conceived in four divisions: farming, commerce, cow protection and moneylending. Out of these, we as a community are always engaged in cow protection.
