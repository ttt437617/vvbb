Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 53: Krishna Kidnaps Rukminī

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.53.1

śrī-śuka uvāca

vaidarbhyāh sa tu sandeśam

niśamya yadu-nandanah

pragrihya pāninā pānim

prahasann idam abravīt

SYNONYMS

śrī-śukah uvāca — Śukadeva Gosvāmī said; vaidarbhyāh — of the princess of Vidarbha; sah — He; tu — and; sandeśam — the confidential message; niśamya — hearing; yadu-nandanah — Lord Krishna, the descendant of Yadu; pragrihya — taking; pāninā — by His hand; pānim — the hand (of the brāhmana messenger); prahasan — smiling; idam — this; abravīt — said.

TRANSLATION

Śukadeva Gosvāmī said: Thus hearing the confidential message of Princess Vaidarbhī, Lord Yadunandana took the brāhmana's hand and, smiling, spoke to him as follows.
