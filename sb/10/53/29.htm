Canto 10: The Summum BonumChapter 53: Krishna Kidnaps Rukminī

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 10.53.29

sā tam prahrishta-vadanam

avyagrātma-gatim satī

ālakshya lakshanābhijñā

samapricchac chuci-smitā

SYNONYMS

sa — she; tam — him; prahrishta — filled with joy; vadanam — whose face; avyagra — unagitated; ātma — of whose body; gatim — the movement; satī — the saintly young woman; ālakshya — noting; lakshana — of symptoms; abhijñā — an expert knower; samapricchat — inquired; śuci — pure; smitā — with a smile.

TRANSLATION

Noting the brāhmana's joyful face and serene movements, saintly Rukminī, who could expertly interpret such symptoms, inquired from him with a pure smile.
