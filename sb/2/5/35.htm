Canto 2: The Cosmic ManifestationChapter 5: The Cause of All Causes

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 2.5.35

sa eva purushas tasmād

andam nirbhidya nirgatah

sahasrorv-ańghri-bāhv-akshah

sahasrānana-śīrshavān

SYNONYMS

sah — He (the Lord); eva — Himself; purushah — the Supreme Personality of Godhead; tasmāt — from within the universe; andam — Hiranyagarbha; nirbhidya — dividing; nirgatah — came out; sahasra — thousands; ūru — thighs; ańghri — legs; bāhu — arms; akshah — eyes; sahasra — thousands of; ānana — mouths; śīrshavān — with heads also.

TRANSLATION

The Lord [Mahā-Vishnu], although lying in the Causal Ocean, came out of it, and dividing Himself as Hiranyagarbha, He entered into each universe and assumed the virāt-rūpa, with thousands of legs, arms, mouths, heads, etc.

PURPORT

The expansions of the planetary systems within each and every universe are situated in the different parts of the virāt-rūpa (universal form) of the Lord, and they are described as follows.
