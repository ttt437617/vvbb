Canto 2: The Cosmic ManifestationChapter 10: Bhāgavatam Is the Answer to All Questions

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 2.10.2

daśamasya viśuddhy-artham

navānām iha lakshanam

varnayanti mahātmānah

śrutenārthena cāñjasā

SYNONYMS

daśamasya — of the summum bonum; viśuddhi — isolation; artham — purpose; navānām — of the other nine; iha — in this Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam; lakshanam — symptoms; varnayanti — they describe; mahā-ātmānah — the great sages; śrutena — by Vedic evidences; arthena — by direct explanation; ca — and; añjasā — summarily.

TRANSLATION

To isolate the transcendence of the summum bonum, the symptoms of the rest are described sometimes by Vedic inference, sometimes by direct explanation, and sometimes by summary explanations given by the great sages.
