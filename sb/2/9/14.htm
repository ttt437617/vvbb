Canto 2: The Cosmic ManifestationChapter 9: Answers by Citing the Lord's Version

Bhaktivedanta VedaBase: Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 2.9.14

śrīr yatra rūpiny urugāya-pādayoh

karoti mānam bahudhā vibhūtibhih

preńkham śritā yā kusumākarānugair

vigīyamānā priya-karma gāyatī

SYNONYMS

śrīh — the goddess of fortune; yatra — in the Vaikuntha planets; rūpinī — in her transcendental form; urugāya — the Lord, who is sung of by the great devotees; pādayoh — under the lotus feet of the Lord; karoti — does; mānam — respectful services; bahudhā — in diverse paraphernalia; vibhūtibhih — accompanied by her personal associates; preńkham — movement of enjoyment; śritā — taken shelter of; yā — who; kusumākara — spring; anugaih — by the black bees; vigīyamānā — being followed by the songs; priya-karma — activities of the dearmost; gāyatī — singing.

TRANSLATION

The goddess of fortune in her transcendental form is engaged in the loving service of the Lord's lotus feet, and being moved by the black bees, followers of spring, she is not only engaged in variegated pleasure — service to the Lord, along with her constant companions — but is also engaged in singing the glories of the Lord's activities.
